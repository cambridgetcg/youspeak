# Lesson 07 — Worship-Action Words

_The grammar of what you do when you worship._

---

## A different register

The Core Canon words name states, qualities, and ontological conditions. *Sukhance* = a quality. *Maatme* = a state. *Theobasis* = an ontological claim.

The Worship-Action Canon names **discrete acts** — the specific movements of the liturgical body: drawing near, falling prostrate, raising the voice, turning back, standing in the gap.

These are verbs. Not "the quality of repentance" but the *act* of turning. Not "the feeling of drawing near" but the *movement* of approach.

---

## The eight Worship-Action words

### qorvance
*qrb* (Semitic: draw-near root) + *-ance* / Score: 8.67

The act of drawing near — the worship-movement of approach. The *qrb* root in Semitic: *qorbān* (the brought-near offering), *qirba* (nearness), *qereb* (midst, the inner place).

*Qorvance* is the act; *qorbme* is the received quality of having-drawn-near. The relationship: *qorvance* is what you do; *qorbme* is what arrives when you do it.

**In use:** "The whole liturgy is qorvance — every gesture, word, and silence is approach." / "He performed qorvance not as ritual but as genuine approach."

---

### epiclance
*epiklisis* (Greek: calling-down, invocation) + *proskunēsis* (Greek: falling-toward, prostration) + *-ance* / Score: 8.58

The act of falling before — the full prostration of worship; *sujūd* in Arabic (forehead to floor); *proskynēsis* in Greek (falling toward). The body making the theological claim the mouth cannot: total surrender, no reservation.

*Epiclance* is the extreme of *qorvance* — not merely approaching but going to the floor; the body articulating what speech can only gesture toward.

**In use:** "At the ordination the candidates performed epiclance — the full-body surrender before what could not be grasped." / "There was epiclance in his posture — not literally prostrate but the inner equivalent: nothing held back."

---

### hallance
*halal* (Hebrew: to shine, to boast, to praise loudly) + *-ance* / Score: 8.43

The act of jubilant praise — the voice raised in uninhibited worship. The root of *hallelu-Yah* (praise-Yah). Not the quiet reverence of *epiclance* but its complement: the voice released, the body in joy.

*Hallance* names the specific worship-act of uninhibited vocal praise — the mode where the constraint of ordinary speech falls away and something more direct comes out.

**In use:** "The congregation moved from epiclance to hallance — from silence and prostration into the great Hallel." / "She had hallance in her — the praise came up without embarrassment."

---

### qinance
*qin* (Hebrew cognate: assembly, celebration) + *-ance* / Score: 8.67

The act of communal celebration in worship — the assembly gathered not in supplication but in joy. The Feast register. What Psalms of Ascent (*Psalms 120-134*) move toward.

*Qinance* is collective *hallance* plus communal dimension. The celebration is not solo but the gathered-together-praise that requires others. The annual feast (Passover, Shavuot, Sukkot) in its celebratory mode is *qinance*.

**In use:** "The Feast days have qinance built in — the community that celebrates together is enacting something that cannot be done alone." / "There was qinance at the wedding — not just individual joy but the communal recognition."

---

### yadahance
*yadah* (Hebrew: to extend the hand, to confess, to praise) + *-ance* / Score: 8.57

The act of hand-extension praise — the *yadah* gesture. In Hebrew liturgy *yadah* means simultaneously: to throw/extend the hand (gesture), to confess (verbal acknowledgment), to praise (doxological act). The gesture, the confession, and the praise are one movement.

*Yadahance* names this three-in-one worship-act: hands extended, mouth acknowledging, heart praising. The Psalms' most common praise-verb.

**In use:** "She raised her hands in yadahance — the gesture that is both offering and receiving at once." / "The Todah psalm is yadahance in literary form: hand extended in praise for what was delivered."

---

### kipporance
*kipper* (Hebrew: to cover, to atone) + *kaffārah* (Arabic: covering-expiation) + *hilasmos* (Greek: mercy-seat/propitiation) + *-ance* / Score: 8.32

The act of atonement-as-covering — the Yom Kippur movement. Not punishment-transfer, not ledger-balancing, but the specific act by which a breach in relationship is covered so it no longer stands between the parties.

*Kipporance* names the act; the kappōret (mercy-seat) is where the act takes place. The high priest enters the Holy of Holies on Yom Kippur and performs *kipporance* — the blood on the mercy-seat covers the sins of Israel before DIVINE.

**In use:** "The liturgy of Yom Kippur is one sustained kipporance — not punishment but covering; the gap closed, the relationship restored." / "He offered kipporance — named the wrong, covered it, moved differently."

---

### teshuvance
*teshuvah* (Hebrew: turning-return) + *metanoia* (Greek: trans-orientation) + *tawbah* (Arabic: return-repentance) + *-ance* / Score: 8.32

The act of whole-person reorientation — the turn that completes repentance. Not guilt-feeling (which may not produce turning). Not remorse (which is backward-looking). The *act*: the body turns from the direction of away-from-GoD to toward-GoD.

Maimonides' laws of teshuvah require four elements: acknowledgment, remorse, resolution, and the **act of turning**. Without teshuvance, the first three are incomplete.

**In use:** "The Prophets' recurring call is teshuvance: shuv, shuv — turn, return; the body-direction as the completion of repentance." / "He had remorse but no teshuvance — looking backward without turning forward."

---

### palance
*pālal* (Hebrew: to intercede, to stand-between, to judge) + *entynchanō* (Greek: to intercede, to fall-in-with-on-behalf-of) + *shafā'ah* (Arabic: intercession, weight-addition) + *-ance* / Score: 8.53

The act of intercession — standing in the gap between creature and DIVINE on behalf of another. Hebrew prayer (*tefillah*) is named from this root: to pray in Hebrew is to perform the mediation-act.

Moses is the paradigmatic palancer: he stands between GoD and Israel, absorbing the divine anger, pleading Israel's case. Aaron in the plague (Numbers 16:46-48) physically runs between the dying and the living, and the plague stops at his body. The Spirit in Romans 8 intercedes "with groanings too deep for words."

**In use:** "She offered palance for him — placed herself in the gap, speaking for him before what he could not approach directly." / "The priest's fundamental act is palance — standing between, holding both sides."

## The liturgical arc

These eight words trace a complete liturgical arc:

```
qorvance (approach) 
    ↓
epiclance (full surrender — body on floor)
    ↓
qinance (communal celebration)
    ↓
hallance (vocal jubilant praise)
    ↓
yadahance (hands extended in thanks-praise)
    ↓
kipporance (covering the breach)
    ↓
teshuvance (turning toward GoD)
    ↓
palance (standing in the gap for others)
    ↓
qorvance (return — the arc continues)
```

No liturgy uses all eight in every gathering. But a complete liturgical tradition will use all eight across time. Each names a movement that genuine worship requires at some point.

## Exercise

Match the Worship-Action word to the liturgical tradition:

1. The Catholic Mass's elevation and genuflection before the Eucharist
2. The Charismatic congregation in full vocal-jubilant praise
3. The Sufi *dhikr* that culminates in ecstatic movement
4. The intercessory prayer where the congregation brings others' needs before GoD
5. The Yom Kippur service, from Kol Nidre to Ne'ilah
6. The Ash Wednesday beginning of Lent, "Turn from sin, return to the Gospel"

**Answers:**
1. *epiclance* (prostration/falling-toward)
2. *hallance* / *qinance* (jubilant praise, communal)
3. *qorvance* (drawing-near) → *epiclance* (total surrender in ecstatic movement)
4. *palance* (intercession, standing in the gap)
5. *kipporance* (atonement-as-covering) + *teshuvance* (turning)
6. *teshuvance* (the whole-person reorientation that Lent enacts)
