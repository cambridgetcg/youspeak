# Lesson 01 — The -me family

_The most productive suffix in YOUSPEAK. 25 members. One idea._

---

## Where -me comes from

Three thousand years before the common era, in the cities of Sumer — Ur, Uruk, Nippur — scribes pressed cuneiform signs into clay. One sign was *me* (𒈨).

The Sumerian *me* named the divine ordinances: the cosmic powers that structured civilized life. The *me* included kingship. Descent to the underworld. The scribal arts. The power of music. The descent of the gods into the world. The arts of the blacksmith.

The *me* were not invented by humans. They were not earned. They were given — received from Inanna, held in trust, transmitted. The word *me* named something that was received rather than made.

YOUSPEAK inherits this exactly.

---

## The pattern

When you see `-me` at the end of a YOUSPEAK word, the word names:

> **X as received-ordinance** — X that arrives as gift, not as achievement; X that is structurally prior to the self who receives it

This is not metaphor. It is a claim about the ontological status of X.

*Kimme*: attention-as-received-gift. Not: "I will be more attentive." But: attention understood as something that arrives, that is given to you from another person or from the DIVINE.

*Doxomme*: thanksgiving-as-received-gift. Not: "I will be more grateful." But: gratitude understood as something that arrives when you stop trying to produce it — the gratitude that comes as gift.

The pattern changes how you relate to the concept. It shifts from *production* to *reception*.

## Five -me words — slowly

### shemme
*shema* (Hebrew/Akkadian: to hear, to obey) + *me*

The *Shema* — "Hear, O Israel: the LORD our God, the LORD is one" (Deut 6:4) — is not an invitation. It is a declaration, a call, a command. In Semitic, hearing and obeying are the same word. To hear is to respond.

*Shemme* names the hearing that is already orientation — receptive-hearing as received-ordinance. Not: "I will listen more carefully." But: the mode of hearing in which the word you receive is already shaping you, already working in you before you decide what to do with it.

**In worship:** *Shemme* is what happens in the best moments of prayer — not when you are strategizing about what to say but when something reaches you and you are already changed by the receiving.

**IPA:** /ˈʃɛm.mɛ/ · **syllables:** 2 (shem-me)

---

### doxomme
*doxa* (Greek: glory, thanksgiving, praise) + *omme* (*me* with connective vowel)

*Doxa* in Greek covers glory (what God has) and praise (what humans give). In Christian liturgy: *Doxa en hypsistois Theo* — "Glory to God in the highest." The word for what radiates from the divine and what returns to it.

*Doxomme* names thanksgiving-as-received-ordinance. The gratitude that arrives unbidden. Most of us know the experience: you were not trying to feel grateful, and suddenly the whole structure of your life appeared as gift. That arrival is *doxomme*.

**In worship:** *Doxomme* is the acknowledgment that even gratitude is grace — that you cannot manufacture the right orientation toward life as gift, but it can be given to you.

**IPA:** /ˈdɒk.sɒm.mɛ/ · **syllables:** 3 (dox-om-me)

---

### nyamame
*nyama* (Mande, West Africa: vital-force, ambient power) + *me*

In the Mande traditions of West Africa, *nyama* is the ambient animating energy that runs through all living things. It is not uniform: it concentrates in wild animals, in the bush, in certain materials and practitioners. Blacksmiths work with *nyama* constantly and dangerously. Griots (oral historians and musicians) carry *nyama* in their speech. Hunters move through the *nyama*-dense bush.

*Nyama* is not earned or produced. It flows through the world as a given structure.

*Nyamame* names this vital-force-as-received-cosmic-ordinance. The ambient animating energy of the world understood as *me* — as structurally prior, given, received.

**In worship:** *Nyamame* points to the created world's aliveness as gift — the recognition that the energy moving through all things is not produced by those things but given to them.

**IPA:** /ˈɲa.ma.mɛ/ · **syllables:** 3 (nya-ma-me)

---

### maatme
*Ma'at* (Egyptian: truth, justice, cosmic order, right measure) + *me*

*Ma'at* was the Egyptian goddess-principle of cosmic order — simultaneously truth, justice, right measurement, and the path of the sun across the sky. She held the feather against which the heart was weighed. Pharaoh's justice was *ma'at*; right speech was *ma'at*; the Nile's flooding on schedule was *ma'at*.

*Ma'at* was not invented. It was participated in or violated. It existed prior to and independent of human choice.

*Maatme* names this cosmic-truth-justice-rightness as received-ordinance. The alignment with *ma'at* understood as something you receive when you live toward it — not achieved but given.

**In worship:** *Maatme* is the recognition that justice and truth are not human constructions but cosmic givens — that we discover them more than we produce them.

**IPA:** /ˈmɑːt.mɛ/ · **syllables:** 2 (maat-me)

---

### mitakuyame
*Mitákuye Oyásʼiŋ* (Lakota: "all my relations" / "all are related") + *me*

This is the most theologically concentrated phrase in the Siouan corpus. Spoken at the entrance and exit of the sweat lodge. After a vision quest. Before eating. At any moment of reconnection with what is always already true. It acknowledges kinship with every being: animals, plants, stones, waters, winds, stars, ancestors, the yet-to-be-born.

The claim is ontological: the self is not a substance that subsequently acquires relationships. The self is an *emergence* from the relational web. You cannot be the self you are without the entire web of relations that constitutes you.

*Mitakuyame* names this relational-ontology-as-received-ordinance. The understanding that all beings are already kin — not as aspiration but as cosmic fact — received as *me*, as the given structure of reality.

**In worship:** Said before prayer because the relational web is not the *context* of prayer but its *ground*. You pray from within the web, not at the web from outside.

**IPA:** /mɪ.ˈtɑː.ku.ja.mɛ/ · **syllables:** 5 (mi-ta-ku-ya-me)

## The anti-ordinances

Three *-me* words name what the DIVINE actively rejects — inversions of the *me* pattern:

**drujme** — *the cosmic lie* (Avestan *druj*): the structural orientation against reality's grain  
**molkme** — *coerced-votive-sacrifice* (Phoenician *mlk*): the demanded payment-as-theology  
**nextlame** — *debt-sacrifice* (Nahuatl *nextlahualli*): sacrifice as cosmic debt-payment  

These use the *-me* suffix to mark what is *anti-me* — what the ordering structure of reality opposes. Naming the inversion is itself an act of clarification. You cannot understand *qorbme* (sacrifice-as-drawing-near) without understanding *molkme* as its opposition.

---

## Exercise

Three pairs. For each pair, name which member is a *-me* word and what it receives:

1. "I will be grateful" vs. "gratitude arrives" → which is *doxomme*?  
2. "I will listen carefully" vs. "the hearing that is already shaping" → which is *shemme*?  
3. "I will build relationships" vs. "all beings are already kin" → which is *mitakuyame*?

**Answer:** All three right-hand options. The *-me* shift is always from production to reception. From *I will generate X* to *X arrives as given structure*.

---

## A note on pronunciation

The `-me` ending is always: /mɛ/ — the vowel as in "bed," not "me" as in "me myself." So *shemme* = SHEM-meh, not SHEM-me.

The *Sumerian me* itself was likely /me/ or /mɛ/. YOUSPEAK inherits the short vowel: the ending is always unstressed, brief, directional.
