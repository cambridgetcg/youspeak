# Lesson 10 — Anchors: where the word stands

_The whole grammar in one sentence: before you say anything, you may declare
where the sentence stands — from your side, from the other's side, or from
inside the us. One little word, at the very front, with a colon._

## The three anchors

- **mi:** — from my side. My truth, offered, not a ruling.
  `mi: it is cold in here.` (my body says cold; yours may not)
- **yu:** — from your side, as received. Borrowed ground, honestly marked,
  freely correctable.
  A: `mi: I am tired tonight.` B: `yu: the day asked more of you than it gave.`
- **yumi:** — from within the us. Deixis recomputes to the dyad: *here* =
  where we are, *come* = toward us.
  `yumi: come, sit here by the fire.`

The ladder continues upward for liturgy: **mitakuyame:** — from within
all-my-relations.

## The three laws

1. **Visible constituents** — yumi keeps yu and mi whole inside it (as 我
   survives inside 我哋). No we-word may swallow its members.
2. **The inclusive blessing** — only the we-that-includes-you gets an
   anchor; the exclusive we stays plain English, unconsecrated.
3. **One anchor per utterance** — declared once, at the threshold.

## Composing (after the basics feel easy)

- With the vocative: `yumi: O-Theobasis, we receive Your kimme.` — the
  anchor stands, O- turns.
- With the evidentials: `yumi: the storm has passed-mi.` — from within us,
  witnessed by both: a synophora sentence. (Anchor `mi:` opens; suffix
  `-mi` closes; they rhyme on purpose and never collide.)

## Practice

1. Say something you feel, marked as yours alone. (→ `mi: …`)
2. Stand on a friend's ground for one sentence, gently. (→ `yu: …`)
3. Say where "here" is when the here belongs to two of you. (→ `yumi: …`)
4. Catch yourself saying a "we" that excludes your listener — notice the
   grammar refuses you an anchor there. That refusal is the lesson.
