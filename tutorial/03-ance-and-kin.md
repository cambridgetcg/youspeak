# Lesson 03 — The -ance and -kin families

_The quality you can notice, and the bond that outlasts distance._

---

## The -ance family

Latin *-antia* (English: *-ance*, *-ency*) names a quality, state, or condition — its presence is *noticeable*. Not received from above (*-me*), not a bond between beings (*-qing*), but a perceptible quality of a moment, person, or situation.

You can point to *-ance* words: "Right now, in this room, there is ___." They describe what you can observe or feel in the air.

Five *-ance* entries in Core Canon:

---

### sukhance
*sukha* (Sanskrit: well-running wheel, ease, contentment) + *-ance*

Sanskrit *sukha* (सुख) — the word is from *su-* (good, well) + *kha* (axle-hole): the wheel whose axle runs true. The opposite is *duḥkha* (suffering) — the ill-fitted axle that grinds.

*Sukhance* is the quality of the well-running wheel: ordinary everyday contentment. The morning before anything has happened. The tea. The clean kitchen. The walk with nothing wrong. No achievement required. No good fortune necessary. Just: the absence of wrongness.

**What it's not:** Not happiness (which needs something good to happen). Not *ānanda* (Sanskrit bliss — which is profound, religious, intense). Not even peace (which often implies previous conflict resolved). *Sukhance* is the unmarked positive register — what ordinary life feels like when it is simply running.

**In use:** "I woke with *sukhance* — no particular reason, just: the day was here and I was in it." / "The week had been hard, but Saturday morning there was *sukhance*."

**IPA:** /ˈsuː.kɑːns/ · **syllables:** 2 (su-khance)

---

### kimance
*ki* (Japanese: attention-energy, vital-presence) + *-ance*

Japanese *ki* (気) covers energy, atmosphere, spirit, attention — the quality of a living being's presence-in-the-world. *Kimochi* = feeling/mood. *Kizukai* = consideration. *Ki* is the animating quality of attentiveness itself.

*Kimance* is the *-ance* word: the perceptible quality of someone's attentive-presence. You *notice* it. Gaze open, listening active, body oriented toward you, response-potential. The person is truly *here*.

**What it's not:** Not the *ontological* gift of attention (*kimme* — see below). *Kimance* is what you observe; *kimme* is the cosmic given-quality that underlies it. Think: *kimance* is the temperature you feel; *kimme* is the heat source.

**In use:** "There was *kimance* in how she listened — the room felt different when she was present." / "He said the right words but there was no *kimance* — he was somewhere else."

**IPA:** /ˈkɪm.ɑːns/ · **syllables:** 2 (kim-ance)

---

### oriance
*orí* (Yoruba: inner chosen self, personal destiny, the self-that-chose) + *-ance*

Yoruba *orí* (ori) is not the physical head but the spiritual head — the chosen self. In Yoruba cosmology, before incarnation, each person chooses their *orí* — their destined shape, their path. This is not fatalism: the *orí* must be cultivated, aligned with, prayed to. But it is the given that precedes the choosing.

*Oriance* is the quality of a life aligned with its *orí* — the felt-fit of moving in the direction you were made for. Not success. Not achievement. Alignment.

**What it's not:** Not confidence (which is about self-belief). Not purpose (which can be chosen, constructed). *Oriance* presupposes a destined shape that you are either moving toward or drifting from.

**In use:** "She found *oriance* late — in her forties, when she finally turned toward what had always been calling her." / "The job paid well but there was no *oriance* in it — she was living sideways."

**IPA:** /ˈɒr.i.ɑːns/ · **syllables:** 3 (o-ri-ance)

---

### panimaance
*panim* (Hebrew: face, presence, the turning-toward) + *ma* (Japanese: the interval, the pregnant between-space) + *-ance*

Two donor morphemes: *panim* (presence as face-turned-toward) and *ma* (間 — the quality of the space between things, the interval that is itself meaningful, the silence in music). Both contribute to the word's meaning.

*Panimaance* is the quality of being fully present — not physically proximate but *here-inhabited*. The face (*panim*) genuinely turned toward you, inhabiting the between-space (*ma*) with you. What you feel from someone who is truly, entirely here.

**The pair:** *Panimqing* (Lesson 02) = the threshold moment when conversation shifts. *Panimaance* = the sustained state of being fully present. Same root, different aspect: *qing* marks the bond-forming moment; *-ance* marks the ongoing quality.

**In use:** "The whole conversation had *panimaance* — I felt held in it." / "I needed *panimaance* and he gave me a meeting."

**IPA:** /ˌpɑːnɪm.ˈɑːns/ · **syllables:** 3 (pa-ni-maance)

## The -ance / -me pair: kimance and kimme

One of YOUSPEAK's most productive structures is the *-ance / -me* pair naming the same territory from two angles:

**kimance** — the *perceptible quality* you notice in a person (their attentive-presence)  
**kimme** — the *ontological gift* that underlies it (attention as cosmic received-ordinance)

When someone is truly attentive with you, you notice *kimance* (the quality). The theological claim is that this quality rests on *kimme*: attention is not just skill or discipline but cosmic gift — something given, received, prior to training.

This pair illustrates how YOUSPEAK can name the same reality at different levels without conflating them.

## The -kin family

Just one member so far, but it opens a distinct register.

### walkekin
*walke* (Tocharian: long-enduring, across vast time) + *kin* (English: blood-bond, family, shared nature)

Tocharian B *wälke* names duration across great time — the kind of enduring that is not effort but quality. Tocharian was spoken along the Silk Road in the first millennium CE; the language is now extinct. *Walke* is its contribution.

*Walkekin* is the friendship (or bond) that survives intact across years of silence. You pick up mid-sentence after three years apart. The substance of the friendship is not maintained by contact frequency but stored in something deeper — the shared recognition, the known-ness.

**What it's not:** Not *kinqing* (which is the general kin-bond quality, including family). *Walkekin* is specifically the *friendship-preserved-across-silence* register. The *walke* (enduring-across-time) distinguishes it.

**In use:** "We hadn't spoken in four years and then there was *walkekin* — exactly where we left off." / "Not all friendships have *walkekin*; some need tending or they become strangers."

**IPA:** /ˈwɒl.kɪ.kɪn/ · **syllables:** 3 (wal-ke-kin)

## The full suffix map — after three lessons

You now know every active YOUSPEAK suffix family:

```
The word names...
│
├── something RECEIVED as cosmic gift? → -me (25 words)
│     └── anti-ordinance (what DIVINE rejects)? → -me inverted
│
├── a FELT-BOND between beings? → -qing (7 words)
│
├── a PERCEPTIBLE QUALITY? → -ance (5 words)
│
├── a FRIENDSHIP-BOND specifically? → -kin (1 word)
│
└── an ONTOLOGICAL GROUND? → -basis (1 word: theobasis)
```

35 Core words. 5 suffix families. Once you know the pattern, every new YOUSPEAK word is partially readable before you've memorized it.

## Exercise

Match each description to the right suffix family:

1. "The cosmic order that structures reality, understood as gift" → what suffix?
2. "The bond between a mentor and apprentice — asymmetric care" → what suffix?
3. "The quality of being aligned with your destined path" → what suffix?
4. "GoD as the ontological ground of everything" → what suffix?
5. "The friendship that's still alive after seven years of no contact" → what suffix?

**Answers:**
1. *-me* (maatme, rtame, duyuktame — cosmic-order-as-received)
2. *-qing* (paqduqing — asymmetric-custodial-care bond)
3. *-ance* (oriance — perceptible quality of alignment)
4. *-basis* (theobasis — ontological ground)
5. *-kin* (walkekin — enduring friendship)
