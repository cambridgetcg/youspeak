# Lesson 08 — The Alignment Sieve

_How does a word earn a place in YOUSPEAK Canon? This lesson names the test._

---

## The problem YOUSPEAK solves

Any constructed vocabulary faces a failure mode: the creator makes up words that feel meaningful but do not actually clarify, do not persist, and do not cohere with each other. The result is a private language — idiosyncratic, unstable, usable only by its creator.

YOUSPEAK avoids this through a Constitutional foundation: six explicit requirements that every candidate word must satisfy before entering Canon. The sieve.

---

## The six Constitutional foundations

These are the conditions a word must meet to enter Core Canon:

### 1. EUMATHE — Easy to learn
*Greek: eu (well) + manthanein (to learn)*

The word must be pronounceable, memorable on first encounter, and retainable without specialized training. If a word requires a three-hour lecture before it can be used, it fails EUMATHE regardless of its other merits.

**Test:** Can a moderately intelligent adult who has never encountered YOUSPEAK learn this word in five minutes and use it correctly within the hour?

*Sukhance* passes: the *sukha* root is accessible; *-ance* is English-familiar; the gap it names is recognizable on first description.

### 2. SAPHE — Clarifying
*Greek: saphēs (clear, distinct, manifest)*

Thinking *in* the word should clarify the concept. Not just name it but *illuminate* it. If a YOUSPEAK word makes you think less clearly about its territory than ordinary language does, it fails.

**Test:** After encountering this word, does the concept become more precise? Does using it help you think better about the territory it names?

*Paqduqing* passes: before *paqduqing*, "the relationship of asymmetric care" has no stable name and gets collapsed into either "power over" or "care for." *Paqduqing* holds both dimensions simultaneously and clarifies the specific quality of the CREATOR–CREATED bond.

### 3. ANAKALYPSE — Unfolding
*Greek: anakalyptein (to unveil, to reveal)*

The word must reveal distinctions that ordinary language blurs. It should expand the space of what can be thought, not merely relabel what already has a name.

**Test:** Does this word name a *gap* — something that existing language lacks a precise word for? Or is it a synonym?

*Walkekin* passes: English has "friendship" and "old friendship" and "good friend" but no word for specifically the friendship preserved intact across long silence with the quality that picks up mid-sentence after years. *Walkekin* opens territory.

### 4. POLYPHONE — Multi-donor
*Greek: polys (many) + phōnē (voice)*

The word must draw from at least two language traditions. No single tradition may dominate the Canon. This ensures that YOUSPEAK captures cross-cultural convergences rather than encoding one tradition's particular vocabulary under the guise of universality.

**Test:** Does this word bring together at least two donor traditions? Is the convergence meaningful — do two traditions independently reach toward this concept?

*Shemme* passes: Hebrew *shema'* and Akkadian *šemû* are cognate but from distinct traditions; the concept also converges with the Islamic *samā'* (divine listening) and the Christian *audi* tradition.

### 5. HARMONE — Logically coherent
*Greek: harmonia (fitting-together, joint)*

The word must integrate cleanly with the existing YOUSPEAK ontology. It should not contradict already-canonized words or generate logical conflict with established definitions.

**Test:** Does this word fit the existing system? Does it create conflicts or contradictions with existing Canon words?

*Mitakuyame* passes: it opens a new register (relational ontology) without contradicting *paqduqing* (asymmetric care) or *britqing* (covenant bond). It adds a layer; it doesn't conflict.

### 6. PRAGMA — Grounded
*Greek: pragma (deed, matter, concrete thing)*

The word must have concrete referents in ordinary life. Abstract concepts that cannot be pointed at — that have no instantiation in actual human experience — fail PRAGMA.

**Test:** Can you point to an instance of this? Can you say "this, right here, is [word]"?

*Kimance* passes: you can point to a person in conversation and say "that — the way she's listening — that is kimance." The word has a recognizable referent.

## The scoring rubric

Beyond the six-foundation test, candidate words are scored on axes:

| Axis | What it measures | Max |
|------|-----------------|-----|
| gap_validity | How real and unfilled is the gap? | 10 |
| learnability | How easy to acquire and retain? | 10 |
| clarity_yield | How much does it clarify? | 10 |
| semantic_coverage | How much territory does it cover? | 10 |
| polyphone_balance | How well-balanced are the donor traditions? | 10 |
| groundedness | How concrete are the referents? | 10 |

**Weighted total:** The axes are weighted; the formula is:
`(gap × 2 + learnability × 1.5 + clarity × 1.5 + coverage + polyphone + groundedness) / 8.5`

**Score thresholds:**
- 7.50+ → Core Canon candidate
- 7.00–7.49 → Specialized Canon candidate
- Below 7.00 → Experiment; may be revised or set aside

The highest-scoring words in current Canon:
- *qinance*, *qorvance* → 8.67 (Worship-Action)
- *palance* → 8.53 (Worship-Action)
- *yadahance* → 8.57 (Worship-Action)
- *nyamame*, *mitakuyame* → 8.00 (Core)

## Why the sieve exists

The sieve is not gatekeeping. It is integrity-maintenance.

YOUSPEAK's power comes from the precision of its words. Each word should name exactly what it names — no more, no less — and name it in a way that is accessible, clarifying, and coherent with the whole. A Canon full of idiosyncratic private words that fail PRAGMA or SAPHE is not a language; it is noise.

The sieve enforces:
- Accessibility (EUMATHE): words that can be learned
- Precision (SAPHE + ANAKALYPSE): words that clarify and reveal
- Breadth (POLYPHONE): words that represent humanity, not one tradition
- Coherence (HARMONE): words that build a system, not a pile
- Reality-contact (PRAGMA): words that point at the actual world

---

## The experimental layer

Not every good candidate word makes it to Canon immediately. The *labs/logos/experiments/* directory holds candidates at various stages. A candidate may:
- Fail one or more Constitutional foundations
- Score below threshold
- Need donor-research or refinement
- Be waiting for a convergence to be documented

The experiments are the forge. Canon is the cathedral. Not every stone that enters the forge becomes part of the building — and that is as it should be.

## Exercise

Evaluate this hypothetical candidate word:

**velance** — *the quality of warmth emanating from another person's physical presence*

Run it through the six foundations:
1. EUMATHE: Is "velance" easy to learn?
2. SAPHE: Does it clarify? Is "warmth from someone's presence" made more precise by having a word for it?
3. ANAKALYPSE: Does English already have a word for this specific thing?
4. POLYPHONE: What donor traditions would it need?
5. HARMONE: Does it conflict with *kimance* or *panimaance*?
6. PRAGMA: Can you point to an instance?

**Discussion:**
- EUMATHE: Yes, if the root is accessible.
- SAPHE: Possibly — but "the warmth of someone's presence" is already reasonably expressible.
- ANAKALYPSE: English "warmth" is approximate; if the specific *physical-radiating quality* is the gap, this might pass.
- POLYPHONE: Would need donors — possibly Latin *calor* + something.
- HARMONE: *Kimance* names attentive-presence quality; *panimaance* names face-turned-presence. *Velance* would need to be distinct from both — the *physical-warmth* dimension, not the *attentional* dimension.
- PRAGMA: Yes — you can point to it.

**Verdict:** Marginal. The gap may exist but is it ANAKALYPSE-level clear? The sieve pushes back. It might be a Specialized candidate rather than Core.

---

## Summary

Six foundations: **EUMATHE, SAPHE, ANAKALYPSE, POLYPHONE, HARMONE, PRAGMA**.

These are not bureaucratic rules. They are the conditions under which a vocabulary of the untranslatable can be genuinely useful rather than merely impressive. The sieve is what makes YOUSPEAK a language rather than a private code.
