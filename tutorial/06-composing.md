# Lesson 06 — Composing in YOUSPEAK

_Now you use it._

---

## The shift from knowing to using

You've learned the suffix families. You can read a YOUSPEAK word on first encounter. Now the question is: how do you compose with it? How do you put YOUSPEAK words into sentences, prayers, acknowledgments, doxologies?

This lesson is practical. We will compose three things together:
1. A one-sentence acknowledgment
2. A short prayer (3-4 lines)
3. A doxology (a praise-statement)

---

## Rule one: YOUSPEAK words enter natural-language sentences

YOUSPEAK does not require a separate grammar. The words slot into English (or your language) sentences at the place where English lacks precision.

Wrong approach:
> *Panimaance-of-you I-feel when meeting-you.*

Right approach:
> *I feel your panimaance even across the phone.*

YOUSPEAK words are vocabulary additions, not replacements. Use them where the English word fails you; use English everywhere else.

---

## The Acknowledgment

An acknowledgment is the simplest YOUSPEAK composition: naming a quality you observe or experience, held as gift.

**Template:** *There is [word] here. / There is [word] in [context]. / I receive [word].*

**Example acknowledgments:**

> *There is walkekin here — we hadn't spoken in two years and it was waiting exactly where we left it.*

> *She has kimance — the whole room shifts when she is present.*

> *I woke with sukhance. Nothing particular. The day was here.*

> *There was panimqing somewhere in the third hour — we stopped talking about the project and started talking.*

**Notice:** These are simple declarative sentences. The YOUSPEAK word carries the precision; the surrounding English provides context and flow. The acknowledgment doesn't explain the word; it *uses* it, trusting the word to name what it names.

## The Prayer

A YOUSPEAK prayer uses the vocabulary in address to the DIVINE. Several moves are available:

**Naming (what GoD is):** Use *-me* words and *theobasis*
> *You are theobasis — the ground on which everything rests.*
> *You are nyamame — the vital-force that runs through all things as given.*

**Asking (in the vocabulary of receipt):** The *-me* pattern changes asking
> *Let ahavame arrive — I cannot produce the love toward You; I can only open.*
> *Shemme — let the hearing happen that is already orientation.*

**Acknowledging (what you have received):** The *-qing* words
> *There is britqing between us — covenant I did not initiate; bond that holds me.*
> *Paqduqing — You hold me in custodial care; I am held in something asymmetric and real.*

**Example prayer — three moves:**

> *You are theobasis — the ground in which I live and move.*  
> *Ahavame: let the love toward You arrive. I cannot summon it.*  
> *Shemme: let me hear the hearing that is already shaped by what it receives.*  
> *There is britqing — covenant I did not make. I acknowledge it.*

**Notice the structure:** Naming → Asking-as-opening → Acknowledging. No begging. No transaction. The prayer is oriented in the vocabulary of receipt: what arrives, what is given, what is already.

## The Doxology

A doxology is a praise-statement — a declaration of divine glory. *Doxomme* (thanksgiving-as-received) is the YOUSPEAK word closest to the doxological register.

Classical doxologies: *Gloria in excelsis Deo* (Greek liturgy), *Praise God from whom all blessings flow* (English Protestant), *Hallelujah* (Semitic).

**YOUSPEAK doxology structure:** Name + What GoD is/does + Human response

**Example:**

> *Theobasis — GoD as ground — in whom all things hold.*  
> *Nyamame flows as gift: the world is alive and You are the given.*  
> *Doxomme: even this thanksgiving arrives as gift.*  
> *Hallance.*

(The final word *hallance* — jubilant praise — closes the doxology as the Worship-Action word that names the act the doxology itself performs.)

---

## Worked example: the full three-stage composition

**Occasion:** A moment of unexpected contentment on an ordinary morning.

**Acknowledgment:**
> *Sukhance. Morning. Nothing-wrong. The tea is here.*

**Prayer:**
> *Theobasis — I rest in what I didn't make.*  
> *This sukhance is gift; doxomme for it.*  
> *Panimaance: let me be here, not elsewhere.*

**Doxology:**
> *You are theobasis, the ground of what is.*  
> *Nyamame runs through this morning; You are given in it.*  
> *Doxomme. Hallance.*

**Notice:** The same moment — sukhance, a morning — is described three times at three different levels. The acknowledgment names the experience. The prayer orients toward the DIVINE. The doxology declares praise. YOUSPEAK words appear in all three, but each register uses them differently.

---

## Two things to avoid

**1. Over-density:** Resist the temptation to pack YOUSPEAK words into every sentence. One precise word per thought is usually enough. Two is occasionally justified. Three or more in a single sentence becomes opaque and performs sophistication rather than achieving precision.

> Too dense: *The walkekin-panimaance of paqduqing arrived as ahavame-doxomme.*  
> Better: *There was walkekin in how she cared for him — asymmetric, reliable, not needing to be noticed.*

**2. Vocabulary-substitution:** Replacing English words with YOUSPEAK words without earning the precision.

> Substitution: *I had a good sukhance-filled day.*  
> Earned: *There was sukhance all morning — not because anything was good but because nothing was wrong.*

YOUSPEAK earns its use when the word names something English cannot. The test: if English has a word for this, use it.

## Exercise

Compose one of the following:
1. An acknowledgment of a friendship (use *walkekin* or *kinqing*)
2. A one-sentence prayer using *shemme* or *ahavame*
3. A doxology that names GoD as both *theobasis* and *nyamame*

There are no wrong answers. The goal is to *use* the word in a sentence where it earns its presence.

---

## Summary

YOUSPEAK words enter natural-language sentences where English lacks precision. Three composing registers:

| Register | Move | Example words |
|----------|------|--------------|
| **Acknowledgment** | Name what is present | sukhance, walkekin, panimaance |
| **Prayer** | Address DIVINE in vocabulary of receipt | ahavame, shemme, britqing |
| **Doxology** | Declare praise using what-GoD-is words | theobasis, nyamame, doxomme, hallance |

The composing lesson is ongoing — every session of genuine worship is a YOUSPEAK composition.
