# Lesson 02 — The -qing family

_Seven words for what lives between beings._

---

## Where -qing comes from

Mandarin Chinese *qíng* (情) covers a range English splits across several words: emotion, feeling, affection, sentiment, the quality of a relationship, the bond between persons. It is always relational — you cannot have *qíng* alone.

The character: 情 = 忄(heart-radical) + 青 (green/clear/young). The heart that is clear and alive. The felt quality of connection when it is real.

YOUSPEAK uses *-qing* to mark words naming the **felt-bond** quality of a relationship — not the relationship itself but the specific qualitative texture that makes it what it is.

---

## The contrast with -me

Both *-me* and *-qing* mark something received rather than constructed. But they receive differently:

| | *-me* | *-qing* |
|-|-------|---------|
| **Direction** | descends from above; arrives as gift | lives between beings; binds across |
| **Quality** | ontological (structurally prior) | relational (exists between two) |
| **Example** | *doxomme* — thanksgiving arrives | *panimqing* — the bond forms when faces turn |
| **Question** | "What has been given to you?" | "What lives between you?" |

A *-me* word names what you receive; a *-qing* word names the bond you are in.

---

## Four -qing words — slowly

### panimqing
*panim* (Hebrew: face, presence, the turning-toward) + *qing*

Hebrew *panim* (פָּנִים) is grammatically plural but conceptually unified — the face-that-is-presence, the being-turned-toward. "The LORD make His face shine upon you" (Num 6:25). *Panim* is not just the physical face but the orientation of the whole person toward the other.

*Panimqing* names the moment a conversation crosses from transactional to relational — from function-exchange to faces turned toward each other. You know this moment: you were talking about logistics and then something shifted and suddenly you were actually *with* each other.

**In use:** "There was *panimqing* when she stopped looking at her phone." / "We never found *panimqing* in that meeting — it stayed transactional."

**Distinction from panimaance:** *Panimqing* is the threshold moment (the *qing*-shift, the bond-forming); *panimaance* is the sustained state of being-fully-present. Same territory, different aspect.

**IPA:** /ˈpɑːnɪm.tɕɪŋ/ · **syllables:** 3 (pa-nim-qing)

---

### britqing
*berīt* (Hebrew: covenant) + *qing*

Hebrew *berīt* (בְּרִית) is the covenant — the formal agreement between God and people, sealed in blood, marking a relationship with obligations and promises. Abraham. Sinai. David. But the Prophets, especially Hosea, describe the covenant with marriage language — not legal instrument but living bond.

*Britqing* names the covenant as felt-bond: not the legal architecture of the covenant but the relational quality that the covenant expresses and creates. The specific texture of the CREATOR–CREATED bond that includes obligation but exceeds it — the bond you feel when you know you are held and you hold.

**In use:** "The liturgical year moves through *britqing* — each festival a renewal of what was never really severed." / "He fulfilled his obligations without any *britqing* — technically correct, relationally dead."

**IPA:** /ˈbrɪt.tɕɪŋ/ · **syllables:** 2 (brit-qing)

---

### paqduqing
*paqādu* (Akkadian: to entrust, to care-for, to hold in custodial responsibility) + *qing*

Akkadian *paqādu* appears in the legal and administrative literature of ancient Mesopotamia — the word used when a king entrusts a province to a governor, when a shepherd is given a flock, when a temple places its property in someone's keeping. It is asymmetric: the greater entrusts to the lesser, who now holds what they have been given with full responsibility.

*Paqduqing* names the felt-bond of this asymmetric-custodial relationship. The CREATOR–CREATED bond in which the Creator holds the creature in care (not domination), and the creature is entrusted with what has been given. The asymmetry is acknowledged and not dissolved; the care is real.

**In use:** "The relationship between parents and young children has deep *paqduqing* — asymmetric care on both sides, each holding what they've been given." / "The pastor who sees their congregation as property rather than *paqduqing* has lost the thread."

**Distinction from britqing:** *Britqing* is the covenant-bond (formal, mutual promise); *paqduqing* is the custodial-care bond (asymmetric, entrusted responsibility). Both describe CREATOR–CREATED relationship, from different angles.

**IPA:** /ˈpɑːk.du.tɕɪŋ/ · **syllables:** 3 (paq-du-qing)

---

### mahabbahqing
*maḥabbah* (Arabic: love, especially Sufi mystical-love) + *qing*

Arabic *maḥabbah* (محبّة) is the Sufi term for the love that moves toward the divine Beloved. The word appears in the *ḥubb* root — love as the seed buried in the heart. Rābi'a al-'Adawiyya (8th century): "I love You with two loves — love of desire and a love that You are worthy of." Ibn 'Arabī (12th century) systematizes it. Rumi: "I was raw; I was cooked; I was burned."

The endpoint of *maḥabbah* at its fullest is *fanāʾ* (annihilation) — the dissolution of the self into the Beloved. The lover ceases; only the love remains.

*Mahabbahqing* names the felt-bond of this mystical-love: the specific quality of the relationship in which love moves toward the DIVINE with the willingness to be dissolved. Not sentimental affection but the bond that includes *fanāʾ*-implication.

**In use:** "The Psalms at their most intense carry *mahabbahqing* — "as the deer pants for streams of water, so my soul pants for You." / "There was no *mahabbahqing* in his theology — God as object of analysis rather than Beloved."

**IPA:** /mɑ.ˈhɑb.bɑ.tɕɪŋ/ · **syllables:** 4 (ma-hab-bah-qing)

## The full -qing family

| Word | Gap | Bond registered |
|------|-----|----------------|
| **britqing** | covenant-felt-bond | CREATOR ↔ CREATED (covenant) |
| **danaqing** | gift-giving-as-felt-bond | Giver ↔ Receiver (voluntary gift) |
| **ifeqing** | Yoruba warmth-love-felt-bond | Person ↔ World (expansive love) |
| **kinqing** | silence-proof bond | Person ↔ Person (kin-register) |
| **mahabbahqing** | mystical-love-toward-Beloved | Seeker ↔ Divine (Sufi) |
| **panimqing** | conversation's face-turning | Speaker ↔ Speaker (threshold) |
| **paqduqing** | asymmetric-custodial-care | Greater ↔ Lesser (entrusted care) |

---

## A note on pronouncing -qing

The *-qing* ending is /tɕɪŋ/ — like English "ching" but with the affricate of Mandarin *q*. In casual YOUSPEAK, /kɪŋ/ (rhyming with "king") is acceptable. The full aspirated-affricate /tɕ/ signals precision.

## Exercise

Which *-qing* word fits each moment?

1. You are giving a substantial gift to someone who needs it — purely freely, no expectation of return.
2. The Sunday liturgy begins with a reminder that this community exists in a covenant with God — not because they chose it but because they were chosen and responded.
3. You are talking to a friend and something shifts — suddenly you're both in it, no more small talk.
4. A father holds his newborn: responsible for, caring for, entrusted with this creature who did not ask to be born.

**Answers:**
1. *danaqing* — gift-giving-as-felt-bond
2. *britqing* — covenant-felt-bond
3. *panimqing* — conversation's face-turning
4. *paqduqing* — asymmetric-custodial-care

## Summary

The *-qing* family names what lives between beings. Where *-me* descends, *-qing* binds across. Where *-me* arrives as cosmic gift, *-qing* forms in the space between two.

Together, the *-me* and *-qing* families account for **32 of the 35 Core words**. Learn these two patterns and you can read most of YOUSPEAK.
