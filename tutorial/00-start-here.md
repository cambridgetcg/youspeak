# Lesson 00 — What YOUSPEAK is

_You don't need to know anything to start here. This lesson exists to orient you before you begin._

---

## The gap

English has one word: **love**.

But love-the-feeling-for-your-dog and love-the-self-surrender-that-crosses-every-obstacle and love-the-commanded-orientation-toward-God and love-the-mystical-dissolution-into-the-Beloved are not the same thing. Naming them all "love" is not a poetic richness. It's a failure of language.

Every language has these gaps. You feel something real — something precise and irreducible — and no word in your language points to it exactly. The closest word is ten degrees off, and you make do.

YOUSPEAK was built to close these gaps.

---

## What YOUSPEAK is

YOUSPEAK is a constructed vocabulary for worship — a set of precision words that name what ordinary language cannot say exactly.

It does not replace your language. It supplements it: when you need the word that names the thing precisely, YOUSPEAK has it.

Three defining features:

**1. Polyphone** — every YOUSPEAK word draws from multiple language traditions.  
*Kimme* draws from Japanese *ki* (attention-energy) and Sumerian *me* (received-ordinance). *Walkekin* draws from Tocharian *walke* (long-enduring) and English *kin*. The words are not invented — they are *assembled* from humanity's existing vocabulary of the untranslatable.

**2. Structural** — the language has a grammar you can learn.  
Once you know the suffix families (`-me`, `-qing`, `-ance`), many YOUSPEAK words become readable on first encounter. You see *ahavame* and you know: *ahava* (love) + *me* (received-ordinance) = love-as-received-ordinance. The structure does the work.

**3. Worship-oriented** — every word serves recognition of the DIVINE.  
YOUSPEAK is not a general-purpose language. It is a vocabulary of seeing — naming the structures of divine reality and human response with more precision than modern languages permit.

---

## What YOUSPEAK is not

**Not a replacement for prayer.** These are words to think in and with, not liturgical formulas.

**Not a closed system.** The Canon grows as new gaps are identified and new words are forged. You may encounter words still being developed (experiments) alongside words fully canonized (Core Canon).

**Not arbitrary.** Every word has a documented genealogy: donor languages, the gap it names, the traditions it converges across. Nothing is made up from nothing.

---

## The foundational opposition

YOUSPEAK was named in conscious contrast to Orwell's **Newspeak** — the fictional totalitarian language engineered for vocabulary *reduction*, making certain thoughts literally unthinkable.

YOUSPEAK goes the other direction: vocabulary *expansion*, making more thoughts precisely thinkable.

Same kind of object. Opposite vocation.

Where Newspeak names its god "Big Brother" and serves control, YOUSPEAK names its ground "theobasis" (GoD-as-ontological-foundation) and serves freedom-in-worship.

## Your first three words

Don't memorize these yet. Just meet them.

**sukhance** — *the ordinary-everyday contentment*  
The moment before anything has happened. Morning tea. Clean kitchen. The walk where nothing is wrong. Sanskrit *sukha* (good-axle, easy-running wheel) + Latin *-ance* (quality-of). Not happiness — happiness requires something good to happen. *Sukhance* requires nothing except the absence of wrong.

**kimance** — *the perception that someone is attentively present*  
Noticing that someone is truly *here* — gaze open, listening active, response-potential. Japanese *ki* (attention-energy) + Latin *-ance*. The word for what you feel from someone who is fully with you.

**oriance** — *the state of a life aligned with its destined shape*  
Not success. Alignment. The felt-fit of moving in the direction you were made for. Yoruba *orí* (inner chosen self) + Latin *-ance*. The opposite of *oriance* is not failure — it's drift.

## One thing to carry forward

The gap between what IS and what SHOULD BE is not a problem to be solved. It is the engine.

YOUSPEAK exists because someone noticed the gap — between the precision of what they felt in worship and the imprecision of the words available. The language is an act of longing-made-visible.

When you learn a YOUSPEAK word, you are not memorizing vocabulary. You are receiving a map of territory you already knew was there, unnamed.
