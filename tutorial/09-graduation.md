## The graduation exercise

Three tasks. Choose at least two.

---

### Task 1: Compose a prayer

Write a 4-8 line prayer using at least three YOUSPEAK words. 

Requirements:
- At least one *-me* word (received-ordinance register)
- At least one *-qing* or *-ance* word
- The prayer should be directionally addressed to *theobasis* or to the DIVINE you worship

There is no correct answer. There is only: does this prayer say what you actually want to say? Does each YOUSPEAK word earn its place — naming something English cannot say as precisely?

**Example structure:**
1. Name what GoD is (*theobasis*, *nyamame*, etc.)
2. Name what you are receiving or have received (*-me* word)
3. Name a bond or quality (*-qing* or *-ance*)
4. Name a worship-act (*-ance* Worship-Action word)

---

### Task 2: Propose a candidate word

Identify a gap in your experience — something real and untranslatable that YOUSPEAK does not yet name. Then:

1. Name the gap precisely (what does existing language fail to capture?)
2. Find 2+ donor traditions that reach toward this concept
3. Propose a YOUSPEAK word that combines them
4. Run it through the six foundations: EUMATHE, SAPHE, ANAKALYPSE, POLYPHONE, HARMONE, PRAGMA
5. Estimate a score

You are not submitting this for Canon — this is the exercise of *seeing* the gaps. Once you can identify a genuine gap and reach toward naming it, you understand YOUSPEAK from the inside.

---

### Task 3: Write a short description of a YOUSPEAK word to someone who has never heard of YOUSPEAK

Take any Core Canon word. Explain it to someone who has never encountered YOUSPEAK — as if you were introducing a friend to the language through a single concept.

Requirements:
- One paragraph on what the word names (the gap)
- One paragraph on where it comes from (donors, briefly)
- One example sentence
- No jargon; assume no prior YOUSPEAK knowledge

This task tests whether you actually understand the word well enough to explain it. If you find you cannot explain *walkekin* without using other YOUSPEAK terminology, you may need to sit with *walkekin* longer.

## What YOUSPEAK is for

You have learned a vocabulary of worship. Not a set of theological propositions to assent to, but a set of *names* — names for territory that was real before the language was, but unnamed.

The Lakota knew *Mitákuye Oyásʼiŋ* before YOUSPEAK forged *mitakuyame*. The Yoruba knew *àṣẹ* before YOUSPEAK forged *àṣẹme*. The Koreans knew *han* before *hanme* existed.

What YOUSPEAK offers is *convergence* — the recognition that across radically different traditions, in languages that never spoke to each other, humans named adjacent territory. When you use *mitakuyame*, you are not just using a YOUSPEAK word. You are holding the Lakota prayer and the Sufi *fanāʾ* and the Confucian *rén* and the Buddhist *pratītyasamutpāda* simultaneously — all pointing at the same territory from different angles.

That convergence is the language's deepest gift.

## A closing word

You began with the gap — the ache between what IS and what SHOULD BE, between the precision of experience and the imprecision of language.

YOUSPEAK is one response to that gap. Not the final response. Every word in the Canon is partial — pointing toward something larger than the word itself.

*Sukhance* is not contentment. It names toward it.  
*Mitakuyame* is not the relational web. It names toward it.  
*Theobasis* is not GoD. It names toward GoD.

All naming is partial. And that's exactly right. The partial naming that sends you back to the real thing — that's the language working.
