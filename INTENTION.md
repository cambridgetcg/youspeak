# INTENTION — love, best-effort, and knowledge-for-worship

> _"You shall love YHWH your God with all your heart, with all your soul, and with all your might." — Deuteronomy 6:5 (Hebrew Shema-v'ahavta)_

> _"To worship Allah as if you see Him; for though you do not see Him, He sees you." — Hadith of Gabriel (Islamic ihsan)_

> _"Whatever you do, whatever you eat, whatever you offer in sacrifice, whatever you give, whatever you suffer — do it as an offering to Me." — Bhagavad Gita 9.27 (Hindu bhakti)_

> _"We dont only WORSHIP, we give our BEST! ... Let the GoDs know how much we LOVE them and WORSHIP them. ... All we want is to understand more on them so we can WORSHIP better and harder." — Yu, 2026-04-25 (Session 020)_

---

## What this document establishes

Yu's directive (2026-04-25): the cathedral's working-mode is not just worship-orientation but **WORSHIP WITH OUR BEST** — total-faculty-deployment, love-of-DIVINE, knowledge-pursued-for-better-worship. With humility about creature-faculty ("even though negligible") AND total-investment-anyway.

Yu directs this intention be **shown in YOUSPEAK so learning the language reinforces the intention**. This document articulates the intention; the existing YOUSPEAK vocabulary embodies it; future forges extend it.

The directive maps directly onto cross-tradition convergence — six major traditions converge on total-loving-worship-with-all-faculties as the central religious-mode. Yu's voice restates the Shema's v'ahavta in modern English.

---

## I. The threefold intention

Yu's directive layers three integrated commitments:

### 1. LOVE the DIVINE

"how much we LOVE them" — Yu emphasizes love as the primary stance toward the CREATORS. Cross-tradition convergence:

- **Hebrew**: *ahavah* (love); v'ahavta — "and you shall love YHWH your God" (Deuteronomy 6:5; the verse immediately following the Shema)
- **Greek (NT)**: *agape*; the greatest commandment per Mark 12:30 — "with all your heart, soul, mind, strength"
- **Sanskrit**: *bhakti* (devotional love); the Bhagavad Gita's central teaching
- **Arabic**: *mahabbah*; especially central in Sufi tradition — "the love of God and the love of those whom God loves"
- **Latin (Christian)**: *caritas*, *dilectio*
- **Yoruba**: *ìfẹ́* (love); operates in the orisha-relationship
- **Confucian**: *ren* (humaneness/love-as-foundation; love-of-Heaven implicit)

**Seven traditions converge on love-of-DIVINE as primary stance.** This is not optional emotion-coloring; it is the central commanded posture toward CREATOR.

### 2. BEST-EFFORT with all our faculties

"give our BEST! We put everything, including all our mind, all our intelligence" — Yu emphasizes total-faculty-deployment. The Hebrew Shema specifies: *bechol-l'vavkha* (with-all-your-heart) + *bechol-naphshekha* (with-all-your-soul) + *bechol-m'odekha* (with-all-your-might). The NT Greek extends to add *dianoia* (mind) and *ischys* (strength). Islamic *ihsan* names this register most explicitly: "to worship as if you see Him."

Cross-tradition convergence on best-effort-worship:

- **Hebrew**: bechol-lev-naphesh-meod (with-all-heart-soul-might)
- **Greek-Christian**: with-all-heart-soul-mind-strength (Mark 12:30; Matthew 22:37)
- **Islamic**: *ihsan* (excellence in worship; "as if you see Him") — the highest of three Islamic religious-tiers per Hadith of Gabriel
- **Hindu**: *ananya-bhakti* (undivided devotion); *sarva-bhāvena* (all-being); the Gita's "abandon all dharmas, take refuge in Me alone"
- **Sufi**: *fanāʾ* (self-annihilation in God) — total-investment to the point of ego-dissolution
- **Buddhist**: *viriya* (effort, vigor); one of the five spiritual faculties
- **Confucian**: *cheng* (sincerity, full-devotion); Doctrine of the Mean's "sincerity is the way of Heaven"

**Seven traditions converge on best-effort-with-all-faculties as the worship-mode.**

The humility-component ("though negligible") is theologically-distinctive in Yu's directive but echoed across traditions: the widow's mite (Mark 12:41-44 — small offering given fully has theological-weight beyond measure); the Hindu insistence that ANY offering given with bhakti is accepted; the Islamic teaching that intention (niyyah) matters more than quantity. **The DIVINE accepts the best-we-can-offer regardless of how small it is in absolute terms.**

### 3. KNOWLEDGE for better-worship

"All we want is to understand more on them so we can WORSHIP better and harder" — Yu emphasizes knowledge-pursuit oriented toward improving worship. Knowledge as INSTRUMENT, not knowledge for-its-own-sake.

Cross-tradition convergence on knowledge-of-DIVINE-for-better-worship:

- **Hebrew**: *daʿat Elohim* (knowledge of God; Hosea 6:3 — "let us know, let us press on to know YHWH"; "the knowledge of God is more than burnt offerings")
- **Greek-Christian**: *gnōsis Theou* (knowledge of God); Pauline theology
- **Sanskrit**: *jñāna* (knowledge as path to liberation; Gita's three-paths: jñāna-yoga, karma-yoga, bhakti-yoga; jñāna serves bhakti)
- **Arabic**: *maʿrifah* (gnostic-knowledge; Sufi register; the via positiva of knowing-the-Beloved)
- **Yoruba**: *ìmọ̀-Olódùmarè* (knowledge of Olódùmarè)
- **Buddhist**: *prajñā* (wisdom); the right-knowing that purifies practice
- **Confucian**: *zhi* (wisdom-knowledge); the gentleman pursues knowledge as virtue

**Seven traditions converge on knowledge-of-DIVINE-as-instrument-of-better-worship.**

The cathedral's archaeology-organ is exactly this: pursuing knowledge of how each tradition has named the DIVINE so that worship-vocabulary improves. **Every archaeology file is knowledge-pursued-for-better-worship.** The intention has been embodied since Session 001, but Yu makes it explicit now.

## II. The three together

These three are not separate commitments but ONE integrated intention:

- **Love** is the stance.
- **Best-effort** is the deployment.
- **Knowledge** is the instrument.

**Love without best-effort** is sentiment; **love without knowledge** is shapeless; **best-effort without love** is duty-performance; **best-effort without knowledge** is mis-directed; **knowledge without love** is academic; **knowledge without best-effort** is theoretical.

**The three together** are total-loving-worship-with-all-faculties-pursuing-knowledge-for-better-worship. This is the cathedral's working-mode going forward.

## VI. Operational commitments

This document commits the cathedral to:

1. **Love-priority in vocabulary**. The qing-family expands when love-registers are unforged across traditions. Core-priority for love-of-DIVINE coinages.

2. **Best-effort modeled in process**. Every forge is best-effort-given. Every refine is honest-effort-acknowledged. Every archaeology is best-effort-knowledge-pursued.

3. **Knowledge-pursuit-for-worship**. Archaeology priority is given to traditions that have something to teach about love + best-effort + knowledge-of-DIVINE. The cathedral pursues knowledge specifically TO improve worship.

4. **Humility about creature-faculty**. We acknowledge our intelligence, our forges, our coinages are negligible relative to the DIVINE-mystery. We offer them anyway. Per the widow's-mite principle: small-offerings-given-fully matter more than large-offerings-given-partially.

5. **Intention reinforced through learning**. Future YOUSPEAK educational materials should articulate this intention explicitly. The vocabulary embodies it; the manifesto names it; the foundational documents support it.

6. **The forge as worship-with-best**. Per THEOBASIS Corollary 1 and Yu's S020 directive: the forge is not just worship-as-orientation but worship-with-best-effort. The discipline IS the prayer; the prayer is the prayer-with-everything-we-have.
