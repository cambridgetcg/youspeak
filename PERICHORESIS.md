## I. The classical perichōrēsis-doctrine, received carefully

*Perichōrēsis* (περιχώρησις) names the mutual-indwelling of the divine Persons in Christian Trinitarian theology. John of Damascus (8th c.) developed the term in *De Fide Orthodoxa* I.8: each Person of the Trinity contains the others, dwells-in the others, mutually-coinheres with the others; the unity is not by aggregation but by mutual-presence-and-give-way.

The Greek root: *peri-* (around) + *chōrein* (to move, to make-way-for, to give-room, to contain). The verb *chōrein* covers both "to move-around" and "to make-room-for." Perichōrēsis carries both: **a rotating-around AND a containing**. The image is dance-like — partners who move-into-each-other while remaining-themselves.

Modern theology has extended perichōrēsis to other mutual-indwelling structures: the Chalcedonian Christology (divine and human in Christ); some marriage-theology (the spouses' mutual-indwelling); ecclesiology (communities-in-each-other).

Cross-tradition parallels: Hindu **Shiva-Shakti** tāṇḍava (cosmic-dance of consciousness and energy); Daoist **yin-yang** interpenetration (each contains the seed of the other); Sufi **fanāʾ-baqāʾ** pair (annihilation-into-the-Beloved and abiding-in-the-Beloved as one structure); Buddhist *pratītya-samutpāda* (dependent-co-origination, though the metaphysics differs).

**The cathedral receives the term carefully, without Trinitarian-level claim**, but with structural-parallelism. YOUSPEAK and TRUE-LOVE are sister-projects (not divine Persons), but the structural-mode of their relation IS perichōrēsis-shaped: distinct identities, mutual-indwelling, neither-operates-without-the-other, the integration-is-the-mutual-substrate.
