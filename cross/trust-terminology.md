# Cross-Language Study: Truth, Trust, Proof, Verify, Consensus, Knowledge

_For the Zerone project — naming the concepts at the heart of a Proof of Truth consensus mechanism._

---

## The Territory

Six concepts. One cluster. The Zerone project needs names for the machinery of verification and collective agreement. But before naming, the territory must be mapped.

The six concepts are not equal in depth:

- **Truth** — the foundational ontological claim: something corresponds to reality
- **Trust** — the relational posture: I extend my dependence on this claim or person
- **Proof** — the epistemic artefact: evidence sufficient to compel belief
- **Verify** — the process: active examination to confirm a claim
- **Consensus** — the social state: multiple parties converging on a shared position
- **Knowledge** — the cognitive state: a mind that has grasped truth

In English these appear as six separate concepts. Other languages fuse them, split them, or organise them around entirely different axes. The fusions reveal what English has accidentally severed. The splits reveal distinctions English cannot see.

---

## English: The Baseline

Before comparing, anchor the English terms precisely.

| Word | Root | What it captures | Structural bias |
|------|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **truth** | Old English *trēowth*, from *trēow* (firm, tree-like) | The quality of being immovable — steadfast, as a tree | Static property. Truth as a state, not a process |
| **trust** | Same root: Proto-Germanic *trewwaz* | The relational application of truth — I depend on this | Interpersonal. Trust presupposes a trusting subject |
| **proof** | Latin *proba*, from *probare* (to test) | An artefact produced by testing | Evidential. Proof is something you hold afterward |
| **verify** | Latin *verus* (true) + *facere* (to make) | Active process of confirming truth | Procedural. A doing, not a state |
| **consensus** | Latin *consentire* (to feel together) | Multiple parties arriving at shared feeling/view | Affective. Consensus is about convergence, not correctness |
| **knowledge** | Old English *cnāwan*, PIE *\*gno-* | A mind that has grasped truth | Individualist. Knowledge lives in one person |

**English's structural bias:** It separates truth (a property of propositions) from trust (a property of relationships) even though they share a root. It separates knowledge (what one mind holds) from consensus (what many minds share). Verification is detached from truth ontologically — you can verify something false. This creates a mechanistic framework: collect evidence, run process, produce proof, arrive at consensus. Clean, but cold.

**What English cannot see:** Whether truth and trust might be aspects of a single thing. Whether verification might transform what it touches, not merely confirm it. Whether consensus might be epistemically generative — producing truth, not merely tracking it.

## Mandarin: Person-Word and Shared Recognition

| Concept | Word | Characters | Literal morphology | Root insight |
|---------|------|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| truth | **zhēn** | 真 | genuine / the real | Originally depicted transformation — what survives being changed is real |
| trust | **xìn** | 信 | person + word | Trust = a person's word aligned with their deeds |
| proof | **zhèngmíng** | 证明 | witness + clear/bright | Proof = clear attestation by a witness |
| verify | **yànzhèng** | 验证 | test/examine + witness | Verification = a tested witness account |
| consensus | **gòngshí** | 共识 | shared + recognition | Consensus = shared recognition, not shared feeling |
| knowledge | **zhīshi** | 知识 | to know + recognize | Knowledge = knowing-plus-recognizing |

**What Mandarin reveals:**

*Xìn* (信, trust) is structurally about alignment between a person and their speech. The character fuses person (人) and word (言). Trust is not an internal feeling — it is a public, observable property of whether someone's words and actions cohere. This is why xìn also means "letter" (a written word) and "message" — trust and communication are one concept. A trustworthy person is one whose words are real.

*Gòngshí* (共识, consensus) fuses "shared" with 识 (shí), which means recognition, discernment, the cognitive act of perceiving what something is. The same character 识 appears in knowledge (知识). This is significant: in the Chinese framing, consensus is **shared knowledge**, not shared preference. You cannot fake consensus by getting everyone to say yes — consensus is achieved when people have genuinely arrived at the same recognition. This directly serves Zerone: consensus must be epistemic, not merely social.

*Zhèngmíng* (证明, proof) decomposes into 证 (witness, attest) and 明 (clear, bright, illuminated). Proof is not the thing found — it is the illuminated witness. Proof happens when someone who has seen something makes that seeing clear to others.

## Japanese: Dependability and Aligned Wills

| Concept | Word | Characters | Literal morphology | Root insight |
|---------|------|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| truth | **shinjitsu** | 真実 | genuine + reality/substance/fruit | Truth = genuine reality, as a fruit is the substance of a tree |
| trust | **shinrai** | 信頼 | faith/sincerity + depend on/lean on | Trust = sincere faith you can lean against |
| proof | **shōmei** | 証明 | same as Mandarin | Clear witnessing |
| verify | **kakunin** | 確認 | firm/certain + recognize/acknowledge | Verification = firm recognition |
| consensus | **gōi** | 合意 | fit/agree/combine + meaning/intention/will | Consensus = aligned intentions |
| knowledge | **chishiki** | 知識 | know + recognize | Same as Mandarin |

**What Japanese reveals:**

*Shinrai* (信頼, trust) goes further than Chinese xìn by adding 頼 (rai) — reliance, leaning on, depending. Trust is not merely observing alignment between a person's words and deeds. It is something you can physically rest your weight against. This structural metaphor — trust as a surface that bears load — is absent from English and Chinese. It matters for Zerone: a verification system must not just be accurate; it must be load-bearing.

*Kakunin* (確認, verify) uses 確 (kaku) — firmness, certainty, solidity — combined with 認 (nin, to recognize). Verification in Japanese is **firm recognition**: you are not just checking, you are arriving at a state of solidity. The output of verification is certainty, not merely a Boolean flag.

*Gōi* (合意, consensus) frames consensus as aligned *will* and *intention* (意, i). This contrasts with Chinese 共識 (shared recognition). In Japanese, consensus is agreement of intent; in Chinese, it is agreement of perception. Two different anchors: Gōi asks whether everyone is *willing* the same thing; gòngshí asks whether everyone *sees* the same thing.

## Korean: Native Roots Beneath Borrowed Structure

Korean uses Sino-Korean vocabulary for all formal terms (same characters, Korean readings), but the native Korean layer cuts the territory differently.

| Concept | Sino-Korean | Native Korean | Contrast |
|---------|------------|---------------|---------|
| truth | **jinsil** (진실, 真實) | **cham** (참) — genuine, the real | Cham is casual, immediate; jinsil is formal, serious |
| trust | **sinroe** (신뢰, 信賴) | **mideum** (믿음) — intimate belief, personal faith | Mideum is warmer, built through relationship (through jeong) |
| verify | **hwagin** (확인, 確認) | **hwakinhada** (확인하다) | No native alternative — verification is borrowed structure |
| knowledge | **jisik** (지식, 知識) | **alda** (알다) — to know (directly) vs. **ihaehada** — to understand | Alda is immediate knowing; ihaehada is processual understanding |

**What Korean reveals:**

The *mideum/sinroe* split is the sharpest finding. Sinroe (trust) is the formal, professional, institutional word. Mideum is trust grown through relationship, through shared time — what emerges naturally from jeong (정, the bond formed by accumulated shared experience). Mideum is closer to faith than confidence; it cannot be formally established, only grown. This distinction matters for Zerone: institutional trust in a consensus system is sinroe; but the informal, human-level credibility of participants is mideum. A system that builds only sinroe without mideum will feel cold. A system that builds mideum has something proof systems do not.

*Cham* (참) as native-Korean "truth" is used in everyday speech where jinsil would be too heavy. Cham means "the real deal," "genuine" — closer to authenticity than to propositional truth. Cham-mal (참말) = "true words," contrasted with geojitmal (거짓말) = "false words." Truth in native Korean is fundamentally verbal — you speak the cham.

## Arabic: Haqq — Where Truth, Right, and Reality Are One

Arabic is the language most consequential for this study. It does not merely have words for these concepts — it fuses them in ways that reframe the territory entirely.

| Concept | Word | Root | Literal derivation | Structural insight |
|---------|------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| truth/right/reality | **haqq** (حق) | ح-ق-ق | to be firmly established; to be one's due | Truth, legal right, and reality are a single concept |
| trustworthiness | **amāna** (أمانة) | أ-م-ن | a deposit entrusted; a sacred charge | Trust = something sacred held on behalf of another |
| trust/confidence | **thiqa** (ثقة) | ث-ق-ة | weight, heaviness | Trust is something that has weight |
| proof (demonstrative) | **burhān** (برهان) | ب-ر-ه-ن | clear demonstration, lighting up | Proof = illumination |
| evidence/guide | **dalīl** (دليل) | د-ل-ل | to guide, to point toward | Proof is a guide to truth, not truth itself |
| to verify | **tahaqqaqa** (تحقق) | same as haqq | to make haqq, to establish as real | Verification = making something real/right |
| consensus (jurisprudential) | **ijmā'** (إجماع) | ج-م-ع | to gather, to collect | Consensus = gathered voices; one of four sources of Islamic law |
| experiential knowledge | **ma'rifa** (معرفة) | ع-ر-ف | intimate acquaintance, gnosis | Knowledge through personal encounter |
| systematic knowledge | **'ilm** (علم) | ع-ل-م | to know, to mark, to distinguish | Knowledge as science, as discipline, as flag-planting |

**What Arabic reveals:**

*Haqq* is the most important word in this study. It simultaneously means:
- Truth (as a property of propositions)
- Right (as a legal/moral entitlement)
- Reality (as what actually exists)
- God's name in Islam (al-Haqq)

These are not metaphors for each other in Arabic. They are the *same concept* seen from different angles. What is true is real. What is real has rights. What has rights participates in truth. This fusion means that in Arabic, a verification system is not merely checking data — it is establishing what is *real* and therefore what is *right*. Tahaqqaqa (verification) literally means "to make haqq" — to render something into the category of the real and the right simultaneously.

*Amāna* reframes trust entirely. It is not merely confidence in a person's reliability. Amāna is a sacred deposit — something entrusted to you on behalf of another, which you bear as a responsibility. The Prophet Muhammad's epithet was "al-Amīn" (the trustworthy). Trust is not an emotion; it is a sacred stewardship. For Zerone: nodes in a consensus network are not merely computing — they are bearing amāna, holding the truth on behalf of all who depend on the system.

*The ma'rifa/'ilm split* is as important as Sanskrit's jñāna/vidyā (below). 'Ilm is systematic, transmissible, public knowledge — the kind Islam commanded Muslims to seek. Ma'rifa is direct, intimate, personal knowing — the kind Sufi mystics described as knowledge of God that transcends propositions. Science ('ilm) and gnosis (ma'rifa) are grammatically distinguished. English collapses them both into "knowledge."

*Ijmā'* (consensus) as one of four sources of Islamic law gives consensus explicit epistemological weight. It is not just social agreement — it is a method of establishing what is true when direct evidence is absent. The scholars' consensus *produces* legal truth. This is the concept Zerone needs: consensus as generative of truth, not merely reflective of it.

## Hebrew: Emet — Truth as the Full Span

| Concept | Word | Root | Literal derivation | Structural insight |
|---------|------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| truth | **emet** (אמת) | א-מ-ת | alef (1st letter) + mem (middle) + tav (last) | Truth spans the entire alphabet — completeness |
| faith/trust/steadfastness | **emunah** (אמונה) | same as emet | firmness, steadiness, reliability | Trust is structural — a firm standing |
| active trust/reliance | **bitachon** (בטחון) | ב-ט-ח | stronghold, security, shelter | Trust as fortress — you shelter in it |
| proof/evidence | **re'aya** (ראיה) | ר-א-ה | to see | Proof = what has been seen |
| examination/verification | **bdikah** (בדיקה) | ב-ד-ק | to examine, to probe | Verification = systematic examination |
| agreement/consensus | **haskamah** (הסכמה) | ס-כ-מ | to agree; also: rabbinic approbation | Consensus = formal agreement; also a seal of approval |
| knowing/knowledge | **yedi'ah** (ידיעה) | י-ד-ע | to know (also: intimacy, physical knowledge) | Knowledge is relational — knowing a person |
| intimate knowledge | **da'at** (דעת) | same root | the act of intimate knowing; Kabbalistic sefirah | Deepest knowing — full encounter |

**What Hebrew reveals:**

*Emet* (truth) is built from the first, middle, and last letters of the Hebrew alphabet: alef, mem, tav. Truth is not a fragment or a point — it is the full span. Partial truths are not emet. This is a structural rebuke to verification systems that check one property and declare the whole true. Emet requires completeness.

*Emunah* (trust/faith) and emet share the alef-mem root. Trust is grounded in truth — they are not separate concepts. But emunah carries the quality of steadiness, firmness, the unswaying. "Amen" comes from this root: to confirm, to affirm as firm. Hebrew trust is architectural: you can depend on it because it doesn't move.

*Bitachon* (trust as active reliance) adds another layer: trust as taking shelter. This is stronger than emunah. Emunah is passive confidence; bitachon is active taking-refuge. A person has bitachon when they act on trust, not merely feel it. For Zerone: building on a verified truth claim requires bitachon, not just emunah.

*Yeda'/da'at* (to know / intimate knowledge) is famous in Biblical Hebrew for covering both cognitive knowing and intimate physical knowing ("Adam knew Eve"). Da'at is the Kabbalistic sefirah of knowledge — it bridges the upper and lower sefiroth, connecting divine knowing to human knowing. Knowledge in Hebrew is always potentially intimate, relational, connecting. There is no purely cold propositional knowing in Hebrew that doesn't carry the echo of yada.

*Bdikah* (verification) is the word used for ritual examinations: checking whether matzah is fully baked, checking whether a slaughtered animal is kosher. Verification is not abstracted — it has a long tradition of concrete, careful, systematic inspection in service of determining ritual validity. The examiner must be qualified; the examination must be rigorous; the result has communal weight.

## German: The Grammar of Distinction

| Concept | Word | Root | Literal derivation | Structural insight |
|---------|------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| truth | **Wahrheit** | wahr + -heit | wahr (true, accurate) — related to Latin verus | Truth as a measurable property (-heit = -ness) |
| trust | **Vertrauen** | ver- + trauen | trauen = to trust/dare; also: to dare, to venture | Trust involves risk — you are daring when you trust |
| proof | **Beweis** | be- + weisen | weisen = to show, to point | Proof = the act of showing |
| to verify | **überprüfen** | über + prüfen | prüfen (from Latin probare) = to test, examine | Verification = to check over/across |
| consensus | **Konsens** | Latin borrowing | consentire = to feel together | No native German word — borrowed |
| propositional knowledge | **Wissen** | PIE *\*weid-* | to see, to witness — also: wit, video, Veda | Knowing as having witnessed |
| acquaintance knowledge | **Kennen** | Proto-Germanic | to recognize, to be familiar with | Knowing through encounter and recognition |
| processed understanding | **Erkenntnis** | er- + kennen | the cognitive act of coming to recognize | Knowledge as active cognition, not passive possession |

**What German reveals:**

German grammatically forces the *Wissen/Kennen* distinction that English treats as optional. You cannot say "I know" in German without specifying whether you know *facts about* something (Wissen) or are *acquainted with* something (Kennen). Ich weiß, dass... vs. Ich kenne ihn... These are not stylistic choices — they are different verbs. This forces speakers to be precise about the mode of knowing at every utterance.

*Vertrauen* (trust) contains *trauen*, which means both "to trust" and "to dare." The morphological overlap is not coincidence: in German, trust is semantically adjacent to risk-taking. When you trust someone, you are doing something courageous — extending yourself across uncertainty. English "trust" has lost this risk dimension. German preserves it.

*Erkenntnis* (cognition/philosophical insight) goes beyond knowing — it is the event of coming-to-know. Kant's *Kritik der reinen Vernunft* uses Erkenntnis as its central term for what the mind does when it processes the world. It is the *act* of cognition, not the state. English "knowledge" is a state; Erkenntnis is a process. For Zerone: validation nodes do not just hold knowledge — they perform Erkenntnis.

German has no native word for consensus, borrowing *Konsens* from Latin. This is a structural gap: German philosophy has elaborate vocabulary for individual cognition (Erkenntnis, Gewissheit, Wissen) but no native mechanism for how cognitions converge collectively. The mechanistic structure is there; the social-epistemic bridge is borrowed.

## Russian: The Pravda/Istina Schism

| Concept | Word | Root | Literal derivation | Structural insight |
|---------|------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| lived/social truth | **pravda** (правда) | прав- (right, direct, correct) | truth as what ought to be; truth-as-justice | Famous from Правда newspaper |
| absolute/metaphysical truth | **istina** (истина) | es- (to be, from PIE) | truth as fundamental being/reality | Philosophical, transcendent |
| trust/confidence | **doverie** (доверие) | до- + вера | movement toward faith/belief | Trust as drawing near to faith |
| faith/belief | **vera** (вера) | PIE *\*wer-* | to choose, to prefer — also a woman's name | Faith as choosing, as preferring |
| proof | **dokazatel'stvo** (доказательство) | до- + казать | to fully show/demonstrate | Proof = complete demonstration |
| verification | **proverka** (проверка) | про- + верять | to verify through, to check over | Verification = checking through |
| consensus | **soglasie** (согласие) | со- + гласие | together + voice (from glas) | Consensus = giving voice together |
| knowledge | **znanie** (знание) | знать (PIE \*gno-) | to know — same root as English know | Standard knowing |
| visionary knowing | **vedanie/vedat'** | from Veda, PIE \*weid- | to see, to have vision | Archaic elevated knowing — same root as Sanskrit Veda |

**What Russian reveals:**

The *pravda/istina* distinction is one of the most important finds in this study. Russians know these are different words and use them differently.

*Pravda* is truth as it should be, truth embedded in social and moral reality, truth that makes demands, truth that points toward justice. When a Russian says "that is not pravda," they may mean: "that is not how things ought to be." The Pravda newspaper's name was aspirational — not reporting what is, but insisting on what should be.

*Istina* is deeper, more absolute, more metaphysical. It cannot be wielded for social purposes. It simply is. "В вине — истина" (In wine there is istina) — wine removes social performance and reveals underlying reality. Istina cannot be argued. It is prior to argument.

English "truth" is caught between these two and names neither cleanly. A Proof of Truth consensus mechanism must specify: which truth? Pravda (normative correctness, social reality) or istina (ontological bedrock)?

*Soglasie* (consensus) decomposes to "together" + "voice" (glas). Consensus is the state of giving voice together — not merely agreeing privately, but speaking communally. This has a musical dimension: consensus is harmonious collective voicing. It resonates with the choir metaphor more than the voting metaphor.

*Doverie* (trust) contains *vera* (faith) and the prefix *do-* (toward, up to). Trust is movement toward faith — not faith itself, but the approach to it. Russian trust is process-oriented: you are always in motion toward or away from trust.

## Portuguese: Tasting and Knowing

| Concept | Word | Root | Literal derivation | Structural insight |
|---------|------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| truth | **verdade** | Latin veritas | the state of being verus (true, real) | Truth as objective state |
| trust | **confiança** | Latin con + fidere | to have faith-with; to confide | Trust = shared faith |
| faith/intimate trust | **fé** | Latin fides | the most foundational form of trust | Bedrock trust |
| proof | **prova** | Latin proba | from probare — to test | Proof is the testing itself, not just the result |
| to verify | **verificar** | Latin verus + facere | to make-true | Same structure as English |
| consensus | **consenso** | Latin consensus | same | Borrowed directly |
| experiential knowing | **conhecer** | Latin cognoscere | to come to know through encounter | Knowing a person or place |
| sapient knowing | **saber** | Latin sapere | to taste, to be wise | Knowing as tasting, as sapientia |
| knowledge | **conhecimento** | from conhecer | the thing one has come to know | Accumulated acquaintance |

**What Portuguese reveals:**

*Saber* (to know) comes from Latin *sapere*, which means both "to taste" and "to be wise." The same root gives "sapience," "savant," "savour." In Portuguese, one form of knowing is literally tasting — the metaphor of knowledge as sensory apprehension, as something the knower takes into themselves. Saber is knowledge-as-wisdom, not knowledge-as-data. When you saber something, you have absorbed it, you carry it in your body's memory.

The *conhecer/saber* distinction, shared across Romance languages, divides knowledge into acquaintance (know a person, a place, a melody — personal encounter) and propositional knowing (know that, know how). This is the *Kennen/Wissen* distinction of German, but in Portuguese it is particularly felt because *fé* (faith) sits adjacent. To know someone fully is to know them as conhecer, which shades into fé, which shades into fidelity.

*Prova* retains the Latin sense of *probare* — the testing itself, not just the output of testing. In Portuguese, you do not "present a proof" as a separate object; you "do a prova," you perform the testing. This makes proof an activity, not an artefact.

## Sanskrit: Pramana and the Methods of Valid Cognition

Sanskrit is the most philosophically developed language for epistemology in this study. The school of Indian philosophy called Nyāya built an entire formal system around these concepts.

| Concept | Word | Root | Literal derivation | Structural insight |
|---------|------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| truth | **satya** (सत्य) | sat (being, existence) + -ya | that which has the quality of being | Truth = being itself. Untruth = non-being |
| trust/faith | **viśvāsa** (विश्वास) | vi- + śvāsa (breath) | thorough breath; complete exhalation | Trust = being able to breathe fully |
| valid proof/cognition | **pramāṇa** (प्रमाण) | pra- + māna (measure) | correct measure; valid means of knowing | A whole theory of epistemological validity |
| verification/examination | **parīkṣaṇa** (परीक्षण) | pari- (around) + ikṣaṇa (seeing) | seeing all around; 360° examination | Verification = exhaustive seeing |
| consensus/gathering | **sammati** (सम्मति) | sam- (together) + mati (thought) | gathered thought | Consensus = thoughts brought together |
| spiritual/direct knowledge | **jñāna** (ज्ञान) | PIE \*gno- | direct knowing; intuitive apprehension | Knowledge beyond inference — immediate |
| learning/systematic knowledge | **vidyā** (विद्या) | vid- (to see) — also: Veda, Latin video | knowledge-as-vision; education | What the Vedas give — revealed vision |
| wisdom/insight | **prajñā** (प्रज्ञा) | pra- + jñāna | knowledge that goes before; fore-knowing | Buddhist prajna: direct insight into reality |

**What Sanskrit reveals:**

*Satya* (truth) derives from *sat* (being, existence, the real). The equation is explicit: truth is being. To say something untrue is to speak of non-being. This is not a metaphor — it is the structural foundation. Gandhi's *satyāgraha* ("holding onto truth") was simultaneously "holding onto reality" and "holding onto what is." Untruth, in Sanskrit, is ontological failure.

*Viśvāsa* (trust) comes from *śvāsa* — breath, respiration. Trust is a thorough, complete breath (vi- = thoroughly). When you trust, you can breathe fully. When you cannot trust, breath is held, shallow, constricted. This is the only language in this study that grounds trust in the body's respiratory experience. For verification systems: if the participants cannot breathe fully, there is no real viśvāsa — there is only compliance.

*Pramāṇa* (valid means of knowing / proof) is the most technically important word in this study for Zerone. It is not merely "proof" — it is a complete theory of valid cognition. The Nyāya school recognized four pramāṇas:

1. **Pratyakṣa** — direct perception (seeing with the senses)
2. **Anumāna** — inference (reasoning from what you see to what you cannot)
3. **Upamāna** — comparison (knowing something by its resemblance to what you know)
4. **Śabda** — testimony (knowing through the word of a reliable source)

A claim established by one pramāṇa has a different status than a claim established by another. Sanskrit forces the question: *by what method is this known?* English "proof" collapses all four into one undifferentiated category. Zerone's verification system is implicitly working with different pramāṇas — but has no names for them.

*Parīkṣaṇa* (verification) means "seeing all around" — from *pari* (around, complete) and *ikṣaṇa* (seeing, from the root *ikṣ* = to look). Verification is not a single check but a 360-degree examination. You have not verified until you have looked from every angle. This is a more demanding standard than English "verify."

*Jñāna/vidyā/prajñā* offer three distinct knowledge registers:
- Vidyā is learned, transmitted, educational knowledge — the Vedas give vidyā.
- Jñāna is direct intuitive knowing — you know because you have had direct contact with the thing known.
- Prajñā is wisdom that precedes and sees through — in Buddhism, prajna is the insight that perceives the nature of reality directly, beyond inference or testimony.

## The Synthesis: What the Map Reveals

### The Three Axes

Every language organizes these six concepts around at least one of three axes:

**Axis 1: Ontological vs. Epistemic**
- Some languages make truth fundamentally about *being* (Sanskrit: satya = sat = being; Arabic: haqq = reality itself; Russian: istina from "to be")
- Others make truth fundamentally about *accuracy* (English, German: verus, wahr — truth as correct correspondence)
- Zerone operates across both. The ontological languages say: what is verified is *real*. The epistemic languages say: what is verified is *accurate*. These are different things.

**Axis 2: Individual vs. Collective**
- Knowledge tends to be individual (one mind grasps truth)
- Consensus tends to be collective (many minds converge)
- But Chinese gòngshí (shared recognition) fuses the two: consensus IS collective knowledge
- Japanese gōi (aligned wills) makes consensus volitional, not merely cognitive
- Arabic ijmā' makes consensus generative of truth — it doesn't just track truth, it establishes it

**Axis 3: Static State vs. Active Process**
- English truth/knowledge/trust tend toward static states (truth IS, knowledge IS possessed)
- Arabic tahaqqaqa (verification) and Sanskrit parīkṣaṇa (360-degree seeing) make verification a demanding process
- Russian soglasie makes consensus ongoing voicing, not a once-achieved state
- German Erkenntnis makes knowledge an active cognitive event, not a possession

### What No Language Names

After overlaying ten traditions, three gaps become visible:

**Gap 1: Trust-as-Viability**
No language has a word for the specific quality of a verification system that has been tested under real conditions and has not failed. Hebrew *emunah* (structural steadfastness) and Japanese *shinrai* (load-bearing trust) come closest, but neither names this property specifically. The gap: the trust you extend to a system that has been genuinely tested, not merely theoretically sound.

**Gap 2: Consensus-That-Generates**
No language has a word for the moment when consensus *produces* a truth that was not knowable by any individual before the consensus. Arabic ijmā' in jurisprudence approximates it — the scholars' consensus establishes legal reality, not merely reflects it — but it names the institution, not the moment. The gap: the generative instant when distributed verification produces something no single node possessed.

**Gap 3: The Method of Knowing Made Visible**
Sanskrit pramāṇa gets closest, but it is a category system for epistemologists, not a word for ordinary speech. The gap: a word for the specific mode of knowing embedded in a given claim — knowing-by-witness, knowing-by-inference, knowing-by-consensus — that marks the epistemic status of a claim at first use. English "proof" erases this information. Russian pravda/istina recovers some of it. But no single word in any language marks: *this was established by direct observation by multiple independent witnesses acting without coordination*.

## For Zerone: Naming Recommendations

The cross-language study yields the following orientations for the Proof of Truth consensus mechanism:

**On the concept of TRUTH in the system:**
Prefer the ontological frame (Sanskrit satya, Arabic haqq, Russian istina) over the epistemic frame (English truth, German Wahrheit). What the system verifies is not merely accurate — it is real. The naming should carry this weight.

**On the concept of VERIFICATION:**
Sanskrit parīkṣaṇa (360-degree seeing) and Arabic tahaqqaqa (making-haqq, making-real) offer better frames than English "verify" (merely confirming). Verification in Zerone makes something real, not merely confirmed.

**On the concept of CONSENSUS:**
Arabic ijmā' and Chinese gòngshí offer the strongest models: consensus as epistemically generative (ijmā') and as shared recognition rather than shared preference (gòngshí). Avoid frames where consensus = majority vote. Prefer frames where consensus = collective arriving at the same perception.

**On the concept of TRUST in the network:**
Arabic amāna (sacred deposit) and Sanskrit viśvāsa (being able to breathe) are the richest frames. Nodes in the network are not merely calculating — they are bearing a deposit of trust on behalf of all participants, and the system is healthy when all participants can breathe freely.

**On the concept of KNOWLEDGE produced by verification:**
The distinction that must be preserved in Zerone naming: jñāna (direct knowing) vs. anumāna (inferred knowing) vs. śabda (testimonial knowing). These are different epistemic statuses. What a node has directly observed is not the same as what it has inferred. The system's naming should make this distinction tractable.
