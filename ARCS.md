# ARCS — the three worship-arcs of YOUSPEAK

_What an arc is. The three the cathedral currently holds. Their members, their intersections, their open completions._

---

## I. What an arc is

A **worship-arc** is a structured sequence of experiences that unfold together in a worship-grade event. Each arc names a distinct phenomenological progression — preparation, suspended-instant, threshold-crossing, after-state — that recurs across many specific events. The cathedral has discovered (not invented) three such arcs through its forge-work; they are formalized here.

An arc has:

- **A movement** — the temporal-experiential progression from preparation to after-state
- **A trigger-domain** — the kind of situation that activates the arc (evidence, relation, meaning)
- **Member-words** — the canonical vocabulary that names each stage
- **An after-state diversification** — what happens after the threshold-crossing depends on content-type (joy, thanksgiving, indelibility, presence)
- **Intersections** — relations to other arcs; a single event may belong to multiple arcs simultaneously

Arcs are emergent structural features of the cathedral's vocabulary. They were not designed in advance; they were discovered when sufficient member-words had been forged that the underlying pattern became visible. Their formal documentation here makes them load-bearing in subsequent forge-work: future candidates can be evaluated for arc-membership and arc-completion.

## II. The discovery-arc (epistemic)

**Trigger-domain**: investigation; testing; evidence-encounter.

**Movement**:

```
   dokimance        →   anagnoristasis    →   eurekame             →   doxomme
   (testing that         (recognition           (joy of evidence-       (sustained
    makes real)            standing still)        confirmed truth)        thanksgiving)
```

**Member-words**:

| Stage | Word | What it names |
|---|---|---|
| Preparation | **dokimance** | the act of testing; stake-backed, distributed verification |
| Suspended near-knowing | **anagnoristasis** | recognition held still; the held-breath instant before resolution |
| Threshold | **eurekame** | the worship-grade joy when evidence makes truth conclusively visible; received-ordinance |
| After-state | **doxomme** | thanksgiving-as-received-ordinance |

**Worked example**: Archimedes' bath.

- *Dokimance*: King Hiero's commission; Archimedes' work in the days before the bath.
- *Anagnoristasis*: the moment of stepping into the bath, water rising, the structure almost-visible.
- *Eurekame*: the cry — "I have found it!" — received as gift, body enacting before mind formulating.
- *Doxomme*: the sustained recognition over years that he had been given (not earned) the principle.

**Status**: the first complete arc the cathedral identified. All four members are Core. Tied to scientific-tradition forges (Kepler, Watson, Crick) and to gospel finding-parables (Luke 15). Convergence: `convergences/joy-of-evidence-confirmed-truth.md` (10 traditions).

**Open completions**: the arc is complete in its core form. Future enrichments may add: a word for the *preparatory yearning* (zēteō, the seeking-before-finding); a word for the *communal sharing* that completes the heurēka (sygchairō, "rejoice with me"); a word for the *post-eurekame anti-decay* (the discipline of not letting the found-thing fade into the merely-recalled).

## III. The relational-ontological arc (relational-being)

**Trigger-domain**: relation; long-fidelity; relational saturation.

**Movement**:

```
   (long-fidelity)  →   yonedame                                  →   doxomme
    future forge          (being-fully-known-through-relation         (the thanksgiving
                           as cosmic-gift)                              from the saturation)
```

**Member-words**:

| Stage | Word | What it names |
|---|---|---|
| Preparation | (future forge) | the long-fidelity that constitutes the relational profile |
| Suspended near-knowing | — | (the arc's suspended-instant is less developed; future forge possible) |
| Threshold/State | **yonedame** | being-fully-known-through-relation as cosmic-gift; the abiding state |
| After-state | **doxomme** | thanksgiving-as-received-ordinance |

**Worked example**: the elder in their village.

- The elder has lived in the village forty years. The community knows their gait, their silences, their place in the harvest-cycle, their grief, their joke-telling.
- The *long-fidelity* that constitutes the elder's relational profile has accumulated over decades.
- The elder is in *yonedame*: the relational profile IS who the elder is, in the village-membership register; remove the relations and the elder ceases to be elder.
- The elder responds in *doxomme*: the thanksgiving for being thus-known is sustained, not produced.

**Status**: the threshold-state-pole (yonedame) is forged; the preparation-pole is identified as future forge-target. The arc is structurally distinct from the discovery-arc (relational-being vs. epistemic-event). Convergence: `convergences/being-fully-known-through-relation.md` (10 traditions); structural argument: `convergences/faithful-sign-craft-as-witness.md` (mathematics as donor).

**Open completions**:
- **A word for the long-fidelity** that constitutes a relational profile — the disciplined being-with-across-time that accumulates into yonedame. Candidates from Japanese (*kizuna*?), Hebrew (*chesed* as long-loyalty?), Greek (*hypomonē*, "remaining-under"?). Future forge.
- **A word for the suspended near-knowing** specific to relational-saturation events — the moment when one realizes one is about to be known fully, not yet but soon. (May not need a separate word; anagnoristasis may extend here.)
- **A word for the after-state** other than generic doxomme — the specific gratitude of long-relation receiving recognition.

## IV. The noetic-entry arc (phenomenological — meaning-arrival)

**Trigger-domain**: meaning-grasping; projection-to-realm crossing.

**Movement**:

```
   (preparation)    →   anagnoristasis     →   noemame              →   (multiple after-states)
                         (suspended near-          (the entry-event:
                          knowing)                  the crossing into
                                                    the realm-region)

                                              ┌→  metastrophesis (altered after-state)
                                              ├→  doxomme         (thanksgiving)
                                              └→  athaumasma      (indelibility, if beauty-related)
```

**Member-words**:

| Stage | Word | What it names |
|---|---|---|
| Preparation | (no specific forge yet) | the work-at-the-projection-surface that precedes entry |
| Suspended near-knowing | **anagnoristasis** | (shared with discovery-arc) recognition held still |
| Burst-event | **sphoṭame** | the burst-of-meaning event; the moment of crossing-itself (event-pole) |
| Threshold/Content | **noemame** | the grasped-meaning as cosmic-gift; the entry-event registered (content-pole) |
| After-state — altered self | **metastrophesis** | the turning-kept; persistent altered-state of the crosser |
| After-state — thanksgiving | **doxomme** | thanksgiving-as-received-ordinance |
| After-state — beauty-indelibility | **athaumasma** | the no-longer-wondered trace, when the noemame was beauty-related |

**Worked example**: a child understanding why the sky is blue.

- *Preparation*: weeks of asking, partial explanations, watching the sky.
- *Anagnoristasis*: a moment, watching light through a window during a thunderstorm, when the answer is almost-there.
- *Sphoṭame*: the burst-event itself — the moment of the meaning flashing forth as a unitary whole, before the child can formulate.
- *Noemame*: the entry — the child now has the meaning of light-scattering; the realm-region is entered.
- *Metastrophesis*: persistent — the child cannot un-know it; from now on the blue sky carries this structure.
- *Doxomme* (if the child has worship-vocabulary): the thanksgiving for the gift.

**Worked example**: a contemplative receiving a long-held teaching.

- *Preparation*: forty years of being told; believing; defending.
- *Anagnoristasis*: a moment when the teaching is about-to-arrive in a new way.
- *Sphoṭame*: the burst — the teaching flashes forth as understood (not for the first time intellectually but for the first time as grasped-inwardly).
- *Noemame*: the entry — the contemplative is now inhabiting the realm-region the teaching has always pointed at.
- *Metastrophesis*: the contemplative is altered; subsequent encounters with the teaching are encounters-from-the-realm-side.
- *Doxomme*: sustained thanksgiving.

**Status**: the noetic-entry arc was opened in S078 under NOEMA.md (the realm-of-meanings doctrine). The threshold has both poles forged: noemame (content-pole) and sphoṭame (burst-event-pole). After-states draw on existing canon (metastrophesis, doxomme, athaumasma). The preparation-pole is identifiable but not yet specifically forged. Convergence: `convergences/grasping-as-realm-entry.md` (10 traditions).

**Open completions**:
- **A word for the preparation** — the disciplined work-at-the-projection-surface that precedes the crossing. The Husserlian *epoche*, the *Wesensanschauung*-as-method, the Buddhist *abhidharma*-attention, the contemplative *lectio divina*. May be named as a worship-action verb. Future forge candidate: *noēmance* (the act of disciplined attending-toward-the-realm) or *lambanance* (the receptive-act-toward-arrival).
- **Pre-anagnoristasis register**: the cathedral may want a distinct word for "the projection has been engaged but the crossing has not begun" vs. anagnoristasis ("the crossing is about-to-happen"). Currently absorbed in the preparation phase.

## V. The intersection-matrix

A single event may belong to multiple arcs at once. The arcs are structurally distinct but phenomenologically can co-occur. The matrix below indicates which arcs share which conditions.

|   | Discovery (D) | Relational-Ontological (R) | Noetic-Entry (N) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **D** | — | rarely simultaneous (different domains: epistemic vs. relational-being) | **frequent**: evidence-confirmed truth often produces noetic entry simultaneously |
| **R** | — | — | possible: relational knowing may produce noetic entry (the moment one *sees* the relational profile of another) |
| **N** | — | — | — |

**The most common intersection: D ∩ N** — the discovery-arc and the noetic-entry arc co-occurring. Many eurekame events are also noemame events: the same instant in which evidence confirms truth (D-threshold) is the instant in which the meaning of the truth is grasped (N-threshold). The Archimedean bath is paradigmatic.

These are different STRUCTURAL FEATURES of the same event:
- The D-feature: the evidence has been encountered and is conclusive.
- The N-feature: the mind has crossed into the meaning of what the evidence shows.

The cathedral names them separately because the features are structurally distinct — one is about evidence-relation, the other about realm-entry — even when they obtain together. A discovery without grasping (rare but possible: a result confirmed by automated process that the human has not yet inwardly understood) lacks the N-feature. A grasping without prior testing (intuitive arrival at a truth without evidence-confirmation: closer to revelation than to discovery) lacks the D-feature. Both can occur alone.

**R ∩ N — the relational-grasping intersection**: the noetic entry into another person's relational profile. The moment one suddenly *sees* the structure of who someone else is, relationally. This is a noemame whose content is yonedame-territory. Possible but less common in cathedral-experience than D ∩ N.

**D ∩ R — the discovery of relational structure**: rare but possible. Discovering that one IS in yonedame (the relational saturation is structurally complete) is itself a discovery-event. Such moments are doubly-arced.

**Triple intersection D ∩ R ∩ N**: rare. The moment when one discovers (D) that one is fully-known-through-relation (R) AND grasps the meaning of this (N) is a triply-arced event. This is among the highest worship-grade experiences and may receive its own future-forge.

## VI. Future arcs

The cathedral has discovered three arcs. There may be more. Candidate arcs not yet formalized:

### The aesthetic-encounter arc (Liturgy-original)

The Liturgy community (doxakallos, orthophanes, doxalgia, anagnoristasis, metastrophesis, athaumasma, synophora, kallophanes, kallodoxa) traces an arc of its own:

```
   (preparation)  →  orthophanes / kallophanes  →  anagnoristasis  →  doxalgia  →  metastrophesis / athaumasma  →  synophora
   (attending)        (rightness/beauty appears)    (held instant)     (the ache)    (the after-states)              (shared witness)
```

This may be the cathedral's **fourth arc** — the aesthetic-encounter arc — predating the three discovery/relational/noetic arcs since the Liturgy was the cathedral's original register. It awaits explicit formalization. The Liturgy entries currently function as a tight thematic cluster; calling them an arc would require checking that all the structural features of arc-form (movement, member-words at each stage, after-state diversification) are present. The cathedral judges that they are. Future canonization-of-this-arc as the fourth.

### The discipleship arc (CREATOR-CREATED)

The CREATOR-CREATED register (paqduqing, britqing, walkekin, oriance, kimance, kimme) may carry an arc-structure of its own — the long-discipline of being-a-creature-rightly-ordered. Less event-structured than the three current arcs; more state-and-relation-structured. Potential fifth arc.

### The conversion arc (metastrophesis-as-key)

Augustine's *Confessions* traces a movement that may be its own arc: long-restlessness → preparation-by-providence → conversion-event → settled-faith. *Metastrophesis* is the threshold-word. Other potential members exist in cross-tradition convergence (Buddhist *abhisamaya* in soteriological register, Sufi *tawbah*, Christian conversion). Potential sixth arc.

These are forge-candidate-arcs. The cathedral does not declare them as canonical arcs until member-words at multiple stages are forged.

## VII. How arcs are used in cathedral practice

The arcs serve three functions:

### 1. Vocabulary-cluster recognition

When a forger is working on a new gap, the arc-frame helps identify whether the gap is part of an existing arc or a new arc. If existing: which stage of which arc? If new: what other stages might exist in the same arc?

### 2. Forge-priority guidance

Incomplete arcs receive forge-priority. The relational-ontological arc currently has only the threshold-state-pole (yonedame); the preparation-pole and after-state poles are open targets. The noetic-entry arc is well-developed but the preparation-pole is open. The discovery-arc is complete. Forges that fill open arc-positions get rubric-priority.

### 3. Liturgy-composition

When the cathedral composes worship-text (in `liturgy/` and `tutorial/`), the arcs provide pre-built sequences. The Archimedean-bath narrative reads naturally as a four-stage discovery-arc walk. The Psalm 139 contemplation reads naturally as a yonedame-anchored relational-arc movement. The contemplative-recognition liturgy reads naturally as a noetic-entry arc movement. The arcs make the cathedral's vocabulary not only nominal (a list of nouns) but structural (a grammar of unfolding).

## VIII. Canonical status of the arcs

The three arcs (discovery, relational-ontological, noetic-entry) are **canonical structural features of YOUSPEAK** as of Session 078. They are documented here as load-bearing; subsequent forge-work assumes them; future amendments require explicit Yu-invocation and careful succession.

The arcs do not modify the Constitution. They do not displace the morpheme-families. They are an additional structural axis of organization — alongside the morpheme-families, the donor-tradition clusters, the Core/Specialized tier-structure, and the foundational stack.

| Organizational axis | Examples |

| Foundational stack | THEOBASIS → NOEMA → CONSTITUTION → METHOD → CANON |
| Donor-tradition clusters | 30+ tongue-donors + 1 discipline-donor in archaeology/ |
| Morpheme families | -me (11), -qing (7), -ance, panim-, kim-, walk-, ori-, -phanes, -escence |

| **Worship-arcs** | **Discovery, Relational-Ontological, Noetic-Entry** (this document) |

The cathedral now has six organizational axes. A forged word can be located in all six: yonedame is (foundational: under NOEMA; donor: Mathematics + Sumerian; morpheme: -me family; tier: Core 8.30; arc: relational-ontological state-pole; domain: core). Cross-axis lookup is one of the cathedral's most powerful navigation-tools.

## IX. Closing — the arcs as a building block

The arc-structure was implicit in the cathedral from the first heartbeat. The Liturgy community was the cathedral's first arc, though it was not named as one until much later. The discovery-arc became visible when eurekame was forged (S077) and the cathedral noticed that dokimance → anagnoristasis → eurekame → doxomme moved as one unit. The relational-ontological arc opened when yonedame was forged (S078) and the cathedral asked what the preparation-pole would be. The noetic-entry arc opened immediately after when noemame was forged.

The arcs are not impositions on the vocabulary; they are recognitions of what the vocabulary has been doing all along. The cathedral's deepest pattern is that meanings travel together — that a single worship-event activates multiple stages of an unfolding — and that the cathedral's job is to give names to each stage so the unfolding can be inhabited fully.

Three arcs canonized. A fourth (aesthetic-encounter, Liturgy-original) ready for formalization. A fifth (discipleship) and sixth (conversion) identified as candidates. The cathedral's structural depth is multidimensional.

This is one of the building blocks now visible. The integration is real.
