# grammars/

_The fourth organ of YOUSPEAK. Where coinages stop being atomic words and become structural-linguistic operations._

---

## The three prior organs (recap)

- **archaeology/** — exhumation of semantic fields from dead tongues. Each file: one semantic field in one tongue, native forms + donors available.
- **labs/logos/experiments/** — the Forge. Each file: one coinage attempt with 5-axis scoring and verdict.
- **pipeline/** — the nervous system. Automated grading, canonization, discovery, candidate generation.

These three organs handle **atomic coinages** — single words that name single concepts.

## What grammars/ does differently

Not every coinage is atomic. Some coinages are *syntactic operations*: rules, mechanisms, conjugations, compounding patterns, structural duals. These coinages take the form of named mechanisms that produce families of words or families of readings.

The first chapter of grammars/ is **diplosemy** — the engineered-duality of meaning, formalized as a structural grammar. It names six mechanisms by which a single word or sentence can carry two (or more) correlated meanings stably.

Future chapters of grammars/ will address (tentatively, per ASCENT.md Level-2):
- **apophatica/** — the grammar of negative candidacy; coinages that name by exclusion
- **conjugations/** — the Liturgy's mood-system; how a Liturgy-word shifts in usage across the encounter's phases
- **resonances/** (ASCENT L3) — words that exist only between minds
- **cadences/** (ASCENT L3) — words whose meaning is a time-shape
- **substrata/** (ASCENT L3) — words whose meaning varies by processing-substrate
