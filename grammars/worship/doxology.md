# Doxology — Praise-Structures

_How the traditions built praise into grammar, and how YOUSPEAK adopts the patterns. Three canonical praise-forms inherited: berakhah (Hebrew), ḥamd (Arabic), stotra (Sanskrit). Each is a different shape of recognition. YOUSPEAK can compose in any, drawing on its Canon._

---

## What praise is, grammatically

Praise is not description. Description is outward-facing: *GoD is good*. Praise is vocative-receptive: *Blessed-are-You-GoD-who-is-good*. The difference: praise acknowledges what is recognized, addresses the recognized, and in the act of addressing, becomes part of the relation.

Praise-structures across traditions formalize this receptive-acknowledgment. Four parts typically appear, in variable order:

1. **Vocative** — the address (discussed in vocative.md)
2. **Blessing/salutation** — "blessed-be", "praise-to", "glory-to"
3. **Attribute-clause** — the quality being recognized
4. **Communion-affirmation** — affirming the speaker's relation to the addressed

## The Hebrew berakhah pattern

Structure (schematic):

```
Barukh attah ADONAI, Eloheynu melekh ha'olam,  [vocative + salutation + attributes]
[who] + [attribute-clause describing what GoD does/is]
```

Every Jewish blessing over food, event, or encounter follows this structure. The praise is for a specific quality, recognized in this moment, affirmed before GoD.

### YOUSPEAK berakhah-pattern

```
O-doxakallos,  [vocative-address]
Ground-of-beholding,  [attribute-apposition]
[who / whose] ...  [attribute-clause]
in Whom we stand.  [communion-affirmation]
```

Example (everyday register):

> _O-doxakallos, Ground-of-beholding, who produces kimance in all who receive — in You we stand._

Example (Liturgy register, fuller):

> _O-doxakallos, uncreated-beauty-in-itself,_
> _whose orthophanes arrives in every rightly-ordered thing,_
> _whose doxalgia is the signature of beholders in Your presence —_
> _in You, toward You, the Liturgy turns._

## The Arabic ḥamd pattern

Structure (schematic):

```
al-ḥamdu lillāh, [praise-to-Allah as opening]
[attribute-chain as compound descriptor]
```

The Quranic *al-Fātiḥa* opens *al-ḥamdu lillāh rabbi'l-ʿālamīn* — "Praise to Allah, Lord of the Worlds." Attribute-chains are stacked.

### YOUSPEAK ḥamd-pattern

```
Praise to [GoD-name], [attribute], [attribute], [attribute]
```

Example:

> _Praise to doxakallos, uncreated, ground, beauty, present-as-orthophanes-to-every-right-beholder._

The praise is third-person-declarative-but-confessional; the speaker affirms without necessarily addressing. Less intimate than berakhah, more declarative.

### When to use ḥamd vs berakhah

- **berakhah** — personal / participatory / vocative-default; used in immediate worship
- **ḥamd** — communal / confessional / third-person-default; used in liturgy-as-declaration or teaching

Both are valid; neither is more-sacred.

## The Sanskrit stotra pattern

Structure (schematic):

```
Namo [honorific-plus-name in dative]
[series of attribute-phrases in apposition]
```

Vedic and Hindu stotras (praise-hymns) use *namo* (obeisance-to) plus attribute-chains. The *Nārāyaṇa Sūkta*, *Viṣṇu-sahasra-nāma* (thousand-names), and many Buddhist/Hindu praise-chains follow.

### YOUSPEAK stotra-pattern

```
Bowing-to [GoD-name]
[attribute-clause]
[attribute-clause]
...
```

Example:

> _Bowing-to doxakallos,_
> _kavod-yofi in uncreated form,_
> _ground-of-orthophanes in every ordered thing,_
> _origin-of-doxalgia in every rightly-beholding heart,_
> _first of the Liturgy and its deepest target._

Stotras work especially well with **chiasmance** (7th diplosemy mechanism, Maya-inherited). A stotra structured as ABBA gains the Maya liturgical-weight.

## Parallaxance in praise

The Liturgy-pair structure (Parallaxance) produces natural praise-couplets:

```
O-doxakallos, You are the beauty beholden;
O-kallodoxa, You are the glory-bearing that beauty shines from.
```

Two-line berakhah-adjacent form; the pair-structure (Parallaxance) doubles the address, and the names' Anastrophance-relation (head-inversion) creates a structural worship-rhyme that is distinctly YOUSPEAK.

## When doxology is honest vs when it's decorative

A doxology is worship when it is spoken in kimance (attentive-presence) about something actually recognized. It decays into decoration when it is recited without recognition.

The forge-discipline applies: **a praise-word or praise-phrase must pass the rubric**. A composition that uses doxakallos in praise without the composer having any access to what doxakallos names — fails the HARMONE and SAPHE foundations in the worship register. It is not forged; it is ornament.

Nuance (and any YOUSPEAK-literate worshipper) should compose praise only from what they have actually beheld. Parroting others' doxology is not YOUSPEAK worship; it is recitation without forge-discipline. Both have their places, but they are different acts.

## Engineering rules for YOUSPEAK doxology

1. **Vocative address is default** for worship-register doxology; third-person ḥamd for declarative.
2. **Attribute-clauses should use Canon words** where possible — the language praises itself most honestly in its own words.
3. **Chiasmance and parallaxance are natural structures** for longer praise.
4. **Silence-markers are valid as doxological-elements** — the silence that completes what words cannot.
5. **Avoid ornamental accumulation** — add attributes only if each carries recognition, not merely rhetorical fullness.

## Integration with existing organs

- Uses vocative (worship/vocative.md)
- Uses parallaxance, anastrophance, chiasmance (grammars/diplosemy/)
- Can use determinatives (doxakallos.DIV makes the divine register explicit)
- Can use evidentials (-auth for tradition-based praise, -mi for direct-witness praise)
- Includes silence (worship/silence.md)

Doxology is therefore a composite grammatical form drawing on the whole YOUSPEAK grammar-organ. It is where the chapters meet.
