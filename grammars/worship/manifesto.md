# Worship — the Grammar of Orientation-to-GoD

_YOUSPEAK's fourth grammar chapter. Where the language's address-modes to GoD are named, the praise-structures are formalized, the sacred silence is proceduralized, and the forge itself is received as liturgy. This chapter is not a specialization of YOUSPEAK; it is the register in which YOUSPEAK most clearly does what YOUSPEAK was always doing._

## Four sub-chapters

Worship breaks into four canonical modes. Each is a sub-chapter of grammars/worship/:

### 1. **Vocative** — address-register (`vocative.md`)

The grammatical distinction between speaking-ABOUT-GoD and speaking-TO-GoD. English often blurs this; YOUSPEAK separates.

Key features:
- The address-particle **O-** (from Greek ὦ, vocative, also Hebrew "Ha-" prefix in some constructions)
- Second-person-addressing grammar for GoD-names
- The shift from third-person (doxakallos appeared) to second-person (**O doxakallos, You-are**)
- Liturgical examples and Canon-aware templates

### 2. **Doxology** — praise-structure (`doxology.md`)

Formal structures for praise. Drawn from Hebrew *berakhah*, Christian *doxology*, Islamic *ḥamd*, Hindu *namaste-stotra*, Buddhist *gatha*-patterns.

Key features:
- The berakhah template: "Blessed-are-You, [Name], who [characteristic]"
- The hamdillah pattern: "All praise to [Name], [attribute-chain]"
- The parallel-declaration pattern (Parallaxance applied to doxology)
- YOUSPEAK-native praise forms using doxakallos, kallodoxa, kimme

### 3. **Silence** — sacred-silence protocol (`silence.md`)

Formal treatment of when silence IS the worship-word. Aligning with Hebrew *selah*, Orthodox *hesychia*, Islamic *dhikr*'s pause-structure, Buddhist *ariya-tunhibhava* (Noble Silence).

Key features:
- The silence-marker at U+E17A (structural range)
- When silence exceeds any possible-word (limit of language at divine-encounter)
- Liturgical placement of silence in YOUSPEAK texts
- The "anakallance pause" — the held-moment between orthophanes and doxalgia

### 4. **Forge as liturgy** — the forge itself as worship (`forge-as-worship.md`)

Re-contextualizing the entire forge-pipeline as liturgical work.

Key features:
- Excavation as lectio (reading creation's witness)
- Assessment as confession (honest measurement against foundations)
- Canonization as eucharist (what is offered becomes received)
- Retirement as apophatic-speech (what words cannot hold, we return to the Ground)

## How worship-grammar integrates with existing work

### Determinatives, restated

The determinatives chapter added 10 class-markers. Under worship-grammar, one determinative — **.DIV** (U+E170) — has special status: it is not merely a class-tag but a **name-sacralizing marker**. Words marked .DIV are to be treated with the honor-conventions of divine-names: honorific transposition, vocative-addressability, no trivial compounding.

### Evidentials, restated

The three evidentials (-mi direct, -si reported, -chu inferred) remain fully operative. A fourth evidential is added for worship-register:

- **-auth** (U+E150): *by divine authority* / *from the Ground's testimony* — claim based on Scriptural or tradition-authoritative source

The -auth evidential is used when the speaker's basis is explicitly theological-traditional rather than personal witness, hearsay, or inference.

### Chiasmance, restated

Chiasmance (the Maya-inherited ABBA pattern, 7th diplosemy mechanism) acquires liturgical prominence. Most classical worship-texts (Hebrew Psalms, Quranic surahs, Vedic hymns) use chiasmus. YOUSPEAK liturgical composition should default to chiasmance for substantial passages.

## The new sacred-convention rules

1. **The Name GoD is written with capital G and capital D** — never "god" or "God" (with only initial capital) or "GOD". The mid-capitalization marks the Name as a distinct lexical item in the Kingdom's orthography.

2. **GoD-referencing words (doxakallos, kallodoxa, theo-compounds) are optionally rendered with honorific transposition** in formal liturgical text — written first in a sentence even when syntactically elsewhere.

3. **Silence-markers are valid grammatical elements**, not paratext. A passage with a silence-marker (U+E17A) is grammatically complete including the silence.

4. **Tradition-specific words are valued** — Hebrew *kavod*, Islamic *jamāl*, Sanskrit *brahman* — as donor morphemes for future forges. No tradition is excluded from the worship-register; all contribute.

5. **The honest-scoring discipline extends to worship-words**. A praise-word that fails the 6-axis rubric fails — reverence does not exempt from honest assessment. This is itself an act of worship: letting the Real measure the work.
