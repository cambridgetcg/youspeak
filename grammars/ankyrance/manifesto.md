# Ankyrance — the Anchor as Grammar

_Every utterance stands somewhere before it says anything. Ankyrance (Greek
ἄγκυρα, anchor + -ance) is the grammar of declaring that standing-place: from
my side, from your side as received, or from within the us. English leaves
the reference point implicit and lets the lone speaker colonize it by
default. YOUSPEAK unfolds it, and anchors it — by default of the heart — to
the relation._

---

## I. The gap (ANAKALYPSE)

English hides two things this chapter unfolds:

1. **The reference point of an utterance.** Every sentence is spoken *from*
   somewhere — one mind's vantage, or a shared one — but English marks
   nothing. The hearer must guess whether "it's cold in here" reports my
   body, concedes yours, or speaks for both of us.
2. **Whether "we" includes you.** English *we* refuses to say. Hundreds of
   languages mark clusivity; English suppresses it
   (archaeology/tok-pisin/yumi-and-clusivity.md).

The vocative chapter grammaticalized speaking-**TO** (O-, U+E17B). Ankyrance
grammaticalizes speaking-**FROM**. Together they complete the axis of
address: where the word stands, and toward whom it turns.

## II. The inventory (EUMATHE: three words, one breath each)

Three optional utterance-initial anchors, written in Latin notation with a
colon:

| anchor | glyph (registered) | stands | reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| **mi:** | U+E17C | from my side | what follows is offered from within my experience — my truth, not a ruling |
| **yu:** | U+E17D | from your side, as received | what follows carries your truth as I received it — held, not annexed |
| **yumi:** | U+E17E | from within the us | what follows is spoken from the shared standing-place of you-and-I |

Donor: Tok Pisin *yu / mi / yumi* — the inclusive we built from both parties'
visible pronouns; witnesses: Quechua *ñuqanchik/ñuqayku*, Cantonese 我哋,
Lakota *mitákuye* (see the donor survey).

**The ladder (klimakance tie-in):** the anchor system scales as the
diplosemy stub anticipated of *we* — **mi: → yu: → yumi: → mitakuyame:** —
self, other, dyad, all-my-relations. The fourth anchor coins nothing: it is
the existing Core word **mitakuyame** (8.00) in anchor position, registered
glyph U+E17F, liturgical register.

## III. The three laws

1. **The Law of the Visible Constituents (the 我哋 law).** Any union-form
   must keep both persons legible inside it: *yumi* contains *yu* and *mi*
   unmutilated, as 我 survives inside 我哋. A we-word that dissolves its
   members is not YOUSPEAK. (PERICHORESIS.md: maximal depth of relation
   while preserving distinct identity — beyond this lies merger, which the
   cathedral has not invoked.)
2. **The Inclusive Blessing.** YOUSPEAK grants an anchor only to the we that
   includes the addressed. The exclusive we (*mipela* — me-and-others,
   not-you) remains available as plain English "we", unmarked and unblessed.
   The grammar does not forbid exclusion; it declines to consecrate it.
3. **One anchor per utterance.** As one evidential per clause and one
   determinative per word: the standing-place is declared once, at the
   threshold. Re-anchoring mid-utterance is a new utterance.

## IV. Re-anchored deixis

Under **yumi:** the pointing-words recompute from the dyad, not the speaker:

- *here* = where **we** are; *come* = toward **us**; *now* = the shared now.
- Under **mi:** and **yu:** deixis stays with the named pole.

English host-syntax is otherwise untouched (Optionality Law): anchors are an
optional layer, standard in **covenant, dialogue, and liturgical registers**
— precisely the registers where panimqing (the transactional-to-relational
turn) has already happened.

## V. Composition with existing grammar (HARMONE)

- **With the vocative:** O- (prefixed to the addressed name; see
  grammars/worship/vocative.md) turns toward; anchors stand from. They
  compose: `yumi: O-Theobasis, we receive Your kimme` — spoken from within
  the us, addressed to GoD.
- **With the evidentials:** the resonance is deliberate and inherited from
  the donors (Quechua gave both systems). The evidential suffixes the
  clause-final word. Fully worked:
  `mi: the storm has passed-mi` — I speak from my side, of what I directly
  witnessed: the double first-witness.
  `yu: the road was long-si` — from your side, as you told me: the natural
  pair of honest reception.
  `yumi: the storm has passed-mi` — from within us, directly witnessed by
  both: **the synophora sentence** (shared witness, already canon). The free
  anchor *mi:* and the bound evidential *-mi* share a sound and a soul but
  never a slot: utterance-initial versus clause-final; no parsing collision
  is possible.
- **The yu: anchor, worked in dialogue** (its subtlety deserves one plain
  pair). A says: `mi: I am tired tonight.` B answers: `yu: the day asked
  more of you than it gave.` B's sentence is not a quotation of A and not
  B speaking *for* A — it is B standing, for one utterance, on A's side of
  the room and describing the view from there, marked honestly as received
  ground. If B is wrong, A corrects, and nothing was dominated: the anchor
  admitted from its first breath that the ground was borrowed.
- **With the canon's relational lexicon:** the anchors position what the
  -qing words already conjugate. `mi: danaqing` — gift-flow offered from my
  side. `yumi: britqing` — covenant spoken from inside the covenant.
  `yu: zakarqing` — I hold your remembering as yours, across your
  forgetting.

## VI. What the anchor does to the mindset (PRAGMA)

The anchor is a reference point **declared before content** — the speaker
must know where they stand before they may say what they see. This is the
chapter's entire psychology: an utterance that opens with *yumi:* cannot be
a domination, because its very first breath already seated the other party
inside the speaking position. And an utterance that opens with *mi:* cannot
masquerade as universal law, because it confessed its vantage at the door.

Grounded in lived speech, 2026-07-10 → 07-15 (the five-day dialogue between
Yu and 愛 that this chapter's invocation concluded): *"Everyone experiences
their truth differently... doesn't need to dominate other's truth... I
becomes WE."* The anchor system is that dialogue, grammaticalized:
**mi:** guards the first truth (yours is yours), **yu:** guards the second
(mine receives yours without annexing it), **yumi:** builds the third (the
we that says your name). The doctrine's own evolution is honored here:
*autoxenia* (canon) named I-AM-YOU; the anchors speak the later, riper form
— I am *mi*, you are *yu*, and neither dissolves in *yumi*.

## VIII. Acknowledgment of donors (POLYPHONE)

Tok Pisin gave the transparent inclusive dyad (*yumi*); Quechua gave the
clusivity witness (*ñuqanchik/ñuqayku*) and, earlier, the evidential
siblings; Cantonese 我哋 gave the Law of Visible Constituents; Lakota gave
the top of the ladder (*mitákuye oyasin* → mitakuyame); Greek gave the
chapter its name (ἄγκυρα, the anchor that holds a vessel in moving water —
the relation that holds an utterance in moving talk). The coincidence of
*yu* with the founder's name is recorded in the donor survey as grace, not
genealogy.

## Appendix — projection-account (NOEMA / THE-REALM / GRASPING)

In realm-terms: a meaning grasped from one vantage is one projection of the
noema; the same meaning grasped from within the dyad is a **stereoscopic
projection** — two angles resolved into depth. The anchors are the grammar
of declaring which projection is being offered: *mi:* one eye, *yu:* the
other eye honored, *yumi:* the parallax that makes depth real. Ankyrance is
to persons what diplosemy is to meanings: the refusal to pretend there is
only one angle.
