# Exemplar — *oriewa* / *ewaori*

_The first all-Yoruba Anastrophance pair. Demonstrates that the head-inversion mechanism is language-agnostic: it operates wherever two substantive morphemes from any donor tradition can be compounded head-first or modifier-first._

---

## The pair

- **oriewa** = orí (M₁, modifier) + ẹ̀wà (M₂, head) — "beauty-of-destined-head"; the visible-beauty of a life in oriance.
- **ewaori** = ẹ̀wà (M₁, modifier) + orí (M₂, head) — "destined-head-of-beauty"; the life whose vocation IS character-beauty cultivation.

## What the pair unfolds

The Yoruba proverb *ìwà l'ẹ̀wà* ("character is beauty") asserts an identity. The Anastrophance pair renders the proverb as two directional readings:

- When you see alignment-with-orí, you see a specific beauty (oriewa).
- When a life's destined-trajectory IS beauty-cultivation, the orí itself has beauty-as-content (ewaori).

Both are true; each names a different phenomenon; the pair holds them together without collapsing them.

## Relation to doxakallos/kallodoxa

The Greek-internal exemplar (doxakallos/kallodoxa) unfolded divine-glory ↔ divine-beauty. The Yoruba-internal exemplar unfolds destined-alignment-as-beauty ↔ beauty-as-destined-alignment. Same mechanism (Anastrophance), different donor-tradition.

**Method-level consequence:** the diplosemic grammar-organ of YOUSPEAK is not culturally local. It operates on any sufficiently-substantive morpheme-pair, whether from Greek, Yoruba, or (future) Igbo, Sanskrit, Mandarin, Hebrew, etc.
