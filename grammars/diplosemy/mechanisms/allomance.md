# Allomance — the alternate-parse mechanism

_The diplosemic operation of engineering a compound whose morpheme-boundary admits two or more legitimate parse-paths, each yielding a correlated-but-distinct meaning._

---

## The mechanism

A YOUSPEAK compound `X₁X₂X₃` where the character-stream can be parsed as either:

- `X₁X₂ + X₃` (binary compound with X₁X₂ as modifier, X₃ as head)
- `X₁ + X₂X₃` (binary compound with X₁ as modifier, X₂X₃ as head)

Each parse yields a distinct meaning. Both meanings are legitimate. Context may lean one way, but the written form carries both.

```
Parse A:  X₁X₂ | X₃       meaning A
Parse B:  X₁ | X₂X₃       meaning B

(X₁X₂X₃ as single sequence; parse-boundary is the diplosemic variable)
```

The reader who notices both parses holds both meanings. A sentence that is consistent under only one parse flags the intended reading; a sentence that is consistent under both preserves the duality.

## Engineering rules

1. **Both parse-paths must be morphologically valid.** Each sub-morpheme must exist in the donor language, and each possible head must support substantival use.
2. **Both parses must name real concepts in correlated domains.** If one parse is a real concept and the other is nonsense or an unrelated concept, the compound is not Allomance-diplosemic.
3. **The concepts must be in the same semantic field.** The two meanings should feel like siblings, not strangers.
4. **The forger specifies both parses explicitly in the coinage's documentation.** Allomance cannot be discovered-post-hoc; it is declared.

## Engineered exemplar — hagiochrony

Consider the compound **hagiochrony** (a hypothetical coinage).

**Parse A:** *hagio-chrony* (hagio- "sacred" + chronos "time")
- Meaning: the time-quality of the sacred; sacred-time as distinct from clock-time; the phenomenology of liturgical duration.
- Usage: "Liturgy exists in hagiochrony, not clock-time."

**Parse B:** *hagi-ochrony* (hagi- truncated form of hagio- + ōchros "pale/yellow")
- Meaning: sacred-paleness; the specific pale-gold visual quality of ikon-faces or relic-bone-in-reliquary; a visual register of the sacred.
- Usage: "Her face held hagiochrony in the candle-light — the sacred-paleness one sees in saints."

Both parses are morphologically valid. Both yield real concepts. Both belong to the sacred/liturgical conceptual field. A sentence like "Her expression was hagiochrony" could mean *she was in sacred-time* OR *she had the sacred-pale-quality*. The parse remains in superposition unless context collapses it.

A skilled use of Allomance places the compound in a sentence that supports both parses, preserving the duality.

## Use cases

### Encryption

Allomance is strong encryption because the parse-boundary is visually invisible. Two readers look at the same word; each parses differently; neither knows they are reading different things. Unlike Synaphemia (phonetic), Allomance works in writing.

### Jokes

An Allomance joke plays the two parses against each other. "She speaks with hagiochrony" — the sentence is ambiguous between "with sacred-time" (she speaks liturgically, slowly, weightily) and "with sacred-paleness" (her voice is pale, faint, ghostly). Both parses attached to *speaking* produce different characterizations; the humor is the tonal-contrast between them.

### Dual-delivery

A sentence engineered to be consistent under both parses delivers both meanings. The reader who notices the parse-ambiguity receives depth; the reader who parses only once receives one legitimate reading. Nothing is lost either way.

## Failure modes

- **Asymmetric validity** — one parse is obviously correct; the other is a stretched-parse that requires special pleading. Not Allomance; ordinary compound with a pun.
- **Cross-domain parses** — the two parses belong to unrelated domains. The diplosemic correlation fails; the compound is a bad pun, not a diplosemic coinage.
- **Parse-dominance by phonology** — English speakers' pattern-matching strongly prefers one parse over the other regardless of which is intended. The intended duality does not survive perception.

## The Allomance discipline

Allomance requires the forger to:

1. Draft the coinage with both parses in mind from the start.
2. Verify both parses yield real, correlated concepts.
3. Write trial-sentences that work under both parses.
4. Submit to readers who are not told about the duality; test whether both parses are recovered by different readers (or the same reader on different passes).

Without step 4, the forger cannot know whether the Allomance is real or self-projected.

## Correlation requirements

- **C1 structural-cohesion:** Both parses must be grammatically valid in the donor languages.
- **C2 depth-yield:** Holding both together must yield a third-meaning (the concept-family's more-complete picture).
- **C3 register-stability:** Both parses must read naturally in the coinage's intended usage-contexts.

## Future Allomance candidates

The mechanism is most productive when:

- The donor-languages' morpheme-boundaries are naturally ambiguous at certain phoneme-sequences (e.g., Greek compounds where -os, -a, -on endings can blur).
- The target-concept has natural internal-duality that can be expressed by choice-of-morpheme-boundary.

Possible candidates for future forging:

- **dokimance** itself admits a latent Allomance: *doki-mance* (testing-prediction, via a possible -mance sense) vs *dokim-ance* (the testing-event). Currently canonized under the second parse; the first parse is a latent candidate for future forge-work if the prediction-concept becomes load-bearing.
- **Anastrophance-Allomance chimeras** — a compound that admits both head-inversion AND alternate-parse. Engineering such a four-meaning-carrying coinage is a future forge-challenge.
