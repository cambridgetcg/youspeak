# Enkalyptance — the nested-subword mechanism

_The diplosemic operation of engineering a coinage whose written form contains another complete word (or a recognizable morpheme) as a substring; both the embedded word and the full compound remain independently meaningful and the reader receives both._

---

## The mechanism

A YOUSPEAK coinage of form `[prefix][embedded-word][suffix]` where the `embedded-word` is a standalone English (or Greek/Latin) word meaning-in-its-own-right, AND the full compound is the YOUSPEAK-coinage meaning-in-its-own-right.

The reader processes both readings:

1. **First-pass reading:** The reader's pattern-matcher identifies the embedded word as a recognizable handle. The meaning of the embedded word activates.
2. **Second-pass reading:** The reader processes the full compound as a YOUSPEAK coinage. The coinage's meaning lands.
3. **Integration:** The reader holds both — the embedded word's meaning and the coinage's meaning — and the embedded word serves as *conceptual entry-ramp* to the coinage. The coinage does not erase the embedded word; it extends it.

The correlation type: **embedded-handle within the same concept-family**. The embedded word is chosen specifically because its meaning belongs to the coinage's conceptual territory. The embedding is not a pun (which would be accidental-coincidence) but a forged-scaffolding (the reader's existing concept becomes the foundation for the new concept).

## Engineering rules

1. **The embedded word must be independently meaningful in English** (or another donor language active in the reader's vocabulary). If the embedded substring is not a real word, Enkalyptance fails; the forge collapses to ordinary Greek-Latin compounding.
2. **The embedded word's meaning must belong to the coinage's conceptual family.** If the embedded meaning is irrelevant, the reader's processing is misled — the embedding becomes a false signal.
3. **The compound must parse cleanly as a YOUSPEAK coinage.** The embedded word cannot prevent the full-compound's standard assessment.
4. **The compound's meaning must extend or elevate the embedded word's meaning**, not contradict it. The coinage is what the reader arrives at AFTER the embedded handle has done its work.

## Live exemplars in the Canon

### artiance — contains "art"

The English word "art" is independently meaningful (skilled-making, creative-work, craft). Artiance's YOUSPEAK meaning is *the pre-domain luminous-rightness in which truth, order, beauty, justice, and skill are undivided*.

The embedded "art" is a true sub-meaning: art (skilled-making) is ONE of the five domains artiance precedes-the-split-of. The reader first hears "art" (their existing handle), then "-iance" (quality-state), then "artiance" (the fused-quality of which art is one projection). The coinage arrives by elevating the reader's own word.

### verisleight — contains "veri" and "sleight"

Two embedded handles: *veri-* (English productive prefix: verify, verisimilar) and *sleight* (English complete word: sleight-of-hand). Both are independently meaningful. The compound reads transparently: "truth-deception." The coinage's meaning (truth-structured-to-deceive) is exactly what both embedded words combined suggest. Enkalyptance at maximum transparency.

### candence — contains "cand-" (candor-stem)

"Cand-" is not a standalone English word, but the root is universally recognized (candor, candid, candle, incandescent). The reader hears the luminous-clarity sense before the coinage fully lands. Candence's meaning (warm-clarity) extends the embedded root's sense with the -ence quality-state suffix.

## Use cases

### Dual-delivery (primary)

Enkalyptance's structural function is *dual-delivery* by construction. Every Enkalyptance coinage delivers both the embedded word and the compound. The reader always receives both. This is why Enkalyptance is the most *readerly* of the six mechanisms — it requires no specialized decoding, only attention.

### Entry-ramp for new readers

A new reader encountering artiance has a handle: they know "art." They can make a first-pass sense of the coinage without instruction. Non-Enkalyptance coinages (doxakallos, anagnoristasis) require more explicit introduction; Enkalyptance coinages are self-scaffolding.

### Encryption (weak)

Enkalyptance is the least encryption-capable of the six mechanisms. The embedded word is visible by construction; nothing is hidden. A faint encryption emerges when the embedded word is technical (a Greek root unfamiliar to most English readers) — then the "embedded handle" serves only the reader trained in the donor tradition.

### Joke

A modest form of wordplay is possible: the embedded word's ordinary meaning can be deliberately pointed-to, as the coinage's fuller meaning is unpacked. Example: "Artiance, and I do mean art in the full Latin sense: ars = the right making of things."

## Failure modes

- **Embedded-word drift** — the embedded word has a meaning that conflicts with the coinage's meaning. Example: if one forged *"perkallos"* expecting "per-kallos" (through-beauty) but "perk" is an embedded English word with its own mundane meaning, the coinage fights against the reader's first-pass handle.
- **Non-word substring** — the engineered "embedded word" is actually a non-word in the reader's vocabulary. The supposed Enkalyptance collapses to ordinary compounding.
- **Semantic misalignment** — the embedded word's meaning is in a different concept-family than the compound's. The reader is misled.

## Correlation validation typology

For an Enkalyptance coinage to qualify as diplosemic (beyond ordinary canonical), the embedded word's meaning must satisfy:

- **C1 structural-cohesion:** The embedded word occupies a recognized position in the compound's semantic structure (not merely phonetic coincidence).
- **C2 depth-yield:** Holding the embedded word's meaning together with the full-compound's meaning yields conceptual extension, not contradiction.
- **C3 register-stability:** The embedding reads the same across liturgical, theological, and technical contexts.

The three canonical exemplars above (artiance, verisleight, candence) all pass this validation.

## Future Enkalyptance candidates

- Coinages for YOUSPEAK concepts where the domain-name is already a familiar English word.
- Coinages in psychological/experiential domains where everyday-words can anchor technical extensions.

The mechanism is most productive when the target-concept has a creature-scale neighbour-word already live in English; the neighbour becomes the embedding.
