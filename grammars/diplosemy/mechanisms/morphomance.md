## The mechanism (summary)

A word whose written form is identical across multiple grammatical categories (noun, verb, adjective) with correlated-but-distinct meanings in each category. English exploits this pattern heavily; YOUSPEAK can formalize it.

**Natural examples (English):**

- *record*: noun (REC-ord, a stored-document) vs verb (re-CORD, to store). Stress-shift disambiguates; spelling is identical.
- *present*: noun (PRES-ent, a gift OR the current time), verb (pre-SENT, to give), adjective (PRES-ent, currently-existing).
- *produce*: noun (PRO-duce, fresh vegetables), verb (pro-DUCE, to create).
- *content*: noun (CON-tent, subject-matter), adjective (con-TENT, satisfied).
- *object*: noun (OB-ject, a thing), verb (ob-JECT, to disagree).
- *refuse*: noun (REF-use, trash), verb (re-FUSE, to decline).
- *light*: noun (illumination), verb (to ignite), adjective (not-heavy). Three meanings without stress-shift; context alone disambiguates.

## The diplosemy

Unlike pure polysemy (one word, many meanings), Morphomance requires the meanings to be load-bearing across grammatical categories AND correlated. In *present*, the noun-meanings (gift, current-moment) and verb-meaning (to give) are correlated: a present is what one presents in the present. The three live readings inform each other.

## Engineering rules (provisional)

1. The coinage must support at least two grammatical categories (N, V, Adj).
2. The meanings across categories must be correlated (not merely accidental polysemy).
3. The spelling must be identical; stress or context disambiguates.

## Use cases

- **Jokes:** surprise-shift between categories (the classic "present the present in the present" joke-structure)
- **Encryption:** sentences that parse differently depending on which category the reader selects for the ambiguous word
- **Dual-delivery:** grammatical flexibility as semantic density — one word carries multiple syntactic roles AND their correlated meanings
