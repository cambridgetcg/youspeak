# Chiasmance — the Cross-Inversion Mechanism

_The diplosemic operation of composing a passage in ABBA (or ABCBA, ABCDDCBA) pattern where the structural inversion at the center carries the depth-meaning. Extension of Parallaxance: where Parallaxance is paired-statements (AA'), Chiasmance is crossed-statements (ABBA)._

---

## The mechanism

A passage of four or more YOUSPEAK-canonical assertions structured so that:

- Line 1 (A) pairs with Line 4 (A')
- Line 2 (B) pairs with Line 3 (B')
- The outer pair (A/A') provides frame
- The inner pair (B/B') provides center
- The crossing — A encloses B, B mirrors B', A' closes to match A — IS the teaching

This is ABBA structure. Extended forms (ABCBA, ABCCBA) follow the same principle: the center-line is crossed-reflected on either side.

## Examples

### Example from Maya inscription (schematic)

```
A:  the eagle soared above the pyramid
B:  the pyramid raised the offering
B': the offering received the eagle's shadow
A': the shadow was the eagle returning
```

Outer pair (A/A'): eagle departing / eagle returning — the cycle
Inner pair (B/B'): pyramid-as-raiser / offering-as-receiver — the exchange

The meaning in the chiasmus: the act of offering completes the departure-and-return of the divine. Neither lines alone nor Parallaxance-pairs alone can carry this; the ABBA structure does.

### Example engineered from YOUSPEAK Canon

```
A:  doxakallos appears in the beholder
B:  orthophanes is produced visibly
B': orthophanes produces the beholder's doxalgia
A': doxalgia is doxakallos in the beholder
```

Outer pair (A/A'): doxakallos-appears-in-beholder / doxalgia-is-doxakallos-in-beholder — the same beholder encountering divine beauty from inside (subjective) and outside (objective) the ache
Inner pair (B/B'): orthophanes-produced-visibly / orthophanes-produces-doxalgia — the object-pole as both made-visible and making-aching

The chiasmance yields: the beholder is the site where doxakallos and doxalgia are one-thing, manifested through orthophanes's double-mode (received and effective).

## Engineering rules

1. **Minimum 4 lines** for ABBA; 5 for ABCBA; 6 for ABCCBA. Longer chiasms are possible (ABCDDCBA, etc.) but rarely sustain coherence past 8 lines.

2. **Strict position-wise correspondence**:
   - Subject of A ↔ Subject of A'
   - Predicate of B ↔ Predicate of B' (often inverted semantically)
   - The inversion is structural, not merely synonym-swap

3. **The center of the chiasmus (B/B' junction or the single middle-line in ABCBA) is the passage's weight-bearing pivot.** A Maya or Biblical reader would look there for the teaching.

4. **Each line must be grammatically valid YOUSPEAK-English on its own.** The chiasmus is an additional layer, not a replacement for clear-sentence-construction.

5. **Canonical chiasms use canonical YOUSPEAK words** at corresponding positions. Mixed YOUSPEAK/English is permissible but dilutes the mechanism.

## Use cases

### Liturgy / ritual

Chiasmance is Maya's and Biblical Hebrew's primary liturgical structure. A YOUSPEAK-liturgy passage can be composed as chiasmance to give it the canonical Maya-Hebrew weight. Example prayer-structure:

```
A:  doxakallos is sought
B:  kallophanes is waited-for
B': orthophanes is received
A': doxakallos is encountered
```

Four-line chiasmance tracing the full encounter-sequence.

### Teaching / demonstration

A teaching point rendered in chiasmance carries pedagogically: the student experiences the return-through-inversion as meaning-integration, not mere repetition.

### Memorization

Chiasmance-structured passages are easier to memorize than unstructured prose — the structural symmetry provides recall-scaffolding. Classical oral traditions exploited this; YOUSPEAK inherits.

### Proof / test of text integrity

If a chiasmus fails to close (A' doesn't reflect A, B' doesn't mirror B), the passage is corrupt or the reader is mis-parsing. Chiasmance provides a diagnostic: if the pattern holds, the passage is internally consistent.

## Failure modes

- **Forced parallelism** — the syntactic structure is superficially ABBA but semantic correspondence is absent. The structure becomes decoration.
- **Missing center** — the B/B' junction doesn't actually pivot; the passage is just two Parallaxance-pairs adjacent.
- **Overextension** — chiasm attempted at 8+ lines; coherence degrades; the pattern is noise.
- **Subject-object mismatch** — A's subject ≠ A''s subject in role (one is agent, the other is patient); correspondence broken.

## Correlation requirements (for a chiasmic-composition to qualify as diplosemic)

- **C1 structural-cohesion**: strict position-wise correspondence across A/A' and B/B'; center pivots visibly
- **C2 depth-yield**: the cross-inversion yields a meaning not present in either pair alone
- **C3 register-stability**: the chiasm holds across liturgical, teaching, and contemplative uses

A chiasmic-composition that passes all three is a diplosemic-exemplar under Chiasmance.

## Chiasmance vs. Parallaxance

| | Parallaxance | Chiasmance |
|---|---|---|
| Pattern | AA' | ABBA (or ABCBA, etc.) |
| Minimum lines | 2 | 4 |
| Inversion | no | yes (cross-pattern) |
| Center | none | single point (the B/B' or middle-line pivot) |
| Use | concise two-line teaching | extended liturgical / ritual / teaching passage |
| Depth-yield | through correspondence | through inversion-and-return |

Parallaxance is the 2-line form; Chiasmance is the 4+-line form with inversion at center. Both are legitimate; each serves different registers.
