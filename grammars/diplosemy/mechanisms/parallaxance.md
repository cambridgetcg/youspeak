# Parallaxance — the parallel-structure mechanism

_The diplosemic operation of placing two syntactically-parallel clauses (or sentences) such that corresponding positions each hold a YOUSPEAK word, and the position-wise correspondence creates a third meaning that neither clause alone carries._

---

## The mechanism

Classical Chinese parallel couplets (对联 duìlián) are the canonical historical instance: two lines of identical syntactic structure, with each character in line 2 corresponding positionally to a character in line 1. The two lines speak about nominally-different things; the parallelism creates a silent third-meaning that the reader discovers by attending to the correspondence.

YOUSPEAK adopts this mechanism with its canonical word-pairs in the parallel-position roles.

```
Clause 1:  [subj_1] [verb_1] [obj_1] [prep_1] [noun_1]
Clause 2:  [subj_2] [verb_2] [obj_2] [prep_2] [noun_2]

Diplosemic when:
  (subj_1, subj_2) is a YOUSPEAK pair
  (obj_1, obj_2)  is a YOUSPEAK pair
  (noun_1, noun_2) is a YOUSPEAK pair (or corresponds meaningfully)
  verb_1 ≈ verb_2 (or relates by explicit inversion)
  The correspondence yields a third-meaning beyond either clause alone.
```

## Canonical exemplar

The Liturgy's most natural Parallaxance sentence:

```
Orthophanes produces doxalgia   in the beholder.
Kallophanes produces doxakallos in the beholding.
```

Position-by-position correspondence:

| Position | Clause 1 | Clause 2 | Relation |
|---|---|---|---|
| subject | orthophanes | kallophanes | sibling compounds (phainō-family, Anastrophance-adjacent) |
| verb | produces | produces | identity (parallel-axis) |
| object | doxalgia | doxakallos | canon pair (doxa-family, Anastrophance-adjacent) |
| prep-noun | in the beholder | in the beholding | locus-shift (subject-locus → event-locus) |

The two clauses are not saying the same thing. Clause 1 is the creature-scale encounter (orthophanes of created thing → doxalgia in the beholder). Clause 2 is the divine-scale encounter (kallophanes as divine-beauty-appearing → doxakallos as the quality that is the ontological source of what Clause 1 describes).

The third-meaning that emerges from the parallelism: *the creature-scale encounter participates in the divine-scale encounter; what is in the beholder (doxalgia) in Clause 1 is what is in the beholding-itself (doxakallos) in Clause 2*.

This unity is not asserted explicitly. It is demonstrated by the parallelism. A reader who holds both clauses holds the Liturgy's full architecture.

## Engineering rules

1. **The syntactic parallelism must be strict.** Each position in Clause 1 must have a correspondent in Clause 2 in the same grammatical role.
2. **The YOUSPEAK pairs must occupy the same syntactic positions.** If doxakallos is subject in Clause 1, its pair (kallodoxa or doxalgia) must be subject in Clause 2.
3. **The verb-axis must be honest.** The verbs are either identical (emphasizing parallel action) or explicitly inverted (emphasizing complementarity). Hidden verb-variation corrupts the parallel.
4. **The third-meaning must be retrievable.** A reader attending carefully must be able to articulate what the parallelism adds. If no third-meaning is specifiable, the Parallaxance is a stylistic flourish, not a diplosemic structure.

## Use cases

### Dual-delivery (primary)

Parallaxance is the most *pedagogically-generous* mechanism. Both readings (each clause) are fully available to any reader; the third-meaning is available to the reader who attends. Surface-reading loses nothing; depth-reading gains the unity.

### Teaching / theology / liturgy

Parallaxance is the Liturgy's preferred sentence-level mechanism. A liturgy-text can carry its full architecture in parallel-couplet form while remaining accessible as simple declarative sentence at surface.

### Encryption (moderate)

The third-meaning is retrievable only by readers who recognize the YOUSPEAK pairs AND notice the parallel-structure. To the uninitiated, the sentence reads as two ordinary statements with incidental similarity; to the initiated, the structure is the message.

### Joke

A Parallaxance joke plays with the PARTIAL-correspondence — two clauses that appear parallel but diverge in one specific position, and the divergence is the punchline. E.g.:

```
Orthophanes produces doxalgia.
Verisleight produces verisleight.
```

The first clause is standard Liturgy; the second's self-referential structure (verisleight producing itself) is the joke — a comment on the self-sustaining nature of truth-that-deceives.

## Failure modes

- **Pseudo-parallelism** — the syntactic structure is superficially parallel but semantically diverging in ways that don't yield a third-meaning. The structure is noise, not signal.
- **Position-mismatching** — corresponding positions hold words from unrelated YOUSPEAK families. The parallel creates confusion rather than depth.
- **Over-engineering** — forced parallelism that the grammar cannot support. The sentence becomes stilted; the mechanism fails the register-stability test.

## Correlation requirements

- **C1 structural-cohesion:** The syntactic correspondence must be strict and the YOUSPEAK-pair-placements must be in corresponding positions.
- **C2 depth-yield:** The third-meaning must be specifiable and valuable (not merely a repetition of what either clause says).
- **C3 register-stability:** The parallel must work across spoken, written, and contemplative uses of the sentence.

## Future Parallaxance constructions

The mechanism is productive for any Liturgy-text. Recommended structural patterns:

1. **Creature-scale / divine-scale:** As in the canonical exemplar above — one clause about the created encounter, the parallel clause about the divine ground.
2. **Act-pole / state-pole:** One clause about the event (orthophanes, anagnoristasis, doxalgia); the parallel about the resulting state (athaumasma, metastrophesis).
3. **Subject-pole / object-pole:** One clause foregrounding the beholder (doxalgia); the parallel foregrounding the beheld (doxakallos, orthophanes).
4. **Dokimance / verisleight opposition:** Clause 1 about the testing-that-makes-real; the parallel about the truth-that-deceives; the third-meaning is the chain's dual constitution as both reality-producing and deception-testing.
