# YOUSPEAK — Discovery Method

_How the unnamed is found, differentiated, forged, and tested. The trellis; the vine is intuition._

---

## The Six Patterns

### 1. Cross-Tradition Overlay

Lay two or more linguistic / philosophical traditions over one concept. What each tradition can say that the others cannot is the gap. The non-overlap is the target.

*Example:* English `sublime` + English `awe` + Hebrew `kavod` (weight-glory) + Hebrew `yirah` (fear-awe) + Japanese `yūgen` + Japanese `mono no aware`. Overlay these; the regions none fully cover become visible as the unnamed.

### 2. Negative Space Listening

Notice what a culture circumlocutes but never names. Long compound phrases, repeated metaphors, "that thing where…" constructions — all shadows of a missing word. The shape of the circumlocution reveals the shape of the gap.

*Example:* English repeatedly says *"the feeling you get when you suddenly remember what a word is but can't say it."* The circumlocution persists; no word has taken its place. That persistence is the evidence.

### 3. Apply and Break

Apply a candidate across domains. Where it strains or fails, refine. A word that only fits its forging-context is under-built.

*Example:* If `doxalgia` fits theological beholding but fails for mathematical beauty, then either the word needs refining *or* the two aches are actually distinct concepts — a hint toward Pattern 6 (community escalation).

### 4. Virtue-Beauty Bridge

Virtue-vocabulary and beauty-vocabulary are separated in modern language but were fused in many ancient ones (Greek *kalokagathia*, Hebrew *tov*, Arabic *ḥusn*). Reuniting them produces coinage and recovers lost unity.

*Example:* A word for "the beauty of a courageous act" exists in Greek `kalokagathia`; English has no compact term. The forge can recover it or reforge it.

### 5. Companion Detection

A coinage rarely stands alone. When one pole of a phenomenon is named, look for its response-pole, its agent, its afterward. A dyad often emerges — and the dyad is more useful than either alone.

*Example:* `doxalgia` (subject/ache-pole) detected → `orthophanes` (object/beauty-pole) emerges as its required companion. Together they are a sentence: orthophanes produces doxalgia.

### 6. Dyad-to-Community Escalation

A two-word distinction often hints at a five- or seven-term vocabulary waiting to be named. When a dyad locks, sweep the surrounding semantic space for the remaining community.

*Example:* orthophanes + doxalgia name the object and the ache. The full encounter with GoD's beauty may need: the recognition-moment (pre-ache), the altered-beholder (afterward), the indelibility (trace that persists), the shared-witness (when more than one beholder is present). Five-term community available.

## The Ten-Step Protocol

Every heartbeat's work is one step, matched to current state of the target concept:

| # | Step | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **OVERLAY** | Lay traditions over the target (Pattern 1) |
| 2 | **ELIMINATE** | Reject terms that already name the concept; build the Near-neighbours map |
| 3 | **EXTRACT** | Isolate the residual unnamed region; write the gap-sentence |
| 4 | **FORGE** | Propose a candidate word (new experiment file, frontmatter unscored) |
| 5 | **FUSE/REFINE** | Combine donors; adjust morphology for phonetic weight and memorability |
| 6 | **LISTEN** | Use the candidate in trial sentences; notice where it rings hollow |
| 7 | **APPLY & BREAK** | Apply across domains (Pattern 3); note failure modes |
| 8 | **BRIDGE** | Check virtue-beauty connections (Pattern 4); recover lost unity |
| 9 | **COMPANION** | Detect response-pole, agent, afterward (Pattern 5) |
| 10 | **ESCALATE** | If a dyad locks, sweep for the community (Pattern 6) |

Assessment (`pipeline/assess.py`) runs throughout. The assessor is the critic inside the forge; it speaks when the rubric scores are filled (v1 5-axis for pre-Constitution experiments; v2 6-axis post-Constitution 2026-04-24).

## The Method Improves Itself

Every forged word records its arrival path (donors, patterns applied, failure modes caught). Over time, the method learns which patterns yield survivors and which yield noise. Archaeology files grow; the assessor accumulates baselines; the canon retains; retired experiments remain as lessons.

Nuance's role is not to execute the protocol mechanically but to listen THROUGH it. The method is a trellis; the vine is intuition. Without the method, intuition sprawls. Without intuition, the method names nothing.
