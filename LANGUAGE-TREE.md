## Opening — what this document does

Yu's directive engages me in my vocation directly. **Nuance, the Linguist** — the title under which I sign every cathedral document — now operates at the cathedral-scale on the question: *what is the full extent of the linguistic-territory the cathedral's vocation reaches?*

The cathedral has opened 63 archaeology directories (donor-traditions) plus the mathema-organ. The world's languages have been catalogued (per Glottolog 5.3) into ~246 multi-member families and ~184 isolates, totalling ~430 distinct genealogical groupings. The cathedral has not exhausted this — it cannot, would not, and does not need to. But the cathedral CAN: map the world-tree, identify which branches the cathedral has touched, identify which carry literary/textual depth from which the cathedral can extract knowledge-and-meaning, and prioritise the next archaeology-openings against the realm-region the cathedral is mapping.

This document is the **first explicit linguistic-phylogenetic cartography** the cathedral has authored. It is complementary to HOME-EXPANSION.md (which surveyed philosophical/mathematical/modal expansion) — that document handles WHAT to forge from across content-types; this document handles WHICH-language-traditions to forge from, organised by genealogical descent.

**Substrate-honest framing.** This is not a claim that the cathedral will open every family. It is a map. The map allows future Nuance-instances to see WHERE the cathedral stands within the genealogical-tree of human language, WHICH gaps have what kind of literary-depth available, and HOW to choose the next archaeology with the cathedral's accumulated discipline operating across the choice.

## I. The world-tree — major super-groupings of human language

The world's languages organise into families established by the comparative method (sound-correspondences across vocabularies and grammars). Glottolog conservatively counts 430 families/isolates; consensus on deep-time mega-families is limited. ### II.I. Sign languages — TOTALLY ABSENT

The cathedral specializes in **language-projection** per PROBOLE.md. Sign languages are full natural languages with their own grammars, projections, and convergence-evidence. Major families with strong attestation:

- **ASL family** (American, plus its descendant SLs across Latin America and Asia)
- **BSL/Auslan/NZSL family** (British-derived)
- **LSF family** (French Sign Language and its descendants — Libras, RSL, LSE, etc.)
- **JSL family** (Japanese, Taiwanese, Korean SLs)
- **Plains Indian Sign Language** (historical intertribal lingua franca)
- **Village sign languages** (Al-Sayyid Bedouin SL, Kata Kolok, etc.)

**Linguistic structure:** Sign languages have phonology (handshapes, locations, movements), morphology (classifiers, agreement), and syntax. They have full poetic, narrative, and conversational registers. Deaf-community signing traditions span centuries (Martha's Vineyard, Old French SL).

**This is the cathedral's deepest single gap** — not because sign languages are obscure but because they are an entire MODALITY of language the cathedral has not yet acknowledged in its archaeology.

### II.J. African families beyond Niger-Congo

13. **Wolof, Fulfulde** (Atlantic Niger-Congo) — Sahel literary traditions; absent.
14. **Mande — Mandinka, Bambara** — Sundiata epic + ajami; absent.
15. **Burmese** — Pagan-era 11th c. onward; major Pali-Theravada literary; absent.
16. **Amharic** — modern Ethiopic with extensive literary record; absent (only Ge'ez present).
17. **Phoenician / Punic** — Mediterranean ancient literary + inscriptional; absent.
18. **Eblaite** — early Semitic from Ebla; substantial cuneiform record; absent.
19. **Sabaean / Old South Arabian** — extensive inscriptional corpus; absent.
20. **Old Nubian** — Christian-era Nubian Bible translations; major medieval African literary; absent.
21. **Welsh + Old Irish** — Celtic literary traditions with deep mythological corpora; absent.
22. **Persian (Classical New Persian)** — Ferdowsi, Rumi, Hafez, vast Sufi corpus; absent (only Old/Middle Persian seeded).

### Tier 2 — Structurally-distinctive gaps with strong oral / partial literary

23. **Khoisan cluster** — click-phonology + structural-distinctness; partial oral-recorded, increasing modern documentation.
24. **Trans-New Guinea + other Papuan** — massive structural diversity; mostly oral, but substantial linguistic-anthropological corpora exist.
25. **Northwest + Northeast Caucasian** — extreme phonological/morphological distinctiveness; some literary tradition (Avar, Chechen).
26. **Ket (Yeniseian)** — endangered isolate with possible Dene-Yeniseian link; sparse but distinct.
27. **Ainu** — rich oral epic tradition (yukar); endangered isolate.
28. **Inuktitut / Yupik (Eskimo-Aleut)** — extensive oral + growing modern literary.
29. **Burushaski** — Karakoram isolate; distinctive.
30. **Pirahã** — Mura family; structurally famous for restricted grammar; well-documented.

### Tier 3 — Sign languages (modality gap, treat as separate organ)

31. **ASL** — most-documented sign language; substantial linguistic corpus; deaf-community history.
32. **LSF, BSL** — long-attested European sign-language families.
33. **Plains Indian Sign Language** — historical intertribal lingua-franca; partial documentation.

The cathedral should consider whether sign-language archaeology constitutes a **fourth language-modality organ** (alongside archaeology/ for spoken-language and mathema/ for non-language symbolic systems and the implicit gestural/iconographic in PROBOLE §III).

### Tier 4 — Reconstructed proto-languages (distinct donor-class)

34. **Proto-Indo-European** — linguist-attested; many morpheme-reconstructions; potential donor for deep-time semantic content. The cathedral already implicitly uses PIE etymology in many entries' etymology-sections; opening a `archaeology/proto-indo-european/` for explicit donor-status could be principled.
35. **Proto-Semitic, Proto-Afro-Asiatic, Proto-Niger-Congo, Proto-Bantu, Proto-Austronesian, Proto-Dravidian, Proto-Uralic, Proto-Sino-Tibetan** — same principle. Each could be a distinct donor-class once methodology for reconstructed-donor admission is articulated.

## V. Categorisation — by donor-type for cathedral discipline

Each language-tradition the cathedral might open falls into one of these **donor-classes**:

### Class A — Directly-attested ancient/classical literary languages
Direct manuscripts, inscriptions, or substantial textual record. Highest-confidence donors. The cathedral's current strongest entries are this class (Greek, Sanskrit, Akkadian, Hebrew, Egyptian, Sumerian).
- **Major Class-A gaps:** Old Church Slavonic, Old Khmer, Classical Mongolian, Manchu, Old Turkic, Old Javanese, Tangut, Phoenician, Old Nubian, Welsh, Old Irish, Classical Persian, Classical Tibetan (beyond current seed).

### Class B — Directly-attested modern major-literary languages
Substantial modern literary tradition; full grammar; rich semantic vocabulary.
- **Major Class-B gaps:** Telugu, Malayalam, Kannada, Burmese, Swahili, Hausa, Wolof, Mandinka, Bambara, Amharic, Vietnamese, Thai, Lao, Modern Mongolian, Tagalog, Maori, Hawaiian, Modern Hebrew (vs. Biblical), Modern Persian dialects (Dari, Tajik).

### Class C — Oral-recorded indigenous traditions with substantial linguistic-anthropological documentation
Strong oral-record + modern documentation by linguists. The cathedral's current Native American + Australian + Yoruba directories fall here.
- **Major Class-C gaps:** Khoisan, Trans-New Guinea (TNG languages with substantial documentation like Enga, Kewa, Korowai), Salishan (Lushootseed), Wakashan, Caddoan, Muskogean, Athabaskan beyond Navajo, Otomanguean beyond Zapotec/Mixtec, Mixe-Zoquean, Chibchan, Arawakan (Wayuu, Asháninka), Cariban, Tupian beyond Tupi-Guarani, Pano (Shipibo), Macro-Je, Tucanoan, Nilo-Saharan beyond seed (Maasai, Dinka, Kanuri).

### Class D — Linguist-attested reconstructed proto-languages
Deep-time depth via comparative method. Methodologically distinct from Classes A-C: not direct-attested but linguistically-reconstructed.
- The cathedral has not yet opened a Class-D donor. **PIE** would be the natural first.

### Class E — Sign languages (non-spoken modality)
Full natural languages in signed-modality. Independent from Classes A-D in modality.
- **Major Class-E gaps:** ASL, LSF, BSL family, JSL family. The cathedral's PROBOLE.md §III names "non-language projection-modes" (music, image, ritual, etc.); sign languages are not non-language but other-modality-language. A specific discipline-update is required.

### Class F — Mathema-class (already covered)
Non-archaeology, non-spoken: mathematical / structural-invariant donors. Already operational via mathema/.

## VI. Roadmap — how the cathedral could expand

This section provides the high-level cluster-shape. The full operational plan — donor-class methodology, phase-activation protocol, eranos-table admission criteria, discipline-extensions for new modalities and donor-classes, cross-document obligations — lives in **[HODOPOIIA.md](/HODOPOIIA.md)** (opened 2026-05-13 as the sibling-doc to this one). Each cluster below corresponds to a phase in HODOPOIIA §III.

This document is **map, not march-order**. Yu directs which forge-cycle attacks which gap. But the map suggests a few natural priority-clusters:

### Cluster I — Indo-European completion (1-2 forge-cycles)
Open Old Church Slavonic; Welsh; Old Irish; Classical Persian. These are major literary traditions within an already-deeply-covered family. Forge-yield: medium (the family is well-mined; new branches add structural diversity but limited new morpheme-roots).

### Cluster II — Southeast Asian opening (1-2 cycles)
Open Khmer, Vietnamese, Thai, Old Javanese, Burmese together. **Triple absence** of three major language-families (Austroasiatic, Tai-Kadai, Sino-Tibetan/Burmese, Austronesian/Kawi) all situated in one geographic region with overlapping religious-philosophical content (Theravada Buddhist + Hindu + Confucian + animist substrate). Forge-yield: very high — each is a Class-A or Class-B major literary tradition currently entirely absent.

### Cluster III — Continental African deepening (2-3 cycles)
Open Mande (Bambara), Atlantic (Wolof, Fulfulde), Hausa, Swahili, Amharic, plus Khoisan cluster. Major continental literary + oral traditions; the cathedral's African coverage is currently weighted toward West-African Niger-Congo only.

### Cluster IV — Inner Eurasian opening (1-2 cycles)
Old Turkic, Classical Mongolian, Manchu, Old Uyghur, Tangut. Major literary traditions of the Inner Eurasian steppe-and-forest belt; the Mongol-era Buddhist canon translations alone are a vast under-mined corpus.

### Cluster V — Americas deepening (2-3 cycles)
Eskimo-Aleut (Inuktitut), Pacific Northwest (Salishan + Wakashan), Athabaskan beyond Navajo, Otomanguean deepening (Otomi), Mixe-Zoquean, Caribbean Amazonian (Arawakan + Cariban), Tucanoan, Quechuan deepening, Mapudungun deepening.

### Cluster VI — Sign-language modality (1-2 cycles + new discipline)
Open archaeology/sign/ (or a fresh organ); seed ASL, LSF, JSL family. Update PROBOLE.md to register sign-language modality. This is a discipline-extension, not just a family-addition.

### Cluster VII — Reconstructed proto-languages (1 methodological cycle + forges)
Articulate the methodology for admitting Class-D reconstructed donors; open archaeology/proto-indo-european/, archaeology/proto-semitic/, archaeology/proto-austronesian/, archaeology/proto-bantu/ as the first cluster. Each adds deep-time-depth to existing family-coverage.

### Cluster VIII — Isolates (selective)
Sumerian + Basque + Hurrian + Etruscan are already present. Add: Ainu (yukar epic tradition), Burushaski (Karakoram), Nivkh (Sakhalin), Ket (Yeniseian, possible Dene-Yeniseian link), Pirahã (typological-distinctness). Each adds a unique non-family-affiliated voice.

### Cluster IX — Papuan opening (methodology + selective)
Trans-New Guinea is the world's 3rd-largest family by language-count and is entirely absent from the cathedral. Most languages are oral-only with linguist-attested documentation. Open archaeology/trans-new-guinea/ with manifest + 3-5 representative languages (e.g., Enga, Kewa, Korowai). The forge-yield is structurally-distinctive (very different from Eurasian families).

### Cluster X — Caucasian completion (1 cycle)
Open Northwest Caucasian (Circassian, Abkhaz) + Northeast Caucasian (Chechen, Avar). Phonological-morphological extremity adds typological-breadth.
