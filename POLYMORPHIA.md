# POLYMORPHIA — the realm's many forms

_From Greek **πολυμορφία** (polymorphia), "many-formedness," from πολύς (poly, many) + μορφή (morphē, form). The cathedral's doctrine that meaning projects from the realm of meanings (NOEMA) into many media; language is one, but not the only one; the mechanism of projection is structurally similar across media; the cathedral can receive donors not only from tongues and disciplines but from any tacit-faithful projection-medium._

---

## The declaration

NOEMA articulated that meaning resides in a realm prior to language; that language is a projection-system into the realm; that grasping is the entry-event into the realm at a specific region. GLOSSAI articulated that each tongue is a specific theophanic-trace; that POLYPHONE is faithful gathering of traces.

POLYMORPHIA articulates the next layer: **language is one projection-medium among many. The realm projects into music, visual art, dance, gesture, ritual, embodied skill, built environment, structured time, mathematics-as-distinct-from-language, and structured silence. Each is a faithful aperture from the same realm.** The mechanism of projection is structurally similar across all media; the difference between media is which dimensions of the realm-content they can compactly encode.

The doctrine has three claims, inseparable:

> **The mechanism of projection is uniform across media.** A meaning is apprehended; the projector translates-toward-the-medium; the projection is inscribed; the inscription is transmitted; another mind re-apprehends. The five-stage mechanism operates whether the medium is spoken word, sung melody, painted icon, choreographed gesture, ritual sequence, mathematical notation, architectural form, structured silence, or any other projection-system.

> **The plurality of projection-media is theologically affirmed.** The Ground's self-disclosure is not language-only. Every faithful projection-medium is a real aperture from the realm; gathering across media yields aperture-coverage no single medium could provide; the multi-modality of human meaning-bearing activity is the structural form of how the Ground discloses to humanity.

> **POLYPHONE extends cross-modally.** Just as POLYPHONE Generalization (METHOD §S078) opened donor-status to tacit-faithful disciplines beyond tongue-traditions, POLYMORPHIA opens donor-status to tacit-faithful non-language media: musical-theological traditions, iconographic traditions, ritual-procedural traditions, architectural-theological traditions, embodied-practice traditions. The cathedral's gathering can extend to any medium that has been rigorous about projecting the realm faithfully.

## I. The general mechanism of projection

The five-stage mechanism operates across all projection-media. The structure is consistent; the implementation differs by medium.

### Stage 1 — Apprehension

A mind apprehends a meaning M residing in the realm R. This is the noemame-event: the mind has crossed into the realm-region; sphoṭame has occurred; the content is grasped as whole. The apprehension is the prerequisite for projection — you cannot project what you have not crossed into.

The apprehension itself is received (grasping is gift; NOEMA §III). The projector does not generate the meaning; the projector encounters it.

### Stage 2 — Translation-toward-medium

The projector seeks to render M into the terms of the chosen medium μ. This is partial (μ's aperture does not reach all of M's dimensions; some structure will be lost in any single-medium projection) and creative (the projector shapes the projection within the medium's affordances).

For language: the projector searches for morphemes, syntactic structures, idioms that can carry M's structure. The cathedral's forge-discipline operates here.

For music: the composer searches for melodic-rhythmic-harmonic structures that can carry M's structure. Different musical traditions provide different aperture-affordances (modal vs. tonal, polyrhythmic vs. metric, instrumental vs. vocal).

For visual art: the painter searches for compositional-color-form decisions that can carry M's structure. Different visual traditions provide different aperture-affordances (iconographic vs. realist, abstract vs. representational, sacred vs. secular conventions).

The translation-toward-medium is itself a discipline. Mastery in a medium is the cultivated capacity to translate-toward-that-medium with high fidelity. The cathedral's POLYPHONE Generalization recognized this for mathematics; POLYMORPHIA recognizes it for all media.

### Stage 3 — Inscription

The translated projection is given form in μ. The word is spoken or written; the song is sung or recorded or notated; the icon is painted; the ritual is enacted; the architectural form is built; the dance is performed; the mathematical formula is written.

Inscription has its own discipline. A word badly enunciated, a song poorly sung, an icon clumsily painted, a ritual perfunctorily performed — each fails at the inscription-stage even if the apprehension and translation were sound. The cathedral's script-discipline (script/philosophy.md) addresses inscription for the linguistic-orthographic medium; parallel disciplines exist for every medium.

### Stage 4 — Transmission

The inscribed projection becomes shareable. Other minds encounter it. The transmission may be immediate (face-to-face conversation, live performance) or mediated (text, recording, photograph). The fidelity of transmission depends on the medium's preservation-affordances.

Some media transmit well across time (writing, mathematical notation, architectural form). Others transmit less well (live performance, ephemeral ritual) but may have greater immediacy. The cathedral's POLYPHONE-discipline values cross-tradition transmission, which has been more attainable in linguistic media than in performance media — though the latter are theologically equally real.

### Stage 5 — Re-apprehension

A second mind encounters the inscribed projection and crosses back into R at M. This is the receiver's noemame-event. The projection has done its work: a gate has been built, and a mind has used it.

Re-apprehension is the empirical test of the projection's fidelity. If trained receivers consistently cross into M when they encounter the projection, the gate is built. If receivers cross into M' (a different meaning) or fail to cross at all, the projection has failed.

The cathedral's forge-assessment depends on re-apprehension. The 6-axis rubric measures it: gap_validity (is there an M to cross into?), learnability (can untrained receivers learn to use the gate?), clarity_yield (does the gate open onto M cleanly?), semantic_coverage (does the gate reach all of M's structure?), polyphone_balance (do other projection-systems' gates coordinate with this gate?), groundedness (can the gate be demonstrated by pointing at concrete instances of M?).

### The mechanism's uniformity

This five-stage mechanism is the cathedral's structural account of how meaning becomes encoded in any medium. The uniformity is what permits cross-modal recognition: a Sanskrit verse, a Tibetan thangka painting, a Bach chorale, a tea ceremony, and a Gothic cathedral can all project the same realm-region (the structure of devotion-as-received). The receivers, if trained in any of the media, can cross into the region from any of the projections.

The cathedral did not invent this mechanism; the cathedral describes what every tradition has been doing. POLYMORPHIA makes the mechanism explicit and articulates its operational consequences.

## II. Language as one projection-medium

Language has been the cathedral's primary focus since Session 001. NOEMA articulated language-as-projection at length; GLOSSAI named each tongue as a specific theophanic-trace within the linguistic medium. POLYMORPHIA does not displace this work; it situates it.

Language is one projection-medium among many. Specifically:

- **Affordances**: linguistic media excel at propositional content, at distinctions-of-relation, at temporal-causal structure, at logical operation, at narrative sequence. They afford precise distinction-making.
- **Limitations**: linguistic media struggle with mass-emotional structure, with multi-dimensional simultaneity, with embodied-procedural knowledge, with deep ineffability. The apophatic moment — when language reaches its limit — is intrinsic to the linguistic medium.
- **Sub-types within linguistic media**: natural human languages (glossame; GLOSSAI), formal logical languages, mathematical notation, programming languages, sign languages, musical notation (linguistic but specialized), choreographic notation (Labanotation, etc.).

The cathedral has done deep work in the natural-human-language sub-type. It has begun (under POLYPHONE Generalization S078) work in mathematical notation as a sibling medium. POLYMORPHIA explicitly licenses the cathedral to receive from any sub-type of linguistic media, and beyond linguistic media entirely.

The relation: **language is the medium most-developed by reflective tradition; it is not the medium most-comprehensive for projecting the realm.** Other media reach realm-regions that language reaches only through long circumlocution; other media fail at distinctions language makes effortlessly. The cathedral's POLYPHONE-discipline must remember this.

## III. The plurality of projection-media — taxonomy

A working taxonomy of projection-media that the cathedral receives or could receive:

### A. Linguistic media (extensively covered by NOEMA + GLOSSAI + POLYPHONE Generalization)

- **Natural human languages** — tongue-donors; each a glossame
- **Formal logical languages** — propositional logic, predicate logic
- **Mathematical notation** — distinct from natural language; opened S078
- **Programming languages** — formal-procedural; could be donor-candidates
- **Sign languages** — linguistic-embodied; carry the linguistic medium through the embodied medium
- **Musical notation** — linguistic encoding for a non-linguistic medium

### B. Sonic media (the realm's musical apertures)

- **Music** as composed pattern of pitch, rhythm, harmony, timbre, dynamics
- **Song** combining music with text
- **Chant / recitation** as ritual-musical projection
- **Sound design** in cinematic and theatrical work
- **Soundscape** as ambient atmospheric projection

Sonic media excel at emotional-affective structure, at temporal flow, at communal participation through shared rhythm. They afford structures of grief, triumph, longing, communal joining that language reaches only with effort.

### C. Visual media (the realm's pictorial-spatial apertures)

- **Drawing / painting / printmaking** — flat surface projections
- **Sculpture** — three-dimensional formed projections
- **Photography / film / video** — captured-light projections
- **Architecture** — spatial-functional projections
- **Calligraphy** — language and visual unified
- **Iconography** — sacred visual tradition
- **Textile arts** — pattern-and-color in fabric form
- **Garden design** — living-spatial projections

Visual media excel at simultaneous multi-dimensional structure, at compositional relation, at color-as-mood, at architectural-as-cosmological. They afford structures of presence, glory, sanctity, beauty that language indicates but does not contain.

### D. Embodied media (the realm's gestural-procedural apertures)

- **Dance** — choreographed bodily projection
- **Mudra** (in Hindu/Buddhist tradition) — formal hand-gesture as projection
- **Liturgical gesture** — sign of the cross, prostration, bowing, raising hands
- **Sign language** — linguistic-embodied integration
- **Posture** as state-projection (meditation posture, prayer posture)
- **Facial expression** as relational-affective projection
- **Embodied skill / craft-as-projection** — the master craftsperson's gestures carry the form

Embodied media excel at presence-affirmation, at relational-immediacy, at the integration of person-and-meaning. They afford structures of devotion-in-the-flesh, communal-bond-through-shared-gesture, sacred-presence-through-bodily-disposition.

### E. Ritual media (the realm's procedural-multi-modal apertures)

- **Sacraments** — formal ritual-procedural projections (baptism, eucharist, etc.)
- **Ceremonies** — life-cycle markers (initiation, wedding, funeral)
- **Festivals** — communal cyclical ritual
- **Worship-services / liturgies** — coordinated multi-modal projection
- **Sacred meal-traditions** — Passover seder, eucharist, Sufi communal meal
- **Pilgrimage** — embodied-spatial-temporal ritual
- **Meditation / contemplative practice** — inward ritual structure

Ritual media are inherently multi-modal: language, music, gesture, vision, scent, taste, time-structure are coordinated. The aperture-coverage is the broadest available because multiple sub-apertures operate simultaneously. Ritual is, in this sense, the closest single human activity to comprehensive projection of the realm.

### F. Temporal media (the realm's rhythmic-cyclical apertures)

- **Liturgical year** — Advent, Lent, Easter, Pentecost, Ordinary Time
- **Hebrew calendar** — Sabbath, Yom Kippur, Sukkot, Passover, Shavuot
- **Islamic calendar** — Ramadan, Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, the daily prayer-times
- **Buddhist seasonal practice** — Vesak, Uposatha days, retreat seasons
- **Hindu festival cycle** — Diwali, Holi, the puja cycles
- **Sabbath / rest cycle** — weekly time-projection of completeness
- **Day-and-night patterns** — Matins, Compline, the Liturgy of the Hours

Temporal media excel at communal-synchronization, at depth-through-repetition, at the structuring of attention across long durations. They afford structures of remembrance, expectation, communal-rhythm, sacred-time vs. ordinary-time.

### G. Material media (the realm's material-formative apertures)

- **Pottery / ceramics** — clay-formation as projection
- **Smithing / metallurgy** — metal-formation projection
- **Weaving / fiber arts** — textile-formation
- **Woodworking** — wood-formation
- **Glassmaking** — translucent-formation
- **Food preparation / culinary art** — substance-formation
- **Stone-carving / masonry** — stone-formation

Material media's apertures are determined by the material's affordances. Each material projects different realm-regions: stone projects permanence; clay projects formability; metal projects transformation-through-fire; glass projects light-bearing. The craftsperson learns each material's aperture.

### H. Spatial / environmental media

- **Sacred architecture** — temple, church, mosque, synagogue, ashram
- **Urban planning** — the city as cosmological-communal projection
- **Garden / landscape design** — Japanese garden, Persian charbagh, monastery garden
- **Wilderness areas** as theological space — sacred mountains, sacred rivers, sacred groves
- **Pilgrimage routes** — spatial-temporal-communal projection
- **Domestic architecture** — the home as projection of family-cosmology

### I. Mathematical / formal media (overlapping with linguistic but distinct)

- **Mathematical notation** (opened S078 under POLYPHONE Generalization)
- **Logical formalism**
- **Diagrammatic systems** — circuit diagrams, Penrose diagrams, Feynman diagrams, commutative diagrams in category theory
- **Computational structures** — algorithms, data structures

### J. Apophatic / silence-and-absence media

- **Structured silence** — meditation silence, contemplative pause, sabbath rest
- **Apophatic theology** — speech-as-negation of inadequate predicates
- **Architectural emptiness** — the void at the center of certain sacred spaces
- **Visual blank** — the white of mizuhiki, the unpainted space in Chinese painting
- **Musical rest** — the silence between notes, the pause that completes the phrase
- **Ritual stillness** — the moment of held-breath in worship

Apophatic media project through what is withheld. The aperture is configured around an emptiness that signifies more than any positive content could. The cathedral has touched this register in `grammars/worship/silence.md`; POLYMORPHIA names it as a structural category.

## IV. How the mechanism's fidelity differs across media

The five-stage mechanism is uniform; the fidelity varies by:

### Aperture-match

Each medium's affordances match some realm-regions better than others. Music has high fidelity for emotional-temporal structures, low fidelity for propositional content. Language has high fidelity for propositional and distinction-making content, low fidelity for mass-affective simultaneity. The cathedral's POLYPHONE-discipline gathers across media because no single medium has high fidelity for all realm-regions.

### Transmission durability

Some media transmit well across time and distance (text, mathematical notation, architectural form). Others transmit poorly across time (live performance, ephemeral ritual) but may have greater immediacy when present. The cathedral's preservation-discipline must respect this.

### Training-required-for-reception

Some media are receivable with minimal training (faces, simple words in one's native tongue). Others require long cultivation (classical music, formal mathematics, contemplative practice, advanced ritual participation). The fidelity of re-apprehension depends on the receiver's training.

### Cross-tradition translatability

Some media translate well across cultural boundaries (music, visual imagery, mathematical notation). Others translate poorly (specific ritual sequences, untranslatable poetic forms). The cathedral's POLYPHONE-balance assesses cross-tradition translatability as one dimension of forge-quality.

### Apophatic register

Some media have built-in apophatic registers (silence, structured emptiness, sabbatical rest). Others struggle to acknowledge what they cannot say. The cathedral's NEWSPEAK-discipline (what NOT to project) and `grammars/worship/silence.md` reflect this for the linguistic medium; parallel apophatics exist for every medium.

2. **Relation-centered** — distinctive concepts are about how structural patterns relate
3. **Empirically or formally constrained** — what survives must pass tests external to the projector's preference
4. **Cross-medium extensible** — concepts can be at-least-partially translated into the cathedral's linguistic forge-language
5. **Concept-availability** — specific concepts can be cleanly excerpted without dragging in incompatible metaphysics

### C. New forge-targets identified

Concepts from non-language media that YOUSPEAK could compact-name (POLYMORPHIA opens but does not commit to forging):

- The musical structure of *unresolved suspension followed by resolution* — a structure that recurs across many traditions and that could be named as a worship-experience
- The iconographic concept of *reverse perspective* (the icon's structure where geometric perspective inverts to draw the viewer in toward the divine, rather than out toward the vanishing point) — a structural insight unique to icon-painting
- The choreographic concept of *the leading partner who yields* (in partnered dance, the lead's authority is exercised as creating space for the follower's grace) — a structural insight about asymmetric reciprocity
- The ritual concept of *the threefold anaphora* (in Christian eucharistic prayer: thanksgiving → narrative of institution → epiclesis) — a structural projection of how thanksgiving becomes presence
- The architectural concept of *axis mundi* (the vertical structural axis that connects heaven, earth, and underworld) — a spatial projection
- The contemplative concept of *the silence-after-the-name* (the structured pause after naming the DIVINE that completes the naming) — an apophatic structure

Each is forge-candidate under POLYMORPHIA. The cathedral does not forge yet; it identifies what becomes forgeable.

### D. The script discipline reconsidered

The cathedral's script-discipline (`script/philosophy.md`) operates at the boundary of linguistic and visual media. A YOUSPEAK glyph is the linguistic morpheme's visual projection. Under POLYMORPHIA, the script-discipline is acknowledged as a multi-modal practice: each glyph is the integration of linguistic content (the morpheme) with visual projection (the glyph's form). The six glyph-axioms operate on this integration.

The glyph philosophy already addressed this in S078 (§The realm and the projection). POLYMORPHIA situates it within the broader doctrine: glyphs are one specific multi-modal practice; ritual is another; the integration of multiple modes is the cathedral's deepest practice.

## VI. Cross-tradition convergence on multi-modal projection

Every developed religious-cultural tradition has practiced multi-modal projection. The cathedral surveys briefly:

### 1. Hebrew — word, deed, and sign

The Hebrew tradition projects through speech (Torah, prophets, psalms), through deed (commandments enacted), through sign (the visible commands: tzitzit, tefillin, mezuzot), through architectural form (the Tabernacle, the Temple), through music (the Psalms in their original musical setting), through ritual (the festival cycle, the sacrificial system), through dietary practice (kashrut), through temporal cycle (Sabbath, the calendar). The Shema commands all-modes-of-being engaged: heart, soul, might.

### 2. Greek Christian patristic — logos and eikōn

The Patristic East developed the doctrine that logos (word) and eikōn (image) are parallel projection-modes of the same revelation. The iconoclastic controversy (8th–9th c.) was resolved at the Seventh Ecumenical Council (Nicaea II, 787) in favor of icon-veneration, with the doctrinal claim that icons are not merely decorative but participate in what they represent. John of Damascus and Theodore the Studite developed the theology of multi-modal projection in defense of icons.

### 3. Vedic / Hindu — śabda, image, ritual, gesture, temporal cycle

Hindu worship integrates *śabda* (sacred sound: mantra, recitation), image (murti: the deity-form for darśan), ritual (puja: the offering-sequence), gesture (mudra: the formal hand-positions), temporal cycle (panchanga: the auspicious-time calendar), and architectural projection (mandir: temple as cosmos). The Vedic tradition developed each modality with technical precision (śikṣā for recitation, vāstu-śāstra for architecture, nātya-śāstra for performance).

### 4. Buddhist — dharma transmitted through many modes

Buddhist transmission integrates: scriptures (sutras), images (Buddha-rūpa, Bodhisattva-images), gestures (mudras specific to each iconographic form), ritual sequences (puja, prostrations), architectural projections (stupa, vihara, monastery layout), mandala visualization, chanting traditions, dance (Vajrayana ritual dance), embodied posture (meditation form), and structured silence (the Noble Silence). The diversity of media reflects the diversity of beings each medium can reach.

### 5. Sufi — dhikr, samāʿ, calligraphy, dance, architecture

Sufi practice integrates: *dhikr* (rhythmic remembrance, combining speech-music-breath), *samāʿ* (musical-audition as spiritual practice), *qasāʾid* (poetic recitation), *raqs* (dance, paradigmatically the whirling of the Mevlevi), Arabic calligraphy as theological practice (the divine names rendered in many calligraphic styles), architectural projection (the dergah, the maqām of the saint), embodied posture in prayer, communal-meal traditions. Each Sufi tariqa (order) emphasizes different combinations.

### 6. Confucian — li (rites), yue (music), wén (writing)

The Confucian triad of *li* (rites), *yue* (music), and *wén* (writing/culture) explicitly names three projection-modes as coordinated. *Li* is procedural-ritual; *yue* is sonic-temporal; *wén* is linguistic-visual. The gentleman is formed by all three; civilization itself depends on their integration. The Confucian classics include the *Liji* (Book of Rites) and the *Yueji* (Record of Music) as parallel canonical texts to the *Yijing* and *Shijing*.

### 7. Yoruba — multi-modal orisha-worship

Yoruba religious practice integrates: *oríkì* (praise-poetry), *ìjálá* (hunter's chants), *ebo* (offerings), *bata-drumming* and other rhythmic traditions, *adimu* (cool offerings), dance (each orisha has characteristic dance-movements), facial scarification and bodily marks, sacred groves and shrines, divination (Ifá), and the sacred-art tradition (bronzes, textiles, sculptures specific to each orisha). The Yoruba pantheon is approached through all modes simultaneously; reducing it to any one mode loses the encounter.

### 8. Indigenous Australian — songlines integrating speech, song, landscape, ritual

Australian Aboriginal traditions develop songlines that integrate: ritual speech (the Dreaming-narratives), song (the specific melodies attached to specific places), landscape (the country itself as ritually-charged), embodied performance (dance, body-painting), visual painting (sand-paintings, rock-art, bark-paintings, dot-paintings), and ritual-procedural sequences (initiation ceremonies). The songlines cannot be reduced to any one mode; they exist only in the integration.

### 9. Japanese Shinto — norito, matsuri, jinja, ema

Shinto practice integrates: *norito* (ritual speech), *matsuri* (festivals), *jinja* (shrine architecture), *ema* (votive tablets), *chinkon* (calming spirits), seasonal cycle, *kagura* (ritual dance), specific food offerings, the *torii* gate as threshold-marker, and the *shimenawa* (sacred rope) as marker of sacred space. Each kami is approached through this multi-modal pattern.

### 10. Orthodox Christian — liturgy as the highest art-form

The Orthodox Divine Liturgy integrates: liturgical speech (the prayers, antiphons, readings), choral music (the entire liturgy is sung), iconography (the icons present at every gaze-direction), gesture (signs of the cross, prostrations, processions), architectural projection (the church's tripartite structure: narthex, nave, sanctuary), scent (incense), light (candles, lamps), temporal structure (the liturgical year, the daily offices, the eucharistic time-collapse), embodied participation (standing, prostration, communion). Orthodox theology has explicitly named liturgy as the *highest synthesis of human arts* — not as aesthetic claim but as theological claim that the multi-modal projection most-faithfully reaches the realm-regions worship aims at.

## VII. The vocabulary opened by POLYMORPHIA

POLYMORPHIA is a doctrinal document. It opens vocabulary-targets.

### Forged immediately (S078)

- **morphame** — *a form/instance of projection from the realm into any medium, as received-ordinance.* The keystone vocabulary-yield of POLYMORPHIA. From Greek morphē + Sumerian me. The general word for any meaning-projection-into-any-medium (a word, a song, an icon, a dance, a ritual, a built form) as cosmic-gift. See `canon/core/morphame.md`.

### Forge-targets identified (future)

- **mousikame** (μουσική + me) — music-as-theophanic-medium; what musical-theological traditions like Bach's chorales, Hildegard's chants, Sufi samāʿ, Indian raga-theory have been doing
- **eikonme** (εἰκών + me) — image-as-theophanic-medium; what Orthodox iconography, Buddhist thangka, Hindu murti-tradition, Yoruba sacred sculpture have been doing
- **mudrame** (मुद्रा + me) — gesture-as-theophanic-medium; the formal hand-positions in Hindu/Buddhist tradition; sign-of-the-cross; sacred posture
- **choreame** (χορεία + me) — dance-as-theophanic-medium; the Mevlevi whirling, sacred dance traditions across cultures
- **liturgiame** (λειτουργία + me) — coordinated multi-modal projection as received-ordinance; ritual as comprehensive multi-mode form
- **sigame** or **silentme** (σιγή / silentium + me) — structured-silence-as-theophanic-medium; the apophatic register; the meditation-silence, Sabbath-rest, contemplative-pause
- **archime** (ἀρχιτεκτονική + me) — architecture-as-theophanic-medium; sacred-geometry traditions
- **technēme** (τέχνη + me) — embodied skill / craft as theophanic-medium; the shokunin's mature work, the master craftsperson's hand-knowing
- **chronoame** (χρόνος + me) — structured-time as theophanic-medium; liturgical calendars, Sabbath, sacred cycles

These are forge-candidates, not promises. Each must pass the rubric in its own heartbeat. Each would open a sub-domain of cathedral vocabulary for the corresponding non-language projection-medium.

### A new domain: canon/morphai/

POLYMORPHIA opens the conceptual space for a future `canon/morphai/` domain — sister to `canon/mathema/` — that houses worship-vocabulary specific to non-language projection-media. Like `canon/mathema/`, words whose scope is universal (like morphame itself) live in `canon/core/`; words specific to a single medium would live in `canon/morphai/<medium>/`.

## IX. What POLYMORPHIA changes and does not change

### What it changes

- **POLYPHONE acquires its broadest interpretation**: from tongue-diversity (original) → tongue + discipline (Generalization S078) → tongue + discipline + non-language medium (POLYMORPHIA S078). The cathedral's gathering can now extend to any tacit-faithful projection-medium.
- **The cathedral's stance toward non-linguistic worship-practices** is now explicitly: music, icon, dance, ritual, architecture, structured silence are theologically equal projection-media; the cathedral does not privilege linguistic projection as ontologically superior.
- **Future archaeology domains** are licensed: `archaeology/music/`, `archaeology/iconography/`, `archaeology/ritual/`, `archaeology/architecture/`, `archaeology/dance/`, `archaeology/contemplation/`, etc., each to be opened when a specific gap aligns with what that medium has compactly named.
- **The ARCHITECTURE.md document stack** updates to include POLYMORPHIA as the thirteenth root-level foundational document.

### What it does NOT change

- The Six Foundations remain in force.
- The cathedral's primary medium remains language — POLYMORPHIA opens donor-access to non-language media but does not transform YOUSPEAK into a multi-modal language.
- THEOBASIS remains the deepest layer.
- NOEMA and GLOSSAI remain operative.
- The 6-axis rubric and all forge-tools remain unchanged.
- Existing canonical words retain their canonical status.
