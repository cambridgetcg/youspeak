III · The Chapels of Love

English has one word for love. That has always been a kind of poverty. The cathedral keeps ten chapels — one per register, each carved in the tongue that taught it — and they climb: from the love that descends, to the love that prepares a room.

    agápē · Greek
    agapeme
    /ə.ˈɡɑː.peɪ.meɪ/

    Sacrificial self-giving love as received-ordinance — love that descends to the unworthy at ultimate cost, and creates worth in the beloved by the act of loving.

    the love unmotivated by merit

  2. אהבה

    ahavame
    /ə.ˈhɑː.və.meɪ/

Two patterns open half the canon

𒈨

-me — Sumerian 𒈨, received-ordinance: X as gift received, never earned. kimme — attention as gift. shemme — hearing that already obeys. doxomme — glory beheld.

情

qing — Mandarin 情, felt-bond: the bond you feel and did not manufacture. kinqing — the bond that holds across silence. panimqing — the moment small-talk turns to presence. zakarqing — remembering-toward.

The gift you receive; the bond you feel. Learn these two and half the canon opens.

IV · The Scriptorium

Latin transliteration is the canonical machine form; the glyphs are a gift to the eye. Where the font falls silent, the Latin remains — the script never blocks meaning.

How to read a stone

  • Top-left, the donor sigil — the quarry-mark of the lending tongue: ● Greek · ■ Latin · ▽ Hebrew · ○ Arabic · ⋮ Sanskrit · □ Egyptian · ≡ PIE · ⊻ Sumerian wedge-pair · ▿ Akkadian wedge · 人 Mandarin · ✕ YOUSPEAK’s own coinages · a torii gate for Japanese, a khipu knot for Quechua, bar-and-dot for Maya — and no mark at all for English: absence-is-English.
  • The body — the iconographic core, shaped by its semantic domain: beauty opens upward, truth stands balanced, light radiates.
  • Bottom-right, the class mark — carried by grammatical and structural stones; content morphemes stand unmarked.
  • One glyph, one codepoint, one morpheme — the glyph is visually rich; the tokenization is atomic. Every debt to a donor tongue is recorded in the stone itself.

VIII · The Crypt

Built for worship

This is a language of faith. Every coinage serves recognition — of the beauty that appears, the ache that arrives, the bond that holds, the Giver of all three. The theology lives here in the crypt, not shouted from the door; you will have felt it in the words before you read it in a creed.

The counter-Newspeak

Where Newspeak engineered vocabulary-reduction for thought-control, YOUSPEAK engineers vocabulary-expansion for truthful naming. Same kind of object; opposite vocation. Where the sayable shrinks, so does the thinkable — so this house only ever builds outward.
