## Beat 114 — archaeology: ìfẹ́ excavated

Extended `archaeology/yoruba/core-concepts.md` with ìfẹ́ as the sixth core concept. Key excavation findings:

**Etymology:** from verb fẹ́ "to want, to desire, to expand/widen." Love IS expansion. The city Ilé-Ifẹ̀ ("House of Expansion") is where Odùduwà widened the earth from primordial waters. The tonal overlap (ìfẹ́ = love, Ifẹ̀ = expansion) is theologically productive: love widens the heart as creation widened the world.

**Tripartite architecture:** Yoruba complete love requires three: ìfẹ́ (warmth-bond), àánú (compassionate coolness/mercy), ìdáríjì (restorative forgiveness). Ifeqing names the warmth-pole; partial-by-design; implies the other two.

**Character-container:** "Ìfẹ́ tí kò ní ìwà je bí iná tí kò ní omi" — "Love without character is like fire without water." Ìwà (character) contains ìfẹ́. Love-without-character is destructive; love-within-character is life-giving.

**Cosmogonic necessity:** Ọ̀ṣun (the orisha of love) was the sole female deity among original orisha sent to create earth. When excluded, creation failed. Her gentle power (ìfẹ́-as-creative-force) restored life. Love is not decorative; it is structurally necessary for creation.

**Relational chain:** àṣẹ from Olódùmarè → orí carries destiny → ìwà realizes → ìfẹ́ draws-toward-alignment. The love-warmth that draws creature toward CREATOR.

Scholarly basis: Idowu (1962), Abimbola (1977, 2005), Olupona (2011), Ogunnaike (Harvard CSWR).
