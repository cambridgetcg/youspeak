## Beat 34 — Amendment II proposal: divine-property register threshold

**Observation across Session 005 (two forges): divine-property-register words cluster at weighted 7.40-7.45**, structurally-cousin to worship-register words (proskynance 7.05, shukhance 7.20, kavvance 7.05, sraddhance 7.30, satance 7.25, eikhalgia 7.40). Eight total near-Core-but-below words now across the two registers.

Both registers share:
- Cross-tradition gaps (real, multi-tradition-attested, no compact-existing-word)
- Tradition-specific donors carrying learnability cost (sacred-tongue morphemes cost syllables)
- Polyphone typically 7-8 (cross-family when the tradition-diversity is honored)
- Groundedness 7-8 (narrower-audience penalty from theological-literacy prerequisite)
- Clarity 7-8 (strong unfolding, though register-bound)

The cumulative evidence from two sessions (002 proposed Amendment I, 005 proposes Amendment II) establishes: **the v2 rubric's uniform 7.5 threshold is calibrated for everyday-register; applying it to worship-register OR divine-property-register systematically excludes valid coinages**. The solution is not to relax the rubric (everyday-register calibration is correct) but to recognize register-specific thresholds.

### Proposed Amendment II

> _For words in the divine-property-register (domain: liturgy / divine-property), the Core threshold is 7.0 weighted, provided the candidate also passes the same tradition-diversity check as Amendment I: the candidate's donor-traditions include at least one that appears in fewer than 30% of existing Core Canon entries, OR the candidate's cross-tradition convergence (gap-validity) scores ≥8 AND no single tradition's contribution dominates._

### Rationale

1. **Divine-property-register has register-bound learnability cost.** Each divine property requires specific tradition-donor(s) for precision; those donors are sacred-tongue-specialized and cost learnability.

2. **The v2 rubric's uniform threshold was calibrated for everyday-register** (per the DIAGNOSTIC, Session 003). The rubric does not fail; it merely assumes everyday-register audiences. Register-bound audiences need register-bound thresholds.

3. **Tradition-diversity is achievable.** Walkehesed (Tocharian + Hebrew) and theokimme (Greek + Japanese + Sumerian) both pass tradition-diversity. Future divine-property forges can maintain this constraint, preventing Amendment II from flooding Core with one-tradition-dominant words.

4. **The Liturgy-Original tier (THEOBASIS Corollary 2) is structurally prior to Core.** Per THEOBASIS: "the Liturgy is not a specialization OF YOUSPEAK; it is the **origin** of YOUSPEAK." Applying the everyday-register threshold to Liturgy-register entries is ordering-inverted. The Original should have its own threshold.
