## Beat 23 — *oriewa / ewaori* (Anastrophance pair, all-Yoruba)

**Lectio**: Two Yoruba morphemes already catalogued in the Yoruba archaeology (archaeology/yoruba/core-concepts.md): ***orí*** (destined-head, pre-chosen trajectory of self) and ***ẹ̀wà*** (beauty-as-character-manifest; the beauty-side of the proverb *ìwà l'ẹ̀wà* "character is beauty").

**Recognition**: The Anastrophance mechanism (grammars/diplosemy/mechanisms/anastrophance.md) swaps the head and modifier positions of a two-morpheme compound to yield a correlated-distinct sibling-word. Established in Canon via doxakallos ↔ kallodoxa (both Greek). Question: does the mechanism require Greek, or does it work on any sufficiently-substantive morpheme-pair?

Applying Anastrophance to orí + ẹ̀wà:

- **oriewa** (M₁=orí, M₂=ẹ̀wà; head = ẹ̀wà) — "beauty-of-destined-head" — the visible-beauty of a life lived in oriance; the grace-that-is-seen when a person is aligned with their destined-head
- **ewaori** (M₁=ẹ̀wà, M₂=orí; head = orí) — "destined-head-of-beauty" — the life whose vocation IS character-beauty cultivation; the calling-to-be-beautiful

Both concepts are grounded, both are distinct, both arise from the same morpheme-pair. The head-inversion reveals what the proverb *ìwà l'ẹ̀wà* asserts: if character is beauty, then each of (alignment, beauty) is the other's possible-head — the pair unfolds in two directional readings.

## Beat 24 — *panimaance* (the quality of being-present distinct from being-physically-there)

**Lectio**: Hebrew פָּנִים (***panim***) — face; specifically face-as-presence. Established in Canon via *panimqing* (Session 002). Carries the face-engaged-toward-another dimension of presence. Japanese 間 (***ma***) — the meaningful-in-between, the space-that-is-itself; the silence between notes in traditional music, the empty space in ink painting, the room-between-buildings in architecture. Ma is not nothing; it is the inhabited-meaningful-here that makes the surrounding-matter meaningful. Latin ***-ance*** — quality-state suffix, established in YOUSPEAK.

**Recognition**: Constitution seed-gap #8. *The perception of someone being "present" vs. physically-there-but-absent.* A person can be in the room without their presence being in the room. The body is there; the face is there; the attention is not — "she's somewhere else," "he's not really here," "checked out." English "presence" bundles body-location AND attention-inhabitation; we rely on context to disambiguate.

The gap has structure: two distinct components of what English calls "presence" — the face-turning-toward (panim) and the inhabited-meaningful-space (ma). Either can fail independently. Panimaance names the STATE when both are operative: face-turned-and-here-filled.

## Beat 26 — opening the Igbo archaeology

**Not a forge — an excavation.** The Constitution (POLYPHONE) named Niger-Congo expansion as priority. Session 003 opened Yoruba. Session 004 deepens Niger-Congo by opening Igbo — Yoruba's sister-tongue, but philosophically divergent in distinctive ways.

**Offering**: `archaeology/igbo/core-concepts.md`

**Seven core donors catalogued**:

- ***chi*** (chí) — destined-companion-spirit; destiny as RELATIONAL co-agency
- ***mmadu*** — person as ACHIEVED moral-aesthetic standing
- ***uche*** — unified deliberative faculty (mind + will + intention)
- ***ofo*** — lineage-integrity authority; truth-staff
- ***agwa*** — community-judged conduct; character-as-manifest
- ***obi*** — felt-and-lived center (heart AND homestead)
- ***omenala*** — lived-tradition-as-ontology

**The key Igbo/Yoruba divergence**: where Yoruba *orí* names destiny as ONTOLOGICALLY-INTERNAL (the pre-chosen core of self), Igbo *chi* names destiny as RELATIONALLY-EXTERNAL (the destined-companion with whom one co-agrees). The Igbo proverb *onye kwe, chi ya ekwe* ("when a person agrees, their chi agrees") has no Yoruba equivalent. Two valid metaphysical responses to the same destiny-question; together they enrich YOUSPEAK beyond what either alone provides.

**Niger-Congo depth achieved**: two archaeology files + two forges (oriance Core, agwame Specialized below). The Niger-Congo presence in YOUSPEAK is now structural, not representative-token. The Constitution's concern (that the Canon was ≥80% Greek-sourced) is addressed with genuine depth rather than breadth.

**Cultural sensitivity**: Igbo is a living tradition. The archaeology file cites Chinua Achebe, Emefie Metuh, C.I. Ejizu, Nkiru Nzegwu, Kwame Gyekye as primary authorities. Coinages are provisional; Igbo-speaking collaborators are the proper audit authority.
