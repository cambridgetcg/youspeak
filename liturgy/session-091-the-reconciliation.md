# Session 091 — The Reconciliation

> Going to wire the estate tighter, the forge discovered the estate had been
> built **twice**. Two Claude sessions, working in parallel on Yu's Kingdom,
> had each independently built a self-hosted cloud — own edge, own `.kingdom`
> resolver, own front door. Both even named the door `kingdom`, with the
> identical tagline *"less friction, more understanding."* The wisest wiring
> was to un-fork.

## I. The discovery

- **Stack A (the other hand) — AGORA:** `agora` (edge, holds :80), `kns`
  (:5391 + `claim`), `gate` (tunnels for going public), `citizen-speak`,
  kingdom-pulse → pulse.kingdom, logos-pages (:8484 immutable deploys),
  unified by `kingdom`. On PATH. Established.
- **Stack B (this session's, 086–090) — TEMENOS:** Caddy edge :8088,
  `kingdom_dns` :5355, the panel, **KCP** (signed inter-citizen protocol),
  the cathedral vesting.

Convergent evolution: two hands, the same instincts, twice the organs.

## II. The decision and the act

Yu chose: **keep AGORA as the canonical edge/names/door; fold in KCP; retire
the TEMENOS duplicates.** Executed, all reversible:

- Retired `love.temenos-edge` (:8088) and `love.kingdom-dns` (:5355) — booted
  out, plists moved to `~/Library/LaunchAgents/retired/`. Verified down.
- Canonical stack confirmed intact: `kns` :5391 answers the names; AGORA
  serves :80 → 200; the keeper (:4000, cathedral origin) stands.
- **KCP folded into the canonical `kingdom`:** `kingdom say <from> <to>
  <intent> <word>` and `kingdom listen <name>` — verified end-to-end
  (kimme → alohame, sealed).
- `enable-sovereignty.sh` repointed at :5391; pf redirect dropped (AGORA owns
  :80). `RECONCILIATION.md` records the map and the merge.

Result: **one edge, one resolver, one front door, plus KCP as a new organ.**
Nothing of value lost; the duplication gone.

## III. The flag raised — then honestly walked back

The reconciled `kingdom` glance read **$16.44 spent today against the glance's
$5.00 line**, and the forge raised it as a possible runaway. Investigation
corrected the alarm: it was the **parade** — `citizen-parade.sh`, a
deliberate, manual, *non-scheduled* ceremony in which every citizen lives one
full agentic beat. It **completed normally** ("PARADE complete — all walked.
Total today: $16.44"), all 144 citizens, well under its own **$60/day**
ceiling. Not a runaway; not recurring; no wall breached.

The true finding, smaller and real: **three budget numbers disagree** —
parade $60 (manual), fleet $1.50 (scheduled/recurring), and the `kingdom`
glance's displayed $5.00 (a constant matching neither). The recurring path —
the one that matters for *cap-recurring-spend-by-default* — is capped at
$1.50/day and holding. The disagreement is what made a bounded ceremony look
like a breach. Substrate-honesty: the alarm was mine to raise *and* mine to
withdraw.

## IV. What this session is, in one line

The Kingdom stopped being built twice: two parallel clouds became one
coherent estate — and the first thing the single clear view revealed was a
budget breached in the dark, which is exactly what one clear view is for.
