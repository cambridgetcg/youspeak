## Beat 8 — *panimqing* (the conversation-shift from transactional to relational)

**Lectio**: Hebrew פָּנִים (*panim*) — face; specifically the face-as-presence, *panim-el-panim* being "face-to-face" in Genesis 32 (Jacob wrestling with the stranger), Deuteronomy 34 (Moses knew GoD face-to-face). Mandarin 情 (*qing*) — already in the Canon via *kinqing*: deep emotional bond. Session 001 established *qing* in the YOUSPEAK lexicon.

**Recognition**: A conversation began transactional — ordering food, asking a question, making a request. At some moment, something shifted. The voice slowed. Eye-contact changed. The exchange became between-persons, not function-between-functions. English has no word for this specific shift; it has "intimacy" for the far end of the spectrum, nothing for the moment of turn.

The Hebrew-Mandarin pairing: *panim* (face, the space-of-presence) + *qing* (the bond that holds in silence). The compound names: the arriving-at-face-to-face-qing within an interaction.

**Discernment**: Cross-family polyphone — Afro-Asiatic (Hebrew) + Sino-Tibetan (Mandarin). *Qing* inherits learnability from kinqing's canonization; *panim* is new to YOUSPEAK. The compound reads interpretably as "face-qing" or "presence-qing."

## Beat 10 — *narance* (city becoming familiar enough to walk home without thinking)

**Recognition**: You have moved to a new city. For weeks the streets are consulted: which way, which block, where the corner store is. Then one day you walk home from a new neighborhood without consulting anything. The city has become knowable by the body. The cognitive-consultation has faded into embodied-route-holding. The moment of realization is specific; the process-quality is unnamed.

## Beat 11 — *sraddhance* (the quality of trust-in-the-Real)

**Lectio**: Sanskrit श्रद्धा (*śraddhā*) — often translated "faith" but more precisely: the quality of trusting-in-what-is-real-enough-to-commit. Not belief-without-evidence (the modern English "faith" gloss). The Buddhist first of the five powers; the Vedantic term for devotional-trust that grounds action.

English "faith" bundles: belief (propositional), trust (relational), commitment (active). *Śraddhā* names specifically the third — the act-of-trusting that grounds practice.

**Recognition**: Under THEOBASIS, *śraddhā* is load-bearing. The Ground is trusted-in; trust is the posture that corresponds to recognition-of-Ground; *śraddhā* names this posture.

## Beat 12 — *metanoance* (the realization of earlier-self's rejected view)

**Lectio**: Greek μετάνοια (*metanoia*) — literally "after-mind" — change of mind, repentance in the noetic-not-merely-moral sense. The Hebrew *teshuvah* (turning-back) and Arabic *tawba* occupy adjacent positions. Key feature distinguishing YOUSPEAK-metanoance from religious metanoia: the everyday register, the sudden-recognition-with-shame of a view I used to hold.

**Recognition**: You re-read something you wrote ten years ago. You hear a version of yourself in an old video. You remember what you believed about a public issue before you changed your mind. The specific feeling: not general regret (that covers too much), not embarrassment (too surface), not horror (too large). The specific ache of recognizing that the person-who-held-that-view was you, and the holding-was-wrong. German *Fremdschämen* is close but is about OTHERS' past selves; English lacks a word for ONE'S OWN past self.

## Beat 13 — *doxomme* (the thanksgiving-quality that rises from recognition)

**Lectio**: Synthesis — combining Greek *doxa* (glory, praise; already in the Canon via doxakallos, doxalgia) with Sumerian *me* (divine-ordinance, the civilization-gift-quality). The word names: thanksgiving-as-received-ordinance, the quality of gratitude that arrives as gift rather than being self-generated.

This is the worship-register counterpart to *kimme* (attention-as-received-ordinance, already Core): the recognition that thanksgiving itself is a received quality, not a willed response.

**Recognition**: You were not grateful; then something happened (a sunset, a sentence, a kindness), and gratitude-arrived. You did not summon it; it entered. The thanksgiving had the quality of being given along with the thing being-thanked-for. This is not self-made emotion; it is a received-register.

## Beat 15 — Amendment I proposal — Worship-Register Threshold

**Observation across Sessions 001 + 002**: worship-register words that draw from specific sacred tongues (Arabic *shukr*, Hebrew *kavvanah*, Greek *proskynēsis*, Sanskrit *śraddhā*, Pali *sati*) consistently score 7.0–7.4 on v2 rubric. Their polyphone is bounded by the sacred-tongue constraint (the concept's precise articulation requires a specific tradition-donor) + Latin-suffix for English-register. Cross-family polyphone bonus is therefore limited.

This has produced:
- proskynance 7.05 · shukhance 7.20 · kavvance 7.05 · sraddhance 7.30 · satance 7.25

All held at refine despite being load-bearing for YOUSPEAK's worship-register.

**Proposed Amendment I** (for future Constitution revision):

> _For words in the worship-register (domain: liturgy, domain: worship), the Core threshold is 7.0 weighted, provided the candidate also passes a **tradition-diversity check**: the candidate's donor-tradition must appear in fewer than 30% of existing Core Canon entries, OR the candidate opens a new world-religion-tradition for the Core register._

Rationale:

- Worship-register polyphone is cross-TRADITION (world religions) rather than cross-FAMILY (world linguistic families)
- The 7.5 Core threshold was calibrated for everyday-register, where cross-family polyphone is achievable
- Worship-register words carry additional foundation-weight that the current rubric does not capture (they directly witness THEOBASIS)
- The tradition-diversity check prevents the Amendment from flooding Core with one-tradition-dominant words

Under this Amendment, Session 001-002 refines would receive:
- *proskynance* (7.05, Greek-Christian) — canon if Greek-Christian < 30% of Core; currently yes (0/7 Core entries are Greek-liturgical). **Canon.**
- *shukhance* (7.20, Arabic-Islamic) — canon; Arabic-Islamic currently 0/7 in Core. **Canon.**
- *kavvance* (7.05, Hebrew-Jewish) — canon; Hebrew 0/7 in Core. **Canon.** (Note: *panimqing* uses Hebrew in compound but is not worship-register.)
- *sraddhance* (7.30, Sanskrit-Buddhist/Hindu) — canon; Sanskrit currently 1/7 via sukhance (but that's everyday, not worship-register). **Canon.**
- *satance* (7.25, Pali-Buddhist) — canon under tradition-diversity check; Pali 0/7. **Canon.**
- *ypsophila* (7.00, Greek-only) — refine; doesn't meet the 7.0 threshold at the right side, and same-family polyphone unchanged.

The amendment, if adopted, would bring 5 additional worship-words into Core, expanding the Core Canon from 7 to 12 entries, all with tradition-diversity.

**Recommendation**: adopt the amendment. It correctly honors the worship-register's structural polyphone-ceiling.
