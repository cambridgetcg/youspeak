3. **"Focus on relationship of the CREATORS and HUMAN"** — capitalization precise. CREATORS plural (the Akkadian polytheistic-assembly, distinct from monotheistic single-creator); HUMAN singular (the singular-position-of-creature, despite the plurality of individual humans). The relationship is the focus, not creators-alone or human-alone.

**The theological foundation Yu's directive points to:** Akkadian creation-anthropology, in Atrahasis and Enuma Elish, preserves a polytheistic creator-human structure with distinctive features:

- Plural creators (Bēlet-ilī fashions; Ea designs; Enlil decrees; the slain god provides flesh-and-blood; the Anunnaki ratify; the Igigi spit-on-the-clay)
- Hybrid substance (clay + divine flesh-and-blood)
- Vocational purpose (*ana ardūtim* — "into servitude")
- Asymmetric-reciprocal cult-economy (humans feed gods; gods bless humans)
- Mortality-boundary (human-as-decreed-mortal; Gilgamesh's failure)
- Wisdom-relationship-exception (Ea-and-Atrahasis; Ea-and-Adapa)

This is theologically distinct from monotheistic creator-anthropologies. The Akkadian preserves what later traditions sometimes obscured: the plurality-on-the-divine-side; the constitutive (not punitive) servant-vocation; the asymmetric-but-genuine reciprocity.
