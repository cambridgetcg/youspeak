## Beat 105 — *mahabbahqing* (Sufi felt-mystical-binding)

**Lectio**: Arabic ***mahabbah*** (محبة) — love; from root ḥ-b-b "to love." Central in Sufi tradition: Rabia of Basra (~d. 801 CE), Rumi (~13th c), Ibn ʿArabi (~13th c), al-Ghazali (~12th c) all center on mahabbah-of-Allah. The Sufi-mystical register: heart-binding-to-the-Beloved with implication of *fanāʾ* (self-annihilation in the Beloved). Mandarin ***qing*** — felt-bond; established YOUSPEAK qing-family marker.

Rabia's "two loves," Rumi's mathnawi, Ibn ʿArabi's *Tarjumān al-Ashwāq* — all are mahabbah-poetry. The register adds to ahavame's commanded-disposition the FELT-MYSTICAL-BINDING dimension specifically.

## Beat 106 — *bhaktime* (Hindu disciplined-devotional-love)

**Lectio**: Sanskrit ***bhakti*** (भक्ति) — devotion; from root *bhaj-* "to share, partake, divide" → "sharing-in" or "participating-in" the Divine through loving-devotion. Central in Bhagavad Gita; the path of bhakti-yoga; Gita 18.66: "abandoning all dharmas, take refuge in Me alone." Vaishnava saint-poets (Caitanya, Mirabai, Surdas, Tulsidas) elaborate. Sumerian ***me*** — established YOUSPEAK morpheme.

**Recognition**: Bhakti-tradition (~7th c onward in India) is one of the most theologically-rich love-of-DIVINE traditions. The disciplined-devotional-self-surrendering register that bhakti articulates complements ahavame (commanded-disposition) and mahabbahqing (felt-mystical-binding).
