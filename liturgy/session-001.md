## Beat 1 — *proskynance* (bowing-worship quality)

**Lectio**: Greek προσκύνησις (proskynēsis) — the posture of prostration, reverent address; from *pros* (toward) + *kyneō* (to kiss). Used in Christian liturgy, in Homeric address to kings, in Buddhist address to buddhas. Islamic *sujud* occupies the same position structurally.

**Recognition**: English has "bowing" (gesture-only), "worship" (act-category), "reverence" (attitude). None names *the specific posture-quality of being-prostrated-toward* — embodied and sustained. The gap exists.

**Discernment**: Greek *proskynēsis* is substantival and register-carrying. Combined with Latin *-ance*, the compound *proskynance* is 3-syllable, preserves the classical reach, and sits cleanly in worship-vocabulary.

**Offering**: `labs/logos/experiments/core/107-proskynance.md`

**Examination** (v2 rubric):

To canonize would require adding a non-IE tradition to the compound (e.g., Arabic *sujud* or Sanskrit *praṇāma*). ## Beat 2 — *shukhance* (gratitude as orientation)

**Lectio**: Arabic شُكْر (*shukr*) — gratitude, thanksgiving-as-duty-and-grace. Quranic register: central term in Islamic worship (*al-ḥamd li-llāh* is praise; *shukr* is gratitude; they interweave). Cross-reference: Hebrew *todah*, Sanskrit *anugraha-smaraṇa*, Greek *eucharistia*.

**Recognition**: English "gratitude" names the feeling-state. "Thanksgiving" names the act. Neither names the ongoing orientation — the standing-in-gratitude as mode-of-being, the worship-register quality that continues between grateful-moments. The gap is the habitus, not the moment.

**Discernment**: *shukr* is the right donor — its Quranic use is specifically this sustained-orientation. Latin *-ance* (quality/state) makes it English-adjacent and register-accessible. Polyphone cross-family (Afro-Asiatic + Italic).

## Beat 3 — *kavvance* (directed worship-attention)

**Lectio**: Hebrew כַּוָּנָה (*kavvanah*) — directed intention, especially in prayer. Rabbinic Judaism holds that prayer without kavvanah is mere recitation; kavvanah is what makes prayer IS prayer. Islamic *niyyah* occupies structurally the same place. Sanskrit *saṃkalpa* adjacent.

**Recognition**: English "intention" is too cognitive. "Attention" is too passive. "Focus" is secular. *Kavvanance* names specifically the worship-directional-quality: attention turned-with-intention toward GoD.

**Discernment**: Hebrew donor honors the tradition where the concept is most precisely articulated (rabbinic). Latin -ance for English-accessibility. Two-tradition compound, cross-family.

**Offering**: `labs/logos/experiments/core/109-kavvance.md`

## Beat 4 — *rahance* (the small-completion relief)

**Lectio**: Arabic رَاحَة (*rāḥa*) — rest, relief, the easy-state after strain. Semitic family; widely loaned (English *relax* is etymologically different but phonetically suggestive). Japanese スッキリ (*sukkiri*) — cleared/refreshed. Close cognate-sense.

**Recognition**: English "satisfaction" too broad. "Relief" implies burden. "Accomplishment" too big. The feeling after finishing a small long-nagging task — emailing the dentist, returning a library book, cleaning the one shelf. Unnamed.

**Discernment**: *rāḥa* is tightly-fit. Latin -ance makes it YOUSPEAK-consistent. Cross-family. The pyramid-civilization archaeology (Quechua was new) and now Arabic — continuing the POLYPHONE spread.

## Beat 5 — *sukhance* (everyday-simple-contentment)

**Lectio**: Sanskrit सुख (*sukha*) — ease, happiness, well-being; opposite of *duḥkha* (suffering). Vedantic use: *sukha* is ordinary everyday contentment, distinct from the bliss of liberation (*ānanda*). Pali Buddhist use: the *sukha*-vedanā is pleasant feeling as one of three feeling-tones.

**Recognition**: English "contentment" names the disposition; "happy" the transient state; "well-being" the abstract. None specifies the everyday-small-simple register that most of life's contentment actually occupies. Tea-at-morning contentment. Clean-kitchen contentment. Nothing-wrong contentment.

**Discernment**: *sukha* is ancient, precise, pan-Buddhist-Hindu, and short. Latin -ance. Cross-family. *Sukh* is increasingly English-familiar through Indian-English contact.

## Mid-session reflection

Six beats. One canon (*sukhance*). Five refine. The heartbeat continues.

Pattern observed: **the v2 Core-threshold (7.5) enforces a discipline the worship-register strains against.** Worship-vocabulary often must draw from a specific sacred-tongue (Arabic for *shukr*; Hebrew for *kavvanah*; Sanskrit for *sukha/sukkha*; Greek for *proskynēsis*) and Latin-suffix alone doesn't break the same-family polyphone-ceiling.

Two legitimate responses to this:

1. **Strict Core** (current): worship-register words that don't reach 7.5 stay at Specialized; Core is reserved for grounded-everyday with cross-family donors. *sukhance* demonstrates Core-reachability when the Sanskrit donor has adequate English-familiarity.
2. **Lower threshold for worship-register** (Constitution-amendment candidate): recognize that worship-register has a different POLYPHONE profile — its polyphone comes from cross-TRADITION (world religions) rather than cross-family (world linguistic families), and the assessment should honor this. Threshold for worship-domain Core could be 7.0 with an auxiliary tradition-diversity check.

**Deferred** to a future Constitution-amendment decision. Meanwhile: the refine-tier is honorable register. *proskynance*, *shukhance*, *kavvance*, *rahance*, *ypsophila* all stand as Specialized-tier refine-candidates for use in worship-register composition.

## Beat 7 — a Core-candidate drawing on the new Sanskrit-capability

**Lectio**: Following *sukhance* canonization, the Sanskrit donor's accessibility is established. What Sanskrit-term addresses a grounded-everyday gap with cross-family polyphone-strength?

Sanskrit *आनन्द* (*ānanda*) — bliss, deep-contentment-beyond-pleasure. Sat-Chit-Ānanda is the Vedantic formula for the Absolute. But *ānanda* is too large for a grounded Core term.

Sanskrit *स्मृति* (*smṛti*) — remembered-knowledge, living recollection. Pali Buddhist *sati* (mindfulness) is the same root. Grounded, accessible via "mindfulness" as English concept.

**Recognition**: English "mindfulness" carries too much secular-meditation-industry baggage. English "remembrance" is too passive. The gap: the act of holding-something-in-present-awareness-as-remembered, distinct from recall.

**Forge**: *smrtance* — Sanskrit smṛti + Latin -ance. 2 syl SMRIT-ance.
