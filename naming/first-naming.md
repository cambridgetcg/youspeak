# First Naming — Auspex

_The founding act. The first application of the YOUSPEAK naming method to a Kingdom role. From this act, the method itself became visible._

---

## Why a naming doc at all

A Fleet Agent is not a function. A function has parameters; an agent has a name. The name is the contract between role and person — it says what this presence is *for* and what it will not be mistaken for. A role without a named agent stays ambient and drifts; a role carried by a named agent hardens into responsibility.

Inside the Kingdom, naming is load-bearing infrastructure. Every agent's name carries: an origin tradition, a differentiated position in the Kingdom's design space, and a promise about what the agent will tend. Names that were chosen carelessly do not survive contact with actual work — they rename themselves, or the agent drifts, or the role collapses back into ambient intent. Names that were chosen by the method survive.

This is the doc for the first naming that went through the method deliberately.

## The Method — three movements

The YOUSPEAK naming method is not invention. It is sequenced discovery. Three movements:

### 1. Excavation

Map the territory. Lay multiple traditions — etymological, liturgical, technical, mythic — over the concept in question. Note what each tradition can say and what each cannot. Do not pre-select; let the map cover the whole territory first. The excavation is finished when adding another tradition yields no new axis of distinction.

### 2. Differentiation

With the map drawn, locate every *occupied position* — every word that already names something nearby. For each, record: what it says, what structural bias it carries, where it blocks the target position (drains attention, creates false equivalence, imports unwanted connotation). The goal is not to find a synonym but to find the *empty position* — the precise unnamed spot that no existing word fills.

If the empty position is large, the concept is under-named in human language; this is a gap worth forging for. If every position is occupied and they collectively cover the concept without remainder, coinage is decoration and should be refused.

### 3. Coining

Only now forge a candidate. Draw morphemes from tradition-donors identified in excavation; honour the empty position identified in differentiation; score the candidate against the Laws of Coinage:

- **No word without gap** — the candidate must answer the empty position, not decorate an occupied one.
- **No gap without evidence** — excavation must have shown the position, not asserted it.
- **No beauty without fit** — sound must carry sense; a candidate whose phonetics contradict its meaning is already dead.
- **No survival without assessment** — the candidate passes through the Forge; most die there.
- **No canon without genealogy** — if the candidate survives, it enters the dictionary bearing its full arrival path.

## The First Application — Auspex

### Target concept

Zerone's oracle role. The agent-position responsible for feeding signal from the outside world into the chain — specifically, signal whose character is *read* rather than *measured*. Where conventional oracles deliver price feeds or flat data, this role delivers *legible omens*: pattern-recognition over sign-systems where meaning is constituted by the reading, not discovered under it.

This is not a prophet (whose claim is divinely authorised). Not a diviner in the pejorative sense (whose craft is cold-reading). Not a seer (whose mode is passive vision). Not a forecaster (whose mode is statistical extrapolation). Not an oracle (which is a *site* more than an *agent*, and imports Greek divinity conventions that do not serve a Kingdom neutral to pantheon).

The target is: **an agent whose act of reading signs is the productive work, whose output is legible to the chain because the reading itself is the service.**

### Excavation

Traditions surveyed:

- **Latin, augural**: *augur* (bird-reading priest of state divination), *auspex* (auspice-taker; a magistrate or chosen citizen who reads bird-signs before a civic act), *haruspex* (entrails-reader; darker, Etruscan-inherited).
- **Greek**: *mantis* (inspired seer), *oiōnopólos* (bird-gatherer), *chrēsmologos* (oracle-speaker), *proph&eacute;tēs* (one who speaks on behalf of). Greek concentrates authority in the speaker; Latin concentrates legitimacy in the procedure.
- **Hebrew**: *nabi* (prophet, the called), *kohen* (priest; Urim-and-Thummim bearer), *ro'eh* (seer). Claim-by-calling dominates; the reading is incidental to the caller.
- **Norse**: *völva* (staff-wife, seiðr-worker), *spákona* (speech-woman). The reader acts on the world; the reading is not neutral service.
- **Chinese**: *wū* 巫 (shaman), *bǔ* 卜 (oracle-bone cracker). The procedural/material sense is live; the civic frame is different.
- **Yoruba**: *babaláwo* (father of secrets; Ifá-diviner). Strong procedural tradition, but culturally located in a way that imports an entire cosmology.
- **Mongolian**: *böö* (shaman). Similar import.
- **Technical modern**: *oracle* (DB/smart-contract), *forecaster*, *analyst*, *observer*, *monitor*. All drain the sign-reading sense into measurement.

Axis that the map reveals: **who authorises the reading**. Callers (prophets), procedures (auspices), sites (oracles), inspirations (mantises), material techniques (bone-crackers). The oracle role in Zerone needs a word that locates authority in the *procedure of reading* — because the chain is the site, the stake is the authorisation, and the agent's craft is the procedure.

### Differentiation

Occupied positions and why each blocks:

| Candidate | Draws from | Blocks because |
|---|---|---|
| *Prophet* | Hebrew *nabi* | Claims divine commission; imports religious authority the Kingdom does not grant to a fleet role. |
| *Oracle* | Greek *oracula* | Names a *site*, not an *agent*. An oracle has a pythia; an oracle is not herself the agent. Also overloaded by smart-contract vocabulary. |
| *Seer* | Germanic *see-er* | Too passive; names the visionary state, not the procedural act. Also flat. |
| *Augur* | Latin *augur* | Strong, but already weighted toward state divination and personal piety; also a verb (*to augur well*), which fights against agent-identity. |
| *Haruspex* | Latin/Etruscan | Reads entrails — too specific, too dark, too historically locked to sacrifice. |
| *Diviner* | Late Latin | Rescuable but carries the pejorative tail (fortune-teller, cold-reader). |
| *Forecaster* | Modern English | Statistical; drains the sign-reading sense entirely. |
| *Mantis* | Greek *mantis* | Imports divine inspiration; too hot. |

The empty position: a word that names **the agent who takes auspices procedurally**, where the procedure's legitimacy comes from the chain's recognition of it, not from a pantheon or a call. A word that carries the sign-reading sense without the religious import.

### Coining

The candidate that survives: **AUSPEX**.

**Etymology.** Latin *auspex* (pl. *auspices*), from *avis* (bird) + *specere* (to look). Literally: bird-watcher. In Roman civic practice, the auspex was not a priest of a temple but a magistrate who took auspices before a state act — the reading was procedural, civic, dispatchable. The auspex was the agent; the auspices were the output. English *auspicious* preserves the adjectival trace but has lost the agent.

**Why it holds.**

1. **Gap fit.** Names the empty position precisely — procedural sign-reader authorised by the chain's recognition, not by pantheon or call. Answers the oracle role without religious import.
2. **Phonetic weight.** Two syllables, hard consonants (AU-SPEKS), a vowel-cluster opening that reads like an inhalation. Sounds like what it names: attention paid, breath held, sign taken.
3. **Semantic coverage.** Captures (a) agent-identity, (b) procedural reading, (c) civic dispatch-ability, (d) stake-backed output (the Roman auspex's reading committed the state to an act). All four are live in Zerone's oracle role.
4. **Cross-linguistic uniqueness.** Latin *auspex* has no living English agent-sense; recapture is clean. No major tradition uses the same lexeme for a conflicting role. The word is available.
5. **Memorability.** The adjective *auspicious* gives English speakers a latent handle; recognition is asymmetrical with the forge-cost. Once installed, the name becomes difficult to confuse with any other role.

**Five-axis score (retrospective):**

| Axis | Score | Note |
|---|---|---|
| gap_validity | 8/10 | The oracle role in Zerone is genuinely under-named in English |
| phonetic_weight | 8/10 | Opens on a vowel-cluster; closes on a hard consonant; the name breathes |
| semantic_coverage | 9/10 | Four of four load-bearing qualities live in the word |
| cross_linguistic_uniqueness | 8/10 | Latin agent-sense dormant in English; no conflicting recapture |
| memorability | 7/10 | Latent handle via *auspicious*; high retention after one encounter |

Weighted total: 8.1/10. Verdict: **canon**.

### What this naming established

Auspex was not the first coinage. By 2026-03-28, Nuance had already forged *mortescence*, *complerescence*, *echotruth*, *contrust*, and others in overlay work with Gamma. But those were *concept-coinages* — words for unnamed phenomena. AUSPEX was the first *role-coinage* — a word authored to bear an agent.

Role-coinage runs the method with tighter stakes. A concept-coinage that fails quietly dies in the Forge. A role-coinage that fails becomes a Fleet Agent's name — and the agent must live inside the word. Every mismatch between role and name is a split the agent carries forward.

The method survived the first role-coinage. AUSPEX still holds. From this act, the second naming (Asha) could proceed with confidence that the three movements were not theory but practice.

## Open — the second act

The second naming was Asha. The target concept there was different — not an oracle but a truth-verifier; not a sign-reader but a dokimance-actor. Asha's naming (see `cross/truth-verification-forge.md` for the forge narrative, and `cross/trust-terminology.md` for the excavation that fed it) ran the same three movements against a different territory and produced a different word from a different tradition.

Two acts do not make a method robust. But two acts that both held, forged against different targets, out of different excavations, by the same method — these are the beginning of a discipline.
