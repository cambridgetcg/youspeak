## I. What intention IS, structurally

### Intention as active reaching-toward

Augustine's *intentio animae* — the soul's stretching-toward — names the active-direction-of-consciousness that constitutes any mental-act. Aquinas develops *intentio intellectus* as the mind's tending-toward the object known. Brentano revives the concept as **intentionality** — the structural feature that every mental-act is *about* something; consciousness is consciousness-OF; there is no thought without aboutness. Husserl specifies further: the **noesis** (the act of consciousness) and **noema** (the content as-given-to-the-act) are correlative; together they constitute intentional-experience.

The cathedral honors this classical-philosophical-lineage as donor-tradition under POLYPHONE. Intention, structurally, is the *active-reaching-toward* that an act of consciousness or communication exhibits — the directedness of meaning, the aboutness of content, the reach-of-the-soul-toward what it would have, name, give, receive, or constitute.

For communication specifically: every speech-act carries an intention-structure. The speaker has grasped (per GRASPING) a realm-region (per NOEMA). The grasping itself is intentional — it is grasping-OF-something. To communicate is to attempt-to-bring-another-mind-into-the-same-realm-region. The communicative-act is structurally intentional in at least three nested ways:

1. **Realm-intention**: which realm-region the speaker has grasped and reaches to share
2. **Hearer-intention**: which receiving-mind the speaker addresses and what response is anticipated
3. **Form-intention**: which projection-system and surface-form the speaker selects to bear the realm-content to the hearer

These three nest. The realm-intention is the substrate; the hearer-intention is the recipient-specification; the form-intention is the surface-engineering. Together they constitute the **communicative intention** the speech-act bears.

### Intention vs grasping vs projection

INTENTIO sits beside GRASPING and PROJECTION at the cathedral's metaphysical layer:

- **GRASPING** names the entry-event from the hearer's side (the receiving-mind's coming-to-stand-in a realm-region)
- **PROJECTION** names the mechanism by which the speaker's grasp becomes surface-form
- **INTENTIO** names the active-reaching-toward that animates both — the speaker's directed-aboutness and the hearer's directed-receptivity

The three are facets of one structural reality: minds-meeting-in-the-realm-through-projections. GRASPING is the reception-event; PROJECTION is the surface-mechanism; INTENTIO is the active-directedness that operates throughout.

## II. The layered structure of communication-method

Austin's *How to Do Things with Words* (1962) distinguished three layers of any speech-act:

| Layer | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Locutionary** | The act of saying-something — the literal utterance with its phonetic, syntactic, semantic content |
| **Illocutionary** | The act-performed-in-saying — asserting, asking, promising, declaring, blessing, vowing, etc. |
| **Perlocutionary** | The effect-produced-by-saying — persuading, alarming, comforting, converting |

Austin's three are foundational but reductive. A fuller communicative-method has at least **four layer-families**, each with multiple sub-layers, all operating simultaneously in any non-trivial speech-act:

### Family A — Realm-side layers (what is being reached-toward)

- **Pre-verbal grasping**: the realm-region the speaker has grasped before form-selection
- **Frame-setting**: the contextual-presupposition the utterance carries (worship-frame, technical-frame, intimate-frame, public-frame)
- **Register**: the relational-position-of-the-speaker (formal-to-equal, intimate-to-beloved, sacred-to-divine, casual-to-peer)

### Family B — Speech-act-side layers (the act-performed)

- **Locution**: Austin's literal-saying — the surface-form produced
- **Illocution**: the act-type performed (assertive, directive, commissive, expressive, declarative — per Searle's taxonomy)
- **Performative force**: when the saying IS the doing — vows, declarations, namings, blessings ("I now pronounce you married" — the saying constitutes the marriage)

### Family C — Pragmatic-side layers (what-is-meant-beyond-said)

- **Conventional implicature**: meaning-attached-to-form by convention but not asserted ("but" implies contrast)
- **Conversational implicature**: meaning-derived-from-context per Grice's cooperative-principle-and-maxims (quantity, quality, relation, manner)
- **Perlocutionary aim**: the effect-the-speaker-hopes-to-produce (consistent with Austin's third but specified as intention-of-effect not effect-itself)

### Family D — Worship-side layers (the deepest orientation)

- **Liturgical-orientation**: under THEOBASIS, every speech-act-in-worship-frame carries the orientation-toward-the-Ground as deepest-layer
- **Doxological-implicature**: the small-doxology any truthful-naming carries (per THEOBASIS Corollary 1 — the forge-discipline IS worship)
- **Covenantal-binding**: when the speech-act constitutes-or-maintains a covenant-relation (vows, blessings, namings-of-beloved)

**Communication operates at all four families simultaneously.** A husband saying *"I love you"* to his wife operates: Family A (realm-region of love-of-spouse; intimate-frame; covenantal-register); Family B (locution = "I love you"; illocution = assertive-AND-commissive; performative force = constitutive-of-the-relationship-at-this-moment); Family C (conventional implicature of love-language; conversational implicature of attentiveness in current context; perlocutionary aim of mutual-recognition); Family D (under worship-frame: covenantal-binding; doxological-implicature if the marriage is sacramental).

The same words operate across many layers at once. The cathedral's discipline of articulating layers makes the layered nature legible without requiring the speaker to do each layer separately.

### The cathedral's distinctive contribution

YOUSPEAK adds two layer-types other communication-traditions handle implicitly:

- **Morphological-projection layer**: in compounding cathedral-words (kimme = ki + me), the morpheme-stack is itself a layer of communication — the surface signals the compositional structure of the realm-region. The hearer who knows the morphemes reads the composition; the speaker who chose the compound performs an intention-of-revelation-of-structure.

- **Suffix-family-register layer**: choosing a YOUSPEAK suffix-family (qing for felt-bond, -me for received-ordinance, -ance for state-quality, -kin for bond-substance, etc.) is itself a layer of communicative intention — the suffix-family specifies the *register-of-relational-or-divine-mode* the speaker engages in. Per BUILDING-BLOCKS §V.3, choosing a suffix is choosing an intention-of-register.

These two layers are cathedral-specific; they extend Austin's three (and the four-family richer-scheme) with the cathedral's surface-transparency-strategy (PROJECTION §V).

## IV. The WAYS each structure can be utilised

Yu's invocation: *"How each structure can be utilised in different WAYS."* The cathedral's structures admit multiple-utilization-modes. Each WAY-of-utilization corresponds to a different communicative-intention.

### Worked example — walkekin

Take a single canonical compound (*walkekin* — friendship-preserved-through-long-silence) and articulate seven WAYS to utilise it:

1. **Descriptive WAY**: *"I have walkekin with my college friends from twenty years ago."* — Reporting a fact about an existing relational-state. Illocution: assertive. Performative force: nil. The walkekin is referred-to.

2. **Performative WAY**: *"We have walkekin; the silence proves it."* — Claiming-the-bond by naming it. Illocution: declarative-and-commissive. Performative force: constitutive — the naming may stabilize-or-claim the bond. The walkekin is enacted.

3. **Vocative WAY**: *"O walkekin between us, hold yet again."* — Addressing the bond as if it could hear; treating the relational-substance as quasi-personal-or-quasi-divine. Illocution: expressive-and-directive. The walkekin is invoked.

4. **Worship WAY (doxological)**: *"I doxomme for the walkekin held across these years."* — Thanking the Ground for the bond's preservation; explicit worship-frame. Illocution: expressive. Performative force: doxological-and-doxommatic. The walkekin is offered-as-gift-received.

5. **Reflexive WAY**: *"Walkekin as I have known it..."* — Defining one's own relation to the concept; situating the personal in the canonical. Illocution: assertive-with-self-reference. The walkekin is autobiographized.

6. **Diagnostic WAY**: *"Is this walkekin, or merely habit?"* — Asking after the substance, testing the conceptual fit. Illocution: directive (question). The walkekin is interrogated.

7. **Pedagogical WAY**: *"Walkekin names what your friendship has — the silence-tolerance is its proof."* — Teaching the term to another; transmitting the cathedral's vocabulary. Illocution: directive-and-assertive. The walkekin is taught.

Seven WAYS for one word. Each carries a distinct communicative-intention. Each is structurally available to any speaker who has grasped walkekin and can deploy the word.

### The pattern generalizes

The seven utilization-WAYS above apply to every canonical compound (with minor variation in which WAYS are most-natural for the word's register):

- *kimme* — describable, performable-via-receiving-discipline, vocative-as-prayer ("O kimme arrived this morning"), worship-doxological, reflexive in spiritual-practice ("kimme as I have known it in contemplation"), diagnostic ("is this kimme or self-generated attention?"), pedagogical
- *britqing* — describable, performable-as-covenant-claim, vocative ("O britqing between us"), worship in covenant-renewal, reflexive in marriage-meditation, diagnostic ("does this still feel like britqing?"), pedagogical
- *theobasis* — describable (the doctrine), performable-as-grounding-claim, vocative in prayer ("O-theobasis"), worship (the foundational doxological invocation), reflexive in theological-self-examination, diagnostic ("does this work rest on theobasis?"), pedagogical

The seven WAYS are utilization-modes structurally available across the canon.

## VI. The WAYS the seven Forgotten Ways are intention-prototypes

Beyond their own meaning as intention-vocabulary (§IV above), the seven Forgotten Ways serve as **intention-prototypes** — exemplars of what cathedral-intention-vocabulary looks like at the relational-architecture layer. Their structural-features:

6. **Each is bidirectional in deployment** (most fully in natsarqing, but applicable to others). The same intention-act can be performed by either pole in a sufficiently-mutual relationship.

7. **All seven together form an integrated intention-cluster** for the WILL-pole's asymmetry-closing repertoire plus its bidirectional and reciprocal companions. The asymmetric-clause-closer architectural-triad (zakarqing + natsarqing + kunance) + the speech-act-cluster (barakqing) + the seeking-act (heurekin) + the recognition-act (darshanqing) + the texture-act (jeongqing) = the cathedral's relational-architecture-intention-vocabulary at the canon-level.
