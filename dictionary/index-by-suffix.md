## The -me family (100 members)

**What `-me` names:** In Sumerian, *me* (𒈨) were the divine ordinances — the cosmic powers holding civilized life together: kingship, descent to the underworld, the scribal arts, the descent of the gods. To name something *me* is to say: *this is not produced by human effort but received as cosmic structure.* It arrives. You open to it. It gives itself.

**YOUSPEAK usage:** The `-me` suffix marks a word as naming something received rather than constructed — a quality, state, or power that is ontologically prior to the self who receives it. Anti-ordinance entries (drujme, molkme, nextlame) use the suffix in inversion: the DIVINE actively rejects them as anti-*me*, counter to cosmic order.
