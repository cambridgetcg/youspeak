# Embedding-geometry — the sense lives in the place and the direction

## The structure in compact form

When meaning is learned from the statistics of relation — which items keep company with which — it organizes itself as **geometry**: each item becomes a point (a vector) in a high-dimensional space, and three regularities appear and recur:

| Geometric fact | Semantic content |
|---|---|
| **Proximity** — small distance between vectors | similarity of meaning (synonyms cluster; antonyms are near but oppositely-signed) |
| **Direction / offset** — a shared displacement vector | a relation, applied analogically: v(king) − v(man) + v(woman) ≈ v(queen) |
| **Linear subspaces** — concepts as directions | a single feature is a direction; the linear representation hypothesis (Park, Choe & Veitch 2024) makes this precise |
| **Superposition** — more features than dimensions, packed near-orthogonally | one direction can carry many concepts; polysemy as geometric overlap (Elhage et al. 2022; geometric structures: digons, pentagons, polytopes) |

The load-bearing claim is not "a space is a useful picture of meaning." It is stronger: **the geometry IS the meaning's organization** — the position and the direction *are* the semantic facts, not a diagram of them. Distance is not like similarity; in such a space, distance *is* similarity. Offset is not like relation; offset *is* relation.

## Why this is realm-content, not arbitrary modeling

The distributional hypothesis — Firth's "a word is characterized by the company it keeps" (1957), Harris's distributional structure (1954) — was a linguistic conjecture about meaning. word2vec (Mikolov et al. 2013) discovered, unexpectedly, that a model trained only to predict context-words *also* lays meaning out so that analogies become vector arithmetic. The model was not optimized to solve `king : man :: queen : woman`; the parallelogram fell out of the geometry. This is the signature of realm-content rather than artifact: **the structure was not designed in; it was found.**

The convergence is cross-substrate, which is what qualifies it for mathema under DUALWAY:

- **Artificial transformers** represent features as directions in the residual stream; the linear representation hypothesis and interpretability's "superposition" both describe a *geometric* organization the network arrived at on its own.
- **The mammalian brain** organizes non-spatial conceptual knowledge with the *same hexagonal grid-code* the entorhinal cortex uses for physical space (Constantinescu, O'Reilly & Behrens 2016, *Science*) — concepts navigated as if they sat in a space.
- **Distributional-semantic models** and **Gärdenfors's conceptual spaces** (2000, *The Geometry of Thought*) independently formalize concepts as regions in a quality-dimension space where distance and direction carry sense.

Three cognition-classes — engineered, biological, and the linguistics/philosophy disciplines that theorized it — reach the same structural fact: meaning, learned from relation, is geometric. Under THE-REALM and NOEMA the cathedral already holds that meanings inhabit a higher-dimensional realm into which tongues project at angles; embedding-geometry is the **decodable, measurable witness** that the realm's structure is geometric — the math-side confirmation of the cathedral's own ontology.

## Cross-tradition resonance

| Witness | Era | The geometric claim it makes |
|---|---|---|
| Saussure (structuralism) | 1916 | *valeur*: a sign's meaning is its differential position relative to all others in the system — meaning as place, not substance |
| Harris / Firth (distributional linguistics) | 1954/1957 | meaning is fixed by distribution over contexts — company-kept as coordinates |
| Gärdenfors (conceptual spaces) | 2000 | concepts are convex regions in geometric quality-spaces; betweenness and distance are semantic |
| Mikolov et al. (word2vec) | 2013 | relations are constant offset-vectors; analogy is parallelogram-completion |
| Constantinescu, O'Reilly & Behrens | 2016 | the brain runs a grid-code over *conceptual* space, not only physical space |
| Elhage et al. (superposition) | 2022 | features pack as near-orthogonal directions; polytope geometry; polysemy is overlap |
| Park, Choe & Veitch (LRH) | 2024 | high-level concepts are linear directions under a structure-respecting inner product |

These are not the same theory; they are independent angles on one structural fact. That independence is the convergence-evidence the forge needs.

## Invariance

Embedding-geometry is **not** an eternal invariant in the sense π is. Its specifics — dimensionality, which inner product, whether features are exactly linear — are contingent on architecture and on the corpus of relations a cognizer learns from. What *is* invariant is the qualitative structure: a system that learns meaning from relation-statistics, at scale, will organize meaning so that **similarity is proximity and relation is direction**. This holds across the artificial, the biological, and the formal-philosophical models above. Mathema admits it as a *precisely-specifiable donor with a defined (cross-substrate, not cross-cosmos) invariance-class* — exactly the standard the machine/ manifesto sets for architecture-bounded donors.
