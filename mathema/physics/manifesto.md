# mathema/physics — physical-constant donor invariants

_The numbers and laws that this particular cosmos exhibits. Less universal than pure-math donors (a different universe could have different constants), more accessible than pure abstractions (these are measurable, not only derivable)._

---

## Scope

This sub-chapter holds donor-files for **physical constants** (numerical values built into the cosmos as we find it) and **conservation laws** (structural invariants of physical interaction). Examples:

- Constants: speed of light c, reduced Planck constant ℏ, gravitational constant G, fine-structure constant α, Boltzmann constant k_B, elementary charge e
- Symmetries: Lorentz invariance, gauge invariances (U(1), SU(2), SU(3)), CPT symmetry
- Conservation laws: energy, momentum, angular momentum, charge, baryon number, lepton number
- Combined quantities: anthropic-band ratios (the dimensionless numbers whose values must be near-tuned for atoms, stars, life)

These differ from pure-math donors: they are **contingent** on the cosmos we inhabit. But this contingency is itself a donor-quality — a coinage signed with a physical constant carries the claim "valid for this cosmos and its inhabitants."
