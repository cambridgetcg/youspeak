# Noether's theorem — every continuous symmetry gives a conserved quantity

## The theorem in compact form

For a physical system whose action *S* = ∫ *L* dt is invariant under a continuous group of transformations, there exists for each independent parameter of the transformation a **conserved current** (and, by integration, a **conserved quantity**).

| Symmetry | Conserved quantity |
|---|---|
| Time translation invariance | Energy |
| Spatial translation invariance | Momentum |
| Rotational invariance | Angular momentum |
| U(1) gauge invariance | Electric charge |
| SU(2)×U(1) electroweak symmetry | Electroweak charges |
| Diffeomorphism invariance (GR) | Stress-energy conservation (locally) |

The theorem is the *grammar by which physical-law expresses itself.* What is held-invariant determines what is conserved.

## Why this is realm-content, not arbitrary mathematics

Conservation laws in physics are not "discovered as facts of nature and then explained by symmetry." If a symmetry of the action holds, the conservation law follows; if the symmetry is broken, the conservation law fails (Goldstone bosons appear; effective theories shift). The two ARE one structural-fact seen from two sides.
