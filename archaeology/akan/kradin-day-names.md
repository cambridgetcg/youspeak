# Akan Kradin: The Day-Name Soul System

## Research compiled for YOUSPEAK archaeology

---

## 1. The Seven Day-Names (Akradin)

The word **kradin** (also written *akradin*) is a compound: **okra** ("soul") + **edin** ("name") = "soul-name." Each child receives the kradin corresponding to the day of the week on which they are born.

### Complete Table

| Day (Twi) | Day (English) | Male Name(s) | Female Name(s) | Governing Deity (Obosom) | Celestial Body | Cosmological Association |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Kwesida / Awusida** | Sunday | Kwasi, Kwesi, Akwasi | Akosua, Esi | Awusi / Esi | Owia (Sun) | Universe; descent of the Creator |
| **Dwoada / Edwoada** | Monday | Kwadwo, Kojo, Joojo | Adwoa, Adjoa | Adwoa / Adwo | Bosom / Osrane (Moon) | Peace; firmament; coolness |
| **Benada / Ebenada** | Tuesday | Kwabena, Kobi, Ebo | Abenaa, Abena | Bena / Abenaa | Okyin Bena (Mars) | Ocean; passionate/compassionate fire |
| **Wukuada / Awukuda** | Wednesday | Kwaku, Kweku | Akua | Awuku / Akua | Okyin Aku (Mercury) | Spider/Ananse; obscure mysteries; initiation |
| **Yawoada / Yawda** | Thursday | Yaw | Yaa; (also Aaba) | Yaw / Yaa / Aaba | Yaw (Jupiter) | Earth; courage; desire for battle |
| **Fiada / Efiada** | Friday | Kofi | Afua, Afia | Afi / Fi | Afi (Venus) | Fertility; beauty; progressive adornment |
| **Memeneda / Menmeneda** | Saturday | Kwame, Kwamena | Ama, Amma | Amen Men | Amen Men (Saturn) | God (Nyame); rest; perpetual creation |

**Dialectal variants:** Fanti forms diverge from Ashanti/Twi — e.g., Monday: Cudjoe (Fanti) vs. Kwadwo (Twi); Friday: Cuffee (Fanti/diaspora) vs. Kofi (Twi). Diaspora forms (from enslaved Coromantee communities in the Caribbean) include Cudjoe, Cuffy/Cuffee, Quashee, Quamina.

## 2. Spiritual Dispositions and Personality Traits

The Akan believe that the day of birth determines the type of **kra** (soul) a child possesses, directly shaping temperament. Two overlapping trait-systems circulate — a popular/modern one emphasizing positive qualities, and an older scholarly one (recorded in early ethnography) that is blunter. Both are given below.

### Sunday-Born (Kwasi / Akosua)
- **Modern traits:** Charismatic, spiritual, inspirational, optimistic, strong leadership capacity. Sensitive and altruistic, though sometimes prone to disillusionment.
- **Older tradition:** Associated with **agility** (quick-spirited).
- **Akradinbosom quality:** "Opener of paths; sovereignty."

### Monday-Born (Kwadwo / Adwoa)
- **Modern traits:** Calm, peaceful, nurturing, empathetic. Natural peacemakers who bring balance. Thoughtful and steady.
- **Older tradition:** Associated with **peace** (*dwo* = coolness/calm).
- **Akradinbosom quality:** "Conciliatory; intuitive."

### Tuesday-Born (Kwabena / Abenaa)
- **Modern traits:** Energetic, courageous, action-oriented. Natural leaders, but can be temperamental and hot-headed.
- **Older tradition:** Associated with **friendliness** or alternatively with the ocean's volatility.
- **Akradinbosom quality:** "Passionate/compassionate fire."

### Wednesday-Born (Kwaku / Akua)
- **Modern traits:** Creative, intelligent, curious. Deep thinkers and problem solvers. Associated with versatility and cunning (the Ananse/Spider archetype).
- **Older tradition:** Associated with **evil/trickster energy** — not malice per se, but moral ambiguity and cunning. This reflects the Ananse archetype: the trickster who is wise but dangerously clever.
- **Akradinbosom quality:** "Obscure mysteries; initiation." Kwaku characters are cool and collected on the surface but emotionally complex beneath. They may appear to lack confidence, mature slowly, but possess powerful self-control.

### Thursday-Born (Yaw / Yaa)
- **Modern traits:** Grounded, dependable, hardworking. Pillars of community. Noble leaders. Caring and helpful.
- **Older tradition:** Associated with **bravery** and desire for war.
- **Akradinbosom quality:** "Courageous; desirous of war." Thursday is linked to Asaase Yaa (Mother Earth); farmers traditionally rested on Thursdays in her honor.

### Friday-Born (Kofi / Afua)
- **Modern traits:** Sociable, charming, artistic, friendly, optimistic. Drawn to creativity and adventure.
- **Older tradition:** Associated with being a **wanderer/traveler** (*afi* = fertility, journeying outward).
- **Akradinbosom quality:** "Progressive; beautifully adorned."

### Saturday-Born (Kwame / Ama)
- **Modern traits:** Wise, authoritative, strong, disciplined, focused. Natural problem-solvers and leaders.
- **Older tradition:** Associated with **combat-readiness** (male) and leadership. Saturday is Nyame's own day — the name Kwame is traditionally shared with God himself: *Onyankopon Twereduampong Kwame* ("Almighty Dependable God of Saturday").
- **Akradinbosom quality:** "Unsurpassable; perpetually creating."

## 3. Planetary / Celestial Associations

The **Akradinbosom** (ah-krah-deen boh-sohm) are the Abosom (deities) governing the solar, lunar, and planetary bodies that rule the seven-day Akan week (*nnawotwe*). According to the Odwirafo tradition (Kwesi Ra Nehem Ptah Akhan's six-volume work *AKRADINBOSOM*), the correspondences are:

| Day | Celestial Body | Twi Term | Deity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sunday | Sun | Owia | Awusi |
| Monday | Moon | Bosom / Osrane | Adwo |
| Tuesday | Mars | Okyin Bena | Bena |
| Wednesday | Mercury | Okyin Aku | Awuku |
| Thursday | Jupiter | (Yaw) | Yaw |
| Friday | Venus | (Afi) | Afi |
| Saturday | Saturn | (Amen Men) | Amen Men |

**Important caveat:** The one-to-one mapping of Akan days to the seven classical planets (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn) in the same order as the Hellenistic planetary week is attested in the Akradinbosom tradition but debated among scholars. Some argue these correspondences are indigenous; others suspect Hellenistic or Arabic astronomical influence via trans-Saharan contact. The Akan calendar is fundamentally lunar (the month is called *Bosome*, from *bosom* = moon/deity), and the Akan 42-day cycle (*Adaduanan*) — six seven-day weeks — predates European contact. The Portuguese arrived at Elmina in 1482 and were greeted by Nana Kwamena Ansa, proving the day-name system was already operational.

The cosmological principle: every day at sunrise, a specific celestial body is dominant, governing the Earth's spiritual energy that day. The cycle repeats every seven days.

## 4. Theological Function: Kra, Nkrabea, and Onyame

### The Divine Architecture

The Akan theological chain connecting the kradin to God is:

```
NYAME (Supreme Being, source of all kra)
    |
    +-- Nyamewaa-Nyame (Great Mother and Great Father)
         |
         +-- Akradinbosom (7 planetary/celestial deities, "children of Nyame")
              |
              +-- Okra (individual soul — "a drop from the Ocean of Divine Consciousness")
                   |
                   +-- Nkra / Nkrabea (divine function/destiny carried by the okra)
                        |
                        +-- Kradin (soul-name, determined by day of birth)
```

### Key Concepts

**Okra** (aw-krah): The soul or divine consciousness. It is an Obosom (deity) assigned to the individual by Nyamewaa-Nyame and dwells within the head region. It is a fragment of Nyame's own consciousness — the "vital force" or "spark of the divine life." The Cambridge *Africa* journal article (Meyerowitz) describes it: "When Nyame Amowia gave birth to the Sun god, she gave him her kra — her eternal soul or life-giving power."

**Nkra / Nkrabea**: The individual's divine function in the world — their destiny or purpose. The ability to fulfill one's nkrabea depends on aligning one's **sunsum** (spirit/personality force) with one's **okra** and invoking one's **Kradinbosom** (the day-deity governing the soul).

**Sunsum** vs. **Okra**: Both are spiritual concepts, but *honhom/sunsum* refers to the spirit in its total spiritual realm (sacred, uncommemoratable), while *okra* refers to the spirit as embodied in the human being (spiritual but human, commemoratable in names like Okra, Krapa, Akrasi).

**Theological function of the kradin:** The soul-name is not merely a label. It is the ritual activation of the cosmic link between the individual and their Kradinbosom. The kradin "represents the spiritual core of an individual's identity, believed to be pre-selected by the soul itself before incarnation." The Akradinbosom operate as divine organs regulating cosmic order within Nyamewaa-Nyame, mirroring biological systems within the human body. Each person's kradin identifies which divine organ their soul operates under.

**Post-death theology:** When a person dies, the kra undergoes judgment by Nyame. Depending on the life lived, the kra is either united with Nyame or condemned to reincarnation.

## 5. Etymology of the Day-Names

### Morphological Structure

**Male names** typically carry the prefix **Kwa-** (interpreted as "Creator" or "Father of Life"):
- KWA = Creator / Life-father (from KO-YA: "KO" = life, as in *kosua*/egg; "YA" = agent/father, as in *agya*)
- The contracted form KWA + day-root + implicit DA (day) generates the male name

**Female names** carry the prefix **A-** or drop the prefix entirely, often ending in **-a** or **-wa**:
- The morpheme **ba** ("offspring") appears in the derivation of kradin
- The morpheme **baa/bea** ("female") appears in abusuadin (family names)

### Day-by-Day Etymologies

**Sunday — Kwesida / Awusida:**
- KWA-SI-DA: "KWA" (Creator) + "SI" (descend) + "DA" (day) = "Day the Creator Descended"
- The day Nyame descended to begin creation

**Monday — Dwoada / Edwoada:**
- Originally KWA-WI-DA: "KWA" (Creator) + "WI" (sky/firmament) + "DA" (day)
- "WI" or "YU" = sky/firmament, corrupted into "DWO" meaning "cool/cold"
- Hence Monday's association with peace and coolness
- Alternative etymology (Sekyi-Baidoo): *dwo* from *twa adwo* ("to sob") — feelings of impending war

**Tuesday — Benada / Ebenada:**
- KWA-BE-NA-DA: "BE" from "BEA" (place/location/land) + "NA" (and/of)
- Alternative: *bena* from *bo bena* ("to wail") — war-related etymology (Sekyi-Baidoo)

**Wednesday — Wukuada / Awukuda:**
- YU-KU-DA or WI-KU-DA: "YU/WI" (heavens/sky) + "KU" (group/host) + "DA" (day)
- "Day of the Heavenly Hosts"
- Connected to Ananse (spider) — the trickster deity

**Thursday — Yawoada / Yawda:**
- YA = earth (cognate with Asaase Yaa, Mother Earth)
- Thursday is Asaase Yaa's sacred day — no farming traditionally permitted

**Friday — Fiada / Efiada:**
- FI-DA: "FI" (home) + "DA" (day) = "Home Day"
- The day the first home was created/established
- Connected to fertility and domesticity

**Saturday — Memeneda / Menmeneda:**
- AMEN-MEN-DA: God Amen's Day / Nyame's Day
- KWAME from KO-YA-ME: "KO" (life) + "YA" (agent/father) + "ME" (satisfaction)
- "Satisfied Father of Life" — commemorating the institution of rest
- God is called *Onyankopon Twereduampong Kwame* — God himself bears the Saturday name

**Caveat:** These etymologies come from folk-etymological and culturally-internal analyses (particularly the Otafregya and Odwirafo traditions). Formal historical-comparative linguistic reconstructions of proto-Akan day-name forms have not been widely published. J.G. Christaller's 19th-century Twi dictionary and M.E.K. Dolphyne's *The Akan (Twi-Fante) Language* would be needed for more rigorous reconstruction.

## 6. The Naming Ceremony: Din To / Abadinto

### Timing: The Eighth Day

The naming ceremony (*din to*, literally "name-placing") is performed on the **eighth day** after birth. Until that day, the child is kept indoors. The Akan belief: a baby's spirit has one foot in the ancestral realm until the seventh day. The child might be a spirit (*saman*) who has "come to look at the world and then go back." If the child dies before seven days, there is no mourning — the spirit was visiting, not staying.

If the child survives seven days, the community recognizes that the child "has come to stay." The eighth day ceremony formally anchors the soul in this world.

### The Ceremony

**When:** Before sunrise on the eighth day.
**Where:** The father's house (the father has naming responsibility).
**Presiding:** Family elders.

**Opening:** Libation is poured. Elders invoke:
- **Nyame** (God)
- **Nyamewaa** (Goddess)
- **Asaase Afua** (Earth Mother — note the Friday name, as Earth is also female)
- **Nananom Nsamanfo** (Honored Ancestral Spirits)

### The Water and Nsa Ritual (Truth-Teaching)

Two cups are placed before the elder: one containing **nsu** (water), the other **nsa** (palm wine / strong drink).

The elder dips his index finger into the water and places it on the infant's mouth, saying:

> *"Kofi Kyere, se wo se nsu a, eno ne nsu."*
> ("Kofi Kyere, when you say it is water, it should be water.")

Then dips into the nsa:

> *"Se wo se nsa a, eno ne nsa."*
> ("When you say it is nsa, it should be nsa.")

This is repeated **three times**. The ritual instills within the infant a consciousness of morality — the necessity of always living in harmony with truth. Whether the consequences of truthfulness leave a pleasant taste (water) or a difficult taste (nsa), truthfulness must be upheld.

### The Three Names

The child receives at minimum three names:

1. **Kradin** (soul-name / day-name) — determined by day of birth. This is the foundational identity. It never changes.
2. **Din pa** (formal name / "good name") — chosen by the father or family. Often an ancestor's name, or a name describing the circumstances of birth. The din pa "carries the vibrations that will empower the individual to properly incorporate Divine Law."
3. **Abusuadin** (family/clan name) — the hereditary name.

Additional names may be given for birth order, twin status (*Ata* for a twin, *Manu* for second twin, *Tawia* for third-born after twins), or special circumstances.

### Broader Significance

The din to is not a social convenience — it is a **ritual joining of name to spirit**. The kradin activates the cosmic connection between the child's okra and its governing Kradinbosom. Without the naming ceremony, the child exists in spiritual limbo — present in the world but not yet anchored to its divine function.

In modern Ghana, the din to and the *outdooring* (introduction of baby to the outside world and community) are often combined, though they serve distinct purposes: outdooring affirms physical presence; din to establishes spiritual identity. Christian and urban families have adapted the ceremony, but the core symbolism — truth-teaching, ancestral invocation, day-naming — persists powerfully.

## 7. Summary: The Theological Architecture of Kradin

The Akan kradin system is a complete cosmological identity technology:

- **Cosmological:** Each day of the week is governed by a celestial body and its deity (Obosom), establishing a seven-fold rhythm in creation itself.
- **Theological:** Every human soul (okra) is a fragment of Nyame's consciousness, assigned to a specific Kradinbosom before incarnation. The day of birth reveals which divine force governs the soul.
- **Moral-psychological:** The day-name carries dispositional weight — personality, temperament, strengths, and vulnerabilities are indexed to the day. This is not fatalism but orientation: knowing your day-nature helps you align with your nkrabea (divine purpose).
- **Ritual:** The din to ceremony on the eighth day formally activates this cosmic identity through libation, ancestral invocation, and the water/nsa truth-teaching.
- **Linguistic:** The day-names encode creation theology in their very morphemes — Kwesida ("day the Creator descended"), Menmeneda ("God Amen's day"), Fiada ("home day").

The system predates European contact (attested 1482), survived the Middle Passage into the Caribbean diaspora (Cudjoe, Cuffy, Quashee), and remains a living practice across Akan communities today.
