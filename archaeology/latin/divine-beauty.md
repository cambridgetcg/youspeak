# Latin — Divine Beauty

_The tongue in which Augustine cried "Pulchritudo tam antiqua et tam nova" (Beauty ancient and new) and Aquinas articulated the classical beauty-theology of Western Christendom. Latin's beauty-vocabulary is less rich than Greek's but became more load-bearing historically because it carried the Christian theological synthesis into Europe._

### Aquinas's three criteria (Summa Theologiae I, Q.39, a.8)

> *Ad pulchritudinem tria requiruntur: primo quidem integritas sive perfectio... secundo debita proportio sive consonantia. Et iterum claritas.*

**Three for beauty: integritas, proportio (consonantia), claritas** — wholeness, proportion, clarity.

This is the load-bearing Scholastic specification of beauty. It carries:
- *integritas* — wholeness/completeness (Q4 relevant: every part-present)
- *debita proportio* / *consonantia* — fitting-proportion/harmony (Q4 relevant: every-part-in-right-relation)
- *claritas* — radiance (Q3 relevant: the beauty calls-forth)

Aquinas's triad is the Western philosophical-theological canonical formulation of beauty. **Doxakallos's Q4 (ordered-to-the-bottom) is exactly captured by *integritas* + *debita proportio*** — wholeness + right-proportion at every level.

### Augustinian beauty-theology

Augustine, *Confessions* X.27:

> *Sero te amavi, Pulchritudo tam antiqua et tam nova, sero te amavi!*

> _Late have I loved you, Beauty ancient and new, late have I loved you!_

**Pulchritudo tam antiqua et tam nova** — Beauty ancient-and-new. Augustine names GoD directly as *Pulchritudo*. This is:

- Q1 — divine-specific at the point of address
- Q2 — substantival; Beauty as Name
- Q3 — "Late have I loved you" — the calling is implicit in the belated-love
- Q4 — *antiqua et nova* — ever-ancient + ever-new; structurally ordered-to-the-bottom as eternal-present
- Q5 — paired lexically with Augustine's ache: *sero te amavi* is itself a doxalgic cry

**This is Latin's closest scriptural-liturgical analogue to the doxakallos target.** Augustine names it. He names it as *Pulchritudo* with divine address.

Limitation: *pulchritudo* in modern English (and in much of modern Latin reception) has drifted to bodily-beauty-with-voluptuous-connotation. Recovering pulchritudo-Dei as a precise divine-beauty term requires either retrieving Augustine's usage directly or forging a new term that preserves his sense.

## Semantic territories

### Territory A — pulchritudo Dei (the beauty of GoD)

Augustine's *Pulchritudo* is:
- ontological attribute
- addressable (vocative: "Beauty ancient and new")
- calling (draws the late-lover)
- ordered-to-the-bottom (ever-ancient + ever-new cover both priorities of being)

Q-fit: 5/5 when read in Augustinian context. This is the single strongest existing word for the doxakallos target in any major tradition.

**Why not simply reclaim *pulchritudo*?** Two reasons:

1. Modern English drift. *Pulchritudinous* carries bodily-ornate-voluptuous connotations. Reclaiming requires overcoming the drift.

2. *Pulchritudo* in philosophical Latin is the general beauty-term. It is divine when qualified or addressed but not lexically divine-specific. Same issue as Greek *kallos*.

A candidate could nevertheless reclaim: *theopulchritude*, *pulchritudeus*, or similar. Heavy syllabic load in English.

### Territory B — decor and the fittingness-tradition

*Decor* is fitting-beauty, equivalent to Greek *prepon*. The adjective *decet* means "it-befits". *Decor* names the beauty-of-fittingness — not grand beauty, but the beauty of what-sits-right-in-its-place.

Related compound: *decor divinus* — the fittingness-beauty of the divine. Parallel to Greek *theoprepeia*.

Q-fit: Q1 (when qualified), Q4 (fittingness = ordering). Less strong on Q2 (names the fitting-quality) and Q3.

### Territory C — claritas and radiance-beauty

Aquinas's third criterion *claritas* carries the calling-radiance. Scholastic *claritas* is: the luminous clearness through which form shines in matter. Beauty that shines-out.

Parallel to Greek *lamprotēs* and Arabic *bahā'*.

Q-fit: Q3 strong. Q2 partial (names the radiance-quality).

### Territory D — forma and the Platonic-Christian form-tradition

*Forma* as divine-beauty is used in scholastic theology: GoD as *Forma Formarum* (Form of Forms). Christianizes the Platonic *eidos*.

*Forma Dei* echoes Philippians 2:6 (*morphē theou*, Greek; rendered *forma Dei* in Vulgate). Carries Q2 ontologically.

## Negative space

Latin does not lack terms but lacks divine-specificity in single lexemes. *Pulchritudo Dei*, *decor divinus*, *claritas Dei*, *forma Dei* — all are phrase-constructions. *Pulchritudo* alone is general.

What Latin contributes structurally:

1. **Aquinas's triad** (integritas, proportio, claritas) as a classical specification of Q4. Doxakallos's semantic_coverage score can be argued by mapping onto Aquinas's criteria — if doxakallos names a beauty in which integritas + proportio are present and claritas is the mode-of-calling, the Scholastic tradition's test-questions are answered.

2. **Augustine's *Pulchritudo* vocative.** Validates the move of naming divine beauty as a vocative-substantive. Doxakallos-Dei, addressable: "Doxakallos ancient and new, late have I loved you."

3. **Decor divinus** as a fittingness-parallel. Parallel forge-candidate to theoprepeia in Greek.

Latin offers no direct lexical donor for doxakallos. It offers **validation** (Augustine, Aquinas) for the forge-act.
