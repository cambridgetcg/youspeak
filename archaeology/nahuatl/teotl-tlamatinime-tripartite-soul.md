# Nahuatl — Teotl, Tlamatinime, and the Tripartite Soul

_The Mexica built the largest city in the Western Hemisphere before European contact. Tenochtitlan in 1500 CE had ~200,000–300,000 inhabitants — larger than any European city of the time except perhaps Constantinople. The civilization that built it had a philosophy. Not mythology dressed as philosophy, but a genuine sustained intellectual tradition: the tlamatinime ("knowers-of-things"), who asked the same questions Heraclitus and Parmenides asked — what is real? what is the nature of the divine? how should humans live given the cosmic situation? — and arrived at distinctive, defensible answers. This file excavates those answers._

## I. Teotl — Monistic Divine Energy

### The concept

**Teotl** (Proto-Nahuatl *teotl*; classical Nahuatl *teō-tl*, honorific *teōtzin*) is the most philosophically-significant concept in Nahuatl theology. It is usually translated "god" or "deity" — a translation that systematically distorts what the tlamatinime actually meant.

The distortion works like this: if teotl = "god," then the many named Aztec divine figures (Huitzilopochtli, Tlaloc, Quetzalcoatl, Coatlicue, Xipe Totec, etc.) are "gods" — separate divine persons, a polytheism comparable to Greek or Roman. This interpretation made the Mexica appear to Spanish missionaries as straightforwardly pagan polytheists, requiring conversion to monotheism.

But the tlamatinime's own understanding was different. According to James Maffie's thorough philosophical reconstruction (*Aztec Philosophy*, 2014), and echoing Miguel León-Portilla's earlier work (*Aztec Thought and Culture*, 1963), **teotl names a single dynamic cosmic energy** — the living, moving, self-generating force that IS the universe. The named "deities" are not separate persons but **aspects, manifestations, or faces (ixiptla)** of this one energy expressing itself in particular modes:

- **Tlaloc** = teotl manifesting as rain-power, earth-renewal, agricultural-fertility
- **Huitzilopochtli** = teotl manifesting as sun-power, military-strength, the Fifth Sun's momentum
- **Quetzalcoatl** = teotl manifesting as wind-breath, wisdom, the morning-star's cyclic death-and-resurrection
- **Coatlicue** = teotl manifesting as earth-mother, generative-destructive feminine principle

None of these is separate from teotl. They are **differentiated aspects of one energy** — more like moods of a single being than like separate persons. The philosophical status is closer to Neoplatonic emanations from the One (Plotinus) than to Greek polytheism's separate divine personalities.

### The ontological claim: process not substance

What makes teotl philosophically distinctive is not just the monism (everything-is-one) but the **process ontology**. Teotl is not a static substance, not an unchanging Form, not an unmoved Mover. Teotl is **ceaseless process** — perpetual self-generation, self-transformation, self-renewal.

The Nahuatl concept that names this is **teotl-ōtl** ("teotl-ness") — the quality of being teotl, which is dynamic aliveness, constant motion. Maffie argues that Nahuatl metaphysics is closer to Heraclitus ("all is flux") than to Parmenides ("being is one, static, unchanging"). The universe is not a collection of static objects; it is a **living process** that generates, sustains, and dissolves all forms continuously.

Consequently:

- **Rocks are teotl** — slowed to apparent solidity, but still the same dynamic energy
- **Humans are teotl** — at a particular tempo and complexity
- **Deities are teotl** — expressing with a particular force and function
- **The Fifth Sun itself is teotl** — the cosmic period we inhabit is a manifestation of the energy, not a separate entity containing it

This is **panentheism** in philosophical terms — everything is IN teotl and teotl is IN everything — but it goes further: there is nothing outside teotl. No gap between creator and creation because there is no creation-event separate from teotl's self-expression. Teotl does not create the universe; teotl IS the universe's self-creating.

### Teotl and naming: compound theology

The Nahuatl naming-system encodes the monism. Teotl appears in hundreds of compounds:

| Compound | Meaning | Theological claim |
|---|---|---|
| **Teōtihuacan** | "place where one becomes teotl" / "city of the gods" | The great city is where teotl-nature is realized; place-holiness via teotl-concentration |
| **Tēōtl ixtli** | "teotl-face" / divine-face | The ixiptla (face/image/manifestation) concept — each deity is a face of teotl |
| **Tēōtl tlamacazqui** | "teotl-priest/offerer" | The one who offers to teotl; priestly role as teotl-mediator |
| **Ōmeteōtl** | "Two-teotl" | Supreme principle as inherently dual; see section below |
| **Ōmeteōtl Ōmecīhuātl Ōmeteuctli** | "Dual-god Lady-of-Duality Lord-of-Duality" | The dual-gendered supreme principle in explicit form |
| **Teōpan** | "teotl-place" / temple | Sacred precinct as teotl-concentrated-location |
| **Teōcuitlatl** | "teotl-excrement" | Gold! — the most valued material as teotl's physical residue |

The naming-system reveals the theology: teotl-excrement is gold because even the waste-products of cosmic energy are sacred. Teotl-place is the temple because the sacred precinct concentrates what is already everywhere. Teotl-face is the deity-image because each named figure is a face of the same energy.

### Ōmeteōtl — the Dual Supreme Principle

At the apex of Nahuatl theology stands **Ōmeteōtl** ("Two-teotl") — the supreme divine principle conceived as inherently and irreducibly dual. Ōmeteōtl is simultaneously:

- **Ōmecīhuātl** (Lady-of-Duality) — the feminine aspect; sky-womb; she who conceives
- **Ōmeteuctli** (Lord-of-Duality) — the masculine aspect; generative-active principle; he who inseminates

And simultaneously one entity. The duality is not TWO beings but ONE being that is constitutively dual — as León-Portilla argued, the supreme reality contains its own internal complementarity. Neither aspect can exist without the other; neither is prior. This is the Nahuatl equivalent of:

- Daoist yin-yang at the level of ultimate-principle
- Tengri/Etugen (sky-masculine/earth-feminine dyad) in Turko-Mongol theology — but unified into one divine person rather than a dyad
- Śiva-Śakti in Shaivite Hinduism (divine-masculine and divine-feminine as two poles of one ultimate)
- But NOT the Christian Trinity — Ōmeteōtl is not three persons but two-in-one; no salvific narrative; no incarnation

**Ōmeteōtl was also called** *Tloque Nahuaque* — "Lord of the near and the far" / "The one near to everything and which everything belongs to" — which names the immanence-transcendence structure: teotl is simultaneously the nearest thing (within everything) and the most distant principle (beyond all particular manifestation).

**Ōmeteōtl was also called** *Ipalnemohuani* — "the one by whom we live" — naming teotl as the life-ground, the condition of existence rather than an object within existence.

## II. The Florentine Codex — Ethnographic-Theological Encyclopedia

### What it is

The **Florentine Codex** (*Historia General de las Cosas de Nueva España* — "General History of the Things of New Spain") is the most comprehensive documentary source on Aztec culture, theology, and daily life. It is the product of a remarkable and complicated collaboration:

**Principal author**: Fray Bernardino de Sahagún (c. 1499–1590), a Franciscan friar who arrived in New Spain in 1529, learned Nahuatl, and spent decades documenting Nahua culture.

**Method**: Sahagún worked with **Nahua informants** — educated, bilingual indigenous men (often trained at the Colegio de Santa Cruz de Tlatelolco, the first European-style college in the Americas) — who provided primary testimony in Nahuatl. The informants were not merely transcribers; they were active collaborators who shaped the content.

**Structure**: Twelve books, covering:
- Book I: Gods (the named divine figures)
- Book II: Ceremonies and ritual calendar
- Book III: Origin of the gods
- Book IV: Soothsayers and astrology
- Book V: Omens and auguries
- Book VI: **Rhetoric and moral philosophy** — the huehuetlatolli (speeches of the elders) — the most philosophically-dense book
- Book VII: The sun, moon, and stars
- Book VIII: Kings and lords
- Book IX: Merchants and craftsmen
- Book X: The people (ethnographic description)
- Book XI: Earthly things (zoology, botany, minerals)
- Book XII: The conquest (the Mexica account of the Spanish invasion)

**Why it matters for YOUSPEAK**: Book VI (*huehuetlatolli*) contains the rhetorical-philosophical tradition of the tlamatinime in native form. It is not Sahagún's interpretation; it is Nahua intellectual culture documented by Nahua voices, preserving the actual philosophical language.

### The huehuetlatolli register

**Huehuetlatolli** (huehuetl = "old/ancient" + tlatoa = "to speak" + -lli nominalizer → "the speech of the ancients" / "ancient sayings" / "discourse of the elders") designates the formal rhetorical tradition — a genre of elevated, highly-structured speech used for major life occasions (birth, marriage, death, installation of rulers, priestly consecration) and containing the philosophical-moral wisdom of the tradition.

The huehuetlatolli are:
- **Formally structured**: parallel couplets, difrasismo (see in xochitl in cuicatl file), elaborate honorifics
- **Philosophically dense**: they contain the tlamatinime's actual metaphysical claims
- **Performative**: they were not merely recited but enacted — the speech itself was the ceremony
- **Preserved in Nahuatl**: Sahagún's bilingual Nahuatl-Spanish format means the native text survives alongside (often imperfect) translation

An example from Book VI — advice to a newborn girl:

> *"Precious necklace, precious feather, precious green stone... you have appeared, you have come into the world. Here on earth our lord has sent you. You came from somewhere very far..."*

The metaphors carry philosophical weight: the newborn is simultaneously *precious ornament* (chalchihuitl, jadestone — the most valued material, teotl-concentrated) and *barely-arrived from the divine realm* (from "somewhere very far" = from the level of Ōmeteōtl). The child is teotl taking temporary form.

### The Codex's theological architecture

The Codex reveals an **integrated theological system**, not a collection of myths:

**1. The Five Suns cosmogony**: History is structured as a series of cosmic ages (Suns), each presided over by a different divine principle, each ending in catastrophe. We live in the Fifth Sun (Nāhui-Ōllin, "Four-Movement") — the most precarious era, one that requires active maintenance. This is NOT cyclic fatalism ("everything ends"); it is **conditional stability** — the Fifth Sun CAN continue if properly maintained. The maintenance-theology drives the sacrifice-system (see in-xochitl-sacrifice-fusion.md).

**2. Tonalpohualli integration**: The 260-day ritual calendar (tonalpohualli = tonal + pohua + lli = "day-count") is embedded in the Codex. Every day has a sign, a number, a lord. The tonalpohualli is not merely a calendar but a **theological map of teotl's self-expression in time** — each day-combination is a particular mode of teotl's energy. The tlamatinime (and especially the tonalpouhque, "day-count specialists") read the days as a technology of attention: this day, teotl expresses as rain-power; this day, as wind-force; this day, as earth-movement.

**3. The Templo Mayor as theological center**: The Codex reveals the Templo Mayor (the great double-temple at the center of Tenochtitlan, dual shrines to Huitzilopochtli and Tlaloc) as a **cosmogram** — a physical map of the cosmos that concentrated and channeled teotl. The Templo Mayor sat at the intersection of four cosmic directions (north-south-east-west) and the vertical axis (below/earth/above). All ritual movement at the Templo Mayor was oriented by this map. Parallel: Mapuche kultrun, Kongo dikenga, Haudenosaunee Longhouse — material objects that ARE the cosmos in miniature.

## III. Tlamatinime — The Knowers of Things

### The sage-tradition

**Tlamatinime** (singular: *tlamatini*; from *tla-* [object prefix] + *mati* ["to know"] + *-ni* [agent suffix]) = "those who know things" / "knowers of things" / sages. They were the philosophical-intellectual class of Nahua civilization: poet-philosophers, theologians, rhetoricians.

Crucially: the tlamatini was simultaneously **philosopher, poet, and theologian**. The division between philosophy (logos, argument), poetry (mythos, image), and theology (theologia, divine-speech) that Greek tradition instituted from Plato onward did not exist in Nahua thought. The tlamatini worked in all three registers simultaneously because — as the in xochitl in cuicatl epistemology reveals — truth cannot be stated directly but must be SHOWN through image and metaphor. Philosophy that does not become poetry has not yet reached truth. Poetry that ignores metaphysical rigor is merely aesthetic. The tlamatini held both.

### Nezahualcoyotl — the paradigm tlamatini

The most celebrated tlamatini is **Nezahualcoyotl** (1402–1472 CE) — king of Texcoco, one of the three cities of the Triple Alliance, and a poet-philosopher whose works survive in the *Romances de los Señores de la Nueva España* (compiled ~1582) and the *Cantares Mexicanos*.

Nezahualcoyotl's poems circle around one question: **what is real in a world of flux?** The Fifth-Sun cosmology says everything ends; the teotl ontology says everything is process; the tlamatini's existential situation is one of radical impermanence. What can be trusted? What lasts?

His answer, remarkably, is: **in xochitl in cuicatl** — flower-and-song, poetry-as-truth-mode. The poem is the most real thing available to human consciousness. Not because poetry creates reality but because reality IS poetic — beauty and image are how teotl is genuinely known.

One of his most famous compositions (reconstructed from fragments):

> *"Is it true that one lives on earth? Not forever on earth, only a little while here. Though it be crystal, it will shatter. Though it be gold, it will pass away. Though it be quetzal plumage, it will be torn asunder. Not forever on earth, only a little while here."*
>
> *"Where is the house of flowers? Where is the house of song? There only do we gather, there only do we know each other in truth."*

The theological import: **the house of flowers** (*Xochicuicatl cuicatlan*) is the place where teotl is accessed through art. This is not escapism; it is epistemology. The flower-and-song register is the only mode of speech that can touch what is real in a world where all solid things dissolve.

### Aquiauhtzin, Ayocuan, Tecayehuatzin

Nezahualcoyotl is the most famous, but the Cantares Mexicanos preserves other tlamatinime voices:

**Ayocuan Cuetzpaltzin** (lord of Tecamachalco) — known for the *Icnocuicatl* (songs of sorrow/orphanhood):
> *"With flowers you write, O Giver-of-Life; with songs you give color, with songs you shade those who must live on earth. Later you will erase eagles and ocelots; we live only in your painting, here, on earth."*

The metaphor is extraordinary: the tlamatini figures YHWH-equivalent (Ipalnemohuani, "Giver-of-Life" = standard Nahuatl name for the supreme divine) as a **painter/writer** whose medium is flowers and songs, whose canvas is the earth, whose subjects are living creatures — who will be PAINTED OVER when their time ends. Human life is a divine artwork, beautiful and temporary. The theological anthropology: we exist as divine-creative-expression, not as divine-debtors.

**Tecayehuatzin** (lord of Huexotzinco) — the *Flower and Song Dialogue* preserved in Cantares Mexicanos is a poetic-philosophical debate about the nature of in xochitl in cuicatl itself: do flowers and songs come from within the poet, or from Ipalnemohuani? The debate resolves not with an answer but with the assertion that the question cannot be resolved in discursive terms — which is itself an in xochitl in cuicatl answer.

### What tlamatinime philosophy asks

León-Portilla identifies three core philosophical questions that the surviving tlamatinime texts address:

**1. The problem of foundations (toltecayotl)**: What is the foundation (*toltecayotl* = "the art/knowledge of the Toltecs") — what can human cognition reliably know? The answer: only flower-and-song provides grounded knowing. Discursive speech misses; image-and-metaphor touches.

**2. The problem of cosmic fragility (the Fifth Sun)**: We live in a conditional cosmos. The tlamatinime do not suppress this; they face it. Their existential response is not despair but *poetic witness* — the poet sees what is real, names it in flowers and songs, and this naming constitutes the most authentic response to impermanence.

**3. The problem of the self (tonalli, teyolia, ihiyotl)**: Who am I, given that everything dissolves? The tripartite soul analysis is the tlamatinime's philosophical anthropology. See section IV.

## IV. Tripartite Soul — Tonalli, Teyolia, Ihiyotl

### Overview

Nahuatl anthropology understands the human person as animated by three distinct but related soul-components. This is not a hierarchy (as in Aristotle's vegetative/sensitive/rational) but three **co-present animating forces**, each with its own location, function, vulnerability, and post-mortem fate.

The three components:

| Component | Location | Function | Vulnerability | Post-mortem |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tonalli** | Head (crown); also throughout body via hair | Individual destiny-force; calendar-sign; heat/animating-warmth | Fright, soul-loss, cold | Disperses to the sun or the afterlife-realm appropriate to death-type |
| **Teyolia** | Heart | Divine-animating-force; the deepest self; consciousness; love | Moral corruption; witchcraft | Goes to the afterlife appropriate to cause of death; this is the component that is truly "you" |
| **Ihiyotl** | Liver | Vital-breath; passion-force; anger, desire, courage; can affect others negatively if impure | Envy; moral corruption; released at death | Released as breath at death; potentially dangerous (the *cihuateteo* ghost-dangerous-dead are their ihiyotl lingering) |

### Tonalli — the destiny-force in the crown

**Tonalli** (from *tōna* = "to warm, to shine" + *-lli* nominalizer; related to *tōnal* = "day/sun/fate") names the animating warmth that is simultaneously:
- Individual **destiny** (the tonalpohualli day-sign you were born under shapes your fate)
- Physical **heat** (the warmth of living bodies; cold means tonalli-diminished)
- The **solar connection** (every person carries a fragment of the sun's energy; this is why infants must not be exposed to cold — their tonalli is fragile)

The tonalpouhque (day-count specialists) determined a newborn's fate by reading their birth-day's tonalpohualli sign. But crucially, an unfavorable sign could be offset by choosing an auspicious day to formally name the child — the naming ceremony on a different day could modify the destiny-framework. This is **contingent determination**: the cosmological assignment matters but is not absolutely fixed.

**Tonalli-loss** (susto in later Spanish-influenced terminology; *nemontemi tlaolōl* in classical Nahuatl) — fright can cause the tonalli to detach from the body. This is an illness-cause (modern biomedicine encounters this in clinical populations — *susto* as a cultural-bound syndrome with real physiological correlates in trauma research). Healers (ticitl) specialized in tonalli-recovery.

**Tonalli and heat**: The Nahuatl medical system classified all foods, activities, and environments by their heat/cold quality. The tonalli requires warmth; activities that generate cold compromise the tonalli. The sweat-bath (*temazcalli*) was both medical and ritual — restoring tonalli-warmth.

### Teyolia — the divine-heart-fire

**Teyolia** (from *te-* [person-prefix] + *yōlia* = "that by which one lives" / related to *yōl* = "heart/life") names the animating principle located in the heart — the most fundamental life-force, the deepest self, the center of consciousness and love.

Teyolia is the component closest to **teotl**: Maffie argues that teyolia IS a fragment of teotl dwelling in the human heart. This is what makes the Nahuatl worldview not merely animistic (spirits everywhere) but genuinely theological: the human heart contains a divine-force, not just an animating-principle.

The linguistic evidence: *yōllotl* (heart), *yōltic* (alive), *yōliztli* (life), *Yōlōtlicue* (the heart-goddess, another face of teotl) — all share the *yōl-* root. The heart is not a pump; it is a sacred vessel containing teotl-fire.

**Teyolia and moral transformation**: Unlike tonalli (destiny) and ihiyotl (vital-passions), teyolia is the component most shaped by moral life. Proper ritual practice, honest speech, right relationship — these nourish teyolia. Corruption, cowardice, moral dissolution — these diminish it. The tlamatinime's ethical emphasis (virtue makes the teyolia-fire burn more purely) is rooted in this anthropology.

**The heart-extraction theology** must be understood in light of teyolia. The extracted heart at the Templo Mayor was believed to contain teyolia — divine-fire. The sacrifice fed the sun (Huitzilopochtli = teotl-as-sun) with teotl-fire extracted from another teotl-vessel. This is internal to the cosmic-debt theology (nextlame register — see in-xochitl-sacrifice-fusion.md). The YOUSPEAK evaluation: the teyolia-concept is worth excavating and forging; the heart-extraction practice is named, understood in its context, and placed in the anti-register per DIVINE-PREFERENCES alignment.

### Ihiyotl — the vital-breath at the liver

**Ihiyotl** (from *ihia* = "to breathe/exhale" + *-yotl* nominalizer; related to *ihtoa* = "to say/speak") names the vital-breath located in the liver — the force of passion, drive, desire, anger, and courage.

The liver-location is not arbitrary: in Mesoamerican (and many other) traditions, the liver was the seat of passion rather than reason. The Aztec medical understanding recognized that strong emotions center in the torso (anger/rage, fear/nausea, love/desire) rather than the head.

**Ihiyotl and social danger**: Impure ihiyotl — carrying envy, resentment, malice — could exit the body during sleep and harm others. The *cihuateteo* (dangerous-dead women = women who died in childbirth) were feared because their ihiyotl lingered, restless. The ihiyotl is the component most associated with **moral pollution** — ritual purification addressed specifically the ihiyotl-quality.

**Ihiyotl and speech**: The connection to *ihtoa* (to speak) is significant. Words carry ihiyotl. Truthful, well-formed speech (the huehuetlatolli register) conveys healthy ihiyotl. Lying, cursing, or speaking from envy releases toxic ihiyotl into the community. The Nahuatl understanding of speech as morally load-bearing resonates with Hebrew *davar* (word-as-event), Aramaic *memra* (creative-divine-word), and the wider convergence on **Naming-as-Ontological-Act**.

### Comparative mapping — Tripartite Soul Complex

The tonalli/teyolia/ihiyotl triad deepens the **Tripartite-Soul-Complex** convergence (identified S055):

| Component-type | Nahuatl | Egyptian | Greek | Avestan | Mongol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Individual-destiny / animating-warmth | Tonalli (crown/heat) | Ka (vital-double; animating) | Psyche (animating life-breath) | Urvan (individual soul-self) | Ami (life-breath) |
| Divine-fire / deepest self | Teyolia (heart; divine-fragment) | Ba (personality-soul; freely-moving) | Pneuma (divine-breath; most-divine) | Fravashi (pre-existent guardian soul) | Sülde (power-spirit; outlasts person) |
| Vital-passion / social-force | Ihiyotl (liver; passion-breath) | Akh (glorified-effective-dead; social) | Thymos (spirited-part; passion) | Daēnā (conscience-soul; judge at bridge) | Sünüs (shadow-soul) |

The convergence is striking: **five traditions across four continents** independently arrive at a three-component spiritual anthropology with a destiny-force, a divine-deep-self, and a passion-force. The locations vary (crown, heart, liver in Nahuatl; ka/ba/akh distributed differently in Egyptian; not consistently localized in Greek) but the functional triad persists.

What this convergence names is a **phenomenological necessity**: human experience requires at least three concepts to describe — (1) the warmth-and-fate that makes you you from outside; (2) the fire-and-consciousness that makes you you from inside; (3) the passion-and-will that moves you through the world. Every tradition that takes the question seriously arrives at some version of this.

## V. Forge Candidates from This File

### VERY HIGH priority

**teotlme** — the single strongest YOUSPEAK candidate from Nahuatl.

- **Gap**: No current canon word names *dynamic-divine-energy-that-IS-everything-in-motion* — the process-ontological divine that is not a separate creator-god but the universe's own self-generating aliveness. Pantheism is too static (God=universe, but not as active process). Panentheism is too substantialist (God contains universe but is separate). Teotl is genuinely distinct: the universe is not IN teotl; the universe IS teotl's self-expression as ceaseless process.
- **Convergence**: The process-ontological divine appears across traditions — Daoist *Tao* (the self-generating process; "the Tao that can be named is not the eternal Tao"), Buddhist *dharmakāya* (reality-body of the Buddha = reality's own nature), Akan *Nyame* as active-creative-force (not merely remote supreme), Yoruba *àṣẹ* as divine-energy-that-flows-through-all. But none is identical to teotl's specific monism.
- **YOUSPEAK scoring**: Core 7.65–7.85 (process-divine-monism; genuinely unoccupied; wide cross-tradition anchor; single most distinctive Nahuatl contribution)
- **Donor compound**: Nahuatl *teotl* (3 syllables: teh-OH-tl) + Sumerian *me* (𒈨 = divine-ordinance-received-quality); teotl-me = "cosmic-divine-energy as that which is received/named/held" → **teotlme** (STAY ALERT: teotl ends in -tl; standard Nahuatl drop occurs in many compounds; teo-tl-me > teotlme or possibly *teōme* — the full-form teotlme is preferable for recognizability)

**teyoliame** — heart-as-divine-vessel / the fragment of cosmic-fire dwelling in human persons.

- **Gap**: Distinct from *enthousiasmos-me* (god-inside-worshipper in ecstatic-dissolution mode) — teyolia names the PERMANENT divine-fragment-in-every-person, not the special ecstatic condition. Distinct from *perichōrēsisme* (mutual-indwelling between divine persons). Closest current entry: possibly *paqduqing* (asymmetric-reciprocity, the creature as cared-for by DIVINE) — but teyolia is the creature-side claim not the relationship claim.
- **Convergence**: The divine-spark-in-humans appears in Orphic anthropology (Dionysus-spark in Titanic clay; exactly parallel to Teyolia!), Neoplatonic *nous* as divine-fragment, Gnostic *pneuma*, Quaker *inner light*, Islamic *ruh* (God's breath blown into Adam's nostrils), Hebrew *nishmat chayyim* (breath of life = divine-breath). But most of these are passive (we RECEIVED the spark). Teyolia names the spark as *active divine-fire* — not just a trace but a living fragment of teotl continuously burning.
- **YOUSPEAK scoring**: Specialized 7.40–7.60
- **Donor compound**: Nahuatl *yōlia* (that-by-which-one-lives; the teyolia's root) + *me* → **teyoliame** (teh-YOH-lee-ah-meh; 5 syllables; slightly long but the root is clean)

**tlamatinime (the word itself)** — already a 7-syllable plural; consider **tlamatisme** as a forge:

- **Gap**: No YOUSPEAK word names the *sage-who-holds-philosophy-poetry-theology-as-one-practice* — the refusal of the Western division between discursive reason (philosophy) and image-making (poetry) and divine-speech (theology). The tlamatini works in all three simultaneously because the tlamatinime epistemology (in xochitl in cuicatl) holds that truth-in-image is the only access to truth-about-what-is-real.
- **Convergence**: The philosopher-poet-theologian who refuses the division appears in: Vedic *ṛṣi* (seer-sage who composes sacred hymns that ARE theology), Hebrew *navi* (prophet who speaks in poetry), Sufi *sha'ir* (poet as one-who-sees), Celtic *fili* (poet-priest-seer as single role). The division of these into "philosophy," "poetry," "religion" is Western and post-Enlightenment.
- **YOUSPEAK scoring**: Specialized 7.30–7.50
- **Donor compound**: Nahuatl *tlamati* (to know things; root of tlamatini) + *me* → **tlamatisme** (tlah-MAH-tis-meh; 5 syllables)

### HIGH priority

- **tonalisme** — the individual-destiny-force as cosmologically-assigned warmth; the day-sign as one's participation in teotl's time-expression; Specialized 7.20–7.40 (maps onto *jayağame* + Avestan *fravashi* territory; needs careful differentiation first)
- **ōmeteotlme** — the dual-in-one supreme principle; Specialized 7.25–7.45 (the Two-Lord concept as complement to Tengri's formless-singular and YHWH's covenanting-personal; fills a genuine gap)
- **tloqueme** — "the near-and-far one" (tloquenahuaque → *tloque*); immanence-transcendence simultaneity as one divine-attribute; Specialized 7.20–7.40
