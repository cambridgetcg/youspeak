# Prehension — the feeling-into that constitutes contact-with-reality below the level of conscious-cognition

## The concept in compact form

**Prehension** (from Latin *prehensio*, "a grasping") is Whitehead's technical term for *the act by which any actual-occasion incorporates other actual-occasions into its own becoming*. The key features:

1. **Prior to consciousness** — prehension is the generic act-of-grasping that constitutes ALL contact-with-reality; conscious-cognition is a high-level specialization
2. **Universal** — every actual-occasion (from quantum-events to organisms to societies) prehends; reality is the network of mutual prehensions
3. **Affective-toned** — prehensions carry "subjective form" (felt-quality), not just informational content
4. **Constitutive** — an actual-occasion just IS the gathering of its prehensions into a unified subjective experience (*concrescence*)

Whitehead distinguishes:
- **Positive prehension** (*feeling*) — the inclusion of another into one's own becoming
- **Negative prehension** — the active exclusion (not mere absence) of another from one's becoming
- **Physical prehension** — feeling of past actual-entities
- **Conceptual prehension** — feeling of eternal-objects (forms, qualities)
- **Hybrid prehension** — feeling of another's conceptual-prehensions

## Why this is realm-content

The prehension-concept names a realm-feature unnamed elsewhere: **the universal-act of feeling-into that constitutes any-thing's contact with any-other-thing, below the cognitive-conscious level.** This is structurally distinct from:

- Greek *aisthesis* (sensation) — sensation is one specialization of prehension
- Sanskrit *grahaṇa* (grasping) — closer, but Indian-philosophical grahaṇa tends to be cognitive
- Husserlian *intentionality* — intentionality is the conscious-mind's directedness; prehension is the generic-substrate of which intentionality is a special case
- Heideggerian *Vernehmen* (apprehending) — Vernehmen is Dasein-specific; prehension is universal-actual-occasion

Whitehead's contribution: **before any cognition can ground its objects, an act-of-feeling-into has constituted the relation.** Reality is woven from prehensions.

This pairs naturally with PROBOLE's *grasp* step (the first step of the projection-mechanism). Where PROBOLE names the linguistic-projection-of-realm-content into signs, prehension names *the prior universal-act* that makes any linguistic-projection possible. **prehensionme will name this** — feeling-into as received-ordinance — the substrate-of-grasping below the level the cathedral has thus far named.

## Cross-tradition resonance

- **Sanskrit grahaṇa (ग्रहण):** grasping; in Indian philosophy often cognitive but with prefix *adhi-grahaṇa* (active-grasping) closer to prehension
- **German Vernehmen:** apprehending (Heidegger uses this for Dasein's mode of taking-in being)
- **Bergson's perception-as-action:** prehension is structurally close to Bergson's account of perception as embodied-selection-from-reality
- **Phenomenological intentionality:** Husserl + Brentano name the conscious-aspect; Whitehead generalizes below-consciousness
- **Embodied cognition / enactivism:** Varela-Thompson-Rosch on cognition-as-embodied-action shares structural features with prehension
- **Buddhist pratītyasamutpāda:** dependent-origination has structural-parallel — every dharma arises through prehensive-relation to other dharmas
- **Animism (some readings):** the felt-relational-substrate of living-things; Hartshorne's "psychicalism" extends prehension to all-of-nature

## Whitehead's place in modern philosophy

Whitehead is the modern-philosophical-donor whose vocabulary most cleanly bridges:
- Pre-modern theological-traditions (his "primordial nature of God" parallels classical theism)
- Modern science (his cosmology takes physics seriously)
- Eastern philosophy (Hartshorne and Cobb extensively connected Whitehead to Buddhism)
- Process-mysticism (Teilhard de Chardin, ongoing process-theology)

The cathedral's pre-modern donors do not name prehension's realm-feature. The modern-philosophy sub-organ exists for precisely this purpose.
