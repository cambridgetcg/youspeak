# modern-philosophy/ — the sub-organ rationale

## Why a new sub-organ

Cathedral archaeology has been organized by language-tradition (greek/, hebrew/, sanskrit/, etc.) because most pre-modern philosophical-vocabularies are *embedded in* their language-traditions — Greek philosophy in Greek, Vedanta in Sanskrit, Sufism in Arabic/Persian, etc.

**Modern philosophy operates differently.** Modern philosophical schools (phenomenology, process philosophy, pragmatism, hermeneutics, decolonial-thought, etc.) are **transnational-discipline-traditions** that:

- Cross language boundaries within one school (Husserl-German, Levinas-French, Heidegger-German, Merleau-Ponty-French — all phenomenology)
- Have school-specific technical vocabularies that the language-traditions themselves did not contain (the German *Dasein* is Heideggerian, not generic-German; the French *visage* in Levinas's sense is technical, not generic-French)
- Develop *as schools* through cross-language scholarly-dialogue post-19th-century

Placing Whitehead under English/ or Levinas under French/ would obscure the *school* as the donor-tradition. The school IS the tradition; the language is the medium.

## Principle of inclusion

A modern-philosophical-school enters archaeology/modern-philosophy/ when:

1. It has *school-specific technical vocabulary* not derivable from the host-language
2. The vocabulary names *realm-features the cathedral's pre-modern donors have not articulated with comparable precision*
3. The school operates with *substrate-honest discipline* (not pure-relativism; not unbounded-deconstruction; not mere-ideology)

## Discipline notes

Forges drawing on modern-philosophy donors should be especially careful about:
- **Audience-accessibility** (EUMATHE) — much modern-philosophical-vocabulary is technical; the cathedral compound must be more accessible than its source
- **Substrate-honesty** about contested-claims — phenomenology's deepest claims are contested even within phenomenology; the cathedral's forge attributes claims to schools rather than treating them as cathedral-doctrine
- **Cross-tradition convergence** — every modern-philosophical forge should identify pre-modern traditions that name the same realm-feature (otherwise risk forging a school-jargon rather than realm-projection)
