# Haudenosaunee — Creation, Sky-Woman, and the Thanksgiving Address

_Excavation of the cosmological and liturgical core of Haudenosaunee thought. Two documents ground this excavation: the Creation Narrative (Sky-Woman, the twin creators, the origins of the world) and the Thanksgiving Address (Ohén:ton Karihwatéhkwen — "the words that come before all else"). These are not separate: the Thanksgiving Address RE-ENACTS the creation each time it is spoken, naming the world into present-tense relationship._

---

## Premise: The Haudenosaunee Conceptual Contribution

Haudenosaunee theological thinking offers YOUSPEAK at least five conceptual structures not found elsewhere in the catalog:

1. **Gratitude-as-enumeration** — the Thanksgiving Address names each being in the creation in order, speaking to them directly, expressing gratitude for their continued existence and function; this is not petition, not praise-of-a-deity, not sacrifice-as-maintenance — it is **sequential relational acknowledgment as the constitutive worship-grammar**; each being is recognized as continuing to fulfill its purpose ("still doing its work"); the world is held together by this daily naming
2. **Earth-as-first-mother (Ohén:ton Karihwatéhkwen opens with Turtle Island)** — the earth is not merely a platform for human life but the first being addressed in the liturgy; the sequence matters theologically: earth first, then waters, then grasses, then plants, then berries, then animals, then trees, then birds, then four winds, then thunder-beings, then sun, then grandmother moon, then stars, then teachers/guides, then the Creator last; the order is a theology of attention
3. **Twin-creator structure as constitutive ambiguity** — Sapling (Okwiraseh / Tharonhiawako, the good creator, Sky-Holder) and Flint (Tawiskaron, the adversarial twin) do not represent simple good/evil dualism; their opposition is the generative tension that produces a world with both ease and resistance, beauty and obstacle; the world is MADE of their disagreement; more nuanced than Zoroastrian dualism (which is cosmic warfare) and Taoist complementarity (which is cycle) — this is **creative conflict as world-constitution**
4. **Sky-Woman as the first displaced person** — she falls from the Sky World through a hole left by an uprooted tree; she is caught by birds and placed on a turtle's back; she is not punished, not saved, not exalted — she simply falls and is received; the earth is built beneath her out of mud brought up by a muskrat from the deep water; this is not creation ex nihilo, not divine world-speech — it is **creation-as-collaborative-emergence**; the beings of the lower world contribute to the making of the new world for this stranger who fell
5. **Law-as-peace-power-righteousness (Sken:nen Ko:wa, Ka'shatsténshera, Kariwiio)** — the Great Law of Peace is not a legal code but a living spiritual teaching; its three principles (peace = sken:nen; power = ka'shatsténshera = the power of the united mind; righteousness = kariwiio = "the good word/message") constitute a political theology in which peace IS the spiritual condition and law IS its visible form

## Part I: The Creation Narrative — Sky-Woman and the Twin Creators

### The Sky World and the Fall

The Haudenosaunee creation narrative begins in the Sky World — a realm of beings who existed before this earth. At the center of the Sky World stood a great tree, often called the Tree of Light or the Ancient One, whose flowers illuminated the world.

A powerful Sky Chief dreamed that the tree should be uprooted. In the Haudenosaunee understanding, dreams are instructions from deeper reality — to dream is to receive; to follow the dream is to fulfill one's purpose. The tree was uprooted. A woman — known in most traditions as Aataentsic (Wyandot/Huron), Iáhsen (various Iroquoian forms), or simply Sky-Woman — leaned over to look into the hole left by the uprooted tree. She fell.

**The significance of the fall**: Sky-Woman's fall is not the Fall. It is not exile for transgression. It is not punishment. She falls because she looks. The uprooting creates a possibility; her curiosity is not condemned. She falls into the creative situation that will become the world. Different versions offer different precipitating stories (some say she was pushed by a jealous husband; some say she carried the seeds of the Sky World in her arms; some say she was already pregnant with the child who would become the mother of the twin creators). But across all versions: the fall is the beginning, not the rupture.

**The birds' reception**: Birds from the lower world — ducks, geese — see her falling and rise to receive her, spreading their wings to catch her and bear her safely. This moment is **the first act of the Thanksgiving logic**: the creatures of the new world act to sustain a stranger, before the world even exists for her. Relationship precedes structure.

**The muskrat's mud**: The birds place Sky-Woman on the back of the Great Turtle. But she needs land. Various water creatures — loon, beaver, others — dive into the deep water to try to bring up mud from the bottom. Each fails and surfaces nearly dead from the depth. Finally the muskrat — small, apparently inadequate — dives. The muskrat surfaces and dies, but in its paw is a small amount of mud. Sky-Woman places the mud on Turtle's back. It spreads and becomes Turtle Island — the continent. Creation is not the act of an omnipotent being but the **cooperative last-effort of the small ones whose adequate contribution is a handful of earth**.

### The Conceptual Structure: Creation-as-Collaborative-Emergence

This creation pattern is distinct from every other in the catalog:

- **Sumerian/Babylonian**: creation from cosmic conflict (Tiamat slain by Marduk; her body becomes the world)
- **Hebrew/Christian/Islamic**: creation ex nihilo by divine speech or divine will
- **Greek/Hesiodic**: creation as genealogy — beings emerge from prior beings in sequence
- **Hindu (Vedic)**: creation from primordial sacrifice (Purusha dismembered; parts become cosmos)
- **Egyptian**: creation from primordial hill, solar will, or divine word (Ptah/Atum/Ra)
- **Diné/Navajo**: emergence through successive world-levels; creation as a community journey upward
- **Igbo**: creation not mythologically elaborated — world is as-it-is, animated by Chukwu through chi distribution
- **Haudenosaunee**: creation as collaborative rescue — birds receive the falling stranger; creatures attempt the impossible; the smallest succeeds through total expenditure; the world grows from the act of generosity meeting the stranger's need

**Forge note**: There is a possible candidate here — but it is not obviously distinct from Diné emergence. The specifically Haudenosaunee contribution is: **creation-as-collaborative-response-to-the-stranger**. The world is made in response to a fallen being who does not belong to this world yet. Let that sit.

### Sky-Woman's Daughter and the Twin Creators

Sky-Woman is pregnant when she falls (or becomes pregnant after arrival, in some versions). She gives birth to a daughter. The daughter grows and becomes pregnant, conceived not by a man but by the West Wind in some versions, or by the Earth and her presence in others. She dies giving birth to twin boys.

The first to be born is **Okwiraseh / Tharonhiawako** — variously translated as Sky-Holder, Creator, the Good-Minded One; he is associated with summer, warmth, growing things, gentle paths. He creates beneficial things: food plants, calm rivers, clear days, animals that can be hunted easily.

The second twin **Tawiskaron** (Flint, Ice-Being) refuses the natural birth path and exits through his mother's side, killing her. He creates the adversarial complement: rough terrain, thorns, animals that are hard to catch, rapids in rivers, winter, disease. He is not simply evil — he makes the world *difficult*, which is different from making it bad.

**Sky-Woman is grief-stricken by her daughter's death.** She buries her daughter. From the daughter's body grow the Three Sisters — corn, beans, and squash — along with other food plants. The daughter does not die and disappear; she becomes the sustenance of the living world. This is the **first instance of the Haudenosaunee pattern**: what seems like loss is transformation into sustenance; the dead are not gone but reorganized into the forms of continuing life.

### The Twin Conflict and the Constitution of the World

The twin creators are in perpetual creative tension. Some stories say they fight directly and Tawiskaron is defeated but not destroyed — he retreats to the place where he belongs (the cold edges of the world). The tension between them is not resolved; it is **held**. The world is constituted by the holding.

**Theologically distinctive**: Most dualisms in the catalog resolve toward one pole:
- Zoroastrian: Ahura Mazda will ultimately defeat Angra Mainyu
- Hebrew: Yahweh will ultimately vindicate and restore
- Christian: Christ's resurrection is the defeat of death
- Egyptian: Re defeats Apep each night but Apep is never permanently destroyed — closest analog
- Haudenosaunee: the twins' tension is **constitutive and permanent**; the world is not on the way to resolution; it IS the tension held

**Forge candidate note**: "creative-conflict-as-world-constitution" — the state in which opposing creative forces produce a world precisely through their sustained opposition, not through the victory of one. Not *yin-yang* (complementary balance) but twin-creators-in-permanent-conflict. Let this sit for Part II of the excavation.

## Part II: The Thanksgiving Address — Ohén:ton Karihwatéhkwen

_"The Words That Come Before All Else"_

### What It Is

The Ohén:ton Karihwatéhkwen is a ceremonial address spoken at the opening of all formal Haudenosaunee gatherings — councils, ceremonies, longhouse meetings, schools (in revitalized traditions), community events. It is also taught as a daily practice.

It is not a prayer in the Western sense — it does not petition. It is not a hymn — it does not praise a distant deity. It is not a creed — it does not state doctrinal positions.

It is **a sequential address to each being in creation**, moving from earth to sky to the Creator, in which the speaker:
1. Names each category of being
2. Acknowledges that they continue to fulfill their purpose ("still doing the work they were given at the beginning")
3. Expresses gratitude for their continuation
4. Calls for all minds to join in agreement ("let our minds be one")

The closing formula for each section is a version of: *Oneh neha niwahsháen ronkwetares* — "Now our minds are one" or "Let our minds be of one accord." This is crucial. The Address is not a solo performance. It is a collective re-agreement with the structure of the world.

### The Sequence

The sequence is itself theological. Here is the canonical order as given in various recorded versions (primarily from the Six Nations teaching materials and John Stokes' collaborative work with Chief Jake Swamp):

1. **The People** — We acknowledge ourselves, gathered here; we express gratitude that we are still moving and breathing; let our minds be one
2. **Mother Earth** — We express gratitude to the earth, our mother, who continues to care for us; she still provides what we need to survive; let our minds be one
3. **The Waters** — Springs, streams, rivers, lakes, the great oceans; they continue to perform their duties; they quench our thirst, carry us; let our minds be one
4. **The Fish** — All the fish in the waters; they still perform their duties; let our minds be one
5. **The Plants** — All the plants that grow from the earth; they still perform their duties; they are medicines and foods; let our minds be one
6. **The Food Plants (Three Sisters)** — Corn, beans, and squash; they remain with us; the life-sustaining sisters; let our minds be one
7. **The Medicine Herbs** — The medicines that the plant world has given us; they heal; they still perform their duties; let our minds be one
8. **The Animals** — All the animal life in the world; they still perform their duties; they are our brothers and sisters; let our minds be one
9. **The Trees** — The trees, especially the maple (leader of the trees, who gives sap at the awakening of spring); the trees still perform their duties; they give us shade, wood, fruit; let our minds be one
10. **The Birds** — The birds, especially the eagle (highest of the birds, the messenger who carries our words highest); the birds still sing and do their duties; let our minds be one
11. **The Four Winds** — The winds from the four directions; they cleanse the air, bring weather, move clouds; they still perform their duties; let our minds be one
12. **The Thunderers (Thunder Beings)** — The grandfathers who bring the rains, who cleanse the skies, who care for the fresh waters; they still perform their duties; let our minds be one
13. **The Sun** — Our Elder Brother, the Sun; he continues to rise each day, bringing light and warmth, making the plants grow; he still performs his duties; let our minds be one
14. **Grandmother Moon** — She governs the tides, the growth of plants, the cycles of the female body; she still performs her duties; let our minds be one
15. **The Stars** — They still light the night sky; they guide travelers; they tell the seasons; they still perform their duties; let our minds be one
16. **The Enlightened Teachers / The Four Beings** — The spiritual teachers who guide human beings, the messengers of the Creator, those who were given the task of watching over humanity; let our minds be one
17. **The Creator** — We have now reached the point where we cannot know; we do not presume to know the form of the Creator; we express our gratitude for all the gifts; let our minds be one

**Note**: The sequence moves from immanent (us, here, this ground) to transcendent (the Creator, about whom we cannot say much). This is the reverse of most Western worship, which typically addresses God first and works downward. The Thanksgiving Address **builds toward the Creator through the accumulated acknowledgment of every other being**. You do not reach the Creator except through first naming the earth, the waters, the fish, the plants, the medicine herbs, the animals, the trees, the birds, the winds, the thunder, the sun, the moon, the stars, the teachers. This is a deliberate theological grammar.

### The Theological Grammar of the Thanksgiving Address

**1. Presence-before-petition**. The Address establishes the world's presence before any request is made. You cannot ask the world for anything — in any Haudenosaunee ceremony — before first naming that the world is here, that it is doing its work, and that you are grateful for this. Western worship structures typically move petition first or petition concurrent with praise. The Address makes acknowledgment the only precondition for ceremony.

**2. Continued function as the ground of gratitude**. Each being is not thanked for what it did in the past but for what it **continues to do** — "still performing its duties at the beginning of time." This present-tense, ongoing acknowledgment is distinct from thanksgiving for a past gift. You are not grateful for creation-once; you are grateful for the earth's continued bearing-of-you, the waters' continued flowing, the sun's continued rising. Gratitude is renewable at every moment because the gift is renewed at every moment.

**3. "Let our minds be one" as collective re-agreement**. The closing formula (Oneh neha niwahsháen ronkwetares) is not a declaration that minds ARE one but an invitation/affirmation that minds BECOME one — a collective agreement to be in right relationship with the being just named. This is not consensus in the political sense; it is **unanimous relational realignment** with each stratum of the creation. The Address re-weaves the web of relationship by securing agreement at each node.

**4. The "still" theology**. The English translation consistently renders the Haudenosaunee term as "still" — "the waters are still flowing," "the sun is still rising," "the animals are still doing their work." This "still" is not merely temporal ("continuing to this day") but a form of wonder: against the possibility that the waters might stop, that the sun might not rise, that the animals might abandon their function — they have not. "Still" carries a note of relief, of miracle-in-constancy. Each day the world could fail to continue; each day it continues. The Address registers this constancy as a marvel.

**5. Sequence as theological argument**. The order is not arbitrary. Moving from people → earth → waters → fish → plants → animals → trees → birds → winds → thunder → sun → moon → stars → teachers → Creator is a progression from what is closest, most tangible, most immediate to what is most distant and most difficult to name. You start with yourself (still breathing), move to the ground beneath your feet (still bearing you), and work outward and upward through concentric circles of relation until you reach the one about whom you can say least. The Address teaches: you know the Creator through the creation, not despite it.

**Forge candidate — PRIMARY**: The Thanksgiving Address generates what may be the catalog's most important convergence candidate: **Gratitude-as-Sequential-Naming-of-the-World**. This is genuinely distinct from:
- Hebrew *hodayot* (thanksgivings): directed to God for past acts of deliverance; historical in orientation
- Christian Eucharist (*eucharistia*): thanksgiving as sacramental meal re-enacting a past event
- Hindu puja: offering to the deity present in the image; devotional-relational
- Avestan Yasna: sacrificial ceremony to maintain the cosmic order through correct ritual
- Igbo Iri Ji: first-fruits as acknowledgment of Ala's agricultural sovereignty
- **Haudenosaunee Thanksgiving Address**: naming each being in creation-sequence while speaking directly to each, acknowledging its continued function, and calling for collective agreement with its continued existence

The forge candidate: **ohéntome** — from *Ohén:ton* (words-before-all-else, what-comes-first, the address-that-precedes-all-action) — naming the act of enumerating-the-world-in-gratitude-as-the-constitutive-liturgical-act; the mode of acknowledgment that names each being in sequence before asking anything of anyone. Possible CORE candidate (7.60–7.80).

### The Practice of the Address

The Thanksgiving Address is not the property of specialists. In traditional Haudenosaunee culture, it is the right and responsibility of any person — man, woman, child — to speak it. A child learning the Address learns the sequence of the world. A council begins only after someone has spoken it. Schools in Six Nations communities open each day with it.

**Jake Swamp** (Mohawk, 1941–2010), Faithkeeper of the Wolf Clan, traveled the world teaching the Thanksgiving Address under the title "A Gift to the Human Family." He understood it as a document not merely of Haudenosaunee tradition but of a grammar of gratitude available to all humans: "The people need to give thanks. It is the most powerful thing we have."

The address was adapted and published for children as *Giving Thanks: A Native American Good Morning Message* (Chief Jake Swamp, 1995), which brought it into wide use. This popularization is itself theologically significant: the Address is understood by the Haudenosaunee themselves to be shareable, not proprietary.

## Part III: Three Sisters and Agricultural Theology

### Corn, Beans, and Squash as Theological Entity

The Three Sisters — corn (onón:ta'), beans (osahé:ta'), squash (ohnahkwá') — are not simply crops. They are beings: specifically, the daughters born from the body of Sky-Woman's daughter (in one reading) or gifts given by the Creator specifically for the sustenance of the people.

They are addressed in the Thanksgiving Address directly, as a distinct category between "plants" and "medicine herbs." They receive individual acknowledgment: "still providing for us the food our bodies need."

**Agricultural theology**: The Three Sisters are planted together in a system (milpa-style companion planting). Corn grows tall; beans fix nitrogen and climb the corn; squash spreads wide and shades the ground, preventing weeds and retaining moisture. They sustain each other in growth. This is not just ecology — it is the model for human cooperation made visible in the garden. The Three Sisters teach by being: **interdependence as the structure of flourishing**.

**Theological claim**: You cannot plant the Three Sisters extractively. You cannot pull corn without returning something to the earth; you cannot harvest beans without leaving some to seed; you cannot harvest squash without leaving some to fall and decay. The agricultural system requires reciprocity as its structural principle. The yield comes FROM the reciprocity, not despite it.

**Forge candidate**: There is a possible candidate in the Three Sisters pattern — **the-theological-object-that-teaches-by-its-structure-without-words** — but this overlaps with several existing candidates. Let it sit; note for convergences file.

## Method Findings — Haudenosaunee File 1

**1. The Thanksgiving Address is a distinct worship-grammar type.** In the excavation of 55+ traditions, no other tradition has been found to use the specific grammar: sequential naming → acknowledgment of continued function → gratitude → collective agreement → move to next being in the cosmic order. Buddhist metta meditation (radiate loving-kindness outward in expanding circles) is structural but lacks the naming and the specific "still performing its duty" register. Indigenous Australian address-to-Country is close but not the same form. The Thanksgiving Address is singular.

**2. "Still doing its work" is a theology of constancy-as-gift.** The Haudenosaunee theological attention to the continued functioning of creation reveals a register YOUSPEAK does not yet have words for: the **renewed miracle of unchanged constancy**. English "gratitude" is typically directed toward exceptional gifts or special favors. The Thanksgiving Address directs gratitude toward *what has always been happening*, *what continues to happen*, *what we rely on so completely we forget to notice*. This is a different register of gratitude than any in the current catalog.

**3. The sequence-from-earth-to-Creator reverses Western liturgical grammar.** Western worship (across Hebrew, Christian, Islamic, and most Western-derived traditions) addresses God first, works downward toward creatures and creation. The Thanksgiving Address builds from earth upward to Creator. This is not just a stylistic difference — it encodes a theology: you encounter the Creator *through* the creation, not *despite* it; the creation is the only reliable path to the Creator; you cannot skip ahead.

**4. Sky-Woman's fall is not the Fall.** This distinction has forge implications. The Christian-influenced catalog has an implicit default toward "fall = punishment/rupture." The Haudenosaunee pattern: falling is how you arrive in the world of making; curiosity-that-leads-to-falling is not condemned; the creatures of the new world act to receive you before the world even exists for you. The fall is a creative displacement, not a moral failure. This enriches the convergences around descent/emergence themes.

**5. Twin-creator structure is its own dualism-type.** The Haudenosaunee twin-creator pattern is not simple good-vs-evil dualism but **productive-adversarial-creation**: the difficult things in the world (thorns, rapids, disease, winter) are not the work of an evil being in rebellion against a good Creator — they are the work of the Creator's twin, who is also a creator, whose creations are difficult but generative. The world needs both; you cannot extract difficulty from the creation without unmaking it.
