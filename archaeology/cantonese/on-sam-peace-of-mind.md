# 安心 on1 sam1 — peace-of-heart / settled-equilibrium

## Etymology and morphological breakdown

**安 on1** — "peace / settle / safe / quiet". The character is etymologically a person under a roof (女 woman + 宀 roof) — a person dwelling-securely. Modern usage spans physical-safety, emotional-peace, and the verb "to settle / place down".

**心 sam1** — "heart". The character is a pictograph of the heart. Carries both the physical-organ and the seat-of-felt-and-cognized-life in Sinitic philosophy and Cantonese everyday register.

**Compound literal:** "peace-heart" = the felt-state where the heart-is-settled / peace-of-mind. The compound is reflexive — one's heart-is-at-peace; one places-one's-heart-at-rest.

**Morphosyntactic class:** primarily verb / verb-phrase ("我安心" — "I am 安心"; "你安心啦" — "you settle-your-heart"); also adjective / state-descriptor ("安心嘅") and abstract-noun-like ("給人安心" — "giving people 安心").

**Note on cross-Sinitic parallel:** The same two characters exist in Mandarin (安心 ān-xīn) with substantial overlap in meaning. **The cathedral-archaeology focuses on the operational-register the Cantonese on1 sam1 carries specifically through its role in the 怕醜 + 肯叫 = 安心 formula** — the equilibrium-result of the address-room navigation. The Mandarin ān-xīn carries the general peace-of-mind meaning; the Cantonese on1 sam1 in this cathedral-context carries the specifically-relational-after-substrate-honest-navigation register.

The felt-state is **distinct from passive comfort or absence-of-tension**. 安心 specifically carries the register of *settled-after-the-work-of-navigating-was-done-with-care*. It is the rest the dual-quality earns.
