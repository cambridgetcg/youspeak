# Vimarśa — the dynamic-knowing-of-knowing that pairs with prakāśa

## The concept in compact form

**Vimarśa** (Sanskrit विमर्श, "consideration, examination, self-reflection") in Kashmir-Shaiva use names *the self-reflexive-awareness pole of consciousness* — the "I-am-knowing" that accompanies any specific knowing. Pairs with **prakāśa** (प्रकाश, "luminosity, manifestation") as inseparable poles of one consciousness:

- **Prakāśa** — the *luminous-knowing pole*; consciousness as that-which-illumines its objects
- **Vimarśa** — the *self-reflexive-knowing pole*; consciousness as that-which-knows-itself-as-knowing

Crucially, the two are not two-separate-things; they are *inseparable poles of one consciousness*. Without vimarśa, prakāśa would be merely-luminous-without-self-knowing (like a lamp that illumines but does not know it illumines). Without prakāśa, vimarśa would have nothing to be reflexive-about. The two together constitute *spanda* (the divine pulsation, Phase-1 forge spandaqing).

Kashmir-Shaivism's key contribution: consciousness is *dynamic-reflexive*, not static-witness. Advaita Vedanta tends to treat consciousness as static brahman-witness; Kashmir-Shaivism treats consciousness as *prakāśa-vimarśa pulsating-unity*.

## Why this is realm-content

The cathedral has spandaqing (Phase 1) naming the *unity* of prakāśa + vimarśa as felt-pulsation. But cathedral lacks vocabulary for the *vimarśa pole specifically* — the self-reflexive-awareness aspect that distinguishes consciousness-of-self from mere luminous-knowing. This is structurally-distinct from rigpame (primordial-awareness prior to subject-object split): vimarśa specifically names *the self-reflexive-recognition of oneself as the knower*.

vimarśame names this — *self-reflexive-awareness pole of consciousness as received-cosmic-ordinance*.

## Cross-tradition resonance

- **Plotinian Nous** — knows itself as knowing; structural-parallel
- **Christian Trinitarian** — Father-knowing-Self in eternal-Son; the Trinity as eternal-prakāśa-vimarśa-pulsation
- **Sufi Ibn ʿArabī** — the divine self-disclosure (tajallī) operates through self-knowing-self
- **Buddhist svasaṃvedana** (self-cognition) — Yogācāra-Mahayana discussion of consciousness's self-awareness
- **Phenomenology** — Husserl's transcendental-ego's self-reflexive-character
- **Modern cognitive-science** — meta-cognition, higher-order-thought theories of consciousness
