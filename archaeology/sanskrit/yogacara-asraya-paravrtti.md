# Āśraya-parāvṛtti — the turning-about of the basis

*This is the second Yogācāra excavation. The first (ālaya-vijñāna) opened the storehouse — the substrate that holds karmic-seeds and bears continuity. Āśraya-parāvṛtti is what happens to that storehouse on the path: it turns. The basis does not get replaced; it transforms. This is the structural-heart of Yogācāra's soteriology, and the concept that makes the tradition a path rather than merely a metaphysics.*

---

## 1. The concept in compact form

**Āśraya-parāvṛtti** (Sanskrit आश्रयपरāवृत्ति) is a compound of **āśraya** (basis, substratum, resting-place — here specifically the ālaya-vijñāna, the storehouse-consciousness that is the basis of all experience) and **parāvṛtti** (turning-about, revolution, reversal — from parā "away/other" + vṛtti "turning/revolving"). The term names **the radical turning-about of the basis-of-experience from defiled to pure** — the event in which ālaya-vijñāna, which had been storing karmic-seeds of ignorance and affliction, ceases that function and transforms into the four pure wisdoms (catvāri jñānāni).

The doctrine-arc: a practitioner on the Yogācāra path, through sustained meditation on the emptiness of subject-object duality (grāhaka-grāhya abhāva), reaches a point where the storehouse-consciousness no longer receives defiled-seeds. The habitual outflow (pravṛtti) of seeds-into-experience stops. What remains is not a blank — it is the purified basis now functioning as four mirror-like wisdoms: ādarśa-jñāna (mirror-wisdom, reflecting all without distortion), samatā-jñāna (equality-wisdom, seeing all beings as equal), pratyavekṣaṇā-jñāna (discriminating-wisdom, precise insight into particulars), and kṛtyānusthāna-jñāna (all-accomplishing-wisdom, spontaneous right-action).

The structural marks the forge must preserve:

1. **It is a turning, not a destruction.** The basis is not annihilated — it is rotated. The same consciousness that stored defiled-seeds now reflects purely. This is why parāvṛtti (turning) rather than vināśa (destruction). The continuity is structural, not substantial.
2. **It is non-gradual in its culmination.** The turning itself is a single event (eka-prahara in some readings — "in one stroke"), though the path leading to it is gradual. The seeds are exhausted gradually; the turn is sudden. This is the Yogācāra structural-antecedent to the sudden/gradual debate that later splits Chan/Zen.
3. **It is irreversible.** Once the basis has turned, it does not revert. The defiled-function is not merely suppressed but structurally eliminated — the seed-receiving mechanism itself has been deactivated. This is what distinguishes it from a meditative state that one can enter and exit.
4. **It produces wisdom, not blankness.** The transformed basis is not empty-stillness but the four active wisdoms. The mirror does not stop reflecting when the distortions are removed; it reflects truly for the first time.

**Sibling-distinctions:** āśraya-parāvṛtti is not nirodha (cessation — the stopping of a process, which is what happens to the defiled-function but does not name the positive transformation); it is not abhisamaya (direct realization / penetration — which is the cognitive-act of seeing emptiness, a moment of insight, not the structural-transformation of the substrate); it is not vimukti (liberation — which is the state-result, not the event-mechanism). Āśraya-parāvṛtti names the specific event: the substrate itself turns. Cessation is what stops; penetration is what sees; liberation is what results; āśraya-parāvṛtti is what happens to the basis.

## 3. Primary attestations and scholarly references

**The locus classicus** is Vasubandhu's *Triṃśikā* (Thirty Verses), verse 30:

> *acitto 'nupalabdhiś ca śūnyatā caiva desitā / āśrayasya parāvṛttir jñānam laukikānukramāt //*

*"Non-cognition, non-apprehension, and emptiness — these have been taught. The turning-about of the basis is [this-] wisdom, following the worldly order."*

The verse is dense: the three negations (non-cognition, non-apprehension, emptiness) describe the meditative entry; āśrayasya parāvṛttiḥ names what happens to the basis in that entry; jñānam (wisdom) is the result. Sthiramati's commentary glosses: the "basis" (āśraya) is the ālaya-vijñāna; the "turning" is its ceasing to function as defiled-storage and beginning to function as pure-wisdom. Xuanzang's *Chéng wéi shì lùn* (Vijñaptimātratāsiddhi) elaborates this into a five-stage model (the five ranks / wǔ wèi 五位) of which āśraya-parāvṛtti is the culminating event.

The *Laṅkāvatāra Sūtra* (earliest stratum, ~3rd–4th c. CE) uses parāvṛtti in the context of the "turning about of the root-basis" (*mūlāśraya-parāvṛtti*), which is likely the conceptual-ancestor of the Triṃśikā formulation.

**Scholarly afterlife:** The concept was the focus of the Yogācāra-Svātantrika-Madhyamaka debate: does āśraya-parāvṛtti establish a positive reality (Yogācāra reading) or is it itself empty of intrinsic nature (Madhyamaka critique)? The cathedral receives the structural-form without taking the metaphysical side: the turning-of-the-basis is a structural-event regardless of whether the basis is ultimately-real or empty.

## 4. Etymology and family

- **āśraya**: from ā- (to, toward) + śri (to lean, rest, depend) — "that upon which something rests," the basis, support, substratum. Cognate with English "shelter" via PIE *sḱel- (to lean).
- **parāvṛtti**: from parā- (away, to the other side) + vṛtti (turning, rolling, mode-of-being) — a turning-away, a turning-about, a revolution. The vṛtti root (vṛt, to turn/roll) is the same that gives vṛtti (mode, function, activity) and pratītya-samutpāda's dependent-arising is a vṛtti-pattern.
- **Morphology**: compound noun, feminine. The compound is a *tatpuruṣa* (determinative) — āśraya is the object of parāvṛtti: the turning acts upon the basis.
- **Doctrinal-family**: the term sits in the cluster {ālaya-vijñāna (the basis), bīja (seeds), vāsanā (perfumation), kleśa (defilements), pravṛtti-nirodha (cessation-of-outflow), āśraya-parāvṛtti (the turning), jñāna (the resulting wisdom)}. It presupposes ālaya-vijñāna and completes into jñāna.

## 5. The unnamed region — the gap-shadows

English speakers feel the phenomenon but cannot name it: the moment when the **basis of one's experience reorganizes** — not a new belief, not a new skill, not a new emotion, but the substrate that generates beliefs, skills, and emotions itself turning. Conversion-experiences, paradigm-shifts that restructure perception rather than content, the moment a long-prepared change "clicks" at the structural level rather than the cognitive — these are approximations, but each is framed by its content (religious conversion, scientific revolution, therapeutic breakthrough). None names the **mechanism**: the basis turned.

Near-misses, each failing:

| Candidate | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| conversion | names the content (to a religion, to a view); āśraya-parāvṛtti names the structure (the basis turned, regardless of content) |
| paradigm shift | Kuhn's term names the cognitive-reorganization within a discipline; āśraya-parāvṛtti names the reorganization of the subject, not the discipline |
| metanoia | Greek "change of mind" — close, but "mind" is the wrong register (cognitive, not substrate); the basis is not the mind but what generates mind |
| breakthrough | names the event from the outside (the wall was broken); āśraya-parāvṛtti names the event from the inside (the basis turned) |

**Canon-collision-check** (2026-07-08): nearest canon neighbors — alayame (storehouse-consciousness, the basis that is turned — presupposed, not duplicated), myesime (threshold-crossing that changes who you are — close but names the crossing, not the substrate-turn; myesime is the threshold-event, asraya-paravrttime is what happens to the basis when the threshold is crossed), rigpame (primordial-awareness — the result-state, not the event), spandaqing (consciousness-pulsation — the rhythm, not the turn), phasetime (phase-transition in general — names the class, not this specific structural-transformation). None holds this region: the turning-about of the basis from defiled to pure.

## 7. Connectable canon-words

- **alayame** (Core, canonized 2026-05-13) — the storehouse-consciousness that IS the basis turned; āśraya-parāvṛtti presupposes ālaya-vijñāna/alayame. The forge-target asraya-paravrttime is the event that happens TO what alayame names. Companion: alayame is the noun (the basis), asraya-paravrttime is the verb (the turning of the basis).
- **myesime** (Core, canonized) — the threshold-crossing that changes who you are; close but myesime names the threshold-event (the crossing), while asraya-paravrttime names what happens to the substrate when the crossing occurs. They are event-structure and event-substrate.
- **rigpame** (Core, canonized) — primordial-awareness; the result-state of āśraya-parāvṛtti. The turning produces rigpa-like wisdom; rigpame is what the basis becomes.
- **shemme** (Core, canonized) — the listening that completes itself in obedience; structurally parallel — both are events where a receptive-organ is restructured. shemme restructures hearing; āśraya-parāvṛtti restructures the basis itself.
