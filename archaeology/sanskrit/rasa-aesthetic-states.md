# Rasa — the felt-aesthetic-state evoked by skilled artistic-projection

## The concept in compact form

**Rasa** (Sanskrit रस, literally "juice, essence, sap, taste, flavor") names *the aesthetically-experienced affective-state* that arises in a receptive-aware connoisseur (*sahṛdaya*, "same-hearted") when a skilled artist projects realm-content through a medium (drama, dance, music, poetry, painting). Bharata's foundational schema names eight rasas + nine bhāvas; Abhinavagupta later added a ninth rasa (*śānta*, the peaceful):

| Rasa | Felt-state | Sthāyī-bhāva (stable underlying emotion) |
|---|---|---|
| **Śṛṅgāra** | erotic-aesthetic love | rati (attraction) |
| **Hāsya** | comic | hāsa (mirth) |
| **Karuṇa** | compassionate-sorrow | śoka (grief) |
| **Raudra** | wrathful | krodha (anger) |
| **Vīra** | heroic-courageous | utsāha (vigor) |
| **Bhayānaka** | fearful | bhaya (fear) |
| **Bībhatsa** | disgust | jugupsā (revulsion) |
| **Adbhuta** | wondrous | vismaya (wonder) |
| **Śānta** | peaceful | śama (tranquility) — Abhinavagupta's addition |

**Crucially:** rasa is NOT the artist's emotion; it is NOT the audience's ordinary-emotion. Rasa is the *aesthetically-transformed* affective-state — distilled, distanced, savored as essence rather than experienced as biography. Abhinavagupta's *rasa-experience-as-camatkāra* (wonder-savoring): rasa is the audience tasting realm-content through the artist's projection, with self-awareness that the tasting is aesthetic-distanced rather than confused with lived-emotion.

## Why this is realm-content

The rasa-doctrine names something *no other tradition has articulated with comparable precision*: the structural-fact that **aesthetic-states are realm-content projected through artistic-mediation**, not subjective-emotions of artist-and-audience. Western aesthetics has approximated this (Schopenhauer's "Will-less knowing," Kant's "disinterested pleasure") but never reached the operational-precision of Bharata + Abhinavagupta.

Under PROBOLE doctrine, rasa is the *received-projection-of-affective-realm-content through medium*. The cathedral's existing affect-vocabulary (qing-family) names felt-bonds; rasa names *felt-aesthetic-states-as-projected-realm-content* — a structurally-distinct category that pairs naturally with morphame (form-as-projection) at the level of medium-and-affect.

rasame will name this — felt-aesthetic-state as received-ordinance (received from the artist's projection of realm-content; cosmic-gift quality preserved by the -me suffix).

## Cross-tradition resonance

- **Greek-Pythagorean:** *ethos-of-modes* — Dorian (firmness), Phrygian (ecstatic), Lydian (relaxed), Mixolydian (grief). Modes carry character-affective-states the soul tunes-to.
- **Arab maqam:** *tarab* (the ecstasy-state induced by skilled performance); each maqam carries a *sayr* (melodic course) with affective-resonance
- **Chinese painting (Xie He):** *qiyun shengdong* (spirit-resonance / life-movement) — the first of the six laws, naming the artwork's resonant-vital-quality
- **Japanese:** *aware* (poignant beauty), *yūgen* (mysterious depth), *wabi-sabi* (imperfect / aged / ephemeral) — affective-aesthetic states the artwork projects
- **Christian Orthodox iconography:** the *kataphatic* affects icons project (joy, awe, compassion, peace)
- **Western music-theory:** Affektenlehre (Baroque doctrine of musical-affects); modern emotion-recognition-in-music research

The rasa-doctrine is the *deepest-articulated* of these traditions. The forge rasame will be the cathedral's canonical-name for the realm-feature that all these traditions independently name.

## Sanskrit etymology and family

- Root: रस् (ras-) — to taste, to relish, to be juicy
- Cognate-family: rasāyana (alchemy-as-tasting-cosmic-juices), rasa-līlā (Krishna's ecstatic-play), rasātmaka (rasa-natured)
- The juice-metaphor is operative: rasa is what you *taste* of the artwork; the artwork *exudes* rasa for the sahṛdaya to taste
