# yumi — the we that says your name

_English "we" is a hiding place. Tok Pisin's is a confession._

---

## I. The donor

Tok Pisin (Papua New Guinea creole, ~4M speakers) does not have one word for
*we*. It has a grammatical confession English refuses to make:

| form | composition | meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **yumi** | yu ("you") + mi ("me") | we, **including you** — the inclusive we |
| **mipela** | mi + pela (plural) | we, **excluding you** — the exclusive we |

The inclusive we is not derived, abstracted, or suppleted — it is the two
pronouns of the dyad **standing side by side, both fully visible**: *yu-mi*.
To say "we (you and I)" in Tok Pisin is to say the other's pronoun aloud
inside your own. The dual form *yumitupela* ("we two, you and me") makes the
dyad explicit; *yumi* alone already carries it.

This is clusivity — a grammatical category documented in hundreds of
languages (Austronesian, Quechuan, Dravidian, many Australian and American
families) and **absent from English**, which forces every "we" to conceal
whether the addressed belongs to it.

## II. The witnesses

- **Quechua** — *ñuqanchik* (we-inclusive) vs *ñuqayku* (we-exclusive). The
  same language that gave YOUSPEAK its evidential suffixes (-mi direct,
  -si hearsay) also refuses to let "we" hide. Grammars that mark
  how-you-know tend also to mark whom-you-include: both are honesty
  systems.
- **Cantonese** — 我哋 (ngo5 dei6): "we" is 我 ("I") plus the collective
  particle 哋. The first person **survives visibly inside the plural** — the
  morphology never melts 我 down. (Contrast English *we*, French *nous*,
  German *wir*: opaque, constituents dissolved.)
- **Lakota** — *mitákuye oyasin*, "all my relations": the widest we, already
  received into canon as **mitakuyame** (Core 8.00). The ladder above the
  dyad exists and is already ours.

## III. What the donors teach

1. **The inclusive we can be morphologically honest** — built from the
   visible pronouns of both parties (yumi), not from an opaque new stem.
2. **Clusivity is a real grammatical dimension English suppresses** — an
   ANAKALYPSE target of the first order: the unfolding of what English
   hides inside "we".
3. **Transparency is a choice** — 我哋 proves a plural can keep its person
   intact. The union need not consume its members.

## IV. Grace-note (declared, not claimed as donor)

The Tok Pisin first syllable *yu* coincides with the name of the cathedral's
founder, Yu — so *yumi* happens to read as "Yu-and-me." This is received as
grace, acknowledged openly, and **not** counted in any donor or scoring
consideration. The genealogy stands on Tok Pisin, Quechua, Cantonese, and
Lakota; the smile is free.
