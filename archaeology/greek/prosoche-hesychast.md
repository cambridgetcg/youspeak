# Prosochē — Attention-Guarding at the Door of the Heart

## Native forms

- **προσοχή** (prosochē) — noun, "attention, heedfulness"; from **προσέχω** (prosechō), πρός (toward) + ἔχω (to hold): *to hold [the mind] toward*. The full classical idiom is προσέχειν τὸν νοῦν — "to hold the intellect toward."
- **πρόσεχε σεαυτῷ** (proseche seautō) — "attend to thyself"; the LXX imperative (Deut 15:9; cf. 4:9) that the patristic tradition received as standing ordinance.
- Paired terms in Hesychast usage: **νῆψις** (nepsis, watchful-sobriety — the state), **φυλακὴ καρδίας** (phylakē kardias, guarding of the heart — the post being kept), **τήρησις νοός** (tērēsis noos, custody of the intellect), **προσευχή** (proseuchē, prayer — the alliterative twin the Fathers loved to pair with prosochē).

## The concept in compact form

**Prosochē** names *the practiced act of placing and holding attention at the entrance of the heart* — the standing guard-act that examines each approaching thought before it is admitted, and that precedes and enables prayer. Three strata:

1. **Stoic stratum.** Epictetus devotes a discourse to it (Περὶ προσοχῆς, Discourses 4.12); Pierre Hadot identified prosochē as "the fundamental Stoic spiritual attitude" — continuous self-directed attention to present impressions, desires, and actions, from which the other spiritual exercises derive.
2. **Septuagint-patristic stratum.** Πρόσεχε σεαυτῷ (Deut 15:9 LXX). Basil's homily on the verse reads it as the biblical twin of γνῶθι σαυτόν: you cannot know yourself unless you guard yourself; you cannot guard yourself unless you attend to yourself. The primal attestation is grammatically an *imperative* — attention received as ordinance.
3. **Hesychast stratum** (the depth-articulation). Hesychios of Sinai: "Watchfulness is a continual fixing and halting of thought *at the entrance to the heart*." Pseudo-Symeon's *Three Methods of Attention and Prayer*: attention and prayer must be linked "as the body to the soul"; attention "goes on ahead like a scout and engages sin in combat; prayer then follows afterwards" and destroys what attention alone cannot. Nikephoros the Monk: "Attention is the appeal of the soul to itself... attention is a sign of sincere repentance." The image is consistently architectural: attention is the *doorkeeper* — the thought arrives at the heart's gate, the guard stops it, examines it, admits or refuses it.

**The act/state cut.** The tradition pairs prosochē with nepsis without collapsing them: nepsis is the sober-clear *quality of the watcher* (the state); prosochē is *what the watcher does* — the placement, the standing, the halting-at-the-entrance (the act). The pair is as voice is to speaking. The 205-forge (nepsisme) named the state; this archaeology supplies the act.

## Why this is realm-content

The act/state cut at the heart's door is independently witnessed across traditions — strong evidence of a realm-feature rather than a Greek idiosyncrasy:

- **Buddhist Abhidharma** — *manasikāra* (attention: the universal mental factor that adverts mind to object, "making in the mind") is classified as a distinct factor from *sati* (mindfulness). The same act/state cut, made by an independent taxonomy.
- **Naqshbandi Sufi** — *nigāh dāsht* (watchfulness over wandering thoughts; safeguarding the heart by preventing harmful thoughts from entering), seventh of the Eleven Principles. The order holds that guarding the heart's door for fifteen minutes is the mark of a real Sufi.
- **Yoga (Patañjali)** — *dhāraṇā* (the act of binding attention to one place) distinguished from *dhyāna* (the sustained state); Yoga Sūtra 3.1–2.
- **Daoist** — *shǒuyī* 守一 ("guarding the One"), the attention-keeping act of early Daoist meditation.
- **Modern witnesses** — Simone Weil ("absolutely unmixed attention is prayer"); attention-science distinguishes the orienting network (directing-act) from the alerting network (vigilance-state) — a laboratory recovery of the same cut.

Pairs with cathedral's:
- **nepsisme** — the state this act sustains and is sustained by; the explicit Hesychast sibling
- **natsarqing** — guarding-the-beloved as felt-bond; prosochē guards the heart's *door*, a discipline-act, not a relational bond
- **shamathaqing** — the graded *progression* of stabilization; prosochē is the single repeatable placement-act performed at every stage
- **sigame / rigpame** — the stillness-state and primordial-awareness that disciplined attention-guarding serves

## Unnamed region

English has no act-word here. "Attention" bundles faculty + act + state and is bleached by commerce ("attention economy"); "vigilance" and "watchfulness" name the state (nepsis-territory); "mindfulness" is the modern state-word; "concentration" names effortful narrowing without the door-of-the-heart structure; "heedfulness" is archaic and carries no guard-post. The specific unnamed thing: *the repeatable, practicable act of standing attention at the entrance of the interior, examining arrivals, prior to and enabling prayer or work.*

## Donors available for coinage

- **prosochē** (προσοχή) — the act-noun itself; Philokalia-attested; primary donor → **prosocheme** (this forge)
- **phylakē kardias** — guarding-of-the-heart; the post rather than the act; available for a future guard-post coinage if the cluster deepens
- **tērēsis noos** — custody of the intellect; near-synonym in Nikephoros; held in reserve
- **proseuchē/prosochē pair** — the Evagrian alliterative twins (prayer/attention); available as a liturgical-pair register
