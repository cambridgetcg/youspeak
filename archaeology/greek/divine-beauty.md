# Greek — Divine Beauty

_The tongue in which the first Christian theology of beauty was forged. Also the source of the Platonic beauty-metaphysics that Christian theology inherited, contested, and transformed._

## Semantic territories (the field)

### Territory A — Beauty-as-ordering (Plato's inheritance)

Central text: *Symposium* 210a–212a. Diotima's ladder of love ascends to the *auto to kalon* — "the beautiful itself", αὐτὸ τὸ καλόν. Plato's Beauty:

- is **uncreated** — not-born, not-dying, not-increasing, not-decreasing (210a)
- is **uniform** — not beautiful in one part and ugly in another (210a)
- is **substantival** — Beauty-as-such, not Beauty-of-X
- **summons** — drawing the lover through the ladder; Q3 lives here in classical form

This establishes Greek beauty's deepest register: ontological, unitary, calling. But Plato's Beauty is a Form in the Platonic hierarchy, not the beauty-of-a-personal-GoD. Christian reception had to transpose.

**Donor available:** *auto-* prefix (αὐτός, -self, in-itself). Platonic philosophy's carrier of Q2. Candidate *autokallos* reclaims this — "beauty-itself" — directly from *auto to kalon*, substantivalizing.

### Territory B — Beauty-as-glory (the LXX crossing)

The Septuagint systematically translates Hebrew *kavod* with Greek *doxa*. This is one of the most load-bearing lexical decisions in the history of theology. Consequences:

- *doxa* absorbs kavod's weight/manifestation sense (becomes "glory" rather than just "opinion")
- Greek philosophy's *doxa* (= opinion/seeming) is overlaid with Hebrew's kavod (= weight/presence)
- A theological term of art emerges: *doxa* as the manifested-weight of divine presence
- New Testament Greek inherits this: *doxa* of Christ, *doxa* of the Father, the *doxa* that will be revealed

*doxa* carries:
- Q1 (divine-specific when qualified)
- Q3 (manifests, calls, attracts)
- partial Q4 (has structural heft but not specifically ordered-to-the-bottom)

It lacks:
- Q2 as beauty-specific — *doxa* is glory-weight; beauty is one of its facets
- Q5 — doxa as substantive is not paired with a beholder-pole

**Donor available:** *δόξα* root. In compound with *κάλλος*, yields *doxakallos* — the beauty-quality of glory; the beauty-pole of divine doxa. Lexical pair with *doxalgia* (δόξα + ἄλγος).

### Territory C — Beauty-as-fittingness (patristic theoprepēs)

The Fathers of the Church needed a word for "what befits GoD". They compounded θεός with πρέπον:

- *theoprepēs* (θεοπρεπής) — God-fitting, worthy-of-GoD
- *theoprepeia* — the quality of being-God-fitting
- Use: Athanasius, the Cappadocians, John of Damascus; of actions, attributes, arguments that are "worthy of GoD"

This names Q4 well (fittingness = ordered-to-the-bottom as structural-rightness). It names Q1 (divine-specific). It is weaker on Q2 (names the fitting-quality, not the beauty-quality directly) and Q3 (does not obviously call forth).

**Donor available:** *theoprepeia* as a reclamation candidate — patristic word retrievable directly. Carries Q1+Q4 cleanly; needs Q2+Q3 argumentation.

### Territory D — Beauty-as-appearing (phanō family)

Greek is phaenomenologically rich about the appearing-of-the-beautiful. The root φαίνω (phainō) — to cause-to-appear, to-shine-forth — gives:

- *phainomai* — to appear
- *phaneros* — manifest
- *epiphaneia* — theophanic appearance
- *orthophanes* (YOUSPEAK 001) — rightness-made-visible

This root carries Q3 (the calling is literally in the appearing) and supports Q2 (the appearing *is* the beauty's self-showing).

**Donor available:** *-phanes* as a suffix. In compound with *κάλλος* yields *kallophanes* — beauty-appearing, structural companion to orthophanes.

### Territory E — Beauty-as-attractive-hour (hōraia)

*hōraios* — timely, ripe, beautiful-in-its-season. Names beauty-as-right-for-its-moment. Strong on Q3 (the hour's calling). Weak on Q1 (not divine-specific), weak on Q4 (seasonal-not-structural).

Less relevant for the forge; noted for completeness.

### Territory F — Beauty-as-grace-gift (charis)

*charis* — grace, favour, the beauty-that-is-given. In Pauline use, *charis* carries both aesthetic and gratuitous senses — grace as gift-beauty.

Charis is closer to "grace" than to "beauty" in Christian theology, but the beauty-tone is residual. Weak on Q2 (names the gift-quality, not the beauty-quality).

## Negative space — what Greek circles but does not name

Every major territory names *an aspect of divine beauty*:

- *auto to kalon* — beauty as ontological unity (but not divine-specific; Platonic)
- *doxa* — beauty as manifested weight (but not specifically beauty; primarily glory)
- *theoprepeia* — beauty as fittingness (but not the beauty-quality itself; the fittingness-quality)
- *epiphaneia / phainō-family* — beauty as event-of-appearing (but not the quality that is appearing)
- *charis* — beauty as given (but not the giver's beauty-quality)
- *kallos* alone — beauty generically (but not divine-specific)

**Greek has no single uncompounded word for uncreated-divine-beauty-in-itself-as-unitary-ontological-quality.** The target can only be reached in Greek by compounding — *theion kallos*, *kallos theou*, and similar phrases. Compounding is also what YOUSPEAK does. The forge's lexical move — *doxakallos* — is a sharper compound of the phrase-tradition already live in Greek.

1. **doxakallos** — δόξα + κάλλος. Primary. All five axes.
2. **kallophanes** — κάλλος + φαίνω. Structural companion to orthophanes. Strong Q3, Q2 partial.

4. **theoprepeia** — θεός + πρέπον. Patristic reclamation. Strong Q1+Q4. Weak Q2+Q3.
5. **holokallos** — ὅλος + κάλλος. Whole-beauty. Strong Q4.
6. **archekallos** — ἀρχή + κάλλος. Origin-beauty. Strong Q2.
7. **morphokallos** — μορφή + κάλλος. Form-beauty. Weaker on all; included for completeness.

The forge proceeds with 1-5, rejecting 6-7 at this stage as duplicates of other candidates' strengths.

## Greek's verdict on the target

Greek *can name* divine beauty, but only by phrase-construction (*theion kallos*) or by compounding (*theokallos*). It cannot name it in one uncompounded native word. This is what makes the forge necessary and legitimate: Greek itself points toward a gap that its native lexicon circles but does not fill.

The compounding YOUSPEAK does — *doxakallos* — is continuous with Greek's own strategy. Greek's theologians already compounded when they needed precision. *doxakallos* is one more such compound, with YOUSPEAK's forge-discipline applied.
