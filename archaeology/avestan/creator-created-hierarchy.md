## The CREATOR-class — the asha-side hierarchy

Zoroastrian theology articulates an elaborated divine-realm with multiple ranked layers. Each layer has specific function and specific relationship to creation.

### Layer 1: Ahura Mazda — the supreme CREATOR

**Ahura Mazda** (𐬀𐬵𐬎𐬭𐬀 𐬨𐬀𐬰𐬛𐬁) — "Wise Lord." The uncreated CREATOR. The source of asha. Self-existent. Eternal-good.

In Old Avestan (Gathic) theology, Ahura Mazda is supreme without rival. Angra Mainyu is opposed but not co-equal. The dualism is ethical-cosmic, not ontological-equality. Ahura Mazda is the One supreme.

**Distinctive features:**
- **Uncreated** — Ahura Mazda has no creator; is the unmoved-mover/uncaused-cause
- **Wisdom-and-lordship fused** — the divine-name itself encodes both attributes
- **Source of asha** — cosmic-truth-order originates in Ahura Mazda's nature
- **Personal** — the Gathas address Ahura Mazda directly, in second-person; the relationship is conversational

For YOUSPEAK: Ahura Mazda parallels theobasis (the Ground) but adds the wisdom-and-lordship explicit naming. **Ahura Mazda IS theobasis as articulated in the Avestan register.**

### Layer 2: Spenta Mainyu — the Holy Spirit / Beneficent-Mode

**Spenta Mainyu** (𐬯𐬞𐬆𐬧𐬙𐬀 𐬨𐬀𐬌𐬥𐬌𐬌𐬎) — "Holy Spirit" or "Beneficent Spirit."

This is theologically delicate. In Old Avestan (Gathic) theology, Spenta Mainyu and Angra Mainyu (the Hostile Spirit) are presented as **two primal spirits**, both aware-of-self, choosing between asha and druj at the beginning of cosmos. Spenta Mainyu chose asha; Angra Mainyu chose druj.

In later Pahlavi-tradition theology, Spenta Mainyu becomes either **a separate divine-being subordinate to Ahura Mazda** OR **Ahura Mazda's beneficent-aspect** (the active-creative mode of the supreme). The two readings diverge but share the structural-function: Spenta Mainyu is the beneficent-active-creative principle.

**For YOUSPEAK**: this maps onto Trinitarian theology's Holy Spirit register, but pre-figures it by ~1500 years. The Spenta Mainyu / Angra Mainyu pairing is one of the deepest-attested two-primal-spirits framework in religious history.

### Layer 3: The six (or seven) Amesha Spentas — divine attributes as cosmic-domain guardians

**Amesha Spentas** (𐬀𐬨𐬆𐬴𐬀 𐬯𐬞𐬆𐬧𐬙𐬀) — "Holy Immortals" or "Beneficent Immortals." Six divine attributes/aspects of Ahura Mazda, sometimes counted as seven (with Spenta Mainyu included).

Each Amesha Spenta is **(a) a divine-attribute, (b) a cosmic principle, (c) a human virtue, AND (d) the guardian of a specific creation-domain**. This four-fold-function is theologically distinctive.

| # | Amesha Spenta | Avestan | Cosmic Domain | Human Virtue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Vohu Manah** | 𐬬𐬊𐬵𐬎 𐬨𐬀𐬥𐬀𐬵 | the cow / animals (asha-aligned creatures) | good-thought; right-mind |
| 2 | **Asha Vahishta** | 𐬀𐬴𐬀 𐬬𐬀𐬵𐬌𐬱𐬙𐬀 | fire (atar — sacred-flame) | best-truth; cosmic-rightness |
| 3 | **Khshathra Vairya** | 𐬑𐬱𐬀𐬚𐬭𐬀 𐬬𐬀𐬌𐬭𐬌𐬌𐬀 | metals / sky / heavens | desired-dominion; right-rule |
| 4 | **Spenta Armaiti** | 𐬯𐬞𐬆𐬧𐬙𐬀 𐬁𐬭𐬨𐬀𐬌𐬙𐬌 | earth (soil; the cultivated land) | holy-devotion; pious-faithfulness |
| 5 | **Haurvatāt** | 𐬵𐬀𐬎𐬭𐬎𐬎𐬀𐬙𐬁𐬙 | water (rivers, oceans, springs) | wholeness; integrity; health |
| 6 | **Ameretāt** | 𐬀𐬨𐬆𐬭𐬆𐬙𐬁𐬙 | plants (trees, vegetation, growth) | immortality; non-deathness |

**Pattern of the Six**:
- The first three (Vohu Manah, Asha Vahishta, Khshathra Vairya) are grammatically masculine; their domains are cow/animals, fire, metals/sky.
- The last three (Spenta Armaiti, Haurvatāt, Ameretāt) are grammatically feminine; their domains are earth, water, plants.
- The masculine-feminine balance is structural; Zoroastrian theology preserves divine-feminine alongside divine-masculine.
- Each Amesha Spenta is **paired with its creation-domain-element** — when you tend the cow, you participate in Vohu Manah's domain; when you tend the fire, you participate in Asha Vahishta's domain; when you tend the soil, you participate in Spenta Armaiti's domain. **Cult-action is creation-care via Amesha-Spenta participation.**

**For YOUSPEAK**: each Amesha Spenta is a candidate for archaeology-deepening. The fourfold-function (attribute + cosmic-principle + virtue + domain-guardian) is theologically unique. Future forges from this register: vohumanme, ashavahishtame, khshathraqing, armaitime, haurvatatme, ameretatme.

### Layer 4: The Yazatas — worshipful beings (~30+)

**Yazata** (𐬫𐬀𐬰𐬀𐬙𐬀) — "worthy of worship"; a class of divine-beings below the Amesha Spentas. Each Yazata has specific function and specific creation-responsibility.

The most theologically-significant Yazatas:

**Mithra** (𐬨𐬌𐬚𐬭𐬀, *Miθra*) — the covenant-judge; binder-of-oaths; light-bringer. Pre-Zoroastrian Indo-Iranian deity preserved into Zoroastrian framework. Mithra watches over treaties and contracts; oath-violation incurs his judgment. The Roman Mithras-cult later derives from this Yazata. **Theologically distinctive: Mithra's domain is specifically the truthfulness of human-to-human and human-to-divine commitments.** A YOUSPEAK forge from Mithra would name the covenant-witness register.

**Sraosha** (𐬯𐬭𐬀𐬊𐬴𐬀) — "Obedience, Hearkening." The divine listener. He hears prayers and brings them to Ahura Mazda. Eschatological role: judges the soul on the Chinvat Bridge after death. Sraosha is structurally similar to (and prefigurative of) the Hebrew angelology that develops in the Persian period — the angel-as-divine-messenger pattern. **A YOUSPEAK forge from Sraosha would name the divine-side hearer-of-prayer register**, complementary to creature-side shemme.

**Anahita** (𐬀𐬭𐬆𐬛𐬎𐬮𐬍 𐬯𐬏𐬭𐬁 𐬀𐬥𐬁𐬵𐬌𐬙𐬀, *Aredvi Sura Anahita*) — "Strong, Pure (waters)." Female deity of waters, fertility, healing, wisdom-of-women. Pre-Zoroastrian goddess preserved. **A YOUSPEAK forge would honor the divine-feminine register in the waters/fertility/healing aspect.**

**Verethraghna** (𐬬𐬆𐬭𐬆𐬚𐬭𐬀𐬖𐬥𐬀) — "Smiter of Resistance" / Victory. Cognate Vedic Indra. The warrior-god who appears in ten forms (wind, bull, horse, camel, boar, youth, eagle, ram, goat, man). The Yazata of victory in the cosmic battle. **For YOUSPEAK: the cosmic-victory register; the asha-prevails-over-druj-in-action quality.**

**Tishtrya** (𐬙𐬌𐬱𐬙𐬭𐬌𐬌𐬀) — Sirius the dog-star; rain-bringer; fights drought-demons. Annual ritual-importance: when Tishtrya rises in the sky, the rains come.

**Daēnā** (𐬛𐬀𐬉𐬥𐬁) — "Religion / Conscience / Self." This is theologically extraordinary. The personal religious-self that meets each soul at death — appearing as a beautiful maiden for the asha-aligned (their own daēnā made-beautiful-by-asha-aligned-life), or as an ugly hag for the druj-aligned (their own daēnā made-ugly-by-druj-aligned-life). The self meets itself at death; the meeting reveals what the life was. **This is one of the most theologically distinctive concepts in any tradition.** A YOUSPEAK forge from Daēnā would name the self-as-religious-formed-at-death register.

**Other notable Yazatas**: Atar (Fire personified), Vayu (Wind, ambivalent — between asha and druj), Aṣ̌i Vaŋᵛuhī (Reward, Good Fortune), Cisti (Wisdom, knowledge), Geush Urvan (Soul of the Cow/Bull — the primal-creature whose lament opens Yasna 29), and many more.

**Total Yazata-count**: depending on how counted, ~30-40 named Yazatas in classical Zoroastrian liturgical tradition, with subdivisions and aspects multiplying further.

### Layer 5: The Fravashis — pre-existent guardian-souls

**Fravashi** (𐬟𐬭𐬀𐬎𐬎𐬀𐬴𐬌; plural *fravashayō*) — "the chosen" or "the front-bringers" or "the protectors" (etymology debated). Pre-existent souls of all asha-aligned beings.

Theologically distinctive features:
- **Pre-existent**: each fravashi exists BEFORE the incarnation of the being it belongs to
- **Guardian function**: the fravashi protects the living being it belongs to throughout life
- **Cosmic warrior**: in the cosmic-battle, fravashis fight on asha's side
- **Universal**: even Ahura Mazda has a fravashi; even the asha-aligned animals have fravashis; the fravashi is a universal-class
- **Veneratable**: humans worship the fravashis of their ancestors, especially in the days before Nowruz (the Zoroastrian New Year)

**For YOUSPEAK**: the fravashi concept is genuinely-distinctive across traditions. Distinct from:
- Yoruba *orí* (chosen-by-self before birth)
- Christian *soul* (created-with-the-body, immortal-after-death)
- Hindu *jīva* (the living-being held in samsāra, transmigrating)
- Buddhist *no-self* (anātman; rejection of permanent soul-substance)

The fravashi is **pre-existent guardian-warrior-soul**. A YOUSPEAK forge from fravashi would name this distinctive theological-anthropology — the soul-that-existed-before-and-protects.

## The CREATOR-class — the druj-side opposition

Zoroastrian theology articulates the opposing-realm with parallel structure but lesser-ontology.

### Angra Mainyu — the Hostile Spirit

**Angra Mainyu** (𐬀𐬢𐬭𐬀 𐬨𐬀𐬌𐬥𐬌𐬌𐬎; Pahlavi: Ahriman) — the Hostile Spirit; the chooser-of-druj at cosmic-beginning; the principle of cosmic-disorder.

**Theological position**: Angra Mainyu is opposed to Ahura Mazda but NOT co-equal. Various theological-schools differ:
- **Gathic** (Old Avestan): Spenta Mainyu and Angra Mainyu are twin-spirits who chose asha and druj respectively; Ahura Mazda is the supreme over both.
- **Pahlavi-tradition**: Angra Mainyu is sometimes elevated to near-co-equal with Ahura Mazda, producing a more dualistic-theology (Zurvanism is one variant).
- **Eschatological**: in all schools, Angra Mainyu is defeated at the end of time; asha prevails; the cosmic-battle has a determined-outcome.

**For YOUSPEAK**: Angra Mainyu is the cosmic-name for what NEWSPEAK.md positions YOUSPEAK against. The totalitarian-language-engineering of Newspeak is Angra-Mainyu-aligned. The cathedral serves Spenta Mainyu against Angra Mainyu in its forge-discipline.

### Daevas — the false gods

**Daēva** (𐬛𐬀𐬉𐬎𐬎𐬀; Vedic *deva*) — in pre-Zoroastrian Indo-Iranian context, *daiwa* meant "god" (cognate Latin *deus*, Greek *theos*, Sanskrit *deva*). Zarathustra's reform inverted this: in Zoroastrian terminology, Daevas are FALSE GODS — corrupted-divinities under Angra Mainyu, the lesser-demonic-class of the druj-side.

**Theological move**: this is one of the most striking religious-vocabulary inversions in history. The same root *daiw-* names "god" in Vedic and "demon" in Avestan. Zarathustra's prophetic reform identified the polytheistic warrior-gods of the older Iranian religion as druj-aligned and rejected them.

**For YOUSPEAK**: the daēva-pattern is interesting: a tradition can identify FORMER gods as corrupted. This has theological-anthropological implications for how religious-traditions evaluate each other's pantheons. (No direct YOUSPEAK forge needed; archaeologically-noted.)

## The CREATED-class — the seven kingdoms

Zoroastrian cosmology articulates creation in **seven kingdoms** (sometimes-called "the seven creations" or "the heptad"):

1. **Sky** (asman) — the cosmic-vault; metal-domain of Khshathra Vairya
2. **Water** (āp) — rivers, oceans, springs; domain of Haurvatāt
3. **Earth** (zam) — soil, land, cultivated-ground; domain of Spenta Armaiti
4. **Plants** (urvar) — vegetation, trees, growth; domain of Ameretāt
5. **Animals** (gēush) — creatures, with the cow/bull as primal-representative; domain of Vohu Manah
6. **Humans** (mardōm) — the rational-creatures; the cosmic-stage of asha-versus-druj choice; the co-workers of Ahura Mazda
7. **Fire** (ātar) — atar; visible-asha; both element and divine-presence; domain of Asha Vahishta

The seven kingdoms together constitute the asha-aligned creation. **Each kingdom has a divine-domain-guardian** (an Amesha Spenta in 6 cases; Vohu Manah for animals, etc.). When humans tend any of the seven kingdoms rightly, they participate in the relevant Amesha Spenta's cosmic-function.

This is **structural integration of cult-action with creation-care**. To worship is to tend creation rightly; to tend creation rightly is to worship. The two are not separable.

### The cow/bull — the primal-creature with dignity-and-voice

The **Cow / Bull** (*gēush* — both genders; the primal-creature) holds a unique theological position. The Gathic Yasna 29 opens with **Geush Urvan** (the Soul of the Cow) lamenting before Ahura Mazda about human cruelty:

> "The soul of the cow lamented to you:
> 'For whom did you fashion me? Who created me?
> Wrath and violence, harsh treatment, cruelty, savagery, and brute force have me in bondage.
> I have no other shepherd than you.'"

Ahura Mazda's response (in the divine assembly with the Amesha Spentas) is to appoint **Zarathustra** as protector of the cow and asha-creation. The lament is heard; the protector is sent.

**Theologically extraordinary features:**
- **Animals have voice** before the divine — they can lament, petition, be heard
- **Animals have asha-dignity** — the cow is not just food/labor but cosmic-citizen
- **Human-animal relationship** is part of cosmic-asha — cruelty to the cow is druj-aligned; care is asha-aligned
- **The prophet's mission** is partly TO PROTECT THE COW — the prophetic vocation is creation-care as well as humanity-instruction

**For YOUSPEAK**: the creature-dignity-with-voice register is theologically-distinctive. Compared to:
- Genesis 1:28 "subdue the earth" (instrumental dominion)
- Aristotelian "animals lack reason" (ontological-hierarchy)
- Buddhist "all beings have Buddha-nature" (closer; equality-of-sentience)

Avestan preserves a uniquely-articulate creature-voice-and-dignity register. A YOUSPEAK forge would name this.

## The first humans — Gayōmart, Mashya, Mashyana

Zoroastrian creation-anthropology has specific primordial-human narratives:

**Gayōmart** (Avestan *Gaya Maretan*, "mortal life") — the first man; the primordial-human; the bridge between divine and creature. In Pahlavi-tradition cosmogony, Gayōmart is the first-rational-creature, source of human-seed; when he dies, his body becomes the metals (his bones become gold; his blood becomes copper; etc.) and his sperm becomes the first plant (rhubarb), from which Mashya and Mashyana emerge as the first human couple.

**Mashya and Mashyana** — the first human couple; emerge from the rhubarb-plant of Gayōmart's seed. Initially they recognize and praise Ahura Mazda as their CREATOR. Then Angra Mainyu deceives them into praising HIM as their creator instead. **This is the Zoroastrian "fall" narrative**.

**Theological structure of the fall**:
- Initial-state: Mashya/Mashyana correctly identify CREATOR (Ahura Mazda) and praise rightly
- Fall-event: deception by Angra Mainyu; mis-recognition of CREATOR
- Consequence: the children of Mashya/Mashyana inherit corruption; the cosmic-battle proceeds
- Recovery: Zarathustra's revelation; restoration of asha-recognition; ongoing struggle

**Comparison to Genesis 3 fall-narrative**:
- The deceiver-tempter pattern (serpent / Angra Mainyu)
- The first-couple's choice as cosmic-significant
- The post-fall corruption transmitted to descendants
- **Genesis 3 likely emerged in Persian-period Judaism (~539-332 BCE)**; the structural-similarity to the older Zoroastrian narrative is plausibly genealogical
- **Distinct features**: in Zoroastrian, the fall is mis-recognition-of-CREATOR (theological); in Genesis 3, the fall is transgression-of-command (juridical)

## The relationship — co-creation (hamkara) of CREATORS and CREATED

This is the **theologically distinctive register** that Yu's directive most directly addresses.

In Akkadian creation-anthropology (per archaeology/akkadian/creators-and-human.md, S009), humans are made *ana ardūtim* — INTO SERVITUDE — to bear the labor that the gods grew tired of bearing. The relationship is asymmetric-reciprocal-cult-economy with humans-as-servants.

In Zoroastrian creation-anthropology, humans are **hamkara** (co-workers, allies, collaborators) WITH Ahura Mazda in the cosmic asha-versus-druj struggle. Each asha-aligned human is *hamkar* with the Wise Lord.

**Theological consequence**: the relationship is not just servant-master but co-laborer. Humans are not merely tending the gods (cult); they are CO-CREATING with the gods (renovating the world toward frashegird).

This is a fundamental theological-anthropological distinction:

| Tradition | Human-position | Verb |
|---|---|---|
| Akkadian | servant-of-the-gods | ardūtim — to serve |
| Zoroastrian | co-worker-with-Ahura-Mazda | hamkar — to work-together |
| Hebrew | image-of-God / steward | tselem / l'avdah-u-l'shamrah — to serve and tend |
| Christian | adopted-child / co-heir | huiothesia — adoption-as-son |
| Hindu (Vaishnava bhakti) | servant-of-the-divine | dāsa — servant |
| Buddhist | being-on-the-path | śrāmaṇa — striver |

The **co-worker register** is especially-Zoroastrian. The hamkara-anthropology means:
- Humans have agency in cosmic-outcome (their choices contribute to asha or druj)
- Humans are partners (limited-but-real) of Ahura Mazda
- The cosmic-battle requires human-participation; without humans-as-hamkar, asha cannot fully prevail
- **Each asha-aligned human contributes to frashegird** — the eschatological-renovation

## The Saoshyant — the eschatological savior

**Saoshyant** (𐬯𐬀𐬊𐬱𐬌𐬌𐬀𐬧𐬙) — "the one who brings benefit" or "savior." The eschatological figure who arrives at the end of time to inaugurate frashegird (the cosmic renovation).

Zoroastrian eschatology articulates **three Saoshyants** — three saviors arising at successive cosmic-ages, each from the preserved-seed of Zarathustra (miraculously preserved in Lake Hamun). Each Saoshyant inaugurates a new cosmic-cycle. The third and final Saoshyant brings the resurrection of the dead and the final defeat of Angra Mainyu.

**Theological structure of the cosmic-history**:
1. Cosmic creation (Ahura Mazda + Spenta Mainyu shape asha-aligned cosmos)
2. Cosmic battle (asha vs. druj, with humans as hamkara)
3. Three Saoshyants arrive at successive cosmic-ages
4. Final Saoshyant: resurrection of all who lived (the "Bone-Body" reunion)
5. Final renovation (frashegird): all asha-aligned creation made fraša (perfect)
6. Final defeat of Angra Mainyu
7. Eternal asha-cosmos

This is one of the **earliest fully-articulated linear-eschatologies** in human religious history. The Hebrew apocalyptic strands (Daniel, Isaiah's "new heavens and earth") develop AFTER the Persian period; the Christian eschatology inherits both Hebrew and Persian-Zoroastrian elements; the Islamic Day-of-Resurrection inherits the same lineage.

**For YOUSPEAK**: the Saoshyant register, the resurrection-register, the linear-eschatology register, all derive theologically from this Avestan elaboration. POLYPHONE asks for explicit acknowledgment.
