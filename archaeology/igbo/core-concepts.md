# Igbo — Core Concept Field

_Excavation of semantic terrain. Igbo is a living West African tongue of the Niger-Congo family (Volta-Niger branch), primary language of the Igbo peoples of southeastern Nigeria and their global diaspora. Igbo preserves a distinctive philosophical vocabulary whose conceptual structures diverge from Yoruba's (its sister-tongue opened in Session 003) in theologically-meaningful ways._

_The most significant Igbo/Yoruba divergence: where Yoruba's **orí** names the destined-self as ontologically-internal (the pre-chosen core of one), Igbo's **chi** names the destined-companion as relationally-external (the guardian-spirit that accompanies one). Yoruba metaphysics is internal-alignment; Igbo metaphysics is relational-agreement. Both are valid responses to the same underlying question (what is destiny's relation to self?) — and together they enrich YOUSPEAK's POLYPHONE beyond what either tongue alone provides._

_The Constitution's POLYPHONE foundation is operative here: Niger-Congo now has TWO archaeology files (Yoruba + Igbo), supporting a genuinely-intra-family diversity rather than a single-representative approach._

---

## Premise

Igbo's distinctive philosophical contributions:

1. **Destiny as relational companionship (chi)** — each person has a chi that accompanies them through life; destiny is lived WITH the chi, not merely inside the self. The Igbo proverb *onye kwe, chi ya ekwe* — "when a person agrees, their chi agrees" — names a dual agency unknown in European metaphysics.

2. **Personhood as achieved-quality (mmadu)** — *mmadu* literally means "there is life" or "the life is" (mma = "beauty/goodness" + du = "is/stands"); as a noun, it names personhood specifically as the ACHIEVED quality of being-fully-human. Chinua Achebe made this central: "a man is a man by the strength of his deeds, not by the accident of his birth."

3. **Truth-authority as material-staff (ofo)** — the *ofo* is a physical staff held by elders; it represents moral authority grounded in integrity-of-lineage. Truth-speaking requires ofo; without ofo, speech has no binding weight. This materializes authority in a way European legal-philosophical traditions abstract.

4. **Intention as uche** — mind, will, intention, purpose as one unified faculty. English disaggregates these; Igbo holds them as one.

5. **Custom-as-lived-ontology (omenala)** — "what-is-done-in-the-land," tradition as the substrate of reality. Omenala is not custom-as-habit but custom-as-cosmos; the ways-of-the-ancestors ARE the structure of the lived world.

## The Core Seven (donors available for coinage)

### 1. chi (chí)

**Gloss:** personal destined-guide-spirit; guardian-spirit allocated to each person before birth; the companion who accompanies one's life and with whom one's destiny is co-agreed.

**Igbo metaphysics:** each person has a chi allocated at birth. Chi is not identical to the self; chi is a companion. When a person acts well, their chi acts with them; when a person suffers misfortune inexplicably, their chi is said to have caused it (chi efuo m = "my chi has failed me"). The proverb *onye kwe, chi ya ekwe* ("when one agrees, their chi agrees") names the co-agency pattern: personal agency and chi-agency must align for action to succeed.

**Distinctions English fuses:**
- English "destiny" bundles: pre-determined shape, allocated fate, personal trajectory. Chi specifically names the COMPANION through which destiny is mediated.
- English "guardian angel" is close in the Christian-diaspora register but subordinates the relationship under a divine hierarchy. Chi is more autonomous.
- Yoruba *orí* is the destined-core-of-self (internal); Igbo chi is the destined-companion (relational).
- Mandarin 氣 (qì/chi, "vital breath/energy") is unrelated despite English-transliteration collision; Igbo chi is NOT the Taoist qi.

**Cautionary note:** The English-transliteration "chi" creates collision-risk with Taoist qì. For YOUSPEAK donor-use, consider maintaining the Igbo tone-mark (chí) or using disambiguating compounds.

**YOUSPEAK donor use:** morpheme for "destined-companion," "relational-destiny," "co-agency." Candidate compounds: *chikin* (the kinship-with-one's-chi), *chiance* (the state of chi-agreement).

---

### 2. mmadu (mmadụ)

**Gloss:** person; full-human-being; personhood as achieved-quality.

**Etymology:** *mma* (beauty, goodness, moral-aesthetic rightness) + *du/dụ* (is, stands, exists). "There-is-beauty" or "goodness-stands." Personhood as the STANDING of goodness/beauty in a life.

**Igbo metaphysics:** Mmadu is not the biological fact of being human (for which Igbo has other terms). Mmadu is the ACHIEVED personhood — the quality of having lived in a way that realizes the full moral-aesthetic potential of human life. Chinua Achebe foregrounded this: a person is a mmadu by their deeds, not by birth alone. One can fail to become mmadu; one can cease to be mmadu; one can be mmadu only partially.

**Distinctions English fuses:**
- English "person" bundles biological-human and moral-full-person. Mmadu specifies only the moral-aesthetic-achieved dimension.
- English "adult" is chronological; mmadu is achievement-based.
- Yoruba *ọmọlúwàbí* is close (complete-character-person) but emphasizes well-formedness-by-upbringing; mmadu emphasizes achieved-deeds.

**YOUSPEAK donor use:** morpheme for "person-as-moral-achievement," "human-as-standing-goodness." Candidate compounds: *mmaduance* (the state of being-mmadu), *mmadukin* (the bond-between-mmadu-and-mmadu).

---

### 3. uche

**Gloss:** mind, will, intention, thought, purposiveness — as one unified faculty.

**Igbo metaphysics:** uche names the conscious-volitional-cognitive dimension as ONE thing. Where English disaggregates (mind/will/intention/purpose), Igbo holds them together. A person's uche is their full deliberative capacity, not merely their rationality or their will or their intention.

**Distinctions English fuses:**
- English "mind" is cognitive; uche includes volition.
- English "will" is volitional; uche includes cognition.
- English "intention" is directional; uche includes the full capacity for deliberation.
- Latin *voluntas* is closer to uche but still narrower.

**YOUSPEAK donor use:** morpheme for "unified-deliberative-faculty." Candidate compounds: *uchance* (state of uche-active deliberation), *ucheome* (uche + omenala = deliberation-aligned-with-tradition).

---

### 4. ofo (ọ̀fọ̀)

**Gloss:** truth-staff; the physical wooden staff held by elders and lineage heads; symbol of moral-authority-grounded-in-integrity-of-lineage.

**Igbo metaphysics:** Ofo is ontologically load-bearing. An elder holding ofo can speak with binding truth because the ofo itself carries the weight of lineage-integrity. To speak against ofo is to call down consequence; to speak with ofo is to commit the ancestors. Ofo materializes truth-authority — it is not merely a symbol of authority but an instrument that transmits and embodies it. A person "has ofo" (has moral standing from integrity) whether or not they hold the physical staff.

**Distinctions English fuses:**
- English "authority" is legal-political; ofo is lineage-integrity-grounded.
- English "moral standing" is abstract; ofo is materially-mediated through kinship-lines.
- Yoruba *àṣẹ* (performative-force) is close but differently-grounded: àṣẹ is the power-to-actualize (any divine or human source); ofo is the specifically-lineage-anchored authority-to-speak-binding-truth.

**YOUSPEAK donor use:** morpheme for "lineage-integrity-authority," "truth-staff-grounded speech." Candidate compounds: *ofoance* (the state of speaking-with-ofo), *ofokin* (the bond-of-shared-ofo-inheritance).

---

### 5. agwa

**Gloss:** character, conduct, habitual-manner-of-being; the visible expression of inner disposition.

**Igbo metaphysics:** Agwa is what OTHERS see of a person's character through their repeated actions. Where uche is the inner deliberative faculty, agwa is the outer consistent conduct. Agwa is judged across time; a person's agwa is known by their community over years, not by single acts. Yoruba *ìwà* is the closest sister-term; both refer to character-as-manifest-habitual-conduct.

**Distinctions English fuses:**
- English "character" is too abstract; agwa is specifically the observed-and-judged-by-community dimension.
- English "behavior" is momentary; agwa is consistent-across-time.
- Yoruba *ìwà* is close (near-synonym across the sister-tongues).

**YOUSPEAK donor use:** morpheme for "character-as-manifest-habitual," "community-judged-conduct." Pairs naturally with ìwà for intra-Niger-Congo diplosemic experiments.

---

### 6. obi

**Gloss:** heart (literal and metaphorical); also "homestead, compound" in some Igbo dialects; the center of felt-being.

**Igbo metaphysics:** Obi names the heart as the locus of felt-experience (joy, sorrow, longing, satisfaction) and also — in a remarkable semantic extension — the home-as-center-of-family-life. One's obi is both interior (the heart) and exterior (the homestead). Felt-center and lived-center are named by one word.

**Distinctions English fuses:**
- English "heart" is anatomical and metaphorical-emotional.
- English "home" is physical-dwelling.
- Obi fuses them: the felt-center IS the lived-center.

**YOUSPEAK donor use:** morpheme for "felt-and-lived-center." Candidate compounds: *obiance* (the state of being-in-one's-obi), *obikin* (kinship-of-the-heart-and-home).

---

### 7. omenala (omenala / omenani)

**Gloss:** tradition; "what-is-done-in-the-land"; the customary-law-as-lived-ontology.

**Etymology:** *omen-* (that-which-is-done) + *ala* (land, earth, earth-goddess). The ways-of-the-land.

**Igbo metaphysics:** Omenala is not custom-as-habit but custom-as-cosmos. The ways of the ancestors ARE the structure of reality; to violate omenala is to transgress not merely social-norm but metaphysical-order. Ala (the earth-goddess) sanctions omenala; grave violations are *nso ala* (abomination to the earth) and require specific ritual reparation.

**Distinctions English fuses:**
- English "tradition" is weak; omenala is ontologically load-bearing.
- English "custom" is descriptive; omenala is prescriptive-and-cosmic.
- English "customary law" is juridical; omenala is theological.

**YOUSPEAK donor use:** morpheme for "lived-tradition-as-ontology." Candidate compounds: *omenalance* (the state of living-within-omenala), *omekin* (tradition-kin).

## Uniqueness vector

What Igbo carries that no other Niger-Congo tongue so cleanly names:

1. **Destiny as relational dual-agency (chi)** — destiny is not inside the self; it is agreed-with.
2. **Personhood as achieved standing (mmadu)** — "person" is earned through deeds, not granted by birth.
3. **Truth-authority as materially-mediated (ofo)** — the staff is not symbol; it is instrument.
4. **Custom as cosmic-ontology (omenala)** — tradition is not practice but world-structure.
5. **Cognitive-volitional unity (uche)** — thinking and willing are one faculty, not two.

Paired with Yoruba:
- **Chi (Igbo) : orí (Yoruba)** — destined-companion : destined-core-self. Relational-external vs. ontological-internal. Two valid metaphysical responses to the destiny-question.
- **Mmadu (Igbo) : ọmọlúwàbí (Yoruba)** — achievement-earned personhood : cultivation-raised personhood. Deeds-tested vs. formation-completed.
- **Ofo (Igbo) : àṣẹ (Yoruba)** — lineage-integrity authority : performative-force-authority. Material-staff vs. cosmic-power.
- **Agwa (Igbo) ≈ ìwà (Yoruba)** — community-judged conduct : character-as-manifest. Near-synonyms; intra-Niger-Congo convergence.

The two archaeology files together constitute a **Niger-Congo philosophical pairing** within YOUSPEAK. Future forges can deploy Igbo-Yoruba compounds for intra-family diplosemic work, or Igbo-other-family compounds for cross-family polyphone.
