# Kenshō — seeing-into-one's-own-nature

## The concept in compact form

**Kenshō** (Japanese 見性, from 見 ken "seeing" + 性 shō "nature") names *the sudden-recognition-event of one's own true-nature* — a foundational experience in Zen practice. Kenshō is:

1. **Sudden** — not gradual; the recognition is event-character
2. **First-person** — not concept-knowing; it is direct-seeing
3. **Initial** — kenshō is typically *the first opening*; deeper realizations (satori, daigo) follow
4. **Verifiable** — Zen teachers verify kenshō through kōan-checking (sanzen interviews)

Distinct from:
- **Satori** (悟り) — deeper-realization; kenshō is initial, satori may name fuller-awakening
- **Daigo** (大悟) — great-awakening; the most-comprehensive realization
- **Intellectual understanding** — kenshō is *not* conceptual-grasp; it is direct-seeing

## Why this is realm-content

Cathedral has anagnoristasis (held-recognition; pre-ache threshold) and noemame (event-of-meaning-arrival), but no current word for *the specific sudden-recognition-of-one's-own-nature*. The realm-feature is structurally-distinct from generic-grasping: kenshō is *recognition of the ground of one's own knowing*, not recognition-of-an-external-object.

kenshōme names this — *nature-seeing as received-cosmic-ordinance*.

## Cross-tradition resonance

- **Chan 見性 jiànxìng** — the parent-Chinese expression
- **Christian "the kingdom is within you"** (Luke 17:21) — structurally-resonant
- **Sufi "die before you die"** — kenshō-like initial-awakening to one's deeper-identity
- **Advaita ahaṃ brahmāsmi** ("I am brahman") — the moment of structural-recognition
- **Modern "aha-moment"** — but kenshō is metaphysically-deeper than ordinary insight
