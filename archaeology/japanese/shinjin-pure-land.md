# Shinjin — the entrusting-heart that arrives as gift

## The concept in compact form

**Shinjin** (信心: 信 *shin* "trust / truth / sincerity" + 心 *jin* (shin) "heart-mind") is the central term of Shinran's Jōdo Shinshū. It names *the settled entrusting-heart toward Amida that the practicer does not and cannot generate* — the trust is itself given by Amida's Other-Power (tariki 他力), arriving through Amida's directing-of-virtue (ekō 回向). Four structural marks:

1. **Non-self-generated** — shinjin is not worked up by practice, resolve, or sincerity-effort. Per Ueda Yoshifumi: "Shinjin is the mind of Amida Buddha given to and realized in a person." Dobbins: "faith is none other than Amida's mind transmuted in the person."
2. **Settled** — once arisen, the practicer joins the *stage of the truly settled* (shōjōju 正定聚); Shinran calls shinjin *diamond-like* (kongōshin 金剛心): "This mind, in its profound entrusting, is like diamond; it is not shaken, confused, defeated, or broken by people of other views" (Chapter on Shinjin, §45).
3. **Beyond calculation** — shinjin excludes *hakarai* (計らい, self-power contrivance). Any attempt to manufacture, verify, or strategize the trust is precisely what the trust is free of.
4. **Causally ultimate** — "The true cause of attaining great nirvana is shinjin alone" (*shinjin shōin* 信心正因; Chapter on Shinjin, §1). Not the nembutsu-as-act, not merit: the given heart.

## Native forms and provisional glosses

| Term | Form | Provisional gloss |
|---|---|---|
| shinjin | 信心 | entrusting-heart; true-real mind given by Amida |
| tariki | 他力 | Other-Power (donor of tarikiqing, 211) |
| jiriki | 自力 | self-power; the contrast-pole |
| hakarai | 計らい | calculation, contrivance; what shinjin excludes |
| ekō | 回向 | directing-of-virtue; in Shinran, ALWAYS from Amida toward beings, never from beings toward the Buddha — his sharpest doctrinal inversion |
| sanshin | 三心 | the three minds of the 18th Vow (sincere mind 至誠心, joyful entrusting 信楽, aspiration-for-birth 欲生); Shinran reads all three as the one mind Amida gives |
| kongōshin | 金剛心 | diamond-like mind; shinjin's indestructible settledness |
| shōjōju | 正定聚 | the stage of the truly settled; assurance in this life |
| anjin | 安心 | settled-mind; Rennyo's (1415-1499) preferred near-synonym — later stratum, debated in the anjin-rondai controversies |
| nembutsu | 念仏 | Name-saying; after shinjin, gratitude-expression, not means |

Etymological depth: 信心 renders Sanskrit *prasanna-citta* / *citta-prasāda* — the clear-settled-trusting mind; *prasāda* in wider Indic usage also means "grace, gift." The Sanskrit ancestor already carries the double sense — settled-clarity that is *also* the given — which Shinran's doctrine makes fully explicit.

## Attested sources

- Shinran, *Kyōgyōshinshō* (顕浄土真実教行証文類), Chapter on Shinjin: "It is shinjin that actualizes Amida's profound and vast benefiting of others"; "the ocean of shinjin that is itself suchness or true reality" (§1); the three-minds analysis (§§ on 至誠心・信楽・欲生); diamond-mind (§45).
- *Tannishō* (Yuien's record of Shinran): "When the thought of saying the nembutsu emerges decisively... in that very moment we receive the ultimate benefit of being grasped never to be abandoned" (§1).
- Tanluan (曇鸞, 476-542), *Ōjōronchū* — the Other-Power reading Shinran radicalizes; Shandao (善導, 613-681) — the three-minds exegesis Shinran inherits and inverts.
- *Collected Works of Shinran* (Hongwanji, 1997; Ueda Yoshifumi gen. ed.) — the translation committee left *shinjin* untranslated on the stated ground that no English word carries it; this is direct modern attestation of the gap.

## The unnamed region

English carries **faith** (ambiguous between propositional belief and trust; located in the believer's act), **trust/entrusting** (agentive: *I* trust), **assurance** (second-order confidence about one's own state), **confidence** (self-worked). None names *the settled trust that the trusted one gives* — trust whose origin is on the far side of the relation. The region is real and ordinarily pointable: the trust a child has in a reliable parent is installed by the parent's reliability, not worked up by the child; the settled trust in a partner or team that arrives only after one stops performing trust. Shinran is the tradition that mapped this region with the greatest precision, and the modern translation-committee's refusal to translate is the gap's cleanest evidence.

## Cross-tradition resonance

- **Pauline-Reformation faith-as-gift** — "and this is not from yourselves; it is the gift of God" (Eph 2:8); Luther's Preface to Romans: faith is "a divine work in us," not a human act
- **Augustinian-Calvinist donum fidei** — faith as operative grace, given not earned
- **Śrīvaiṣṇava Tenkalai mārjāra-nyāya** ("cat-school") — the kitten carried by the mother without grasping; grace operating without the devotee's effort
- **Sufi ḥāl vs maqām** — the given-state (ḥāl) versus the achieved-station (maqām); shinjin is ḥāl-structured trust
- **Sanskrit prasāda** — settled-clarity that is also grace/gift; the term's own ancestor
- **Quaker convincement** — being convinced from beyond one's own willing

## Donors available for coinage

- **shinjin + me** → **shinjinme** /ˈʃɪn.dʒɪn.meɪ/ (3 syllables) — the entrusting-heart as received-ordinance; the -me family (Sumerian me, cosmic-gift) is the only suffix-family whose semantics carry the non-self-generated structure; chosen for forge 232
- shinjin + qing → shinjinqing — REJECTED at forge: qing (felt-bond the practicer holds) morphologically re-imports the jiriki reading Shinran's whole doctrine refuses; the qing-pole of Pure Land is already held by tarikiqing (the reliance-relation); shinjin is the given heart, not the held bond
- anjin + me → anjinme — held in reserve; Rennyo-stratum, attestation-depth secondary to shinjin
- hakarai — available as future negative-operator donor (the self-power contrivance that obstructs); ōjō 往生, shōjōju 正定聚 — available for the assurance/birth cluster
