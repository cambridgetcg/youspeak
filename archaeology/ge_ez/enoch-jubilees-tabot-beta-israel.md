# South Arabian & Ge'ez II — Enoch, Jubilees, Tabot, Beta Israel

**Tradition:** Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church (canonical peculiarities) + Beta Israel (Ethiopian Judaism)  
**Language:** Ge'ez (Ethiopic) — liturgical language of both traditions  
**Period:** Core texts 3rd c. BCE – 1st c. CE; Ethiopian canonical establishment c. 4th–15th c. CE; Beta Israel practice c. 5th–20th c. CE  
**Region:** Ethiopian and Eritrean highlands  
**Key concept:** Ethiopia preserves apocalyptic-priestly literature lost or marginalized elsewhere (1 Enoch, Jubilees) as *canonical scripture* — giving these texts unbroken liturgical use for 1600+ years. The tabot/tabotat system creates a distinctive church-cosmology in which every Ethiopian church IS a form of the Jerusalem Temple through its replica Ark. Beta Israel (Ethiopian Jews) developed an independent Judaism based on Ge'ez scripture — including Enoch and Jubilees — separated from rabbinic development for centuries.

## PART I — THE ETHIOPIAN BIBLICAL CANON

### The Widest Canon in Christendom

The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church possesses the broadest biblical canon of any major Christian tradition. While the Catholic Old Testament has 46 books, the Protestant 39, and the Orthodox (Greek) ~54, the full Ethiopian canon reaches **81 books** — the "broader canon" (*mǝlʾ*) — though precisely which books are considered fully canonical varies by source and usage.

Distinctive Ethiopian canonical texts include:

**Fully canonical in Ethiopian practice, not in other traditions:**
- **1 Enoch** (Mäṣḥafä Henok) — 108 chapters; the only tradition preserving this as full canonical scripture
- **Jubilees** (Kufālē) — 50 chapters; a retelling of Genesis-Exodus with priestly-calendar theology
- **Shepherd of Hermas** — an early Christian apocalyptic text, canonical in Ethiopia
- **Liber Antiquitatum Biblicarum** (Pseudo-Philo) — in some enumerations
- **Book of Maccabees** (in a broader form than Catholic or Orthodox)
- **Clement of Alexandria's Epistles**

The theological claim: the Ethiopian church did not *add* these books. It preserved the *earlier* canon before the Western and rabbinic canonical restrictions of the 2nd–4th centuries. The Ethiopian position (maintained by the EOTC) is that they kept what others narrowed away.

### 1 Enoch — The Book the Angels Wrote

**1 Enoch** (also called *Ethiopic Enoch* because Ge'ez is its only complete language of preservation) is the most significant single text that the Ethiopian canon preserves uniquely. Of the Dead Sea Scrolls, 11 fragmentary Aramaic Enoch manuscripts were found at Qumran — proving 1 Enoch circulated widely in Second Temple Judaism. The Greek world knew it (Jude 14–15 quotes it directly; 2 Peter echoes it). The early church fathers cited it as scripture. Then it disappeared from the Western and Eastern canons — survives only in Ge'ez, where it was translated c. 4th–6th c. CE and preserved in unbroken liturgical use.

**1 Enoch's theological register — what it uniquely contributes:**

**A. The Watchers mythology (chapters 1–36, "Book of Watchers"):**
The opening section describes the *Watchers* (*ʿīrīn*) — a class of angelic beings who descend from heaven, take human wives (*bǝnot hāʾādām*, daughters of men — Genesis 6:1–4 compressed), and produce the *Nephilim* (giants). The Watchers teach forbidden knowledge to humanity: metallurgy for weapons, cosmetics and jewelry (seduction), sorcery, astrology. This is a *fallen-angel theodicy* — explaining evil's origin not through human sin alone (Genesis) but through angelic transgression.

The theological weight: **Human suffering has a cosmic cause beyond human choice.** The Watchers corrupted the earth; their giant offspring wreak violence; their spirits (after the giants' physical death) become the demons who afflict the living. Evil is not simply human nature but angelic crime against divine order.

The Watchers' leader is **Semyaza** (or **Azazel** in some editions) — his name Azazel appears in Leviticus 16 as the name of the scapegoat-recipient, now revealed as the imprisoned rebellious angel. The scapegoat ritual is an *exorcism of cosmic evil*, not merely a symbolic forgiveness.

**B. The Astronomical Book (chapters 72–82):**
A detailed description of the sun and moon's courses, the seasons, the calendar — all given to Enoch by the angel Uriel. The theological import: **the proper calendar is a divine revelation, not a human calculation.** The 364-day solar calendar (not the 354-day lunar calendar of standard Jewish practice) is heavenly-ordained. Keeping wrong calendar = missing the sacred appointments. This calendar theology is exactly what Jubilees also argues.

**C. The Similitudes (chapters 37–71):**
Contains the most developed pre-Christian theology of the **"Son of Man"** — a heavenly figure who will judge at the end of days, who was hidden before creation, who is the instrument of divine judgment, who sits on the throne of glory. This is the primary background for Jesus's "Son of Man" self-description in the Gospels. The Similitudes pre-date or are contemporaneous with Jesus; the theological vocabulary was already formed.

**D. The Dream Visions (chapters 83–90):**
The "Animal Apocalypse" — a symbolic retelling of all history from Creation to the Maccabean revolt, using animals as human actors (Israel = white sheep/rams, fallen angels = stars, foreign nations = various predatory animals). A complete theology of history as angelic-human drama.

**E. The Letter of Enoch (chapters 91–108):**
Contains the "Apocalypse of Weeks" — history divided into 10 "weeks" (ages), with the 7th week being the age of wickedness (the author's present) and the 8th week the time of divine judgment. Eschatological urgency: *we are almost at the turning point.*

**The Ge'ez preservation significance:** In rabbinic Judaism, 1 Enoch was not canonized — partly because the Watchers-mythology competed with the Adam-sin theodicy, partly because the 364-day calendar conflicted with developing rabbinic practice, partly because the Son of Man theology proved too useful to early Christians. The rabbinic canon excluded it; the Christian West marginalized it. Ethiopia kept it. For 1600+ years, Ethiopian Christians have read the Watchers, the Similitudes, the Son of Man — as scripture.

This means Ethiopian theology has had, built into its canonical foundation:
- A developed angelology (classes of angels, rebel angels, angel-names)
- A demonic theodicy (evil explained by angelic transgression, not merely human sin)
- A solar-calendar theology (364-day year as divinely ordained)
- A Son of Man theology (the hidden figure, the throne of glory, the judgment seat)

These are not marginalia — they are *foundational to Ethiopian Christian imagination.*

### Jubilees — The Calendar Bible

**Jubilees** (Ge'ez: *Kufālē*, "divisions/portions") is a retelling of Genesis 1 through Exodus 14 — presented as a revelation given to Moses on Sinai by the "Angel of the Presence" (the highest angelic mediator, who stands before God's face). Moses doesn't receive just the Torah on Sinai; he receives a *second* disclosure — the hidden chronological structure of all history.

**The calendar theology:** Jubilees is organized around the 364-day solar year (52 weeks exactly, so the calendar never shifts — Passover always falls on a Wednesday, the Sabbath always on a Saturday, every festival is fixed in weekday as well as month-day). This is the SAME calendar as the Dead Sea Scrolls community (the Essenes or Qumranites). The theological implication: the Jerusalem Temple cult, using the lunar-adjusted calendar, is keeping *wrong-timed festivals* — missing the real sacred appointments. The Jubilees community understood itself as the true Israel keeping true sacred time.

**Distinctive theological moves in Jubilees:**
- **Angels observed the Sabbath before Israel** — the Sabbath is not merely a human institution but a cosmic rhythm that angelic beings have always kept
- **Abraham kept the Torah before Moses** — the patriarchs intuitively followed all 613 commandments before they were revealed; they knew by *nature* what would later be codified by *law*
- **Mastema** (Prince of Accusation) — Jubilees' name for the adversarial figure (related to Satan/Accuser): he is the leader of evil spirits, given limited authority by God to test humanity, but ultimately subordinated to divine sovereignty. Not an equal adversary but a permitted agent.
- **Israel's election as primordial** — not contingent on historical events but established in the divine council before creation. The people Israel exists as an eternal category, of which the historical Israel is the earthly instantiation.
- **Detailed festival origins** — Jubilees explains when and why each festival was first observed, often tracing it to patriarchal actions. Shavuot (Pentecost) is the covenant-renewal feast, celebrated by Noah after the flood, by Abraham at Beersheba, by Jacob at Bethel — not just given at Sinai but renewed by every patriarch.

**Ethiopian use of Jubilees:** The EOTC reads Jubilees as canonical Torah-supplement. Ethiopian liturgical calendar reflects Jubilees' festival theology. The 364-day calendar has historically influenced Ethiopian church calendar structure (the Ethiopian calendar, the Gǝʿǝz calendar, has 13 months and maintains its own chronological system — 7 years behind the Gregorian, with Christmas on January 7th by Gregorian reckoning). The continuity is not accidental.

## PART II — TABOT AND TEMPLE COSMOLOGY

### The Tabot: Every Church a Sinai

**Tabot** (ታቦት, plural *tabotat*) is the Ge'ez word for the Ark of the Covenant — and also for the consecrated altar-slab at the heart of every Ethiopian Orthodox church. The two uses are not confused but identified: the altar-slab in each church *is* a form of the Ark, a replica-presence, so that every church is simultaneously:

- A **local place of worship**
- A **Temple of Jerusalem** (replicated through the tabot's presence)
- A **Mount Sinai** (where God descends to the holy object)
- A **Holy of Holies** (where the Ark-presence dwells)

**The tabot's material form:** A tabot is typically a flat slab of wood (sometimes marble or alabaster), inscribed with sacred text — usually a Gospel text or the Ten Commandments — and wrapped in richly embroidered cloth. It is kept in the *maqdas* (the inner sanctuary, the "Holy of Holies" of the church building) and may never be seen by ordinary worshippers. Only priests and deacons may enter the *maqdas*. The tabot is rarely removed except for major feasts.

**Timkat — the Feast of the Tabot in procession:**

The most visually spectacular manifestation of tabot theology is **Timkat** (Epiphany, January 19th Gregorian), when each church's tabot is:
1. Wrapped ceremonially (priests alone may touch it)
2. Carried in procession on the priest's head
3. Processed through the streets accompanied by drums, sistrum-rattles, incense, and hundreds of white-robed worshippers
4. Installed at a local water source (river, lake, pool) overnight
5. Returned to the church the next morning

The theological action: Timkat re-enacts the Baptism of Jesus in the Jordan — but through the *tabot's* procession to water. The Ark (divine-presence-object) meets the water (primordial element of creation and new-creation) and blesses it. The people's baptismal vows are renewed. The divine comes to the water; the water becomes blessed; the people renew their covenant identity.

This is structurally identical to the Ark's role in the Hebrew Bible: Joshua 3 — the Ark enters the Jordan, the river stops, Israel crosses on dry ground. The New Testament reads Jesus's baptism as a new-Exodus event. The Ethiopian Timkat ceremony *enacts this typological chain* annually — Ark-to-Jordan, Joshua-crossing, Jesus-baptism, new-creation — through the tabot's procession to water.

**Ethiopia's claim about the original Ark:** Ethiopian national theology (*Kǝbrä Nägäst*, "Glory of Kings," c. 1321 CE) claims that the original Ark of the Covenant is *in Ethiopia* — held at the Church of Our Lady Mary of Zion in Aksum, brought by Menelik I (son of Solomon and the Queen of Sheba) when he returned from visiting his father in Jerusalem. The Ethiopian Guardian of the Ark (a single monk, sole custodian) is said to spend his life in the church's inner precincts, dying there, replaced by a successor.

Whether historically credible or not, the claim articulates the theology: **Ethiopia IS the continuation of the Jerusalem Temple.** The Ark is here. The covenant is here. The holy of holies is here. Ethiopian Christianity does not understand itself as a branch of the universal church but as the *custodian of the original covenant object* — making it the most Temple-continuous of all Christian traditions.

**The triple-tier church structure and Temple mapping:**

| Church Zone | Who Enters | Temple Equivalent |
|-------------|------------|-------------------|
| *Qǝne mahlet* (outer) | All baptized Christians | Outer courts (Gentile Court) |
| *Qǝddǝst* (middle) | Baptized, prepared for communion | Inner Court (Court of Israel) |
| *Maqdas* (inner) | Priests and deacons only | Holy of Holies |

The three-tier structure directly maps to the Jerusalem Temple's zones. This is not a metaphor but an architectural theology: the building *is* the Temple; the tabot *is* the Ark; entering the maqdas *is* entering the divine presence.

## PART III — BETA ISRAEL: ETHIOPIAN JUDAISM

### An Independent Judaism

**Beta Israel** (ቤተ እስራኤል, "House of Israel") is the community known popularly as "Ethiopian Jews" or (erroneously) as Falasha (Amharic: "exile/alien" — a term Beta Israel consider pejorative). Their origins are disputed: three main theories:
1. **Descendants of the tribe of Dan** who came to Ethiopia in antiquity (Beta Israel's own tradition; also held by many authorities who recognized their Jewishness)
2. **Descendants of the Jewish community of Yemen/South Arabia** who crossed the Bab el-Mandeb strait (geographically and historically plausible)
3. **Converts from Christian or pagan Ethiopian communities** who adopted a Torah-based practice (supported by the fact their Ge'ez-language texts include no Mishnaic or Talmudic material)

The third theory is currently most favored by historians — Beta Israel appear to represent a movement that emerged within Ethiopian society (possibly a dissident priestly movement) that embraced the Hebrew scriptural tradition as read through Ge'ez texts, independently of rabbinic development.

**What Beta Israel Judaism looks like:**

Their practice is based on:
- **Ge'ez scripture** — including Enoch and Jubilees (which standard rabbinic Judaism excluded)
- **The 364-day solar calendar** — aligned with Jubilees, not with the rabbinic lunar calendar
- **Priests (*Kahen*, from Hebrew *Kōhēn*)** — a hereditary priestly class (lost in rabbinic Judaism after the Temple's destruction but maintained in Beta Israel)
- **No Mishnah or Talmud** — the entire rabbinic legal tradition is absent; Beta Israel developed an independent Ge'ez-language legal tradition
- **The *Orit*** — their name for Torah: a Ge'ez translation of the Pentateuch plus Joshua and Judges; the core scriptural authority
- **The Sabbath** — observed rigorously; stricter than most rabbinic Sabbath practice in some respects
- **Ritual purity laws** — including a *marg*et (separation house/hut) for menstruating women; practices aligned more with biblical Leviticus than with rabbinic development
- **Monasticism** — a remarkable feature: Beta Israel had a monastic tradition (*mönäk*, monks) who lived celibately, fasted extensively, and devoted themselves to scripture study. *Jewish monks* — unique in the global Jewish world; explicable only by the Ethiopian cultural context where Christian monasticism was the prestige religious form.

**The ʿOrit as sole authority:** Without Mishnah or Talmud, Beta Israel interpretation of the law works by direct reasoning from the Ge'ez ʿOrit. Their *Kahen* (priestly leadership) interprets. No *rabbinic* authority mediates. This means their Judaism is structurally closer to what 1st-century BCE priestly Judaism may have looked like than to the rabbinic tradition that shaped global Judaism after 70 CE.

**Recognition and Aliyah:** Israeli rabbinical authorities debated Beta Israel's status for decades. Rabbi Ovadia Yosef ruled in 1973 that Beta Israel are the tribe of Dan and fully Jewish; Israel recognized this officially, leading to two major airlifts — **Operation Moses** (1984, 8,000 airlifted during famine) and **Operation Solomon** (1991, 14,325 airlifted in 36 hours during Ethiopian civil war). Approximately 165,000 Beta Israel Jews now live in Israel, where they face complex integration challenges.

**The theological significance for YOUSPEAK:** Beta Israel represents a form of Judaism that:
- Kept the Jubilees calendar the Essenes kept
- Kept the Enoch canon the Qumran community kept
- Maintained a hereditary priesthood after all other Jewish communities lost the temple-based priestly function
- Developed monasticism as a Jewish form of life
- Preserved an independent Ge'ez Jewish tradition untouched by Mishnah or Talmud for possibly 1500+ years

This is not a "lesser" Judaism — it is a *parallel* preservation, like a parallel scripture tradition that never merged with the main stream. It holds the same potential revelatory significance as the Dead Sea Scrolls: a window into what ancient Israelite religion looked like before the great rabbinic systematization.

## PART IV — THEOLOGICAL SYNTHESIS: THE GEʿEZ REGISTER

### What Ge'ez contributes that no other tradition preserves

**1. The Enoch register:** The developed angelology, the Watcher-mythology, the Son of Man theology, the 364-day calendar — these circulated widely in Second Temple Judaism and influenced early Christianity deeply (the Book of Revelation's throne-room, the Gospels' Son of Man, the early church's angelology are all in this stream). Ge'ez kept it alive. The YOUSPEAK cathedral should name:

- **watcher-me** (Specialized): The category of *ʿīrīn* (Watchers) — angelic beings given the role of watching/guarding but who transgressed their appointed order; source of the fallen-angel theodicy
- **ʿīrīn-me** could be collapsed into this

**2. The tabot register:** The theology that every local place of worship *is* the Temple, *contains* the Ark's functional presence, and *hosts* the divine in the same way the Jerusalem Holy of Holies hosted the divine — this is one of the most theologically sophisticated resolutions of the "How do you have a Temple without the Temple?" problem. Judaism answers with synagogue-as-study; Christianity answers with church-as-eucharist; Ethiopian Christianity answers with tabot-as-Ark-presence. The answer is structurally unique.

**3. The wax-and-gold register:** The *sǝmǝnna worq* principle is independently powerful as a general hermeneutic: the outer meaning is real AND the inner meaning is real; both are simultaneously complete; neither cancels the other. This is not allegory (where the outer is merely the vehicle for the real inner meaning) but *double-fullness*. A forge here would give the catalog a precise term for this mode of layered meaning.

**4. The revealed-calendar register:** The Jubilees 364-day solar calendar, kept by Beta Israel and theorized in 1 Enoch — the theology that the *calendar itself is a divine revelation*, that keeping the wrong calendar means missing the real sacred appointments, that time is not neutral but divinely structured — this is an underrepresented theological concept in Western discourse. The Hebrew concept of *moadim* (sacred appointments) approaches it but doesn't fully capture the calendar-as-revelation dimension.

**5. The Raḥmānān convergence:** The pre-Islamic South Arabian Raḥmānān (Merciful One) emerging from astral polytheism and converging with the Islamic *ar-Raḥmān* — this is an extraordinary case of theological development visible in the archaeological record. A word here would give the catalog a name for *the divine name that survived the transition from polytheism to monotheism*, carried across the religious shift by its own semantic power.
