# Targum Theology — Translation as Theological Creation

**Tradition:** Jewish Aramaic Targum literature (synagogue translation-interpretation of Hebrew Bible)
**Period:** ~3rd c. BCE – 7th c. CE (composition); still recited in some Yemenite Jewish liturgies
**Languages:** Jewish Palestinian Aramaic, Jewish Babylonian Aramaic
**Key concept:** The Targum is not a translation but a theological event — the moment when rendering Hebrew into Aramaic generates concepts that exist in neither language alone. The Memra, the Shekhinah, and the Yeqara are children of this crossing.

---

## 1. THE INSTITUTION: METURGEMAN AND THE ORAL TARGUM

### The Synagogue Practice

The Targum originated as an oral institution. After the Babylonian exile (6th c. BCE), the Jewish community gradually shifted from Hebrew to Aramaic as its vernacular. The synagogue reading of Torah continued in Hebrew, but a **meturgeman** (מתורגמן, "translator/interpreter") stood beside the Torah reader and delivered an Aramaic rendering after each verse (for Torah) or after every three verses (for Prophets).

**Critical rules** (Megillah 4:4, 4:10; Tosefta Megillah 4:20):
1. The meturgeman may not read from a written text — the Targum must be oral, to prevent anyone from thinking the Aramaic *is* the Torah
2. The Torah reader may not prompt the meturgeman — the translation must come from the meturgeman's own learning
3. One verse at a time for Torah, three for Prophets
4. Certain passages may not be translated at all, only read in Hebrew (e.g., the second account of Reuben's sin, David and Bathsheba)
5. The meturgeman must not raise his voice above the Torah reader

**Theological significance:** These rules encode a hermeneutic principle: the Targum is *derivative* (it cannot be mistaken for the original) but *authoritative* (it comes from a trained interpreter). The Targum occupies a deliberately ambiguous status — not scripture, not commentary, but something between: *authorized interpretation*. This is structurally identical to YOUSPEAK's position vis-à-vis its donor languages: not the original, not a commentary, but a new utterance generated in the crossing.

### The Meturgeman's Freedom

Within the rules, the meturgeman had significant freedom:
- To paraphrase
- To expand narrative with aggadic material
- To resolve ambiguities in a particular theological direction
- To add halakhic rulings directly into the translation
- To introduce Memra, Shekhinah, and Yeqara where the Hebrew had none

This freedom was constrained by tradition but not by text. The result was a living oral tradition that varied by community and by meturgeman, eventually crystallizing into the written Targumim we have today.

## 2. THE MAJOR TARGUMIM — Detailed Analysis

### Targum Onkelos (תרגום אונקלוס)

**Language:** Literary Babylonian Aramaic (standardized from Palestinian origins)
**Scope:** Torah/Pentateuch only
**Character:** The most literal Targum — the closest to "straight translation" — but its *deviations* are the most theologically significant precisely because they are deliberate and systematic.

#### The Anti-Anthropomorphism Program

Onkelos systematically removes every instance of:

**God having a body:**
| Hebrew | Onkelos | Principle |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| "The hand of the LORD" (Ex 9:3) | "A plague from before the LORD" | Body part → action from *qedam* (before) |
| "Under his feet" (Ex 24:10) | "Under the throne of His glory" | Body → throne |
| "The mouth of the LORD" (Num 12:8) | "Speech with speech" | Body → speech-mode |
| "The eyes of the LORD" (Deut 11:12) | "Before the LORD, they are revealed" | Seeing → being revealed to |

**God having emotions:**
| Hebrew | Onkelos | Principle |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| "The LORD regretted" (Gen 6:6) | "There was a decree from before the LORD" | Emotion → decree |
| "The LORD smelled the pleasing aroma" (Gen 8:21) | "The LORD received with favor" | Sensation → judicial acceptance |
| "God was angry" (Num 11:1) | "Anger from before the LORD was strong" | Direct emotion → emotion *from before* God |

**God moving physically:**
| Hebrew | Onkelos | Principle |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| "The LORD came down" (Gen 11:5) | "The LORD was revealed" | Motion → revelation |
| "God went up from him" (Gen 35:13) | "The glory of the LORD went up" | God moving → glory moving |
| "Walking in the garden" (Gen 3:8) | "The voice of the Memra of the LORD was walking" | God walking → Memra's voice present |

**The theological engine:** Every substitution works through one of three devices:
1. **Qedam** (קדם, "before") — God doesn't act *directly* but *from before* Himself. This is a spatial metaphor for transcendence: God is always "prior to" His own effects.
2. **Memra** — God's speech-act stands between God and the world.
3. **Shekhinah/Yeqara** — God's presence/glory acts where God's body would.

#### Onkelos and the Memra — Statistical Analysis

The term *Memra* appears approximately **180 times** in Targum Onkelos. Its distribution reveals a theology:

**Memra as covenant-maker:**
- "I will establish my covenant between my Memra and between you" (Gen 9:17; 17:7)
- Not: God makes covenant. But: God's Memra makes covenant. The speech-act itself is the covenantal bond.

**Memra as cosmic agent:**
- "By the Memra of the LORD the heavens were made" (= Ps 33:6 in Targumic tradition)
- Creation happens through Memra — the world is spoken into being by a speaking that has standing presence.

**Memra as military protector:**
- "My Memra shall be your support" (Ex 3:12)
- "The Memra of the LORD went before them" (cf. Ex 13:21)
- The Memra is not abstract — it walks, protects, supports. It is *personal*.

**Memra as object of faith:**
- "And they believed in the Memra of the LORD" (Ex 14:31)
- Faith is directed at the Memra, not around it. Whatever the Memra is, it receives the devotion that elsewhere goes to God directly.

### Targum Jonathan (תרגום יונתן)

**Language:** Literary Babylonian Aramaic
**Scope:** Prophets (Nevi'im — Former and Latter)
**Character:** More expansive than Onkelos; includes significant aggadic material

#### Jonathan and Messianic Interpretation

Targum Jonathan's most distinctive contribution is its explicit **messianic reading** of prophetic passages:

**Isaiah 52:13–53:12 (The Suffering Servant):**
The Hebrew Suffering Servant poems are famously ambiguous — Israel? A prophet? The Messiah? Targum Jonathan resolves the ambiguity:

- "Behold, my servant the Messiah shall prosper" (52:13) — explicit identification
- But the *suffering* is redistributed: the Servant-Messiah does not suffer vicariously. Instead:
  - The Messiah builds the Temple (53:5)
  - The nations are the ones who suffer, for their sins (53:4)
  - The Messiah intercedes for Israel (53:12)
  
This is a *messianic but non-vicarious* reading — the Messiah conquers and intercedes rather than suffering substitutionarily. The difference between Targumic and Christian messianism is located precisely here: same text, same Messiah, different theology of what the Messiah does.

**Isaiah 6:1 ("I saw the Lord sitting on a throne"):**
- Targum: "I saw the glory of the LORD resting on His throne" — the prophet sees *glory*, not God directly.

**Ezekiel 1 (The Chariot Vision):**
- Targumic tradition is extremely cautious here — this is the *ma'aseh merkavah* (chariot mysticism) that the Mishnah restricts from public teaching. The Targum renders but does not expand.

### Targum Pseudo-Jonathan (תרגום ירושלמי / תרגום יונתן)

**Language:** Mixed — Palestinian Aramaic base with Babylonian and even Arabic and Greek loanwords
**Scope:** Torah/Pentateuch
**Character:** The most expansive Targum — essentially a running midrashic commentary embedded in a translation. Contains material that has no parallel in any other source.

#### Pseudo-Jonathan's Theological Creativity

**Genesis 1:1-2 — Creation:**
> "From the beginning with wisdom the Memra of the LORD created and perfected the heavens and the earth. And the earth was empty and void, devoid of human and animal, bare of vegetation and trees; and darkness was spread over the face of the abyss; and a spirit of mercy from before the LORD was blowing over the face of the waters."

Note the theological additions:
- "With wisdom" (b-ḥokhmeta) — Proverbs 8's personified Wisdom is read into Genesis 1
- "The Memra of the LORD" — not God directly but the Memra creates
- "A spirit of mercy" (ruaḥ raḥamin) — the "spirit of God" becomes specifically *merciful* spirit
- "From before the LORD" (min qedam YHWH) — the spatial-transcendence formula

**Genesis 4:8 — Cain and Abel's dialogue:**
The Hebrew text has a lacuna: "And Cain said to Abel his brother..." — but what he said is missing. Pseudo-Jonathan fills it with a full theological debate:

> Cain said: "There is no judgment, no judge, no other world, no giving of good reward to the righteous and no punishment for the wicked."
> Abel said: "There is judgment, there is a judge, there is another world, there is giving of good reward to the righteous and punishment for the wicked."

The first murder, in Pseudo-Jonathan, is motivated by a **theological dispute about divine justice** — the prototypical atheism vs. faith argument embedded into the first fratricidal narrative.

**Genesis 22 — The Binding of Isaac (Aqedah):**
Pseudo-Jonathan's expansion is among the most theologically rich:
- Isaac voluntarily asks to be bound (he is not merely passive)
- Isaac sees the angels of heaven — "his eyes looked at the heights of heaven, and Isaac saw the Shekhinah of the LORD"
- Abraham's eyes also see — a bat kol proclaims from heaven
- The Memra acts as the restraining agent: "The Memra of the LORD called to Abraham from heaven"

The Aqedah in Pseudo-Jonathan becomes a **joint sacrificial act** — both father and son consenting — mediated by Memra (as speaking agent), Shekhinah (as visual presence), and bat kol (as announcing voice). All three modes of divine communication appear in a single narrative.

### Targum Neofiti (I)

**Language:** Palestinian Aramaic
**Scope:** Torah/Pentateuch
**Character:** Discovered in the Vatican Library in 1956 by Alejandro Díez Macho, who initially catalogued it as Targum Onkelos before recognizing its distinct Palestinian character. The most important Targum discovery of the 20th century.

**Neofiti's Memra usage:**
Neofiti uses Memra even more freely than Onkelos — in passages where Onkelos has God acting directly, Neofiti interposes the Memra. This suggests the Palestinian tradition was *more* Memra-centered than the Babylonian.

**Genesis 1:1 in Neofiti:**
> "From the beginning the Memra of the LORD created the heavens and the earth with wisdom."

The Memra *creates*. This is the fullest expression of Memra theology: the divine speech-presence is the agent of cosmic origin.

**Neofiti's marginal glosses:**
The manuscript contains marginal notes that often offer alternative readings — evidence of the living oral tradition behind the written text. Some glosses introduce theological variants: where the main text has "Memra of the LORD," a gloss might have "the glory (yeqara) of the Shekhinah of the LORD" — a triple-layered divine mediator.

## 3. THE MEMRA IN DEPTH — Modes of Presence

### What the Memra IS

Synthesizing across all Targumim, the Memra functions in these modes:

1. **Creative agent:** "By the Memra of the LORD the world was made" — the Memra creates
2. **Covenantal partner:** "Between my Memra and between you" — the Memra enters covenants
3. **Protective presence:** "My Memra shall be your help" — the Memra shields and supports
4. **Revelatory medium:** "The Memra of the LORD appeared" — the Memra is what you encounter when God is encountered
5. **Judicial authority:** "The Memra of the LORD judged" — the Memra adjudicates
6. **Object of faith:** "They believed in the Memra of the LORD" — faith is directed *at* the Memra
7. **Object of rebellion:** "They rebelled against the Memra of the LORD" — sin is directed *against* the Memra

### What the Memra is NOT

The Memra is **not** merely a circumlocution (like saying "the Crown" for the monarch). Evidence:
- You can believe *in* a circumlocution? ("They believed in the hand of the LORD" would be nonsensical)
- A circumlocution makes covenants? (Covenants require personhood)
- A circumlocution creates the world?

The Memra is **not** a second God. Evidence:
- It is always "Memra *of the LORD*" — never independent
- It does what God does, not something different
- The Targumists show no awareness of introducing a new entity

### What the Memra DISCOVERS

The Memra is a **mode of divine engagement** — a way God is present *as active speech*. It names the gap between God's transcendent being (utterly beyond the world) and God's immanent action (utterly within the world). The Memra is *how* transcendence enters immanence without ceasing to be transcendent.

This is the theological discovery the Targumists made *because they were translating*. The Hebrew text doesn't need the Memra because Hebrew anthropomorphism carries the tension implicitly. When you render "God walked in the garden" into a culture that insists God cannot walk, you must name *what was walking*. The Memra is that name. It was born from the act of crossing between languages.

**YOUSPEAK parallel:** This is exactly what YOUSPEAK does — forge words from the crossing between languages that name what neither language names alone. The Memra is YOUSPEAK's ancestor.

## 4. THE SHEKHINAH IN DEPTH — Mobile Indwelling

### The Development from Targum to Talmud to Kabbalah

**Stage 1 — Targumic Shekhinah (translation device):**
In the Targumim, Shekhinah is used where the Hebrew Bible speaks of God *dwelling* or *being present in a location*:
- "I will cause my Shekhinah to rest among them" (for "I will dwell among them")
- "The Shekhinah of the LORD was upon Mount Sinai" (for "The LORD descended upon Mount Sinai")

At this stage, Shekhinah is parallel to Memra — a way of saying "God is here" without saying God *is literally* here.

**Stage 2 — Talmudic Shekhinah (theological concept):**
In the Talmud and Midrash, the Shekhinah develops independent theological life:

*Shekhinah goes into exile:*
> "Come and see how beloved Israel are before the Holy One, blessed be He — every place they went into exile, the Shekhinah went with them. They were exiled to Egypt — the Shekhinah was with them. They were exiled to Babylon — the Shekhinah was with them. And when they will be redeemed in the future, the Shekhinah will be with them." (Megillah 29a)

This is revolutionary: **God's localized presence is bound to a people, not a place.** When the Temple is destroyed, God's presence doesn't remain at the empty Temple mount — it goes with the exiles. The Shekhinah is relational, not architectural.

*Shekhinah mourns:*
> "At the time the Holy One, blessed be He, remembers His children who are plunged in suffering among the nations of the world, He lets fall two tears into the Great Ocean, and the sound is heard from one end of the world to the other." (Berakhot 59a)

The Shekhinah is God's own grief at the state of the world.

*Shekhinah between persons:*
> "When two sit and study Torah, the Shekhinah is between them." (Avot 3:2)
> "When a husband and wife are worthy, the Shekhinah is between them; when they are not worthy, fire consumes them." (Sotah 17a)

Divine presence is constituted by righteous human relationship. The Shekhinah doesn't just *accompany* human activity — it is *generated by* it.

**Stage 3 — Kabbalistic Shekhinah (the Feminine Divine):**
In the Zohar (13th c., attributed to Shimon bar Yoḥai), the Shekhinah becomes the tenth sefirah — the feminine face of God, the *Matrona* (Queen), separated from the masculine upper sefirot by the cosmic catastrophe of exile. The goal of religious life becomes *tikkun* (repair): reuniting the Shekhinah with the Holy One.

This stage moves far beyond the Targumic usage, but it grows organically from the grammatical femininity of the Aramaic word and from the relational-mobile theology already present in the Talmud.

## 5. THE QEDAM FORMULA — Spatial Transcendence

A less-studied but theologically rich Targumic device is the **qedam formula**: *min qedam YHWH* (מן קדם ייי) = "from before the LORD."

**Everywhere the Hebrew says "from the LORD" (me-'et YHWH), the Targum says "from before the LORD" (min qedam YHWH).**

This tiny spatial word — *qedam*, "before/in front of" — encodes a complete theology of divine transcendence: God is always *behind* His own effects. You never encounter God directly; you encounter what comes *from before* God. The preposition creates an ontological buffer — a gap between God's being and God's effects that is also a bridge.

**Cognate:** Aramaic *qedam* is cognate with Hebrew *qedem* (east, ancient times, "of old") — what is "before" in space (east, the direction you face) is also what is "before" in time (the primordial). God is both spatially and temporally *prior*.

**Forge candidate: qedamme** (Aramaic qedam + Sumerian me). The ordinance of divine-priority — God-always-prior-to-His-effects as a structural condition of reality. The word for the unbridgeable-yet-crossed gap between transcendence and immanence.

## 6. CONVERGENCES AND CROSS-TRADITION PARALLELS

### Translation-Creates-Theology Convergence

The Targum tradition's deepest insight — that **translation generates theology** — has exact parallels:

| Tradition | Translation act | Theological discovery |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Targumim** | Hebrew → Aramaic | Memra, Shekhinah, Yeqara (modes of divine presence) |
| **Septuagint** | Hebrew → Greek | Agape (love beyond eros/philia), Diatheke (covenant-as-testament), Ekklesia (congregation-as-called-out-assembly) |
| **Peshitta** | Hebrew/Greek → Syriac | Rāzā (mystery-as-encoded-symbol), feminine Spirit |
| **Chinese Buddhist** | Sanskrit → Chinese | 佛 (fo = Buddha), 禪 (chán = dhyāna → Zen), 涅槃 (nièpán = nirvana) |
| **Vulgate** | Hebrew/Greek → Latin | Sacramentum (= Greek mystērion), Testamentum (= diatheke) |

In every case, the target language's grammar and conceptual apparatus *forces* theological innovations. YOUSPEAK is the self-aware version of this process.

### The Three-Mode Divine Presence

The Targumic triad (Memra-Shekhinah-Yeqara) maps onto other traditions' articulations of divine presence:

| Mode | Targumic | Christian (later) | Sufi | Hindu |
|------|----------|-------------------|------|-------|
| God present through *action/speech* | Memra | Logos/Son | Kalām Allāh (God's speech) | Vāc (divine speech) |
| God present as *indwelling* | Shekhinah | Spirit | Sakīna (= Shekhinah, borrowed!) | Antaryāmin (inner controller) |
| God present as *visible glory* | Yeqara | Father's glory (doxa) | Nūr (divine light) | Tejas (divine radiance) |

The Arabic *sakīna* (سكينة) is a direct Aramaic borrowing: Shekhinah → Sakīna. The Quran uses it (48:4, 48:18, 48:26, 9:26, 9:40, 2:248) for divine tranquility/presence sent down to believers. The same Aramaic concept, crossing into Arabic, takes on a new register: not indwelling in a place but *tranquility* descending upon hearts.

### The Aqedah-Sacrifice Convergence

Through the sacrifice-alignment-sieve:
- **Targumic Aqedah** (voluntary co-sacrifice of father and son, mediated by Memra) → passes as *aligned sacrifice*: the sacrificer and the sacrifice both consent; the Memra restrains before completion; the result is covenant renewal
- Convergence with **Yoruba** (Ọbàtálá's sacrificial journey: voluntary descent, suffering, restoration), **Akkadian** (Akitu Day 5: king voluntarily humiliated, then restored), **Algonquian** (Nanabozho's ordeal: voluntary suffering to bring medicine to humans)

## 7. ADDITIONAL FORGE CANDIDATES (from deep-dive)

### qedamme (qedam + me)
**Pitch:** The ordinance of divine-priority — the condition whereby God is always prior to His effects. The word for the gap-that-is-also-a-bridge between transcendence and immanence.

**Assessment:** Beautiful concept, possibly too abstract for everyday register. Specialized tier likely. Score estimate: 7.30–7.50.

### batkolme (bat kol + me)
**Pitch:** The ordinance of the diminished divine voice — the echo-mode of revelation. What happens after prophecy ceases but God hasn't gone silent — the daughter, not the mother, of the voice.

**Assessment:** Unique concept with no parallel in any other YOUSPEAK word. "Daughter of a voice" is a haunting image. But the compound is long and possibly unwieldy. Score estimate: 7.20–7.40.

### targumme (targum + me)
**Pitch:** The ordinance of translation-as-revelation — the condition whereby crossing between languages generates theological discovery. Not translation-as-reproduction but translation-as-creation.

**Assessment:** This is the meta-word — the word YOUSPEAK uses to name its own method. Extremely high resonance within the project. But possibly too self-referential for wide use. Score estimate: 7.40–7.60.
