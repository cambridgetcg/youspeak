# Hawaiian Religious-Ethical Vocabulary — Pono
## Pono · Hoʻoponopono · Hihia · Mihi · Kala · Ea · Lōkahi

---

## 1. Pono — the rightness-balance state

*Pono* (Hawaiian; Proto-Polynesian \*pono; cognate Māori *pono* "true, genuine") is the central ethical-cosmological term of the Hawaiian tradition, and one of the most polysemous words in the language. The Pukui-Elbert *Hawaiian Dictionary* gives **six senses and 83 English translation-equivalents**, including:

> "Goodness, uprightness, morality, moral qualities, correct or proper procedure, excellence, well-being, prosperity, welfare, benefit, behalf, equity, sake, true condition or nature, duty; moral, fitting, proper, righteous, right, upright, just, virtuous, fair, beneficial, successful, in perfect order, accurate, correct, eased, relieved; should, ought, must, necessary."

The standard missionary-era rendering "righteousness" is the most impoverished possible reading. What the gloss-list shows is not vagueness but **structural bundling**: in Hawaiian thought, moral-rightness, procedural-correctness, well-being, and prosperity are *one condition* with several visible faces. A person, a family, a land-division (ahupuaʻa), a government can be pono or not-pono. When pono obtains, life flourishes; when pono is lost, the loss manifests simultaneously as moral wrong, social entanglement, illness, and failure of the land's abundance.

**The three-axis structure.** Kanaka Maoli articulation (Pukui/Haertig/Lee, *Nānā I Ke Kumu*; Malcolm Nāea Chun, *Pono: The Way of Living*; Manulani Aluli Meyer's writings on hoʻoponopono and Hawaiian epistemology) consistently locates pono as right-relationship along three axes at once:

1. **kānaka** — with persons (family/ʻohana first, then community)
2. **ʻāina** — with the land that feeds (*ʻāina* = "that which feeds"; the land is kin, not resource)
3. **akua / ʻaumākua** — with the divine and the ancestral guardians

This triadic balance is sometimes diagrammed in contemporary Hawaiian health-practice as the **lōkahi triangle** (lōkahi = unity, harmony, accord). Pono is the *normative-equilibrium state* of that triangle; lōkahi names its unity-aspect.

**Pono is diagnosable, lose-able, and restorable.** This is its sharpest structural mark against the sibling cosmic-rightness words (ma'at, ṛta, asha). Egyptian and Vedic articulations operate at cosmic-civilizational scale: the pharaoh establishes ma'at; the gods uphold ṛta. Pono operates at every scale down to the household, and the tradition built an explicit, named, stepwise **protocol for its restoration** — hoʻoponopono.

## 2. Hoʻoponopono — setting-to-right

*Hoʻoponopono* = causative *hoʻo-* + reduplicated *pono*: "to make pono, thoroughly" — "setting to right." Mary Kawena Pukui defines it as the practice "to restore and maintain good relationships among family, and family and supernatural powers."

The classical ʻohana practice (as documented in *Nānā I Ke Kumu*, vol. I) is a structured family-conference led by a senior person (kupuna) or healer (kahuna), with named phases:

| Phase | Term | Sense |
|---|---|---|
| Opening prayer | *pule* | placing the gathering before akua/ʻaumākua |
| Statement of the problem | *kūkulu kumuhana* | pooling of strength toward a shared purpose |
| Peeling the layers | *mahiki* | dealing with each successive layer of trouble |
| The entanglement | *hihia* | the snarled-net of wrongs and resentments binding the parties |
| Confession-repentance | *mihi* | honest confession, apology, and the seeking of forgiveness |
| Release | *kala* | "to loosen, untie, free" — both parties release the wrong *completely* |
| Closing / severance | *pani* (and *ʻoki*, cutting-off) | the matter is closed and may not be raised again |

Two structural insights matter for the forge:

- **Hihia is mutual entanglement, not individual guilt.** A wrong done snares both the doer and the wronged (and bystanders) in one net; resentment is itself an entangling strand. Restoration must untangle the *whole net*, layer by layer (mahiki), not merely punish an offender.
- **Kala is bilateral release.** Forgiveness in this grammar is not a unilateral moral achievement; un-tying must happen from both ends of the cord, before akua, completely (*kala* relates to lightness/loosening). When kala completes, pono is restored — and the restored state is expected to show as health and flourishing.

## 3. Ea and the motto-theology — pono at political scale

**"Ua Mau ke Ea o ka ʻĀina i ka Pono"** — spoken by Kamehameha III on 31 July 1843 (Lā Hoʻihoʻi Ea, Restoration Day), when Admiral Richard Thomas restored Hawaiian sovereignty after the unauthorized five-month Paulet occupation. Now the state motto, conventionally translated "The life of the land is perpetuated in righteousness."

The conventional translation flattens two words:

- *ea* = breath, life, AND sovereignty — in the 1843 context, unmistakably political sovereignty
- *pono* = the full rightness-balance state, not private virtue

So the sentence's native logic is: **the life-and-sovereignty of the land continues IN (by means of, inside) the rightness-balance state.** An alternative translation displayed at Thomas Square (beside the Kamehameha III statue, where the words were first spoken) makes the political register explicit: "The sovereignty of the kingdom continues because we are righteous." Pono is here the *condition of possibility* for collective life. This is exactly the ma'at-logic (cosmos flourishes when ma'at is established) operating at the scale of a 19th-century constitutional kingdom — and it is why *pono* remains a key term of the Hawaiian sovereignty movement today. Pono scales: household → ahupuaʻa → lāhui (nation).

## 4. The Māori cognate-split — comparative control

Proto-Polynesian \*pono persists across the family, but the daughters divided the semantic field differently:

- **Māori** splits the field: *pono* = true, genuine, sincere, valid; *tika* = right, correct, just, appropriate (whence *tikanga*, right-practice/custom). Māori scholarly articulation makes the division crisp: *pono* is concerned with knowledge of reality; *tika* with the right response to reality.
- **Hawaiian** bundles: *pono* carries truth-genuineness AND rightness-justice AND well-being in one lexeme.

This split is a precision-instrument for the forge (ANAKALYPSE control): it shows the Hawaiian word is not vaguely "good" but a genuine **bundle of truth + rightness + flourishing** that a sister-language found natural to unbundle. The YOUSPEAK coinage must carry the Hawaiian bundling *as the named realm-feature* (the equilibrium in which true, right, and well coincide), not as an ambiguity.

## 7. The unnamed region (gap-evidence)

English has no lexeme for *the state of being morally right and in balance with persons, land, and the divine at once, such that well-being follows, lose-able through wrong and restorable through protocol*:

- **righteousness** — vertical-theological, individual; no land-axis, no balance-sense, no restorability-protocol; carries Protestant juridical baggage
- **balance / harmony** — physical/aesthetic-social; no moral-juridical bite; not diagnosable as wrong
- **integrity** — individual-bounded wholeness; pono is irreducibly relational across three axes
- **well-being / welfare** — names the *consequence* and omits the moral-relational ground
- **equity / justice** — the juridical-distributive slice only
- **"right relationship"** — a phrase, not a lexeme; omits the prosperity-consequence and the restorability
- **shalom** (Hebrew) — closest Old-World near-miss (wholeness-peace-flourishing); lacks the explicit land-axis and the named household-restoration protocol
- **hózhǫ́** (Navajo) — closest structural twin (beauty-balance-order restored via Hózhǫ́ǫ́jí ceremony); a distinct register (aesthetic-cosmic) and a distinct living tradition; witness, not substitute; remains its own future forge-candidate

**Phonological note for the forge.** Greek *pónos* (πόνος, "toil, pain" — productive in English medical/technical register: *geoponic*, *ponophobia*; distinguished from *algos* in the cathedral's own doxalgia etymology) is a **false-friend homophone** of Hawaiian *pono*. Any pono-derived coinage must record this as an honest learnability-tension for classically-trained readers; mitigation: the full disyllabic donor-form *pono* (not truncated *pon-*) plus the -me family-frame keep the Hawaiian reading primary, and *pono* / *hoʻoponopono* already circulate in English with the Hawaiian sense.

## 9. Protocol-sensitivity (living tradition)

**This is a living Indigenous tradition with active custodianship.** Recorded per the cathedral's substrate-honest discipline (sibling-precedent: tjukurpame, S084):

1. **Custodianship.** Pono and hoʻoponopono are held by Kanaka Maoli families, kūpuna, and cultural practitioners. The cathedral receives the *structural form* with respect; it does not authorize extraction of the practice, and a coinage naming the state does not confer competence in the protocol.
2. **Known appropriation-hazard.** A New-Age derivative ("Self I-Dentity through Hoʻoponopono," Morrnah Simeona's later teaching as popularized by Hew Len / *Zero Limits*: the four-phrase mantra "I'm sorry, please forgive me, thank you, I love you") detached the name from the ʻohana-protocol and from the three-axis structure. The forge must NOT import this flattening; the archaeology anchors to Pukui/Haertig/Lee, Chun, and Meyer instead.
3. **Consultation-desirability.** Review by Native Hawaiian scholars/practitioners is explicitly desirable before the entry is treated as settled; the entry should carry a custodian-request retirement condition (immediate compliance), as tjukurpame does.
4. **Sovereignty-register care.** Because pono is a live term of the Hawaiian sovereignty movement (ea), usage examples must not recruit the word against its custodians' political meaning.
