# Ibn ʿArabī's Fixed Essences — al-Aʿyān al-Thābita

## Native forms

- **الأعيان الثابتة** — *al-aʿyān al-thābita*, "the fixed entities / immutable entities"
- **عين ثابتة** — *ʿayn thābita* (singular), one fixed-essence
- **ʿayn** (عين) — radically polysemous: *eye*, *spring/fountainhead*, *essence/entity*, *the-thing-itself*. Ibn ʿArabī works the polysemy deliberately: the ʿayn is the thing as the Real's eye eternally sees it, and the spring from which its manifestation flows.
- **thābit/thābita** (ثابت) — fixed, firm, established; from the root th-b-t, whose verbal noun **thubūt** (ثبوت, "fixity") Ibn ʿArabī opposes technically to **wujūd** (وجود, "existence/being-found")

## The doctrine

The aʿyān thābita are *the things of the cosmos as eternally known by God* — the determinate individual essences (quiddities-as-particulars) subsisting in the divine knowledge from beyondless eternity. Their ontological status is the doctrine's precision-instrument:

1. **Thubūt without wujūd.** The fixed entity has the "thingness of fixity" (shayʾiyyat al-thubūt) — fully determinate, fully known — but not the "thingness of existence." It is neither existent (it has no wujūd of its own) nor sheer-nonexistent (it is determinately known, distinguishable, addressable). Chittick: "the fixed entity is the thing inasmuch as it is nonexistent in itself but known to God."
2. **"Never smelt the fragrance of existence."** Ibn ʿArabī's celebrated formula: the fixed entities *mā shammat rāʾiḥat al-wujūd* — they have never smelt a whiff of wujūd, and never will. Manifestation does not transfer existence to them.
3. **The engendering command and the hearing.** Existence-as-manifestation arrives through the divine command *kun* ("Be!" — Qur'an 16:40). The nonexistent entity "hears" the command and complies — yet what becomes manifest is not the entity-now-existing but **Existence itself delimited and defined by the entity's thingness**. The colored-glass image: light shines through colored glass; the glass does not change; light alone becomes manifest, colored by the glass.
4. **One face per divine name.** Each possible entity has a specific divine name that gazes upon it and gives it its specific face, distinguishing it from every other entity. The aʿyān are the loci-of-manifestation (maẓāhir) of the names.
5. **Preparedness (istiʿdād).** Each fixed entity carries its own eternal receptivity, which determines exactly how — and how much of — the divine self-disclosure (tajallī) manifests through it. Disclosure is given universally; the measure is the entity's own.
6. **The secret of destiny (sirr al-qadar).** Because "knowledge follows its object" (al-ʿilm tābiʿ li-l-maʿlūm), God knows each thing as that thing eternally is in its fixity; the entities thus "give" God the knowledge of themselves. Destiny is not imposed from outside but read off the thing's own eternal face.

## What the aʿyān are NOT

- **Not Platonic Forms.** The Forms are universals shared by many particulars; each ʿayn thābita is an *individual, particular* essence — the fixed identity of *this* thing. Hämeen-Anttila explicitly rejects "permanent archetype" and "fixed prototype" as misleading on exactly this point. (The convergence with Plato is real but corrected: Ibn ʿArabī gives what Neoplatonism could not — a God who knows particulars without mutability.)
- **Not creatures-in-waiting.** They never enter existence; nothing "leaves" the divine knowledge.
- **Not the imaginal archetypes (mithāl) of Malakūt.** The mithāl are subtle *existent* forms in the imaginal world; the aʿyān are prior — nonexistent objects of knowledge in the divine presence. (See ibn-arabi-hadarat-barzakh.md: the ḥaḍarāt-hierarchy locates mithāl; the aʿyān stand upstream of the whole hierarchy of manifestation.)

## Realm-feature named

**The eternally-fixed individual essence of a thing, determinately known in the Ground, neither existent nor nonexistent, according-to-which manifestation is delimited.** This is the most precise theological articulation any tradition offers of the ontological status of *realm-residents* under THE-REALM.md and NOEMA.md: contents prior to projection, fully determinate, graspable, awaiting no creation of themselves but only the projection that manifests according to them. The modal register of THE-REALM §III ("the absence of a name is the possibility-of-a-name not-yet-actualized") receives here its donor-tradition depth.

## Cross-tradition resonance

- **Platonic eidos / Plotinian intelligibles** — eternal determinacy prior to manifestation (corrected: universals vs Akbarī particulars)
- **Maximus the Confessor's logoi** — the divine logoi of beings: God's eternal wills/predeterminations for each creature, in which creatures pre-subsist
- **Augustine's rationes aeternae** — divine ideas as the eternal reasons of things in God's mind
- **Leibniz's complete individual concepts** — the full determinate concept of each individual in the divine understanding prior to creation (strikingly close: individual, not universal)
- **Whitehead's eternal objects in the primordial nature of God** — pure potentials envisaged eternally, ingressing into actual occasions without themselves becoming actual
- **Kabbalistic soul-roots / the primordial-Torah register** — each soul's root eternally inscribed in the divine thought
- **Muʿtazilite shayʾiyyat al-maʿdūm** — the "thingness of the nonexistent": the kalām substrate Ibn ʿArabī inherited and transfigured
- **Yogācāra bīja (partial-structural)** — stored potentials manifesting as cosmos; but bīja are conditioned, momentary, deposited — not eternal-fixed (see canon/core/alayame.md; the contrast is itself clarifying)

## Unnamed region

English has no word for *the fixed-but-nonexistent individual essence known in the Ground*. "Archetype" is Jungian-psychological and universal; "Form/Idea" is Platonic-universal and self-subsistent; "essence/quiddity" lacks the fixity-in-knowledge and the neither/nor status; "blueprint/template/prototype" are artifact-metaphors implying design-then-build or an existent first instance; "potentiality" loses determinacy and knownness; Whitehead's "eternal object" is a two-word technical phrase naming universal qualities, not individual identities. The region — *each thing's eternally-given determinate identity, real-as-known, unreal-as-existent, which manifestation realizes without exhausting* — is nameless in ordinary speech.
