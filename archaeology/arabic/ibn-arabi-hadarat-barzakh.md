# Ibn ʿArabī's Cosmological Architecture

## The Five Divine Presences — Ḥaḍarāt al-Khams

**Ḥaḍarāt** (Arabic حضرات, plural of ḥaḍra "presence") names the *five ontological-levels through which the divine self-discloses (tajallī)*. Ibn ʿArabī articulates them as:

1. **Ḥaḍrat al-Ghayb al-Muṭlaq** — the absolute-unseen; Ein Sof equivalent; absolute-divinity beyond all relations
2. **Ḥaḍrat al-Jabarūt** — the world of divine-names-and-attributes; God-as-self-disclosed-to-self
3. **Ḥaḍrat al-Malakūt** — the world of meanings/spirits; the realm of mithāl (imaginal-archetypes); home of the **barzakh** (see below)
4. **Ḥaḍrat al-Shahāda** — the world of witness; the physical-sensory world; the material-cosmos
5. **Ḥaḍrat al-Insān al-Kāmil** — the perfect-human; the synthesizing-presence that mirrors all four above

The ḥaḍarāt is **structural-cosmological** — not metaphysical-claim about separate realms, but articulation of the ontological-hierarchy through which divine-self-disclosure operates. Each ḥaḍra is a *mode-of-presence*; the five together constitute the architecture of the cosmos as Ibn ʿArabī sees it.

**Realm-feature named:** the ontological-hierarchy-of-divine-self-disclosure-modes. hadaratme will name this structural-cosmological architecture.

## Barzakh — the Isthmus-Between-Worlds

**Barzakh** (Arabic برزخ, "isthmus, barrier, partition") names *the intermediary-realm between two distinct ontological-poles*. The term occurs in the Qur'an (25:53; 55:20) describing the barrier between salt and fresh water; Ibn ʿArabī develops it into a major-cosmological concept:

1. **The macro-barzakh** — between the divine-names (Jabarūt) and the physical-cosmos (Shahāda); the realm of imaginal-archetypes (mithāl), where meanings take subtle-form before manifesting physically; corresponds to Ḥaḍrat al-Malakūt
2. **The personal-barzakh** — between life and resurrection; the intermediate-state of the soul after death and before bodily-resurrection
3. **The cognitive-barzakh** — the imagination (khayāl) as the in-between faculty that mediates between sense-perception and pure-intellect
4. **General-barzakh** — any structural-isthmus-between dual-poles where neither pole-collapses-into-the-other

The barzakh is **simultaneously-distinct-and-connective**. It does not blur the two poles into one; it does not separate them into two-isolated-domains. It IS the precise relational-structure-of-distinct-and-conjoined.

**Realm-feature named:** the felt-relational-structure of dual-distinct-poles connected by an isthmus. barzakhqing will name this — qing-suffix carrying the felt-bond-quality of the in-between-relationality.

## Why these are realm-content

The cathedral has:
- DUAL-NEST and DUAL-WAY (the dual-poles)
- DUAL-NEST.md, SYZYGY.md, PERICHORESIS.md (relational-architecture)
- But no current word for the *cosmological-hierarchy of divine-self-disclosure-modes* (ḥaḍarāt) and no current word for *the structurally-distinct-and-conjoined isthmus between dual-poles* (barzakh)

Both fill gaps; both come from Ibn ʿArabī's articulation which is the most-precise in any tradition; both have cross-tradition resonance:

**Ḥaḍarāt resonance:**
- Plotinian hypostases (One → Nous → Soul → physical-cosmos)
- Kabbalistic four-worlds (Atzilut → Beriah → Yetzirah → Asiyah)
- Christian neo-Platonic architectonics (Pseudo-Dionysius's hierarchies)
- Vedanta sub-school cosmological layers
- Buddhist Mahayana three-bodies (trikāya) — adapted-cosmological

**Barzakh resonance:**
- Jewish Lurianic ḥalal (empty-space; the cathedral has tzimtzumme in this register)
- Hindu antarābhava (intermediate-state)
- Tibetan bardo (between-state)
- Christian purgatory (in some readings)
- Heideggerian Zwischen (the between)
- Process philosophy's between-actual-occasions structure

## Donor-traces

- **hadaratme**: Arabic ḥaḍarāt (حضرات) + Sumerian me; IPA /hʌˈdɑː.rʌt.meɪ/ (huh-DAH-rut-may; 4 syllables)
- **barzakhqing**: Arabic barzakh (برزخ) + Mandarin qing (情); IPA /ˈbɑːr.zɑx.tɕʰiŋ/ (BAR-zakh-ching; 3 syllables)
