# Arabic — Divine Beauty

_The tongue in which the Sufi tradition developed the most specific lexicon of divine beauty in the monotheistic world. Beauty is paired with majesty (jamāl/jalāl) as a primary theological dyad; self-disclosure is paired with hiddenness (tajallī/istitār). What Greek reached philosophically and Hebrew reached phenomenologically, Arabic reached structurally, through Sufi metaphysics._

---

## Native forms

### Beauty root — ج م ل (j-m-l)

| Form | Transliteration | Gloss | Register |
|---|---|---|---|
| جَمَال | *jamāl* | beauty | primary; theological when unqualified in Sufi context |
| جَمِيل | *jamīl* | beautiful (adj.) | general; divine name: *al-Jamīl* ("The Beautiful") |
| الجَمِيل | *al-Jamīl* | The Beautiful One (divine name) | from hadith: *"Inna-Llāha jamīlun yuḥibbu al-jamāl"* — "God is beautiful and loves beauty" |
| تَجَمُّل | *tajammul* | beautification, adornment | reflexive — adorning-oneself |

### Majesty root — ج ل ل (j-l-l) — the dyadic partner

| Form | Transliteration | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| جَلَال | *jalāl* | majesty, severity, overwhelming-ness |
| جَلِيل | *jalīl* | majestic |
| الجَلِيل | *al-Jalīl* | The Majestic (divine name) |

*jamāl* and *jalāl* are the **primary Sufi dyad** for the two faces of divine self-presentation. Jamāl is the drawing-near, sweetness, beauty, intimacy; jalāl is the overwhelming-awe, severity, distance. Neither exhausts GoD; both are aspects of the Essence.

### Self-disclosure root — ج ل و / ج ل ي (j-l-w, j-l-y)

| Form | Transliteration | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| تَجَلِّي | *tajallī* | self-disclosure; theophanic appearing; the flashing-forth of divine beauty |
| تَجَلَّى | *tajallā* | to disclose itself (Qur'an 7:143, of GoD to Moses and to the mountain) |
| مَجْلَى | *majlā* | locus of self-disclosure |
| جَلِيّ | *jalīy* | evident, disclosed |

*tajallī* is the theological technical term for the event of divine self-disclosure. In Ibn Arabi's metaphysics (Futūḥāt al-Makkiyya), tajallī is continuous — the moment-by-moment flashing-forth of divine beauty across all creatures, each creature being a mirror of one tajallī. 

**Qur'an 7:143** is the source-text: when Moses asks to see GoD, GoD says "you shall not see Me, but look at the mountain; if it stays in place, then you shall see Me." *Fa-lammā tajallā rabbuhu li-l-jabali ja'alahu dakkan* — "When his Lord **disclosed-Himself** to the mountain, He made it crumble." *Tajallā* here is the self-disclosure-verb in its most dramatic scriptural instance.

**Nuance's coinage *tajalliance*** (in primer v4.4) draws from this root to name "the descending arc" of divine beauty into creation.

### Radiance — ب ه ا (b-h-a)

| Form | Transliteration | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| بَهَاء | *bahā'* | radiance, splendour, glow |
| بَهِيّ | *bahīy* | radiant |
| البَهِيّ | *al-Bahīy* | The Radiant (non-canonical divine name, but used in Shi'a and Baha'i traditions) |
| الأَبْهَى | *al-Abhā* | the Most Radiant (Baha'i superlative form; "Baha'u'llah" = Glory of GoD, same root) |

*bahā'* is radiance-splendour, adjacent to kavod and doxa. Carries Q3 strongly (radiance calls); weaker on Q2.

### Goodness-beauty fused — ح س ن (ḥ-s-n)

| Form | Transliteration | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| حُسْن | *ḥusn* | beauty-goodness (fused); excellence |
| حَسَن | *ḥasan* | good, beautiful |
| إِحْسَان | *iḥsān* | excellence, right-doing-beautifully |

The root *ḥ-s-n* fuses moral-goodness and aesthetic-beauty without seam. *Iḥsān* is a hadith technical term for the highest stage of religion after *islām* (submission) and *īmān* (faith): "to worship GoD as if you see Him, for though you see Him not, He sees you." *Iḥsān* is worship-as-beauty-enacted, the excellence-beauty-fused of right-doing.

This root carries an important structural feature Greek lost: **beauty and goodness as one lexeme**. In Greek, *kalos k'agathos* (beautiful-and-good) is a compound phrase; in Arabic, *ḥusn* is one word.

### Adornment — ز ي ن (z-y-n)

| Form | Transliteration | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| زِينَة | *zīnah* | adornment, ornament, beautification |
| مُزَيَّن | *muzayyan* | adorned |

Closer to the ornament-side; less ontological than jamāl.

## Semantic territories

### Territory A — jamāl as divine-beauty-quality

In Sufi metaphysics, *jamāl* is one of the two fundamental modes of divine self-manifestation. It is:

- **divine-specific** in canonical Sufi usage — even though *jamāl* can describe creature-beauty, in tasawwuf the unqualified *jamāl* is divine
- **ontological** — it is an attribute of the Essence (*dhāt*), not a phase or event
- **calling** — jamāl is the name of the face-of-GoD-that-draws-near; the *rahmah* (mercy) aspect
- **paired with jalāl** — the two are a fundamental dyad, not a unity

Q-fit:
- Q1: YES (divine when unqualified)
- Q2: YES (ontological attribute)
- Q3: YES (drawing-near)
- Q4: partial — jamāl carries harmony and perfection, but "ordered-to-the-bottom" is not its primary semantic; it is contrasted with jalāl, making the duality the structure
- Q5: not directly paired with an ache-pole; the Sufi ache-vocabulary (*shawq*, longing; *wajd*, finding-ecstasy) exists but is separate

**Donor strength:** Very high. *jamāl* is the closest single-word cross-linguistic cognate to the target.

**Reclamation-cost:** moderate-high. *jamāl* is already a Sufi technical term; loaning it to English requires importing either the full Sufi framework (including the jamāl/jalāl dyad) or stripping it from its home-tradition. Nuance's existing coinage *tajalliance* takes a related morpheme (*tajallī*, self-disclosure) rather than *jamāl* itself — which is a more surgical borrowing, since it names the event (disclosure) rather than the quality (beauty).

A candidate drawing directly from *jamāl* — e.g., *jamalance* — would need to address the reclamation-cost.

### Territory B — tajallī as self-disclosure-event

*tajallī* names the event of divine beauty coming forth. Not the beauty itself, but the disclosing. Its structure:

- divine subject (the one disclosing)
- locus (*majlā*, where the disclosure lands)
- content (the beauty disclosed)
- effect (the beholder's transformation)

*tajallī* is event-structured, not quality-structured. Its YOUSPEAK descendant *tajalliance* names the descending-arc-event.

**This territory is closed for the beauty-noun forge.** The beauty-noun is not the event but the quality. *tajallī* stays where it is.

### Territory C — ḥusn as beauty-goodness fused

*ḥusn* is structurally fascinating: in one lexeme, beauty and goodness are not separated. This is the Arabic answer to Plato's kalos k'agathos. *Al-ḥusn* as a divine attribute names the beauty-goodness-fused of GoD.

Q-fit:
- Q1: divine-specific when prefixed with *al-* or in construct
- Q2: YES — ḥusn is an ontological quality
- Q3: partial (ḥusn is attractive but less foregrounded than jamāl)
- Q4: partial via the goodness-fusion (perfect ordering implies ḥusn)
- Q5: not paired

**Donor available:** *ḥusn* could seed a candidate (*husnance*?) for the beauty-goodness-fused register. Different gap than doxakallos; parallel forge.

### Territory D — bahā' as radiance

*bahā'* is the radiance-splendour. Adjacent to doxa / lamprotēs. 

Q-fit:
- Q1: when qualified (Bahā'u'llāh = "glory of GoD")
- Q3: YES (radiance calls)
- Q2: weaker (radiance is appearance, not beauty-quality)
- Q4: absent

**Donor available:** *bahā'* could feed a radiance-candidate. Weaker for the unitary-beauty-quality target; stronger for a specifically-radiance-beauty position.

## Negative space — what Arabic reveals that Greek and Hebrew do not

1. **The structural dyad jamāl/jalāl.** Divine beauty is *not alone*; it exists against divine majesty. No single word names their unity (beyond *dhāt*, the Essence, which is not specifically beauty). The target-gap sits precisely at the unity-that-precedes-the-dyad — the beauty-side of the Essence when that side is named in itself.

2. **Event/quality separation.** Arabic cleanly separates *tajallī* (event) from *jamāl* (quality). Greek and Hebrew do this less cleanly (*doxa* blurs event and weight; *kavod* blurs presence and beauty). Arabic clarifies: the target is on the quality side, not the event side.

3. **Goodness-beauty fusion.** *Ḥusn* shows that some languages do not feel the need to split beauty from goodness. English, having split them, has a gap that Arabic does not. Relevant for later forges; not load-bearing for doxakallos.

## Verdict on Arabic's contribution

Arabic contains, in *jamāl*, the closest single-word cross-linguistic match to the target. The target-gap is not absolute: *jamāl* is genuinely live in classical Arabic as "divine-beauty-quality-qua-ontological-attribute." 

But the target-gap is *for English*. English does not have *jamāl*, and transliteration is not coinage. A reclamation-candidate *jamalance* (jamāl + -ance) would carry Arabic's precision into a YOUSPEAK morphology — but would face a higher memorability cost than Greek-rooted candidates because English has no latent handle on *jamāl*.

The Greek-rooted *doxakallos* has the advantage of English speakers' latent handles on *doxa* (orthodox, doxology, heterodox) and, after canonization, on *doxalgia*. This matters for Q5 (memorability) even more than for Q3 (calling-forth).

Arabic therefore contributes: (a) conceptual clarity — event/quality distinction; (b) structural insight — beauty/majesty dyad; (c) a parallel candidate *jamalance*; (d) the precedent of *tajallī* → *tajalliance*, validating Nuance's earlier Arabic-root borrowing.
