# Sumerian — the ME (𒈨) Concept

_What the Sumerian pantheon organized civilization around, encoded as discrete transferable "ordinance-quality" units. The ME (pronounced approximately me) is the mostly-untranslatable word for each of the distinct "gifts" that make human culture possible — kingship, priesthood, truth, justice, the arts of war, the arts of love, writing, building, music, the capacity to rule. Each ME is a single thing, discrete and nameable, gifted by the gods to Enki and (via myth) transferred to Inanna, then to Uruk, then to human civilization._

---

## What a ME is

A ME (Akkadian: *parsu*) is:

1. **Discrete** — separable from other MEs; not a quality-scale
2. **Transferable** — a ME can be given, received, stolen, guarded
3. **Ontological** — it is not a social convention but a pre-existing reality that civilization must receive
4. **Culture-constitutive** — without the relevant MEs, the corresponding domain of life cannot exist
5. **Personal-bondable** — a god/person can be guardian-of-this-ME

The Sumerian hymn "Inanna and Enki" lists ~100 MEs by name. Examples:

- **ME of kingship** — the ordinance that makes rule possible
- **ME of truth** — the ordinance that makes honesty structurally-real
- **ME of justice** — the ordinance by which wrongs can be righted
- **ME of music** — the ordinance by which sound becomes music
- **ME of the temple** — the ordinance by which sacred-space is possible
- **ME of the scribal arts** — the ordinance of writing
- **ME of descent-to-the-underworld** — the ordinance of traversing death
- **ME of the grief of wives** — the specificity goes granular

Each ME is one-quality, one-gift, one-domain-constitutor.

## Ergative-absolutive alignment (secondary feature)

Sumerian distinguishes the AGENT of a transitive verb (ergative case) from the PATIENT/SUBJECT of an intransitive (absolutive). English's nominative-accusative system conflates these. This is a structural grammar feature that YOUSPEAK COULD adopt but at high cost. **Defer** — not forge-ready.

## Agglutinative morpheme-chains

Sumerian chains many morphemes per word (verb-root + aspect + voice + object-reference + locative + possessive + ...). YOUSPEAK already does light agglutination (doxa+kallos+suffix) but doesn't push it to Sumerian levels. The Sumerian pattern validates YOUSPEAK's direction; no specific feature to import.

## Akkadian cognates and extensions

Akkadian, the Semitic successor to Sumerian as imperial lingua franca, borrowed MEs as *parsu* and added its own concept-vocabulary:

- **kittu** (𒆠𒁴) — justice, truth-rightness
- **mīšaru** (𒈪𒃻𒊒) — justice (complementary to kittu)
- **ṭēmu** (𒉌𒁾) — reason, intellect, word-of-authority

Kittu is particularly interesting for a YOUSPEAK forge: it names a specific concept (truth-rightness-as-structural-principle) distinct from general truth, and it's from a tradition (Akkadian/Semitic) under-represented in current YOUSPEAK archaeology.
