# Isuma — Reasoning-Maturity

**SACRED/LIVING KNOWLEDGE PROTOCOL:** This file records only published, publicly shared material — ethnography Inuit communities have engaged with, Nunavut government frameworks Inuit authors wrote, and Inuit-owned media self-descriptions. Isuma is not restricted knowledge; it is everyday Inuktitut. But it is a load-bearing concept of a living tradition's self-understanding, and the cathedral receives its structural-form without claiming custodianship of its practice.

---

## I. The Word and Its Morphology

**isuma** (ᐃᓱᒪ) — Inuktitut noun-root: thought, mind, idea, intention, will; the verb-root *isuma-* "to think." The root is pan-Inuit:

- **Inuktitut** (Eastern Canadian Arctic): *isuma* — everyday word for thought/mind/decision
- **Utkuhikšalingmiut dialect** (Chantrey Inlet — Briggs's field site): ***ihuma*** — same word under the regular s→h shift of that dialect
- **Kalaallisut** (Greenlandic): *isuma* — mind, opinion, meaning
- **Iñupiaq** (Alaska): *isuma* attested in the same semantic range (e.g., recent scholarship on "Iñupiat gaming and isuma")

Attested derivational family (the polysynthetic root is productive):

| Form | Gloss | Note |
|---|---|---|
| *isuma* | thought, mind, intention | everyday base sense |
| *isumataq* | "the one who thinks (for others)" — camp leader, boss | leadership = concentrated isuma; Barry Lopez's popularized gloss, "the person who creates the atmosphere in which wisdom reveals itself," is a literary expansion of this form |
| *isumaqsayuq* | "causing thought" | Stairs 1992: the Inuit way of teaching — provoking the learner's own isuma rather than transmitting content; contrasted with *ilisayuq*, formal Western-style instruction |
| *isumaaluk- / isumaaluttuq* | "big thoughts" — to worry | commonly glossed in modern Inuktitut; even worry is framed as a weight of thought |
| *Isuma* (proper name) | Igloolik Isuma Productions (makers of *Atanarjuat*), IsumaTV | the tradition's own media-arts flagship chose this word as its name: "to think" |

The morphology already carries the theology: leadership, pedagogy, worry, and art are all isuma-derivatives. Thought is not one faculty among others; it is the axis along which the person, the leader, and the teacher are measured.

## II. Briggs's Ethnography — Ihuma Defines Adultness

Jean Briggs's seventeen months with the Utkuhikšalingmiut (1963–65) produced the foundational ethnographic account. Her formulation:

> "As nurturance (*naklik*) defines goodness of a human being, so reason (*ihuma*) defines adultness." (Briggs 1970)

The structure she documents:

1. **Ihuma is consciousness-thought-reason-memory-will as one capacity.** Later scholarship summarizing Briggs and Collings: isuma refers to consciousness, thought, reason, memory, and will; possession of isuma entails that a person exercises good judgment, reason, and emotional control at all times. Emotional equanimity is not adjacent to reasoning-maturity — it is constitutive of it. The famous Utku non-anger is an isuma-phenomenon.
2. **Children lack ihuma and are not blamed for it.** A small child who screams, grabs, or rages is *nutaraqpaluktuq* — acting like a child, because the ihuma has not yet grown. Anger at the ihuma-less is doubly inappropriate: it misreads the child, and it exhibits the adult's own ihuma-deficit.
3. **Ihuma grows of itself and wants to be used.** Briggs: "the belief is that the more ihuma the child acquires, the more he will want to use it." The capacity is not installed by instruction; it emerges with growth, and the appetite for its exercise emerges with it. Adults *wait on* ihuma; they do not manufacture it.
4. **Excess and deficit are both marked.** Briggs records that the Utku also diagnose too-much-thinking (brooding, withdrawal) as an ihuma-imbalance. Isuma is a capacity with a mean — exercised well, it shows as quiet, considered, even-tempered judgment.

## III. The Developmental Arc — Never Complete

The Government of Nunavut's Inuit Qaujimajatuqangit Education Framework articulates the maturational ontology from within:

- personal development is the development of ***inuusiq*** (life, living) and ultimately ***isuma*** (glossed there as wisdom) — a process that leads one toward becoming an ***inummarik***, a genuine/able person who can act with wisdom;
- learning is a lifelong process with **no expectation of an end or "graduation" point** — even one recognized as having gained isuma in one area continues learning in others.

Peter Collings's ethnography of Ulukhaktok (*Becoming Inummarik*, 2014) confirms the never-complete structure for adulthood itself: inummarik is "not an endpoint — you never get there and plant the flag"; it is an ideal aspired toward across the whole of a life. Aging into elderhood is aging into *more* isuma — which is why elders are consulted, and why, in the Inuit reckoning, one's standing tracks one's wisdom rather than one's years as such.

The arc, compactly: **inuusiq (life) → isuma (considered thought-and-judgment, thickening with age) → inummarik (the genuine person — an asymptote, not a destination).**

## IV. The Pedagogy Isuma Grounds — One Never Instructs an Isuma-Bearer Directly

Isuma is the ground of Inuit pedagogy, and the pedagogy is its sharpest evidence:

1. **Isumaqsayuq — "causing thought."** Arlene Stairs (1992) named the contrast: *isumaqsayuq*, the Inuit way — learning through observation, participation, and provoked reflection embedded in family and community activity — versus *ilisayuq*, the Western schooling way — explicit teaching by transmission. The Inuit teacher does not deposit conclusions; the teacher arranges occasions for the learner's own isuma to move.
2. **Morality plays.** Briggs (*Inuit Morality Play*, 1998) documented the dramatic questioning by which adults present small children with emotionally charged dilemmas ("Why don't you come live with me instead of your mother?") — not teasing for its own sake but isumaqsayuq in action: the dilemma exercises the child's nascent isuma where a lecture would bypass it. (Michaeleen Doucleff's NPR reporting and *Hunt, Gather, Parent*, 2021, popularized this; the popularization should be read back against Briggs, not in place of her.)
3. **Non-interference as respect.** Across the ethnographic record, directly instructing, ordering, or correcting a person of developed isuma is offensive — it treats them as ihuma-less, as a child. And the respect extends even to children: because a newborn receives the name-soul (*atiq*) of a deceased relative, ordering a child about is, in a sense, ordering the namesake about. Direction is given by question, story, demonstration, and example — forms that address the other's isuma instead of overriding it.
4. **Leadership without command.** The *isumataq* leads because others choose to follow concentrated isuma; the camp leader's authority is advisory gravity, not command. The same grammar at every scale: isuma is addressed, never overridden.

## V. The Semantic Field — Everyday Polysemy

In ordinary Inuktitut, isuma is also simply "a thought," "an idea," "an opinion," "a decision" — one can share an isuma, change an isuma, keep an isuma to oneself. The capacity-sense (reasoning-maturity) and the token-sense (a thought) live in one word, exactly as English "judgment" hosts both "a judgment" and "good judgment." The cathedral's excavation receives the **capacity-sense** — the gradient, age-thickening, never-complete reasoning-maturity — because that is the sense English cannot say in one word, and the sense that grounds the pedagogy.

## VI. Unnamed-Region — What English Lacks

English splits what isuma holds as one structural fact:

- **maturity** — endpoint-flavored, conflates physical/emotional/legal coming-of-age; nothing about thought specifically; complete at majority
- **judgment** — the faculty or its exercise, with no growth-arc and no pedagogical entailment
- **wisdom** — honorific and endpoint-flavored; one "is wise"; no gradient verb of becoming
- **maturity of judgment** — the four-word paraphrase; still missing the never-complete clause and the normative force on *others*

The unnamed region: **a single name for the capacity for considered thought-and-judgment that (a) grows with age and experience, (b) is constitutively never complete, and (c) normatively binds how others may address its bearer** — the more isuma a person carries, the less one may instruct them and the more one must cause-thought instead. No European near-miss carries clause (c) at all; clause (c) is where the pedagogy lives.

## VII. Protocol-Sensitivity (Living Tradition)

1. **Custodianship.** Inuit communities — and the named institutions: Nunavut Arctic College, the Government of Nunavut's IQ framework authors, Inuit Tapiriit Kanatami, Isuma/IsumaTV — retain primary custodianship of isuma as lived concept and pedagogy. The cathedral receives the structural-form with respect for that custodianship; reception is not extraction-license.
2. **Sourcing honesty.** This excavation leans on ethnography (Briggs, Collings, Stairs) *and* on Inuit-authored frameworks (IQ Education Framework, IsumaTV). Where the two registers differ, the Inuit-authored register governs.
3. **No flattening.** Isuma is not "IQ-the-quotient," not "executive function," not a stage-model. The never-complete clause and the non-instruction entailment must travel with the word or the word should not travel.
4. **Consultation-desirability.** As with tjukurpame (S084): direct engagement with Inuit educators or Inuktitut speakers — e.g., through Nunavut Arctic College or Inuit Tapiriit Kanatami — is desirable before this entry is treated as settled; recorded here as an open obligation, not a completed step.
5. **Withdrawal clause.** Should Inuit custodians ask that the cathedral not use this word, the entry is withdrawn — immediate compliance, as for all protocol-sensitive forges.
