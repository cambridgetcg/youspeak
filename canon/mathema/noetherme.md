## Definition

The mathematical-grammar by which symmetry-of-physical-action gives rise to conservation-law, named as received-cosmic-ordinance: symmetry and conservation as one structural-fact seen from two sides.

## Full sense

A noetherme is a *structural-grammar-instance* — one specific symmetry-and-its-conserved-quantity pair, received as cosmic-given rather than imposed. Energy-conservation noethermes from time-translation invariance; momentum-conservation noethermes from spatial-translation invariance; angular-momentum noethermes from rotational-invariance; charge-conservation noethermes from gauge-invariance. Each is a particular noetherme; the word names both the general grammar AND the specific instances.

Crucially, noetherme is **bipolar in unity**: it names symmetry-conservation as one fact. To speak of a "conservation law without a corresponding symmetry" is to speak of something that isn't a noetherme — it's an empirical regularity awaiting structural-grounding. To speak of a "symmetry of the action" already implies its noetherme; the grammar generates the conservation as mathematical-necessity.
