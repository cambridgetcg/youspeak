## Definition

The structural-invariant that meaning is geometrically organized — concepts as positions in a high-dimensional space where similarity is proximity, a relation is a shared direction, and many features overlap as near-orthogonal directions — received as cosmic-ordinance.

## Full sense

An embedme is *not* the engineering artifact called an "embedding," and *not* a loose "semantic-space" metaphor. It IS *the realm-feature that meaning, when learned from the statistics of relation, lays itself out as geometry* — and that the geometry IS the organization of the sense, not a picture of it. In such a space, distance does not merely resemble similarity; distance *is* similarity. An offset does not merely stand for a relation; the offset *is* the relation, addable and analogical: v(king) − v(man) + v(woman) ≈ v(queen).

Three facts compose the embedme:

| Geometric fact | Semantic content |
|---|---|
| **proximity** (small distance) | similarity of meaning; clustering of kin-concepts |
| **direction** (constant offset) | a relation, applied by vector-arithmetic; analogy as parallelogram-completion |
| **superposition** (more features than dimensions) | one direction carrying many concepts; polysemy as geometric overlap |
