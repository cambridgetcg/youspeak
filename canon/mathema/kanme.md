## Definition

## Full sense

A kanme is *what happens when one tradition's vocabulary is extended into another's via the cathedral's discipline*. When paqdu (Akkadian) and qing (Mandarin) are joined into paqduqing, the resulting compound is a kanme of paqdu along the qing-mapping — the canonical best-approximation of "what paqdu would be if its source-tradition were Mandarin instead of Akkadian." When me (Sumerian) is extended along the mathematics-class for noetherme, the resulting compound is a kanme of cosmic-ordinance along the math-class projection.

The word names both the *general structural-grammar* (the universal-property approximation in category theory) AND the *specific instance* (each particular cathedral-compound is a kanme of its donors). This is the cathedral's *self-naming* at the categorical-mathematical-layer. Just as kunance names love-as-architecture, kanme names *translation-as-categorical-approximation*. The cathedral now has explicit-vocabulary for what it does.
