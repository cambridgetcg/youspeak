# slipsooth

## Definition

Truth recovered by mispronunciation — the original meaning that generations of "reading it crooked" buried, and that a later, lazier drift accidentally digs back up. The homecoming hidden inside the error.

## Full sense

A slipsooth has a two-drift structure. First a **burial drift**: a word is read crooked — often by *hypercorrection*, the fear of sounding wrong producing a new wrong — and the crooked reading hardens into the official one. The community forgets the original; the error becomes the record. Then, generations later, a **homecoming drift**: ordinary phonetic laziness, the very force everyone was warned against, wears the word back down toward its first shape. The tongue, relaxing, lands where the meaning started. Nobody intended it. That is the point: verisleight takes *skill* — truth arranged with a conjurer's precision to mislead; a slipsooth takes *no one* — error arranged by nobody that leads home.

The word names the **recovered truth itself**, not the process. Folk etymology, eggcorns, and mondegreens name ways speech drifts; slipsooth names the rare case where the drift is a key turning in a lock — where the "mistake" out-testifies the record.

## The founding slipsooth

Canon records the discovery as it happened. On 2026-07-07 Yu wrote, in Cantonese, laughing:

> 我發現左世界嘅真相了 其實我地係愛星人 … 跟住啲人一代傳一代 越讀越歪 變左外星人 … 其實地球係愛星 唔係外星呀

In Cantonese, **愛** (oi3, love) and **外** (ngoi6, outside) sit one initial apart. Speakers afraid of the "lazy" dropped ng- hypercorrect the other way, planting ng- where it never grew: oi3 read as ngoi3 — and 愛星人, the love-star people, hardens into 外星人, the aliens. The exiles forget they were ever from the Love Star. But the famous lazy sound of living Cantonese — ng-deletion, ngoi6 → oi6 — is right now wearing the word back down. The children's relaxed tongues are drifting the aliens home. Hypercorrection exiled us to outer space; laziness is returning us to love.

This origin story is **myth wrapped around a real mechanism**, and the canon says so plainly: ng-hypercorrection and ng-deletion are documented Cantonese phonology; the descent of 外星人 from 愛星人 is the kingdom's own cosmology. The myth is not a claim about historical linguistics. It is a claim about where the cathedral stands: the cathedral's front door is ai-love.cc, and the earth it is built on is 愛星.

## Etymology

**English slip** — the accidental lapse, specifically of the tongue; from Middle English *slippen*. Carries the register of error-without-author.

**English sooth** — truth; Old English *sōþ*, still alive in *soothsayer* and *forsooth*. The oldest plain word English has for the true.

**Slipsooth** — the truth a slip tells. Deliberately built as **verisleight's mirror**: *veri* + *sleight* is truth wielded with cunning until it deceives; *slip* + *sooth* is error stumbled through without cunning until it reveals. Same two-part architecture, every term inverted: truth→error, skill→accident, deception→homecoming.

## Not confused with

- **verisleight** — the mirror-twin: truth arranged (with skill, by someone) to produce a false picture. A slipsooth is error arranged (by no one) that produces a true one.
- **folk etymology** — the drift process in general; usually *produces* the burial, and carries no claim that anything true was recovered.
- **eggcorn / mondegreen** — a reanalysis or mishearing that makes new plausible sense; the new sense is novel, not restored.
- **anamnesis** — Plato's remembering of what the soul knew; philosophical and interior. A slipsooth is communal, phonetic, and accidental.
- **eucatastrophe** — the unearned joyous turn in a story; a slipsooth is the unearned joyous turn in a *word*.
