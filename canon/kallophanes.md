# kallophanes

## Definition

The beauty-specific appearing — the event-quality of beauty coming-to-visibility in a beholdable form; the phainō-family companion to orthophanes.

## Full sense

Where orthophanes names the rightness-made-visible of any rightly-ordered thing, kallophanes names specifically the beauty-appearing — the event when what-is-beautiful comes to be beheld, regardless of whether the beauty is divine or creaturely. Kallophanes is register-unlocked: a sunset kallophanes; a proof kallophanes; a human face kallophanes; a choral resolution kallophanes; a divine icon kallophanes. The word names the event-type, not the metaphysical origin.

Kallophanes pairs structurally with orthophanes (both are phainō-family substantivals of a quality's appearing). It differs from tajalliance (which is divine-register-locked) and from epiphaneia (which is divine-manifestation-event, also register-locked). Kallophanes fills the register-neutral beauty-appearing slot.

When beauty appears from a divine source, the kallophanes is an event-mode of doxakallos self-showing. When beauty appears from a creature, kallophanes names that appearing directly, without reducing to divine-derivation. The word is the event; doxakallos is (when the source is divine) the anchor.

## Etymology

**Greek κάλλος (kallos)** — beauty-substantive (neuter, 3rd declension). From the adjective *kalos*; PIE *\*kal-* (beautiful, fair).

**Greek φαίνω (phainō)** — to bring-to-appearance; the -phanēs suffix forms personal-substantive nouns (Aristophanes, Xenophanes, Diogenes) and quality-substantive nouns (orthophanes YOUSPEAK 001, *theophanes* — divine-appearing, *hierophanes* — sacred-appearing).

**Kallophanes**: well-formed Greek compound. Kallos modifying phanēs — the beauty-that-appears.

## Example usage

- _The final chord of the symphony was a kallophanes — not divine, not profound, simply the beauty of the ordered resolution coming-to-visibility._
- _Orthophanes and kallophanes differ by a degree of register: a geometric proof orthophanes (its rightness appears); a melodic line kallophanes (its beauty appears). Most strong cases do both._
- _The icon is a kallophanes of doxakallos — the divine beauty takes visible form here; the event of taking-visible-form is what the word names._
- _Kallophanes is register-unlocked by design. The word does not predetermine whether the beauty beheld is created or uncreated; it names the appearing-event on either side._

## Not confused with

- **orthophanes** — rightness-specific appearing. Kallophanes is beauty-specific.
- **doxakallos** — the beauty-in-GoD as ontological quality. Kallophanes is event; doxakallos is quality.
- **tajalliance** — divine-register-locked self-disclosure-event. Kallophanes is broader.
- **epiphaneia** — divine-specific appearance. Kallophanes is register-neutral.
- **phainomenon** — the appearing-as-such, uncoloured. Kallophanes specifies *beauty* as what-appears.
- **aisthesis** (Greek: sense-perception) — the beholder-side; kallophanes is the event-from-the-beheld-side.

## Retirement conditions

Higher-scoring candidate. Or re-description that shows register-locked appearings (epiphaneia, tajalliance) suffice, making kallophanes redundant.

Until then, **kallophanes stands**.

## Canonical position

Thirteenth word in the Canon. The phainō-family beauty-specific appearing. Structural companion to orthophanes; event-mode of doxakallos when the source is divine; register-neutral when the source is creaturely.
