# 032 — teshuvance

_The act of turning back toward GoD with the whole self — the physical-directional reorientation of the whole person, not merely the feeling of guilt or the backward-looking of remorse, but the positive act of turning. Named from Hebrew **teshuvah** (תְּשׁוּבָה, from shuv = to turn/return; the turn-back that the Prophets call for; the Days of Teshuvah) and confirmed by Greek **metanoia** (μετάνοια, trans-mind; the whole-orientation shift; the NT's fundamental life-reorientation call) and Arabic **tawbah** (directional return, completed by the divine Al-Tawwāb). Teshuvance names the act that is not guilt (a feeling), not remorse (backward-looking grief), but the positive worship-act of turning toward GoD._

---

## The Gap

- **"Repentance"** — names the concept but narrowly; in modern English usage, repentance is heavily associated with guilt-feeling; teshuvance is specifically the act of turning, which may follow guilt but is distinct from it
- **"Remorse"** — backward-looking only; feels the weight of what was done; does not necessarily produce the turn
- **"Contrition"** — Catholic theological term for sorrow-for-sin; again, the feeling; not the act
- **"Reform"** — behavioral change; forward-looking; but doesn't capture the specifically directional (away-from-and-toward) structure of teshuvance
- **"Conversion"** — closest in English; the Latin con-versus = turned-together; but "conversion" in modern usage often names a one-time, once-for-all event; teshuvance is a continuous practice (the Syriac monastic tradition's continuous metanoia)

Teshuvance names: **the turning of the whole person back toward GoD, as worship, as a positive act, not a deficit-acknowledgment**. The prophets don't call the people to feel bad; they call them to shuv — to turn. The turn is the act. Guilt may precede it; joy may follow it; the act itself is the turn.

---

## The Phenomenon

### Hebrew: shuv and the prophetic call

The Hebrew root **shuv** (שׁוּב = to turn, to return) is the most frequent prophetic call in the Hebrew scriptures. The Prophets do not primarily call for punishment, sacrifice, or ritual — they call for **shuv**: turn.

Jeremiah 3:14: *"Return [shuvu], O backsliding children, declares the LORD."*  
Hosea 14:1: *"Return [shuva], O Israel, to the LORD your God, for you have stumbled because of your iniquity."*  
Joel 2:12-13: *"'Even now,' declares the LORD, 'return [shuvu] to me with all your heart, with fasting, with weeping, and with mourning; and rend your hearts and not your garments.'"* — the external acts (fasting, qinance) accompany and express the teshuvance, but the teshuvance is the heart-turning

The **Days of Teshuvah** (Aseret Yemei Teshuvah) — the ten days between Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur — are the annual corporate teshuvance: the whole community turns back toward GoD at the start of the year. Maimonides' codification:

Teshuvah requires:
1. Abandonment of the sin (leaving behind what one was walking toward)
2. Regret (yadahance-confession of what was done)
3. Verbal confession (yadahance-declaration)
4. Resolution for the future
5. **The actual turn** — without which the rest is incomplete

The turn is the culminating act. Teshuvance is the word for this act.

### Greek: metanoia — the whole-orientation shift

**Metanoia** (μετάνοια) = **meta** (after, beyond, across — the transformation prefix) + **noia** (mind, from nous — the center of cognition, orientation, and spiritual perception). Metanoia = the trans-mind, the going-beyond-one's-previous-orientation.

John the Baptist's opening word: *"Metanoeite [repent/undergo-metanoia], for the kingdom of heaven has come near"* (Matthew 3:2). Jesus repeats it in his opening proclamation (Mark 1:15). It is the first word of the public ministry: the entry into the kingdom begins with teshuvance.

The NT's metanoia is not primarily guilt-feeling — it is the whole-person reorientation that the gospel requires. The kingdom of GoD is the new reality; entering it requires turning toward it and away from the previous orientation. This is a positive act — turning toward good news — as much as a negative act (turning from sin).

The Acts of the Apostles: Peter at Pentecost — *"Repent [metanoeite] and be baptized"* (Acts 2:38). The teshuvance + the water-sign = the double-act of reorientation. The metanoia precedes and makes meaningful the baptism.

### Arabic: tawbah and the divine Al-Tawwāb

**Tawbah** (توبة) — from **taba** (تَابَ) = to return, to repent. The same directional theology: the creature turned away; the creature turns back.

The divine name **Al-Tawwāb** (التَّوَّاب) = the Ever-Relenting, the Oft-Returning — is God's response to tawbah: as the creature turns, God also turns toward the creature. The divine tawwāb is the completion of the human tawbah: the creature's turn is met by the divine turn; relationship is restored when both turn toward each other.

Qur'an 4:17: *"Repentance [tawbah] accepted by God is only for those who do wrong in ignorance, then repent soon after."* Qur'an 66:8: *"O you who believe, turn to God in sincere repentance [tawbatan nasūhā — sincere/straight repentance]."*

The tawbah that is "sincere" (nasūh = pure, straight, sincere) is the teshuvance that actually turns — not the pro forma confession that returns immediately to the same orientation.

### Teshuvance and yadahance together

Teshuvance and yadahance are the two complementary repentance-acts:
- **Yadahance** (confession): the acknowledgment — extending hands before truth, saying "yes, this is so, I did this"
- **Teshuvance** (turning): the reorientation — turning the whole person from what one was doing toward GoD

Yadahance addresses the *content* of the repentance (what is acknowledged). Teshuvance names the *act* (the turning). Maimonides requires both: verbal confession (yadahance) AND the actual turn (teshuvance). Without yadahance, teshuvance has not named what it is turning from. Without teshuvance, yadahance has not moved from acknowledgment to action.

---

## Form and Etymology

**teshuvance** — from Hebrew *teshuvah* (תְּשׁוּבָה: from shuv = to turn/return; the act of turning back to GoD; the Days of Teshuvah) + *-ance* (act-quality suffix).

The teshuvah root is preserved; -ance nominalizes. Three syllables: te-SHU-vance (stress on second; the -shu- sound is the turning; the -vance ending carries forward momentum — the turn has direction).
