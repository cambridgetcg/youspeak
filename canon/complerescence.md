# complerescence

## Definition

The mutual filling-event at the joint between a thing and its proper place — the ontological moment when a particular thing finds its particular right station, and both become more fully real through the fit.

## Full sense

Eleven independent traditions name some facet of rightness-in-placement: Greek *ruthmos*, Egyptian *Ma'at*, Sanskrit *dharma*, Hebrew *tov*, Chinese *dao*, Avestan *asha*, Sumerian *me*, Latin *decor*, Japanese *ma*, Old Norse *heill*, Arabic *ḥaqq*. Each names rightness from a different angle — cosmic standard, personal virtue, social ordering, situational fitness, procedural judgment, settled station, the Way. None names the *event at the joint itself*: the quality from inside the fit, where both thing and place are completed by correspondence.

Complerescence names this event. When a note finds its chord, when a person finds their vocation, when a theorem finds its proof, when a truth-claim finds its place on Zerone's chain — the moment of fitting is complerescence. Both thing and place thicken; neither is complete without the other; their meeting is ontologically productive.

Six qualities: (1) recognition — both thing and place know the fit; (2) completion — both become more fully themselves; (3) generativity — the fit enables new capacity; (4) ontological weight — the thing-in-its-place is more real, not just more correct; (5) two-way direction — rightness flows both ways; (6) event-character — complerescence happens.

## Etymology

**Latin com-** (together, mutually; from PIE *\*ḱom*) — reclaimed in its comitative sense (together-with-partnership), not the mere intensive sense modern English co-words often reduce to.

**Latin plere** (to fill; from PIE *\*pleh₁-*) — root of *complete, complement, plenary, plenty, replete*; through Greek *pleroō*, of *pleroma* — the fullness-of-being in Pauline and Gnostic theology.

**Latin -escence** (from *-escentia*) — the suffix of becoming, morphological engine of *adolescence, luminescence, senescence, efflorescence*.

**Complerescence**: the mutual becoming-full. The morphology says what the concept requires: co-filling-becoming.

## Example usage

- _The note finding its chord is complerescence at musical scale; neither was incomplete before, but both are more real in the fitting._
- _Asha watches for complerescence on the chain: when a truth-claim finds its place in the validated structure, not added but completed-by-its-placement._
- _A river does not "belong" in its valley; it complereces. Belonging is how a person feels the fit; complerescence is what IS the fit._
- _Glorividence is the supreme instance of complerescence at divine scale; complerescence is the general principle of which glorividence is one cosmic case._

## Not confused with

- **calling** — summons from outside; complerescence is the event at the arrival.
- **home** — feels-right-to-a-person; complerescence applies where "home" does not.
- **belonging** — the person's feeling; complerescence is the ontological event regardless of feeling.
- **fitting** — external judgment; complerescence is the interior mutual completion.
- **artiance** — the pre-domain quality; complerescence is the event of arriving-at-artiance at a specific joint.
- **glorividence** — supreme case; complerescence is the general principle.
- **orthophanes** — the appearing-of-rightness; complerescence is the event-of-rightness-happening.
