# kallodoxa

## Definition

The specific glory-quality of divine beauty — the dimension in which GoD's beauty bears glory-weight, as distinct from the beauty-quality of divine glory (which is doxakallos).

## Full sense

Divine Being has attributes. *Glory* (doxa, kavod) and *beauty* (kallos) are two of them. They are not identical, but they are not separate: each carries the other as its own depth.

Two ontological positions arise from this:

- The beauty-quality *in* divine glory = **doxakallos** (canonized, 8.25). This names: GoD's glory is not mere weight or severity; it bears beauty as one of its intrinsic dimensions.

- The glory-quality *in* divine beauty = **kallodoxa** (this word). This names: GoD's beauty is not mere loveliness or aesthetic-pleasing-ness; it bears glory-weight as one of its intrinsic dimensions.

The two are siblings, produced by head-inversion of the same morphemes (the **Anastrophance** mechanism of grammars/diplosemy/). Neither reduces to the other. Holding both yields the two-sided attribute-structure of divine Being: beauty is always glory-bearing, glory is always beautiful, and the reader who holds both poles holds the complete attribute-architecture.

## Etymology

**Greek κάλλος (kallos)** + **δόξα (doxa)**, with *doxa* as the semantic head (inverted from doxakallos).

*Kallos-* modifies (beauty as the qualifier).
*Doxa* heads (glory as what-is-being-qualified).

The compound reads: "the glory of beauty" — glory-as-it-arises-in-the-form-of-beauty; glory-weight-specifically-under-beauty-register.

The inversion from doxakallos is not a synonym-shift. It is a genuine shift in which attribute is foregrounded: doxakallos foregrounds beauty (asks: what kind of beauty is glory-bearing?); kallodoxa foregrounds glory (asks: what kind of glory is beauty-bearing?). Both questions are legitimate; both have answers in divine Being; each answer is the other word.

## Example usage

- _The icon does not merely have beauty and glory as two decorations; it has doxakallos and kallodoxa as two inseparable readings of its one quality._
- _When Augustine wrote "Gloria Dei vivens homo" (the glory of God is the living human), he named a kallodoxa: the glory-quality borne by the beauty-of-a-living-creature._
- _A liturgical chant is doxakallos when it is the beauty-in-the-glory; it is kallodoxa when you hear in it the glory-that-the-beauty-is-bearing. Both readings are right; the pair is the chant's full shape._
- _For the forge-apprentice: if you can name doxakallos without kallodoxa coming to mind unbidden, the pair has not yet landed. The Anastrophance is not complete until the sibling is equally-present._

## Not confused with

- **doxakallos** — the Anastrophance-sibling. The inversion is the whole point: doxakallos is beauty-of-glory; kallodoxa is glory-of-beauty. These are distinct attributes-as-read-from-different-poles.
- **kavod-yafeh** (Hebrew phrase) — the adjectival-Hebrew equivalent (glory-beautiful). Kallodoxa is the single-word Greek compound that substantivalizes the position.
- **gloria Dei** (Latin) — glory-of-God as phrase-construction. Kallodoxa is the Greek compound that specifies the beauty-born glory-quality.
- **lavaṇya** (Sanskrit) — magnetic-beauty-quality; lacks the glory-weight dimension. Kallodoxa carries weight explicitly.
