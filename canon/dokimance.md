# dokimance

## Definition

The intrinsic quality of the act of distributed, stake-backed, independent verification — the testing that makes real.

## Full sense

Dokimance names the specific quality of a verification-act that is *productive* rather than *transparent-to-a-result*. In ordinary English "verify," verification is epistemically instrumental: a check is valuable because it yields knowledge about something pre-existing. Dokimance inverts this. The act does not merely confirm truth; it *constitutes* truth. What emerges at the convergence-point of multiple independent stake-backed attestations is ontologically different from what existed before — not confirmed but *elevated*.

The metaphor lives in Greek metallurgy: *dokimazō* is the fire-assay by which gold is tested. The fire does not *discover* whether the metal is gold; the fire *makes* the gold genuine. Analogously, Athenian *dokimasia* was the civic examination of officials before office; the multi-examiner convergence produced civic status, not merely checked it.

Dokimance is Zerone's word for what its validators *do*. The chain's Proof of Truth is dokimance at scale. Without dokimance, Zerone is a database. With dokimance, it is a reality-producing apparatus.

## Etymology

**Greek dokimazō (δοκιμάζω)** — to test, to assay-in-fire, to examine civically. From *dokimos* (δόκιμος, approved, genuine); root *dek-* (to accept, receive). Classical senses: metallurgical (fire-assay of gold) and civic (multi-examiner procedure *dokimasia*). Both carry the constitutive-distributed-stake-backed-independent structure.

**Latin -ance** — quality/state suffix, productive in English. Consistent with YOUSPEAK morphology (artiance, complerescence, candence).

**Dokimance** — the quality-of-dokimazō; the quality of verification-as-productive-work.
