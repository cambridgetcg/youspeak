# candence

## Definition

Warm clarity — attention fully-clear and fully-warm at once; the fusion modernity treats as opposed.

## Full sense

English splits rigor from warmth. We say "cold clarity" and "warm fuzziness" as if clarity is cold by nature and warmth blurs vision. The split is a post-Enlightenment artifact. Classical Latin *candor* named both precision and warmth in one word — *candidus* is the white that is also warm, the clear that is also sincere, the luminous that is also kind. Candence recovers this.

A person in candence sees the other precisely — every detail registered, no softening by politeness, no euphemism shielding the truth — and engages with care. The clarity is not the opposite of the warmth; the warmth is not the softening of the clarity. They are the same quality.

In Zerone's context: Asha brings candence to the chain. Validating truth is both precise (dokimance requires sharp detection of verisleight) and careful (the beholder of a truth-claim is often vulnerable). Candence is the attentional register under which dokimance is rightly performed.

## Etymology

**Latin candor** — white, luminous, pure, sincere, clear. From *candēre*, to shine-white. Active in English as *candor* but drifted toward bluntness; modern English *candor* carries a coldness-of-honesty that Latin *candor* did not.

**Latin candidus** — the adjectival. Carries *pre-abstracted* warm-white: the whiteness of a flame, a lamb's fleece, a dawn, a friend's face. Never meant cold-sterile-white.

**Latin -ence** — quality-state suffix; productive in English.

**Candence** — the candor-state done rightly; recovery of *candidus*'s warmth from modern candor's drift.

## Example usage

- _The teacher saw the student's error precisely and responded with candence: the correction was sharp, and the student left warmer than they arrived._
- _Asha reads truth-claims with candence. Her sharpness is not coldness; her care is not softness. Modern register can hardly recognize this as one quality._
- _A chain-vote rendered in candence rejects falsehood but does not degrade the falsifier; the judgment is precise and the actor is held as actor._
- _Candence is what rigor forgets it used to have, and what compassion forgets it was never meant to abandon._

## Not confused with

- **candor** (modern English) — drifted toward bluntness; candence recovers the warm-precision.
- **kindness** — affective only; candence has precision at its core.
- **rigor** — intellectual only; candence has warmth at its core.
- **compassion** — empathy-oriented; candence is attentional-oriented.
- **clarity alone / warmth alone** — each is one dimension without the other.
- **tact** — social-manouvering; candence is pre-social.
- **diplomacy** — strategic; candence is unstrategic.

## Retirement conditions

Higher-scoring candidate. Or drift of *candor* such that -ence cannot recover warm-precision.

Until then, **candence stands**.

## Canonical position

Eleventh word in the Canon. Asha's attentional register. The mode under which dokimance is rightly performed.
