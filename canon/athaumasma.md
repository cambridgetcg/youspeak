# athaumasma

## Definition

The indelibility-quality of a beholding-trace after active wondering has migrated into structural load-bearing — the orthophanes that has stopped being wondered-at and started being lived-from.

## Full sense

At first the beholding of orthophanes is marvel. The beholder wonders at it, returns to it, turns it over in attention. Over time — weeks, years, a lifetime — the wonder integrates. The trace no longer requires foreground-attention; it has become part of how the beholder stands. The orthophanes is still present, but it is present as axis, not as object. Athaumasma names this condition: the beholding-trace after thaumazō has ceased.

Athaumasma is healed into the structure. It is not trauma (which is wound-unhealed). It is not nostalgia (which is ache-of-absence; athaumasma is active-carrying). It is not memory (which is something-recalled; athaumasma is something-stood-within). It is the specific quality a significant beholding's trace bears once it has been *integrated*.

One does not own an athaumasma. One is-in-athaumasma-of an orthophanes. The construction is relational: "I am in athaumasma of that evening's music"; "She has lived in athaumasma of that icon for forty years." The preposition matters: *of*, not *about*.

## Etymology

**a- (privative)** + **thaumazō (to wonder-anew)** + **-ma (result-of-action)**.

*Thaumazō*: Plato's word in *Theaetetus* 155d ("wonder is the beginning of philosophy"). Names the event-structure of being-stopped-by, of marveling-at. Alive in English as *thaumaturge* (wonder-worker).

*A-*: Greek privative prefix (a-theist, a-typical). In compound with thaumazō, does not mean "never-wondered" but "no-longer-wondering" — the wondering has ceased because its work is done.

*-ma*: Result-of-action nominal suffix. Greek productive: *phantasma* (what-has-appeared), *stigma* (what-has-pierced), *trauma* (what-has-wounded). *Athaumasma* is what-has-un-wondered — the trace after the wondering.

The morphology says: the thing that was once thaumazō-d but is no longer, now carried as structure.

## Example usage

- _He was not haunted by the old icon; he was in athaumasma of it._
- _The teacher did not instruct; she conveyed the athaumasma of her own prior orthophanes, and the students received it without being told what had been given._
- _Forty years later she stood on a different axis; it was the athaumasma of a single evening's beholding._
- _Athaumasma is what marveling becomes when marveling has done its work._

## Not confused with

- **nostalgia** — ache-of-absence; athaumasma is not ache.
- **trauma** — wound-unhealed; athaumasma is healed into structure.
- **memory** — something-recalled; athaumasma is something-stood-within.
- **formation** — too generic (all experience forms us); athaumasma is specifically beholding-sourced.
- **samkance** (DICTIONARY) — the body's temporal archive of all lived experience; samkance is broader, athaumasma is beholding-specific.
- **metastrophesis** — the standing-turned-condition; athaumasma is the trace-that-did-the-turning. Sibling slots: athaumasma is the seed-retained; metastrophesis is the tree grown from it.
- **core memory** (psychological usage) — treats the trace as still-a-memory; athaumasma names it as post-memory structure.
- **impression** — too superficial.

## Retirement conditions

Higher-scoring candidate. Or re-description of the phenomenon that shows athaumasma and metastrophesis are not separate (merging them) or that shows the indelibility-quality is not single (splitting athaumasma into multiple slots).

Until then, **athaumasma stands**.

## Canonical position

Sixth word in the Canon. Indelibility-slot of the Liturgy. What the orthophanes has become in the beholder after the wondering has done its work.
