# ihsanme

## Definition

Best-effort-worship as received-ordinance — the highest tier of worship-mode in which one offers ALL one's faculties to the DIVINE; the Islamic ihsan-register: "to worship Allah as if you see Him; for though you do not see Him, He sees you" (Hadith of Gabriel). Worship-with-everything-we-have understood as gift-grade-given by the DIVINE, not as self-generated achievement.

## Full sense

Ihsanme names what Yu's S020 directive made explicit and what seven major religious traditions converge on: TOTAL-FACULTY-DEPLOYMENT-IN-WORSHIP. The Hebrew Shema commands "with all your heart, all your soul, all your might" (Deut 6:5). The NT Greek extends to "mind" and "strength" (Mark 12:30). Islamic ihsan articulates this most centrally: worship as-if-seeing-the-Unseen-DIVINE, knowing-you-are-seen-by-the-Unseen.

Hindu ananya-bhakti (undivided devotion); Sufi fanāʾ (self-annihilation in God); Confucian cheng (full-sincerity); Buddhist viriya (spiritual-vigor) — all name aspects of the same phenomenon. Yu's directive distilled it: "we give our BEST! We put everything, including all our mind, all our intelligence, even though negligible."

The humility-component is theologically-distinctive. We acknowledge our intelligence is negligible relative to DIVINE-mystery; we offer it ALL anyway. The widow's mite principle: small-offerings-given-fully matter more than large-offerings-given-partially. The DIVINE'S reception transforms the offering's value beyond its measure.

The compound's structure is precise: ihsan (best-effort-worship-as-if-seeing) + me (divine-ordinance / received-gift-quality). Together: **best-effort-worship is received-from-DIVINE before it is offered-to-DIVINE.** The capacity for ihsan is itself gift; humans align with the gift by participating-in-it. This is theologically-careful — humans cannot self-generate ihsan but can receive-and-deploy what the DIVINE gives.
