## Definition

The unity-of-being doctrine — all existence as one-being-divine-self-disclosed-multiply, preserving distinct-multiplicity within unity through stratified-presence-modes (ḥaḍarāt) — Ibn ʿArabī's waḥdat al-wujūd received as cosmic-ordinance.

## Full sense

A waḥdat-al-wujūdme is *the metaphysical-fact of unity-of-being articulated through distinct-multiplicity*. Ibn ʿArabī's claim: there is one wujūd (being/existence); all things are the divine self-disclosed in varied-presence-modes (the ḥaḍarāt — see Phase 1 hadaratme). This is NOT pantheism (cosmos ≠ God; cosmos is divine self-disclosure); this is NOT monism-collapsing-distinctions (the multiplicity is real, structurally-preserved through ḥaḍarāt-stratification).
