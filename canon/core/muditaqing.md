# muditaqing

## Definition

Sympathetic-joy-as-felt-bond — the joy that lights in one heart because another's came true. Gladness at another's good fortune with no admixture of self: no ledger, no mirror. The direct antidote to envy; the only wealth that multiplies by being witnessed.

## Full sense

English keeps a borrowed word for joy at another's misfortune — *schadenfreude*, imported whole and polished with use — and has nothing for joy at another's good fortune. The hole is so notorious that English has tried to fill it at least three times: *confelicity* (17th-century Latinate, never rooted), *compersion* (1970s, one subculture's register), *freudenfreude* (2020s pop-psychology back-formation). Three failed forgings are the strongest gap-evidence: the realm-region is felt, reached for, and still unnamed.

Pali *muditā* is the most developed name for the region: third of the four brahmavihārā (divine abidings) and of the four appamaññā (immeasurables) — mettā, karuṇā, **muditā**, upekkhā. Buddhaghosa's Visuddhimagga (IX.95) gives it full grammatical machinery: its characteristic is *gladdening*; its function is *unenviousness*; it is manifested as the *elimination of aversion*; its proximate cause is *seeing beings' success* — and it *fails when it produces merriment*, the counterfeit named inside the source tradition itself. Its far enemy is *issā* (envy) and *arati* (the bored aversion that cannot celebrate); its near enemy is *gehasita somanassa*, worldly gladness with identification — joy that has quietly made the other's win its own. The commentarial tradition holds muditā the **hardest of the four to cultivate**: sorrow asks to be helped and so gives the self a role; another's success asks nothing of the self but witness.

The choice of qing (felt-bond) over me (received-ordinance) is deliberate and corrects the forge-status projection of *muditāme*: sympathetic joy is constitutively relational — it cannot occur without another whose fortune lights it. It is joy as a substance *between* persons, not a deed and not a decree. What is named is not a private mood but the bond the joy travels. This is the same move danaqing made (qing over me because the gift-dimension is fundamentally relational); the two now stand as the **unselfed-love pair** — danaqing the giving-pole, muditaqing the beholding-pole — and the pair shares one weight, 8.00/8.00, as the bond-tolerance pair walkekin/jeongqing shares 7.50/7.50.

The convergence is five living traditions wide: Yiddish *farginen* (to ungrudgingly grant another their good — the not-begrudging verb, honest about envy being the default current), Modern Hebrew *firgun* (borrowed from farginen within living memory — proof the register is so needed a modern language imported it), Dutch *gunnen* (*ik gun het je* — joy and grant fused), German *Mitfreude*, rare but carried by Nietzsche into the philosophy of friendship: *Mitfreude, nicht Mitleiden, macht den Freund* — fellow-joy, not fellow-suffering, makes the friend (Menschliches, Allzumenschliches §499).

## Etymology

*muditā* (Pali, Indo-European Indo-Aryan; from root *mud-* "to rejoice, be glad"; Sanskrit √mud, *muditā* "delighted, joyful"; third brahmavihāra; cultivation begins with a *boon companion* per Visuddhimagga IX.84–86 — the intimate who is constantly glad, where the gladness ignites without effort and widens from that live coal toward all beings) + *qíng* (Mandarin 情, "felt-bond, deep emotional connection"; established YOUSPEAK morpheme).

Full morphological read: *[mudita-][-qing]* = rejoicing-at-another's-fortune + as felt-bond. **The joy that lights in one heart because another's came true.**

## Example usage

- "When her name was read out and mine was not, something in me still stood up — muditaqing, before I could decide to feel it."
- "He keeps no mirror in his congratulations; his muditaqing wants nothing back — not even to be noticed wanting nothing."
- "A team with muditaqing multiplies every win by eleven; a team without it divides every win by envy."
- "The losing bench rose first — pure muditaqing, the standing ovation from the ones it cost the most."
- "Muditaqing is the proof that love wants nothing: the friend's win felt as warmth, not as mirror."
- "It is the only wealth that multiplies by being witnessed: joy, unlike the prize, is not divided by the number who feel it."

## Not confused with

- **Joy** (KINGDOM force) — the substance itself, the wage life pays itself for the trouble of existing; muditaqing is that wage felt at another's window — the bond the substance travels
- **Schadenfreude** (German, in English) — the inverted twin: joy at another's misfortune; muditaqing is the antonym English never found
- **Vicarious joy** (English) — living-through, identification; precisely the *near enemy* (gehasita somanassa), excluded by muditaqing's edge
- **Pride-in-another** (English "so proud of you") — carries ownership: *my* child, *my* student; muditaqing owns nothing at the window
- **Congratulation** (English) — the speech-act, not the feeling; performable without a single watt of the light
- **Compersion** (English coinage) — niche register, one relationship-form; muditaqing is boundless in scope, igniting even for strangers
- **Danaqing** (YOUSPEAK Core) — companion-pair: danaqing gives the gift; muditaqing rejoices when the gift was never yours to give — giving-pole and beholding-pole of unselfed love
- **Jeongqing** (YOUSPEAK Core) — kin in the qing-family: jeongqing thickens through shared friction and time; muditaqing can ignite in an instant for a stranger — the slowest and the fastest of the felt-bonds
- **Doxomme** (YOUSPEAK Core) — thanksgiving for what was given *to you*; muditaqing is gladness at what was given to *another* — the two unselfings face different windows
