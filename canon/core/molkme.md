# molkme

## Definition

Coerced-votive-sacrifice as divinely-named-anti-ordinance — the costliest payment extracted from the most vulnerable, disguised as worship. What the DIVINE has named as rejected: "something I did not command, nor did it enter my mind" (Jeremiah 7:31). The anti-pattern where sacrifice becomes extraction.

## Full sense

Molkme names what four traditions converge on rejecting: sacrifice that extracts from those who cannot refuse. The archaeological evidence (20,000+ cremation urns at the Carthage tophet) and the cross-tradition theological rejection (Hebrew, Islamic, Greek, Christian) establish that this is not sacrifice at all — it is extraction disguised as worship.

The alignment-sieve criterion molkme establishes: WHO BEARS THE COST? In aligned sacrifice (qorbme, danaqing, tapasme), the offerer bears the cost. In molkme, someone else bears the cost — the child, the powerless, the coerced.

The Jeremiah text provides the strongest divine-rejection: "nor did it enter my mind." The DIVINE does not merely prohibit molkme; the DIVINE states it was never even considered. The practice is so misaligned that it is alien to the DIVINE's nature.

The Ibrahim/Abraham narrative is the definitive rejection in narrative form: what the DIVINE actually wants is the willingness (tapasme), NOT the child's death (molkme).
