# ngünecheme

## Definition

The divine whose unity is internally differentiated into four aspects on two simultaneous axes — gender (masculine + feminine) and generation (elder + young) — where neither axis is subordinate, the four aspects are one being, and the diagonal crossings are constitutive. Received as cosmic ordinance, not cultural projection.

## Full sense

**Ngünecheme** names a structure no other word in the cathedral covers: the divine whose internal differentiation is simultaneously gendered AND generational, where the four aspects are ONE being, and the crossings (diagonals) matter as much as the axes.

The Mapuche supreme Ngünechen is the most fully elaborated quaternary-androgynous divine in any tradition the cathedral has excavated. Four aspects on two axes:

- **Füchá Wentru** (Old Man / Elder Male): governing-masculine-senior; authority, protection, tradition transmission
- **Küdé Domo** (Old Woman / Elder Female): governing-feminine-senior; generative care, wisdom, deep memory
- **Üllcha Wentru** (Young Man / Junior Male): vitality, strength, warrior-protector; renewal-masculine
- **Üllcha Domo** (Young Woman / Junior Female): fertility, beauty, new life; renewal-feminine

The structure is NOT a pantheon of four separate gods — it is one divine reality articulated through four complementary dimensions. Gender complementarity: neither principle is subordinate. Generational complementarity: authority and vitality are not opposed but paired. The crossings (elder-masculine with young-feminine, elder-feminine with young-masculine) are as theologically significant as the axes.

The -me marks this as received-ordinance: the complementarity is not invented but given — the structural condition of a cosmos where neither masculine nor feminine, neither elder nor young, is sufficient alone, and the divine itself models this insufficiency-resolved-through-plenitude.

### The five structural marks

1. **One being, not pantheon.** The four aspects are constitutive of one reality, not separate gods.
2. **Four aspects, not two or three.** The quaternary exceeds binary complementarity (yin-yang) and trinitarian structure.
3. **Two axes (gender × generation).** The intersection is theologically significant — not just four, but four on two simultaneous axes.
4. **Non-subordination.** Neither axis is primary; neither gender nor generation is subordinate to the other.
5. **Constitutive crossings.** The diagonals (elder-masculine × young-feminine, elder-feminine × young-masculine) are as significant as the axes themselves.

### Cross-tradition parallels

The QUATERNARY-DIVINE structure appears across multiple traditions, each developing a different axis:

1. **Mapuche Ngünechen**: elder/young × masculine/feminine — the gender×generation intersection (the specific concept ngünecheme names)
2. **Navajo Four Sacred Mountains + Four Directions**: spatial quaternary — the four as cosmic-geographic structure
3. **Kongo dikenga four solar moments** (musoni/kala/luvemba/tukula): temporal quaternary — the four as cycle-of-existence
4. **Aztec four Tezcatlipocas**: color-directional quaternity — the four as cosmic-color-aspect
5. **Greek four elements** (Empedocles): cosmic-quaternary — the four as constitutive-substance
6. **Chinese four symbols** (四象 — qinglong/baihu/zhuque/xuanwu): directional quaternary — the four as cosmic-direction
7. **Buddhist four immeasurables** (brahmavihāra): contemplative quaternary — the four as meditative-quality

Seven traditions develop fourfold-divine or fourfold-cosmic structures. Ngünechen is unique in developing the gender×generation intersection — all others develop spatial, temporal, directional, elemental, or contemplative axes.

## Example usage

"The Nuer have kwoth as the wind-is-divine; the Mapuche have ngünecheme as the divine-is-four — not four gods, but one God whose plenitude requires gender and generation to be complete."

"Ngünecheme is what Trinity is not: gendered and generational. The Trinity's three persons transcend biology; Ngünechen's four aspects embody it — the divine whose authority has a face that is old and young, male and female, and all four are one."

"When the machi prays to all four aspects of Ngünechen, she is not praying to four gods — she is praying within ngünecheme, the four-aspect unity where tradition and renewal, masculine and feminine, are constitutive of the divine, not decorative additions to it."

## Not to be confused with

- **barzakhqing** — isthmus between dual-poles (Arabic); barzakhqing is BETWEEN two; ngünecheme is WITHIN one
- **wahdatwujudme** — unity-of-being (Arabic); wahdatwujudme is monism (all is one); ngünecheme is internal-differentiation (the one is four)
- **syzygyqing** — paired-distinct-conjoined (Greek); syzygyqing is the dyad; ngünecheme is the quadruple-on-two-axes
- **tengrime** — sky-as-divine-without-anthropomorphism (Turko-Mongol); tengrime resists anthropomorphization; ngünecheme embraces it — gender and age ARE the divine structure
- **teotlme** — divine-as-ceaseless-process (Nahuatl); teotlme is the flow; ngünecheme is the architecture
- **ubuntume** — humanity-through-relation (Bantu); ubuntume is relational-ontology at the human level; ngünecheme is internal-differentiation at the divine level

## Retirement conditions

This word would be superseded if a more learnable donor were found for the quaternary-complementary-divine concept (the ü and the four-syllable length are the main accessibility barriers), or if the concept were absorbed into a broader divine-structure word that includes quaternary-complementary as one of its sub-types. The Mapuche four-aspect structure is unique in developing the gender×generation intersection; no other tradition provides a more accessible donor for this specific concept.
