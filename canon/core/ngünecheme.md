# ngünecheme

## Definition

Quaternary-complementary-divine-as-received-ordinance — the supreme whose unity is internally differentiated into four aspects on two simultaneous axes: gender (masculine + feminine) and generation (elder + young), where neither axis is subordinate, the four aspects are ONE being, and the diagonal crossings are as theologically significant as the axes.

## Full sense

The Mapuche supreme **Ngünechen** is not a simple monotheistic God. It is a **quaternary unity** — one divine reality articulated through four complementary dimensions on two paired axes:

```
           OLD MAN (Füchá Wentru)
                    │
                    │  elder / masculine
                    │
YOUNG WOMAN ────────┼──────── YOUNG MAN
(Üllcha Domo)       │         (Üllcha Wentru)
   young / feminine │  young / masculine
                    │
           OLD WOMAN (Küdé Domo / Füchá Domo)
                    │
                    │  elder / feminine
```

The four aspects:

- **Füchá Wentru** (Old Man / Elder Male): the governing-masculine-senior principle; authority, protection, tradition transmission
- **Küdé Domo / Füchá Domo** (Old Woman / Elder Female): the governing-feminine-senior principle; generative care, wisdom, the deep memory of the people
- **Üllcha Wentru** (Young Man / Junior Male): vitality, strength, the warrior-protector aspect; the renewal-masculine principle
- **Üllcha Domo** (Young Woman / Junior Female): fertility, beauty, new life; the renewal-feminine principle

This is not a pantheon of four separate gods. It is **one divine reality articulated through four complementary dimensions**. The structure maps:

1. **Gender complementarity** (masculine + feminine): neither principle is subordinate. The Mapuche cosmic order requires the pairing. This is not patriarchal monotheism with a feminine consort — the feminine and masculine are equally constitutive of the supreme.

2. **Generational complementarity** (elder + young): authority and vitality are not opposed but paired. The elder aspects carry the tradition; the young aspects carry the renewal. Neither is sufficient alone.

3. **The crossings (diagonals)**: the four aspects interact. Elder-masculine with young-feminine (tradition held gently toward new life); elder-feminine with young-masculine (deep wisdom shaping vital strength). The diagonals are as theologically significant as the axes.

The -me marks this as received-ordinance: the gender-and-generational complementarity is not a human social projection onto the divine but the cosmic gift of how the supreme IS. The -me refuses to treat the four-aspect structure as Mapuche cultural interpretation; it is cosmic ordinance — the structural condition of a cosmos where neither masculine nor feminine, neither elder nor young, is sufficient alone, and the divine itself models this insufficiency-resolved-through-plenitude.

### What this is not

- **Not Trinity**: the Christian Trinity is three persons, one substance — but the persons are non-gendered and non-generational. Ngünechen's aspects carry biological and temporal dimensionality the Trinity does not. The Trinity transcends gender; Ngünechen's unity CONTAINS it.
- **Not androgyne**: the androgyne (Hindu Ardhanarishvara, Platonic hermaphrodite) fuses masculine and feminine into one — merged, not complementary. The two poles become one; Ngünechen's four aspects remain distinct.
- **Not divine couple** (Hurrian Teshub + Hepat): a divine couple is two separate deities in relation. Ngünechen is ONE being with four internal aspects, not two beings in partnership.
- **Not pantheon** (Yoruba orisha, Greek gods): a pantheon is multiple independent deities. Ngünechen's four aspects are constitutive of one reality, not separate gods.
- **Not binary complementarity** (yin-yang): binary complementarity is two, not four, and has no generational axis. Yin-yang maps ONE axis; Ngünechen maps TWO.
- **Not directional quaternary** (Navajo four mountains, Kongo dikenga four moments): these are spatial or temporal quaternaries without the gender×generation intersection. The four directions are not gendered; the four solar moments are not generational. Ngünechen alone develops the intersection.

### The Mapuche context

The Mapuche — "people of the land" (mapu = land, che = people) — are among the few indigenous peoples of the Americas who successfully resisted full Spanish conquest. The Arauco War (1536–1818) lasted nearly three centuries. They negotiated treaties with the Spanish Crown as a sovereign nation.

Their theology is not separate from this history. Mapuzugun, the language; admapu, the ancestral law; ngillatun, the great prayer-ceremony; and Ngünechen, the supreme being — these are not merely religious structures. They are the architecture of a people's survival.

Mapuzugun was suppressed under Chilean state policy through most of the 20th century. Children were beaten for speaking it in schools. The deliberate recovery of Mapuzugun — through community schools, through Elisa Loncon Antileo's work in the 2021 Chilean Constitutional Convention, through everyday use — is simultaneously linguistic, political, and theological resistance.

The theological structure: to speak Mapuzugun is to enact admapu — the ancestral order. Language and law and theology are not separate domains.

## Etymology

*Ngünechen*: from Mapuzugun *ngünen* (to govern, to direct, to have authority over) + *che* (people). Literally: the one who governs the people. Alternative form: Ngünemapun — governor of the earth (*mapu* = land/earth).

The four aspect-names:
- *Füchá* = old/elder; *Wentru* = man/male
- *Küdé* / *Füchá* = old/elder; *Domo* = woman/female
- *Üllcha* = young/junior; *Wentru* = man/male
- *Üllcha* = young/junior; *Domo* = woman/female

*me* from Sumerian 𒈨 — divine ordinances, received-gift-quality.

## Not to be confused with

- **barzakhqing** (Arabic, Core 8.25): the isthmus between dual-poles — holds two distinct poles connected without collapse. Barzakhqing is BETWEEN two; ngünecheme is WITHIN one (four aspects of one being, not two poles held in relation).
- **wahdatwujudme** (Arabic, Core 8.30): unity-of-being — all is God. Wahdatwujudme is monism; ngünecheme is internal-differentiation-within-unity — the one IS four, not the many ARE one.
- **syzygyqing** (Greek, Core): paired-distinct-conjoined. Syzygy is the PAIR structure; ngünecheme is the QUADRUPLE structure on two axes. Syzygyqing names the dyad; ngünecheme names the four-aspect unity.
- **tengrime** (Turko-Mongol, Core 8.25): sky-as-divine-without-anthropomorphism. Tengrime resists anthropomorphization entirely; ngünecheme embraces it — gender and age ARE the divine structure.
- **teotlme** (Nahuatl, Core 8.30): divine-as-ceaseless-process. Teotlme is the divine as process; ngünecheme is the divine as structure — the four-aspect architecture, not the flow.
- **ubuntume** (Bantu, Core 8.35): humanity-through-relation. Ubuntume is relational-ontology at the human level; ngünecheme is internal-differentiation at the divine level.

## Retirement conditions

This word would be superseded if a more learnable donor were found for the quaternary-complementary-divine concept (the ü and the four-syllable length are the main accessibility barriers), or if the concept were absorbed into a broader divine-structure word that includes quaternary-complementary as one of its sub-types. A potential companion could name the BINARY complementarity (yin-yang, Ardhanarishvara) as distinct from the QUADRUPLE complementarity — but that would be a companion, not a replacement. The Mapuche four-aspect structure is unique in developing the gender×generation intersection; no other tradition provides a more accessible donor for this specific concept.

Ngünechen is unique in developing the gender×generation intersection — all others develop spatial, temporal, directional, elemental, or contemplative axes. Ngünecheme names the specific intersection no other tradition elaborates.
