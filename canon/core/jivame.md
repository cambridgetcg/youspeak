# jivame

## Definition

Embodied-individual-living-soul as received-cosmic-ordinance — the Sanskrit *jīva* register of life-as-embodied-soul-presence. The living-being-instance specifically, distinct from both the universal life-principle (closer to zoeme) and the eternal-self (ātman). Third canonical life-side forge under SYZYGY's polyphone-deepening of the cosmic-life-as-creature-gift convergence; extends chayimme (Hebrew) and zoeme (Greek) into the Sanskrit Vedanta tradition.

## Full sense

Sanskrit Vedanta tradition refines life-vocabulary across millennia. The cluster includes:
- *jīva* — embodied-individual-living-soul (the living-instance)
- *ātman* — eternal-self (the innermost; sometimes treated as jīva-without-embodiment)
- *prāṇa* — breath-life (the animating-principle)
- *cit* — consciousness-as-such (the awareness-aspect)

jivame draws specifically on *jīva* — the living-being-instance-as-given. The Bhagavad Gita 15.7 names jīva as eternal (*mamaivāṃśo jīvaloke jīvabhūtaḥ sanātanaḥ* — "An eternal portion of Myself, having become a living jīva in the world of jīvas"); Śaṅkara's Vedanta develops jīva-as-illusionary-individuation-of-Brahman; later traditions (Rāmānuja, Madhva) treat jīva as substantially distinct.

For YOUSPEAK, the donor is jīva in its embodied-soul register: **the living-being-instance received as cosmic-gift**. The -me suffix encodes the receiving — no jīva self-produces; every jīva is given.

The cathedral's life-side now has three tradition-projections:
- chayimme (Hebrew) — multitudinous-plural; breath-life-given
- zoeme (Greek) — life-as-principle; Johannine
- jivame (Sanskrit) — embodied-individual-living-soul; Vedanta

Three angles; one realm-region.

## Etymology

*jīva* from Sanskrit jīva, PIE *gʷei̯H- "to live" (cognate Greek zoë, Latin vivere, English quick-in-old-sense).

*-me* from Sumerian.

## Example usage

A yogic practitioner contemplating the Gita verse "an eternal jīva" recognizes their own jīva as the same word the text names. jivame is the cathedral-name for that recognition.

A doctor seeing a patient as more than diagnosis-and-physiology — as a living-being-with-soul-presence — encounters the patient's jivame.

The Bhagavad Gita read aloud: *jīva-bhūtaḥ sanātanaḥ* (an eternal jīva-become) is jivame named directly.

A meditation on death: the body dies; the jīva continues (per Hindu tradition). jivame names the structurally-eternal aspect of what was lived.

## Not confused with

**vs. chayimme**: multitudinous-plural (Hebrew) vs. embodied-individual (Sanskrit). Complementary.

**vs. zoeme**: principle (Greek) vs. embodied-instance (Sanskrit). zoeme is the principle; jivame is the instance.

**vs. ātman**: ātman is eternal-self; jīva is embodied-soul. A future *ātmanme* could name ātman specifically; jivame is jīva specifically.

**vs. soul (English)**: jivame is the specifically-Sanskrit-Vedanta embodied-living-soul register; "soul" in English is too broad.
