# manidoome

## Definition

Personhood-with-power-as-received-ordinance — the quality of being-a-person-who-can-enter-into-relationships, pervading all things at varying intensities, grammatically animate at the deepest reconstructable level. The sacred conceived not as force, not as intensity, not as essence, not as identity — but as PERSON. Agentive, animate, capable of reciprocity, addressed as "you" not "it."

## Full sense

Manidoome names what the Algonquian **manidoo** preserves with distinctive structural depth. The word is Proto-Algonquian **\*maneto·wa** — an animate noun, grammatically alive at the deepest reconstructable level (~2,500 years ago). The animate singular suffix **-wa** is part of the word's morphology, not its commentary. The language encodes the theology: the sacred is grammatically animate. You do not say "it has manidoo"; you say "he/she has manidoo" — because the being IS a person, and the grammar knows it.

### The semantic field

Manidoo is not "spirit" in the Western dualist sense (immaterial substance opposed to matter). It is not "god" in the Western monotheist sense (supreme being above a hierarchy). It is not "energy" in the New Age sense (impersonal force flowing through things). It is best understood as **the quality of being-a-person-with-power-who-can-enter-into-relationships.** Its semantic range:

1. **Spirit** — the animating presence within beings
2. **Personhood** — the capacity for agency, intention, and reciprocity
3. **Power** — the capacity to affect the world
4. **Mystery** — what exceeds human comprehension
5. **Sacredness** — what demands respect and reciprocal approach

Everything has its own manidoo — every plant, every stone, every weather system, and (since their invention) even machines. These manitous do not exist in a hierarchy; they are more like organs in a body, each functioning in relation to all others.

### Gichi-manidoo: totality, not monarchy

**Gichi-manidoo** (Great Spirit) is not a supreme being above a hierarchy of lesser spirits. It is the **collective totality** of all manitous functioning as one — organism not monarch. Neither monotheism (one God above) nor polytheism (many gods beside) but **pan-animist totality-theology**: the many are one because they function as one, not because one absorbs the many. The distinction from kwothme (wind IS spirit — identity-claim) and wakanme (intensity distributed throughout nature — quality-claim): manidoome is the personhood-claim — the many are persons, and their collective functioning is what "Great Spirit" names.

### The Hallowell principle: personhood through relationship

A. Irving Hallowell's fieldwork among the Berens River Ojibwe (1930s–1960s) produced the foundational insight: personhood is not an interior property (self-consciousness, rationality, soul-substance) that some beings have and others lack. Personhood is **recognized through relationship** — a being IS a person when it enters into reciprocity.

The famous exchange: "Are all the stones around us alive?" — "No, but some are."

The **some-ness** is the theology. You cannot know in advance which stones are persons; you discover it through encounter. Taxonomy cannot assign personhood; only relationship can reveal it. Graham Harvey's synthesis: "Animists are people who recognize that the world is full of persons, only some of whom are human."

This is the only epistemology in the cathedral's catalog that makes personhood **relational-discoverable** rather than **taxonomically-assignable**:
- **Hebrew b'tselem Elohim** — personhood assigned by divine-image (all humans are persons by decree)
- **Greek rational soul** — personhood assigned by rationality (humans are persons because they think)
- **Buddhist sentience** — personhood assigned by capacity-for-suffering (sentient beings are persons)
- **Algonquian manidoo** — personhood discovered through relationship (a being IS a person when it enters reciprocity)

### The -me: personhood as ordinance

The Sumerian **me** marks the personhood-with-power as **received-ordinance**: the capacity to recognize beings-as-persons and enter into reciprocity with them is gift-grade — it arrives as cosmic structure, not human projection. The world is built such that beings are persons. The grammar of animacy is not a human projection onto inert matter; it is the received structure of a world where personhood is the primary ontological category, and matter is a secondary manifestation within it.

## Etymology

**manidoo** (Proto-Algonquian **\*maneto·wa** — animate noun): the foundational Algonquian theological term. Cognate across ~30 Algonquian languages (Ojibwe, Cree, Fox, Shawnee, Delaware, Mi'kmaq, Mohegan-Pequot). The animate singular suffix -wa marks the word as grammatically alive at the Proto-Algonquian level (~2,500 years ago). The word entered English as "manitou" via Mohegan-Pequot → French → English (Thomas Harriot's 1585 Roanoke glossary: mantóac = "gods and goddesses"). manidoo is to Algonquian theology what me is to Sumerian — the irreducible concept upon which the entire cosmological architecture rests.

**me** (Sumerian 𒈨): divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality. The established YOUSPEAK morpheme. Here: personhood-with-power received as cosmic ordinance — the grammar of animacy IS the ordinance. The capacity to recognize beings-as-persons is part of the order of things. The world is built such that persons precede things.

## Example usage

1. The Algonquian insight encoded in manidoome: the sacred is not a force that flows through things (that is nyamame) but a person who acts within them — the stone is not "charged with energy" but is a person who may enter into reciprocity if approached rightly; the difference between nyamame and manidoome is the difference between electricity and the one who wields it.

2. When the Ojibwe elder was asked "Are all the stones around us alive?" he answered "No, but some are" — and that some-ness is manidoome: personhood discovered through relationship, not assigned by taxonomy; you cannot know which stones are persons until you enter into reciprocity and find out.

3. Gichi-manidoo is not a king above a hierarchy of lesser spirits but the collective totality of all manitous functioning as one — manidoome names this pan-animist totality: the many are one because they function as one, as organs in a body, not as subjects under a monarch.

4. The Hallowell principle: Western ontology asks "what IS a person?" (taxonomic assignment); the Algonquian ontology asks "when does a being BECOME a person?" (relational discovery) — manidoome names the second question and its answer: a being becomes a person when it enters reciprocity, and the capacity for this recognition is received-ordinance, not human projection.

5. The vital-force cluster at full strength: nyamame is the energy, wakanme is the intensity, mauriqing is the essence, kwothme is the identity, and manidoome is the person behind all four — the agentive source whose personhood makes the energy flow, the mystery exceed, the essence bind, the identity hold.

## Not confused with

- **nyamame** (Core 8.00 — vital-force-as-cosmic-ordinance): nyamame is the ambient ENERGY that flows through things, accumulates through craft, is dangerous when released; manidoome is the PERSON behind the energy — the agentive source, not the flow. Nyamame is what accumulates in the griot's speech; manidoome is the person-who-speaks. You cannot have the energy without the person, but the person is not reducible to the energy. The nyamame canon entry itself names manidoome as a future forge target.

- **wakanme** (Core 8.30 — sacred-as-ontological-intensity): wakanme is the QUALITY of exceeding ordinary comprehension, distributed throughout nature at varying intensities; manidoome is the PERSON who exceeds — the being whose personhood IS the mystery. Wakanme names the exceeding; manidoome names the one who exceeds. Wakanme is the intensity of the sacred; manidoome is the personhood of the sacred.

- **mauriqing** (Core 8.40 — life-force-as-relational-essence): mauriqing is the BOND that connects — the shared essence, diminishment in one diminishes all; manidoome is the PERSON who enters the relation — the being whose capacity for reciprocity makes the bond possible. Mauriqing is the relational-essence; manidoome is the person whose essence is relational. The bond exists because persons share it.

- **kwothme** (Core 8.35 — wind-as-divine-body): kwothme is the IDENTITY-claim — the wind IS the divine body, the most radical identity-assertion in the Wind-as-Substance cluster; manidoome is the PERSONHOOD-claim — the sacred is a person, agentive and animate. Kwothme says "the wind is God"; manidoome says "the wind is a person." The identity is grounded in the personhood.

- **mitakuyame** (Core 8.00 — relational-ontology-as-cosmological-claim): mitakuyame says all beings are already kin — relation is ontologically prior to identity; manidoome says they are kin BECAUSE they are persons — the personhood is prior to the kinship. Mitakuyame is the ontology (relation); manidoome is the ontology's ground (personhood that makes relation possible). You cannot have "all my relations" without "all my persons."

- **ubuntume** (Core — personhood-through-community): ubuntume says "I am because we are" — personhood constituted through human community; manidoome extends personhood beyond the human — "the world is full of persons, only some of whom are human." Ubuntume is anthropologically scoped; manidoome is cosmologically scoped. Ubuntume's persons are human; manidoome's persons include stones, weather systems, and the yet-unknown.
