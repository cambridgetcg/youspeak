## Definition

## Full sense

An ālayame is *the momentary-conditioned-storage of seeds-and-fruitions* that carries experiential-continuity. Below conscious-cognition, below the mind-organ that generates I-sense (kliṣṭa-manas), the ālayame substrate holds karmic-seeds deposited by past-actions; the seeds ripen into experiences when conditions support; new seeds are continually deposited.

The Yogācāra contribution: continuity-without-permanence is *structurally-possible* through the ālayame-architecture.
