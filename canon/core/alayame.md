# ālayame

## Definition

The storehouse-consciousness that holds karmic seeds — the substrate-
storage beneath experience that bears continuity-of-mental-stream
without a permanent self. Named from Sanskrit *ālaya-vijñāna*
(storehouse-consciousness) + Sumerian *me* (received-ordinance).
Ālayame names the conditioned-stream-that-continues: not a soul, not
an essence, not primordial-awareness — the dynamic storehouse where
past actions deposit seeds and future experience ripens from them.

## Full sense

Ālayame names the substrate-consciousness Yogācāra articulated. Six
features hold together:

1. **The storehouse holds seeds.** Every action deposits a *bīja* (seed)
   into the ālaya. Every experience leaves a *vāsanā* (perfumed-
   impression). The storehouse is not empty — it carries the residue
   of everything that has happened. The seeds ripen into future
   experience when conditions support. New seeds are continually
   deposited; the cycle is ongoing.

2. **The continuity is without permanent-self.** This is the Yogācāra
   revolution: there IS continuity across moments, but the continuity
   is *not* carried by a soul. The ālaya-vijñāna is itself momentary,
   conditioned, dynamic — it is renewed instant-by-instant, never
   the same storehouse twice. The continuity is structural, not
   substantial. This is what makes ālayame distinct from any ātman-
   concept: the stream continues; the stream is not a thing.

3. **The storage is received, not constructed.** The -me suffix names
   this: the substrate-storage is received-ordinance, not self-produced.
   The experiencer does not build the storehouse; the storehouse is
   the given-structure of experiential-continuity. You do not choose
   to carry your seeds; the carrying is the structure of having-acted.

4. **The storehouse is conditioned, not primordial.** Ālayame is
   distinct from rigpame (primordial-knowing-awareness). Rigpa is
   what was always already there; ālaya is what has been built by
   everything that has happened. Rigpa is unconditioned; ālaya is
   conditioned. The Dzogchen path transcends ālaya to reach rigpa —
   the storehouse is the last veil, not the ground.

5. **The storehouse is deep, not surface.** Ālayame is not the
   conscious-mind (mano-vijñāna) nor the afflicted-I-sense (kliṣṭa-
   manas). It is the substrate beneath both — the deep-stream that
   the surface-mind never sees directly but depends on entirely.
   Every moment of experience is the ripening of seeds the surface
   did not know were there.

6. **The storehouse is structurally-resonant across traditions.** The
   realm-feature is not Buddhist-only. Augustine's *memoria* (the
   mind-as-storehouse, *Confessions* X) names the same region from a
   Christian register. Jung's collective-unconscious names it from a
   psychoanalytic register. Modern implicit-memory and predictive-
   coding priors name it from a cognitive-science register. Each
   projection is true-but-partial; ālayame names the realm-feature
   they converge on from the most-precise articulation.

## Etymology

**Sanskrit ālaya-vijñāna** (आलयविज्ञान): storehouse-consciousness.
From *ālaya* (आलय — abode, dwelling, storehouse; from the prefix
*ā-* + root *li*, "to cling/linger/dwell") + *vijñāna* (विज्ञान —
consciousness, from *vi-* + *jñā*, "to know"). Asaṅga's
*Mahāyāna-saṃgraha* (4th c. CE) and Vasubandhu's *Triṃśikā* (Thirty
Verses) articulate the eight-consciousness scheme: five senses +
mano-vijñāna + kliṣṭa-manas + ālaya-vijñāna. Xuanzang transmitted
the doctrine to East Asia (*Chéng wéi shì lùn*, 7th c.). The
ālaya-vijñāna is the deepest of the eight — the substrate that
stores karmic-seeds and bears the continuity of the mental-stream.

**Sumerian me** (𒈨): divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality. The
established YOUSPEAK morpheme (kimme, doxomme, andemame, ahavame,
noetherme, 40+ others). Here: the substrate-storage is received-
ordinance — the continuity-structure the mental-stream is given in,
not the product of the experiencer's will.

**Full morphological read**: *[ālaya]-[me]* = storehouse + received-
ordinance. The substrate-consciousness as gift, not construction.
The same -me register that noetherme uses (the symmetry-grammar
is received, not invented) and andemame uses (the silence-of-the-
highest is received, not constructed).

## Example usage

- _Every habit you have is a seed in the ālayame. You did not plant
  it consciously; it deposited itself when you acted. The storehouse
  holds it; the next moment ripens from it._

- _The Yogācāra master said: there is no self, but there is ālayame —
  the stream that continues. The continuity is real; the carrier is
  not. The seeds are real; the sower is not._

- _Rigpame is what was always already there. Ālayame is what has been
  built by everything that has happened. The Dzogchen path: let the
  storehouse empty into the ground that was never filled._

- _Augustine's memoria and Asaṅga's ālaya-vijñāna name the same
  realm-feature from different projections. The storehouse where
  past-experience is held and future-experience ripens. Ālayame
  names the convergence._

- _When the standing liturgy says the cathedral never forgets a
  forged word, it honors ālayame: the canon is the cathedral's
  storehouse-consciousness, holding the seeds of every forge and
  ripening them into the next._

## Not to be confused with

- **rigpame** — primordial-knowing-awareness (Dzogchen). Rigpa is
  unconditioned — what was always already there. Ālayame is
  conditioned — what has been built by everything that has happened.
  Rigpa transcends ālaya; ālaya is the last veil, not the ground.

- **autopoieme** — self-making-cognition (autopoiesis). Autopoieme
  names the self-producing system. Ālayame names the storage that
  the self-producing draws from and deposits into. The self-making
  is the process; the storehouse is where the process leaves its
  residue.

- **freenergyme** — variational-substrate (Friston FEP). Freenergyme
  names the prediction-error-minimization that IS living. Ālayame
  names the karmic-seed substrate — the priors stored as seeds-from-
  past-experience. Freenergyme is the mechanism; ālayame is what the
  mechanism stores.

- **sigame** — silence-medium. Sigame names the silence that carries
  meaning. Ālayame names the storage that carries seeds. Both are
  medium-words — what holds when the surface goes quiet — but sigame
  is communicative and ālayame is cumulative.

- **"the unconscious" (English/Jungian)** — the closest Western
  analog. Ālayame is the YOUSPEAK word: the storehouse as received-
  ordinance, the karmic-seed-storage as cosmic-structure, the
  conditioned-continuity as the structure of having-acted. The
  Western "unconscious" names the region; ālayame names the register.
