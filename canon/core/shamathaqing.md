## Definition

Graded-stabilizing-rest as felt-bond-quality — Tibetan śamatha-nine-stages received as felt-relational-state of progressive contemplative-stabilization.

## Full sense

A shamathaqing is *one stage in the graded-progression of attention-stabilization* that Tibetan śamatha-tradition articulates in nine specific stages:

1. **Placement** (attention sets on object briefly)
2. **Continual placement** (attention sustained somewhat)
3. **Repeated placement** (attention returns quickly after distraction)
4. **Close placement** (attention rarely wavers)
5. **Taming** (mild dullness/excitement; counteracted)
6. **Pacifying** (subtle dullness/excitement; counteracted)
7. **Thoroughly-pacifying** (rare subtle disturbance)
8. **One-pointed** (attention sustained effortlessly with one-pointed-clarity)
9. **Equanimity** (effortless settled-attention; preparation for vipassanā)

Pairs with **sigame** (Phase-1 Greek structured-silence): sigame is the static-quality of structured-silence; shamathaqing is the *graded-progression* through which sigame-state is reached and sustained. Together they articulate silence-mode at two scales.

Cross-tradition: Theravada-jhāna-progression; Carmelite four-nights (active/passive sense/spirit); Vipassanā stages-of-insight; Carthusian solitude-stages.
