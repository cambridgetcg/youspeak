## Definition

The settled, non-self-generated entrusting-heart — trust given by the trusted one rather than generated by the truster; Shinran's shinjin received as cosmic-gift.

## Full sense

A shinjinme is *not* something the truster achieves. Shinran's whole doctrine turns on this: shinjin is "the mind of Amida Buddha given to and realized in a person" (Ueda Yoshifumi) — it arrives through Amida's directing-of-virtue (ekō 回向), which in Shinran flows ALWAYS from the Buddha toward beings, never the reverse. Three structural marks carry into the cathedral-word:

1. **Given, not worked-up** — any attempt to manufacture, verify, or strategize the trust (hakarai, calculation) is precisely what shinjinme is free of. "The true cause of attaining great nirvana is shinjin alone" (Kyōgyōshinshō, Chapter on Shinjin).
2. **Settled** — diamond-like (kongōshin): "not shaken, confused, defeated, or broken by people of other views." Once arisen, the heart does not need re-deciding. In cathedral usage "settled" means *at-rest, non-self-monitoring* — the trust no longer checks itself. The full diamond-indestructibility is doctrine-internal to Shinran; ordinary given-trust is defeasible, and the word does not require otherwise.
3. **First-order** — shinjinme is the trusting heart itself, not confidence *about* one's trusting (which is assurance, a different and self-examined thing).

The word completes the cathedral's trust-unfolding (ANAKALYPSE): **sraddhance** (experiment 117) names cultivated trust that grounds practice — the jiriki-pole, śraddhā as the first of the five *cultivated* powers; **shinjinme** names the given settled trust — the tariki-pole. English "faith" bundles both and hides the question; the pair forces it open: *did I generate this trust, or was it given?*

Pairs with cathedral's:

- **tarikiqing** (Other-Power as felt-relational-trust) — tarikiqing names the reliance-relation; shinjinme names the given heart the reliance receives. One Pure Land realm-region, two poles: the relation (qing) and the gift (me). This forge corrects tarikiqing's Hōnen-era phrasing ("shinjin is the appropriate-response") — in Shinran's mature articulation shinjin is not the practicer's response but Amida's gift.
- **autopistme** (self-grounding truth-grade) — autopistme is about truth that needs no argument; shinjinme is about trust that needs no working-up; structural cousins across the truth/trust registers.
- **danaqing / barakqing** (gift-bond registers) — shinjinme is the limit-case where the gift IS the receiving-capacity itself.

## Example usage

1. "She stopped trying to manufacture certainty on the cushion; what arrived in the giving-up was shinjinme — trust she could not claim as her own work." — contemplative application.
2. "A child's trust in a reliable parent is everyday shinjinme: installed by the parent's catching, not worked up by the child." — grounded-ordinary application.
3. "Lutheran faith-as-gift is shinjinme-class in Reformation register; Shinran and Luther witnessed the same realm-feature from opposite ends of Eurasia." — cross-tradition.
4. "The difference between psyching yourself into confidence and shinjinme is the difference between gripping and being held." — pedagogical contrast.
5. "Tarikiqing is the leaning; shinjinme is the heart the leaning was given." — meta-cathedral.

## Not to be confused with

- **Faith (English)** — ambiguous between belief-that and trust-in; both readings locate the act in the believer; shinjinme locates the origin on the far side of the relation
- **Trust / entrusting (English)** — agentive; "I trust" is something I do; shinjinme is something done in me
- **Assurance (Protestant-technical)** — second-order confidence about one's own salvation-state, characteristically self-examined; shinjinme is the first-order given heart
- **Basic trust / secure attachment (Erikson, attachment theory)** — multi-word specialized-psych terms for a developmental *capacity* laid down in infancy; near the everyday region (the reliable-parent case) but naming a disposition of the organism, not the relationally-given settled heart itself
- **sraddhance** (experiment 117) — cultivated trust grounding practice; the complementary jiriki-pole, deliberately distinct
- **tarikiqing** — the reliance-relation toward Other-Power as felt-bond; shinjinme is the given settled heart that relation receives
- **Passivity / quietism** — as with tarikiqing: the given trust issues in active life (nembutsu-as-gratitude); givenness is not inertness
