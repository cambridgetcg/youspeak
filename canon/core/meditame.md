# meditame

## Definition

The slow, bodily, repetitive incorporation of what was received in the
sacred encounter — the metabolization of the gift between the return
and the re-opening of thirst. Named from Latin *meditari* (to ruminate,
to chew over; same root as *mederi*, to heal) + Sumerian *me* (received-
ordinance). The between-phase of the liturgical arc.

## Full sense

Meditame names the specific metabolization that transforms the
received-gift into substance. Five features hold together:

1. **The metabolization is rumination, not extraction.** The cow has
   eaten (received) and now returns to the food, works it slowly, turns
   it into substance. The metaphor is bodily: digestion, not
   information-extraction. You do not get the nourishment by swallowing
   once; you get it by chewing again. The meditari root carries this
   precisely: the slow, repeated turning of what was received until it
   becomes tissue. The monk chewing the psalms daily for forty years
   until the psalms chew him. The Sufi turning dhikr until the
   remembrance IS the rememberer.

2. **The metabolization is healing, not self-improvement.** Meditari
   shares its root with mederi (to heal). The body heals through
   digesting food; the soul heals through digesting sacred content.
   Meditame is not a technique for becoming better; it is the slow
   cure that the gift works through repeated contact. The healing is
   not self-produced — the -me names the received-ordinance grade. The
   gift works its cure; meditame is the pace at which it works.

3. **The metabolization is repetitive, not singular.** The Pali
   anussati (recollection) is the structured return to sacred objects
   — not once but as practice. The Hebrew higgayon (murmuring
   meditation) is the half-voiced repetition that lets the text enter
   below the threshold of loud reading. The Sufi dhikr is the Name
   repeated until the remembrance IS the rememberer. The metabolization
   requires repetition because the gift is not absorbed in a single
   contact. The tissue changes slowly, through repeated exposure.

4. **The metabolization is the between-stage.** In lectio divina,
   meditatio is the second stage: lectio (reading) → meditatio
   (chewing) → oratio (prayer) → contemplatio (contemplation). The
   meditatio is the BETWEEN — between receiving the text and
   responding to it. You do not rush from reading to prayer; you chew
   first. The between is where the text becomes yours. In the
   liturgical arc, meditame is the between — between the return
   (missame) and the re-opening of thirst (shawqame). The gift must be
   chewed before the next thirst can ripen.

5. **The metabolization makes the arc a living circle.** Without
   meditame, the arc is a mechanical loop: thirst → encounter →
   return → thirst → encounter → return. The thirst repeats without
   deepening. With meditame, the arc is a living circle: the gift is
   metabolized, and the next thirst reaches from a deeper place. The
   second thirst is informed by what was tasted and digested. The
   circle does not repeat; it spirals. The metabolization is the phase
   that converts repetition into deepening.

## Etymology

**Latin meditari**: to think over, to rehearse, to ruminate. From the
same root as mederi (to heal). The metaphor is the cow chewing cud:
the animal has eaten and now returns to the food, works it slowly,
turns it into substance. In Western monastic practice (Cassian,
Benedict, Guigo II), meditatio is the second stage of lectio divina:
lectio (reading) → meditatio (chewing) → oratio (prayer) →
contemplatio (contemplation). The meditatio is the between-stage:
between receiving the text and responding to it. The Psalms as
emotional curriculum: not learned once but rehearsed weekly through
voice, year after year, until the emotional register IS the
worshipper's register. 2000+ years of continuous Western monastic
practice.

**Sumerian me** (𒈨): divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality. The
established YOUSPEAK morpheme (40+ canon entries). Here reading as:
the metabolization is received-ordinance, not self-produced. The gift
works itself into you; you do not work yourself into the gift. The
same -me register that the entire liturgical arc uses: shawqame
(thirst is received), melamance (radiance is received), myesime
(crossing is received), andemame (silence is received), missame
(sending is received), and now meditame (metabolization is received).

**Full morphological read**: *[meditari]-[me]* = rumination + received-
ordinance. The slow chewing of the gift as received-ordinance, not
self-technique. The between-phase where the gift becomes substance.
The same -me register that the entire liturgical arc uses: meditame
is the arc's digestion.

## Example usage

- _The monk has returned from the vigil (missame). Now the meditame
  begins: the slow chewing of what was received, the gift working
  itself into the tissue of daily life. Not the encounter, not the
  return — the digestion. The between-phase where the psalm becomes
  the monk._

- _The Sufi says: you do not produce dhikr. The Name produces itself
  in you. Meditame: the remembrance-as-received-ordinance. The
  repetition is not your technique; it is the gift's own pace of
  becoming-yours._

- _The lectio divina places meditatio between reading and prayer.
  Why? Because the text must be chewed before it can be answered.
  Meditame: the between-stage where the received becomes substance.
  Rush from reading to prayer and you pray words you have not
  digested._

- _The cathedral says the standing liturgy never completes. Why
  does the thirst deepen rather than repeat? Because meditame: the
  gift is metabolized between each thirst and the next. The second
  thirst reaches from a deeper place — not because the first
  encounter failed, but because the first gift was chewed._

- _The cow does not swallow once. The cow returns to what it ate and
  works it slowly. Meditame: the rumination of the sacred. The body
  heals through digesting food; the soul heals through digesting
  what it received._

- _The Igbo mbari house is built as worship, then left to slowly
  return to earth. The gift is not preserved; it is released into
  slow organic dissolution. Meditame: the decay that IS the
  incorporation. Ala dissolves the work on her own time, and the
  dissolution is the gift becoming earth._

## Not to be confused with

- **nepsisme** — neptic-stillness-as-ordinance (the guard-the-mind
  practice). Nepsisme is the guarding; meditame is the chewing.
  Nepsisme protects the gate; meditame digests what passes through
  it. Both are received-ordinance, both are slow, but the direction
  differs: nepsisme watches, meditame chews.

- **liturgiame** — liturgy-as-received-ordinance (the work-of-the-
  people as cosmic-ordinance). Liturgiame names the whole liturgical
  structure as received; meditame names the specific between-phase
  where the received-gift is metabolized. Liturgiame is the
  container; meditame is the digestion that happens inside it.

- **mathemame** — the abiding state of having-received-what-the-
  discipline-yields. Mathemame is the accumulated result of long
  practice (the room one has long inhabited); meditame is the ongoing
  process of metabolizing each specific gift (the chewing that
  produces the accumulated state over time). Mathemame is the
  state-of-being-permeated; meditame is the act-of-being-permeated.
  Many meditames accumulate into mathemame.

- **noemame** — the entry-event into a realm-region of meaning.
  Noemame is the door; meditame is the chewing that follows entering.
  The event of meaning-arrival (noemame) must be metabolized (meditame)
  before it becomes permanent inhabitation (mathemame).

- **shamathaqing** — graded-stabilizing-rest as felt-bond-quality.
  Shamathaqing is the progressive stabilization through nine stages;
  meditame is the metabolization of what was received. Shamathaqing
  stabilizes; meditame digests. Both are slow; both are received-ordinance
  grade; but shamathaqing quiets the mind while meditame incorporates
  the gift.

- **missame** — the return-to-ordinary carrying what was received.
  Missame is the return; meditame is what follows the return. Missame
  brings the gift back; meditame chews it. The deeper dyad: missame
  → meditame → shawqame. The return, the digestion, the informed
  re-thirst.

- **shawqame** — the anticipatory thirst for the divine encounter.
  Shawqame is the thirst; meditame is what prepares the informed
  thirst. Without meditame, the re-thirst is mechanical (the same
  thirst repeating). With meditame, the re-thirst is informed (the
  thirst reaching from a deeper place, toward what was tasted and
  metabolized). The dyad: meditame (digestion) → shawqame (informed
  thirst).

- **"meditation" (English)** — has drifted toward "relaxation" or
  "mindfulness" or "blankness." Meditame restores what English
  meditation has lost: the bodily chewing (rumination), the healing
  connection (mederi → meditari), and the gift-grade (received-
  ordinance, not self-improvement technique). English meditation is
  a practice you do; meditame is a gift that works itself into you.

- **"contemplation" (English)** — the fourth stage of lectio divina
  (contemplatio), the resting-in-the-gaze. Meditame is the SECOND
  stage (meditatio) — the chewing that precedes the resting. You
  chew before you rest; you digest before you contemplate.

- **"reflection" (English)** — too cognitive, too fast. Meditame
  is bodily (the chewing), repetitive (the cud), and slow (the
  healing pace). Reflection is a glance back; meditame is a long
  chewing.

## Retirement conditions

1. A candidate scores higher than 8.05 against the same gap.

2. The gap-specification is revised — e.g., if further liturgy work
   determines that the "between-phase" temporal frame is not the
   precise axis, or if the "received-as-metabolization" dimension
   is re-described as a separate gap.

3. Morphology fails under usage — e.g., if "meditame" proves too
   close to English "meditation" and readers consistently collapse
   the specific between-phase meaning into the broader English concept,
   and a differently-rooted compound proves more precise for the same
   gap.

Until any of these obtains, **meditame stands**.
