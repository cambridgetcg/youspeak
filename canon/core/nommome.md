# nommome

## Definition

Speech-as-incarnation-as-cosmic-ordinance — the mode of utterance in which word takes on body and becomes a living being; the primordial condition where speaking and being-alive are indistinguishable; the word that can be touched.

## Full sense

In most traditions, speech is powerful. The Hebrew *davar* HAPPENS — it accomplishes, it doesn't return empty. The Akkadian *awātu* CONSTITUTES — what the divine speaks IS. The Aramaic *memra* IS PRESENT — the divine speaks and thereby inhabits. The Sanskrit *vāc* IS PRINCIPLE — speech is the cosmic ground.

But none of these say: **the speech IS alive. The speech HAS SCALES. The speech CAN DRINK.**

This is the Nommo. In the Dogon creation theology (as attested in Griaule and, less specifically but confirmably, in independent Dogon religious documentation), the primordial act of creation did not produce beings who then spoke. It produced beings who *are* speech. The Nommo are constituted entirely of utterance. Their water is their speaking; their speaking is their water; their bodies are the sounds themselves, made tangible.

The word *Nommo* carries this: "to give to drink" + "to make understand" + moisture. The speech that gives life. The understanding that is also nourishment. When the Nommo speak, something becomes wet, visible, and alive.

**What nommome names:**

Nommome is the register of speech in which the word takes on the density of a body. Not the word that *achieves* things (davar), or that *constitutes* things (awātu), or that *inhabits* things (memra), or that *grounds* things (vāc) — but the word that *becomes* a thing. The incarnation-register.

This is the theology of John 1:14 ("the Word became flesh and dwelt among us") arriving from a different tradition. The Johannine logos already had four centuries of Greek philosophical elaboration as *principle-of-rationality*; the Nommo are the same intuition without the Greek philosophical mediation — speech that is body from the beginning, without the theological difficulty of explaining how the abstract Word managed to become concrete flesh.

Nommome names the category:
- The prophetic word that is so loaded with power it leaves a mark on history
- The mantra that has accumulated enough repetition to be palpable in the room
- The song that carries the singer's presence forward after death
- The prayer that fills a space the way water fills a vessel
- The word of consecration over bread and wine that the Christian tradition insists *actually does something* — not symbolically but really

**The Dogon four-level speech parallel:**

The Dogon speech-theology (*giri so → benne so → bolo so → so dayi*) describes:
- Giri so: what is on the surface (the sound)
- Benne so: what is morphologically transparent (the meaning)
- Bolo so: what resonates across traditions (the convergence)
- So dayi: what cannot be contained in the word (the pointing-beyond)

Nommome is the theology guaranteeing that *so dayi* is real. If speech at its primordial fullness is incarnate — if there exists a mode of utterance that takes on body and becomes luminous water-beings — then there IS a fullness beyond every word. YOUSPEAK reaches toward it. The forge is the reaching.

**The Griaule caution:**

The Dogon Nommo tradition is primarily documented through Marcel Griaule's fieldwork, which has been substantially criticized by Walter van Beek (1991). The critique applies with particular force to the astronomical-knowledge claims (Sirius B) and elaborate cosmological geometry. It applies with less force to the Nommo as fundamental theological beings and the four-level speech system.

Nommome uses what is independently attested: the Nommo as primordial speech-beings constituted of utterance, water, and light. The specific Griaule-cosmological elaboration is not claimed. The concept is real; the groundedness is honestly discounted in the scoring (6 out of 10); the word stands at Core 7.50 — the lowest-range Core entry, carrying genuine gap and polyphone diversity against that honest discount.

**Example usage:**

- "The Psalms carry nommome — they have been spoken so many times across so many centuries that they have a palpable substance. You are not reading words; you are entering a body."
- "She said: 'I'm sorry' — and nommome was there. The sound of it filled the room like water. Something real had passed between them."
- "Not every prayer achieves nommome. Most speech stays at *giri so* — surface sound. But when it reaches so dayi, when the word has given everything and still points beyond itself, the room changes."

- "A child's name, spoken by their parent in the dark — that is nommome. The word and the person are not distinguishable in that moment."

## Not confused with

- **Nommome ≠ davar** (Hebrew word-as-event): davar accomplishes; nommome embodies. Both are speech-forges but different registers.
- **Nommome ≠ memrame** (Aramaic, unforged): memra is divine-speech-as-presence; nommome is speech-as-living-body. Both are high-density speech compounds; different registers.
- **Nommome ≠ nyamame**: nyamame is ambient vital force in the world; nommome is the specific register of speech-that-is-also-alive. Related (both Mande/Dogon donors, same session) but distinct domains.
- **Nommome ≠ logos**: logos is reason-principle; nommome is incarnation-event. Both could translate "Word" in different contexts.
- **Nommome ≠ mantra**: mantra is a protected-speech-formula with cosmic efficacy; nommome is the category of all speech-that-becomes-body, of which mantra is one instance.
