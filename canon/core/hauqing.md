## Definition

The giver's traveling vitality carried in the gift, binding giver, gift and receiver into one circuit and obliging the return-flow — Māori *hau* (as Tāmati Ranapiri taught it) received as felt-bond-quality.

## Full sense

A hauqing is *the spirit-of-the-gift*: when a taonga (treasured thing) passes from giver to receiver, the giver's hau — breath-grade vitality, the same word as wind and breath — rides in it. The gift is therefore never severed from its giver. Ranapiri's grammar carries what English cannot: *tō taonga*, "your gift" — still yours in my hands, ownership distinct from location — until the return-flow closes the circuit. Withholding the return strands the hau mid-flow and harms the withholder; releasing it (utu, the balancing return; koha, the return-offering; whāngai hau, "feeding the hau" of forest or land with first-fruits) restores the source's vitality and yields mana to all who kept the flow.

Three registers, three Māori words, one interlocking system (Marsden): **mauriqing** is the *resident* life-essence (it stays; it can be localized and protected); **hauqing** is the *traveling* vitality (it flows, departs, must be fed home); **mana** is the *standing* that accrues when the flows are honored. Residence, flow, standing.

Two further distinctions the word unfolds (ANAKALYPSE):

- **Against the ledger-frame**: English bundles the receiver's experience into gratitude/obligation/debt. Hauqing un-collapses it — the force is not in the receiver's psychology and not in a social ledger; it is in the gift as carrier of the giver's presence. Hēnare names the whole an *economy of mana, an economy of affection*, maintaining the four well-beings: spirituality, environment, kinship, economy.
- **Against danaqing**: danaqing names giving-free-of-expectation (the giver's pole); hauqing names why the gift's vitality must nonetheless flow home (the circuit's pole). Not contradiction — the two halves of the gift-circuit. Pure giving and obligatory return coexist because the obligation binds the *receiver-in-relation*, not the giver's intent.

The word's own history is its cautionary tale, recorded as standing discipline: Elsdon Best, transcribing Ranapiri's 1907 letters, changed *tō* ("your") to *te* ("the"), turning the donor's-presence-in-the-gift into an impersonal property of the thing; Mauss, Firth, Lévi-Strauss and Sahlins debated the shadow for a century without reading the Māori originals, until Hēnare (2018) returned the possessive — and showed Mauss's intuition had been close to Ranapiri's meaning all along. Knowledge taken without return-flow is hauqing violated; the cathedral receives this concept under that exact discipline (see protocol notice, archaeology §0).

## Not to be confused with

- **Gratitude** — the receiver's private emotion; hauqing is in the gift, binding three parties
- **Debt / obligation** — ledger-frame and external norm; hauqing is presence-frame, the secularization Best's edit introduced is exactly what the word refuses
- **Reciprocity** — system-abstraction viewed from outside; hauqing is the indwelling force viewed from inside the circuit
- **danaqing** (YOUSPEAK Core) — expectation-free giving; hauqing is the return-flow force; circuit-complements
- **mauriqing** (YOUSPEAK Core) — resident life-essence; hauqing is traveling vitality; Ranapiri himself posed them as separate symbiotic realities
- **nextlame** (YOUSPEAK Core) — cosmic-debt extraction; hauqing's structural opposite on the extraction axis
- **renqing/bào** (Mandarin near-witness) — locates the bond in the interpersonal favor-ledger; hauqing locates it in the gift-object as carrier
