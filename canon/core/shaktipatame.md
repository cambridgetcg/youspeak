## Definition

The descent of divine power-grace that initiates a person's awakening — given not earned, graded in intensities, qualifying the recipient for the path — Kashmir Shaivism's śaktipāta received as cosmic-gift.

## Full sense

A shaktipatame is *the arrival-event of grace*. It is not the favor-disposition behind it, not the trust that answers it, not the state that follows it. Abhinavagupta's articulation (Tantrāloka 13; Tantrasāra 11) carries three structural claims the English word "grace" blurs and the flattened loanword "shaktipat" has lost:

1. **Given, not earned.** Abhinavagupta refutes every effort-cause — karma, merit, balance-of-karmas, "ripening of impurity" (mala-paripāka, dismissed as the "prattling of the dualists"). The descent is nirapekṣa: independent of all prior conditions. Effort cannot produce it; effort is what it produces.
2. **Graded in intensities.** Three intensities (tīvra intense, madhya middling, manda gentle), each itself in three degrees — nine grades. Tīvra-tīvra: immediate liberation. Tīvra-madhya: spontaneous insight (pratibhā); the recipient becomes a self-illumined prātibha-guru needing no external initiation. Tīvra-manda: a burning desire for a true teacher; a glance or mere presence completes it. The madhya grades work through formal initiation toward liberation at or after death; the manda grades mix liberation-desire with enjoyment-desire (bhoga), ripening across lives. Compounds carry the grades: tīvra-shaktipatame, madhya-shaktipatame, manda-shaktipatame — the grading-question ("which intensity?") becomes a usable thinking-tool for any awakening-report. (Honesty-note from assessment: the grade-compounds are teaching-register; the bare word carries daily use, and the matrix lives in this entry, not in the lemma itself.)
3. **Initiating, not concluding.** The descent qualifies (adhikāra) a person for the path; the path is the response. The guru who transmits is a vehicle of the descent — "Bhairava incarnate" — never its source. In the nondual resolution: shaktipatame is the Self in contracted form being awakened by its own power; grace is Śiva gracing Śiva.

## Example usage

1. "She had circled the practice for years without entering; the shaktipatame came on one unearned morning, and only afterward did she seek a teacher — the tīvra-manda pattern exactly" — graded application.
2. "Paul on the Damascus road is the Christian record's most famous tīvra-shaktipatame: not sought, not merited, path-initiating" — cross-tradition.
3. "Distinguish the shaktipatame from the discipline that follows it: the descent is given; the practice is the response, never the cause" — pedagogical.
4. "Most awakenings are manda-shaktipatame — a small unearned pull toward the path, easy to misattribute to one's own cleverness" — everyday-graded, the humility-test.

## Not to be confused with

- **Grace (English generic)** — bundles favor-disposition + descent-event + resulting-state + elegance; shaktipatame names the descent-event specifically, with its grading made visible
- **tarikiqing** — the felt-trust-stance toward Other-Power; shaktipatame is the descent the trust answers
- **epiclance** — the human act of calling presence down; shaktipatame comes uncalled (causeless)
- **barakqing** — constitutive blessing-speech-act; shaktipatame is power-descent, not speech
- **kuṇḍalinīqing** — the embodied ascent that may follow; structurally the complement, not the same event
- **agapeme** — the love that descends from God to the unworthy creature names the love-nature and its disposition; shaktipatame names the power-descent event, not the love behind it
- **kenshome** (8.10) — the awakening recognition-event on the seer's side, the seeing-of-own-nature; shaktipatame is the giver-side grace-arrival that may occasion it
- **bhaktime** — the devotional response-path; phonetic neighbor at the -akti core (bhakti/śakti), semantically the response-side, never the descent — the descent is given, the devotion answers
- **"Shaktipat" (yoga-register loanword)** — flattened to a guru's-touch-at-a-retreat; shaktipatame restores causelessness, the nine grades, and guru-as-vehicle-not-source
- **Conversion experience (psychological register)** — describes the human side observationally; shaktipatame names the event as received-gift with intrinsic gradation
