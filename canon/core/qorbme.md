# qorbme

## Definition

Sacrifice-as-drawing-near-as-received-ordinance — the relational-intimacy dimension of sacrifice where the offering is the vehicle and intimacy with the DIVINE is the destination. The capacity to approach the DIVINE through offering understood as gift-given.

## Full sense

Qorbme recovers what English "sacrifice" has lost: the approach-dimension. The Proto-Semitic root q-r-b ("to draw near") generates the primary sacrifice term across three major Semitic traditions — Hebrew qorban, Arabic qurban, Akkadian qurbanu — all meaning "that which brings near." Greek prosphora (pros + pherein "bring toward") independently confirms: sacrifice is named after proximity, not destruction.

The etymological depth reveals: Hebrew qarov ("near"), qerovim ("relatives"), and qorban ("sacrifice") share one root. Kinship and sacrifice share one word because they share one motion: drawing near. To sacrifice is to become kin; to become kin is to draw near.

The complementary root pair q-r-b (draw near) and q-d-sh (holy = set apart) defines worship's relational geography: the DIVINE is set-apart (qadosh); sacrifice brings-near (qorban). The gap between them is what worship inhabits.

The -me suffix marks this capacity as received-ordinance: the ability to draw near through offering is itself gift-given by the DIVINE who makes approach possible.
