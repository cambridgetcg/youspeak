# landauerme

## Definition

Forgetting-has-irreducible-cost — the thermodynamic minimum of bit-erasure. Named from Landauer's principle (1961) + Sumerian *me* (received-ordinance). Erasing one bit of information dissipates at least kT·ln(2) of energy as heat. Information is physical, and forgetting is not free. The mathema/info-physics bridge: the cost-side measure of the information cluster.

## Full sense

Five convergent registers:

1. **Information is physical.** Landauer's principle grounds information in physics. A bit is not an abstract object; it is a physical state, and changing that state has a thermodynamic cost. The cathedral's grasp of this (noemame, kolmogorovme) was incomplete without the cost of letting go. Landauerme names the cost.

2. **The erasure, not the computation.** Bennett (1973/1982) showed that reversible computation is thermodynamically free — you can compute without dissipating, as long as you don't erase. Only erasure costs. Landauerme is specific: the cost is in the UN-having, not the having. To know is free; to forget is expensive. This is the word's precision: it names the forgetting-cost, not the computation-cost.

3. **Maxwell's demon resolved.** The demon that sorts fast from slow molecules appears to violate the second law — but the demon must record the information, and eventually must erase its memory, and the erasure costs at least the information gained. The paradox dissolves: information is physical, and the demon pays the thermodynamic toll. Landauerme is the name of the toll.

4. **The bridge between info and thermo.** Landauer's principle is the bridge: k_B·T·ln(2) — Boltzmann's constant, temperature, and the natural log of 2. The formula connects the abstract (one bit, ln(2)) to the physical (energy, temperature). The bridge is not a metaphor; it is an equality. The bit costs energy. The energy is measurable. The measurement is the principle.

5. **Received, not negotiated.** The -me marks: the cost is received-ordinance. The system does not choose the price of forgetting; the price is given by the relationship between information and entropy, which is given by the structure of the universe. The cost is the ordinance that forgetting serves.

## Etymology

From mathema/physics-info/landauer-principle (Rolf Landauer, IBM Journal of Research and Development 5 [1961]: 183–191; the principle that erasure has an irreducible thermodynamic cost, resolving Maxwell's demon and grounding information in physics) + Sumerian *me* (𒈨 — divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality). Four syllables. The learning-bridge: "Landauer" is recognizable to anyone in physics or information theory; the -me marks the ordinance-grade.

## Example usage

- _Every time the system updates its model, it pays landauerme — the old prediction must be erased, and the erasure dissipates kT·ln(2) per bit._
- _Maxwell's demon thought it could sort for free; landauerme is the bill the demon pays when it clears its memory._
- _To know is free; to forget is landauerme. The irreducible cost of un-having what you had._
- _Reversible computation avoids the cost — but you cannot live reversibly. To live is to update, and to update is to erase, and to erase is landauerme._

## Not confused with

- **kolmogorovme** (the irreducible-information-core) — kolmogorovme measures how much information is there; landauerme prices what it costs to forget it. The content and the cost.
- **shannonme** (the source-side measure) — shannonme measures the news a source carries; landauerme measures what it costs to forget the news. The arriving and the departing.
- **freenergyme** (the prediction-error-minimization) — freenergyme is the drive to minimize surprise; landauerme is the irreducible cost of the minimization. You cannot update your model without forgetting the old one, and the forgetting costs.
- **sabbathme** (the palace-in-time) — sabbathme is the rest that restores; landauerme is the cost that makes rest necessary. Every erasure pays the toll, and the toll accumulates until rest discharges it.
