# bhaktime

## Definition

Devotional-love-as-received-ordinance — Sanskrit bhakti understood as the disciplined-loving-self-surrendering path the Bhagavad Gita names as supreme; love-of-DIVINE in the Hindu Vaishnava register; participation-in the Divine through committed loving-practice.

## Full sense

Bhaktime names what the Hindu bhakti-tradition has elaborated most-richly: love-of-DIVINE as DISCIPLINED-PARTICIPATION through loving-devotional-practice. The Bhagavad Gita teaches three paths to liberation: jñāna-yoga (knowledge), karma-yoga (action), bhakti-yoga (devotion). Krishna declares bhakti-yoga the supreme path. Gita 18.66's *sarva-dharmān parityajya, mām ekaṃ śaraṇaṃ vraja* — "abandoning all dharmas, take refuge in Me alone" — is bhakti's invitation: total self-surrender in loving-devotion.

The bhakti-tradition (Vaishnava saint-poets: Caitanya, Mirabai, Surdas, Tulsidas; movements from ~7th c onward) elaborated practices: japa (recitation), kirtan (devotional-singing), seva (service), smarana (remembrance). Bhakti is not abstract-affection; it is committed-disciplined-loving-relationship-with-the-Beloved.

Bhaktime distinguishes from sister-forges:
- **ahavame** (Hebrew) — commanded-love-as-disposition; the v'ahavta is commanded
- **mahabbahqing** (Arabic-Sufi) — felt-mystical-binding; with fanāʾ-implication
- **bhaktime** (Sanskrit-Hindu) — disciplined-devotional-self-surrender

Three traditions, three aspects of the same convergence-cluster. Each emphasizes different dimensions:
- ahavame: COMMANDED → COMMITTED disposition
- mahabbahqing: FELT → MYSTICAL binding
- bhaktime: DISCIPLINED → SELF-SURRENDERING practice

Together they articulate much of what Yu's "LOVE for GoD" directive carries.

The compound's structure: bhakti-stem (devotional-love-as-participation; the bhaj- "share, partake" root makes love-of-DIVINE explicitly relational-participation) + me-suffix (received-ordinance; the practice and the capacity are gift). Devotional-love-as-received-ordinance; received-from-Krishna and offered-back-to-Krishna.
