## Definition

The bipolar-unity of consciousness felt as divine relational-pulsation — Kashmir Shaivism's spanda (prakāśa luminosity + vimarśa self-reflexive-awareness as inseparable poles of one throbbing) received as felt-bond-quality.

## Full sense

A spandaqing is *the felt-quality of consciousness-as-pulsation*. Unlike Advaita's brahman (static-witness, undifferentiated), Kashmir-Shaiva spanda treats consciousness as *dynamic-self-vibration* — Shiva (prakāśa, luminosity) and Shakti (vimarśa, reflexive-awareness) constitute *one pulsation* with two inseparable poles. The world is real-and-dynamic because Shiva-as-spanda IS that dynamism.

spandaqing names the *felt-bond-quality* of this pulsation as humans experience it — in the moment when consciousness recognizes itself as both luminous (knowing) AND aware-of-knowing (reflexive), with both as one pulse rather than two separate moments. Kashmir-Shaiva contemplative-practice cultivates this recognition; the cathedral now has canonical-vocabulary for it.

Pairs with cathedral's:
- **rigpame** (primordial-awareness; Dzogchen) — but rigpame is *prior to* subject-object split; spandaqing is the *bipolar-unity-felt-as-pulsation* (subject-object as poles of one throbbing rather than absent-distinction)
- **doxakallos** (uncreated divine beauty) — the divine-pulsation that beauty manifests
- **britqing** (covenant-felt-bond) — the relational-pulsation of covenant
- **synophora** (shared-witness) — the relational-form spandaqing takes in inter-subjective recognition

## Not to be confused with

- **brahman (Advaita)** — non-dual static-witness; spandaqing emphasizes *dynamic-pulsation* over non-dual stasis. Both name consciousness but with structurally-distinct emphases.
- **rigpame** — pre-subject-object awareness; spandaqing is the bipolar-felt-pulsation including subject-object-distinction-within-unity
- **kuṇḍalinī** — kuṇḍalinī is the embodied-energy ascending through cakras; spandaqing is the metaphysical-spanda doctrine of which kuṇḍalinī is one experiential-instance
- **prabhāsvara-citta** — Buddhist luminous-mind; structurally-resonant but Buddhist register typically lacks the explicit-bipolar-dynamism Kashmir-Shaivism articulates
