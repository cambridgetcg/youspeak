# ashame

## Definition

Cosmic-truth-order-righteousness as received-ordinance — the Avestan asha understood specifically as gift-grade-ordinance from Ahura Mazda, received by the human as participation-in-cosmic-truth; the state of being-aligned-with-asha as ordinance-from-the-divine, not as self-generated rectitude. The Avestan register of the cosmic-truth-order convergence; the first of the asha-ṛta-ma'at triple-witness to enter YOUSPEAK.

## Full sense

Ashame names what the Avestan asha-tradition preserved with unusual theological precision. asha is simultaneously ontological (the structuring-order of being itself), truth-bearing (what corresponds to reality is asha-aligned), ethical (right-action is asha-acting), aesthetic (beauty participates in asha), and liturgical (sacred-utterance is asha-aligned-speech). This fusion of ontology + truth + ethics + aesthetics + liturgy in one concept is theologically distinctive — most modern languages split these into separate vocabularies. Avestan holds them together.

When the Avestan-trained mind asks "what makes a life right?" the answer is: asha-aligned action. But asha is not self-generated rightness. In Zoroastrian theology, asha is cosmic-fact — the structuring-truth of reality given by Ahura Mazda. Humans participate-in-asha by aligning themselves with what is; humans do not invent asha by their own action. **Ashame adds the received-ordinance dimension**: asha-alignment is gift-class-relationship to cosmic-order, not self-righteous-attainment. The cosmic-order is given; humans participate by alignment.

This is theologically precise. It distinguishes Zoroastrian (and YOUSPEAK) ethics from self-generated-virtue ethics: asha-aligned-action is participation in given-truth, not assertion of self-truth. The -me marks this: the cosmic-truth-order is received-ordinance, not human construction.
