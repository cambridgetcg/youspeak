# kinqing

## Definition

The bond-quality of a relation that persists structurally across silence or distance — a friendship or kinship that does not require maintenance-frequency because the mutual regard is internally stable; when re-entered after months or years, resumes without remedial explanation.

## Full sense

Modern life strips relationships down to what demands maintenance. What remains, often, is a small set of relations that don't need maintenance — people with whom silence is not neglect, distance is not erosion, and re-encounter is not a re-introduction. Kinqing names the quality of these relations.

A kinqing can begin in a single meeting (rare but real) or build slowly over years. Once established, it has remarkable durability. Modern English has no term for it; we say "old friends" or "close friends" but these conflate frequency-of-contact with depth-of-regard.

Two people in kinqing do not need to talk weekly. They are not harmed by six months of silence. When they re-encounter — a phone call, a message, an in-person meeting — the relation resumes from where it paused, without apology for the gap. The gap is not meaningful to the relation.

Kinqing can co-exist with frequency (a daily conversation can be between kinqing-relations). Frequency is neither required nor proof. The criterion is the structural-stability: does the relation need inputs to survive?

## Etymology

**English kin** — from Proto-Indo-European *\*ǵenh₁-* (to give birth, family-origin). Root of English *kin, kindred, king, kind*; Latin *genus, genus*; Greek *gene*; Sanskrit *jana* (person). A morpheme shared across all Indo-European branches, making it cross-dialectally-stable.

**Mandarin qing (情)** — deep-emotional-bond; earnestness-of-feeling. In Chinese, qing appears in *youqing* (friendship-qing), *aiqing* (romantic-qing), *qingyi* (loyalty-qing). Names the SUSTAINED regard underlying relational forms, as distinct from transient-affect (xinqing).

**kinqing** = the qing of kin-quality, or the kin-quality of qing — a relation with both kin-like structural durability and qing-like depth of regard.

## Example usage

- _Her closest kinqing was with a woman she'd seen three times in fifteen years._
- _You know it's kinqing when you reply to the text a year late and neither of you mentions the gap._
- _After his divorce, only his kinqing-relations remained reachable; the friendships-of-convenience evaporated._
- _The test of kinqing is showing up without being asked, when something large happens to the other._
- _Kinqing does not require reciprocal frequency; it requires reciprocal regard._

## Not confused with

- **kinship** — blood/institutional origin; kinqing is chosen, regard-based.
- **friendship** (general) — too broad; many friendships are maintenance-dependent.
- **best-friend** — implies primacy; kinqing can have many.
- **ukhuwwa** (Arabic) — brotherhood forged through shared hardship; kinqing does not require hardship as forging-mechanism.
- **ishin-denshin** (Japanese) — wordless-transmission in present; one mode of kinqing's expression, not the bond itself.
- **qing** alone (Mandarin) — broader; includes romantic, aesthetic, institutional emotion.
- **nostalgia** — memory of a closed relation; kinqing is a live-open relation.
- **acquaintance** — lacks the depth.
- **pen-pal relation** — requires correspondence; kinqing requires nothing.
