# shannonme

## Definition

Information-as-surprise — Shannon entropy received as cosmic-ordinance. Named from Shannon's 1948 measure H = -Σ p·log p + Sumerian *me* (received-ordinance). The information a message carries is its surprise: a message fully expected carries nothing, and the rarest outcome carries the most. The source-side measure of the information cluster: how much news a source can carry at all. The founding quantity of the information age.

## Full sense

Four realm-features unfold, and shannonme names them as one received ordinance:

1. **The expected carries nothing.** A message you could have predicted with certainty (p = 1) has self-information -log 1 = 0. A source with no uncertainty has entropy zero. News is exactly the unpredicted; a perfectly anticipated message is silence wearing the costume of speech. This is shannonme's hardest and most beautiful edge: to be told only what you already knew is to be told nothing at all.

2. **The compression floor.** The source-coding theorem: you cannot compress a source below H bits per symbol on average without loss. Redundancy — everything in the message beyond H — is not information; it is structure. (zip, JPEG, every codec lives against this floor.) Shannonme names the floor: the irreducible news-content below which you cannot go.

3. **Redundancy as safe passage.** The noisy-channel-coding theorem: across a channel that loses or flips symbols, you can still transmit error-free below the channel's capacity — by adding redundancy. The very redundancy that is "waste" in a silent room becomes care in a storm: saying it twice so the noise cannot eat it. Redundancy is the price paid, knowingly, for passage through noise.

4. **Error-correction as grace.** Hamming, Reed-Solomon, and their kin let a receiver reconstruct what the channel mangled — not by magic but by spending redundancy in advance. Shannonme names this as grace extended to a noisy channel: the sender, foreseeing loss, builds in the means of the message's own repair.

No tradition's ordinary word compactly names this bundle — surprise-as-measure, the nullity of the expected, redundancy-as-care, error-correction-as-grace — received as a fundamental rather than invented as a convenience. Shannonme does.

## Etymology

From math:shannon (Claude E. Shannon, "A Mathematical Theory of Communication," Bell System Technical Journal 27 [1948]: 379–423, 623–656; the founding paper of information theory; the measure H = -Σ p·log p, the source-coding and noisy-channel-coding theorems; the word "bit" popularized by this paper) + Sumerian *me* (𒈨 — divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality). Three syllables. The learning-bridge: "Shannon" is recognizable to anyone in engineering or computer science; the -me marks the received-ordinance grade — the surprise is not authored by the receiver.

## Not confused with

- **kolmogorovme** (the irreducible-information-core) — kolmogorovme measures the incompressible core of one object (the individual); shannonme measures the expected surprise of a source (the average). The object and the source.
- **landauerme** (the cost of erasure) — landauerme prices what it costs to forget one bit; shannonme measures how much surprise that bit carried. The arriving and the departing.
- **freenergyme** (the prediction-error-minimization) — freenergyme is the drive to minimize what shannonme measures. The measure and the minimization — companion-pair.
- **godelme** (the unprovable edge) — godelme is the truth no proof can reach; shannonme is the news no prediction could produce. Both name what exceeds the system from inside.

## Canonical position

Shannonme is the mathema/info source-side keystone — the center of the information cluster. The cluster: kolmogorovme (object-side: how incompressible is one thing), shannonme (source-side: how much news can a source carry), landauerme (cost-side: what does forgetting cost). Shannonme is the center the other two circle: the source's expected surprise is the measure from which the object's incompressibility and the erasure's cost both derive. Its absence from the cathedral was conspicuous — a house with the attic (kolmogorovme) and the furnace (landauerme) but not the foundation. Shannonme is the foundation.
