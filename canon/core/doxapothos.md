# doxapothos

## Definition

The standing-longing toward doxakallos in its non-beholding — the absence-pole counterpart to doxalgia. It is forward-leaning (toward a beholding to come), beauty-directed (toward the doxa-anchor), and not extinguished but deepened by each beholding.

## Full sense

Most of a worshipper's life is lived between beholdings. The interval is not merely empty; it has its own shape: a leaning toward the beauty not now beheld. Doxapothos names that shape. It is not grief, not impatience, not nostalgia. It is the longing that is itself a mode of prayer.

The absence in doxapothos is the absence of the beholding, never of the object. The beloved is present; the beholding is elsewhere. So the word holds even where the object is not spatially lost — the long marriage between recognitions, the dark night in which the lean survives though all felt sweetness has gone, the re-read psalm whose words are already known.

Doxapothos is dispositional and flaring: a standing orientation that catches fire at the spine-glance, the hung harp, the pilgrim's laced shoes. It is memory-fed but forward-directed — the trace (athaumasma) is fuel, not direction. It tolerates maker-mode: the forge itself is one way the longing walks. It tolerates anonymous direction through a transparent created orthophanes — the mathematician's Book, the eclipse-chaser's "when's the next one" — but it breaks when the object becomes terminal: a longing stilled by its beholding was shawq toward a finite thing, not doxapothos.

Two independent traditions name the exact fulfillment-deepened structure: Sufi *ishtiyāq* (longing increased through meeting because the Beloved is inexhaustible) and Gauḍīya *vipralambha / prema-vaicittya* (longing ranked above union, with a named sub-mode for longing-within-union). That convergence is what raises the word from refine to canon.

## Etymology

**δόξα (doxa)** + **πόθος (pothos)**.

*Doxa*: Greek, "glory, manifested-weight" — via LXX of Hebrew *kavod*. The cluster's anchor-morpheme, shared with doxakallos and doxalgia.

*Pothos*: Greek, "longing-for-the-absent." Plato's *Cratylus* 420a defines the pair: the same desire is called *himeros* when its object is present and *pothos* when its object is absent or elsewhere. The LXX Psalm 41:2 (ἐπιποθεῖ) binds the pothos-root to GoD-directed longing. The simplex already carries the absence-direction.

Classical Greek compounding would prefer *doxo-* (the doxology pattern) before a consonant. The cluster keeps *doxa-* for pair-recognizability: a learner who knows doxakallos and doxalgia reads doxapothos at sight, and the induced rule /doxa/ → dox-/doxa- generates the form for free.

## Example usage

- _Between visits to the chapel she lived in doxapothos — not grief, not impatience; a longing that was itself a mode of prayer._
- _The Standing Liturgy runs on doxapothos: the search for the word never completes, and the not-completing is the worship._
- _He expected the beholding to still the longing. Instead the doxalgia passed and the doxapothos returned deeper._
- _Doxapothos is not nostalgia: it does not look back at a beauty lost, but forward, toward a beholding that has not yet arrived._
- _In the dark night the doxapothos was anesthetized, not dead — the lean survived the loss of all felt warmth._

## Not to be confused with

- **doxalgia** — the presence-sibling. Doxalgia arrives at the beholding; doxapothos lives between beholdings.
- **saudade** — backward-facing absence-ache toward the lost; doxapothos leans forward.
- **nostalgia** — homecoming-ache toward what-was.
- **Sehnsucht** — object-unspecified inconsolable longing; doxapothos is anchored to doxakallos.
- **shawq / ishtiyāq** — the closest cross-linguistic cognates; directed toward the Beloved-as-person. Doxapothos inherits the ishtiyāq-structure (not stilled by meeting) and adds beauty-specificity and cluster-pairing.
- **epektasis / epektasisme** — the perpetual stretching trajectory; doxapothos is the felt engine of that trajectory.
- **akēdia / acedia** — the privation of the lean; the contrary of doxapothos, not its synonym.
- **pothos** (English houseplant) — the houseplant *Epipremnum aureum* is a genuine register collision, recorded honestly.

## Retirement conditions

1. A higher-scoring candidate against the same gap.
2. A re-description showing that the absence-pole is already fully covered by doxalgia's contrary, or that the cluster no longer needs the himeros/pothos symmetry.
3. Evidence that *pothos* has drifted in English so far toward the houseplant or casual "yearning" that the cluster-recognizability is lost.

Until any of these obtains, **doxapothos stands**.

## Canonical position

Core Canon word. The absence-pole of the Liturgy wheel. With doxalgia (presence-ache), doxakallos (beauty-in-GoD), orthophanes (made-visible), anagnoristasis (threshold), metastrophesis (turning-kept), and athaumasma (trace), it completes the minimum doxa-cluster: the longing that holds the wheel turning.
