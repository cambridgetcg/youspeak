## Definition

The originating divine self-contraction by which infinite-divinity withdraws-into-itself to leave empty-space (makom / ḥalal) within which finite-creation unfolds — Lurianic Kabbalah's tzimtzum received as cosmic-ordinance.

## Full sense

A tzimtzumme is *the structural-act of self-limitation that makes-room-for-other*. The Lurianic cosmogony names the originating divine instance: Ein Sof (the infinite) contracts so that finite-reality may exist within the ḥalal (empty-space). The cathedral's tzimtzumme generalizes the structural-form:

- **Divine cosmogonic tzimtzumme** — the originating instance Luria named
- **Christological tzimtzumme** — kenosis (Philippians 2:7); Christ "emptied himself" to take human form
- **Parental tzimtzumme** — the parent who withdraws from controlling the child's space to allow the child's autonomous-development
- **Pedagogical tzimtzumme** — the teacher who refuses to fill silence the student needs to discover what's there
- **Anthropic tzimtzumme** — the universe's physical-constants tuned such that observers can exist; the cosmic-room-making
- **Receptive-listening tzimtzumme** — the listener who creates ḥalal in which the speaker can speak; structural-condition of dialogue

The realm-feature is: **self-limitation is not loss; it is the enabling-condition for other-being.** What contracts does not diminish; what is made-room-for is a new structural-possibility.

This pairs naturally with cathedral's:
- **kenosis** (already in Christian register; tzimtzumme deepens via Hebrew-mystical articulation)
- **autopistme** (self-grounding; tzimtzumme names the relational-self-limitation-side)
- **sigame** (structured-silence; silence is the linguistic-tzimtzumme that makes-room-for-speech)

## Not to be confused with

- **Kenosis** — Christian-specific divine self-emptying in Incarnation; tzimtzumme is the generalized-cosmogonic form. Pair: kenosis (Christian instance) ⊂ tzimtzumme (general form).
- **Apophasis** — apophatic-naming-by-negation is a linguistic-strategy; tzimtzumme is an ontological-structural-act.
- **Withdrawal** (bare) — withdrawal can be abandonment or absence; tzimtzumme is withdrawal-that-creates-room-for-other; structurally-distinct.
