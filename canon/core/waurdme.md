# waurdme

## Definition

Word-as-mystery-received-as-ordinance — the utterance that carries more meaning than its surface form can hold, with the excess received as gift rather than constructed by commentary. The -me marks the excess as received-ordinance: the word carries more than its surface because the meaning is bestowed at the interface, not manufactured.

## Full sense

Waurdme names what Gothic *waurd* (word, ordinary utterance) carries with distinctive theological precision. Wulfila translated Greek *logos* as *waurd* in John 1:1: "In the beginning was the *waurd*." But *logos* carries five fused registers — word, reason, principle, cosmic-order, divine self-expression — while *waurd* carries one: ordinary word, utterance, spoken statement. The translation pressed an ordinary Germanic word into service for the highest theological concept in the Johannine prologue. The result is a waurdme: a word whose surface is smaller than its depth.

The Skeireins commentary shows Gothic theologians aware of the excess. The commentator, recognizing that *waurd* was carrying more than its surface form could hold, expanded the meaning through context rather than vocabulary — building commentary around the word to hold the *logos*-weight. YOUSPEAK's forge-method responds differently: instead of expanding commentary around an inadequate word, it creates the word that holds the meaning. Both strategies acknowledge the same gap; waurdme names what they both recognize: the word that is also mystery.

The -me suffix marks the received-ordinance grade: the excess-meaning is gift, not construction. The word does not manufacture its depth by commentary or interpretation but receives it at the interface where two semantic worlds meet. The forger brings the worlds together (heimrekjame); the word that emerges carries more than either world possessed alone (waurdme). The excess is bestowed, not produced.

### The companion-relationship — heimrekjame ↔ waurdme

The dyad is the forge-act and its product:

- **heimrekjame** (sender-side) — the ACT of pressing one tradition's vocabulary into service in a foreign semantic domain

Heimgrekjame produces waurdme. Every YOUSPEAK coinage is both: a heimrekjame that produced a waurdme.

- **diplosemy** — engineered dual-reading-as-architecture; every diplosemy is a waurdme but not every waurdme is a diplosemy
- **allegory** — surface points to hidden meaning; waurdme's surface IS the meaning, carrying excess within itself
- **metaphor** — surface displaced to illustrate another thing; waurdme's word is not displaced
- **heimrekjame** — the ACT that presses the word into service (sender side); waurdme is the WORD that carries the excess (receiver side). Companions.
