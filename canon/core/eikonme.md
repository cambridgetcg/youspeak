# eikonme

## Definition

The image as received-ordinance participatory-projection: visual morphame specialized to the image-medium; the visual form that participates in its prototype rather than merely representing it.

## Full sense

Eikonme names the visual-image projection-form across all traditions: the Orthodox icon, the Catholic Madonna, the Buddhist Buddha-rūpa, the Hindu murti, the Tibetan thangka, the Yoruba sacred sculpture, the Japanese mandala-painting, the Egyptian sacred-relief, the Maya royal-portrait-glyph, the Islamic calligraphic rendering of divine Names. Each is an eikonme in its specific tradition.

What distinguishes eikonme from its neighbours is the combination of five features:

1. **Visual medium**: the projection is into the painterly/sculptural/visual aperture. Not music, not gesture, not language — the image-form specifically.

2. **Participatory (not merely representational)**: the image *participates* in its prototype. This is the patristic Orthodox tradition's distinctive theological contribution. Basil the Great's formula: "The honor paid to the eikōn passes through to the prototype." The image is theologically active because it shares-in what it depicts.

3. **Canonical form**: iconography requires fidelity to received tradition. The Christ Pantocrator, the Theotokos Hodegetria, the specific Buddhas, the deity-iconography of Hindu and Tibetan traditions — each follows received canon. The eikonme is not personally-invented but tradition-given.

4. **Theologically active**: honor paid to the image is licit (Nicaea II, 787) and effective. The image does work; veneration is neither idolatry (worshipping the matter) nor empty (a mere prompt for thought).

5. **Cosmic scope**: from Genesis 1:26-27 (humanity as eikōn theou) through Colossians 1:15 (Christ as eikōn of the invisible God) to icons of saints, the eikōn-doctrine spans theological anthropology, Christology, and ecclesiology.

Eight-plus tradition convergence (in `convergences/multi-modal-projection-of-meaning.md`): Orthodox Christian iconography (the deepest), Catholic religious imagery (Marian, Sacred Heart, saints), Buddhist Buddha-rūpa (especially Vajrayāna thangka), Hindu murti (Vaiṣṇava, Śaiva, Śakta traditions), Tibetan thangka, Yoruba sacred sculpture, Japanese mandala-painting, Egyptian sacred-relief, Maya glyph-image-integration, Islamic calligraphic rendering.

Under POLYMORPHIA.md, eikonme is the visual-medium specialization of morphame, sister to liturgiame (multi-modal-synthesis), sigame (apophatic-silence), and glossame (linguistic). The cathedral's own glyph-discipline (script/philosophy.md) is a sub-domain of eikonme: YOUSPEAK glyphs are small-scale linguistic-iconic eikonme. The six glyph-axioms operate as the cathedral's iconographic-discipline.

## Etymology

**eikōn** (εἰκών):

Greek noun for "image, likeness." From εἴκω (eikō, to be like, to resemble). Source of English *icon*, *iconography*, *iconoclasm*, *iconic*.

The semantic depth:

- **Genesis 1:26-27 LXX**: *kat' eikona theou* (according to the image of God); humanity-as-eikōn-theou is the foundational anthropological doctrine
- **Colossians 1:15**: Christ as *eikōn tou theou tou aoratou* — the image of the invisible God; Hebrews 1:3 echoes with *charakter* (engraver's mark)
- **Patristic icon-theology**:
  - Basil the Great's formula on honor-passing-through-to-prototype
  - John of Damascus's three treatises on divine images
  - Theodore the Studite's *Antirrhetici* (systematic icon-defense)
  - The Seventh Ecumenical Council (Nicaea II, 787): canonical resolution
- **Modern Orthodox renaissance**: Ouspensky, Lossky, Evdokimov, Meyendorff, Schmemann

The patristic East's distinctive contribution: **the image participates in its prototype**. Not philosophical-aesthetic resemblance; theological doctrine that veneration of the image is licit because the depicted prototype's reality reaches through the image to the beholder.

**me** (𒈨):

Sumerian *me*: divine-ordinance, cosmic-gift. Eighteenth -me family member.

**Full morphological read**: *[eikon-][-me]* = the image-stem + received-ordinance-suffix. Brief and clean.

## Example usage

The Russian Orthodox parish gathers before the iconostasis. The Christ Pantocrator gazes from the central panel; the Theotokos Hodegetria stands to the left; the saints fill the upper register. Each is an eikonme: visual-form participating in its prototype, canonical-received, theologically-active. The parishioners venerate the icons by bow and by kissing; the honor passes through to the depicted ones. The icons are not decoration; they are co-celebrants in the Divine Liturgy.

The Hindu family completes their evening pūjā. The murti of Lakshmi has been bathed, dressed, garlanded, and presented with offerings. The mother holds the lamp before the face; the family takes darśan — the sacred-seeing through which Lakshmi's presence is received. The murti is an eikonme in the Hindu register: visual-form participating in the deity's reality; *prāṇa-pratiṣṭhā* has installed the deity's life-breath in the image; the murti is consecrated and active.

The Tibetan thangka-painter completes the Kalachakra mandala after months of work. Every detail follows the prescribed iconography: the colors, the directions, the deities' specific attributes, the geometric proportions. The thangka is then consecrated through the *rab nas* ritual — the deity is "installed" in the image. This thangka is an eikonme in the Vajrayāna register: a participatory visual-form that enables practice (the practitioner visualizes the deity through the thangka).

The Yoruba bronze-worker completes a sculpture of Ogun, the orisha of iron. The sculpture's specific features — the wrought-iron implements, the muscular form, the facial scarification — follow received iconography. After consecration, the sculpture participates in Ogun's reality; it is honored by offerings of iron objects and specific food. This is the Yoruba eikonme.

A YOUSPEAK glyph is a small linguistic-iconic eikonme. The glyph for *-me* (the downward triangle in upward arc) is not arbitrary decoration: its visual logic projects the morpheme's content (cosmic-ordinance descending and being received). The reader who learns the glyph-system reads the visual form participating in the morpheme's meaning. The cathedral's six glyph-axioms govern eikonme at the linguistic-iconic scale.

In every case: the image is canonical, participatory, theologically active, and theologically-grounded. Eikonme names what the image IS.

## Not to be confused with

**morphame**: Morphame is any form-projection in any medium. Eikonme is the visual-image specialization. Specialization-relation.

**liturgiame**: Liturgiame is the multi-modal-synthesis pole. Eikonme is one component within the synthesis (the icons of an Orthodox Liturgy are eikonme; the whole Liturgy is liturgiame).

**sigame**: Sigame is the apophatic-silence pole. Eikonme is positive-visual-form. Different poles; both real.

**glossame**: Glossame is the linguistic specialization (tongue-as-theophanic-trace). Eikonme is the visual specialization. Sister-specializations of morphame.

**The cathedral's YOUSPEAK glyphs**: YOUSPEAK glyphs are small-scale linguistic-iconic eikonme — the cathedral's own sub-domain of eikonme. The six glyph-axioms (script/philosophy.md) are the cathedral's iconographic-discipline.

**"Image," "picture," "representation," "icon" (modern English usage), "art," "sacred art"**: each English candidate names a partial feature. Image is too generic. Picture is casual. Representation contrasts with the participation-doctrine. Icon in modern English has been diluted (computer-icon, celebrity-icon). Art is too broad. Sacred art is phrasal and lacks the worship-register precision. Eikonme compact-names the visual × participatory × canonical × theologically-active × cosmic-scope combination.
