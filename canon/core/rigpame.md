## Definition

The primordial knowing-awareness that is self-knowing prior to any subject-object distinction — Dzogchen rigpa received as cosmic-ordinance; the awareness-shape always-already-present at the substrate of any cognitive-event.

## Full sense

A rigpame is *the awareness-substrate by which any cognition becomes possible*. The Dzogchen four-fold characterization: empty (without inherent substance), luminous (with clear-knowing quality, *gsal-ba*), spontaneously-present (*lhun-grub*, not produced by effort), unobstructed (*ma-'gags-pa*, the basis from which phenomena arise). These four are not properties added to awareness; they are *what awareness IS* before being-aware-of-anything-specific.

rigpame is structurally-distinct from cognition-of-objects (ordinary mind, *sems*) and from substrate-storage (ālaya-vijñāna / *kun-gzhi*). It is *ye-shes* — primordial gnosis, the wisdom-knowing-itself.

The Dzogchen-claim received: rigpame is *the natural-state* (*gnas-lugs*) of mind. Meditation does not produce rigpame; meditation *recognizes* (ngo-sprod) what is always-already-present. The cathedral's contemplative-vocabulary now has a precise-name for the realm-feature multiple traditions independently name (sākṣī in Advaita, prabhāsvara-citta in Yogācāra, nous in Hesychasm, the One's first-radiation in Plotinus, Lichtung in Heidegger, minimal-self in modern cognitive-science).

## Not to be confused with

- **Consciousness (bare)** — bare-consciousness can be cognitive-of-objects; rigpame is prior to subject-object
- **Mindfulness** — mindfulness is attention-discipline; rigpame is the awareness-substrate mindfulness attends-with
- **noemame** — noemame is the *event* of meaning-arrival; rigpame is the *awareness-substrate* by which noemame-events become possible
