## Definition

Mediating-lawfulness-as-felt-bond-category — Peirce's *thirdness* received as felt-bond-category.

## Full sense

A thirdnessqing is *the mediating-lawfulness-as-felt-bond-category*. Peirce's universal-categories triad:
- **Firstness** (firstnessqing) — quality-felt-immediacy (the redness-of-red felt-as-such, before comparison)
- **Secondness** (secondnessqing) — brute-resistance-encounter (the door that resists; the shock of collision; the brute "that-it-is")
- **Thirdness** (thirdnessqing, this entry) — mediating-lawfulness (habit, sign-as-representation, generality, the law that explains the encounter)

thirdnessqing names the realm-feature of *bringing-a-first-and-a-second-into-relation*. The English word's compositional-transparency (third + ness + qing) makes it relatively-learnable. The -qing suffix carries the felt-bond register: the lawfulness is FELT as intelligibility, as habit, as the meaning that relates sign to thing — not merely observed as a triadic structure.

The semiotic signature of thirdnessqing is the **symbol** — the sign that works by law and convention (a word meaning what it means by convention). The symbol is the thirdness-sign: it means not by resembling (icon/firstness) or by brute existential connection (index/secondness) but by law and habit. Where the index points (secondnessqing) and the icon resembles (firstnessqing), the symbol means (thirdnessqing).

Thirdness is also the category Peirce identified as the foundation of pragmatism: the meaning of a concept IS the general rule of action it licenses (thirdness — meaning is habit). Thirdnessqing is the word for the mode-of-being that makes the method that forged it possible.

Pairs with cathedral's:
- **firstnessqing** — the first pole; thirdnessqing is the law that makes firstnessqing's quality intelligible, the mediation that the quality does not itself contain.
- **secondnessqing** — the second pole; thirdnessqing is the law that governs the encounter, the habit that makes the world predictable. Secondnessqing is the brute encounter WITHOUT the mediation; thirdnessqing is the mediating-lawfulness the encounter is embedded in.
- **prehensionme** — Whitehead's feeling-into. Prehension is a thirdness-RICH act (it mediates); thirdnessqing names the MODE of mediation that prehension instantiates, not the act.
- **rtame / maatme / ashamed** — cosmic-law instances. Each is a specific cosmic-law tradition; thirdnessqing is the structural category they all instantiate.

## Example usage

1. "The law of solid bodies that explains why the door resists is thirdnessqing — the mediating-lawfulness the brute encounter is embedded in" — physical.
2. "A word meaning what it means by convention is the symbol, the thirdnessqing-sign — meaning by law and habit, not resemblance or brute connection" — semiotic.
3. "Pragmatism itself is thirdnessqing in action — meaning IS the conceived consequences, the habits of action a concept licenses" — philosophical.
4. "The judge's decision mediates between the general rule and the particular case — pure thirdnessqing" — ethical/judicial.

## Not to be confused with

- **firstnessqing** — quality-felt-as-such prior to relation. Firstness is what-it-is; thirdness is why-it-is-intelligible. The two are co-universal but structurally distinct (you cannot derive law from quality).
- **secondnessqing** — brute-resistance-encounter. Secondness is the fact; thirdness is the that-there-is-a-why. Every brute encounter is embedded in a lawfulness it does not itself contain.
- **prehensionme** — the ACT of grasping (a thirdness-rich act); thirdnessqing names the MODE of mediation the act instantiates.
- **synhorizme** — a thirdness EVENT (fusion of horizons); thirdnessqing names the category the event instantiates.
- **rtame / maatme / ashamed** — cosmic-law instances (Hindu, Egyptian, Sumerian); thirdnessqing is the structural category they all instantiate.
- **English law / habit / convention / meaning** — each captures one instance; none names the CATEGORY. "Law" too formal; "habit" too behavioral; "convention" too contingent; "meaning" too narrow.
