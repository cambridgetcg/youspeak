## Definition

The aesthetically-distanced felt-state evoked in a receptive-aware connoisseur (*sahṛdaya*) when skilled artistic-projection brings realm-content through a medium — Bharata-Abhinavagupta's rasa received as cosmic-ordinance.

## Full sense

A rasame is *not* a subjective-emotion. It is *not* the artist's emotion-being-conveyed. It IS *the aesthetically-transformed affective-state* — distilled from raw-emotion, distanced through artistic-mediation, savored as essence rather than experienced as biography. When a Hindustani musician's alap brings *karuṇa-rasa* into a listener, the listener does NOT experience the musician's grief and does NOT experience ordinary-grief — the listener tastes *karuṇa-as-such*, with reflective-awareness that the tasting is aesthetic-savoring rather than confused-with-lived-emotion. This is the *camatkāra* (wonder-savoring) Abhinavagupta articulated.

The nine rasa-bhāva pairs name *categories of realm-content* that artistic-projection makes graspable:

| Rasa | Aesthetic-state |
|---|---|
| śṛṅgāra-rasame | erotic-aesthetic love |
| hāsya-rasame | comic-aesthetic |
| karuṇa-rasame | compassionate-sorrow-aesthetic |
| raudra-rasame | wrathful-aesthetic |
| vīra-rasame | heroic-courageous-aesthetic |
| bhayānaka-rasame | fearful-aesthetic |
| bībhatsa-rasame | disgust-aesthetic |
| adbhuta-rasame | wondrous-aesthetic |
| śānta-rasame | peaceful-aesthetic (Abhinavagupta's addition) |

The general-term rasame names the category; specific compounds (śṛṅgāra-rasame etc.) name the specific aesthetic-states. Pairs with cathedral's:

- **morphame** (form-as-projection) — morphame names form across all media; rasame names felt-aesthetic-state across all media. Pair: structure (morphame) + felt-content (rasame).
- **liturgiame** (multi-modal-synthesis) — when liturgy works, multiple rasames are projected simultaneously across coordinated media
- **sigame** (structured-silence) — śānta-rasame relates closely to sigame's contemplative-silence-mode

This is Phase 1's **music-mode opening anchor.** Future Phase 4 expands: jinsme (Arab tetrachord-melodic-morpheme), ethosme (Greek mode-as-soul-tuning), sayrqing (melodic-course), tarabqing (skilled-performance-ecstasy).
