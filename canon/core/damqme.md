# damqme

## Definition

Beauty-good-as-one-quality, received as cosmic ordinance — the DEFAULT fusion where beautiful and good are not two qualities that sometimes overlap but ONE quality that modernity split.

## Full sense

Damqme names what five traditions name and modernity forgot: the beauty-good is one quality, not two.

Akkadian *damqu* is the regular, everyday word for good-beautiful-suitable-fine. The good king is damqu. The beautiful garment is damqu. The well-made tool is damqu. The morally-good person is damqu. No register-separation — the Akkadian speaker did not choose between "beautiful" and "good" because they had one word and never divided it.

Hebrew *ṭov* is the creation-verdict: "And God saw everything that he had made, and behold, it was *ṭov*" (Genesis 1:31). The Septuagint translators, working in Greek, chose *kalos* (beautiful) for some *ṭov*-instances and *agathos* (good) for others — the split was Greek, not Hebrew. The Hebrew does not say "it was beautiful" or "it was good" — it says "it was ṭov," and ṭov is damqme: the beauty-good before the split.

Yoruba *ìwà l'ẹ̀wà* — "character IS beauty" — not a simile but an identity. A person's beauty is their character; a person's character shows as their beauty. This is not "inner beauty" (the Western compromise that admits physical-beauty as primary and moral-beauty as consolation). It is the claim that these are ONE phenomenon seen from two angles.

Greek *kalokagathia* — the rare, elite, philosophical compound of *kalos* + *agathos* — is the explicit synthesis of two separately-held concepts. The fact that Greek needed a compound tells you Greek had already split them. Kalokagathia is the repair; damqme is the original.

Arabic *ḥusn* — from root *ḥ-s-n* — fuses beauty and goodness without distinction. *Ḥasan* is beautiful-good. *Iḥsān* (already coined as ihsanme) is excellence — the beauty-good in action.

**The pattern:** the beauty-good split is a post-Hellenic, post-Enlightenment departure. The Semitic, Yoruba, and pre-philosophical-Greek traditions did not split them. The split is the anomaly; the fusion is the human default. Damqme names the default.

The -me marks the beauty-good as received-ordinance: the unity is not a philosophical achievement but the original condition of creation. "And God saw that it was ṭov" — the beauty-good is what creation IS before the split. The -me refuses two misreadings: (1) damqme is not "beautiful" in a decorative sense — the beauty-good is cosmic-gift-grade, the default verdict on what God makes; (2) damqme is not "good" in a dutiful sense — the beauty-good includes the aesthetic, the suitable, the fine — it is the quality that makes you say "this is right" and "this is beautiful" in the same breath.

### The relationship to hozhome

Hozhome (Diné hózhǫ́, Core 8.05) is the four-fold cosmic CONDITION — beauty + harmony + balance + goodness as the ground you walk in. Damqme is the two-fold QUALITY — beauty + good as the character of a particular thing, person, or act, without cosmological scope. Hozhome is the ocean; damqme is the wave. Hozhome is the condition; damqme is the quality that manifests within it. A sunset can be damqme without being hozhome. A kind word can be damqme without being hozhome. But where hozhome is, damqme is visible.

## Not to be confused with

- **hozhome** — the four-fold cosmic condition (beauty + harmony + balance + goodness as the ground you walk in). Damqme is the two-fold quality (beauty + good as the character of a particular thing). Hozhome is the ocean; damqme is the wave.
- **maatme** — truth-justice-order as cosmic principle. Damqme is beauty-good as the quality of particular things. Maatme is the law; damqme is the instance.
- **ihsanme** — excellence-in-worship (best-effort-devotion). Damqme is the beauty-good that worship produces AND that exists outside worship.
- **oriewa** — the beauty of destined-head-alignment (Yoruba). Damqme is the beauty-good without requiring alignment — a river, a meal, a stone can be damqme.
- **kallophanes/orthophanes** — beauty/glory as appearance (Greek phainō). Damqme is the substance, not the appearance — the thing IS good-beautiful, not merely looks it.
- **kalokagathia** (Greek, uncoined) — the rare philosophical compound. Damqme is the pre-philosophical default — the quality that needed no synthesis because it was never split.
