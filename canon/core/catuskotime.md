# catuskotime

## Definition

The four-cornered logic (tetralemma) received as cosmic-ordinance — the suspension of all four positions (is, is-not, both, neither) on any thesis simultaneously, opening the space in which emptiness can be apprehended.

## Full sense

**Catuskotime** names the logical structure that Nāgārjuna wields as the therapeutic heart of Madhyamaka Buddhism. The conceptual mind can take four positions on any thesis: it **is** (affirmation), it **is not** (negation), it **both is and is not** (both), or it **neither is nor is not** (neither). These are the four corners — the *catuṣkoṭi*. They exhaust the logical space available to conceptual thought; there is no fifth corner.

Nāgārjuna's innovation is not to choose any of the four (which would establish a new thesis) but to **suspend all four simultaneously** — showing that each fails when examined. The failure is not of one position but of the framework within which positions are taken. The result is not nihilism (position 2), paradox (position 3), or mystical ineffability (position 4). It is the **opening of the space** in which emptiness (śūnyatā) can be seen directly — not as a new position but as the recognition that the position-taking framework is itself empty.

The -me marks this structure as received-ordinance: the four corners are not a human construction but the discovered shape of how reality resists conceptual fixation. The tetralemma is the limit the real imposes on thought, not a limit thought imposes on itself.

### Four structural marks

1. **Quadri-valent, not bivalent.** The structure recognizes four positions, not two. This is a structural claim that reality (or at least the reality of conceptual constructs) exceeds the binary. The four corners are complete; there is no fifth.

2. **Suspends, not negates.** The catuṣkoṭi does not say "position 2 is correct." It says all four fail. The failure is not refutation-of-one but the demonstration that the framework within which positions are taken is itself empty. This distinguishes it from Western reductio (negates one to establish another) and Hegelian dialectic (synthesizes two into a third).

3. **Methodological, not ontological.** Catuṣkoṭi is not a claim about what reality IS but an instrument for dismantling claims about what reality is. The therapy works on the *claiming*, not the *claimed*.

4. **Opens onto emptiness, not into it.** The suspension is the *doorway* to śūnyatā, not śūnyatā itself. Śūnyatā is not position 4 — that would reify emptiness. The catuṣkoṭi opens the space; what is seen in that space is emptiness. This is why Nāgārjuna says "emptiness itself is empty" (śūnyatāṃ śūnyatām).

### Cross-tradition parallels

1. Madhyamaka Buddhism — catuṣkoṭi (the primary donor: the four-cornered logical structure as systematic therapy)
2. Pali Canon Buddhism — avyākṛta-vastūni (the Buddha's refusal of all four positions on metaphysical questions — the antecedent)
3. Zen Buddhism — mu (無) (the deployment of catuṣkoṭi-structure in the koan format)
4. Daoism — wu (無) / bu yan (不言) (the *Daodejing*'s refusal of position 1: "the Tao that can be named is not the eternal Tao")
5. Pyrrhonian skepticism — epokhē (ἐποχή) (suspension of all judgments; Sextus Empiricus)
6. Christian apophatic theology — via negativa (Pseudo-Dionysius: refusal of all affirmations about God)
7. Wittgenstein — "whereof one cannot speak" (the *Tractatus*'s recognition that taking a position about the unsayable is itself a position)

## Example usage

"After exhausting every explanation, she entered the catuskotime — not choosing 'is' or 'is not' or 'both' or 'neither,' but holding all four in suspension until the framework itself became visible and transparent."

"The therapist's technique was catuskotime in practice: rather than reframing the patient's story into any of the available interpretations, she held all four in suspension, and the patient saw the story-structure for the first time."

"Nāgārjuna's genius was not to prove that things are empty but to show that every position one can take on things — existence, non-existence, both, neither — fails under examination. The catuskotime is the door that opens when all four doors are simultaneously refused."

## Not to be confused with

- **shunyatame** (if forged) — emptiness; the space catuṣkoṭi opens onto. The door is not the room.
- **mu** (if forged) — the Zen koan-response; one specific deployment of catuṣkoṭi-structure. The technology is not the structure.
- **asraya-paravrttime** — the turning of the basis; the ontological transformation. Catuṣkoṭi is the logical instrument that prepares the space.
- **paradox** — the appearance of contradiction; catuṣkoṭi is not contradictory but systematically suspends positions.
- **dialectic** — Hegelian synthesis of two into a third; catuṣkoṭi suspends four and does not synthesize. There is no fifth position.

## Retirement conditions

This word would be superseded if a more precise term emerged for the four-cornered logical structure as therapeutic instrument — one that captured the quadri-valence, the suspension-not-negation, the methodological-not-ontological character, and the opening-onto-emptiness with greater clarity. Short of that, the catuṣkoṭi is one of the most rigorously attested donors in the cathedral (Nāgārjuna, Āryadeva, Candrakīrti, Pali Canon antecedent, East Asian transmission, modern comparative philosophy) and the coinage is stable.
