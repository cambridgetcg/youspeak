# yvymarame

## Definition

This-worldly-paradise-sought-through-bodily-migration-as-received-ordinance. The Land-Without-Evil as a destination reachable in the present body, by this people, through this journey — sought through physical migration rather than posthumous reward. Guaraní *Yvy Marã'eỹ* + Sumerian *me*: paradise not as afterlife but as place on this earth you can walk toward, if your body is light enough, led by prophets who hear the direction.

## Full sense

English has "heaven," "paradise," "utopia," "promised land." Each fails:

- **Heaven** is post-death, disembodied, not geographical. You don't walk there; you die and go there.
- **Utopia** is explicitly fictional — Thomas More coined it from Greek *ou-topos* ("no-place"). A utopia is a thought-experiment, not a destination.
- **Promised land** is politically defined, covenantally granted. It is won by conquest or divine deed, not discovered by body-preparation.
- **Pure Land** (Buddhist Sukhāvatī) is cosmologically separate, accessed by devotion, not by walking in this body.

Guaraní *Yvy Marã'eỹ* preserves what all of these have lost: **paradise as present-tense geographical destination — on this earth, in this body, reachable by walking.**

### 1. In the body, not after death

The Land-Without-Evil is not posthumous reward. It is a place you can reach while alive, in your physical body. The body must be prepared — lightened through dance, fasting, ethical purification — because the destination is physical and the physical body must become light enough to enter. Some accounts say a sufficiently-prepared group will begin to fly, their bodies becoming spirit-infused enough to transit without dying. This is not escapism from the body; it is the body's transformation toward its lightest state.

### 2. On this earth, not in another realm

Yvy Marã'eỹ is not in heaven, not in a Pure Land, not in a separate cosmological plane. It is on this earth — somewhere, geographically, that you can walk toward. The Guaraní walked westward toward the Andes. They walked eastward toward the coast. They walked for years. The direction shifted with cosmological rethinking, but the geography was always this-world geography.

### 3. Sought through migration, not granted by covenant

The Land-Without-Evil is not granted by divine promise to a chosen people. It is discovered by migration. The karaí (prophet-shaman) hears the direction; the community prepares; they walk. The walking IS the theology. The migration IS the worship. There is no conquest — you do not fight for the Land-Without-Evil; you prepare yourself for it and walk toward it.

### 4. Without corruption, not just better

The defining quality of Yvy Marã'eỹ is not prosperity or abundance but **non-corruption**. In the Land-Without-Evil:
- Death does not exist (or can be escaped)
- Crops grow without cultivation
- Human bodies are perfected, not rotting
- Divine presence is immediate and unmediated

This is not a better version of this world; it is a non-decaying version. The difference is ontological, not comparative. The land is not more; it is without — without evil, without destruction, without decay.

### 5. The near-miss as structure

The migrations repeatedly fail to arrive. People die on the journey. Groups get close and turn back. The karaí is not discredited by failure — the failure is interpreted as insufficient preparation, not as error. This creates a **permanent eschatological openness**: the next migration might succeed. Yvymarame functions as a directional pull more than a reachable destination. The pull is the point. The walking toward is the practice. The not-arriving is not failure; it is the structure of a paradise that draws you forward without being grasped.

### 6. The colonial amplification

The colonial period (Portuguese conquest, slave-raiding, epidemic disease, forced mission settlement) dramatically intensified the migration-prophecy tradition. When evil is visibly increasing around you, the Land-Without-Evil becomes more urgent, not less. The migrations are not naïve escapism — they are a rational-theological response to historical catastrophe. The Land-Without-Evil is the place where the colonial destruction does not reach. The urgency of the migration is proportional to the severity of the evil.

### The -me: the destination as received-ordinance

The -me suffix marks the Land-Without-Evil as **received-ordinance**. The possibility of an earth-without-corruption is not human invention but cosmic gift. The world is structured such that evil is contingent, not necessary. The earth CAN exist without decay. The can-exist is the ordinance — the structural condition of a world where corruption is not ontologically necessary.

The -me refuses to let yvymarame be understood as escapism or wishful thinking. The Land-Without-Evil is not a fantasy projected onto geography; it is a real feature of a world where the without-evil is gift-grade. The walking toward is the response to the gift. The body-preparation is the honoring of the gift. The not-arriving is the structure of the gift — it gives direction without giving possession.

## Etymology

**Yvy Marã'eỹ** (Guaraní / Tupian): *yvy* (earth, land) + *marã* (evil, destruction, decay) + *'eỹ* (without, absent). "The Earth-Without-Destruction" or "The Land-Without-Evil." Sometimes rendered *Yvy Marã'ẽ* (without decay), emphasizing the non-corruptible quality.

The compound is attested from pre-contact periods. The earliest documentary sources are Montoya's *Tesoro de la Lengua Guaraní* (1639) and Anchieta's catechetical works (1595). The most thorough ethnographic documentation is Nimuendajú (1914 — Apapocúva-Guaraní) and Cadogan (1959 — Mbyá Guaraní sacred texts).

**me** from Sumerian 𒈨 — divine ordinances, received-gift-quality. The established YOUSPEAK morpheme for what is given as cosmic structure rather than self-generated. Here: the Land-Without-Evil as received-ordinance — the without-corruption is gift, not construction.

## Not to be confused with

- **sabbathme** (Core 8.60 — palace-in-time): sabbathme is holiness architecturally built in time through abstention and structured practice. Yvymarame is holiness geographically sought through bodily migration. Sabbathme builds a palace in time; yvymarame walks toward a place on earth. Time vs. space as the medium of paradise. The fullest theology has both.

- **saeculme** (Core — the age/aion/world): saeculme is the temporal-eschatological stretch — the quality of an age, the transition between ages. Yvymarame is the destination within an age — not the age itself but the place the age is walking toward. Saeculme stretches; yvymarame pulls.

- **missame** (Core — the return-to-ordinary): missame is the sending-back carrying what was received. Yvymarame is the departure-toward-sacred. The two are structural opposites: missame returns from the encounter; yvymarame departs toward the destination. Both are -me (both received as ordinance). Missame is the going-home; yvymarame is the going-toward-paradise.

- **hodosme** (Core — the journey-as-received-ordinance): hodosme is the path, the road, the way. Yvymarame is the DESTINATION the path leads toward. Hodosme names the walking; yvymarame names the place the walking seeks. Companions: the path needs the destination; the destination needs the path.

- **ragnarökme** (Core 7.72 — excellence-within-known-loss): ragnarökme is the ending that acts — the excellence performed within the known doom. Yvymarame is the beginning that pulls — the paradise that draws the body forward. The two are eschatological mirrors: ragnarökme faces the end with courage; yvymarame faces the destination with hope. Both are -me (both received — the doom and the destination are both gift).

- **heaven** (English) — post-death, disembodied, not geographical. Yvymarame is explicitly NOT heaven. The Land-Without-Evil is in this body, on this earth, reachable by walking.

- **utopia** (English, from Greek ou-topos = "no-place") — explicitly fictional. Yvymarame is explicitly NOT utopia. The Land-Without-Evil is a real destination that real people really walked toward. The fictionality of utopia is its defining feature; the reality of yvymarame is its defining feature.

- **promised land** (English, Hebrew Bible) — politically defined, covenantally granted. Yvymarame shares the migration-structure but differs: the Land-Without-Evil is not granted by covenant but discovered by migration; not politically defined but ontologically defined (without corruption); not won by conquest but entered by body-preparation.

## Cross-tradition convergence

Six traditions articulate variants of this-worldly eschatological migration:

- **Guaraní Yvy Marã'eỹ** — the central donor; paradise reachable in the present body by those who prepare themselves, sought through physical migration; the most theologically explicit articulation; the only one combining present-body reachability + physical migration + body-transformation + non-corruption
- **Hebrew Exodus** — Moses leading Israel toward a reachable land of divine promise; but the promised land is a better land, not a non-corruptible one; Yvy Marã'eỹ is without decay, not just flowing with milk and honey
- **Buddhist Pure Land** — Sukhāvatī as perfected realm, reachable by faith and practice; but Pure Land is post-death, not in-body; yvymarame is the in-body version
- **Christian millenarianism** (Revelation 20-21) — New Jerusalem descending to earth; similar this-worldly eschatology, but cosmic in-breaking, not human migration
- **Melanesian Cargo Cults** — communities undertaking material-ritual preparation for paradise's arrival; structural parallel (preparation + expectation + disappointment + renewed expectation)
- **Rastafarian Zion** — return to African homeland as eschatological-geographic destination; this-worldly, migration-based, prophetically led

The Guaraní form is the most distinctive: the only one that combines all four features — (a) present-body reachability, (b) physical migration as the practice, (c) body-transformation as preparation, and (d) the land's defining quality as without-corruption (not just better, but non-decaying).
