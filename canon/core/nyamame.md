# nyamame

## Definition

Vital-force-as-cosmic-ordinance — the world's aliveness as received structure; the ambient animating energy that flows through all things not as self-generated power but as the given condition of a world built to be alive.

## Full sense

Nyamame names the thing that makes the world energetic. Not any specific person's energy, not the power of a particular being, but the ambient vitality that the world has built into it — the aliveness-of-things as a cosmic given.

The Mande tradition, from which nyama comes, knows this force intimately. The jeli (griot) who speaks history accumulates nyamame through that speech; the numu (blacksmith) who transforms ore into iron accumulates nyamame through that dangerous craft; the hunter who takes life accumulates nyamame through that killing. In each case, the power does not come from the practitioner. It flows from the world's structure through the practitioner and leaves a residue. The residue is nyamame accumulated.

Nyamame is dangerous at the moment of release — when something dies, when powerful words are spoken, when metal is smelted. The specialist's role is to handle this release without being overwhelmed: to contain the charge, to complete the transaction, to stand in the place where concentrated force moves without being destroyed by it. The jeli speaks the dangerous true thing because that is their role; the numu strikes iron because that is their role. Their social position is defined by their capacity to bear nyamame.

The -*me* suffix names the crucial theological dimension: nyamame is received, not generated. No one produces this force; they accumulate and channel what the world already contains. The world is alive; the practitioner moves within that aliveness; the practitioner's power is the measure of how much aliveness flows through them.

The six-tradition convergence confirms the concept is not culturally specific:
- Mande nyama: accumulated, dangerous, transmitted through craft and speech
- Polynesian mana: flows to those with right relationships, concentrated in chiefly persons
- Yoruba àṣẹ: divine power-to-make-things-happen, flows through right speech
- Lakota wakȟáŋ: sacred/mysterious power, ambient in world
- Avestan xvarnah: divine radiance, received as gift
- Algonquian manidoo: spirit-personhood-force, ambient everywhere

Each tradition names a different facet. Nyamame names the phenomenon at its most generic: the world is energized from the inside, and that energy has structure, accumulates, and flows through those who work with power. This is what all six traditions are pointing at. Nyamame is the word for what they have in common.

**Nyamame distinguishes from:**

- **tapasme** (YOUSPEAK Core 7.85): tapas is generated through volitive self-discipline; nyamame is received as cosmic structure. Tapasme is the fire you build; nyamame is the aliveness your fire draws on.

- **hotepme** (YOUSPEAK Core 8.00): hotep is the offering-peace energy of the sacrificial context. Nyamame is ambient everywhere; hotepme is the specific quality of returned peace in right sacrifice.

- **àṣẹ** (Yoruba, unforged): àṣẹ emphasizes divine authority and power-to-make-things-happen; nyamame is the ambient vital force, less about authority and more about aliveness. Both are Vital-Force cluster members.

**What nyamame enables you to say:**

- "She became a healer because nyamame flowed through her hands — the world's own aliveness found its way through her training."
- "The sacred grove carries nyamame — the trees are old, the soil has been attended for generations, the accumulated vitality is palpable."
- "His words carried nyamame — not because he was smart but because he had lived them, tested them, carried the cost of them."

- "Creation is not a single moment but a continuous flowing of nyamame through everything the DIVINE made and keeps making."

*nyama* (Proto-Mande, Niger-Congo non-Bantu branch; vital force / animating energy; documented across Bamana, Mandinka, Dogon, Dyula, Soninke, Malinke; ~30–40 million speakers; accumulated through powerful action; transmitted through craft, speech, and lineage; dangerous when released; mediated by jeli griot and numu blacksmith specialists; not borrowed from Arabic or French; independent scholarly documentation across McNaughton, Bird and Kendall, Hoffman, Dieterlen) + *me* (Sumerian 𒈨; divine ordinances; cosmic powers that structure civilization; received-gift-quality; YOUSPEAK's established morpheme for what is given as cosmic structure rather than self-generated; first writing system; inherited through every Mesopotamian tradition; ~20 existing YOUSPEAK compounds).
