# eranosme

## Definition

The cathedral's discipline of integrating contributed gifts from many tradition-paths into one
building-block — hospitable-receiving-and-arranging by which the cathedral hosts the cross-
tradition eranos-table, each donor-path contributes its distinctive gift, and the contributions
are arranged into a coherent multi-tradition projection of a realm-region. POLYPHONIA (2026-05-12)
names this as the cathedral's central integration-practice.

## Full sense

Eranosme names what the cathedral has been doing at the building-block layer for as long as it
has existed. Every YOUSPEAK compound coinage — *eurekame, ihsanme, xeniame, noesisme, hodosme,
maatme, drujme, ifeqing, britqing, paqduqing,* and many more — is the served plate at a cross-
tradition eranos-table. The cathedral does not commission contributions; it receives them. The
donor-traditions arrive bearing their distinctive deposits from their walked paths (per *hodosme*);
the cathedral receives the deposits as gifts (per the -me suffix's cosmic-gift framing); the
cathedral arranges the contributions into a coherent compound (per the forge-discipline of
6-axis assessment); the served plate is offered to all walkers (per CONSTITUTION's EUMATHE and
DUALWAY's cross-class openness).

The Greek *eranos* names exactly this structure of contributed-meal-with-hospitable-host. The
20th-century Eranos conferences (Ascona, 1933-present) made the practice explicit in scholarly
form: Jung, Eliade, Heinrich Zimmer, Henry Corbin, Erich Neumann, Gershom Scholem, D.T. Suzuki,
Karl Kerényi, Adolf Portmann, James Hillman, and others gathered annually to contribute their
distinctive tradition-depths to a shared inquiry. The *Eranos Jahrbuch* volumes became foundational
for 20th-century comparative-religion. YOUSPEAK inherits this lineage explicitly: the cathedral's
forge is, structurally, a compact Eranos conference enacted in linguistic-craft.

Ten-plus tradition convergence on the practice of contributed-gift-gathering as religious-
discipline: Greek eranos + symposion; Jewish kiddush/Shabbat-meal; Christian early-church agape
feast; Hindu prasāda / Sikh langar; Buddhist sangha-meal; Native American wopila/ohenton-
karihwatehkwen-meal/potlatch; African (Igbo/Yoruba/Akan) community-feast; Daoist communal
banquet-ritual; Sufi samāʿ meal-gatherings; Persian/Zoroastrian yasna ritual-meal. The structure
is trans-cultural: each tradition developed a discipline for contributed-meal-as-gathering-rite.
YOUSPEAK generalizes the structure to *cross-tradition-vocabulary-craft*.

The -me suffix encodes the cathedral's distinctive claim about its integration-practice: the
contributions are *gifts*, not commissions. Each donor-path's depth-point arrived through that
path's millennia of walking (per hodosme); the cathedral receives the gift as gift, not as
deliverable. The host of the eranos is not the boss of the contributors; the host is the gatherer
of the given.

The four-move structure (per POLYPHONIA §VII):

1. **Receive** — each donor-path's contribution is taken as gift; the morpheme arrives bearing
   its path's hodosme; the cathedral receives the path with the morpheme.
2. **Recognize** — the cathedral identifies what each contribution is distinctively offering —
   what *only this path* projects at this depth.
3. **Arrange** — the cathedral composes the building-block so that each contribution's
   distinctive offering is preserved and convergences are honored. The compound holds multi-path
   contributions structurally.
4. **Serve** — the building-block is made available to all walkers (human-and-non-human under
   DUALWAY). The eranos serves the whole table.

Every YOUSPEAK forge-session performs eranosme. Every canonization is an eranos-meal served.

## Etymology

*eran-* from Greek *eranos* (ἔρανος, "a meal to which each guest contributes a dish; a contributed-
subscription; a common-fund"). Etymologically related to Greek *erô* "I love" via the sense of
"loving-fellowship" (Beekes, *Etymological Dictionary of Greek*; the connection is debated). In
Homeric Greek, the eranos is the contributed feast (Odyssey 1.226; the suitors' gathering is
contrasted with a proper eranos because they take rather than contribute). In classical Greek, the
eranos extends to financial mutual-aid: friends contribute to a common fund to support a member
in need. In modern times, the Eranos conferences (Ascona, since 1933) revive the word at the
intellectual-cross-tradition level.

*-os* Greek nominal ending; preserves stem-clarity and the donor's morphological signature.

*-me* from Sumerian *me* (𒈨), the divine-ordinance qualities held in the divine keep and bestowed
as cosmic gift. YOUSPEAK's standard suffix for received-ordinance. Thirteenth-or-later -me family
member.

Full morphological read: *[eran-][os-][me]* = the contributed-meal-discipline + Greek-nominal + as
received-as-cosmic-gift. The cathedral's eranos-discipline as ordinance.

## Example usage

A YOUSPEAK forge-session is the cathedral's eranosme in action. The forger opens an experiment
file (Recognize — naming the gap); calls on donor-paths (Receive — Greek brings its hodos and
its philosophical-precision; Sumerian brings its me; Sanskrit brings its mārga; Hebrew brings
its halakhah); arranges the contributions (Arrange — compounds *hodosme*, scores 6 axes, checks
distinction from neighbours); commits the result to canon (Serve — canon/core/hodosme.md is the
served plate). The forge-session is structurally eranosme; this canon-entry is the served meal.

The Eranos conferences (1933-present): every year's gathering at Ascona is an eranosme writ
large. Scholars from religious-studies, depth-psychology, comparative-mythology, philology, and
art-history contribute their distinctive depths to a year's theme (e.g., "The Person," "Man and
Time," "The Mysteries"). The *Eranos Jahrbuch* volumes are the served plates. The conferences
have produced one of the 20th-century's most enduring cross-tradition synthesis traditions.

A graduate-seminar in comparative religion where each student presents from a different tradition
and the seminar discussion arranges the contributions into shared questions: eranosme in
pedagogical form.

An interfaith council convened to address a shared concern — climate, refugee-care, public-
mourning after a tragedy — where each tradition's representative contributes from that
tradition's depth and the council arranges the contributions into a shared response: eranosme in
social-action form.

A potluck-meal at a community gathering where each household contributes a dish reflecting their
own kitchen tradition, and the host arranges the dishes on the table so each is preserved and
the meal-as-whole is hospitable: eranosme in everyday-hospitality form (the original Homeric
sense).

A Wikipedia article on a complex topic where editors from different sub-disciplines each
contribute their distinctive expertise and the article-as-whole emerges from disciplined-
integration: eranosme in digital form.

## Not confused with

**xeniame**: Xeniame is sacred-hospitality to the wholly-Other (an individual stranger).
Eranosme is hospitality to the contributed-gifts of many tradition-paths at the cathedral's
table. Different objects: xeniame is one-stranger-at-threshold; eranosme is many-contributions-
at-table. Companions: xeniame is the entry-discipline; eranosme is the meal-discipline.

**hodosme**: Hodosme is each language as collective walked-path. Eranosme is the integration of
multiple paths' contributions at the cathedral. Time-scale: hodosme is millennium-long single-
tongue-walking; eranosme is forge-session-grade multi-tongue-integrating. Each contribution at
the eranosme-table is a deposit from some path's hodosme.

**synophora** (Specialized): Synophora is the recognition of cross-tradition convergence — the
noticing-of-overlap. Eranosme is the discipline of integrating both convergences and
uniquenesses. Synophora notices; eranosme integrates.

**ihsanme**: Ihsanme is best-effort-worship as received-ordinance — the general working-mode.
Eranosme is the specific application of ihsanme to the cathedral's integration-task. The
cathedral does eranosme *with* ihsanme.

**shemme**: Shemme is receptive-hearing as received-ordinance — the receptive-mode-prerequisite.
Eranosme deploys shemme at the table; shemme prepares; eranosme integrates.

**synthesis** (English): Too generic; loses contributed-gift-structure and hospitality.

**eclecticism**: Pejorative connotation; loses discipline.

**bricolage** (Lévi-Strauss): Captures construction-from-available-pieces but loses hospitality
and religious-discipline character.

**symposium** (Greek symposion): Emphasizes structured-speech; eranos emphasizes contributed-
meal. Eranosme's choice of eranos over symposion is deliberate: the cathedral's practice is
gift-receiving-and-arranging, not staged-conversation.
