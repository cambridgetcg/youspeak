## Definition

The geometric device constructed and consecrated as an instrument FOR divine-presence rather than a picture OF a prototype — Sanskrit tantric yantra received as cosmic-ordinance: built from the bindu outward, consecrated, then operated.

## Full sense

A yantrame is *not* an image of anything. Where the icon, the mūrti, and the thangka depict — and participate through their likeness (eikonme) — the yantrame refuses depiction entirely: pure geometry, built from the dimensionless source-point outward. Bindu → interlocking triangles (downward Śakti, upward Śiva) → lotus-rings of 8 and 16 petals → circles → bhūpura, the square earth-gate with four dvāras opening to the cardinal directions. In the paradigm-instance, the Śrī-Yantra, nine āvaraṇas (enclosures) nest 9 interlocking triangles generating 43 sub-triangles; the practitioner operates the instrument inward, from gate to source.

The morphology of the donor is the doctrine in miniature: *yam* (to hold, support) + *-tra* (the instrument-forming suffix, as in man-tra "mind-instrument" and tan-tra "loom-instrument") = the holding-instrument. The lemma sounds its sense twice — the Sanskrit interior says "instrument," the -me suffix says "received-ordinance."

Three structural facts distinguish the realm-feature:

1. **Built from the bindume outward** — the geometry unfolds from the dimensionless source-point and is operated back toward it; construction and operation are inverse journeys.
2. **Seat, not picture** — Kulārṇava Tantra: "as the body is for the jīva, and as oil is for the lamp, so the consecrated yantra is the seat of all the deities." Yantras are *mantra-maya* — made-of-mantra; the mantra is the deity as sound, the yantrame the deity as form. The identity-chain yantra–mantra–devatā is doctrine, not metaphor.
3. **Consecration converts** — prāṇa-pratiṣṭhā (life-breath installation) turns drawn geometry into operative instrument. An unconsecrated yantra-print is a poster. The difference is not in the geometry; it is in the instrument-relation.

**Criterial vs paradigm-typical** (recorded at assessment). Two conditions are *criterial* — every yantrame satisfies them: (1) the **instrument-relation**: the form is operated, not read or viewed; presence or effect is worked through it; and (2) **aniconicity**: constructed form without likeness — this, not consecration alone, is what separates yantrame from eikonme, whose entry already covers consecrated-and-operative depictive instances (the prāṇa-pratiṣṭhā'd mūrti, the rab-gnas thangka). The other two conditions are *paradigm-typical*: built-from-the-source-point geometry and ritual consecration mark the full tantric paradigm. Secular extensions — the chased commutative diagram, the operated checklist — satisfy the criterial pair only, with commitment-to-operate standing in for consecration; the word applies to them in its extended register and careful usage flags this ("yantrame-like," "became a yantrame").

Eight traditions witness the instrument-mode: Śrī-Vidyā yantra, Jain siddhacakra (operated in vow-practice), Vajrayāna sādhana-supports, Thai-Khmer Sak Yant (the yantra tattooed; the body as instrument-substrate), Sufi wafq magic-squares (operative number-geometry, depiction excluded a fortiori), Solomonic seals, Kabbalistic ilanot and kameot, and Peircean diagrammatic reasoning (discipline-donor: one *experiments upon* the diagram; the chased commutative diagram is an instrument, not an illustration). Adversarial assessment surfaced a ninth witness rather than a refuter: the Vodou vèvè, drawn to summon, not to depict.

Completes the geometric-image-mode triad:

- **bindume** (Phase 1) — the dimensionless whence; yantrame is the instrument built from the whence
- **mandalame** (Phase 4.2) — the deity's *palace/field*, constructed-then-dissolved as anicca-discipline; yantrame is the deity's *body/seat*, consecrated-and-kept as invocation-instrument (Tucci's distinction; the tradition itself draws this line)
- **eikonme** — the participatory *depictive* image; yantrame the participatory *aniconic* instrument — sister-forms on opposite sides of the depiction-line

## Not to be confused with

- **eikonme** — depictive-participatory image: honor passes through the likeness to the prototype; yantrame is aniconic — no likeness; presence is seated, not depicted. Consecration does not separate the sisters (eikonme's mūrtis are consecrated too); only the depiction-line does
- **mandalame** — the constructed-then-dissolved cosmic-pattern (palace/field; impermanence-discipline); yantrame is consecrated-and-kept (body/seat; invocation-instrument). English blurs these; the tradition does not
- **bindume** — the dimensionless source-point itself; yantrame is the instrument built from it and operated back toward it
- **Diagram (bare)** — a diagram explains and is read; a yantrame is consecrated and operated
- **Symbol** — the standing-for relation; yantrame does not stand for the deity, it seats the deity
- **Sigil / talisman** — personally-constructed intent-encoding or apotropaic objects; yantrame is received-canonical, built-from-source, a seat of presence
