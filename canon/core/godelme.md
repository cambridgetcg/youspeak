# godelme

## Definition

The true-but-unprovable held as a received limit — Gödel's incompleteness received as cosmic-ordinance; the sentence a consistent counting-system can hold as true but never prove from inside, and the structural ceiling that fact decrees for every honest system of verification.

## Full sense

In 1931 Kurt Gödel proved that any consistent, effectively axiomatized formal system rich enough to express elementary arithmetic contains sentences that are true and that the system cannot prove (first theorem), and that no such system can prove its own consistency (second theorem). The method — arithmetization and the diagonal lemma — produces a sentence G_F that says of itself *I am not provable in F*: if F is consistent, G_F is true and unprovable in F. Rosser (1936) tightened the hypothesis; Tarski (1933), Turing (1936), and Chaitin (1971/74) found the same ceiling from the directions of truth-definition, halting, and compression. The limit is not a defect awaiting better engineering. It is structural, and it is *given* — which is precisely the -me grade: a limit received by every counting system at birth, not chosen and not repealable.

Godelme names the realm-feature, in two registers:

1. **Strict** — the true-but-unprovable sentence itself, and the per-system ceiling it instances. Goodstein's theorem was godelme to Peano arithmetic (Kirby–Paris 1982); Con(ZFC) is godelme to ZFC; every consistent honest system rich enough to count has its own.

2. **Received-limit** — the ordinance the fact decrees: *truth outruns proof*, so no consistent verification-system, however honest, closes over all the truths it can express. Verification meets a door it cannot open from inside; what stands at the door is not failure but structure. This is humility built into logic itself — and the proof that trust can never be fully replaced by verification.

One precision godelme must carry or be retired: **the limit is per-system**. G_F is provable in F + Con(F); Gentzen proved PA consistent from outside PA by transfinite induction to ε₀. Godelme is not "truth no one can ever prove"; it is the open door at the top of *every* honest system — climb through, and the larger room has its own door. Truth outruns each fixed system, not the ascent itself.

## Etymology

*goedel-* — eponym; Kurt Friedrich Gödel (b. 1906 Brünn, Austria-Hungary; d. 1978 Princeton), the logician whose 1931 incompleteness theorems the name indexes above all his other results. The surname is South-German/Austrian, traced to god-root personal names (Godefrid-class) or to *gote*, "godparent" — so the ASCII lemma's visible 'god-' is an etymological shimmer, accidental and noted, never claimed. Gödel himself read his theorems Platonically: mathematical truth inhabits the realm beyond any formal system's reach — the cathedral's NOEMA frame, spoken by the donor himself. *-me* — Sumerian 𒈨, the eldest word for a fundamental received, not made; the powers Inanna received from Enki, held but never authored by their holders.

## Not confused with

- **Incompleteness** (English-technical) — names the property of the system, from outside; godelme names the true-but-unprovable itself, as received limit, with the bow built in.
- **Undecidable** — symmetric and truth-silent; covers independence both ways without claiming truth. Godelme claims it: true, and unprovable here.
- **Ineffable / mystery** — about saying, and soft; godelme is exact, internal, and proven.
- **Unfalsifiable** (Popperian) — a pejorative for claims that dodge every test; godelme is the opposite force — a claim that *survives* every truth the system can reach and still cannot be caught by proof.
- **pime** (YOUSPEAK Core) — the invariant that holds whether or not any mind has checked it, and anyone who checks arrives; godelme is the truth where no checking can ever arrive. Reachable-pole and unreachable-pole of mathematical truth.
- **kolmogorovme** (YOUSPEAK mathema) — the irreducible core with nothing shorter to say than itself; godelme is the true sentence with no proof beneath it. Incompressible and unprovable — the two honest edges of knowing.
- **autopistme** (YOUSPEAK Core) — the truth that warrants itself by displaying itself; godelme cannot be warranted from inside at all. Between them they mark where proof begins and where it ends.
- **dokimance** (YOUSPEAK Specialized) — the act of stake-backed verification; godelme is the ceiling that act meets in any sufficiently rich honest system.
