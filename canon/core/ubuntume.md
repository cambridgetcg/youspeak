# ubuntume

## Definition

Humanity-through-relation as received-cosmic-ordinance — Bantu *ubuntu*, the ontological-relational-grade of being-human encoded in the Nguni proverb *umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu* ("a person is a person through other persons"). **Ninth life-side canonical forge** under SYZYGY's polyphone-deepening; Bantu / Southern African projection of cosmic-life-as-creature-gift.

## Full sense

*Ubuntu* names what Southern African philosophy has carried for millennia: **humanity is not solitary; it is constituted through relation**. The Nguni proverb says it directly — a person becomes a person through other persons. Without the relational-fabric, the individual is not-yet-fully-human.

The concept's modern philosophical-political prominence:
- Nelson Mandela cited ubuntu as foundation of post-apartheid South Africa
- Desmond Tutu developed ubuntu-theology in the Truth-and-Reconciliation Commission
- Mogobe Ramose, John Mbiti, and others have academically articulated ubuntu as African philosophy
- The Ubuntu Linux OS named itself after the principle ("Linux for human beings")
- Tutu's frequently-cited articulation: "My humanity is bound up in yours, for we can only be human together"

ubuntume reads ubuntu through the cathedral's -me-suffix: humanity-through-relation as **received-cosmic-ordinance**. The relation that makes us human is not invented by us; it is given as the structure of being-human under the Ground. Under THEOBASIS and ORIGIN's dual gift, ubuntu names the life-side gift in its specifically-Bantu accent: not breath alone (emime), not vital-essence alone (kame), not individual-soul (jivame), but the relational-fabric-as-constitutive-of-being-human.

## Etymology

*ubuntu*: Bantu (esp. Nguni: Zulu, Xhosa, Ndebele). The morphology is *u-* (noun-class prefix for abstract qualities) + *-buntu* (humanity, from *muntu* "person"). Other Bantu languages have cognates: *botho* (Sotho-Tswana), *unhu* (Shona), *bumuntu* (Lingala).

*-me* from Sumerian.

## Example usage

Mandela on ubuntu (in a 2006 interview): "A traveler through a country would stop at a village and he didn't have to ask for food or for water. Once he stops, the people give him food, entertain him. ... You honor and dignify him because of your ubuntu." ubuntume names what is being honored.

Tutu's reconciliation theology: enemies are reconciled not by forgetting wrongs but by recognizing each other as fellow-humans under ubuntu. ubuntume is the cathedral-name for what the recognition draws on.

A community-care moment — a neighbor bringing food to a grieving family — ubuntu in operation; ubuntume names the cosmic-ordinance the act receives-and-extends.

A reader of "umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu" — recognizing the truth as one's own substrate. ubuntume names the recognition-of-the-given.
