# heimrekjame

## Definition

Forge-act-as-received-ordinance — the process of pressing one tradition's vocabulary into service in a foreign semantic domain, thereby creating a compound meaning neither tradition possessed alone. The -me marks the generativity as gift: the meaning that emerges from the collision is received, not invented.

## Full sense

Heimgrekjame names what Gothic *heimrekja* (home-journey, home-bringing) carries with distinctive precision. *Heim* (home, world) + *rekja* (to reach, to touch, to bring): the carrying-across of meaning from one semantic world into another. Not translation, which preserves meaning across languages. Not borrowing, which takes a word as-is. Not calquing, which translates morpheme-by-morpheme. Heimgrekjame is the deliberate pressing of one world's semantic field into the service of another world's gap — the compound that emerges carries both sets of overtones and fills a gap that neither donor alone could fill.

Wulfila's Bible translation is the paradigmatic historical instance. When Wulfila translated Greek *kyrios* (abstract authority) as Gothic *frauja* (household-lord, personal loyalty-bond), he was not translating the word — he was carrying the Germanic relational-bond register INTO the Greek authority-register. The result was a compound meaning neither tradition possessed alone: divine authority as personal household-loyalty. This is heimrekja.

The cathedral's own method is the paradigmatic modern instance. Every forge from kimance (Japanese *ki* + Latin *-mance*) to hailsme (Gothic *hails* + Sumerian *me*) is a heimrekjame: two traditions pressed together, yielding what neither possessed alone. 162 compounds, each a collision, each a carrying-across.

The -me suffix marks the received-ordinance grade: the generativity is gift, not invention. The compound-meaning that emerges from the collision of two traditions is received, not manufactured. The forger brings the worlds together; what happens at the interface is bestowed. This is the theological deepening: heimrekjame is not merely a linguistic technique but participation in a cosmic structure of generativity. The forge-act is ordinance.
