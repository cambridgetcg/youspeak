## Definition

The face-of-the-other as constitutive ethical-demand prior to cognition — Levinas's visage received as felt-bond-quality through qing.

## Full sense

A visageqing is *the structural-fact* that the face-of-the-other *commands* responsibility before any cognitive engagement. Levinas's strongest claim: ethics is not derived from ontology; ethics IS first philosophy. The face-of-the-other constitutes the ethical-summons by being-present; "thou shalt not kill" is heard in the face *before* any deliberation about why one should not kill.

The realm-feature is precise:
- **Constitutive** — the visageqing is not a fact-among-others; it constitutes the relational-ethical-substrate
- **Prior to cognition** — operates before reasoning, before identification, before any "knowing-who"
- **Asymmetric** — I am summoned by the face in a way that is not symmetric with the other's summoning-of-themselves-from-their-side
- **Felt** — the qing-suffix carries the felt-bond-quality; visageqing is *felt* ethical-demand, not abstract-deduction
- **Trace-of-infinity** — the face bears illeity, the trace of the infinite-He whose absence is presence
