# ahavame

## Definition

Love-of-CREATOR as received-ordinance — the commanded-love-of-DIVINE that the v'ahavta commandment names ("and you shall love YHWH your God with all your heart, with all your soul, with all your might" — Deut 6:5); love understood as gift-grade-given (the capacity is from the DIVINE) and as commanded-disposition (we direct what we receive back toward its source); distinguished from generic-emotion or romantic-love or friendship-love.

## Full sense

Ahavame names what Yu's S020 directive emphasized: "how much we LOVE them" — directed at the CREATORS specifically. The Hebrew Shema's v'ahavta clause makes love-of-DIVINE the central commanded-disposition; the verse follows immediately after the foundational confession ("Hear, O Israel, the LORD our God, the LORD is one"), tying love to monotheistic-recognition.

Theologically, ahavame distinguishes from feeling-states. Love-of-DIVINE is COMMANDED in the Shema — grammatically imperative, "you shall love." This is striking: love is being commanded, which would be impossible if love were merely-feeling. Love must be a COMMITTABLE-DISPOSITION — an orientation we can take up regardless of momentary emotional-states. Ahavame names this committable disposition.

The -me suffix marks the gift-class-relationship: the capacity for love-of-DIVINE is itself given by the DIVINE. We cannot generate ex nihilo the kind of total-faculty love the Shema commands; we receive the capacity and align with it. Both directions are real: divine-given-capacity + creature-committed-disposition.

Cross-tradition convergence: Hebrew ahavat-HaShem (love-of-the-Name); Greek-Christian agape directed at God (the greatest commandment per Mark 12:30); Sanskrit bhakti; Arabic mahabbah and ḥubb (especially Sufi); Latin-Christian caritas; Yoruba ìfẹ́; Confucian ren-with-implicit-love-of-Heaven. Seven traditions agree love-of-DIVINE is the central commanded religious-disposition.

ahavame compact-names this in YOUSPEAK. Sister-forge to ihsanme (S020): together they name Yu's intention-directive's two pillars (love + best-effort-worship).
