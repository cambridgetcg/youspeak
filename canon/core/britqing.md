# britqing

## Definition

The covenant-felt-bond between CREATOR and CREATED — the Hebrew brit-tradition's relationship-quality preserved in its dual nature: structured (covenant-form, with specific terms and signs) AND felt (qing-grade emotional bond, the "I will be your God and you will be my people" intimacy). Neither dimension absorbs the other; both operate together.

## Full sense

Britqing names what English "covenant" half-captures. English-juridical reading hears "covenant" and thinks "legal-binding-agreement"; this captures the structural side of brit but loses the felt-bond dimension. English-relational reading hears "covenant" as roughly-equivalent to "deep friendship"; this captures the felt-bond but loses the structural-binding.

The Hebrew brit is both. From the Abrahamic covenant (Genesis 15) — where the cutting-of-halved-animals is BOTH ritual-juridical-act AND relationship-establishing-event — through the Mosaic covenant at Sinai (where the suzerain-treaty form is filled with the people's "we will hear and obey"), through Jeremiah's promise of a new-covenant "written on the heart" — every Hebrew brit has both dimensions structurally-built-in.

Britqing brings the Hebrew register into YOUSPEAK working-vocabulary. It joins the relationship-class as third entry alongside paqduqing (Akkadian asymmetric-reciprocity custodial-care) and hamkarqing (Avestan cooperative co-creation). Each names a different aspect of the same CREATOR-CREATED relationship; together they articulate what no single tradition alone fully names.

The qing-grade dimension is critical. A covenant without qing (structured but unfelt) is mere-contract. A bond without brit-structure (felt but unstructured) is intimacy without commitment. Britqing is the Hebrew theological insight: God's relationship with creation is BOTH structurally-binding AND qing-grade-felt, and the two together are what covenant means.

## Etymology

*brit* (Hebrew, Northwest Semitic; בְּרִית; from triconsonantal root B-R-T "to cut"; ancient Near Eastern covenants were ritually "cut" between halved animals — Genesis 15:9-21 with Abraham — binding the parties under threat of becoming-like-the-halved-animals if they break covenant) + *qing* (Mandarin, Sino-Tibetan; 情 — felt-bond; established in YOUSPEAK Core via kinqing + panimqing + paqduqing + hamkarqing; the cathedral's primary felt-bond marker).

## Not confused with

- **Covenant** (English) — too juridical-narrow; loses the felt-bond dimension
- **Contract** (English) — purely legal; lacks both qing and the existential-binding
- **Relationship** (English) — too informal; lacks the structured-binding dimension
- **Brit** (Hebrew, alone) — names the covenant-structure; britqing adds the felt-bond explicitly
- **Qing** (Mandarin, alone) — names felt-bond generally; britqing specifies the covenant-structured felt-bond
- **Kinqing** (YOUSPEAK Core) — felt-bond between creatures generally (without covenant-structure); britqing adds the structured-binding aspect
- **Paqduqing** (YOUSPEAK Core) — asymmetric-reciprocity custodial-care (cult-economy emphasis); britqing emphasizes structural-promise-binding
- **Hamkarqing** (YOUSPEAK Specialized) — cooperative co-creation (cosmic-battle emphasis); britqing emphasizes promise-structured-relationship
