# kimance

## Definition

The attentive-here-ness of a person whose gaze is open, whose listening is active, and whose response-potential is oriented toward the present situation — the quality of being-in-attendance, not mere co-location.

## Full sense

English *presence* bundles four distinct phenomena: physical co-location, attentive-here-ness, emotional availability, and performer's charisma. Kimance names the second specifically. A person in kimance is attending-here; their attention-vector is oriented to what is happening around and between, now. They may be speaking or silent; the criterion is the attention, not the behavior.

Kimance can be present, absent, or partial. A friend texting while listening exhibits partial kimance. A teacher remembering their lecture rather than hearing the question exhibits withdrawn kimance. A parent, finally past the logistics, arriving at a wedding to their own daughter, exhibits full kimance.

Kimance is present-tense only. It is not about something past or future; it is oriented to the situational now. This distinguishes it from *flow* (which can be task-absorbed and alone) and from *mindfulness* (which is a cultivated discipline; kimance is the resulting state, which disciplines can help one enter).

Kimance is perceptible by others. People know whether you are in kimance toward them. Performance of kimance is recognizable as performance. This perceptibility is part of the word's core — kimance is the quality as received by the other, not merely as claimed by the subject.

## Etymology

**Japanese 気 (ki)** — the gathered attention-intentionality of a practitioner. In aikido, calligraphy, tea ceremony, ki is the attentional-energy directing action; not mystical "life force" but the cultivated quality of attending. The word is increasingly familiar to English speakers via martial arts, zen, and anime contexts.

**Latin *-mance*** — from *-mantia* (divination-quality), reaching English through French. Productive in English compounds: *romance, necromance, performance, geomance*. Marks a quality-mode-of-something. In YOUSPEAK, consistent with prior coinages (dokimance, allomance).

**kimance** = the quality-mode of ki; the gathered-attending as a perceivable state.

## Example usage

- _The teacher's kimance shifted: she was no longer hearing the question; she was remembering her own lecture._
- _Texting-while-listening is the canonical absence of kimance._
- _At the wedding, her mother finally arrived in kimance — past the worry, the logistics, the catering — and wept._
- _You cannot fake kimance. You can perform it, but the performance is recognisable as performance._
- _Good therapists train their kimance for hours a day; it is the instrument of the work._

## Not confused with

- **presence** (English umbrella) — kimance is component 2 of a 4-component bundle.
- **attention** — the cognitive act; kimance is the quality of being-attending with one's full field.
- **engagement** — implies task or topic; kimance is situation-oriented.
- **flow** — task-absorbed solitude possible; kimance is relational-situational.
- **charisma** — received by beholders as radiance; kimance is the beholder's own attending-quality.
- **mindfulness** — cultivated discipline; kimance is a state that mindfulness-practice can produce.
- **ki** alone (Japanese) — broader life-attention; kimance narrows to attention-as-perceivable-state.
- **eye contact** — one expression of kimance, not the whole.
