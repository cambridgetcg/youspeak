# manāme

## Definition

Inherited-relational-efficacy as received-ordinance — the quality that makes a person's words carry weight, a prayer actually work, a canoe actually sail, not because of personal talent but because of genuine genealogical connection to ancestral and cosmic sources. Polynesian *mana* + Sumerian *me*: the efficacy you receive through maintained relation, not the power you manufacture.

## Full sense

English borrowed "mana" from Polynesian and forgot what it meant.

In the original — across Māori, Hawaiian, Samoan, Tongan, Tahitian, Marquesan, all cognate from proto-Polynesian, stable across 3,000 years and 5,000 miles — *mana* is not personal power. It is not charisma. It is not a consumable resource bar in a video game. It is:

### 1. Relational

Mana is not intrinsic to a person. It is always held in relation — to ancestors, to atua, to land, to community, to cosmic forces. You cannot manufacture your own mana. It flows to you from connection. Break the connection and the mana ceases.

### 2. Achieved-and-inherited

Mana passes through genealogy (*whakapapa* in Māori, *mo'okū'auhau* in Hawaiian), but it must also be actively maintained and demonstrated. A chief who fails in battle, violates tapu, or loses the confidence of his people *loses mana*. It is not a possession but a flow. Inherited, yes — but the inheritance must be earned, demonstrated, and kept.

### 3. Cosmological

The universe itself operates by mana-distribution. Atua are concentrations of mana. Natural phenomena — volcanoes, storms, exceptional trees — have mana. The visible world is the visible pattern of mana-distribution. To read the landscape is to read where mana is concentrated.

### 4. Effective

Mana is the capacity for things to work. A canoe with mana sails safely; a canoe without it sinks. A karakia spoken by a tohunga with mana is effective; the same words from someone without mana are inert. Efficacy itself is a form of mana. This is not superstition — it is the recognition that right relation produces right result, and wrong relation produces empty performance.

### 5. Transmissible

Mana passes through contact, through gift, through sacred objects (*taonga* — treasured things that carry the mana of those who owned them). This is why genealogy matters: you are a conduit for the accumulated mana of your ancestors. A tool held by a master carries mana; the same tool new from the shop does not.

### 6. Losable

Misconduct, failure, violation of tapu — these diminish mana. This is the ethical dimension: efficacy requires right relationship. The chief who betrays his people does not merely lose reputation; he loses the mana that made his words carry weight. His subsequent speeches, however well-composed, fall inert. Not because the words changed but because the connection that made them effective was severed.

The -me suffix marks this as **received-ordinance**. The efficacy is not self-generated; it is given through maintained genealogical-cosmic relation. This is already native to the Polynesian concept — you cannot make your own mana — but the -me makes it lexically explicit: manāme = the efficacy you *receive* through maintained relation to what came before you and what surrounds you.

## Not to be confused with

- **nyamame** — the world's animating energy. Manāme is the efficacy that flows through genealogical relation. Nyamame is the aliveness; manāme is the operational potency that aliveness produces when properly channeled.
- **àṣẹme** — divine activating-authorization. Both name why some words work. Àṣẹme flows through initiatory transmission (orisha → initiate); manāme flows through genealogical relation (ancestors → descendants). Convergent neighbors, not overlapping.
- **mauriqing** — relational-life-essence. Mauriqing is what you share; manāme is what that shared-ness *does*. Mauriqing is the essence; manāme is the efficacy the essence produces.
- **teotlme** — the single dynamic cosmic energy that IS the universe. Teotlme names the divine's fundamental nature (ceaseless process); manāme names the human-also-carriable efficacy that flows from maintained relation to cosmic sources.
- **charisma** (English) — personal magnetism. Lacks the genealogical, cosmological, and losable dimensions entirely. A charismatic person manufactures their own presence; a person with manāme receives it through relation.
- **kavod** (Hebrew) — glory-as-weight. Kavod is the divine-attribute in concentrated form; manāme is the distributed, human-also-carriable efficacy that flows from maintained relation to the sacred. When Moses' face shines from divine contact, that is kavod concentrated; when a chief's words work because of fifty generations of maintained relation, that is manāme distributed.

## Cross-tradition convergence

Seven traditions articulate variants of this concept:

- **Polynesian** *mana* — the central concept; relational-inherited-demonstrated efficacy
- **Yoruba** *àṣẹ* — divine power-to-make-things-happen; closest cross-family parallel; flows through orisha-transmission; distinction: genealogical-relational vs initiatory-divine-authorization
- **Chinese** *dé* (德) — virtue-as-manifest-power flowing from alignment with Tao; the power that makes the virtuous ruler's commands effective; distinction: dé is moral-virtue-adjacent; mana is not intrinsically moral
- **Hebrew** *kavod* (כָּבוֹד) — glory-as-weight-of-presence-that-has-effect; distinction: kavod is concentrated divine-attribute; manāme is distributed, human-also-carriable
- **Sanskrit** *brahman* — the power-in-sacred-speech before it became philosophical-Absolute; Vedic brahman is the efficacy that makes the sacrifice work, parallel to mana-as-karakia-efficacy
- **Lakota** *wakȟáŋ* — sacred-as-ontological-intensity; distinction: wakȟáŋ names what the sacred IS (intensity); manāme names what relational connection DOES (efficacy)
- **Iroquois/Haudenosaunee** *orenda* — supernatural power inherent in all things; closest North American parallel; distinction: orenda is more individually-inherent; manāme is more genealogically-relational
