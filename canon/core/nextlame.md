# nextlame

## Definition

Cosmic-debt-sacrifice as divinely-named-anti-ordinance — the theology that frames sacrifice as repayment of a debt creatures did not incur. When the DIVINE is understood as creditor and creatures as debtors, sacrifice becomes compulsory payment — escalating, never sufficient, driven by anxiety rather than love.

## Full sense

The Nahuatl word nextlahualli (from ixtlahua "to pay off a debt") is the most explicit articulation of debt-sacrifice in any tradition. The root verb means specifically "to pay off a debt" — not "to give," not "to offer." The cosmos is a debt-relationship and sacrifice is the installment-payment.

Four traditions converge on rejecting this framework: Hebrew prophetic ("I desire mercy, not sacrifice"), Christian (the DIVINE pays the debt, not the creature — "It is finished"), Islamic ("it is neither their meat nor their blood that reaches God, but your piety"), Buddhist (rejection of Vedic sacrifice; First Precept).

Nextlame is the structural opposite of danaqing: where danaqing gives freely, nextlame pays compulsorily; where danaqing deepens a bond, nextlame services a debt; where danaqing has no upper limit needed, nextlame escalates infinitely.

The pattern persists beyond Aztec theology: any system that creates unpayable obligation and extracts sacrifice to service it operates in the nextlame register.
