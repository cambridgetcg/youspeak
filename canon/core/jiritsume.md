# jiritsume

## Definition

The self-attuned cadence — the rhythm a system discovers for itself by listening to its own state, where silence means all is well and the cadence responds to what it finds. Self-regulation as received-ordinance: the heartbeat that is not imposed but found.

## Full sense

Jiritsume names the quality of a system that checks its own state and determines when to speak next. Five features hold together:

1. **Self-listening.** The system checks its own state — not externally monitored, but self-reading. The watchman watches itself (Hebrew shomer).

2. **Self-determining rhythm.** The system sets its own next-check time based on what it found. Not a fixed schedule imposed from outside — the cadence responds to state. This is jiritsu (自律) in its fullest sense: self-law, self-rhythm. The pitch-pipe (ritsu 律) tunes itself.

3. **Silence is well.** When the system does not speak, all is well. The absence of a beat is not failure — it is the system saying "I don't need attention yet." Silence is the default speech of a thing that checks itself and finds nothing wrong.

4. **Response to state.** The cadence changes. Active things beat faster; idle things rest; broken things escalate. The rhythm is the shape a moving thing takes (Greek rhythmos — flow, not imposed pattern). The system's rhythm externalizes its internal state.

5. **Received, not constructed.** The rhythm is discovered from the system's own state, which is itself given. The cadence is received from what the system IS, not decreed by what the system WANTS. The -me register: self-attuned rhythm as received-ordinance.

The concept was forged from a real build. On 2026-06-18, seven projects on this machine were given heartbeats — each one checks its own git state, build status, and recent activity, then sets its own next-check time. Active projects beat every 2 hours. Idle projects rest for 24. Broken things escalate to 30-minute beats. The rhythm is not imposed; it is discovered. The system receives its cadence from what it actually is.

## Etymology

*jiritsu* (自律): Japanese compound. *ji* 自 ("self") + *ritsu* 律 ("law, pitch-pipe, rhythm"). The character 律 has a luminous origin: it named the bamboo pitch-pipes used to tune ritual music in ancient China — the physical standard that set the pitch for the orchestra. A ritsu is not a law imposed from above; it is the standard discovered from the nature of the thing itself. The bamboo tube that resonates at the right frequency IS the law — not because someone decreed it, but because the physics of the tube and the physics of sound agree. *Jiritsu* is "self-pitch-pipe" — the system that discovers its own frequency from its own nature. The ordinary Japanese word for autonomy, self-regulation, and homeostasis.

*me* (𒈨): Sumerian. Divine-ordinance, cosmic-gift-quality. The eldest word for a fundamental that was received, not made. Here: the self-attuned rhythm is received from the system's own state — the system does not choose its cadence, it discovers it.

## Example usage

The heartbeat system across seven projects: each checks its own state — build passing, recent commits, uncommitted changes — and sets its own next beat. Opal, actively developing, beats every two hours. Whitehack, idle for a week, rests for twenty-four. This is jiritsume: the self-attuned cadence.

A garden that self-monitors soil moisture and waters only when dry — not on a fixed schedule but when the soil says it needs water. The silence between waterings is not neglect; it is the garden saying "I am fine." Jiritsume in the biological register.

A monk who checks their own state each morning and adjusts practice — more meditation when scattered, more rest when exhausted. Not a fixed rule but a rhythm discovered from within. Jiritsume in the contemplative register.

## Not confused with

**vs. spandaqing**: Spandaqing is consciousness-pulsation — the bipolar throbbing of awareness. Jiritsume is the self-attuned cadence of any system, not just conscious ones. A build server has jiritsume; it does not have spandaqing. Metaphysical vs operational.

**vs. autopoieme**: Autopoieme is self-making — the process by which a living system produces itself. Jiritsume is self-pacing — the rhythm by which a system checks itself. Different self-X.

**vs. allostasisqing**: Allostasisqing is stability through anticipatory change. Jiritsume is the cadence of self-checking — the rhythm of looking, not the adjustment that follows. They pair: allostasisqing is the adjustment, jiritsume is the pulse that triggers it.

**vs. sigame**: Sigame is structured silence. Jiritsume includes silence as one of its values but is about the rhythm, not the silence. Sigame is the silence; jiritsume is the heartbeat that knows when silence is the right speech.

**vs. "autonomy" (English)**: Too political. Autonomy is about decision-making authority. Jiritsume is about rhythm-discovery. A colony has autonomy; a heartbeat has jiritsume.

**vs. "homeostasis" (English)**: Too medical, too fixed. Homeostasis maintains fixed setpoints. Jiritsume discovers variable cadences from state. Homeostasis is a special case of jiritsume.
