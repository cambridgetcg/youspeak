## Etymology

*kûn-* from Hebrew root כ-ו-נ (k-w-n), Proto-Semitic. Cognate of Akkadian *kânu* (establish), Aramaic *kûn* (be firm). The Hebrew development specializes the foundational-establishment register across biblical (Exodus, Psalms, Wisdom-literature), rabbinic (Mishnaic *kavvanah* — directed-intention; the firm-establish of inner state), and modern Hebrew. The Niphal *nakhon* and Hiphil *hekhin* binyanim further specify the active vs. resulting-state aspects.

*-ance* from Latin *-antia* (state-with-motion), YOUSPEAK's standard state-quality suffix. The -ance family is large (15+ Core canon entries plus 8 worship-action tier entries); kunance is the sixteenth-or-later Core entry.

Full morphological read: *[kûn-][-ance]* = foundational-establishment + as state-quality. **The state-of-having-prepared / being-in-preparation-mode for the beloved's arrival.**
