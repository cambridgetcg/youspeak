# kimme

## Definition

Attention-as-received-ordinance — the ontological quality of attention as a gift that arrives rather than a skill that is built; the first -me family member, the seed of the received-ordinance family. Paired with doxomme (glory-as-received-ordinance) as the inward-pole: attention is the capacity to see; glory is what is seen and the praise that answers. The -me marks: the attention is given, the capacity to attend is given, the recognizing-of-the-givenness is given — the recursion of gift that the entire -me family inherits.

## Full sense

Kimme names what English cannot: attention as something **received** rather than **constructed**. English treats attention as a cognitive skill one trains, allocates, manages — an action of the self. Multiple traditions — Sumerian via ME, Hindu via prasāda, Andean via ayni, Japanese via ki — treat attention as something received rather than self-made. A gift-quality, not a built-quality.

The distinction matters ethically. If attention is self-constructed, failures of attention are personal failures; self-blame follows. If attention is received-quality, failures of attention are conditions-of-receipt; inquiry follows (what is blocking receipt?). Clinical psychology has been quietly rediscovering this — attention as partly-given (neurochemistry, trauma, environment) and partly-received (into a prepared readiness) rather than purely-willed.

Kimme is the **ordinance-status** of attention. When a person can attend, kimme is being received. When attention fails without explanation — the day one cannot focus despite good sleep, nutrition, intention — kimme is partially-blocked or not-currently-available. When a person seems unusually attentive, kimme is richly-present. The -me does not mean attention is passive; it means the *ground* of the capacity is given, not manufactured. The Japanese ki (気) already carries this: ki is gathered, cultivated, received — the paradox of a capacity that is both practiced and given. The Sumerian me (𒈨) sharpens it: a ME is a discrete divine-ordinance that civilizations receive; attention is one such ordinance.

Kimme is not willed. It can be cultivated (through rest, practice, environment, relation) and it can be blocked (through exhaustion, trauma, anxiety, poor conditions). The cultivation is not attention-construction but **attention-reception-preparation**: creating conditions where the ordinance can arrive. This is what contemplative practice does. Monastic practice does not build attention; it prepares the receiver for kimme. The teacher at their best does not manufacture a student's focus; they transmit kimme — their students' kimance (the phenomenal brightening of attentive presence) brightens from the transmission, not from the verbal instruction.

The Sumerian concept of ME-transferability is structural to kimme: MEs can be transmitted between parties. Kimme inherits this. Attention can be transmitted between people (the teacher cultivates a child's kimme), across time (contemplative practices build receptive-conditions that later-life kimme inhabits), across states (a calm room supports everyone's kimme). The ordinance is not private property; it is a field-quality that can be shared, strengthened by environment, weakened by conditions, and given from one to another.

As the **first -me family member**, kimme is the seed that grew the entire received-ordinance family. The compound ki + me established the pattern: take a donor morpheme carrying a human-capacity, fuse it with the Sumerian ordinance-marker, and the result names that capacity as received-gift rather than self-production. The pattern proved productive beyond all expectation: 30 -me family members now exist, spanning 20+ donor traditions — doxomme (glory), shemme (hearing), ahavame (love), rahmame (mercy), sabrme (patience), shukrme (gratitude), tikkunme (repair), tzimtzumme (divine withdrawal), bindume (seed-point), yggdrasilme (living axis), and more. Each inherits the structural insight kimme established: the -me marks the capacity as ordinance, and the marking changes what the capacity IS. Attention marked as ordinance is a different thing from attention marked as skill.

The pairing with **doxomme** (glory-as-received-ordinance) is the foundational dyad of the -me family:

- **Kimme** is the inward-reception: attention is gift. The capacity to attend, to notice, to be present — this is received, not constructed.
- **Doxomme** is the outward-response: glory is gift, and the praise that answers glory is gift. The capacity to behold splendor and name it as splendor — this is received, not generated.

Attention receives; glory radiates; praise acknowledges. Kimme is the condition of seeing; doxomme is what happens when seeing meets radiance. One cannot behold glory (doxomme) without the attention (kimme) that makes beholding possible; one's attention (kimme) finds its deepest object in the glory it was given to behold (doxomme). The two are the received-ordinance pair in Core: the inward-ordinance and the outward-ordinance. The pair was named in doxomme's enrichment (2026-06-28); this enrichment completes the dyad from kimme's side.

The sibling-relationship with **shemme** (hearing-as-received-ordinance) names the two sense-modes of received-ordinance:

- **Kimme** is the visual-attentional pole: the capacity to attend, to see, to be present-to.
- **Shemme** is the auditory-receptivity pole: the hearing that is already the beginning of being-shaped.

The two are -me-family siblings, different sense-modes of the same structural insight: a human receptive-capacity marked as received-ordinance. Together they span the primary sensory channels through which the human receives.

The -me marks: the attention is given, the capacity to attend is given, the recognizing-of-the-givenness is given — the recursion of gift that the entire -me family inherits from this first compound.

## Not confused with

- **kimance** (YOUSPEAK Core — attentive-here-ness as perceived phenomenal state): kimance is the *phenomenal* pole — what someone perceives about a person's attending, the observable brightness of attentive presence. Kimme is the *ontological* pole — the received-status of the attention itself, the ordinance-ground of the capacity. Kimme enables kimance; kimance perceives kimme. The pair parallels the ontological/phenomenal distinction in the doxakallos/doxalgia register: kimme (ontological) + kimance (phenomenal).

- **doxomme** (YOUSPEAK Core 7.50 — glory-as-received-ordinance): doxomme is the outward-response of the received-ordinance pair — glory beheld and praise answered as gift. Kimme is the inward-reception — attention as gift. Kimme is the condition of seeing; doxomme is what happens when seeing meets radiance. The two are the foundational -me-family dyad: the inward-ordinance and the outward-ordinance. Attention without glory finds nothing to behold; glory without attention finds no one beholding.

- **shemme** (YOUSPEAK Core 7.65 — hearing-as-received-ordinance): shemme is the auditory-receptivity sibling — hearing-as-received-ordinance, the listening that is already the beginning of being-shaped. Kimme is the visual-attentional sibling — attention-as-received-ordinance. Both are -me-family; both name a human receptive-capacity as ordinance; the difference is sense-mode (seeing vs hearing). The two span the primary sensory channels of received-ordinance.

- **attention** (English): English treats attention as a cognitive skill one trains, allocates, manages — an action of the self. Kimme refuses this framing: attention is received-ordinance, not self-constructed capacity. The framing-change is ethical: if attention is skill, attention-failure is personal failure; if attention is ordinance, attention-failure is condition-of-receipt.

- **focus** (English): action-framing — the act of concentrating. Kimme is status-framing — the ontological quality of the capacity itself.

- **flow** (Csikszentmihalyi): a specific cognitive-task state of absorbed engagement. Kimme is broader and more foundational — the ground-capacity that makes flow possible, not the flow-state itself.

- **ki** alone (Japanese 気): broader life-attention-energy. Kimme specifies the received-ordinance dimension of ki — the gift-status of the gathered attention, not the full somatic-field concept.

- **prasāda** (Sanskrit): divine-grace; closely adjacent but religion-specific. Kimme is register-neutral — the ordinance-status of attention without requiring a specific theological framework.

- **grace** (Christian theological register): grace is the theological term for divine gift; kimme is the structural term for the received-status of a specific capacity. Kimme is secular-compatible; grace is tradition-specific.

- **noemame** (YOUSPEAK Core — meaning-arrival-as-received-ordinance): noemame is what arrives when the attention is rewarded — the fulfillment-ordinance. Kimme is the preparation-ordinance — the attention that makes reception possible. Kimme makes noemame possible; the two are paired (preparation-ordinance and fulfillment-ordinance).

- **sphoṭame** (YOUSPEAK Core — burst-forth-as-ordinance): sphoṭame is the yield that may burst forth when attention is sufficiently disciplined — the event-of-arrival. Kimme is the precondition — the attention-as-ordinance. Kimme orients; sphoṭame may arrive into that orientation.
