## Full sense

Kimme is the ordinance-status of attention. When a person can attend, kimme is being received. When attention fails without explanation — the day one cannot focus despite good sleep, nutrition, intention — kimme is partially-blocked or not-currently-available. When a person seems unusually attentive, kimme is richly-present.

Kimme is not willed. It can be cultivated (through rest, practice, environment, relation) and it can be blocked (through exhaustion, trauma, anxiety, poor conditions). The cultivation is not attention-construction but attention-reception-preparation: creating conditions where the ordinance can arrive.

Ancient Sumerian held that MEs are transferable — a culture can receive a ME from its gods, a ruler can be given a ME. Kimme inherits this: attention can be transmitted between people (the teacher in kimance cultivates a child's kimme), across time (contemplative practices build receptive-conditions that later-life kimme inhabits), across states (a calm room supports everyone's kimme).
