# frashame

## Definition

The eschatological renovation of all creation as received-ordinance — the Avestan *frashegird* made into a YOUSPEAK Core word. The making-wonderful of the world at the end of time, when asha triumphs over druj and all things are made *fraša* (perfect, wonderful). Not restoration to an original state, not replacement, not human utopia: the divine gift of cosmic completion.

## Full sense

In Zoroastrian theology, cosmic history is not endless cyclical return and not mere linear decay. It is the struggle of **asha** (cosmic truth-order) against **druj** (cosmic lie), with a directed outcome: **frashegird**, the renovation of all creation. The word literally means "making wonderful/excellent" — not restoring an old thing, not making a new thing, but making *this* thing wonderful.

**Frashame** names this renovation specifically as received-ordinance. The -me suffix is load-bearing. Without it, the word could be read as a political program, a self-improvement goal, or an ecological slogan. The -me marks the renovation as gift: the world does not renovate itself; the making-wonderful is the final cosmic ordinance given by Ahura Mazda, received by creation. Human beings participate in frashame by asha-aligned thought, word, and deed (humahukhtahvarshtame), but the renovation itself arrives as gift.

The four structural marks:

1. **Eschatological finality.** Frashame names the end-state of cosmic history, not an intermediate improvement. It is the *telos* toward which all asha-aligned action contributes.

2. **Renovation, not replacement.** The existing creation is not thrown away and rebuilt; it is purified and made wonderful. The refining fire burns away only what is druj-aligned; the asha-aligned creation remains and is perfected.

3. **Divine gift.** The -me marks the renovation as received-ordinance. Humans do not earn it; they receive it. Their role is preparation and alignment, not production.

4. **Victory of asha over druj.** Frashame is the definitive answer to drujme. Where drujme names the cosmic lie as anti-ordinance, frashame names the cosmic truth-order in its completed form.

### Cross-tradition parallels

1. **Zoroastrianism** — *frashegird* (primary donor). The oldest compact attestation of cosmic renovation as making-wonderful.

2. **Christianity** — *apokatastasis* (Origen, Gregory of Nyssa, Isaac of Nineveh). The restoration of all things. Difference: apokatastasis tends toward restoration to an original state; frashame is renovation-to-wonder.

3. **Judaism** — *tikkun olam* and the messianic age. Cooperative human-divine repair. Difference: tikkunme names ongoing repair; frashame names the final eschatological completion.

4. **Islam** — Yawm al-Baʿth / Qiyamah. Resurrection and final judgment. Difference: emphasis on judgment and paradise; frashame emphasizes the renovation of the cosmos itself.

5. **Buddhism** — Maitreya and the restoration of true Dharma. Difference: restoration of teaching and practice; frashame is the renovation of the material cosmos.

2. "Her environmental work was not frashame itself; it was humahukhtahvarshtame in hope of frashame." — distinction.

3. "The Nowruz festival is a seasonal rehearsal for frashame — the spring equinox prefigures the final triumph of light over darkness." — ritual.

4. "Drujme says evil is structural; frashame says the structure will be made wonderful." — dyad.

## Not to be confused with

- **apokatastasis** — Greek Christian restoration to an original state; frashame is making-wonderful, not return.
- **tikkunme** — ongoing human-divine repair of the world; frashame is the final eschatological completion.
- **hailsme** — salvation as wholeness-restoration of the creature; frashame is renovation of the cosmos.
- **shevirame** — the breaking that makes repair necessary; frashame is the far end of that arc.
- **kittume** — structural truth as firm foundation; frashame is eschatological completion, not foundational truth.
- **ragnarökme** — the ending-that-acts-within-fate; frashame is renovation, not destruction-within-destiny.
- **new creation** — Christian/Hebrew replacement-or-renewal narratives; frashame renovates the existing creation.
