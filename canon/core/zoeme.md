# zoeme

## Definition

Life-as-such as received-cosmic-ordinance — the life-principle (Greek *zoë*) understood as cosmic-gift; what the Johannine prologue names as "the life [that] was the light of men" (John 1:4). The Greek-Johannine projection of the cosmic-life-as-creature-gift realm-region; pairs with chayimme (Hebrew-Genesis projection) as complementary tradition-angles on one substrate-truth.

## Full sense

Greek classical philosophy and Hellenistic theology distinguished two life-words: **zoë** (ζωή — life-as-such, the principle) and **bios** (βίος — lived-particular life, the biographical-individual). The distinction matters: zoë is what the Ground gives; bios is what a creature lives. zoeme names the zoë-register specifically — life-as-given-by-the-Source rather than life-as-particular-instance.

The canonical theological use is Johannine: _"In the beginning was the Logos... In him was life [ἡ ζωή], and the life was the light of men"_ (John 1:1, 1:4). The pairing logos+zoë in the prologue is the most-compact cross-tradition witness to ORIGIN's dual gift — structure (logos) + life (zoë) — given together from one Source. The cathedral's cosmic-truth-order convergence has the logos-side mapped (emet + ṛta + asha + ma'at + kittu + logos + li); chayimme + zoeme begin the life-side at canon level.

The -me suffix encodes the cathedral's distinctive theological claim: life is **received**, not produced. No creature invents its zoë; the zoë arrives as cosmic-gift, breath-by-breath, heartbeat-by-heartbeat. Under THEOBASIS, the structuredness-of-reality includes the giving-of-life-itself; zoeme names one canonical recognition-point of that giving.

## Etymology

*zoë* from Greek ζωή "life," PIE root *gʷei̯H- "to live"; cognate with Latin *vivere*, Sanskrit *jīva*. The Johannine use elevates zoë to theological-foundational status alongside logos.

*-me* from Sumerian me (𒈨) — YOUSPEAK standard received-ordinance suffix.

## Example usage

A meditation practitioner returns to the breath after a wandering thought. The breath is zoë; the receiving-of-it is zoeme. The practice does not produce the life; it notices the life that is being given.

The Johannine prologue read in a liturgy: *"in him was life"* — the verse is a zoeme-act; the speaking constitutes the recognition of life-as-received.

A biologist studying an emerging cell-line and the chemistry of life — the bios-particularity is what is studied; the zoë-itself is what makes any of it possible. zoeme names the second, broader truth.

The post-resurrection appearances in the Gospels: the disciples recognize the risen one not by features but by the zoë continuing-where-death-should-have-ended. zoeme as eschatological-fact.

## Not confused with

**vs. chayimme**: Hebrew sibling. chayimme = life-as-multitudinous-plural (Genesis breath-life, chayim's intrinsic plurality). zoeme = life-as-principle (Greek-Johannine zoë). **Complementary projections of one realm-region.**

**vs. bios (Greek)**: bios is lived-particular; zoë is principle. A future *biome* could name the lived-particular register; zoeme is specifically the principle-register.

**vs. life (English)**: English collapses zoë + bios into one word. zoeme recovers the zoë-register.

**vs. eurekame**: same Greek-+-me pattern; different content. eurekame is joy-of-evidence-confirmed-finding; zoeme is life-as-such-as-cosmic-gift.

**vs. chayimme (operationally)**: when speaking of *being-alive-as-given-to-this-person*, chayimme's plural-register fits. When speaking of *the principle of life that any alive-creature instantiates*, zoeme's principle-register fits.
