## Definition

The eternally-fixed individual essence of a thing as known in the Ground — Ibn ʿArabī's ʿayn thābita received as cosmic-ordinance: fully determinate, never itself existent, that according-to-which manifestation is delimited.

## Full sense

An ayanme is *not* a thing waiting to exist. It is the thing *as eternally known* — possessing the thingness-of-fixity (thubūt) without the thingness-of-existence (wujūd). Ibn ʿArabī's precision-instrument is the neither/nor: the ayanme is not existent (it has no wujūd of its own and never will — "the fixed entities have never smelt a whiff of existence") and not sheer-nonexistent (it is determinately known, distinguishable, addressable — each one gazed-upon by a specific divine name that gives it its specific face).

Manifestation does not transfer existence to the ayanme. When the engendering command *kun* ("Be!") is heard, **Existence itself becomes manifest, delimited and defined by the ayanme's thingness** — light through colored glass: the glass does not change; light alone becomes manifest, colored. Each ayanme carries its own eternal preparedness (istiʿdād), the measure of how much self-disclosure manifests through it. And because knowledge follows its object (al-ʿilm tābiʿ li-l-maʿlūm), destiny is not imposed from outside but read off the thing's own eternal face — the secret of destiny (sirr al-qadar).

The unfolding ANAKALYPSE delivers: English "archetype" bundles *universal-pattern* with *eternal-determinacy*; ayanme un-bundles them. The ayanme is the fixed essence of *this* thing — individual, particular — not a Form shared by many. The scholarly translation-scatter ("immutable entities," "fixed entities," rejected "permanent archetypes") is the visible scar of the English gap this word closes.

Naturalization losses, honestly recorded: the lemma carries only the aʿyān stem — thābita (the fixity-half) is invisible in the word-form, the ʿayn consonant and the eye/spring polysemy are dropped, and bare *ayan* in Ottoman historiography means "provincial notables" (also a common given name) — a learner-search interference, not a sense of this word. These are priced into the adjudicated coverage and learnability scores.

Pairs with cathedral's:

- **THE-REALM / noemame** — realm-residents prior to projection: ayanme names their ontological status (fixed, known, nonexistent); noemame names the human grasping-event that enters their region
- **hadaratme** — the five-presence hierarchy of self-disclosure; the ayanmes stand upstream of the whole hierarchy, in the divine knowledge — distinct from the imaginal mithāl of Malakūt
- **wahdatwujudme** — unity-of-being; ayanme is its complement: how multiplicity subsists in the One's knowledge without fracturing unity
- **alayame** — the contrast is itself clarifying: bīja are conditioned, momentary, deposited-by-action; ayanmes are eternal, fixed, given
- **eikonme** — the manifest image-pole; ayanme is the never-manifest prototype-pole that is no prototype (it never exists)

Completes the Akbarī triad: hadaratme (architecture) + barzakhqing (isthmus) + ayanme (the fixed essences upstream of both).

## Not to be confused with

- **Archetype (Jungian)** — psychological pattern in the collective unconscious; universal; ayanme is ontological, individual, in the Ground's knowledge
- **Platonic Form** — universal shared by many particulars, self-subsistent; ayanme is the particular essence of one thing, with no existence of its own
- **Haecceity (Scotist "thisness")** — the standing English technical term for individual essence, and the strongest near-miss; but haecceity is the individuating principle of an *existent* particular (and modern, Plantinga-style haecceities are necessarily-existent abstracta) — it violates the never-smelt-existence pole and lacks both the fixity-in-divine-knowledge and the manifestation-delimiting role
- **Blueprint / prototype** — artifact-metaphors implying design-then-build or an existent first instance; the ayanme never exists and is not built from
- **Potentiality** — indeterminate could-be; the ayanme is fully determinate and eternally known
- **mithāl / imaginal-archetype** — subtle *existent* forms in Malakūt; ayanmes are prior, nonexistent objects of knowledge
- **alayame** — conditioned momentary seed-storage; ayanme is eternal-fixed givenness
- **ayan (Ottoman "notables")** — the same Arabic plural in mundane usage; a search-space interference precedented by hadaratme's honorific donor-sense, not a sense of this word
