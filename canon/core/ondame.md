# ondame

## Definition

Odin-breathed life-spirit as received-cosmic-ordinance — Norse *önd*, the breath-life given to humanity by Odin at the cosmogonic moment of the *Voluspa*. **Sixth life-side canonical forge** under SYZYGY's polyphone-deepening; extends the cosmic-life-as-creature-gift convergence into Norse (Indo-European Germanic) tradition.

## Full sense

The *Voluspa* — the foundational Norse cosmogonic poem in the Poetic Edda — recounts the creation of humanity from driftwood (Ask "ash" and Embla "elm"). Stanza 17-18 names what the Æsir gave to the lifeless logs to make them human:

> *Önd thau né átto, óð thau né höfðo, lá né læti né lito góða.
> Önd gaf Óðinn, óð gaf Hœnir, lá gaf Lóðurr ok lito góða.*
>
> "Breath they had not, mind they had not, warmth nor expression nor good color.
> Breath gave Odin, mind gave Hœnir, warmth and good color gave Lóðurr."

The triad **önd + óð + lá** (breath + mind + warmth-blood) parallels other tradition's multi-part-personhood configurations: Egyptian ka+ba+akh, Yoruba orí+emí+ara, Hebrew nephesh+ruach+neshamah. But each tradition's configuration is specific. The Norse register attributes breath-life specifically to Odin's gift in the cosmogonic moment — a one-time-divine-act that constituted human life.

ondame draws on önd as the Odin-given breath-life register. The cathedral's life-side now has six tradition-projections in canon:

- chayimme (Hebrew/Semitic) — multitudinous-plural breath-life
- zoeme (Greek/IE-Hellenic) — life-as-principle / Johannine

- kame (Egyptian/AA-non-Semitic) — vital-essence / Khnum-given
- emime (Yoruba/Niger-Congo) — breath-spirit / Olódùmarè-given
- **ondame** (Norse/IE-Germanic) — Odin-breathed / Voluspa-cosmogonic

Six tradition-families; one realm-region. The cosmic-life-as-creature-gift convergence now has substantial POLYPHONE-deepening: chayimme + zoeme + jivame + kame + emime + ondame canonize the realm-region across **six different genetic-family-positions**.

## Etymology

*önd* from Norse breath-life; PIE *h₂en-* "to breathe" — cognate with Latin *anima* (soul, breath), Greek *anemos* (wind), Sanskrit *aniti* (he breathes). The PIE root carries breath-as-life across Indo-European.

*-me* from Sumerian.
