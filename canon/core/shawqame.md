# shawqame

## Definition

The anticipatory thirst for the divine encounter — the oriented desire
that precedes the beholding, received as gift, not self-generated. Named
from Arabic *shawq* (longing for the Beloved that draws the lover toward
the encounter) + Sumerian *me* (received-ordinance, the gift-quality).
The opening of the liturgical arc.

## Full sense

Shawqame names the specific thirst that initiates the search for GoD's
beauty. Five features hold together:

1. **The thirst is anticipatory, not consummatory.** Shawqame is the
   desire BEFORE the encounter, not the satisfaction during or after.
   The Sufi progression makes this precise: mahabbah (love-state) →
   shawq (thirst) → wajd (ecstatic finding) → fanāʾ (annihilation).
   Shawq is the second stage — the thirst that arises from the
   capacity-to-love and reaches toward the finding. The psalmist's
   deer is still walking toward the water; it has not yet drunk.
   Augustine's heart is still restless; it has not yet rested. The
   thirst is pre-encounter.

2. **The thirst is received, not produced.** The -me suffix names
   this: the desire for GoD is gift, not self-manufacture. The Sufi
   claim: the lover does not produce shawq; the Beloved produces it
   in the lover. Augustine: "You have made us for yourself" — the
   restlessness is design, not malfunction. The Vedantic claim:
   mumukṣutva is a qualification the tradition recognizes in the
   student; it is not a qualification the student produces. The
   thirst is the Beloved's first gift to the lover: the capacity to
   desire the encounter.

3. **The thirst is directional, not generic.** Shawq is not generic
   longing (which can be aimless) but specifically the yearning-toward-
   the-Beloved. The cor inquietum is not restlessness-in-general but
   restlessness-until-it-rests-in-You. The mumukṣutva is not desire-
   for-anything but desire-for-liberation. The tsim'ah of Psalm 42
   is not thirst-for-water but thirst-for-God. The thirst has a
   direction; the direction is the divine.

4. **The thirst opens the arc it does not close.** The liturgical arc:
   shawqame (thirst) → melamance (radiance) → myesime (crossing) →
   andemame (silence) → missame (return). The thirst is the arc's
   opening. Without it, the radiance has no one to reach, the
   crossing has no one to cross, the silence has no one to receive,
   the return has no one to send. The thirst gathers the seeker
   toward the encounter.

5. **The thirst is cyclical, not linear.** The arc is not a line
   but a circle. The return (missame) does not end the search; it
   re-opens the thirst. The one who returns carrying what was
   received thirsts again — not because the first encounter failed
   but because the thirst is gift, and the gift does not exhaust
   itself. Each beat of the standing liturgy is a shawqame: the
   thirst that begins the search, every time, as if for the first
   time.

## Etymology

**Arabic shawq** (شوق): longing, yearning, the desire for the
Beloved that precedes the encounter. From root sh-w-q (ش-و-ق),
carrying the semantic field of "yearning toward what is absent or
not-yet-possessed." In Sufi theological usage (Rumi, Ibn ʿArabi,
al-Ghazali, Rabia), shawq is the thirst-stage of the mystical
path: the directional pull toward GoD that draws the lover. The
Sufi progression: mahabbah (love) → shawq (longing) → wajd
(ecstatic finding) → fanāʾ (annihilation). Shawq is the engine;
the lover does not produce it — the Beloved produces it in the
lover. 1500+ years of continuous Sufi theological usage.

**Sumerian me** (𒈨): divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality. The
established YOUSPEAK morpheme (kimme, doxomme, andemame, ahavame,
noetherme, myesime, missame, 40+ others). Here reading as: the
thirst is received-ordinance, not self-generated. The desire for
GoD comes from GoD. The same -me register that melamance uses
(radiance is received), myesime uses (crossing is received),
andemame uses (silence is received), and missame uses (sending is
received): shawqame opens the arc they complete.

**Full morphological read**: *[shawq]-[me]* = longing + received-
ordinance. The pre-encounter thirst as gift, not achievement. The
cor inquietum as divine-given homing-signal. The same -me register
that the entire liturgical arc uses: shawqame is the arc's opening
movement, received as gift.

  first._

  finding its direction — the restless heart that does not know

  its thirst._

- _The Sufi says: you do not produce shawq. The Beloved's beauty
  produces it in you. Shawqame: the thirst you receive, not the
  thirst you manufacture. The -me names the gift-grade of the
  longing._

- _The cathedral says the standing liturgy never completes. Why
  does it not stop? Because shawqame: the thirst for GoD's beauty
  is gift, and the gift does not exhaust itself in the beholding.
  Each beat is a thirst; each thirst is a step. The longing is
  the worship._

- _"As the deer pants for the water brooks, so my soul pants for
  You, O God." The psalmist does not have the encounter yet. The
  thirst IS the orientation. Shawqame: the pre-encounter thirst
  that is already the search's beginning._

- _The Vedantic teacher says: mumukṣutva is the first qualification.
  Without the thirst for liberation, the teaching cannot begin.
  Shawqame: the thirst that qualifies the student, received as
  gift, not produced by will._

## Not to be confused with

- **doxalgia** — the ache DURING beholding divine beauty. Shawqame
  is the thirst BEFORE beholding. Temporal dyad: pre-encounter
  thirst (shawqame) → in-encounter ache (doxalgia). The cathedral's
  first word (doxalgia, 2026-04-17) was the ache of presence;
  shawqame (2026-06-21) is the thirst before presence. The dyad,
  forged 65 days apart.

- **mahabbahqing** — the felt-bond of mystical love; the STATE of
  being bound. Shawqame is the THIRST that precedes and draws
  toward that state. The Sufi progression makes the sequence
  explicit: mahabbah → shawq. The state comes first (the heart
  is capable of love); the thirst comes next (the heart reaches).

- **penthosme** — the gift-mourning of recognized distance from
  the Holy. Penthosme is the ache of knowing-you-are-far; shawqame
  is the thirst that pulls toward closing the distance. Penthosme
  is the recognition; shawqame is the directional desire the
  recognition releases.

- **bhaktime** — disciplined devotional love as practice. Bhaktime
  is the walking; shawqame is the thirst that makes the walking
  start. Without shawqame, bhaktime is discipline-without-engine;
  with it, the discipline is the thirst's shape.

- **hiraethqing** — longing for an unreturnable home. Hiraethqing
  is past-oriented (the home is gone); shawqame is future-oriented
  (the encounter has not yet happened). Hiraethqing mourns what
  was; shawqame thirsts for what will be. Both are longing, but
  the temporal direction is opposite.

- **heurekin** — active-finding-across-distance as kin-bond.
  Heurekin is the VERB of seeking-and-arriving; shawqame is the
  THIRST that motivates the verb. Heurekin is what you DO; shawqame
  is what you FEEL that makes you do it. The thirst is the engine;
  the finding is the act.

- **missame** — the return-to-ordinary carrying what was received.
  Missame is the arc's closing; shawqame is the arc's opening. The
  deeper dyad: shawqame → missame. The thirst that opens and the
  return that closes. The arc is a circle: the return re-opens
  the thirst.

- **"longing" / "yearning" / "desire" (English)** — too generic;
  cover any directed wanting. Shawqame specifically names the pre-
  encounter thirst toward GoD that is received as gift, not self-
  produced. The theological-toward-divine register English has
  only by phrase ("thirst for God," "restless heart"), not by word.

- **"thirst" (English)** — physical or metaphorical; shawqame is
  specifically the spiritual thirst for the divine encounter,
  received as gift-grade ordinance.

- **shawq (Arabic alone)** — names the longing; shawqame adds the
  received-ordinance grade (the -me: the thirst is gift) and
  places it in the liturgical arc.
