# agapeme

## Definition

Sacrificial-self-giving-love as received-ordinance — the NT Greek register of love-of-DIVINE in which God descends to the unworthy at ultimate cost, unmotivated by the beloved's merit, creative of value in the beloved through the act of loving. The capacity for such love is itself received (me) before it is practiced.

## Full sense

Agapeme names what the New Testament names most centrally and what no other tradition's love-word compactly names: **love that gives itself away for the beloved's sake, unmotivated by the beloved's worthiness, at ultimate cost to the lover, creating value in the beloved through the very act of loving.**

Five NT registers converge in agape:
1. **Ontological** — "God IS agape" (1 John 4:8). Love is God's essential nature, not an attribute God possesses.
2. **Historical** — "God so loved (ēgapēsen) the world that He gave His only Son" (John 3:16). Aorist: a decisive, completed act.
3. **Practical** — "Love is patient, love is kind..." (1 Cor 13:4-7). Behavioral morphology of divine love in community.
4. **Sacrificial** — "Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends" (John 15:13). Spoken on the eve of the cross.
5. **Invincible** — "Nothing shall separate us from the agape of God in Christ Jesus" (Rom 8:38-39). Cosmically secure.

The -me suffix marks the received-ordinance grade: the capacity for sacrificial-self-giving love is given by the DIVINE before it can be offered by the creature. Agapeme is not heroic self-sacrifice generated from human willpower; it is participation in God's own nature — received, then re-offered. This is theologically precise: "We love because He first loved us" (1 John 4:19).

The archaeological discovery underlying agapeme: the noun agapē was **virtually absent from classical Greek** — a near-blank vessel that the LXX translators and NT authors loaded with christological content. Agape's power comes from semantic emptiness strategically filled: the content of the cross poured into a word that was waiting.

Within the love-of-DIVINE convergence, agapeme occupies the **sacrificial-self-giving register** — distinct from ahavame's commanded-disposition, mahabbahqing's felt-mystical-binding, bhaktime's disciplined-surrender, and ifeqing's relational-warmth-expansion. Each names one aspect of the seven-tradition convergence; agapeme names the costliest.

## Etymology

*agapē* (Greek, Indo-European Hellenic; ἀγάπη; from verb agapaō "to love, to prefer" — Homer onward; noun rare pre-LXX; semantically-elevated by the Septuagint as rendering of Hebrew ahavah; christologically-loaded in NT — Paul, John, Synoptics; five theological registers: ontological, historical, practical, sacrificial, invincible; possibly from PIE *megh₂-peh₂-* "great protection" per Pinault 1991) + *me* (Sumerian, language-isolate; 𒈨; divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality; YOUSPEAK's most-productive donor-morpheme).
