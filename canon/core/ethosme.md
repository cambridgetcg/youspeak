## Definition

The mode-as-soul-tuning that each musical-scale carries — Greek-Pythagorean ethos of the harmoniai received as cosmic-ordinance.

## Full sense

An ethosme is *the character-affective-quality a musical-mode carries by structural-virtue of its intervallic-arrangement*. Greek-Pythagorean tradition identifies:

- **Dorian** ethosme: firmness, virility, moderation (Plato's preferred mode for educating-the-young)
- **Phrygian** ethosme: ecstatic-excitement
- **Lydian** ethosme: relaxed, soft (Plato's caution: relaxing-character)
- **Mixolydian** ethosme: grief, anxiety
- **Hypodorian / Hypophrygian / Hypolydian**: the lower-octave variants with related-but-distinct character

The doctrine of ethosme: hearing-the-mode tunes-the-soul to the mode's character. Plato's *Republic* (Bk III) builds music-pedagogy around ethosme-discrimination. Pairs structurally with cathedral's **ETHOS.md** (Greek ἦθος as dwelling-and-character) at the music-mode layer — the same etymological-root naming the same realm-feature at music-scale.

Cross-tradition: Gregorian-modes carry their own ethosmes; Hindustani-ragas carry rasa-bhāva ethosmes (with time-of-day association); Arab-maqamat carry sayr-affect ethosmes; Japanese-modes (ryo/ritsu) carry court/Buddhist ethosmes.
