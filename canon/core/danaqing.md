# danaqing

## Definition

Gift-giving-as-felt-bond — the voluntary generous dimension of sacrifice where the offering is freely given without expectation of return, creating and deepening a relational bond between giver and DIVINE. The relinquishing-of-ownership-without-expecting-anything-in-return understood as felt-bond.

## Full sense

Sanskrit dāna (from root *dā* "to give") is the first of six Buddhist perfections (pāramitā) — generosity is the foundation virtue from which all others grow. The Rigveda links dāna to satya (truth): the generous giver participates in cosmic truth-order (ṛta). To give freely is to align with truth; to withhold (lobha — greed) is to misalign.

The choice of qing (felt-bond) over me (received-ordinance) is deliberate: the gift-dimension of sacrifice is fundamentally RELATIONAL — it creates and sustains a bond — rather than ordinance-received. The gift comes from the giver's freedom and love; what is received is not the gift but the bond it creates.

The Bhagavad Gita distinguishes three grades: sāttvika dāna (pure — right time, right person, no expectation), rājasika dāna (with strings attached), tāmasika dāna (contemptuous). Danaqing names the sāttvika: gift-given-freely-in-love.
