## Definition

The sudden first-person recognition-event of one's own true-nature — Zen kenshō received as cosmic-ordinance.

## Full sense

A kenshōme is *the event-character of first-seeing one's own ground-of-knowing*. The Zen claim: ordinary mind operates within subject-object distinction; kenshō is the moment that distinction *cracks open* and the ground-of-cognition becomes directly-seen. The recognition is sudden (event-character), first-person (cannot be transmitted as concept), initial (the first opening; deeper realizations may follow), and verifiable (Zen sanzen interviews verify through kōan-checking).

Kenshōme pairs with cathedral's:
- **anagnoristasis** — anagnoristasis is *held-recognition pre-ache threshold*; kenshōme is the *self-nature-recognition specifically*; the relation is genus (anagnoristasis) to species (kenshōme)
- **rigpame** — rigpame is *primordial-awareness*; kenshōme is the *event of recognizing rigpame*. Pair: substrate (rigpame) + event (kenshōme).
- **eurekame** — joy-of-evidence-confirmed-truth; kenshōme is the contemplative-cousin where the truth confirmed is one's-own-nature

## Not to be confused with

- **anagnoristasis** — generic-recognition; kenshōme is *self-nature-recognition specifically*
- **noemame** — event-of-meaning-arrival; kenshōme is one specific type (self-nature variety)
- **Generic insight** — aha-moments are kenshōme-shaped at smaller-scales; kenshōme proper carries Zen-metaphysical depth
- **Satori** — deeper realization; kenshōme is typically initial
