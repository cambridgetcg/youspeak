# daigome

## Definition

Great-awakening-as-received-ordinance — the apex of the Zen deepening-arc that consummates the series of satoris into a totality, recognizing the deepening as always-already-complete.

## Full sense

**Daigome** names the event that Zen calls daigo (大悟): great-awakening — the most comprehensive realization in the Zen deepening-arc. It is the apex of the sequence kenshō → satori → daigo: kenshōme is the seed (the initial-seeing), satorime is the growing (the deepening-awakening), daigome is the fruit (the consummation that completes the arc).

Five structural marks:

1. **Comprehensive** — daigome is not satori-quantitatively-larger; it is satori-qualitatively-transformed. The distinction is between accumulation and integration. One can have many satoris (the Rinzai tradition speaks of "one hundred satoris"); daigome is the awakening in which the many become one, the series becomes a totality, the deepening-arc recognizes itself as an arc.

2. **Non-dual** — daigome marks the collapse of the subject-object structure that even satori may implicitly carry. In kenshō, there is a seer and a seen. In satori, there is a transformed seer and a transformed seen. In daigo, the seer-seen distinction itself drops away — not as a philosophical claim but as a lived-structure. Dōgen's "body and mind dropped away" (身心脱落 shinjin datsuraku) reaches its fullest expression in daigome.

3. **Consummating** — satori is repeatable (a deepening series). Daigome is the consummation: not the last in a series but the end of the series-as-series. After daigome, there is no further deepening — not because awakening is exhausted but because the structure of "deepening" has been recognized as what it always was: the path arriving at where it never left.

4. **Received** — like kenshō and satori, daigome is received, not produced. The -me marks this: the great-awakening is given, not manufactured. Hakuin: "the great ball of doubt shatters" — the practitioner does not shatter it; the doubt shatters itself, and what remains is daigo. One prepares the conditions; the fruit ripens on its own. Daigome is the fruit at its ripest.

5. **Verifiable** — daigome is verified through the sanzen process: the teacher recognizes not just an opening (kenshō) or a deepening (satori) but a consummation. The Zen tradition's insistence on verification is structurally unique — it makes the contemplative event accountable to a community of practice. Daigome is verification at its most comprehensive.

### The Zen deepening-sequence (COMPLETE)

The cathedral now holds all three stones of the Zen deepening-arc:

- **kenshōme** (initial-seeing-as-ordinance, Core 8.10, 2026-05-13) — the seed: the first flash of recognizing one's true-nature
- **satorime** (sudden-awakening-as-ordinance, Core 8.40, 2026-07-12) — the growing: the fire that catches, the deepening that stabilizes and transforms the initial-seeing
- **daigome** (great-awakening-as-ordinance, Core 8.10, 2026-07-13) — the fruit: the consummation that ends the series-as-series, the awakening that recognizes the deepening as always-already-complete

The arc is Hakuin's structural contribution to Zen taxonomy. Each stage is sudden, received, irreversible, verifiable. The distinction is not between three different kinds of experience but between three structural positions in the same deepening: initial-seeing → deepening → consummation. Seed → growing → fruit.

### The satori-rigpame-āśraya-parāvṛtti triad + daigome

The triad (completed 2026-07-12) named the same structural-transformation from three angles: satorime (Zen phenomenological — the event), rigpame (Dzogchen result-oriented — the state one rests in), asraya-paravrttime (Yogācāra ontological — the basis transforms). Daigome extends the Zen angle by naming the APEX of the event: not just the awakening-event (satorime) but its consummation (daigome). The triad's three faces are now joined by a fourth: the apex-of-the-event, which is the Zen face of what Dzogchen calls rdzogs pa chen po (the great perfection) and what the Christian tradition calls theōsis (deification).

### Cross-tradition parallels

1. **Zen daigo** (大悟) — great-awakening as apex of the kenshō → satori → daigo arc
2. **Theravada sammā-sambodhi** — the Buddha's complete-perfect-awakening
3. **Advaita brahmajñāna** — knowledge-of-brahman as the non-dual apex-recognition
4. **Dzogchen rdzogs pa chen po** — "the great perfection"; ground, path, and fruit are identical
5. **Sufi al-fanāʾ al-mutlaq** — "absolute passing-away"; apex of the maqamat-path
6. **Christian theōsis** — deification; "God became human so that humans might become God"
7. **Kabbalistic dvekut bemitasev yaḥid** — "cleaving in the unification"; apex of the Kabbalistic path

## Example usage

After years of practice and many satoris, the arc consummated — daigome: the great-awakening arrived as received-ordinance, and the seer-seen distinction that had structured every prior experience dropped away, not through effort but through recognition that it was never there.

The kenshō was the seed, the satoris were the growing, daigome is the fruit — the great-awakening that completes the arc and recognizes the deepening as always-already-there; one prepares the conditions, but the fruit ripens on its own.

## Not to be confused with

- **kenshōme** — initial-seeing-as-ordinance. The seed; daigome is the fruit. Sequential, not synonymous.
- **satorime** — sudden-awakening-as-ordinance. The deepening; daigome is the consummation. Satori is repeatable (a series); daigo ends the series-as-series.
- **rigpame** — primordial-awareness-as-ordinance. The result-state one rests in; daigome is the apex-event that consummates the arc. State vs. event.
- **asraya-paravrttime** — transformation-of-the-basis. The Yogācāra ontological description; daigome is the Zen phenomenological apex. Same event, different angles.
- **meditame** — meditation-as-ordinance. The practice; daigome is the consummation the practice serves.
- **śūnyatame** — emptiness-as-ordinance. The ontological ground; daigome is the awakening that recognizes the ground as already-realized. Ground vs. recognition-of-ground.

## Retirement conditions

Daigome would be superseded if a better-compounded word from a more diverse donor-pair named the same concept with greater clarity. It would also be superseded if the Zen tradition's own vocabulary were found to have a more precise term for the apex-consummation. The -me marking could be replaced if a different morpheme were found that better captures the received-ordinance character. The word would also be retired if the Zen tradition's living custodians requested withdrawal (protocol-sensitivity for living traditions).
