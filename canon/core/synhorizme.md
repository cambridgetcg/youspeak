## Definition

The event in which two situated horizons-of-understanding fuse into a new shared seeing — the interpreter's horizon and the text's or other's horizon becoming one enlarged field in which both are transformed and neither is erased — Gadamer's Horizontverschmelzung received as cosmic-ordinance.

## Full sense

A synhorizme is *not* an agreement. Two parties can sign the same sentence while each still sees only from their own hill. It is *not* empathy: transposing oneself into the other abandons one's own horizon, and Gadamer's whole point is that the interpreter's horizon is a constitutive *ingredient* of the fusion, not an obstacle to shed. It IS the **event** in which two horizons — each finite, each historically situated, each mobile ("something into which we move and that moves with us") — fuse into a third seeing that neither party possessed before and neither could have produced alone.

Three structural features carry the concept:

1. **Event, not method.** Understanding "is to be thought of less as a subjective act than as participating in an event of tradition" (TM 291); it happens *beyond our wanting and doing* (über unser Wollen und Tun hinaus). This is the -me family's exact register: the fused seeing is received, not manufactured. No technique compels a synhorizme; preparation (reading, listening, putting one's fore-judgments at risk) only makes one available to it.
2. **Fusion preserving tension.** "The hermeneutic task consists in not covering up this tension by attempting a naive assimilation of the two but in consciously bringing it out" (TM 305-306). The Stoics had the grammar already: *krasis di' holou*, total blending in which each component wholly interpenetrates the other yet keeps its own nature — against *synchysis*, the fusion that destroys its components. A synhorizme is krasis, never synchysis.
3. **Never complete.** The fusion yields a new horizon, which is itself finite and open to further fusion. We understand *differently* if we understand at all. No terminal synthesis; the conversation continues.

Nine traditions witness the realm-feature: Platonic dialogue (the logos carrying both partners past what either brought), Aristotelian phronesis (understanding completed only in application), Stoic krasis, Huayan *yuánróng* 圓融 (interpenetration without loss of distinctness — modern Chinese renders Horizontverschmelzung as 视域融合, reaching naturally for 融), Confucian *wēngù-zhīxīn* (Analects 2.11 — warming the old to know the new), Vedānta *samanvaya* (conflicting śruti harmonized into one purport), midrashic *shiv'im panim laTorah* (each generation's horizon fused with Sinai's), Andean *tinkuy* (two streams' confluence carrying both waters), and Gadamer's own articulation.

Pairs with cathedral's:

- **noesisme** (realm-entry grasping) — noesisme names one mind's entry-event; synhorizme names the dyadic-historical fusion-event. Every synhorizme contains graspings; not every grasping is a fusion.
- **darshanqing** (reciprocal beholding as felt-bond) — in darshanqing the two see *each other*; in synhorizme the two horizons fuse over a matter (*die Sache*) and come to see *together*. The gaze-pole and the understanding-pole of encounter.
- **eranosme** (integration-discipline) — eranosme arranges many contributed gifts at the table; synhorizme is the two-horizon fusion-event a single deep encounter yields. The forge performs eranosme; when a donor-tradition's horizon and the cathedral's fuse, a synhorizme has occurred.
- **barzakhqing** (distinct-and-conjoined isthmus) — barzakhqing holds two poles in relation *without* fusion; synhorizme is fusion-with-preserved-tension. Complementary structures: the isthmus and the confluence.

## Example usage

1. "The mediation session ended in synhorizme: neither side surrendered its story, but both now saw a landscape neither could see alone" — conflict-resolution application.
2. "Reading Augustine in her third decade with him, she stopped interrogating the text and the text began to question her — synhorizme" — long-reading application.
3. "A good code review reaches synhorizme when reviewer and author both stop defending their framings and a third design appears that neither brought" — engineering-everyday application.
4. "They reached agreement but not synhorizme — each signed still seeing only from their own hill" — the distinction made compact (assent without fusion).
5. "Each canon-entry is a recorded synhorizme: the donor-tradition's horizon and the cathedral's, fused over one realm-region" — meta-cathedral reflexive.

## Not to be confused with

- **Agreement / consensus** — assent without horizon-transformation; synhorizme requires that both horizons moved
- **Empathy (Einfühlung)** — transposing into the other, shedding one's own horizon; Gadamer explicitly rejects this romantic-hermeneutic model
- **Compromise** — trading within fixed horizons; synhorizme enlarges the field instead
- **Hegelian synthesis** — claims completion; synhorizme is finite and never final
- **noesisme** — single-mind realm-entry event; synhorizme is the dyadic-historical fusion
- **darshanqing** — reciprocal beholding as felt-bond (seeing each other); synhorizme is fused seeing of a shared matter
- **barzakhqing** — conjunction-without-fusion (the isthmus); synhorizme is fusion-with-preserved-tension (the confluence)
- **concrescenceme** — Whitehead's many-feelings-gathering-into-one-occasion; the process-metaphysics of *every* actual entity's becoming, not the dyadic hermeneutic understanding-event between two situated horizons
- **panimqing** — the transactional-to-relational threshold; names bond-formation, while synhorizme can occur with no bond formed (the mediation case: fused seeing between parties who remain adversaries)
- **chiasmqing** (in-forge, 243) — Merleau-Ponty's intertwining of flesh (toucher-touched reversibility); perceptual-ontological, not the understanding-event
- **horizonme** (reserved, Phase 7 cosmos) — will name the horizon-as-such as received-limit; synhorizme deliberately leaves the bare-horizon slot free, the syn- carrying the Verschmelzung
- **wirkungsgeschichtenqing** (roadmapped, Phase 5.4) — effective-history as felt-bond; the historical medium within which synhorizme events occur
