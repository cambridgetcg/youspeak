# sankofame

## Definition

The going-back-that-fetches as received-ordinance — the return along one's own past, made not to dwell there but to retrieve a specific dropped thing and carry it forward, so that the future is fed by what the past still holds. The retrieval is decreed before it is taken: *it is not taboo to go back for that which you have forgotten.*

## Full sense

Sankofame names what the Akan Adinkra tradition compressed into a single bird: **the feet face forward, the gaze faces back, and the beak holds the egg.** Going back, in sankofame, is a species of forward motion — the traveler returns to the archive, the grandmother, the abandoned draft, the shamed-out language, takes the one thing that was dropped, and walks on. The egg is for hatching.

Five features converge in the act:

1. **Backward in direction** — the traveler goes toward the past.
2. **Forward in purpose** — the going-back exists for what comes next; the feet never turn.
3. **Specific in object** — one egg, not the past wholesale: a recipe, a name, a craft, a discarded design, a word.
4. **Ordained, not indulged** — the proverb's register is decree: *yenkyiri*, "we do not taboo it." The return is sanctioned before it is taken.
5. **Bounded by ownership** — what is fetched was yours and was lost. The bird returns along its own track.

The -me suffix marks the received-ordinance grade, and here the weld runs deeper than register: in the Sumerian donor mythology the me are themselves *fetched* — Inanna sails to Eridu, receives the ordinances from Enki, and carries them home to Uruk in the Boat of Heaven so her city's future can be fed. The grandmother's own myth performs the grandchild's meaning. What feeds the future is obtained by going back and carrying it home.

Within the cathedral's memory-community, sankofame is the **fetching-pole**: zakarqing holds the covenant across time from where it stands; sankofame travels back along time to retrieve what was dropped — the holding-pole and the fetching-pole of faithful memory. Teshuvance turns the whole self back toward GoD; sankofame is the errand run on that turned road — teshuvance is the turning, sankofame the fetching. Silame warns "listen to the weather, do not loot it" — sankofame fetches only what was yours and was lost, never what was another's and is coveted.

## Etymology

*sankofa* (Akan/Twi, Niger-Congo > Kwa; *san* "return" + *kɔ* "go" + *fa* "take, fetch" — a serial-verb construction whose morphology enacts the act it names, step by step; Adinkra symbol attested in two visual forms, stylized heart and backward-looking bird with egg; stamped on cloth from at least 1817 — the Bowdich cloth, British Museum Af1818-1114-23, oldest surviving adinkra; in the diaspora the central symbol of cultural reclamation after the Middle Passage) + *me* (Sumerian, language-isolate; 𒈨; divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality; YOUSPEAK's most-productive donor-morpheme).

## Not confused with

- **Nostalgia** (English, from Greek nostos + algos) — the ache of return with no fetch and no forward clause; goes back and stays
- **Retrieval / recovery** (English) — mechanical; no ordinance-grade, no inheritance-register
- **Reclamation** (English) — legal-colonial freight; centers ownership rather than the feeding of the future
- **Heritage** (English) — a possession-noun, not an act
- **Sankofa** (Akan alone) — names the symbol and the value; sankofame adds the decree-grade, the canon-integration, and the family-discipline boundaries
