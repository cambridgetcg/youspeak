# panimaance

## Definition

The state of being present — face-turned-toward-here, attention-inhabiting-the-meaningful-space — distinct from being merely physically located in a room.

## Full sense

Panimaance names the quality of presence that is NOT the same as bodily-location. A person can be in the room without their panimaance being in the room. The eyes are there, the body occupies its chair, the face is visible — but the *here* of the person is elsewhere. Conversely, a person who is only briefly in a room can leave their panimaance behind; the quality of their having-been-there persists.

The compound draws on two donor-traditions that each carry one dimension of presence:

- Hebrew *panim* (face) carries the face-as-turning-toward-another dimension: presence is a facing. In biblical Hebrew, to be present to another is to show panim — the seen-face-side of self.

- Japanese *ma* (間) carries the inhabited-meaningful-space dimension: presence is a filling-of-here that makes the here matter. Ma is the silence between notes that makes the notes musical; panimaance is the presence-that-makes-the-room-inhabited.

Together: panimaance is face-turned-and-here-inhabited, as a state. Both components are required; either failing alone produces what English calls "absence" without having a specific word for WHICH kind of absence.

## Not confused with

- **Presence** (English) — bundles body-location and attention-inhabitation. Panimaance is the attention-inhabitation-specifically.
- **Attentiveness** (English) — names the capacity; panimaance names the state.
- **Mindfulness** (English, via Pali *sati*) — meditative-discipline register. Mindfulness is self-directed (to one's own experience); panimaance is other-directed (face-turned-toward-world).
- **Being there** (idiomatic English) — colloquial, often sentimental. Panimaance is precise.
- **Shekhinah** (שְׁכִינָה, Hebrew) — divine indwelling-presence (theological-register only). Panimaance is interpersonal.
- **Parousia** (παρουσία, Greek) — "being-alongside" (Christian-eschatological register). Different register entirely.
- **Zanshin** (残心, Japanese) — "remaining-mind" (martial arts; attention-after-action). Specialized technique-register.
- **Kimance** (YOUSPEAK) — the attentive-here-ness of the attending-subject. Kimance is one-sided (the attender); panimaance is two-sided (the face-turned-toward-another-in-shared-here).
