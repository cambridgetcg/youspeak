# oriance

## Definition

The state of a life aligned with its destined shape — the felt-fit of who-one-is-now with who-one-was-for.

## Full sense

Oriance names the ongoing quality of a life whose present-form fits its underlying trajectory. It is not fulfillment (a completion-register), not self-actualization (a Maslowian achievement-register), not calling (a received-from-outside register). Oriance is the felt-register of alignment between the person-in-time and the deep-shape the person was-for.

The Yoruba *orí* (destined-head, inner-head) names the pre-chosen trajectory of self — in Yoruba metaphysics, chosen before birth in meeting with Olódùmarè (the creator); whether one accepts the metaphysics or not, the word names a shape English lacks a compact term for: the constitutive-shape-of-a-life that is neither imposed-by-fate nor willed-by-ego but carried-as-core.

Oriance is the state in which that shape is lived. The surgeon's steady hands. The teacher's first-day-back ease. The musician's bone-deep refusal to give up the sound. Every adult has met oriance — in themselves, or in someone they have watched come into it.

## Etymology

*orí* (Yoruba, West African Niger-Congo) — destined-head, inner-head, pre-chosen trajectory of self + *-ance* (Latin-derived state-quality suffix, established in YOUSPEAK). ## Example usage

- "She taught for thirty years; she moved to administration for five; she went back to the classroom and within a week oriance settled on her."
- "He is successful but not in oriance. The life is large; the fit is poor."
- "Not every good marriage is oriance; some are good partnership with a person who is not your shape. Both are honorable, but they are different states."
- "The young often mistake intensity for oriance. Intensity can hide a misfit; oriance is quieter than that."
- "Oriance is not a feeling; it is the state within which certain feelings become possible."

## Not confused with

- **Fulfilled** (English) — completion-register; generic across any satisfied-life.
- **Self-actualized** (English, via Maslow) — achievement-hierarchy register; oriance is not up the pyramid, it is fit with the pyramid's shape.
- **Called** (English, from Latin *vocatus*) — externally-received register; oriance is constitutive, not received.
- **In one's element** (English idiom) — momentary-scale; oriance is life-scale.
- **On purpose** (English) — will-register; oriance is pre-will.
- **Flow** (English, Csikszentmihalyi) — state of effortless-engagement in task; oriance is a life-structural register, not task-register.
- **Authentic self** (English, pop-psychology) — closest approach in English; oriance adds the trajectory-through-time dimension that "authentic" tends to flatten to a moment.
