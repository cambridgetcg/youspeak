# shemme

## Definition

The state of receptive-hearing as a received gift; the listening that is already the beginning of being-shaped by what is heard.

## Full sense

Shemme names what English splits across "hear / listen / heed / obey." When you truly hear something — a piece of news that re-orients your life, a wisdom-saying from an elder, a teaching that lodges in the bones, a music that unmakes you — your listening is not a neutral act of perception. The hearing is itself the beginning of being-shaped. The receptivity is pre-volitional. You did not decide to be moved; you heard, and the moving had already begun.

The Hebrew Shema (*šǝmaʿ Yisrael*, "Hear, O Israel," Deuteronomy 6:4) names this register. So does the Arabic *samiʿan wa ṭāʿatan* ("hearing and obeying" as one act). So does the Sumerian-Akkadian *šēmu*-tradition that is the deepest-attested Semitic root of this phenomenon. So does the Buddhist *śrāvaka* (the hearer-disciple), so does the Sufi *samāʿ* (ritual listening), so does Jesus's "he who has ears, let him hear."

Seven traditions name the same phenomenon. None has a compact word in modern English. *Shemme* compact-names it.

The compound draws on:
- **Akkadian šēmu** (the verbal stem) — to hear-listen-obey as one fused act; ~4500 years of textual attestation; the Semitic-foundational form
- **Sumerian me** (already established YOUSPEAK morpheme) — received-ordinance, divine-gift-quality

Together: hearing-as-received-ordinance. The receptive-listening that is itself a gift, that is itself a posture-toward-shaping.

## Etymology

*šēmu* (Akkadian, East Semitic; ~2400 BCE onward; attested in cuneiform across Akkadian-Empire, Old Babylonian, Standard Babylonian, Neo-Assyrian dialects) + *me* (Sumerian, language-isolate; ~3500 BCE onward; the divine-ordinance-quality morpheme). ## Not confused with

- **Listen / listening** (English) — too general; does not carry receptivity-as-shaping.
- **Hear / hearing** (English) — auditory perception only; lacks the obedience-implication.
- **Heed / heeding** (English) — captures the response-after-hearing but not the fused-as-one-act register.
- **Obey / obedience** (English) — captures the active-response but not the receptive-listening that precedes it.
- **Mindfulness** (English, via Pali sati) — self-directed attention to one's own experience; shemme is other-directed (toward what one hears).
- **Kimance** (YOUSPEAK Core) — visual/attentional attentive-here-ness; shemme is auditory-receptivity. Sister-register.
- **Kimme** (YOUSPEAK Core) — attention-as-received-ordinance; shemme is hearing-as-received-ordinance. -me-family sibling, different sense-mode.
- **Sati / satance** (Pali / YOUSPEAK refine) — meditative remembering; shemme does not require meditation-context.
- **Shema** (Hebrew) — proper noun for the Deuteronomy 6 confession; shemme is the general register that the Shema-call invokes, available without specific tradition-commitment.
