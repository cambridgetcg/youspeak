## Definition

The sacramental-gift of compunction-mourning that cleanses and joins joy-with-sorrow — Hesychast penthos (Climacus's charmolypē) received as cosmic-ordinance.

## Full sense

A penthosme is *the gift-felt-mourning-before-the-Holy that opens the heart to grace*. Climacus articulated it as the seventh-rung of the *Ladder of Divine Ascent*: a disciplined-mourning that "constructs the entire structure of the spiritual life." The mourner is *cleansed* by penthos, not destroyed. The tears of penthos are tears-of-grace.

Three aspects:
- **Joyful-sorrow** (χαρμολύπη charmolypē, Climacus's coinage) — joy and grief paradoxically-fused; the gift-character
- **Compunction** (κατάνυξις katanyxis) — the puncture-of-heart that initiates penthos
- **Tears of grace** — physical-tears as embodied-sign of inward-cleansing

penthosme is *structurally-distinct* from:
- Ordinary-grief over loss (psychological-natural; penthos is gift-supernatural)
- Depression (penthos is cleansing; depression is heavy-stuck)
- Religious-guilt (penthos is gift-of-recognizing-distance; guilt is often punitive-self-judgment)

Pairs with cathedral's:
- **doxalgia** — ache-at-beholding-doxakallos; structurally-related but doxalgia is *before-beauty*; penthosme is *before-holiness as recognition-of-distance*
- **karuṇa-rasame** — compassionate-sorrow aesthetic; Indian-cousin without the Christian-sacramental specificity
- **nepsisme** (sibling Hesychast forge) — nepsis-watchfulness creates conditions for penthos-mourning; the pair operates together

## Not to be confused with

- **Grief** — natural-psychological response to loss; penthosme is sacramental-gift
- **Depression** — heavy-stuck affect; penthosme is cleansing-mobile
- **Guilt** — often punitive; penthosme is gift-of-grace
- **Sentimentality** — surface-tears without depth; penthosme requires the joyful-sorrow paradox
