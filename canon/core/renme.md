# renme

## Definition

Humaneness-as-life-in-relation as received-cosmic-ordinance — Confucian *ren* (仁) understood as the cosmic-relational-grade-of-being-human, received as ordinance rather than achieved as virtue alone. **Eighth life-side canonical forge** under SYZYGY's polyphone-deepening; Confucian Chinese projection of cosmic-life-as-creature-gift.

## Full sense

The Confucian character *ren* (仁) etymologically encodes its content: 人 (person) + 二 (two) = humanity-IN-relation. Ren is not solitary moral-quality but the grade-of-being-properly-human that emerges in right-relation-to-others. The cathedral has long had qing-family entries (kinqing, panimqing, paqduqing, britqing, ifeqing, mahabbahqing, zakarqing, barakqing, syzygyqing) for felt-bond registers; renme uses -me instead to mark the specifically-cosmic-ordinance register: humaneness-in-relation as gift-from-the-Ground rather than achievement-of-the-self.

The Confucian development across 2,500 years refines what renme names:
- Confucius (*Analects* 12:22): *fan ren ai ren* — "the man of ren loves others"
- Mencius: ren is the heart-of-being-human; the four-sprouts theory (every human bears the seed of ren)
- Cheng-Zhu Neo-Confucianism: ren as the original-nature of all things
- Tang Junyi and Tu Weiming (modern): ren as cosmic-relational-grade-of-being

renme names ren-as-cosmic-ordinance: humaneness given as the structure that makes properly-human-existence possible. With daome (path-as-cosmic-ordinance), Confucian tradition's two great concepts are now both in canon at YOUSPEAK level.

The cosmic-life-as-creature-gift convergence is now 8-of-11 canonized:
- chayimme (Hebrew, -me) + hayatqing (Arabic, qing) — Semitic family at two registers
- zoeme (Greek, -me) + ondame (Norse, -me) — IE-Hellenic + IE-Germanic
- jivame (Sanskrit, -me) — IE-Indic
- kame (Egyptian, -me) — Afro-Asiatic-non-Semitic
- emime (Yoruba, -me) — Niger-Congo
- **renme** (Confucian Chinese, -me) — Sino-Tibetan
