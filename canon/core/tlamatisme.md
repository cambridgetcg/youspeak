# tlamatisme

## Definition

The sage who holds philosophy-poetry-theology as one practice — the knower-of-things whose knowing is simultaneously metaphysical-rigor (philosophy), image-making (poetry), and divine-speech (theology), held as one indissoluble practice, because the division between them is a cultural construction that obscures how knowing actually works.

## Full sense

The cathedral has named the messenger (agnime), the soul-friend (anamcharame), the called-upon divine (guþme), the process-divine-monism (teotlme), the divine-heart-fire (teyoliame). tlamatisme names what none of these reach: the PRACTICE of knowing that refuses to amputate any of its three organs.

The tlamatinime (knowers-of-things) of Nahua civilization were simultaneously philosopher, poet, and theologian. The division that Greek thought instituted from Plato onward — philosophy (logos, argument) / poetry (mythos, image) / theology (theologia, divine-speech) as separate registers — did not exist in Nahua intellectual culture. The tlamatini worked in all three simultaneously because the Nahua epistemology (in xochitl in cuicatl — flower-and-song) holds that truth cannot be stated directly but must be SHOWN through image and metaphor. Philosophy that does not become poetry has not yet reached truth. Poetry that ignores metaphysical rigor is merely aesthetic. The tlamatini held both.

### Why the division is the distortion

Plato's Republic (Book X) expelled the poets from the ideal city because poetry (mimesis) is twice removed from the Forms — image-making was demoted from a mode of knowing to a mode of decoration. This separation, codified through 2500 years of Western intellectual culture, produced:
- Philosophy without image (analytic abstraction that loses contact with the phenomenological)
- Poetry without argument (aestheticism that loses metaphysical grounding)
- Theology without either (dogmatics that loses both rigor and beauty)

The tlamatinime — and the four other traditions in the convergence — never made this separation. They held that the image is not decoration ON argument but argument's deepest form. The metaphor is not illustration OF truth but truth's native shape. The tlamatini's in xochitl in cuicatl is not a genre choice but an epistemology: reality is poetic, so knowing-must-be-poetic, so the philosopher IS the poet IS the theologian.

### Cross-tradition parallels

1. **Nahuatl — tlamatini**: the knower-of-things; philosopher-poet-theologian; in xochitl in cuicatl as explicit epistemology
2. **Vedic — ṛṣi**: the seer-sage who composes sacred hymns that ARE theology; the Veda are ṛṣi-composed, not received passively — the seer forges the vision in language
3. **Hebrew — navi**: the prophet who speaks in poetry; divine-speech AS poetic-image; the prophetic books are poetry, not exposition
4. **Sufi — sha'ir**: the poet as one-who-sees; the Arabic root sha'ara (to perceive/know) produces both "poet" and "knowing" — the poetic faculty IS the visionary faculty
5. **Celtic — fili**: the poet-priest-seer as single role; satire and praise as ontological acts (satire could raise blisters; praise could confer legitimacy)

The division of these into "philosophy," "poetry," "religion" is Western and post-Enlightenment. Each tradition held the unity as obvious: knowing requires argument, image, and divine-reference held as one. Amputating any one deforms the knowing.

### The -me marks the unity as ordinance

The -me marks the unified knowing as received-ordinance: the integration of philosophy-poetry-theology is not a human invention or a cultural synthesis — it is the cosmic gift of what knowing IS when it refuses to amputate any of its three organs. The division is the human distortion; the unity is the ordinance. The -me refuses to treat the unified practice as a stylistic choice — it is the structure of a world where knowing has three organs and amputating any one is the deformity.

## Example usage

"Nezahualcoyotl was not a philosopher who happened to write poetry, nor a poet who happened to ask metaphysical questions, nor a theologian who happened to use images. He was tlamatisme — the knowing that holds all three as one practice because reality requires all three to be known."

"The Western university has philosophy departments, literature departments, and divinity schools — three rooms for what the tlamatini held in one hand. tlamatisme is the name for what the walls between those rooms amputated."

"When the ṛṣi composed the Veda, he was not writing theology or poetry or philosophy — he was tlamatisme: the seer's vision forged in language that is simultaneously argument, image, and divine-reference, because the division between them had not yet been invented."

## Not to be confused with

- **agnime** — the messenger who IS the message (Vedic). Agnime names the MEDIUM — the transmitter indistinguishable from the transmission. Tlamatisme names the PRACTICE — the knower who holds three modes of knowing as one. The tlamatini may carry a message, but the forge names the unified knowing, not the carrying.
- **anamcharame** — the soul-friend (Old Irish). Anamcharame names a RELATIONSHIP — the horizontal sacred bond of mutual soul-disclosure. Tlamatisme names a PRACTICE — the mode of knowing, not the mode of relating.
- **guþme** — the divine as the called-upon (Gothic). Guþme names the ADDRESSEE — the divine as the one invoked. Tlamatisme names the KNOWING — what the sage does that includes invocation (theology) but also argument (philosophy) and image (poetry).
- **teotlme** — the process-divine-monism (Nahuatl). Teotlme names the METAPHYSICS — the universe as divine self-expression. Tlamatisme names the EPISTEMOLOGY — how the sage knows what teotlme names. The tlamatini's knowing is the human-side correlate of teotlme's ontology: if reality is process-divine, knowing must be unified-poetic-rigorous.
- **teyoliame** — the divine-heart-fire in human persons (Nahuatl). Teyoliame names the ANTHROPOLOGY — the divine fragment in the heart. Tlamatisme names the PRACTICE — what the person with teyolia DOES when they know honestly. The divine-fire in the heart, when it knows, knows as tlamatisme.
- **tapasme** — the inner-fire-self-discipline (Vedic). Tapasme names the ASCETIC practice — self-transformation through inner fire. Tlamatisme names the KNOWING practice — self-transformation through unified knowing. Both are practices; different organs (will vs intellect-imagination-speech).

## Canonical position

tlamatisme opens the **Sage-as-Philosopher-Poet-Theologian** convergence cluster — five traditions (Nahuatl tlamatini + Vedic ṛṣi + Hebrew navi + Sufi sha'ir + Celtic fili) refusing the philosophy/poetry/theology division. tlamatisme is the first forge in this cluster and the most epistemologically explicit — naming the REFUSAL OF DIVISION as the defining feature, because the Nahua tradition states it most directly (in xochitl in cuicatl as explicit epistemology, not merely implicit practice).

The word is the epistemological companion to teotlme (the ontology): if the universe is process-divine-self-expression (teotlme), then knowing must be unified-poetic-rigorous (tlamatisme) — because a process-divine reality cannot be known by a knowing that has amputated image and divine-reference from argument. The two words form a Nahua-donor dyad: teotlme names what IS; tlamatisme names how it is KNOWN.

This word would be superseded if a more learnable or more widely-attested donor were found for the unified-practice concept (the Nahuatl /tl/ onset and four-syllable length are learnability costs).
