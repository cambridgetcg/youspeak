# doxomme

## Definition

Glory-as-received-ordinance — the radiant splendor of divine presence recognized as gift, and the praise that answers it acknowledged as ordinance rather than self-summoned feeling. The Greek glory-praise register of the thanksgiving-field, paired with kimme (attention-as-ordinance) and complemented by shukrme (gratitude-as-increase). The -me marks: the glory is given, the beholding is given, the praise is given — doxomme is the recognition that all three are received.

## Full sense

Doxomme names what the Greek **doxa** (δόξα) contributes to the cathedral's worship-register after the most load-bearing lexical crossing in the history of theology: the Septuagint's decision to translate Hebrew **kavod** (כָּבוֹד — weight, heaviness, substance, the palpable presence of God) with Greek **doxa** (δόξα — opinion, reputation, what seems true).

Before the crossing, *doxa* meant opinion — what seems true to someone, reputation, subjective assessment. After the crossing, *doxa* meant glory — the manifested-weight of divine presence, the radiant splendor that fills the Temple, appears as fire and cloud, overwhelms the beholder. The LXX did not merely translate a word; it **transfigured** a word. *Doxa* absorbed *kavod*'s weight/manifestation sense and became something it had never been in classical Greek: the visible, material, overwhelming presence of God.

When John writes "we beheld his *doxa*" (Jn 1:14), he fuses both meanings: the reputation/opinion of God (what God is truly like) IS the material glory (the visible presence). The word that once meant "what seems true" now means "what IS glory." This is the semantic ground doxomme stands on.

Doxomme marks this transformed sense as **ordinance**. The -me (Sumerian: divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality) makes a theological claim: the glory is not self-manufactured radiance, and the praise that answers it is not self-summoned feeling. Both are gift. The capacity to behold glory is given. The glory itself is given. The praise that rises in response is given. The recognition that the praise is gift is itself gift — and the structure is recursive, the same recursion that shukrme names from the Arabic side: the grateful one gives thanks for the capacity to give thanks.

The pairing with **kimme** (attention-as-received-ordinance) is structural:

- **Kimme** is the inward-reception: attention is gift. The capacity to attend, to notice, to be present — this is received, not constructed.
- **Doxomme** is the outward-response: glory is gift, and the praise that answers glory is gift. The capacity to behold splendor and name it as splendor — this is received, not generated.

Attention receives; glory radiates; praise acknowledges. Kimme is the condition of seeing; doxomme is what happens when seeing meets radiance. The two are the received-ordinance pair in Core: the inward-ordinance (kimme) and the outward-ordinance (doxomme). One cannot behold glory (doxomme) without the attention (kimme) that makes beholding possible; one's attention (kimme) finds its deepest object in the glory it was given to behold (doxomme).

The polyphone-deepening pair with **shukrme** (gratitude-as-increase-activator) names two faces of the thanksgiving-field from two traditions:

- **Doxomme** (Greek doxa — glory, praise, splendor): the **glory-praise pole**. The upward acknowledgment of divine radiance. The worshipper beholds splendor and renders it as glory. The structure: splendor is given → the beholder recognizes it as splendor → the recognition is praise. The direction is upward (toward the radiance).
- **Shukrme** (Arabic shukr — gratitude, increase): the **gratitude-increase pole**. The recognition that what was given is gift, and that the recognition itself opens the flow of more. The structure: gift is given → the receiver recognizes it as gift → the recognition activates increase. The direction is forward (into the multiplying).

The two name different structures. Doxa is glory-directed: the worshipper acknowledges splendor. Shukr is increase-directed: the worshipper's gratitude opens the channel for more. Doxomme's donor (doxa) actually means glory, not gratitude — the Greek gratitude-word proper is **eucharistia** (εὐχαριστία — well-grace, eu + charis), which the cathedral has not yet forged. If eucharistia is ever forged (as *eucharistime*?), it would form a triad with doxomme (glory-praise) and shukrme (gratitude-increase): three faces of the thanksgiving-field from three traditions. Doxomme is the Greek pole; the glory-beheld-as-ordinance; the one that names the radiance as gift.

## Etymology

**doxa** (Greek δόξα, Indo-European Hellenic; from *dokein* [δοκεῖν] — to seem, to think; classical sense: opinion, reputation, what seems true; LXX sense: glory, the manifested-weight of divine presence, the translation of Hebrew kavod [כָּבוֹד — weight, heaviness, the palpable presence of God that fills the Temple, appears as fire and cloud]; the LXX crossing transfigured the word: doxa absorbed kavod's weight/manifestation sense and became "glory" rather than mere "opinion"; NT inherits: "we beheld his doxa" [Jn 1:14] — the reputation of God fused with the material glory; doxazō [δοξάζω] — to glorify, to render-as-glory; endoxazō [ἐνδοξάζω] — to be-glorified-in; attested from Homeric Greek through Classical philosophy, LXX, NT, patristic, Byzantine, modern theology; ~3 millennia of continuous development; the etymological irony: the word that once meant "what seems true" now names "what IS glory" — the semantic transfiguration IS the theological claim).

**me** (Sumerian 𒈨): divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality. The established YOUSPEAK morpheme. Here: glory-as-received-ordinance — the capacity to behold and acknowledge divine splendor is not self-produced but received. The radiance is gift; the recognizing-of-the-radiance is gift; the praise-that-answers is gift. The -me marks the entire glory-circuit as ordinance: given, not generated.

## Example usage

1. The Temple was filled. The cloud descended — the same cloud that had led them through the wilderness, the visible weight of the presence that no eye could bear directly. The priests could not stand to minister. This is doxomme: the glory is not decoration; it is the manifested-weight of the One who is there. The beholding is gift; the capacity to stand in the radiance is gift; the praise that rises is gift. The -me names all three as received.

2. "We beheld his doxa," John wrote — "doxa as of the only-begotten from the Father, full of grace and truth." The word that had meant "opinion" now names the radiance that filled the tabernacle of flesh. Doxomme is the recognition that this radiance is ordinance: not manufactured splendor, not projected reputation, but the manifested-weight of divine presence given to be beheld. The beholding is the gift; the glory is the gift; the seeing-of-the-glory is the gift.

3. The Psalmist writes: "The heavens declare the doxa of God." Not: the heavens have an opinion about God. Not: the heavens seem to be related to God. The heavens radiate the manifested-weight of the One who made them. Doxomme is the recognition that this declaration is ordinance — the cosmos was given the capacity to declare, and the declaring is not the cosmos's achievement but its reception. The stars do not generate glory; they receive it and radiate it.

4. The worshipper enters the liturgy. The candles are lit. The incense rises. The words are spoken — "Glory to God in the highest." The worshipper did not summon this. The glory was already there; the capacity to perceive it was already given; the praise that answers was already stirring before the words formed. Doxomme names the worshipper's recognition: I did not make this radiance, I did not make this attention, I did not make this praise. All three are gift. The -me marks the entire circuit as received.

5. The doxazō of the NT is transitive — "glorify God." This is not: the human generates glory and attributes it to God. This is: the human recognizes the glory that is already there, already radiating, already given — and the recognition is the glorifying. Doxomme is the ordinance-status of this recognition: the glorifying is not the human's creative act but the human's receptive act. The glory flows through the one who recognizes it; the recognizer does not produce it.

## Not confused with

- **shukrme** (Core 7.78 — gratitude-as-increase-activator): shukrme is the gratitude-increase pole — the recognition that what was given is gift, and that the recognition opens the flow of more. Doxomme is the glory-praise pole — the recognition that the splendor beheld is gift, and that the praise that answers it is gift. Shukrme's direction is forward (into increase); doxomme's direction is upward (toward the radiance). Shukrme uses Arabic shukr (to increase); doxomme uses Greek doxa (glory). The two are the polyphone-deepening pair: two faces of the thanksgiving-field from two traditions, naming two different structures. Doxomme's donor means glory, not gratitude; shukrme's donor means increase, not glory. Neither subsumes the other.

- **kimme** (Core 7.70 — attention-as-received-ordinance): kimme is the inward-reception — the capacity to attend is gift. Doxomme is the outward-response — the glory beheld and the praise that answers are gift. Kimme is the condition of seeing; doxomme is what happens when seeing meets radiance. The two are the received-ordinance pair: the inward-ordinance and the outward-ordinance. Attention without glory finds nothing to behold; glory without attention finds no one beholding. The pair completes: kimme receives the capacity to see; doxomme receives what is seen and the praise that answers.

- **agapeme** (Core — love-as-received-ordinance): agapeme names love-as-ordinance; doxomme names glory-as-ordinance. The Johannine fusion (Jn 1:14: "we beheld his doxa, full of grace and truth") makes the two inseparable: the glory IS the love made visible; the love IS the glory's content. Agapeme is what radiates; doxomme is the radiance-as-recognized. The two may eventually form a convergence: love's outward-radiance (agapeme) + glory's received-beholding (doxomme).

- **eucharistia** (Greek εὐχαριστία — well-grace, thanksgiving; eu + charis): the Greek New Testament term for thanksgiving, carried into Latin as a loanword, becoming the Eucharist. Eucharistia is closer to gratitude than doxa is — it carries the grace-gift semantic. But eucharistia has not been forged as a YOUSPEAK word. If forged (as *eucharistime*?), it would form a triad with doxomme (glory-praise) and shukrme (gratitude-increase): three faces of the thanksgiving-field from three traditions. Doxomme uses doxa (glory); eucharistime would use eucharistia (well-grace-thanksgiving); shukrme uses shukr (increase). Three donors, three semantic fields, one worship-register.

- **"Glory"** (English): from Latin *gloria* (likely from *kléos* via Greek, or Indo-European root meaning "fame, renown"). Names the human perception of splendor, majesty, fame. Doxomme marks the glory as received-ordinance (the -me) AND as manifested-weight-of-presence (the LXX-transformed doxa, not merely "fame"). English "glory" is a perception; doxomme is an ordinance. The English word does not carry the kavod-doza crossing (weight → radiance) that the Greek donor does.

- **"Praise"** (English): from Latin *pretium* (price, value). Names the act of expressing approval or admiration. Doxomme is not the act-of-praising but the ordinance-status of the glory that calls forth praise. Praise is the response; doxomme is the gift-structure that makes the response possible — the glory is given, the beholding is given, the praise is given.

- **"Thanksgiving"** (English): closer to the register but carries neither the glory-semantic (doxa = splendor, not gratitude) nor the ordinance-semantic (the -me). "Thanksgiving" names a practice; doxomme names a cosmic structure participated in. The Greek thanksgiving-word is eucharistia, not doxa — doxomme is the glory-praise face, not the gratitude face, of the thanksgiving-field.

## Canonical position

Doxomme is the second -me family member and the second Core -me, forged in Liturgy Session 006 (2026-04-24) in direct response to Yu's Mesopotamian directive. It pairs with kimme (attention-as-ordinance) as the received-ordinance pair in Core: the inward-ordinance (kimme) and the outward-ordinance (doxomme). It is the first Greek-donor -me entry, opening the Greek tradition's contribution to the -me family — a contribution later deepened by aletheme (alētheia, unconcealment-as-ordinance) and connected to agapeme (agapē, love-as-ordinance).

The polyphone-deepening pair with shukrme (Core 7.78 — gratitude-as-increase-activator) completes the thanksgiving-field's two-pole structure:

- **Doxomme** (Greek doxa — glory-praise): the splendor beheld, the upward acknowledgment. The glory-praise pole.
- **Shukrme** (Arabic shukr — gratitude-increase): the gift recognized, the forward-flow opened. The gratitude-increase pole.

Two traditions, two donors, two semantic structures, one worship-register. The Greek contributes the glory-semantic (the manifested-weight of divine presence, the kavod→doxa crossing). The Arabic contributes the increase-semantic (gratitude as causal mechanism of multiplication, the sh-k-r root). Neither subsumes the other: doxa means glory, not gratitude; shukr means increase, not glory. The pair names the thanksgiving-field as structurally two-faced: beholding-splendor (doxomme) and recognizing-gift (shukrme).

The -me family's Greek tradition-contribution now spans two entries across two semantic fields: glory-as-received-ordinance (doxomme) and unconcealment-as-received-ordinance (aletheme). Both draw on the LXX's transfiguration of Greek philosophical vocabulary into theological term-of-art: doxa from opinion to glory, alētheia from philosophical truth to event-of-disclosure. The LXX's method — taking a Greek word and loading it with Hebrew theological content — is structurally what the -me does: taking a donor morpheme and marking it as received-ordinance. Doxomme is the earliest instance of this pattern in the -me family.

The Greek donor is familiar to any tradition influenced by LXX/NT theology (spanning Orthodox, Catholic, Protestant, and secular-literary registers). The kavod→doxa crossing is one of the most widely-studied lexical events in the history of theology, making doxomme's genealogy accessible to anyone who has encountered the LXX translation tradition.
