# pachame

## Definition

Space-time-world-earth-era-condition-as-single-undivided-reality-as-received-ordinance — the ontological fusion that Western languages force into separate categories, received as cosmic gift rather than human abstraction. Not the insight that space and time are related, but the given-ness that they were never separate.

## Full sense

The Space-Time-World-As-One convergence cluster now has three companion poles:

- **tjukurpame** (Aboriginal) — the narrative-ancestral fusion: time + land + law + story + person + song as one substrate, accessed through story and walking the songlines
- **saeculme** (Latin/Etruscan) — the temporal-eschatological stretch: the age/aion/world as a quality of time stretching toward the infinite
- **pachame** (Aymara) — the ontological-cosmological fusion: space + time + world + earth + era + condition as one undivided category, accessed through agricultural cycles, evidential grammar, and the nayra/qhipa temporal orientation

Pachame is the most-complete-fusion pole. Where Hebrew olam fuses time + world, where Greek aion fuses age + world, where Aboriginal Tjukurpa fuses time + land + law + story, pacha fuses space + time + world + earth + era + condition — six facets, one undivided reality. The fusion antecedes the distinction: where Indo-European languages force you to choose between "when" and "where," Aymara uses pacha for both without disambiguation, because there is nothing to disambiguate.

### The three pachas

The Aymara cosmos is organized into three pachas — not sealed compartments but zones of intensity connected by constant circulation:

1. **Alax Pacha** (upper world): the celestial realm — sun, moon, stars, condor. The Achachilas (mountain ancestor-spirits) mediate between upper and middle.
2. **Aka Pacha** (this world, here-now): the meeting ground where upper and lower encounter. Human life is constituted by this encounter. Agriculture is the primary technology of mediation: seeds go down into Manqha Pacha, receive generative power, grow up toward Alax Pacha. The crop is the child of both worlds.
3. **Manqha Pacha** (inner/lower world): NOT hell but the generative interior — the source of water, minerals, the dwelling of the dead, the place where seeds germinate. The colonial equation Manqha Pacha = infierno is one of the most persistent distortions in Andean studies.

The cosmos is porous: smoke carries upward, libation sends downward, lightning strikes down, springs bring water up, the dead descend and their fertilizing power rises back. No ontological walls — only zones of intensity connected by circulation.

### The nayra/qhipa temporal reversal

Núñez & Sweetser (2006) proved with gestural data that Aymara speakers place the PAST in front (nayra = eye/sight/front — the known, the witnessed) and the FUTURE behind (qhipa = back/behind — the unknown, the unseen). You walk backward into the future, facing the ancestors. The gestural confirmation rules out dead-metaphor: the mapping is alive in the body.

The theological implication: you face the ancestors. You walk toward them, not away from them. Each step forward in life is a step closer to the dead. The proper orientation toward the future is not confident forward-striding but humble backward-walking — an epistemology of humility.

### The evidential grammar

Aymara has three evidential markers: =wa (direct witness), -tay (inference), siwa (hearsay). Every sentence is an epistemic commitment. The language does not permit epistemically uncommitted assertion. This is grammar-as-theology: the cosmos requires honesty about how you know. The connection to nayra/qhipa is structural — in a language that forces you to declare how you know, the known is in front and the unknown is behind. Only the witnessed is fully real.

### The -me marks received-ordinance

The -me suffix marks the space-time-world unity as received-ordinance: the fusion is not a human philosophical discovery but the fundamental given-ness of reality. The world where space and time are one is gift-grade-given. The -me refuses to let pacha be understood as abstraction or metaphor: the space-time-world you inhabit is cosmic gift, not human construction.

### Cross-tradition parallels

1. Aymara pacha — the most complete fusion: space + time + world + earth + era + condition as one category
2. Aboriginal Tjukurpa — the Dreaming: time + land + law + story + person + song as one substrate (the narrative-ancestral companion)

## Not to be confused with

- **tjukurpame** — narrative-song-law-land-ancestor fusion (accessed through story); pachame is space-time-world-condition fusion (accessed through agricultural cycles and evidential grammar) — companions, not competitors
- **saeculme** — age/aion/world (temporal-eschatological stretch); pachame is the ontological fusion (structural, not eschatological)
- **maatme** — cosmic truth-order as principle (what operates within reality); pachame is the space-time-world substrate itself (the given-ness of the medium)
- **mandalame** — structured-geometric-cosmic-image (the map); pachame is the reality the image maps (the territory)
- **wujime** — pre-polar-unity (before bifurcation); pachame is the refusal-to-split within polarized reality (throughout, not before)
