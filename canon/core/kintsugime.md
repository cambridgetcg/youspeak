## Definition

Mending-that-honors-the-break received as ordinance — the Japanese golden-joinery register held as a *me*; the status of a repaired thing whose visible break-line, rejoined with gold, is now its most precious load-bearing line.

## Full sense

A kintsugime is *what the vessel becomes when the shards are rejoined without shame*. The Japanese craft-claim received: broken pottery mended with urushi lacquer dusted with gold (kintsugi 金継ぎ, "golden joinery") is not returned to what it was — it becomes truer. The break-line is displayed, not disguised; the gold traces exactly the path of the damage; the mended vessel is prized above the unbroken one. The second root carries the deepest sense: *tsugu* (継ぐ) means both "to join" and "to inherit, to continue" — the mend is the vessel's life carried forward, an inheritance received from its own breaking.

Crucially, kintsugime is **NOT restoration**. Restoration aims at the counterfeit of an unbroken past — return-to-original by concealment, the break treated as shame. Kintsugime aims at truth: the history of damage converted into the map of survival, structurally load-bearing at the seam. Nothing whole-from-the-start can carry this particular beauty; the gold map is singular to this break, this mending, this history.

The -me suffix marks the received-ordinance grade: the mender does not invent the principle that breaks deserve gold. They enter a discipline that was handed down — urushi, ash, gold-powder, the slow months of curing — and the ordinance beneath the discipline is older than any mender: *the mended thing may outshine the unbroken one.* The way of mending is received, not invented.

Pairs with:

- **shevirame** (companion-pair) — shevirame is the breaking-pole, kintsugime the mended-pole of one arc; shevirame names the shattering that makes creation possible, kintsugime what the vessel becomes when the shards are rejoined with gold
- **tikkunme** (stands with) — tikkunme is the cosmic act of gathering the sparks; kintsugime is the visible grace of the gathered vessel — tikkunme mends the world, kintsugime shows the mend was worth gold
- **hanme** (kin in grief) — hanme carries generational grief properly voiced; kintsugime is what such grief can become when the voicing is done — the seam where sorrow sets into gold
- **tzimtzumme** (arc-opener) — the four-movement sentence now reads: contraction, shattering, repair-work, and the visible grace of the repaired vessel

## Not to be confused with

- **Restoration** — return-to-original by concealment; the precise opposite valence; a restored thing pretends it never broke
- **tikkunme** (YOUSPEAK Core) — the repair-*work* as cosmic vocation; tikkunme gathers, kintsugime is the gathered vessel's status
- **shevirame** (YOUSPEAK Core) — the shattering-pole; says nothing of the afterward
- **Kintsugi** (Japanese, borrowed) — names the pottery craft only; the borrowing stops at the workshop door and carries no received-ordinance grade
- **Resilience** — the agent's capacity to recover; silent about the recovered thing's new form
- **Scar** (English) — the mark valenced as stigma; precisely the valence kintsugime inverts

**The shadow-boundary, held openly:** kintsugime takes no gold to an open wound. Gilding where healing was owed is decoration over harm; aestheticizing damage to skip the mending is the counterfeit; and breaking a thing on purpose to harvest the gold — a corruption the tea-tradition's own records already feared — is the inversion of the whole ordinance. The gold is for the *mended*. Gild the mended; do not break to gild.
