# hayatqing

## Definition

Life-as-state-given-by-Allah as felt-bond — Arabic *ḥayāt* understood as cosmic-state received from the divine source (al-Ḥayy, "the Living"); the Quranic and Sufi register of life as relational-with-its-Source. Seventh life-side canonical forge; first to use qing-family register for life-side (siblings zoeme/jivame/kame/emime/ondame all -me-family).

## Full sense

Arabic theology centers life in Allah's act-of-giving:

- Quran 2:258 — *huwa al-laḏī yuḥyī wa yumītu* — "He is the One who gives life and causes death"
- Allah's 99 Names include *al-Ḥayy* (الحي) — "the Living"; *al-Muhyī* (المحيي) — "the Giver of Life"
- Sufi tradition develops *ḥayāt al-qulūb* (life of the hearts) — the spiritual-aliveness given by Allah to receptive-hearts; distinct from biological-aliveness

The qing-suffix is chosen specifically: ḥayāt in Arabic religious literature is **felt-relational**. Life is not impersonal cosmic-ordinance; it is the felt-relationship-with-the-Living-One. The qing-family register names this. chayimme (Hebrew + -me) uses the received-ordinance frame; hayatqing (Arabic + qing) uses the felt-bond frame. Both true; both traditions' precision honored at canon.

The cosmic-life-as-creature-gift convergence is now 7-of-11 canonized:
- chayimme (Hebrew/Semitic, -me)
- zoeme (Greek/IE-Hellenic, -me)
- jivame (Sanskrit/IE-Indic, -me)
- kame (Egyptian/AA-non-Semitic, -me)
- emime (Yoruba/Niger-Congo, -me)
- ondame (Norse/IE-Germanic, -me)
- **hayatqing** (Arabic/Semitic, qing-)

Seven traditions; six -me entries + one qing entry. The convergence demonstrates **both suffix-families can carry life-side content** depending on the tradition's emphasis.

## Etymology

*ḥayāt* from Proto-Semitic root *ḥ-y-y "to live" — cognate with Hebrew chayim. The form ḥayāt is the Arabic abstract-noun "life," used across Quran, hadith, and Sufi literature.

*-qing* from Mandarin 情 — YOUSPEAK standard felt-bond suffix.

## Example usage

A Muslim reciting the Quranic verse on life-and-death — recognizing al-Ḥayy as the Source. hayatqing is the cathedral-name for what is recognized.

A Sufi practitioner experiencing *ḥayāt al-qulūb* — the heart coming alive through ḏikr. hayatqing names the felt-spiritual-aliveness.

A doctor at a deathbed knowing Allah is the One who gives and takes life — hayatqing names the felt-relational fact of life-as-given.

## Not confused with

**vs. chayimme**: Same Semitic root; different traditions and registers. chayimme (Hebrew, -me) = multitudinous-plural-life-as-cosmic-ordinance. hayatqing (Arabic, qing) = life-as-state-given-by-Allah-as-felt-bond.

**vs. ihsanme**: Same Arabic-donor tradition; different register (best-effort-worship vs. life-substrate).

**vs. mahabbahqing**: Same Arabic-+-qing pattern; different content (Sufi felt-bond-of-love vs. life-substrate).

**vs. zoeme/jivame/kame/emime/ondame**: Life-side -me-family siblings; hayatqing is the qing-family alternative — different register for the same realm-region.
