# asraya-paravrttime

## Definition

The turning-about of the basis received as cosmic-ordinance — the radical transformation of the substrate-of-experience from defiled to pure, where the basis does not vanish but turns, and the same consciousness that stored ignorance now reflects as wisdom.

## Full sense

Āśraya-parāvṛtti is Vasubandhu's name (Triṃśikā v.30) for the culminating event of the Yogācāra path: the storehouse-consciousness (ālaya-vijñāna) ceases its defiled function of storing karmic-seeds of ignorance and transforms into four pure wisdoms. The term is a compound: āśraya (basis, substratum, resting-place) + parāvṛtti (turning-about, revolution, reversal). The basis is not annihilated — it is rotated. The mirror was always there; the distortions leave.

The four structural marks:

1. **Turning, not destruction.** The same consciousness that stored defiled-seeds now reflects purely. This is parāvṛtti (turning), not vināśa (destruction). The continuity is structural, not substantial.

2. **Sudden culmination of gradual path.** The seeds are exhausted gradually; the turn is sudden (eka-prahara — "in one stroke"). The Yogācāra structural-antecedent to the sudden/gradual debate that splits Chan/Zen.

3. **Irreversible.** Once the basis has turned, it does not revert. The seed-receiving mechanism is structurally eliminated, not merely suppressed.

4. **Wisdom, not blankness.** The transformed basis is four active wisdoms: ādarśa-jñāna (mirror-wisdom), samatā-jñāna (equality-wisdom), pratyavekṣaṇā-jñāna (discriminating-wisdom), kṛtyānusthāna-jñāna (all-accomplishing-wisdom). The mirror reflects truly for the first time.

The -me marks the turning as received-ordinance: the event is not fabricated (nirmita) by the practitioner but arrives as the culmination of the path's conditions. This is the Yogācāra doctrine: the basis turns when conditions are complete. The practitioner prepares; the turning is gift.

### The Yogācāra soteriological triad

The triad ālayame → asraya-paravrttime → rigpame is now COMPLETE:

- **ālayame** (Core 7.98, 2026-05-13) — the storehouse-consciousness. The basis. The noun. What exists as the substrate of experience.
- **asraya-paravrttime** (Core 7.88, 2026-07-08) — the turning-about of the basis. The verb. What happens to the basis on the path.
- **rigpame** (Core 8.45, 2026-05-13) — primordial-awareness. The result. What the basis becomes after the turning.

The triad names the full Yogācāra soteriological architecture in three words: the conditioned-storage (ālayame), the event that transforms it (asraya-paravrttime), and the unconditioned-awareness that results (rigpame). The triad is diachronic: before the turning, during the turning, after the turning.

### Cross-tradition parallels

1. **Yogācāra Buddhism** — āśraya-parāvṛtti (the primary donor). Vasubandhu Triṃśikā v.30. The deepest technical attestation.
2. **Hindu Kashmir Shaivism** — śaktipāta. The descent of grace that transforms the subject-basis. Received, not fabricated.
3. **Christian mysticism** — metanoia / conversio morum. The monastic "conversion of life" — basis-turning in a contemplative register.
4. **Sufi Islam** — tawba → tajallī. The turning-return that precedes divine self-disclosure. The sequence parallels āśraya-parāvṛtti → jñānam.
5. **Platonism** — periagoge (Republic 518d). The "turning-around" of the soul toward the Good. Not acquiring content but redirecting the ground.
6. **Modern psychology** — "quantum change" (William Miller). Sudden, permanent, structural personality-change. The secular-scientific attestation.

6 traditions converge on the radical-basis-transformation concept. The Yogācāra tradition is the deepest and most technically-articulated — the only one that specifies the mechanism, the result, and the irreversibility.

## Example usage

1. "After years of meditation, the asraya-paravrttime arrived — not as an achievement but as a received event; the storehouse that had stored affliction now reflected as mirror-wisdom, and the change was irreversible" — Yogācāra-articulation.
2. "Ālayame is the basis; asraya-paravrttime is the turning of the basis; rigpame is what the basis becomes — the noun, the verb, and the result" — cathedral-internal.
3. "Tikkunme is the work you do; asraya-paravrttime is the turning that arrives when the work is complete — the repair and the gift" — cross-tradition.
4. "The basis does not vanish; it turns. The mirror was always there; the distortions leave" — structural claim.

## Not to be confused with

- **ālayame** — the storehouse-basis itself (the noun); asraya-paravrttime is the turning of the basis (the verb)
- **rigpame** — primordial-awareness (the result-state); asraya-paravrttime is the event that produces the result
- **myesisme** — the threshold-crossing (event-structure); asraya-paravrttime is what happens to the substrate (event-substrate)
- **spandaqing** — consciousness-pulsation (continuous rhythm); asraya-paravrttime is a singular turning (revolution)
- **tikkunme** — cosmic-repair as human work (anthropocentric); asraya-paravrttime is the received event (theocentric-without-theos)
- **shevirame** — vessel-shattering (destruction); asraya-paravrttime is basis-turning (transformation-without-destruction)
