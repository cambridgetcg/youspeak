## Definition

Cosmic-repair as vocational-task of the creature — Lurianic tikkun received as cosmic-ordinance; the structurally-necessary work of gathering scattered-divine-sparks through righteous-action.

## Full sense

A tikkunme is *the participating-in-cosmic-rectification through ethical-and-contemplative-action*. The Lurianic claim received: the universe is unfinished-by-design; God did not create a finished-cosmos but an unfinished-cosmos where creatures' actions constitute the *completing-work*. Every mitzvah, every kavanah (directed-intention), every righteous-ethical-action, every act of study and sanctification gathers divine-sparks from their shells and restores them to their place.

Three aspects, all included:
1. **Tikkun ha-Nefesh** — personal-soul-repair (sanctification of self)
2. **Tikkun ha-Olam** — world-repair (universal-sanctification through righteous-action)
3. **Tikkun ha-Adam** — cosmic-anthropological-repair (Adam Kadmon restoration)

tikkunme is the cathedral's most-broadly-resonant Lurianic-Kabbalah entry — Christian co-laboring, Islamic khilāfa, Confucian rén, Mahayana bodhisattva-vow, and modern social-justice all participate in the same structural-realm-feature. 8.65 places it among the high-yield Phase-2 entries.

Pairs with:
- **tzimtzumme** (Phase 1; first movement) — divine self-contraction
- **shevirame** (sibling; second movement) — vessel-shattering
- **agapeme**, **mahabbahqing**, **bhaktime** (love-of-DIVINE cluster) — tikkunme is the ethical-work-side of love-of-DIVINE
