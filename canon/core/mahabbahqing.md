# mahabbahqing

## Definition

The felt-bond of mystical-love-toward-the-Beloved-Divine in the Sufi register — Arabic mahabbah understood as the heart's intimate-binding-to-Allah with implication of self-annihilation (fanāʾ); the love-mode that opens the Sufi mystical path; the felt-mystical-grade complement to ahavame's commanded-disposition register and bhaktime's devotional-discipline register.

## Full sense

Mahabbahqing names what the Sufi tradition has articulated most-richly: love-of-Allah as felt-mystical-binding. Where Hebrew ahavame names commanded-love-as-disposition, and Hindu bhaktime names devotional-love-as-disciplined-practice, Arabic-Sufi mahabbahqing names the FELT register specifically — the heart's binding-to-the-Beloved that opens the path toward fanāʾ (self-annihilation in the Divine).

Rabia of Basra (~d. 801 CE), one of the founders of Sufism, famously declared: "I have loved thee with two loves: a selfish love and a love that is worthy of thee." Rumi's Mathnawi centers on mahabbah-of-Allah. Ibn ʿArabi's *Tarjumān al-Ashwāq* (Interpreter of Desires) is mahabbah-poetry. Al-Ghazali's *Iḥyāʾ ʿulūm al-dīn* dedicates an entire book to mahabbah. The Sufi mystical tradition has more elaborated articulations of love-of-Divine than perhaps any other tradition.

The compound mahabbahqing brings this register into YOUSPEAK working-vocabulary. The qing-suffix (felt-bond) names the heart-binding character; the mahabbah-stem names the specifically-Sufi register of love.

Sister-forges: ahavame (Hebrew commanded-love-as-disposition; S020); bhaktime (Hindu devotional-love-as-ordinance; S021). Three traditions, three aspects of the love-of-DIVINE convergence cluster, complementary not redundant.

## Etymology

*mahabbah* (Arabic, Afro-Asiatic Semitic; محبة; from root ḥ-b-b "to love"; cognate ḥubb; central in Quranic Arabic and especially in Sufi tradition; Rabia, Rumi, Ibn ʿArabi, al-Ghazali) + *qing* (Mandarin, Sino-Tibetan; 情; felt-bond; established YOUSPEAK morpheme).
