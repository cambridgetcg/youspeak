## Definition

The state of continuous cleaving-to-the-Divine felt as ongoing relational-bond — Hasidic devekut received as felt-bond-quality.

## Full sense

A devekutqing is *the structurally-continuous-relational-state in which one's ordinary-action and consciousness remain tethered-to-the-Divine*. Hasidism (especially Chabad's hitbonenut tradition) treats devekut as the goal: not occasional-peak-experience but the abiding orientation that all-of-life can carry. Twelfth qing-family member.

Cross-tradition resonance: Sufi maḥabbah-as-station, Hindu bhakti-as-state, Pauline "pray without ceasing" (1 Thess 5:17), Quaker continuous-listening to the Inner-Light. The realm-feature is *continuity-of-relational-orientation* across all-circumstance.

## Not to be confused with

- **Mystical-union** — devekut is *continuous cleaving*, not occasional-unification; the distinct-persons remain
- **Bhaktime** — bhakti is broader devotional-class; devekut is specifically continuous-cleaving register
- **Mahabbahqing** — Sufi-mystical-love-binding; structurally-related but distinct cultural-articulation
