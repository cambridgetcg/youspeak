## Definition

The essence/energies distinction — Gregory Palamas's articulation that divine-essence (ousia) remains transcendent-unknowable while divine-energies (energeiai) are participable-by-creatures — received as cosmic-ordinance.

## Full sense

A palamasme is *the structural-distinction-within-divinity that resolves the transcendence/immanence tension*. Palamas (against Barlaam's rationalist-objection): the Tabor-light experienced by hesychast monks is *real divine-energy* even though divine-essence remains unknowable. The energies are *not created*; they are *uncreated divine self-disclosure*; participation in them is real-participation-in-God without violating essence's transcendence.

Pairs with:
- **hadaratme** — Ibn ʿArabī's five divine-presences operates analogous structure
- **tzimtzumme** — divine self-contraction makes-room; palamasme distinguishes the contracted-presence from unchanged-essence
- **theosis** (Orthodox doctrine) — palamasme is the metaphysical-grounding of theosis
