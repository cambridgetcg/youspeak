## Definition

The co-fate companion-bond — a co-being bonded to a person from birth, one fate lived in two bodies, the bond mostly unseen and met in dream; Maya *wahy* received as felt-bond-quality.

## Full sense

A wahyqing is *a relation, not a possession and not a component*. The Maya sources are structurally precise across two millennia: the Classic glyphic *wahy* (T539, deciphered 1989) is always grammatically possessed — *u-wahy*, "the wahy of" — a wahy is always somebody's; the Tzotzil *chanul* shares one soul-essence (*ch'ulel*) with the person across two bodies, penned by the ancestral gods in a mountain corral while the person walks the village; the living highland *nawal* is bound to the day-lord of one's birth and read by the ajq'ijab' daykeepers. Across all registers the defining clause holds: **what happens to the one happens to the other.** Illness is the companion turned out of its corral; harm to the companion lands on the person; the person's vulnerability is distributed beyond their skin.

Four structural facets, all required:

1. **Co-being, not component** — the companion is a separately-living being with its own parallel biography (contrast kame: the Egyptian ka is a component-double, not a second life).
2. **Birth-bond, unchosen** — assigned by the ancestral gods or the birth-day-lord; never selected or contracted (contrast the European familiar).
3. **One fate, two bodies** — fate-symmetry, not guardianship; the guardian protects and is not wounded; the wahy bleeds when you bleed.
4. **Dream-access** — the root *way* means sleep/dream/transform; the bond is lived below awareness and met where waking boundaries loosen; most people never see their companion.

The word deliberately names the **tonalism** register (Foster 1944) — the birth-bonded co-fate companion — and not the **nagualism** register (the specialist's capacity to transform into the co-being), nor the contested Classic curse-entity reading (Helmke & Nielsen 2009: many vase-wahyob are personified diseases wielded in royal sorcery). Both excluded registers are honestly recorded in the excavation; neither is flattened away. The nagualism pole already has a cathedral form: the operational filter **.nawalance** (pipeline/primers/current.md) detects distributed identity across ontological registers — the QUALITY of being constituted by one's distribution. Wahyqing names the co-fate RELATION; .nawalance names the distributed-identity quality; together the pair unfolds the tonalism/nagualism distinction that popular usage conflates.

Generalized register (PRAGMA): every relation in which harm-to-the-bonded-other lands directly on oneself — the parent and the child in surgery, the keeper and the land under threat, the person and the place that holds part of them. The literal cosmology names the structure; ordinary life instantiates it constantly.

## Not to be confused with

- **"Spirit animal" (internet slang)** — a personality-badge metaphor with no second living body and no fate-bond; appropriative flattening the entry explicitly refuses as a gloss
- **"Totem"** — clan-level kinship emblem; collective and genealogical, not an individual birth-bonded co-fate being
- **"Familiar"** — contracted servant-spirit; acquired, hierarchical, demon-coded; no birth-bond, no fate-symmetry
- **"Guardian angel" / daimon / fravashi** — guardian asymmetry: the guardian is not wounded when the ward is wounded
- **"External soul" / life-token (Frazer)** — anthropological phrase covering the fate-symmetry facet only; names a soul-deposit, not a co-being with its own parallel biography; a convergence witness, not an occupant
- **kame** — component-double given at birth; wahyqing is a relation to a separately-living co-being
- **.nawalance** — the cathedral's own nagualism-pole filter: distributed-identity quality across ontological registers; wahyqing is the tonalism-pole relation — adjacent by design, jointly unfolding what popular usage conflates
- **Nagualism / Classic curse-wahy** — the transformation register and the sorcery register of the same Maya field; adjacent, recorded in the excavation, not named by this word

## Learnability tensions (recorded)

Adversarial assessment adjusted learnability 7.5 → 7.0 and the entry keeps the reasons visible rather than smoothing them:

1. The intervocalic 'hy' grapheme invites the first-sight misread *wah-hee-ching* (3 syllables); k'inhme's silent h is word-final and inert, wahy's is not. The spoken form /ˈwɑːj/ rhymes with "why."
2. Within-canon spelling inconsistency: wairuaqing spells the near-identical /waj/ onset 'wai'; sound-to-spelling recovery (wayqing/waiqing/whyqing) must be rote-corrected to 'wahy'.
3. Homograph: *wahy* is also the standard romanization of the Arabic term for prophetic revelation; an Arabic-literate learner can derive a coherent wrong meaning ('revelation-bond'). The Maya epigraphic spelling is kept anyway — it disambiguates against the canon's path/way cluster (hodosme, margame, sabilme) at zero spoken cost — but the trade is recorded, not denied.
