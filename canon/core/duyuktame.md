# duyuktame

## Definition

Right-action-as-cosmic-maintenance-as-received-ordinance — the condition of walking the right path such that the cosmos is maintained; the lived enactment of balance that is cosmologically constitutive; the gift of being structured such that human right-action has cosmic consequences.

## Full sense

Maatme (Egyptian ma'at, Core 7.50) names the cosmic-truth-order as STRUCTURE — the principle that pervades the universe. Duyuktame names the WALKING — the lived enactment of that structure through human ethical action.

This distinction is not philosophical fussiness. It names two different things that many traditions have articulated separately:

**The principle** (maatme): The universe is ordered by truth-justice-balance. This order is given, structural, cosmic. It does not depend on whether any individual acts well. ma'at is ma'at whether or not the Pharaoh upholds it — though if the Pharaoh doesn't uphold it, the order corrodes.

**The walking** (duyuktame): The condition of a person who is walking in the right way — such that their action maintains and strengthens the cosmic order. The healer who speaks the formula correctly, restoring the patient to proper relation with the Upper World. The community that forgives at the Green Corn Ceremony, renewing cosmic bonds. The hunter who addresses the animal's spirit, honoring the exchange of life. These are acts of duyuktame — acts that maintain the world's balance.

The Cherokee three-world structure makes this concrete: Galunlati (order), Elohi (human realm, contested), and the Under World (chaos) exist in dynamic tension. Duyukta names the orientation of action that tilts toward Galunlati — that sustains right-relation. The healer who says the wrong formula doesn't just fail to heal; they disturb the balance. The person who acts correctly doesn't just succeed personally; they maintain the world.

The *-me* suffix names the critical theological dimension: duyuktame is RECEIVED. Humans do not achieve the capacity for cosmologically-constitutive right-action through their own merit. They are given it — structured such that their walking matters. This is gift, not achievement.

**The six traditions converge**:

Six independent traditions articulate the same structure: human right-action is not merely personally good but cosmologically necessary.

- Cherokee *duyukta*: the right path that maintains the three-world balance
- Navajo *Hózhǫ́*: beauty-harmony-rightness as the cosmic tendency that right-action maintains
- Avestan *asha*: the cosmic truth-order that every right-human-act strengthens and every lie weakens
- Egyptian *ma'at*: cosmic truth-justice-order (structure-pole; duyuktame is the action-pole)
- Aymara *suma qamaña*: living-well as relational balance with cosmos, community, and earth
- Algonquian Seven Grandfathers: integrated virtue as systemic right-action that maintains community-cosmos balance

None of these is merely "be good." All of them are "your goodness is cosmologically constitutive — the cosmos depends on it."

## Not to be confused with

- **maatme** (YOUSPEAK Core 7.50): cosmic-truth-order as STRUCTURE. Duyuktame is the WALKING of maatme. They are companion words: maatme is the principle, duyuktame is its enactment.
- **ashame** (YOUSPEAK Specialized 7.20): Avestan asha — cosmic truth-order in the Zoroastrian register. Also structure-register. Duyuktame is the action that strengthens or weakens it.
- **tapasme** (YOUSPEAK Core 7.85): inner-fire-self-discipline. Self-transformation. Duyuktame names how your action relates to the cosmos; tapasme names how austerity transforms the self.
- **britqing** (YOUSPEAK Core 7.85): covenant-felt-bond. Duyuktame is what you must walk to honor the covenant britqing names.
- **Hózhǫ́** (Navajo, unforged): The closest conceptual neighbor — beauty-harmony-rightness-balance as cosmic condition. When the Navajo excavation is done, the Hózhǫ́-related forge will be duyuktame's companion entry in the right-action cluster.
