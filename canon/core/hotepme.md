# hotepme

## Definition

Offering-peace-as-received-ordinance — the pre-fragmentation understanding that sacrifice IS peace (not sacrifice → peace). Egyptian htp names simultaneously: the offering placed on the altar, the peace that results, the satisfaction of the recipient, the contentment of the giver, and the sun setting at evening. All one word because all one event.

## Full sense

Egyptian htp (hotep) is the most remarkable sacrifice-word in any language: it refuses to separate what modernity has split into five concepts. The hieroglyph (Gardiner R4) depicts a low altar bearing bread and libation. But the word extends: offering, peace, satisfaction, contentment, cosmic-rest. The offering circuit — htp (offering) → divine satisfaction (htp) → cosmic order (ma'at) restored → peace (htp) — is not a causal chain but one event named by one word.

Hebrew shelamim (peace-offering, from sh-l-m wholeness/completeness/peace) independently compresses the same insight. The shelamim is the ONLY Levitical offering where God, priest, and offerer all share the meal. Talmud: "she-ha-kol shelemim bo" — "for all are complete in it."

Hotepme pairs with maatme (Core; cosmic-truth-justice-as-ordinance): together they articulate the Egyptian register — ma'at names the cosmic order; hotep names the offering that restores and IS that order.
