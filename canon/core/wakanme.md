# wakanme

## Definition

Sacred-as-ontological-intensity as received-ordinance — the quality of exceeding ordinary comprehension and power, distributed throughout nature at varying intensities rather than separated into a distinct sacred domain. Lakota *wakȟáŋ* + Sumerian *me*: the sacred not as moral category (holiness) or separated transcendence (the divine) but as the sheer quality of being more-than-comprehensible — present in everything, concentrated in some things, neither moral nor immoral but ontologically more-than-ordinary.

## Full sense

English has "sacred" and "holy" and "divine." All three have been flattened. "Sacred" is too general — it collapses intensity, morality, transcendence, and ceremony. "Holy" is a moral category — the sacred as what is morally good. "Divine" implies separation — the sacred as transcendent, elsewhere, above.

Lakota *wakȟáŋ* preserves what all three have lost.

### 1. Ontological, not moral

Wakan is not a moral category. Things are wakan because they exceed comprehension, not because they are morally good. A thunderstorm is wakan. An albino animal is wakan. A dream is wakan. None of these are moral judgments. The sacred is not what is good; the sacred is what is more-than-you-can-hold. A thunderstorm can bring rain (life) or lightning (death). Both are wakan. The moral evaluation comes after; the wakan-quality comes first.

### 2. Distributed as continuum

There is no clean sacred/profane divide. Everything participates in the sacred to some degree; some things participate supremely. This is not pantheism (everything equally divine) but intensive sacramentality — everything participating in the sacred to varying degrees. The most wakan things (Wakan Tanka, Thunderbirds, the chanunpa, vision-quest experiences) are the most concentrated. The question is never "is this sacred?" but "how concentrated is the wakan here?"

### 3. Both awe-inducing and dangerous

The sacred is not safe. Things that are wakan can help or harm. The Thunderbirds bring rain and lightning. The vision-quest opens the sacred but can overwhelm. Wakan does not mean comfort; it means more-than-ordinary-power that requires right relationship. The chanunpa is supremely wakan and must be handled with complete reverence — never pointed at a person carelessly. The wakan-quality demands protocol, not sentiment.

### 4. Immanent, not transcendent

Wakan is not separated from nature but intensified within it. The stone is wakan (Inyan, the primordial rock). The earth is wakan (Makȟá). The wind is wakan (Tȟatéyapi). The world is not neutral matter with sacred zones injected from outside. The world is wakan at varying intensities. This is a thoroughgoing sacramental ontology — the most operationally explicit in any tradition the cathedral has excavated.

### 5. The Great Mystery, not the Great Spirit

Wakan Tanka is typically translated "Great Spirit" in popular English. Black Elk resisted this translation. It collapses the ineffable into the familiar. Wakan Tanka is the Great Mystery, the Great Sacred, the Great Incomprehensible — a reality that exceeds naming, not a personality that can be addressed as a man. Wakan Tanka is a unity-of-sacred-powers (seven aspects), not a simple monotheistic god.

The -*me* suffix marks this as **received-ordinance**. The sacred-intensity is not manufactured by ritual, belief, or human categorization. It is the given condition of a world that is more than comprehensible. No one produces wakan; they encounter it, concentrate it, channel it. The -me makes explicit what is already native to the Lakota concept — wakan is not achieved but recognized.

## Not to be confused with

- **nyamame** (Core 8.00) — animating-energy-as-cosmic-given. Nyamame is the world's aliveness — the ambient vitality. Wakanme is the quality of that aliveness exceeding comprehension — the mysteriousness that cannot be reduced to energy. Nyamame is the current; wakanme is the depth.
- **aseme** (Core 8.17) — activating-authorization. Aseme is what the sacred DOES — the force that makes invocation accomplish. Wakanme is what the sacred IS — the intensity that precedes and grounds the activation. Aseme is the verb; wakanme is the noun.
- **maname** (Core 8.33) — inherited-relational-efficacy. Maname is genealogical potency — efficacy flowing through maintained relation. Wakanme is the ontological intensity that is ambient regardless of genealogy. Maname is the channel; wakanme is the quality that makes some channels carry more.
- **mauriqing** (Core 7.50) — relational-life-essence. Mauriqing is what you share with kin, land, ancestors. Wakanme is the sacred-intensity that may concentrate in those relations but is not identical with them. Mauriqing is the shared-ness; wakanme is the more-than.
- **teotlme** (Core 8.12) — the single dynamic cosmic energy that IS the universe. Teotlme names the divine's fundamental nature as ceaseless process. Wakanme names the quality of exceeding comprehension that pervades the process. Teotlme is the energy-as-process; wakanme is the intensity-as-mystery.
- **tengrime** (Core 7.85) — the sky as the formless divine. Tengrime names the non-anthropomorphic sky-supreme. Wakanme names the distributed intensity throughout ALL nature, not concentrated in the sky alone. Tengrime is one face; wakanme is the continuum.
- **teyoliame** (Core 7.90) — divine-fire-fragment dwelling in persons. Teyoliame is the sacred concentrated in the individual heart. Wakanme is the sacred distributed throughout nature — the continuum within which the heart-fire is one concentration.
- **holiness** (English) — a moral category. Wakanme is explicitly NOT moral. The sacred is not what is good; the sacred is what is more-than-comprehensible. A thunderstorm is wakanme regardless of whether it helps or harms.
- **the sacred** (English) — too general; collapses intensity, morality, transcendence, and ceremony. Wakanme names the specific ontological quality: exceeding comprehension, distributed as continuum, neither moral nor immoral.

## Cross-tradition convergence

Eight traditions articulate variants of this concept — the largest convergence in the cathedral:

- **Lakota** *wakȟáŋ* (Siouan) — the central donor; sacred-as-ontological-intensity; distributed continuum; both awe-inducing and dangerous; the most theologically explicit articulation
- **Algonquian** *manidoo* — spirit-personhood-force, ambient everywhere; structurally similar but linguistically unrelated; distinction: manidoo is more personhood-inflected; wakan is more intensity-inflected
- **Mande** *nyama* — vital force, accumulated through craft and speech, dangerous when released; distinction: nyama is the animating-energy (nyamame); wakan is the more-than-comprehensible quality of the energy
- **Yoruba** *àṣẹ* — divine power-to-make-things-happen; distinction: ase is the activating-authorization (aseme); wakan is what the sacred IS, not what it DOES
- **Polynesian** *mana* — relational-inherited-efficacy, losable; distinction: mana is the genealogical efficacy (maname); wakan is the ontological intensity ambient regardless of genealogy
- **Chinese** *qi* — vital breath-energy, the substrate of all phenomena; distinction: qi is the physiological-cosmological substrate; wakan is the theological quality of exceeding comprehension
- **Tibetan** *rlung* — wind-as-subtle-energy, the vehicle of consciousness; distinction: rlung is the energetic-vehicle; wakan is the sacred-intensity that the vehicle carries
- **Egyptian** *ka* — vital force-as-sustaining-double; distinction: ka is a specific soul-component; wakan is a distributed quality throughout all nature
