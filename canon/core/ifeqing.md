# ifeqing

## Definition

Relational-warmth-love-as-felt-bond — Yoruba ìfẹ́ understood as the love that expands: the relational-warmth between creature and CREATOR, the heart-widening that mirrors creation's widening of the world; love-of-DIVINE in the Yoruba register.

## Full sense

Ifeqing names what the Yoruba tradition articulates most distinctively about love-of-DIVINE: love IS expansion. The verb fẹ́ means "to widen, to expand." The city Ilé-Ifẹ̀ — "House of Expansion" — is where Odùduwà widened the earth from primordial waters. When the heart loves, it widens. When the world was created, it widened. The same movement, the same word.

Yoruba theological tradition positions ìfẹ́ within a tripartite architecture of complete love:
- **ìfẹ́** — warmth-bond, the relational-warmth-that-draws
- **àánú** — compassionate coolness/mercy (the *tùtù* dimension: sacred coolness, clarity, divine favor)
- **ìdáríjì** — restorative forgiveness that repairs broken relationship

Together these three constitute complete love. Ifeqing names the warmth-pole specifically — necessary but not sufficient; it implies the other two as companions.

The proverb *"Ìfẹ́ tí kò ní ìwà je bí iná tí kò ní omi"* — "Love without character is like fire without water" — positions ìwà (character) as the container of ìfẹ́. Love-without-character is destructive fire. Love-within-character is life-giving warmth. Ifeqing carries this ethical-container-requirement implicitly.

Within the àṣẹ-orí-ìwà framework: àṣẹ flows from Olódùmarè → orí carries destiny → ìwà realizes or squanders it → ìfẹ́ is the love-warmth-that-draws-the-soul toward alignment. Ifeqing names this drawing-warmth specifically — the felt-bond of the love that draws creature toward CREATOR.

Ọ̀ṣun, the orisha most associated with ìfẹ́, was the sole female deity among the original orisha sent to create earth. When the male orisha excluded her, creation failed. Her "gentle power" — ìfẹ́-as-cosmogonic-necessity — restored life. Love is not decorative in Yoruba cosmogony; it is structurally necessary for creation to proceed. Ifeqing carries this cosmogonic weight.

Ifeqing distinguishes from sister-forges:
- **ahavame** (Hebrew) — commanded-love-as-disposition; the v'ahavta commands
- **mahabbahqing** (Arabic-Sufi) — felt-mystical-binding; with fanāʾ-dissolution
- **bhaktime** (Sanskrit-Hindu) — disciplined-devotional-self-surrender
- **ifeqing** (Yoruba) — relational-warmth-that-expands

Four traditions, four aspects of the same convergence-cluster. Each emphasizes different dimensions:
- ahavame: COMMANDED → COMMITTED (will)
- mahabbahqing: MYSTICAL → DISSOLVED (ecstasy)
- bhaktime: DISCIPLINED → SURRENDERED (devotion)
- ifeqing: RELATIONAL → EXPANDED (warmth)

Ifeqing is the most everyday register of love-of-DIVINE — the heart warming toward the DIVINE. Not yet commanded, not yet mystical, not yet disciplined in practice. Just: the warmth arrives, the heart widens, the bond is felt. This is the ground the other three registers build on.

## Etymology
