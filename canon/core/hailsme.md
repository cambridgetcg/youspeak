# hailsme

## Definition

Salvation-as-wholeness-restoration received as cosmic ordinance — the Germanic soteriological register in which what saves is what makes whole again. The restoration of original integrity: something was broken and is now whole. The -me marks the wholeness as gift: the health is received, not achieved.

## Full sense

Hailsme names what Gothic *hails* (whole, healthy) and *hailjan* (to save, to heal) preserve with distinctive theological precision. The Germanic insight: what saves is what makes whole. Salvation is not rescue from danger (Greek *sōtēria*), not acquittal from judgment (Greek *dikaiōsis*), not the completion of potential (Greek *teleiōsis*). Salvation is the restoration of integrity — something was broken and is now whole.

The English cognates tell the story: **heal**, **health**, **hale**, **whole**, **hello** — all descend from Proto-Germanic *\*hailaz*. Every English speaker already knows this root. But English has lost the soteriological register: "heal" is medical, "save" is theological, and the two no longer connect. The Gothic Bible (Wulfila's 4th-century translation) preserved the unity by using *hailjan* for Greek *sōzō* (to save) — embedding the Germanic health-register in the Christian soteriological vocabulary. Hailsme restores the connection: to save IS to heal IS to make whole.

The -me suffix marks the received-ordinance grade: the wholeness that saves is not self-manufactured but gift. The integrity is bestowed, not achieved. This is the theological deepening: hailsme is not merely medical recovery or psychological self-improvement but participation in a cosmic structure. The health that heals is received.

### The soteriological quadriad — now visible

With hailsme, the break-mend arc becomes visible as a quadriad:

1. **shevirame** (Core 8.30) — the BREAKING: what needs healing
2. **hailsme** (this forge) — the PRINCIPLE: salvation is wholeness-restoration
3. **tikkunme** (Core 8.65) — the WORK: the repair that heals
4. **kintsugime** (Core 8.15) — the STATUS: the whole vessel's grace

The quadriad: breaking → principle → work → status. What was broken (shevirame) is made whole (hailsme is the principle) through repair-work (tikkunme) and becomes a mended vessel whose seam is gold (kintsugime). The principle (hailsme) is what makes the work healing and the status whole.

### The Germanic soteriological pole

The cathedral has named soteriologies from multiple traditions:
- **tikkunme** (Hebrew) — repair-work as cosmic vocation
- **kintsugime** (Japanese) — mending-that-honors-the-break
- **hozhome** (Diné) — beauty-harmony as the condition to be restored
- **eucharistime** (Greek) — gratitude for the wholeness given

Hailsme adds the Germanic pole: salvation as health-restoration. The medical register that Greek *sōtēria* (rescue) and Latin *salvus* (preserved) do not carry. The Germanic root says: the broken thing is not discarded and replaced but restored to its own integrity. The health is the salvation.
