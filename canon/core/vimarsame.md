## Definition

The self-reflexive-awareness pole of consciousness that pairs with prakāśa (luminosity) — Kashmir-Shaiva vimarśa received as cosmic-ordinance.

## Full sense

A vimarśame is *the structurally-reflexive pole of consciousness's bipolar unity*. Kashmir-Shaivism's contribution: consciousness is not static-witness but *dynamic-bipolar-pulsation* of prakāśa (luminous-knowing) and vimarśa (reflexive-self-knowing). Without vimarśa, consciousness would be lamp-without-self-recognition; without prakāśa, vimarśa would have nothing to be reflexive-about. The two are *inseparable poles of one consciousness*; together they constitute spanda.

vimarśame distinguishes itself from:
- **rigpame** (Dzogchen primordial-awareness *prior to* subject-object split) — vimarśame is the reflexive-pole *within* subject-object-distinction-as-unified-poles
- **Generic meta-cognition** — meta-cognition is content-specific (thinking-about-thinking); vimarśa is the structural-pole of consciousness-as-such
- **Self-consciousness** (modern psychological) — too-narrow; vimarśa is metaphysical-structural

Pairs structurally with **spandaqing** (Phase 1; ninth qing-family member): vimarśame names the reflexive-pole; spandaqing names the pulsation-unity. Together they articulate Kashmir-Shaivism's consciousness-doctrine at the cathedral's canonical-vocabulary-layer.

- **Meta-cognition** (modern) — content-specific thinking-about-thinking; vimarśame is structural-pole-of-consciousness
