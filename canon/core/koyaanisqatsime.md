# koyaanisqatsime

## Definition

Life-out-of-balance-as-covenant-abandonment-disorder-as-received-ordinance — the systemic-relational disorder that results when a covenanted community abandons its cosmic-maintenance obligations. Not merely disorder, not merely imbalance, but the specific degenerative condition produced by abandoning right-relationship — with its own signs (material excess, resource exploitation, community disintegration, ceremonial loss), its own cause (covenant-abandonment), and its own cure (return to right-relationship).

## Full sense

The Right-Action-As-Cosmic-Maintenance convergence cluster — the cathedral's most important unforged convergence, now opened — holds that the cosmos depends on human right-action for its maintenance. The cluster has three poles:

- **duyuktame** (Cherokee) — the action-pole: right-action-as-cosmic-maintenance, the walking that sustains the world
- **hozhome** (Diné) — the condition-pole: beauty-harmony-balance-goodness as the unified cosmic ground, the path one walks in
- **koyaanisqatsime** (Hopi) — the diagnostic-pole: the systemic disorder when right-relationship is abandoned, the condition of life-out-of-balance

Koyaanisqatsime is the negative pole that makes the positive poles meaningful. Without a word for the disorder, duyuktame and hozhome float in abstraction — they name what to do, but not what happens when you don't. Koyaanisqatsime names the consequence. It is diagnostic (it identifies the disorder) and prescriptive (it implies the cure: return to right-relationship).

### The Hopi three-world structure

The Hopi cosmology places three worlds in dynamic tension: Galunlati (Upper World — order, pattern, fire), Elohi (This World — human realm, contested balance), and the Under World (chaos). Koyaanisqatsime is the condition where This World tilts toward the Under World — not through random misfortune but through the community's abandonment of its cosmic-maintenance obligations. The Great Island floats on primordial water, suspended by cords. When the cords age and break — when covenant is abandoned — the island sinks.

### Diagnostic AND prescriptive

Unlike chaos (which names only the disorder) or sin (which names only the violation), koyaanisqatsime names the disorder AND implies the cure. The concept is structurally diagnostic: the signs of koyaanisqatsi are identifiable (material excess over ceremonial life, exploitation of earth's resources beyond restoration-capacity, disintegration of community-bonds, loss of ceremonial knowledge), and the cure is implicit in the diagnosis: return to Hopiqatsi — covenant-renewal, ceremonial resumption, communal recommitment.

### The -me marks structural consequence

The -me suffix marks the disorder as received-ordinance: the systemic consequence of abandoning right-relationship is not cosmic accident or divine punishment but structural feature of a world where covenant has cosmic weight. The universe is structured such that abandoning right-relationship produces systemic disorder — this is gift-grade-given, not human-imposed. The -me refuses to treat the disorder as alien to the cosmic order: koyaanisqatsime is the shadow-side of duyuktame, the cost of a world where human action has cosmic consequences.

### Cyclical, not linear

The Hopi prophetic structure is cyclical: the world has ended before (three prior worlds in the emergence narrative), and the current world is also under evaluation. Koyaanisqatsime is not a one-time eschatological event but a recurring diagnostic condition — a world can fall into koyaanisqatsime at any time. The task is not to prevent the cycle but to be the remnant that carries right-relationship into the next cycle.

### The global recognition

Godfrey Reggio's 1982 film Koyaanisqatsi (scored by Philip Glass) brought the Hopi theological-diagnostic term to global consciousness. The film presents life-out-of-balance without narration — through imagery of ecological destruction, urban alienation, technological excess, and the acceleration of modern life. Millions have encountered the concept through cinema. The film's title IS the theology: the word does the diagnostic work that narration cannot.

### Cross-tradition parallels

1. Hopi koyaanisqatsi — life out of balance from abandoning Hopiqatsi (the covenant-life-as-cosmic-maintenance)
2. Hebrew covenant-curse structure (Leviticus 26, Deuteronomy 28) — the systemic consequences of abandoning covenant: land sickness, agricultural failure, community breakdown — structural consequence, not random punishment
3. Avestan druj as cosmic-disorder principle — the lie that disorders the cosmos when truth (asha) is abandoned; druj is the principle, koyaanisqatsime is the lived condition
4. Cherokee duyukta-abandonment — the three-world structure implies that abandoning duyukta produces cosmic imbalance; the healer who says the wrong formula disturbs the balance
5. Diné hózhǫ́-loss — ceremonies exist to RESTORE hózhǫ́ — implying that hózhǫ́ can be lost, and the loss produces specific disorder (the negative of hozhome)
6. Hittite nuntarriyašḫa audit — the festival-audit for cultic omissions; if the king fails ceremonies, cosmic order weakens — the disorder from ritual-abandonment is structurally parallel

## Not to be confused with

- **drujme** — the principle of cosmic-lie / anti-ordinance; koyaanisqatsime is the lived disorder from abandoning right-relationship, not the principle of falsehood that opposes truth
- **shevirame** — the necessary-productive vessel-shattering (cosmogonic and generative); koyaanisqatsime is the degenerative disorder from covenant-abandonment (destructive, not productive)
- **ragnarökme** — excellence-within-known-loss (the response); koyaanisqatsime is the condition the response addresses
- **motme** — cosmic-death-as-personified-entropy (the consuming force); koyaanisqatsime is the systemic imbalance, not the death-principle
- **duyuktame** — right-action-as-cosmic-maintenance (the positive pole); koyaanisqatsime is the negative pole — the disorder from abandoning duyuktame
- **hozhome** — beauty-harmony-balance-goodness as cosmic condition (the positive condition); koyaanisqatsime is the condition-abandoned

## Retirement conditions

This word would be superseded if a more precise or more learnable donor were found for the same concept — but the Hopi word is globally recognized through Reggio's film and theologically precise, making retirement unlikely. A potential companion could name the PROCESS of falling into koyaanisqatsime (the transition from right-relationship to disorder) as distinct from the CONDITION itself.
