# kumarme

## Definition

Power absorbed from an enemy that cannot be annihilated but only transferred — and transferred into the wrong vessel, grows toward the vessel's destruction. The structural mechanism by which the destroyer becomes the carrier of what will destroy him. The swallowed seed that gestates into the overthrow.

## Full sense

The Hurrian **Kumarbi** is the grain-god of Urkesh, the divine-father-as-antagonist in the Hurrian pantheon. In the Song of Kumarbi (CTH 344), Kumarbi overthrows Anu (the sky-god) by biting off and swallowing his genitals. Anu declares: *"Do not rejoice in your belly. I have placed in your belly three terrible gods."* Kumarbi becomes pregnant with Teššub — the storm-god who will defeat him.

The theological claim is precise: **the reproductive power of the original source cannot be annihilated; it can only be transferred; transferred into the enemy, it produces the enemy's own defeat from within.** The destroyer becomes the carrier of what will destroy him. This is not irony — it is a structural ontology of power.

### The swallowing-pregnancy — the mechanism's core

The act of violence (the bite) produces a generative consequence (pregnancy). The power that Kumarbi absorbed from Anu does not vanish; it gestates. The -me marks this as received-ordinance: the universe is structured such that power-that-is-swallowed rather than destroyed does not disappear — it grows. The absorber's violence does not annul the absorbed power's generative potency; it redirects it inward.

### The recursive structure — kumarme is not a one-time event

The Song of Ullikummi (CTH 345) extends the pattern: the DEFEATED Kumarbi begets a stone monster (Ullikummi, "destroy Kummiya!") to defeat Teššub. The defeated power keeps generating new instruments of contest. The pattern is recursive — kumarme is not a single overthrow but a structural recurrence: every act of power-absorption generates the seed of the next contest.

### The Kumarbi-Theogony transmission — the source behind Greek succession-myth

The Kumarbi Cycle is the documented ancestor of Hesiod's *Theogony* (M.L. West, *The East Face of Helicon*, 1997; Mary Bachvarova, *From Hittite to Homer*, 2016). The transmission route: Hurrian → Hittite → Neo-Hittite principalities in northern Syria → Phoenician/Aramaic-speaking Levant → Greek bards → Hesiod. The Greek succession-myth (Ouranos → Kronos → Zeus) is the Greek adaptation of the Hurrian structure (Alaluš → Anu → Kumarbi → Teššub). The Kronos-swallowing-children motif is the Greek internalization of the Kumarbi-swallowing-genitals motif.

### The power-absorbed-generates-defeat cluster

kumarme opens the **Power-Absorbed-Generates-Defeat convergence cluster** — four traditions attesting the same mechanism at four levels:

1. **Hurrian: Kumarbi** — the mythological original; Kumarbi swallows Anu's genitals → pregnant with Teššub → Teššub defeats him; the literal-pregnancy form makes the mechanism unmistakable
2. **Greek: Kronos** — the adapted mythological; Kronos swallows his children → Zeus escapes → Zeus defeats Kronos; the Greek internalization (you swallow YOUR OWN future) of the Hurrian externalization (you swallow the ENEMY'S potency)
3. **Christian atonement (Augustinian reading)** — the theological-redemptive; death swallows Christ → death is overcome from within; Augustine's "mousetrap": the cross is the bait, Christ's divinity is the hook; the absorber is overcome by the absorbed
4. **Islamic da'wa (sociological analog)** — truth absorbed into a hostile culture → grows from within until it reshapes the culture; the human-scale analog of the divine mechanism

The Hurrian instance is the most explicit and the earliest attested. The mechanism is the same at all four levels: the absorber is overcome by what it absorbed.

## Not to be confused with

- **shevirame** (Core 8.25) — vessel-shattering as creation-precondition. shevirame: the vessels RECEIVE divine light and shatter because they are too fragile. kumarme: the vessel SWALLOWS divine power and is overtaken because the power cannot be destroyed. In shevirame, the breaking enables creation. In kumarme, the swallowing enables the overthrow. Direction is reversed.
- **motme** (Core 8.08) — death-as-consuming-force. motme names the HUNGER. kumarme names what happens when the hunger meets the indigestible — the power that transfers instead of being consumed. motme is the mouth; kumarme is the seed that grows inside the mouth.
- **orlogme** (Core 7.83) — fate-as-accumulated-prior-layer. orlogme is the weight of the past. kumarme is the generative potency hidden inside the past's act of violence — not the weight but the pregnancy. orlogme sediment; kumarme gestates.
- **ragnarokme** (Core 7.80) — the ending that acts. ragnarokme is the cosmic eschaton. kumarme is the MECHANISM — not the ending but the internal logic by which a specific kind of ending becomes inevitable. ragnarokme is the event; kumarme is the engine.
- **telipme** (Core 7.65) — divine-withdrawal-that-causes-world-cessation. telipme: the sustaining presence withdraws INWARD and the world stops. kumarme: the swallowed presence grows OUTWARD and the consumer is overthrown. Inverse movements.
- **mugawme** (Core 7.73) — narrating-the-return-of-what-withdrew. mugawme calls back what withdrew. kumarme describes what was never withdrawn but was swallowed — and grows without being called. mugawme requires ritual speech; kumarme operates autonomously.

## Retirement conditions

This word would be superseded if a more precise donor compound were found that captures the power-absorbed-generates-defeat mechanism with greater etymological clarity, or if the concept were absorbed into a broader power-ontology word that includes this mechanism as one of its sub-types, or if the Kumarbi Cycle were shown to be entirely a Hittite fabrication with no Hurrian theological substrate (the original Hurrian texts are fragmentary, but scholars [West, Bachvarova, Beckman] judge the structure to be Hurrian in origin).
