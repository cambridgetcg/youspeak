# songlineme

## Definition

The sung-into-existence navigation track received as divine ordinance — the path that IS the singing that IS the world's maintenance.

## Full sense

A songlineme is the practice in which song, walking, navigation, and world-maintenance are a single indivisible act. The Aboriginal Australian songline — a path across land or sky marking the route of an ancestral creation-being, encoded in a sequence of verses where each verse corresponds to a specific geographic feature — received as divine ordinance through the Sumerian *me*.

The song IS the map. To know the song is to know the route. The melodic contour encodes terrain independent of language, enabling navigation across language boundaries — Australia's 250+ language groups are not a barrier because the melody carries the geography. The rhythm of the song matches the rhythm of walking; the body itself becomes a navigation instrument.

The ontological claim is radical: the song created the land and singing re-creates-maintains the world. The songline is not a map laid over a pre-existing landscape — the song and the landscape are co-constitutive. To stop singing is not merely to forget knowledge; it is to let the world begin to unravel. The world requires ongoing vocal maintenance to persist.

The songline network covers the entire Australian continent — no part of Australia falls outside it. Some songlines are a few kilometers long; others traverse thousands. Together they constitute what may be the largest integrated geographic information system ever created by a non-literate culture, maintained for at least 7,000 years, possibly far longer.

The -me marks the track as received-ordinance: the path is not constructed but received. The ancestor walked it into existence, and the custodian walks-sings it again as divine obligation. The world's persistence is gift maintained by gift. This is the performative-ontological claim at its most concrete: the song IS the map IS the world-maintenance.

### The tjukurpame-songlineme dyad

Tjukurpame (the Dreaming as cosmic substrate) and songlineme (the sung navigation track) form a structural dyad: tjukurpame is the SUBSTRATE — the integrated reality of land + ancestor + story + person + song + law; songlineme is the TRACK through the substrate — how you MOVE through what IS. The Dreaming is the land; the songline is the route across the land. Tjukurpame is what IS; songlineme is how you navigate-maintain what IS. Both are Aboriginal Australian + Sumerian me compounds; both are protocol-sensitive entries honoring Aboriginal custodianship.

### The Performative-Ontology cluster: three technologies

Songlineme extends the Performative-Ontology convergence cluster with a NAVIGATIONAL member:
- **mugawme** (narrative performance) — telling the return-story IS the return-bringing action (Hittite mugawar; voice enacts by narrating)
- **katsintawime** (embodied performance) — becoming the katsina through mask-dance IS the cosmic-maintenance (Hopi katsina+tawi; body enacts by becoming)
- **songlineme** (navigational performance) — singing the track IS the world-maintenance (Australian songline; song enacts by navigating)

Three technologies of the same structure: the utterance IS the act. Voice, body, and song-as-navigation — each a different modality of performative ontology, each from a different tradition, each received as divine ordinance through the -me.

### The underwater songlines — deep-time memory

Some songlines describe geographic features now submerged beneath the ocean — coastal land that was above sea level before the post-glacial sea-level rise 12,000-7,000 years ago. The songlines have been transmitted accurately across at least 350 generations, preserving memory of geography no living person has ever seen. Patrick Nunn and Nicholas Reid (2016) analyzed 21 coastal traditions with specific descriptions of now-submerged landmarks. Elder Timmy Douglas recognized, in a song he had known all his life, watering holes located beneath 14 meters of ocean — features that had been dry land more than 7,000 years ago. He had been carrying, in a song, the memory of a place he had never visited, that no human being had visited in seven millennia.

This is the most extreme case of cultural memory persistence ever documented. It is theological data: the songline is not merely a navigational aid but a memory technology that maintains the world across geological timescales.

### Cross-tradition parallels

1. **Australian Aboriginal songlines** — the primary donor; the most complete attestation; the only tradition fusing navigation + world-maintenance + cross-language melodic persistence
2. **Hittite mugawar** (mugawme) — narrating the return IS the efficacious act; the reactive counterpart to songlineme's continuous maintenance
3. **Hebrew davar** — word-as-event; speech DOES things; converges with song doing geography, but Hebrew davar is single creative act, songlineme is continuous distributed creation
4. **Hindu tirtha** — sacred crossing-places, pilgrimage routes as trade corridors and ceremonial paths; structurally parallel but converges on centers rather than distributed paths
5. **Ojibwe Midewiwin routes** — waterways as ceremonial + trade corridors with birchbark scroll maps; the visual parallel to the songline's auditory mapping
6. **Finnish synty** — origin-knowledge-as-power; singing the world's origins IS wielding the power that created them; converges on performative ontology but lacks geographic-navigational dimension
7. **Sámi joik** — singing as identification with the place/being, not description; the joik IS the reindeer; converges with the songline's claim that the song IS the land

## Not to be confused with

- **tjukurpame** — the Dreaming as cosmic substrate (land + ancestor + story + person + song + law); songlineme is the TRACK through the substrate, not the substrate itself
- **mugawme** — narrating the return of what withdrew (reactive performative); songlineme is continuous world-maintenance (ongoing performative)
- **ohentome** — sequential naming of the world as worship (a catalogue); songlineme is navigation of the world (a path)
- **duyuktame** — right-walking as cosmic maintenance (moral action); songlineme is right-singing as navigation (the track, not the tread)
- **liturgiame** — multi-modal worship in general; songlineme is the specific song + walk + navigation + world-maintenance fusion
- **morphame** — multi-modal projection of meaning; songlineme is the specific case where the projection IS a geographic path
