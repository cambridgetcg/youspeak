# aynime

## Definition

Reciprocity-as-cosmic-ordinance — the bidirectional mutual-feeding dynamic of reality, received as gift rather than human social construction. The cosmos as a network of obligations where every being feeds and is fed, where the feeding IS the bond, and the bond is the structure that holds reality together.

## Full sense

Aymara **ayni** (shared with Quechua, possibly pan-Andean substrate) is the principle of balanced reciprocal obligation governing relations between ALL beings — human, divine, natural, and dead. It is not exchange (market logic, transaction closed). It is never closed — an ongoing state of mutual indebtedness in which the debt IS the bond.

### The constitutive qualities

1. **Balanced but not simultaneous** — I help you plant today; you help me plant tomorrow. The asymmetry across time IS the relationship. The reciprocity is never settled; it is always in motion.
2. **Obligatory but not coerced** — failure to reciprocate is not punished by a court but by social isolation and, ultimately, cosmic imbalance. The obligation is ontological, not legal.
3. **Between ALL categories of being** — not just human-to-human, but human-to-earth (ch'alla libation), human-to-mountain (wilancha blood sacrifice), human-to-dead (Todos Santos feeding), and dead-to-living (the dead ensure rain and harvest). The reciprocity crosses every boundary.
4. **The condition of being a person** — in Aymara society, personhood is not granted to the individual but to the married couple (*jaqi*). Only full participants in the ayni network are persons. The solitary individual is not yet a person.

### The pachame-aynime pair

**Pachame** names the medium (space-time-world fusion — the undivided reality within which everything operates). **Aynime** names the dynamic (the bidirectional mutual feeding that is the operating principle within that medium). Together they complete the Aymara contribution to the cathedral's cosmological architecture: pachame is WHERE reality is, aynime is HOW reality runs.

### The ayni-mink'a-mit'a triad

Aymara labor reciprocity operates in three modes:
- **Ayni** — symmetrical reciprocal labor, household-to-household (the foundational mode)
- **Mink'a** — asymmetrical collective labor, community-for-individual
- **Mit'a** — rotational public labor, community-to-polity

Ayni is the foundational mode — the most intimate, the most ongoing, the most constitutive of personhood. The Inca Empire institutionalized all three at imperial scale; after the Conquest, the Spanish perverted mit'a into the silver-mining labor tax of Potosí.

### The three-world operation

Ayni operates across all three pachas — Alax Pacha (upper: solar festivals, ch'allas directed upward), Aka Pacha (this world: social reciprocity, ecological feeding), Manqha Pacha (lower: feeding the dead, the dead feeding the living). The reciprocity is not confined to the human; it is the structure that connects all levels of reality.

### The -me marks received-ordinance

The -me suffix marks the reciprocity as received-ordinance: the mutual-feeding structure is not a human social construction projected onto a neutral cosmos but the given structure of reality. The cosmos is gift-grade reciprocal. You do not invent ayni; you discover it and participate in it. The ch'alla (pouring for the earth before drinking) is aynime in its minimal form — not a symbol of reciprocity but reciprocity enacted.

### Cross-tradition parallels

1. **Aymara/Quechua** *ayni* — cosmic reciprocity; the pump at the heart of Andean life (Allen 1988)
2. **Akan** *nsaguo* — libation reciprocity; the pouring that feeds the ancestors, who in return ensure fertility
3. **Hebrew** *berith* — covenant as mutual obligation; God and people bound by reciprocal fidelity
4. **Zoroastrian** *hamkar* — the human as co-worker with Ahura Mazda; cosmic maintenance as collaborative
5. **Aboriginal Australian** *kanyini* — caring-connectedness-as-cosmic-principle; responsibility-to-care
6. **Māori** *utu* — reciprocity as cosmic balance; the obligation to return equivalent

## Example usage

> The ch'alla is aynime in its minimal form: pour for the earth first, then drink — the cosmos runs on mutual feeding, and the feeding is gift, not invention.

> She understood aynime when her neighbor helped with the harvest without asking: not charity, not exchange, but the ongoing reciprocal bond where the debt IS the relationship, and the relationship IS the structure that holds the world together.

> "Reciprocity" is aynime stripped of cosmic weight: the word names a social phenomenon but loses the ontological claim — that the cosmos itself is a network of mutual feeding, and the feeding is received-ordinance, not human arrangement.

## Not to be confused with

- **pachame** — space-time-world fusion (the medium); aynime is the reciprocal dynamic (the operating principle within the medium)
- **maatme** — cosmic truth-order (the principle); aynime is the reciprocal feeding (the dynamic that enacts the principle)
- **duyuktame** — right-action-as-maintenance (the unilateral walk); aynime is the bidirectional dynamic (the mutual feeding)
- **hopiqatsime** — covenant-life-as-maintenance (the lived-covenant); aynime is the reciprocity the covenant enacts (the dynamic, not the covenant-form)
- **mitakuyame** — all-are-related (the kinship-recognition); aynime is the feeding (the dynamic, not the recognition)
- **ubuntume** — humanity-through-relation (the personhood); aynime is the cosmic dynamic (not the anthropological condition)
- **danaqing** — gift-giving-as-felt-bond (one-directional); aynime is mutual and bidirectional (not giving but mutual feeding)

## Retirement conditions

This word would be superseded if a more precise compound from a different tradition better captured the cosmic-reciprocity dynamic — but ayni is the most extensively documented and most theologically concentrated reciprocity concept in the cathedral's archaeology. A potential companion could name the asymmetrical mode (mink'a — the community-works-for-individual form), but that would be a companion, not a replacement.
