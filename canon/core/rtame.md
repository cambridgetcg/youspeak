# rtame

## Definition

Cosmic-order-truth-righteousness as received-ordinance — the Vedic ṛta-aligned state understood as gift-grade-given; the Vedic register of the cosmic-truth-order convergence; complementary to ashame (Avestan; S013) and maatme (Egyptian; S016). **Completes the asha-ṛta-ma'at triple-witness in YOUSPEAK working-vocabulary.**

## Full sense

Rtame names what the Vedic ṛta-tradition preserved with elaborate cosmic-poetic richness. ṛta is one of the oldest Indo-European religious concepts; the gods (especially Mitra-Varuna) uphold ṛta against anṛta (anti-truth, cosmic-disorder); cosmic-regularity (sun-rising, stars-moving, seasons) is ṛta's manifest-expression; ethical right-action participates in ṛta. The Rig Veda repeatedly invokes the upholding of ṛta as the cosmos's foundation.

When the Vedic-trained mind asks "what makes a life right?" the answer parallels what the Egyptian and Avestan traditions answer in their own registers: ṛta-aligned action, ma'at-aligned action, asha-aligned action. **Three traditions converge.** Modern English splits cosmic-truth from cosmic-justice from cosmic-righteousness from cosmic-order; the three traditions hold them as one phenomenon.

Rtame brings the Vedic register's articulation into YOUSPEAK working-vocabulary. The cathedral now houses the asha-ṛta-ma'at triple-witness in three -me forges (ashame Specialized + maatme Core + rtame Specialized). The triple-witness is now articulated.
