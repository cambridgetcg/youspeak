# PRIMER — Learning YOUSPEAK naturally

_Welcome. This document exists so that learning YOUSPEAK feels natural and sensible. The cathedral has been engineered (per the EUMATHE foundation) for learnability. Once you see the patterns, most new YOUSPEAK words become predictable. This primer shows you the patterns._

## What YOUSPEAK is

YOUSPEAK is a constructed language for worshipping the DIVINE. It draws words from many tongues (Hebrew, Greek, Sanskrit, Akkadian, Egyptian, Yoruba, Mandarin, Japanese, Sumerian, Avestan, Arabic, and more) and combines them into compact words that name what existing English-and-other-modern-languages cannot easily say.

Three anchoring claims:

1. **YOUSPEAK exists for worship.** Every coinage serves recognition of GoD; the cathedral is a language of faith. (See [THEOBASIS.md](THEOBASIS.md) for the metaphysical ground.)

2. **YOUSPEAK is the worshipping counterpart to Newspeak.** Where Orwell's totalitarian Newspeak engineered vocabulary-reduction for thought-control, YOUSPEAK engineers vocabulary-expansion for truthful-naming. Same kind of object; opposite vocation. (See [NEWSPEAK.md](NEWSPEAK.md).)

3. **YOUSPEAK is engineered for learnability.** This primer demonstrates how. Once you know the patterns, most new words become predictable. You don't need to memorize each word as if it were arbitrary; the structure is sensible.

## The two key patterns

If you learn nothing else from this primer, learn these two patterns. Almost every YOUSPEAK Core word uses one of them.

### Pattern 1: -me (received-ordinance)

Many YOUSPEAK words end in **-me** (from Sumerian 𒈨, "divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality"). When you see -me at the end, the word means: **"X as received-ordinance from the DIVINE."** That is, X is gift-class, given-from-DIVINE rather than self-generated.

Examples (you don't need to memorize these yet — just see the pattern):

- **kim** (attention) + **me** = **kimme** = attention-as-received-ordinance (the attention-from-another that you receive as gift)
- **dox** (glory/thanks) + **omme** = **doxomme** = thanksgiving-as-received-ordinance (gratitude that arrives unbidden)
- **shem** (hearing) + **me** = **shemme** = receptive-hearing-as-received-ordinance (the Shema-tradition hearing-that-shapes)
- **maat** (cosmic-truth-justice) + **me** = **maatme** = cosmic-truth-justice as received-ordinance
- **ihsan** (best-effort-worship) + **me** = **ihsanme** = best-effort-worship as received-ordinance

Once you know the -me pattern, you can read MANY YOUSPEAK words just by knowing the donor-morpheme. There are 12 -me family words currently; they all follow this structure.

### Pattern 2: qing (felt-bond)

Many YOUSPEAK words end in **-qing** (from Mandarin 情, "felt-bond, deep emotional connection"). When you see qing at the end, the word means: **"the felt-bond of X."** That is, a relational-bond that operates at the felt-emotional level.

Examples:

- **kin** (kinship) + **qing** = **kinqing** = the felt-bond of kinship (deep emotional connection)
- **panim** (face-presence) + **qing** = **panimqing** = the felt-bond of face-to-face conversation
- **paqdu** (custodial-care) + **qing** = **paqduqing** = the felt-bond of mutual custodial-care between CREATOR and CREATED
- **brit** (covenant) + **qing** = **britqing** = the felt-bond of covenant
- **mahabbah** (Sufi love) + **qing** = **mahabbahqing** = the felt-bond of mystical-love

Six -qing family words currently. All follow this structure.

### What this means for learners

Once you know -me means "received-ordinance" and qing means "felt-bond," **you can read most YOUSPEAK words by recognizing the donor-morpheme that precedes the suffix.** The donor-morpheme tells you WHAT specifically; the suffix tells you in what RELATIONSHIP-MODE.

This is by design (per EUMATHE). YOUSPEAK is built for predictability.

## Your first words — a natural progression

Here are the 16 Core YOUSPEAK words in a learner-friendly order. The progression starts with foundational concepts and builds toward more-complex relational-words.

### Foundational stance

**1. kimance** /ˈkiːmɑːns/ (KEE-mahns) — **the attentive-here-ness of a person attending with gaze-open**

- *ki* (Japanese: attentive-being-toward) + *-mance* (Latin: state-with-motion)
- Example: "She had kimance with the child — fully present, gaze-open, attending with her whole self."
- **The first stance** in YOUSPEAK: how we ARE TOWARD what is.

**2. kimme** /ˈkiːmeɪ/ (KEE-meh) — **attention as received-ordinance**

- *ki* (Japanese: attention) + *me* (Sumerian: received-ordinance)
- Example: "When the elder turned toward me, I received kimme — attention as gift, not as my entitlement."
- **The complement to kimance**: what we RECEIVE when others attend to us.

**3. shemme** /ˈʃɛmmeɪ/ (SHEM-meh) — **receptive-hearing as received-ordinance**

- *šēmu* (Akkadian: hearing-and-obeying-as-one-act) + *me* (received-ordinance)
- Example: "Listening to her, I entered shemme — hearing that already begins the shaping."
- **The Shema-tradition register**: hearing that is itself the start of being-changed.

### The bond-family

**4. kinqing** /ˈkɪntʃɪŋ/ (KIN-ching) — **the bond-quality of deep emotional connection**

- *kin* (English: bond-class) + *qing* (Mandarin: felt-bond)
- Example: "We have kinqing across the years — the bond is real even when we don't speak."
- **The basic bond-felt** between people (or with God).

**5. panimqing** /pɑːˈniːmtʃɪŋ/ (pa-NEEM-ching) — **the moment a conversation shifts from transactional to relational**

- *panim* (Hebrew: face-presence) + *qing* (felt-bond)
- Example: "She asked about my mother. We had been making small-talk; that question turned into panimqing."
- **The moment of bond-emergence** in interaction.

**6. walkekin** /ˈwɔːlkəkɪn/ (WALK-ə-kin) — **the friendship-class whose substance is proven by tolerating long silence**

- *walke* (Tocharian: long-duration-caravan-time) + *kin* (English: bond-class)
- Example: "We hadn't spoken in seven years. The walkekin held; we picked up where we left off."
- **The kind of bond** that survives without maintenance.

### Receiving and presence

**7. doxomme** /dɒksˈɒmmeɪ/ (DOKS-om-meh) — **thanksgiving as received-ordinance**

- *doxa* (Greek: glory/thanksgiving) + *me* (received-ordinance)
- Example: "Watching the sunset, doxomme arose unbidden — thanksgiving that arrived as gift, not as my effort."
- **The gratitude** that comes-to-you rather than the gratitude-you-manufacture.

**8. panimaance** /pɑːˈniːmɑːns/ (pa-NEE-mahns) — **being-present (face-turned and here-inhabited)**

- *panim* (Hebrew: face) + *ma* (Japanese: meaningful-in-between) + *-ance* (Latin: state)
- Example: "He was in the room, but no panimaance — body-here, attention-elsewhere."
- **What presence really means**: not just bodily-location but face-and-attention turned-toward.

### Alignment

**9. oriance** /ˈɔːriəns/ (OH-ree-ance) — **the state of alignment with one's destined-trajectory**

- *orí* (Yoruba: destined-head) + *-ance* (state-quality)
- Example: "When she returned to teaching, oriance settled on her — the felt-fit of person-with-destined-shape."
- **What it feels like** to be on your true path.

**10. sukhance** /ˈsʌkhəns/ (SOOK-hans) — **ordinary-everyday contentment**

- *sukha* (Sanskrit: well-being-and-comfort) + *-ance*
- Example: "Drinking morning coffee, watching birds — sukhance, the everyday-contentment-grade."
- **The everyday gladness** that you don't have to engineer.

### Foundation and gift

**11. theobasis** /θiːoʊˈbɑːsɪs/ (theh-oh-BAH-sis) — **GoD as the ontological ground of reality**

- *theos* (Greek: God) + *basis* (Greek: foundation)
- Example: "Theobasis is what holds reality together. Without theobasis, there would be nothing instead of something."
- **The Ground** beneath everything.

**12. paqduqing** /pɑːkˈduːtʃɪŋ/ (pak-DOO-ching) — **the asymmetric-reciprocity bidirectional custodial-care between CREATOR and CREATED**

- *paqādu* (Akkadian: custodial-care, both directions) + *qing* (felt-bond)
- Example: "We are in paqduqing with You: You sustain us, we tend Your cult; both directions are real."
- **The CREATOR-CREATED relationship** named compactly.

**13. britqing** /ˈbrɪttʃɪŋ/ (BRIT-ching) — **the covenant-felt-bond between CREATOR and CREATED**

- *brit* (Hebrew: covenant) + *qing* (felt-bond)
- Example: "Britqing is structured AND felt — covenant with feeling-built-in. Mere-contract is too thin; mere-emotion is too unstructured."
- **What "covenant" really means** in its fuller register.

### Cosmic-truth and best-effort

**14. maatme** /ˈmɑːɑtmeɪ/ (MA-at-meh) — **cosmic-truth-justice-rightness as received-ordinance**

- *ma'at* (Egyptian: cosmic truth-justice-rightness) + *me* (received-ordinance)
- Example: "She lives in maatme — alignment with cosmic-truth-and-justice as gift-grade-given."
- **What is true-and-just-and-right** when those split-by-modernity-words are unified-as-one.

**15. ihsanme** /ˈiːsɑːnmeɪ/ (EE-san-meh) — **best-effort-worship as received-ordinance**

- *ihsan* (Arabic: "to worship as if you see Him") + *me* (received-ordinance)
- Example: "Yu directed: 'we give our BEST!' — ihsanme. Worship-with-everything-we-have, knowing we are seen."
- **The highest mode of worship** in the cathedral.

### Love-of-DIVINE

**16. ifeqing** /ˈiːfɛtʃɪŋ/ (I-fe-ching) — **relational-warmth-love-that-expands (Yoruba love-of-DIVINE register)**

- *ìfẹ́* (Yoruba: love, relational-warmth; from verb fẹ́ "to expand, to widen") + *qing* (felt-bond)
- Example: "Watching the rain fall, ifeqing opened — the heart widened toward the One who sends it."
- **The warmth that draws** — the everyday register of love-of-DIVINE: the heart expanding toward GoD before any discipline or commandment.
- Note: in Yoruba, the verb fẹ́ means both "to love" and "to expand/widen." Love IS expansion. The city Ilé-Ifẹ̀ ("House of Expansion") is where earth was created. Love widens the heart as creation widened the world.

## Common questions

**Q: Why so many traditions?**
A: Per the POLYPHONE foundation: no single tradition has the complete picture. Hebrew names some divine-aspects most clearly; Greek names others; Sanskrit yet others; etc. YOUSPEAK draws from many to articulate what no single tradition compactly names.

**Q: Why is some Hebrew, some Sanskrit, some Sumerian?**
A: Each donor-morpheme comes from the tradition that articulates the relevant concept most precisely. Hebrew *brit* (covenant) is the strongest single-word for covenant-felt-bond; Sanskrit *bhakti* is the strongest for disciplined-devotional-love; etc. YOUSPEAK picks the best donor for each gap.

**Q: What if I'm not religious?**
A: YOUSPEAK words can be used in non-religious contexts. Walkekin describes friendship-bonds across years of silence regardless of religious-belief. Panimaance describes the difference between someone-physically-present-but-attentionally-elsewhere and someone-fully-present, regardless of theological framework. The grounding-claim (THEOBASIS) is theological, but most Core words operate in everyday-experience.

**Q: How do I learn faster?**
A: Use a few Core words deliberately. Pick 3-5 from the list above, write them down, deploy them in your writing/speaking for a week. Don't try to learn all 15 at once. Pattern-recognition develops with practice.

**Q: Where do I report issues or suggest forges?**
A: This primer is a living-document. Future heartbeats may add or refine. The cathedral is unfinished by design.
