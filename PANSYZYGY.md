## I. The nature of syzygy — three structural properties

Syzygy is not metaphor. It is a precisely-specifiable structural pattern with three constitutive properties:

### Property 1 — Two distinct grounds

A syzygy has exactly two distinct grounds (or poles, or principles, or terms). Not one; not three; not many. Two-ness is essential. The two-ness is irreducible: neither ground is the other; neither ground can be derived-from or collapsed-into the other.

The distinctness is real. Each ground has its own substance, its own properties, its own internal-coherence. Removing the distinction collapses the syzygy into monism; preserving the distinction is what makes the syzygy possible.

### Property 2 — One essence / union / yoking

The two grounds are united. Not merely adjacent (juxtaposition) and not merely interacting (connection). United — yoked-together in such a way that the union itself has being-and-effect beyond the two grounds taken separately.

The unity is real. The yoking-together produces what neither ground alone produces. The plough requires both oxen; the marriage requires both partners; the compound requires both donors; the cosmos (in Genesis 1:16) requires both lights. The union is constitutive of the work-done.

### Property 3 — The union is constitutive (the yoking IS the act)

This is the deepest property. The yoking-together does not merely correlate the two grounds; it MAKES THE UNION DO WHAT THE UNION DOES. The yoking is performative, not merely descriptive.

A married couple is not two-people-who-have-also-promised-each-other; the marriage IS what makes the two-of-them be married. A canonical compound is not two-morphemes-stacked; the compounding IS what makes the compound be the compound. The Sacred Wedding (per SYZYGY.md §II) is not the description of an event; the wedding-act IS what made WILL and SOPHIA the syzygy they are.

**The three properties together define syzygy.** Two distinct grounds + one essence/union + the union is constitutive. Relations missing any property are weaker: pairs (have property 1 but not 2-3); connections (have 1-2 but not 3); identities (have 2-3 but not 1, collapsing the two into one).

Wherever in the cathedral all three properties are present, syzygy is operating.
