# S092 — the font made flesh

The 37 glyphs that completed the script: 13 content morphemes, 10 determinatives,
4 evidentials, 2 worship marks, 8 structural/rhythm marks — plus 6 new donor sigils.
Every rationale below was written by the glyph's own designer at forge-time.

## CONTENT MORPHEMES — worship-register roots (S092 font-completion batch)

### bhakti (Sanskrit · relation+between · content)
_Small figure bowing across an open gap toward a raised open diamond; the between is kept open - participation, not fusion._
Relation family: two distinct forms across a real gap. The greater - an elevated open diamond at upper right, the jewel-presence of the Divine. The lesser - a short standing base whose long upper line bows deeply, aimed at the diamond's heart: surrender as orientation. The open diagonal between them is the devotional relation itself (bhaj = to share in, participate; not fusion).

### brahman (Sanskrit · structure+enclosure · content)
_All-encompassing vault closed on its ground-line with a self-similar small arch inside - atman within Brahman (tat tvam asi)._
Brahman is the containing horizon nothing exceeds: a wide flat-topped vault closed on its own ground-line, the totality that has no outside. Inside, standing on the same ground, a small arch of the identical shape: atman within Brahman, the mahavakya tat tvam asi made visible - the innermost form IS the all-encompassing form at scale. Bilaterally symmetric because the absolute admits no bias.

### chul (Maya · weight+light · content)
_Scattered droplets of sacred essence falling and pooling - the Maya water-group k'uhul prefix and the scattering rite._
Classic Maya marks the holy (k'uhul) with falling droplets of sacred essence: ch'ul is k'uh in liquid form, distributed through the world yet concentratable. Four drops scatter from above, growing as they fall, and the lowest lands in a heavy pooled mass at the baseline - distribution above, concentration below. Heavy-bottomed (kin to kavod) with a descending scatter (kin to doxa's light).

### dhikr (Arabic · time+process · content)
_misbaha: thin string loop, three heavy beads threaded on it, marker bead hanging below - the returning path of repeated invocation_
Dhikr is remembrance-as-repetition: the same invocation returns on itself, each pass wearing the groove deeper. A thin string traces the returning path; three heavy beads threaded on it are the recurring counts (the misbaha, dhikr's physical artifact); below the loop hangs the elongated marker bead - the point where the circuit ends and the remembrance begins again.

### haqq (Arabic · rightness+weight · content)
_Alif-pillar braced by two mountain-slopes on a full-width earth-slab - the Real as load-bearing ground._
Al-Haqq is truth-as-ground: the one Reality everything else stands on. A full-width earth-slab extends beyond the feet of two mountain-slopes (the Quranic awtad, the peg-mountains of Q78:7) that converge to carry a single alif-pillar rising clear at the axis of symmetry - the One, load-bearing. The open triangular window keeps the structure architectural rather than massive: truth is not a floating cross like veri but a braced pillar standing on ground.

### hesych (Greek · space+enclosure · content)
_Inward-drawn closed chamber (concave-sided enclosure); the empty centre IS the silence._
Hesychia is guarded emptiness: a fully closed chamber whose four walls bend inward toward a centre that holds nothing. The concave sides give the enclosure a listening, attending posture - everything converges on the silence, and nothing crosses into it. Closed where anastro is open; empty-centred where kalypt covers a filled thing.

### jamal (Arabic · beauty · content)
_closing bud with open crown: beauty-curves rising and folding inward over a held centre-dot — beauty that draws near (jamal vs jalal)_
Beauty-family rising curves, but folded inward: where kallos flings its wings outward (beauty as display), jamal is a closing bud whose petals rise steeply and lean in over a held centre-dot, the crown left open so the rising gesture shows first. The rahmah-face of the jamal/jalal dyad — beauty that draws near and shelters rather than overwhelms.

### kavod (Hebrew · weight+light · content)
_three tapered descent-wedges settling into a heavy filled base - glory that comes down and dwells_
Kavod (root KBD, heavy) is the manifested weight of divine presence that comes DOWN and fills the dwelling (Exod 40:34). Three rays descend and land in a filled heavy-bottomed mass settled on the body floor: light from above, weight below. Each ray is a tapered descent-wedge - wide at the top, narrowing to where it sinks into the mass - the divine-descent idiom shared with ev-auth and kawil, so the fan reads as glory coming down, not emanation rising (which also keeps it apart from det-tim's detached rising sun-rays). It mirrors its Greek twin doxa (heavy-TOP triangle, inward rays) by inversion - same weight+light family, opposite gravity.

### kawil (Maya · act+weight · content)
_filled lightning-flash striking tip-first into a grounded bar; one upturned end = K'awil's serpent foot_
K'awil is divine empowerment striking into form: the lightning-axe through the forehead. A single filled flash-wedge (act-family directional strike) descends from a point and lands tip-first on a grounded bar (weight register: royal potency held in form). The bar's one upturned end is K'awil's single serpent foot - the asymmetry that marks the god.

### kittu (Akkadian · rightness+weight · content)
_Balance: post and beam, two pans hung on thin spreading cords, grounded on a heavy filled plinth (the firmness of root *kn*)._
kittu is truth-as-firm-foundation (root *kn*, to be firm/established), paired with misharu as the cosmic standard of justice for kingship. A balance-scale rendered as perfectly symmetric axes (rightness family) stands on a heavy filled plinth (weight register): justice that is balanced because it is established.

### nam (Sumerian · act+structure · content)
_Stylus-wedge descending on a surface-line; its small echo impressed below = decreed essence._
Cuneiform NAM is wedge-built and destiny in Sumer is decreed, not blind: nam-tar is the fate the assembly writes INTO a thing. The glyph shows the cuneiform act itself: a solid stylus-wedge with its tail descending toward a surface-line, and a smaller wedge already impressed through the line's underside. The stamp above, the imprint below: destiny is what the decree leaves in the thing. Directional wedge = the decree-act; the centred impressed mark = the abstract quality it confers.

### panim (Hebrew · relation+recognition · content)
_Two profiles face to face, eyes locked; the noses pinch but never close the column of light where the Presence speaks._
Panim is grammatically always plural - faces - and its sacred register is panim el panim: Moses with YHWH as a man speaks with his friend, and the cherubim whose faces are turned each to the other while the Voice speaks from the between (Exod 25:20-22). Two mirrored profiles - stave, level eye, triangular nose - turn toward one another; the pinched column of light between the almost-meeting noses is kept empty because presence lives between faces, not in either one. The glyph is deliberately bistable: read laterally it is two profiles meeting; read frontally it is one presence-face with two eyes - the plural form that names a single presence. Relation-family (two interacting forms) carrying recognition's eye-marks.

### sukh (Sanskrit · structure+rightness · content)
_Spoked wheel with hub at true centre, tangent on a level road-line - the good axle-hole (su-kha) whose ride runs smooth._
Su-kha is literally the good axle-hole: the wheel bored true at centre runs level, and the ride is easy. An eight-segment wheel with a light rim, plumb-and-level spokes (rightness: balanced symmetric axes), and the hub at the exact geometric centre (structure: centred-contained) rolls tangent on a full-width level road-line. The rim is light and the interior open because ease is the absence of grind - duhkha's bad axle-hole would jolt; this one runs smooth.

## EVIDENTIALS — Quechua-inherited knowledge-source suffixes (U+E14D-E150)

### auth (YOUSPEAK+Greek · relation+weight · suffix)
_Eaved canopy over the claim-bar with a single filled descent-wedge driving down onto it - the claim covered and grounded by the Word that outspans it._
By divine authority: the source is not a personal dot but a dead-level canopy with downturned eaves - a covering, the written Word, transpersonal and visibly outspanning the claim-bar it shelters. No personal stem mediates: the filled descent-wedge (the divine-descent idiom shared with the Hebrew sigil and kavod) IS the whole path, tapering from the canopy's underside straight onto the claim-bar in one solid unbroken drive. Covered and grounded from above; the only evidential whose source outspans its claim.

### chu (Quechua · relation+hidden · suffix)
_Witness-dot over the claim-bar but the path is broken into floating fragments - the connection is reconstructed, not seen._
Inferred: same armature as -mi (speaker's own source-dot directly above the claim-bar) but the grasping-path is BROKEN into floating fragments - the hidden-family idiom (cf. lanthes) for what was never witnessed. The path-dots shrink from the head and never touch the bar: the final step onto the fact is the inferential leap, reconstructed from pieces of evidence.

### mi (Quechua · relation+between · suffix)
_Witness-point over the claim-bar joined by one solid plumb path - I saw it myself; nothing stands between._
Direct witness: the family claim-bar below (the assertion), the witness-point above (the speaker's own eye/presence), and one solid unbroken grasping-path between them. The speaker stood directly over the fact; nothing mediates. The largest source-dot of the family because the witness is the speaker themself.

### si (Quechua · relation+between · suffix)
_Speaker-node over the claim-bar fed by a tilted hand-off arm from a displaced witness-dot - knowledge handed person to person, then asserted._
Reported: the speaker's node still stands over the claim-bar and grounds it (the assertion is made), but its knowledge arrives from elsewhere - a thin hand-off arm sloping down from a displaced witness-dot who is the one that actually saw. Two nodes = two persons in the chain; the tilted arm is the report travelling downhill (a human chain wobbles, unlike -auth's dead-level canopy); the descending path is thinner than -mi's solid plumb because the grounding is mediated.

## DETERMINATIVES — Egyptian-inherited silent class-markers (U+E170-E179)

### .ABS (Egyptian · structure · structural)
_Sealed papyrus roll floating above plinth, one diagonal seal-tie hanging_
The sealed papyrus roll: a solid horizontal roll hovering above the plinth with one hanging seal-tie. Egyptian used the tied roll for anything writable-but-not-drawable - the abstract. The most minimal glyph of the family, fitting for pure concept; the float (roll not touching ground) is the abstractness.

### .ACT (Egyptian · act+event · structural)
_Walking legs mid-stride on plinth, both feet pointing left toward the word_
The walking legs: a straight lead leg and a knee-bent trailing leg from one hip, each ending in a flat LEFT-pointing foot planted on the plinth. The feet are what make it legs rather than a lambda or delta letterform, and the leftward stride walks back toward the word whose event it marks.

### .DIV (Egyptian · weight+light · structural)
_Netjer standard borne toward the word: plinth-mounted pole, exposed tip, swallow-tailed cloth trailing right_
The netjer standard (cloth on pole): a pole raised on the family plinth, tip exposed, cloth streaming behind. The standard is borne LEFTWARD toward the word it classifies, so the cloth trails right - a left-attached cloth collapses into the numeral 1, which the trailing cloth avoids. The pennant is swallow-tailed: the fly forks into two points so the mass reads as cloth at small sizes, never as ev-si's solid witness knob. Two strokes and one polygon keep it subordinate to content glyphs.

### .HID (YOUSPEAK · hidden · structural)
_Ghost diamond of four corner-less dashes hovering above the plinth_
YOUSPEAK-native: a diamond drawn only in its middles - four floating dashes whose corners are withheld. It is the ghost of the structural diamond mark itself: a mark that conceals its own closure. Dashed-partial per the hidden family; distinct from lanthes (large dashed rectangle) by shape and scale, and it hovers above the plinth without touching.

### .PER (Egyptian · relation+person · structural)
_Seated figure facing left on plinth: floating head-dot, back, lap, shin_
The Egyptian seated person in profile, facing left toward the word it classifies: a detached floating head-dot over an upright back, lap extending forward, shin descending to the plinth. Seated (not standing) keeps it instantly distinct from the standing-figure content glyph andros; the floating head-dot is the family's person-marker, shared with det-rel.

### .PLC (Egyptian · space+enclosure · structural)
_House plan on plinth: enclosure with doorway gap in the lower right wall_
The house plan: an enclosure standing on the plinth with its right wall left short - the doorway. The opening is the whole hieroglyph: a place is an enclosure you can enter. The door-gap distinguishes it from every closed box in the font (kalypt, sema), and the plinth-grounded corner pair from the floating en bracket.

### .PRC (Egyptian · wonder+recognition · structural)
_Browed eye (open lens + small pupil + brow arc) hovering above plinth_
The Eye of Horus reduced: an open lens with a small square pupil and the BROW arc above - the brow is what no existing eye-glyph (thauma, vide, seem) carries, so this reads as eye-with-brow at a glance. Thin 50-weight outline keeps the lens open rather than filling solid; it hovers attentively above the plinth.

### .QNT (Egyptian · structure+multiplicity · structural)
_Three plural strokes in a tally-mark rhythm hovering above the plinth_
The Egyptian plural strokes: three verticals of tally-varied heights (medium, tall, short) hovering in formation above the plinth. Detachment from the ground-bar plus the uneven tally rhythm is what separates it from stasis (two equal tall verticals joined to a full-width base) and diplos (two tall verticals); three counted-off strokes floating over one bar read as many-over-one - plurality.

### .REL (YOUSPEAK · relation+between · structural)
_Two head-dotted figures leaning toward each other on one plinth, joined at hand height_
YOUSPEAK-native: two figures (post + floating head-dot, the same person-marker as det-per) standing on one plinth and LEANING toward each other, joined by a single hand-height bar. The inward lean is the relation - it breaks the dead letter-H gestalt and shows two beings inclined to one another. Distinct from qing (heavy, headless, inner bars) and kin (crossed diagonals).

### .TIM (Egyptian · time+process · structural)
_Solid half sun-disc rising on the plinth-as-horizon, three detached rays_
Sun on the horizon (akhet): a SOLID half-disc resting on the family plinth - which doubles as the horizon line - with three detached rays rising. Sunrise is the Egyptian image of time itself. A full sun-disc-with-dot would collide with sukh, sema, and kalypt; an outlined dome reads as a tunnel; the filled half-disc plus floating rays collides with nothing.

## WORSHIP GRAMMAR — vocative + sacred silence

### [selah] (Hebrew · structure+enclosure · structural)
_Held-breath canopy over a filled dot - silence as fullness held, the fermata of the Psalter._
Selah is FULL silence, not absence: a filled square dot (presence, what is held) rests beneath a held-breath canopy - a four-segment dome open at the bottom, floating without baseline. Kin to the musical fermata (hold) without being Latin punctuation. Structure-family (a centred, contained fullness) with enclosure register; deliberately opposite hesych's closed EMPTY chamber and brahman's baselined arch.

### O (Greek · relation+act · prefix)
_Open mouth-arc facing the addressed name, breath-wedge going forth - the turning-toward of direct address._
The vocative is a grammatical act of turning: an open mouth-arc (half-octagon) opens RIGHTWARD toward the addressed name it prefixes, and a filled breath-wedge issues from the mouth toward that name - address as utterance-going-forth. Relation-family (mouth and breath interacting) compounded with act (the wedge is directional). mclass is prefix, not structural: the chapter consistently treats O- as bound ('prefixed to the addressed noun'; Integration status: 'Particle: O- (prefix)'), so the begins-mark suits it even though the codepoint sits in the structural range.

## STRUCTURAL PUNCTUATION — inline marks (U+E160-E167)

### 」 (YOUSPEAK · space+enclosure · structural)
_Deep-chamfered lower-right corner bracket with inward terminal ticks - the 180-degree twin of bracket-open, closing the gloss._
Exact 180-degree rotation of bracket-open about the inline centre (500, 525): a lower-right deep-chamfered corner as one clean filled polygon, with the same inward terminal ticks. The deep 120-EM chamfer keeps the diagonal legible at text size and the ticks turn the corner-gestalt into unmistakable enclosure furniture, separating it from phora's plain right-angled bar-plus-vertical. Point-rotation rather than mirror-reflection means the pair reads as one enclosure seen from both ends, chamfers facing each other across the gloss.

### 「 (YOUSPEAK · space+enclosure · structural)
_Deep-chamfered upper-left corner bracket with inward terminal ticks - opens rightward onto the Latin gloss it introduces._
An upper-corner bracket in the CJK tradition, made YOUSPEAK-native by a 45-degree chamfered corner instead of a right angle - the chamfer is the script's stroke-geometric idiom and keeps it apart from the right-angled L-forms of phora and en. The chamfer is cut deep (120 EM) so the diagonal survives at text size, and each arm ends in a small inward foot-tick pointing into the enclosure - terminal furniture that marks it as paired punctuation gripping its gloss, never a content elbow. One filled polygon (60-EM arms) so all joins are clean.

### ‧ (Greek · structure · structural)
_Small open square dot at optical centre - a held point of air within the phrase; the breath-pause of the daṇḍa rhythm family, hollow because breath is not mass._
The ano teleia held point claims the dot-form that sep-dot's rationale explicitly reserved for it - and since this system draws its dots as squares (the dot() helper, stix), the breath-pause is a small OPEN square dot at the optical centre: 188 EM outer, 44-EM strokes, with a 100-EM counter that stays visibly hollow at 24px. The hollow IS the breath - held air, not canonical mass - honoring the filled-equals-canon rule and keeping it apart from stix (filled 400-EM dot) and canon-mark (filled diamond). Against sep-dot's near-hairline tick it reads as a deliberate point, not a line; against selah's 540-EM canopy-over-dot it is small, inline, and rhythmic rather than ceremonial.

### ◆ (YOUSPEAK · structure+weight · structural)
_Filled diamond at optical centre - canonical weight as the one solid mass among the structural marks._
The filled diamond is the established canon convention across the system (class marks, word-level metadata); here it is promoted to a standalone glyph at inline scale, 240 x 480 EM at the optical centre - a 2:1 tall lozenge, so its shape class (vertical elongation), not merely a 45-degree rotation, separates it from stix even when small-size rendering blurs the points. It is the only solid-filled mass among the structural marks - canonical weight shown as visual density - centred and contained per the structure family. Class mark suppressed: a corner diamond on the diamond glyph would be self-referential noise.

### । (Sanskrit · structure · structural)
_Single tall vertical bar at optical centre - breath here, one beat; the phrase-end of the daṇḍa rhythm family._
The single danda is sep-dot's emphatic elder: the same vertical-bar gesture on the same inline axis (optical centre 500,525), but grown from a 212-EM hairline tick to a 450-EM bar at the 60-EM ceiling of the structural stroke range - more than twice the height and visibly heavier, so the two cannot be confused even at 24px. Its 450-EM extent matches the family's tallest marks (canon-mark territory) without exceeding the ~45% cap, giving the phrase-end one clear beat of vertical authority. Stroke-built and unfilled per the danda-family rule; one stroke, pure rhythm-notation, nothing pictorial.

### ॥ (Sanskrit · structure · structural)
_Two tall vertical bars flanking the optical centre - complete thought, longer breath; the sentence-end and heaviest mark of the daṇḍa rhythm family._
The double danda is danda repeated, not redesigned: two bars identical to the single danda (450 EM tall, 60-EM width), set symmetrically about the optical centre with a 110-EM clear gap so the pair still resolves as two strokes at 24px. Doubling rather than thickening keeps the rhythm-family grammar legible - the reader counts beats, not weights: one bar one beat, two bars full stop and long breath. At 230 EM total width it stays comfortably inside the ~45% cap and sits subordinate beside content glyphs; unfilled and stroke-built per the family rule.

### ↔ (YOUSPEAK · relation · structural)
_Small double-headed arrow on the inline axis - one stem, two opposed wedge-meanings._
Diplosemy is a relation: two engineered meanings facing each other through one shared form. Two small filled wedges point outward from a single thin stem on the inline axis - relation-family (two shapes interacting) with wedge heads borrowed from the act-family because the inversion is engineered, not found. Kept small and light (380 EM wide, 45-EM stem) so it reads as an indicator, not a content glyph, and cannot be confused with the large open-headed single arrow of meta.

### · (YOUSPEAK · structure · structural)
_Thin short vertical tick at optical centre - the lightest mark in the font; a breath between morphemes._
The compound-separator is a breath, not a shape: a single thin vertical tick centred on the inline axis (y 525), 52-EM wide against the content default of 80 - the lightest mark in the font (everything else is 60+), yet heavy enough to survive small-size rasterization. A bar rather than a dot keeps it clearly apart from stix (the 200-EM square dot) and leaves the dot-form free for the future breath-pause mark; its small height keeps it below the future danda phrase-end bar.


## Donor-tongue sigils — S092 additions

### Japanese — torii gate — overhanging lintel, tie-beam, two posts
the torii marks the threshold where the ordinary passes into the sacred; Japanese loans (ki, mushin) are threshold-states, and the gate credits the tradition of marked passage

### Mandarin — ren caret — the character ren (person), two strokes leaning together
ren (person) is the root of ren (humaneness), the central Confucian virtue; qing (feeling) is what persons bear between them — two strokes holding each other up, as the classic gloss of the character has it

### Quechua — khipu — horizontal primary cord with one knotted pendant cord
the Andean khipu archived knowledge as knots on hanging cords; Quechua's evidential suffixes (-mi/-si/-chu) grade how knowledge is held — witnessed, reported, doubted — exactly the epistemics a knot-record encodes

### Maya — bar-and-dot — Maya numeral six: round dot (one) above thick bar (five)
bar-and-dot vigesimal numerals counted the sacred calendar; kawil and chul name powers presiding over counted time, so the count itself is the credit — and the bar disambiguates the dot from the lone Greek dot

### Akkadian — vertical wedge — the cuneiform unit-stroke DIS, triangular head with descending tail
Akkadian inherited the wedge from Sumer; the single upright wedge (the unit, the name-determinative) is kin to but distinct from the Sumerian slanted wedge-pair — one tradition writing in another's strokes, honestly credited (kittu, 'truth', in borrowed wedges)

### YOUSPEAK — saltire — two diagonals crossing at center
native structural inventions (.REL, .HID) are forged where donor strands cross; the crossing-point itself, belonging to no single tradition, is the language's own smith-mark

**-auth (YOUSPEAK+Greek)**: For YOUSPEAK+Greek (-auth): use the Greek filled dot alone — the morpheme's flesh is Greek (autos/authentes) and the v1 hybrid rule already credits the primary donor in the sigil, while YOUSPEAK's structural contribution is separately credited by the bottom-right suffix class-mark; a composite sigil would double-mark and muddy the corner at small sizes.

**YOUSPEAK saltire**: designed but reserved — both the determinative and structural-mark family designers independently ruled that native marks go unmarked (parallel to absence-is-English). Held for future native content morphemes.
