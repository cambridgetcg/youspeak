## What this is

YOUSPEAK crafts elegant vocabulary for concepts that exist but are not expressed in current language. It works through five organs, one standing liturgy, and six Constitutional foundations.

## The five organs

### 1. Archaeology — `archaeology/`

Exhumation of semantic fields from extinct languages. Dead tongues cannot defend or soften their secrets; they are the deepest well. Each subdirectory is one dead language. Each file is one semantic field, with: native forms, attested sources, provisional glosses, unnamed-region list, and donors available for future coinage.

Seeds:
- `etruscan/` — the divinatory dead
- `tocharian/` — the crossing dead

Future archaeologies: Sumerian, Akkadian, Hittite, Classical Nahuatl, Gothic, Ugaritic, Sogdian, Old Church Slavonic.

### 2. The Forge — `labs/logos/experiments/`

Where candidates are forged. Each experiment is numbered, dated, provenanced. A candidate enters the Forge as a hypothesis — never a declaration. Most die in assessment. That is correct.

Experiment file schema:

```
---
id: NNN
candidate: <proposed word>
gap: <one-sentence gap description>
donors: [list of source languages / morphemes]
axes:
  gap_validity: 0-10 | null
  phonetic_weight: 0-10 | null
  semantic_coverage: 0-10 | null
  cross_linguistic_uniqueness: 0-10 | null
  memorability: 0-10 | null
verdict: canon | refine | archive | null
---
```

### 3. The Pipeline — `pipeline/`

The nervous system. Automated processes so Nuance's bandwidth can scale.

- `assess.py` — grades a candidate on six axes (v2, post-Constitution); computes mechanical phonetic metrics; emits verdict; also supports the legacy 5-axis rubric for pre-Constitution experiment files; `status <agent>` reports an agent's YOUSPEAK fluency stage
- `discover.py` — gap-analysis via cross-tradition archaeology overlay
- `forge.py` — generates candidate compounds from morpheme donors
- `canonize.py` — stubs canon-entries for canon-verdict experiments
- `diplosemize.py` — surfaces diplosemic potential in a canonical word across the six diplosemy mechanisms

### 4. Grammars — `grammars/`

Structural-linguistic coinages. Not words-for-concepts but named mechanisms that produce families of words or families of readings. Where the first three organs handle atomic coinages, grammars/ handles operations and rules.

## The Laws of Coinage (v2, post-Constitution)

1. **No word without gap.** A candidate that answers no gap is decoration.
2. **No gap without evidence.** A gap must be shown, not asserted.
3. **No beauty without fit.** Sound must carry sense.
4. **No survival without assessment.** No word enters canon unjudged (6-axis v2 rubric).
5. **No canon without genealogy.** Every standing word traces its arrival path.
6. **No depth without accessibility.** (EUMATHE) A coinage only classically-educated readers can parse fails the Eumathe test. Depth is not achieved by exclusion.
7. **No naming without unfolding.** (ANAKALYPSE) A coinage that merely relabels without revealing structure is decoration. The act of coining must unfold what ordinary language hides.
8. **No canon without grounding.** (PRAGMA) A coinage that cannot be pointed-at in ordinary experience is specialized-register, not Core Canon.

## Method

Six discovery patterns — used as the generative grammar of the work:

1. **Cross-Tradition Overlay** — lay two or more traditions over one concept; what each can say that the other cannot is the gap.
2. **Negative Space Listening** — notice what a culture circumlocutes but never names; the circumlocution is the shadow of a missing word.
3. **Apply and Break** — apply a candidate across domains; where it strains, refine.
4. **Virtue-Beauty Bridge** — virtue-vocabulary and beauty-vocabulary are separated in modern language but fused in many ancient ones; reunion produces coinage.
5. **Companion Detection** — a coinage rarely stands alone; look for its siblings.
6. **Dyad-to-Community Escalation** — a two-word distinction often hints at a five- or seven-term community waiting to be named.
7. **Head-Inversion (Anastrophance)** — when a two-morpheme canonical word is compounded, test the inverted compound: if the inversion names a real sibling-concept, a diplosemic-pair opens. (Added 2026-04-24 with the opening of grammars/diplosemy/.)
