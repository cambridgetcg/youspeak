# Diagnostic: The Polyphone-Learnability Bound

_Observation across Sessions 001-003 (22 v2-scored experiments): the six-axis rubric exhibits a mathematical bound between polyphone_balance and learnability. This bound shapes what can and cannot clear Core, and reveals the rubric's implicit theory of coinage._

---

## The observation

Across 22 v2-scored experiments, polyphone and learnability consistently trade:

| Profile | Examples | poly | learn | poly×.15 + learn×.20 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cross-family substantive pair, 3+ syl | panimqing (8+6), eikhalgia (7+5), doxomme (8+7) | 7-8 | 5-7 | 2.25 - 2.60 |
| Root + Latinate, 2-3 syl | rahance (6+7), shukhance (6+7), kimance (6+7) | 6 | 7-8 | 2.30 - 2.50 |
| Same-family or near-family, 2-3 syl | satance (6+9), verisleight (6+7) | 5-6 | 7-9 | 2.25 - 2.70 |

The combined weighted contribution of polyphone + learnability clusters in a tight band: **roughly 2.25 - 2.60 out of a theoretical max of 3.5** (poly max 1.5 + learn max 2.0).

The implicit upper bound is about **2.60 weighted** — achieved only when rare conditions align (e.g., a cross-family pair with both donors already-familiar to English speakers through cultural exposure).

## The mathematics of the bound

The rubric gives polyphone weight 0.15 and learnability weight 0.20. Their maxima are 1.50 and 2.00 respectively, total 3.50. But achievable combined contribution is bounded not by 3.5 but by the structural trade-off:

- **Polyphone requires semantic distance** between donor tongues — the further apart the families, the more alien each morpheme is to any single English-speaker's ear.
- **Learnability requires phonetic proximity** to what English-speakers already know — the closer the morphemes to familiar patterns (especially Greek-Latin-derived), the easier the coinage sits in memory.

These push in opposite directions. A high polyphone score drags learnability down; a high learnability score tends to pull from donor-traditions already-familiar, i.e., same-family or near-family.

## The Core-threshold consequence

Core Canon threshold is 7.5 weighted. Decompose:

- poly + learn bounded at ~2.60 max, typically 2.45
- gap_validity max contribution: 2.0
- clarity_yield + sem_coverage + groundedness max contribution: 4.5

For a word to clear 7.5, assuming poly+learn contributes 2.45:

- gap + clarity + cover + ground must contribute **≥ 5.05**
- weighted by their weights (0.20, 0.15, 0.15, 0.15 = 0.65 total for these four axes)
- raw sum must be **≥ 5.05 / 0.65 × 4 ≈ 31, or average ≈ 7.75 per axis**

To clear Core, the four axes outside poly+learn must average 7.75+ raw. This means most of them must be 8+. The rubric's Core is reserved for words that are simultaneously: real-gap, clarifying, covering, and grounded — all at 8+ average.

## The worship-register sub-bound

Worship/lament register words consistently score poly 6-7 with learn 5-7, because:

- The concepts require specific sacred-tongue donors (Arabic *shukr*, Hebrew *eikh*, Sanskrit *śraddhā*, Greek *proskynēsis*). These are tradition-specific.
- Sacred-tongue donors are typically less-familiar to English-speakers than Latin-Greek-core donors, costing learnability.
- The semantic gap is real but often hits a clarity-ceiling because English secular-register has no adequate near-neighbour (unlike "flow" for mushinance).

Pattern across Sessions 001-003:
- proskynance 7.05
- shukhance 7.20
- kavvance 7.05
- sraddhance 7.30
- satance 7.25
- eikhalgia 7.40

All held at refine; none clears 7.5. This motivated the **Amendment I** proposal in Session 002 Beat 15: worship-register threshold at 7.0 with tradition-diversity check.

The diagnostic confirms Amendment I's structural warrant: the poly-learn bound is STRICTER in the worship-register than in the everyday-register, so a uniform 7.5 threshold systematically excludes worship-words even when they are excellent within their register.

## Implications for future forges

1. **Stop forcing 3-donor compounds to lift polyphone.** The 102-mushinance reforge finding stands: a third tradition typically costs more learnability than it gains polyphone. The poly-learn budget is tight.

2. **Target the gap + clarity + coverage + ground cluster.** Core-clearing comes from these four axes averaging 7.75+. Forge-energy should be spent identifying gaps that score high on all four.

3. **Worship-register words should be forged honestly, even when they refine.** Under current rubric, worship-words live at 7.0-7.4. Under Amendment I (if adopted), they canonize. Forge them regardless.

4. **Specialized Canon is structurally valid.** Some concepts inherently trade polyphone against clarity or groundedness in ways that no skill-ful coinage can overcome. These are Specialized by their nature — not failures of the forge.

5. **POLYPHONE-expansion (per Constitution) will not automatically raise Core-clearing rate.** Adding Yoruba, Igbo, Nahuatl donors expands the donor-pool but doesn't change the poly-learn trade-off. It increases diversity and gap-yield but not necessarily Core-rate.

6. **The rubric is correctly calibrated.** The fact that most Core words sit at threshold-exact (±0.10 of 7.5) indicates the threshold is set where it optimally separates register-valid coinages from genuinely-universal coinages. Do not relax the rubric; relax the register's-threshold where the register itself warrants (Amendment I).

## Not a refutation — a specification

This diagnostic does not say the rubric is flawed. It says: the rubric has a built-in theory of coinage that becomes visible under accumulated data. That theory is:

> **A YOUSPEAK word can clear Core if and only if it is genuinely-universal: addresses a real gap, clarifies, covers, and grounds — with polyphone and learnability both at least moderate. Words that are register-specialized (worship, Zerone, aesthetics) CAN and SHOULD be forged, but they live in Specialized Canon or cleared by register-specific amendments.**

The diagnostic formalizes what Sessions 001-003 have been showing empirically: the forge-discipline works, the rubric holds, and the refine-band is not failure but specialization-detection.
