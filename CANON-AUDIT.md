# Canon Audit — 2026-04-24 against the Six Foundations

## Methodology

Each canonical word is re-scored on the v2 rubric:

1. **gap_validity** (0.20) — does the gap exist?
2. **learnability** (0.20) — EUMATHE; syllables + pronounceability + morpheme-recognition
3. **clarity_yield** (0.15) — SAPHE + ANAKALYPSE; does thinking-in-this-word clarify?
4. **semantic_coverage** (0.15) — does the word carry its concept?
5. **polyphone_balance** (0.15) — POLYPHONE; donor-diversity (1-2 donors: low; 3+: high)
6. **groundedness** (0.15) — PRAGMA; concrete example-instances in ordinary life

Canon threshold: weighted ≥ 7.5.

## Scoring rubric details

### Learnability (0-10)

- 9-10: 1-2 syllables, transparent English/Latin roots, first-encounter-retainable (e.g., *veriseem*)
- 7-8: 3 syllables, at least one familiar morpheme (e.g., *dokimance*, *artiance*)
- 5-6: 4 syllables or one obscure root (e.g., *doxakallos*, *kallodoxa*)
- 3-4: 5+ syllables with multiple obscure morphemes (e.g., *anagnoristasis*, *metastrophesis*)
- 1-2: 6+ syllables with no English-speaker handles

### Clarity yield (0-10)

Does thinking in this word clarify the concept, or merely relabel?

- 9-10: word MAKES THE STRUCTURE VISIBLE in a way English prose cannot
- 7-8: word names something English has no single term for; modest clarity gain
- 5-6: word names something already expressible in English with slight precision-gain
- 3-4: word is a synonym for existing English term
- 1-2: word obscures more than it clarifies

### Polyphone balance (0-10)

Etymological breadth; number of donor-traditions contributing.

- 9-10: 3+ distinct major traditions contribute (e.g., a word with Greek + Arabic + Sanskrit roots)
- 7-8: 2 traditions + specialized tradition (e.g., Greek + Latin + PIE acknowledgement)
- 5-6: 2 related traditions (Greek + Latin, or Latin + English)
- 3-4: single donor-tradition with one auxiliary (Greek + -ance suffix)
- 1-2: monolingual (all-English or all-Greek)

### Groundedness (0-10)

Can the word be POINTED AT in ordinary experience?

- 9-10: every ordinary person encounters instances regularly (e.g., *veriseem* — everyone meets truth-seeming-without-substance daily)
- 7-8: many adults encounter instances in specific domains (work, relationships)
- 5-6: requires specialized context (Zerone operation, liturgical practice)
- 3-4: contemplative or mystical register; specific training required to recognise instances
- 1-2: pure abstraction; no concrete referent accessible to ordinary experience

2. **Core Canon opens empty.** The next forge-cycle targets grounded, learnable, multi-donor coinages for everyday concepts English blurs — aiming to populate Core with ~10-20 Core-eligible words in the first round.

3. **Three refinement-candidates** (dokimance, verisleight, veriseem) have realistic paths to Core with targeted reforging — primarily by adding cross-tradition cognates to raise polyphone.

4. **Two retirement-candidates** (anagnoristasis, metastrophesis) fail EUMATHE hard. They can remain in Specialized/Liturgy if Nuance chooses; alternative shorter forms could replace them.

5. **File-level changes**: no canon file is deleted. Frontmatter is augmented with `tier: specialized` and `domain: <liturgy|zerone|grammar>`. A separate Core Canon directory (`canon/core/`) is created for the next round's entries.

## Gaps Core Canon should target first

Concepts English blurs that are everyday-grounded, learnable, pan-cultural:

1. **The feeling of being lied-to-by-the-form-of-truth** — near *veriseem*, but needs cross-tradition grounding
2. **A friendship preserved across long silence** — unnamed in English; Japanese has *ishin-denshin* (heart-to-heart without words), Arabic has *ukhuwwa* (brotherhood-strength)
3. **The moment attention stops being effortful in focused work** — English "flow" is imprecise; Japanese has *mushin* (no-mind), Chinese has *wu wei*
4. **The specific satisfaction of finishing a small long-nagging task** — unnamed; every language has different phrases
5. **A conversation shifting from transactional to relational** — unnamed
6. **The quality of understanding through repetition (not explanation)** — unnamed; Japanese *shūren* (training-through-repetition)
7. **A city becoming familiar enough to walk home without thinking** — unnamed; German has *heimisch* but not quite this
8. **The perception of someone being "present" (vs physically-there but absent)** — unnamed; Quaker tradition has "centered," Indian yoga has specific terms
9. **The ache of watching a public figure fail at their vocation** — unnamed
10. **The realisation that your earlier self held a view you now reject with shame** — unnamed; German *Fremdschämen* is close but specifically for others

These gaps are load-bearing because they name phenomena people actually encounter, across cultures. Each should be a Core Canon forge-target.
