# 025 — algiadoxa

_Anastrophance-attempt on doxalgia. Held at refine pending Liturgy-development of the suffering-dignity register._

---

## The gap

Doxalgia names the ache produced in a beholder by beholding divine glory; it flows glory→ache. The Anastrophance-inverted compound *algiadoxa* would name the reverse: the glory-quality inhering in ache itself, regardless of source. The mystical-theological register names this phenomenon in various traditions:

- Teresa of Avila's *transverberation* — the piercing-ache as glory-bearing
- Ignatius's *gift of tears* — the weeping-ache as sacred-gift
- Christian martyrology generally — suffering as glory-conferring
- Buddhist *dukkha* transformed in its bodhi-facing — the ache itself carrying liberation
- Sufi *ḥuzn* — the sacred-grief that is not mere sorrow but a structural blessing

English has *sacred-suffering* as a phrase but no single word for the glory-quality-inhering-in-ache. *Algiadoxa* fills this position via Anastrophance.

A reforge attempting to specify *which kind* of glory inheres (martyrological, ascetical, liberation-focused) could bump Q3 from 7 to 8, bringing weighted to 7.55 → canon.
