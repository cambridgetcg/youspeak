## Full sense

Veriseem names the specific mode in which a statement, argument, or act appears-true without containing truth. Unlike verisleight (where the components ARE true but arranged to deceive), veriseem has no true-components — it is truth-seeming all the way down. The surface wears the uniform of truth; there is nothing behind the uniform.

Common manifestations: the plausible-but-hollow argument, the authoritative-sounding explanation with no evidence, the confident assertion that carries the rhythm of expertise without the substance, the "truthy" statement (in Colbert's sense). These are veriseem.

Asha's vigilance includes veriseem alongside verisleight: the chain must detect both (a) truth deployed to deceive (verisleight) and (b) truth-seeming without truth (veriseem). The former requires structural-analysis; the latter requires substance-check.

## Example usage

- _The policy-paper was veriseem: every paragraph sounded like a conclusion, nothing was a conclusion._

- _Veriseem passes first-glance examination; a veriseem argument is always "very seemly" — and that is the joke of the Synaphemia. The coinage tells you what it is by sounding like what it is._

## Not confused with

- **verisleight** — truth deployed to deceive. Verisleight has truth-content; veriseem does not. Verisleight requires sleight-skill; veriseem requires only surface-confidence.
- **sophistry** — rhetorical persuasion; broader. Veriseem is a specific surface-mode.
- **bullshit** (Frankfurt) — indifference to truth. Bullshit is unconcerned with truth-or-falsehood; veriseem is actively pretending at truth.
- **plausibility** — general appearance-of-truth. Plausibility can be underwritten by evidence; veriseem cannot.
- **truthiness** (Colbert) — nearest colloquial cognate; veriseem is the technical-register equivalent.
