# 008 — autokallos

_αὐτοκάλλος_ — beauty-itself; the Platonic auto to kalon substantivalized; alternative to doxakallos for the unitary-beauty target.

## The candidate

**autokallos**
Pronunciation: /ˈɔː.toʊ.ˈkæl.ɒs/ (AU-toh-KAL-los), 4 syllables
Donors: αὐτός (itself) + κάλλος (beauty)

## The forge

Plato's *Symposium* 210a–212a describes the ladder of love ascending to *auto to kalon* — the-beautiful-itself. This is the Platonic ontological name for the Form of Beauty: uncreated, uniform, substantival, summoning. Autokallos substantivalizes this phrase into one word.

## Why it loses to doxakallos

### Q1 — gap_validity: 7/10

The gap is genuine (the ontological beauty-in-itself position). But autokallos carries the *Platonic* flavour, which is not quite what the doxakallos-gap asks for. Plato's *auto to kalon* is impersonal, Form-abstract, metaphysically-prior. The target-gap is the beauty **of GoD** — a personal GoD, not an impersonal Form. Autokallos is reclaimable but always drifts toward Platonic-metaphysical register.

### Q2 — phonetic_weight: 7/10

Two adjacent vowels at the open (AU-toh) make the beginning slightly diphthong-heavy. Not as clean as doxakallos's opening plosive. 7.

### Q3 — semantic_coverage: 6/10

- Q1 (divine-specific): WEAK — autokallos without qualification is not specifically divine. Needs "theos autokallos" or "autokallos Dei" to reach divinity.
- Q2 (beauty-in-itself): STRONG — this is autokallos's home.
- Q3 (beholder-orienting): partial — auto-prefix suggests self-sufficiency, which can fight against calling-forth.
- Q4 (ordered-to-the-bottom): weak direct carry.
- Q5 (counterpart-to-doxalgia): absent — no doxa-pair.

Coverage score 6/10.

### Q4 — cross_linguistic_uniqueness: 6/10

*Auto-* prefixes are very active in English (autobiography, autocracy, automotive). The word is easily mistaken as "self-beauty" in a modern-self sense. Collision risk high. 6.

### Q5 — memorability: 7/10

Handles: auto-, kallos-. Easy to remember. But the word *competes* with doxakallos for the same slot; it is therefore remembered-in-pair-with-a-rival, which degrades its standalone hold. 7.
