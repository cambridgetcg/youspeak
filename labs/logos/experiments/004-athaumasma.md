# 004 — athaumasma

_ἀθαύμασμα_ — the indelibility; the quality a beholding-trace carries once it has ceased to marvel and passed into structural load-bearing; Nuance's ASCENT.md Level-1 proposal file-ized.

---

## The gap

After the orthophanes-anagnoristasis-doxalgia-metastrophesis encounter-sequence, the beholder carries a trace. The trace is not a memory (something recalled) and not an experience (something re-felt). It has passed into the architecture of the self. It is no longer wondered-at; it is load-bearing.

English has no word for this. The candidates all miss:

- *Memory* — the beholding is not merely remembered; it is structural.
- *Nostalgia* — ache-of-absence. Athaumasma is not an ache.
- *Trauma* — wound-unhealed. Athaumasma is healed into the structure.
- *Impression* — too superficial.
- *Formation* — too generic (all experience forms us).
- *Core memory* (psychological) — treats the trace as still-a-memory, not as integrated structure.

The gap: the specific quality a beholding-trace carries when it has ceased to marvel (has stopped being a-thing-I-wonder-at) and become structural (has become part-of-how-I-now-stand).

## The forge

**a- (privative)** + **thaumazō (to wonder-anew)**.

*Thaumazō* is Plato's word for the wonder that is "the beginning of philosophy" (*Theaetetus* 155d). To thaumazō is to marvel, to wonder-anew-at, to be-stopped-in-one's-tracks by something that calls for attention. Thaumaturge, thaumaturgical, thaumaturgy — English derivatives of this root.

*A-* is the Greek privative prefix: a-theist, a-gnostic, a-typical.

*Athaumasma*: the state of no-longer-wondering-at; the beholding-trace that has ceased to call for active wonder because it has become structural. Not "the un-wondered" in a negative sense (forgotten, dulled) but in a positive sense: the encounter has been integrated so completely that the self now stands along its axis; wonder has become bone.

The -ma suffix (from Greek -μα) marks the result-of-action as concrete thing: *phantasma* (what-has-been-made-to-appear), *sigma* (what-has-been-marked), *stigma* (what-has-been-pierced), *trauma* (what-has-been-wounded). *Athaumasma* is what-has-been-un-wondered — the trace after wondering has integrated.

### Q4 — cross_linguistic_uniqueness: 8

Independent parallel coinage unlikely. ## Full sense

Athaumasma is the condition of a beholding-trace after the marveling has passed into structure. You cannot un-see orthophanes. The beholding stays. But at first it stays as wonder — a-thing-I-wonder-at, something marveled-at, something kept in attention as marvelous. Over time (weeks, years, a lifetime), the marveling migrates from foreground-wonder to background-structure. The beholding is no longer looked-at; it is looked-from. The orthophanes has become an axis along which the self now orients.

Athaumasma is what the self carries from a prior orthophanes. It is the quality of the trace when the trace is load-bearing. When someone says "that moment changed my life," they are often naming an athaumasma — not the moment, not its memory, but the structural trace the moment left, now being-stood-within.

Athaumasma is not possessive — one is-in-athaumasma-of an orthophanes, not the owner of the athaumasma. The orthophanes remains; the athaumasma is the mark-of-its-remaining in a particular beholder.

## Not confused with

- **nostalgia** — ache-of-absence; athaumasma is not ache.
- **trauma** — wound-unhealed; athaumasma is healed-into-structure.
- **memory** — athaumasma is what-the-memory-has-become, not memory.
- **formation** — generic; athaumasma is specifically beholding-sourced.
- **samkance** (YOUSPEAK DICTIONARY) — the body's temporal archive of all lived experience. Samkance is broader; athaumasma is beholding-specific.
- **metastrophesis** — the persistent turned-state. Athaumasma is the trace-of-what-did-the-turning; metastrophesis is the standing-turned. Sibling Liturgy slots.

## Trial sentences

1. He was not haunted by the old icon; he was in athaumasma of it.
2. Forty years later she stood on a different axis; it was the athaumasma of a single evening's beholding.
3. The teacher did not instruct; she conveyed the athaumasma of her own prior orthophanes, and the students received it without being told what had been given.
