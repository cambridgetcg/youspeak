# 002 — Anagnoristasis

_The pre-ache instant. Aristotle's anagnōrisis (tragic recognition-turning) held still by stasis. The frozen threshold before doxalgia lands._

## The Gap

Between orthophanes (the beauty appearing) and doxalgia (the ache in the beholder) there is an instant — measurable perhaps in milliseconds, perhaps outside time — when the pattern has been seen but not yet felt. The beholder's apparatus has matched the pattern; the affective system has not yet delivered the verdict. This instant is the threshold. It is the place where recognition is pure cognition, not yet pain.

English lacks a word for this. *Recognition* is too broad. *Realization* carries the full arrival, not the threshold. *Moment of truth* is journalistic. *Epiphany* collapses into the aftermath. The pre-ache threshold remains unnamed.

## The Forge

**anagnōrisis** (ἀναγνώρισις — the recognition-moment in Aristotle's *Poetics*, the turning-point of tragedy where a character sees the true identity of a person or situation) + **stasis** (στάσις — standing-still, a held position, the arrest of motion).

**anagnoristasis:** the standing-still of recognition. The held instant between seeing and being-changed-by-seeing.

## Companion

Fits between orthophanes and doxalgia in the Liturgy sequence:

1. **orthophanes** — the beauty appears
2. **anagnoristasis** — recognition stands still (this word)
3. **doxalgia** — the ache arrives

Three terms now name the first three phases of the encounter. Community escalation (Pattern 6) confirmed — the dyad was the seed, the triad is the first fruit.

## Near-neighbours

- **recognition** — the arrival of matched pattern; too broad, lacks the held-ness
- **epiphany** — the full burst of realization; collapses the threshold into the fruit
- **moment of truth** — journalistic, decision-framed; lacks the aesthetic register
- **stasis** alone — stillness; lacks the recognition-content
- **aporia** — being-at-a-loss; different valence (confusion, not clarity-held)

## Trial sentences

1. Before the ache came, there was an **anagnoristasis** — the theorem known but not yet wept over.
2. She stood in the **anagnoristasis** a long time; what followed was doxalgia for the rest of the evening.
3. Skilled readers of poetry learn to notice the **anagnoristasis** — the caesura between comprehension and feeling.

## Honest assessment

Gap is real; word is accurate; memorability suffers (six syllables). Verdict `refine` at 6.75. A shorter alternative may be findable (anarrest? anagnoma?) but anagnoristasis is the most precise. The Liturgy accepts precise-over-easy when the concept demands it.
