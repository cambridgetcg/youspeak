# 000 — Genesis

_The first forge-attempt under the rebuilt architecture. The Standing Liturgy begins here, partial and honest._

## The Gap

The experience of beholding GoD's beauty is not adequately named by any standing word:

- **beauty** — too shallow; aesthetic-adjacent
- **glory** — names the radiance but not the beholder's change
- **sublime** — names the overwhelm; loses the rightness and the return
- **awe** — too first-encounter; the phenomenon includes return, recognition, fit
- **tiferet** (Hebrew) — closest; specific to a kabbalistic sefirah, not portable
- **yūgen** (Japanese) — mysterious-depth-beauty; human-sized, not divine-sized
- **kavod** (Hebrew) — weight-glory; names the divine side, not the beholder's

The unnamed region: *the ache-pattern in the beholder who recognizes that what they are seeing is ordered to the bottom* — the pain of sufficiency, the sweet pressure inward when every part of a thing is exactly right, arriving specifically in the beholding of GoD's ordering.

## The Forge

Construction: **doxa** (δόξα — glory, radiant opinion-become-presence) + **algia** (ἄλγος — structural ache, the living pain of tissue-in-tension).

**doxalgia:** the ache produced by glory. Names the beholder-side of the encounter.

The -algia morpheme is alive in English medical vocabulary (neuralgia, myalgia, nostalgia). Extending it into theological aesthetics keeps the morphological register honest: this IS an ache, located in the witness.

## Near-neighbours

- **nostalgia** — ache-of-homecoming. Different axis (temporal/spatial return).
- **neuralgia** — nerve-ache. Medical; same -algia morpheme.
- **kalopsia** — delusion-of-seeing-beauty. Psychiatric; opposite valence.
- **saudade** (Portuguese) — longing-for-what-is-absent. Ache of absence; doxalgia is ache of presence.

## Trial sentences

1. The choir's final cadence produced a moment of **doxalgia** — a pressure behind the sternum that was not sadness.
2. He could not name what he felt before the icon; only later did he call it **doxalgia**.
3. **Doxalgia** is not an emotion one chooses; it is the signature left in a beholder by an ordering too total to resist.

## Honest self-assessment (2026-04-17)

`doxalgia` names the *ache* pole. It does NOT name the *beauty itself* — only the beholder's response to it. A complete answer to the Standing Liturgy requires a second word, or a fusion-word, for the *pattern-made-presence* that produces the ache.

Pre-scoring guess: partial answer. Likely `refine`. The Liturgy continues — next experiments should seek the counterpart noun for the beauty itself, and then a fusion.
