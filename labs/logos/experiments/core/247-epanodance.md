# 247 — epanodance

_Forge-attempt under the Standing Liturgy, session 087. Companion check (Method Pattern 5) executed on the wheel that 246 proposed: the wheel's stations are all named; its arcs are not. This is the candidate for the one arc that traditions demonstrably circle — the return-road._

## The Gap

The Liturgy-wheel as drawn in 246:

```
doxapothos ──?──▶ anagnoristasis ──▶ doxalgia ──▶ athaumasma / metastrophesis ──▶ doxapothos, deeper
```

Every named member of the wheel is a **station** — a state, a stance, a trace, an instant. No member is a **transit**. The arrow between doxapothos and anagnoristasis is doing unpaid work: the going-back itself — the booked passage, the laced shoes, the psalter re-opened to the psalm already known, the feet on the road to the chapel. English flattens this transit into "going back" (mere locomotion), "practice" (mechanical), "observance" (institutional), or — when the longing has gone quiet and only the road remains — "going through the motions" (pejorative, where the traditions instead honor it).

**The companion-check question, asked honestly first:** is this region real, or is it covered by doxapothos's own forward-lean (the longing leans toward the threshold; is the movement just the lean enacted)? It is not covered, and the proof is a two-way dissociation:

1. **Longing without return exists.** The exile of Psalm 137 longs by the rivers of Babylon and cannot walk back; hiraethqing (235) names precisely the bond whose return-road is structurally barred. Doxapothos can stand where epanodance is impossible.
2. **Return without the engine exists — in two grades.** In the dark night the lean survives anesthetized (246's Path-3 finding: doxapothos is orientation, not warmth — Teresa's decades of darkness, returning daily to the chapel), so the engine persists unfelt; but in akēdia the lean itself is lost — and the tradition's counsel is still to walk the road: Evagrius's remedy for the noonday demon is to stay in the cell and keep the office. The road is walkable even where the engine is not merely silent but absent. The traditions call neither case hypocrisy; they call the walking faithfulness.

A region that dissociates in both directions from its nearest sibling is its own region (the same test that separated doxalgia from doxakallos: ache and beauty can each occur in the other's absence only notionally — here the dissociation is empirical and pastoral).

Evidence the gap is real, not asserted (Law 2):

1. **The cathedral's own corpus gestures at it without naming it.** `canon/athaumasma.md`: "The beholder wonders at it, **returns to it**, turns it over in attention." `canon/metastrophesis.md`: "One rarely exits metastrophesis **without another encounter** that renames it." Both presuppose the return; neither names the transit; 246's wheel leaves the arrow blank. (Honest caveat: this is gesture-level evidence, weaker than 246's documented saudade-deferral — scored accordingly below.)
2. **Traditions built whole institutions on the transit and named them** — aliyah la-regel, the Psalms of Ascent, junrei, ziyarah, exitus-reditus, nostos (see Witnesses; consolidated at `convergences/return-to-behold-again.md`).
3. **The cluster's own anchor-verse contains the gap.** Psalm 27:4 — the verse doxakallos cites for the beholding — asks for two things, not one: "to behold (לַחֲזוֹת, *lachazot*) the no'am of YHWH **and to visit-again (לְבַקֵּר, *levaqer*) in his temple*." The piel *baqar* is glossed "inquire" by some translators and "visit morning by morning" by others (Radak, connecting it to *boqer*, morning); the LXX chose the visit-verb: ἐπισκέπτεσθαι. Beholding and returning-to-behold stand side by side in the one verse, distinct and inseparable — the gap-pair, already drawn in the cluster's foundation-text.

The unnamed region precisely: *the beholder's deliberate, repeatable going-back to behold again — a creaturely transit (feet, roads, calendars, re-opened books), presupposing no estrangement, fueled by doxapothos when the longing is alive and kept by metastrophesis when it is not, terminating at anagnoristasis, and returning always to the same inexhaustible beheld.*

## Witnesses (cross-tradition overlay, Pattern 1)

- **Hebrew — aliyah la-regel (עֲלִיָּה לָרֶגֶל), the foot-ascent.** The three regalim (Ex 23:17; 34:23–24) command the return-journey itself, thrice yearly — and the command is phrased in beholding-vocabulary: the consonantal text of יֵרָאֶה allows "to **see** the face of YHWH"; the Masoretic vocalization reads "to **appear before** the face." Either reading makes the pilgrimage a beholding-transaction. The Mishnah names the pilgrimage-obligation **re'iyah** (רְאִיָּה, "seeing/appearing"; Hagigah 1:1), and the Bavli glosses the doubled verb: as he comes to see, so he comes to be seen (Hagigah 2a). A tradition lexicalized return-as-beholding.
- **Hebrew — Shir ha-Ma'alot (Pss 120–134).** Fifteen songs **for the road**, not the arrival — a repertoire composed for the transit ("I was glad when they said to me: let us go," Ps 122:1; the pilgrim of this psalm is glad, not penitent — the return presupposes no estrangement). Ps 84 holds the whole arc in one poem: the longing (*nichsefah*, v.2 — doxapothos's own witness-verse), then "blessed are those **in whose heart are the highways** (מְסִלּוֹת, *mesillot*)" (v.5) — the return-roads internalized as a standing disposition — then "they go from strength to strength; each appears before God in Zion" (v.7). Longing and road, adjacent and distinct, in the same psalm.
- **Greek (Plato) — epanodos (ἐπάνοδος).** *Republic* 532b, the cave recapitulated: "the release (λύσις) from the bonds and the **turning-around (μεταστροφή)** from the shadows … and the **way-up-and-back (ἐπάνοδος)** out of the underground cave to the sun." The donor-text of metastrophesis distinguishes, in the same clause, the turning from the road — Plato drew the precise line this forge needs, on his own authority (the anchor-quality of Cratylus 420a for 246). And *Republic* 521c: the soul's periagōgē from nocturnal day to the true day is "an **epanodos** to being — which we shall declare to be true philosophy." Philosophy itself defined as the return-road. The cave-economy is moreover cyclic: the beholders must go down again (520c) and come up again, by turns — the road walked repeatedly.
- **Greek (epic) — nostos (νόστος).** The return-journey as a genre: the Odyssey is a road home, sung. English took the ache and left the road: *nostalgia* (Hofer, 1688) kept νόστος + ἄλγος's pain and lost the transit. The doxa-cluster is quietly re-splitting Hofer's compound — doxalgia took the algos; the return-road is the nostos-half, still unclaimed.
- **Sufi Arabic — inabah (إنابة) / awbah (أوبة), beyond tawbah.** Al-Qushayrī's *Risāla* (bāb al-tawba) — the same manual that gave 246 the shawq/ishtiyāq distinction — tiers the return: *tawbah* is the return of the common believers (from sin), *inabah* the return of the friends (from heedlessness), *awbah* the return of the prophets — return with nothing to repent of, return simply because to GoD one returns. The Qur'an fixes the highest tier as praise, not penitence: David, Solomon, and Job are each called **awwāb** — "ever-returning" (Q 38:17, 30, 44); "an excellent servant — ever-returning!" A tradition explicitly marked the return-beyond-repentance, which is exactly the line dividing this gap from teshuvance.
- **Persian Sufi — the ney.** The Masnavi opens with the reed cut from the reed-bed, crying of separations; "everyone who remains far from his origin seeks again the day of his union" (Masnavi I.4). Under the alast-covenant (Q 7:172 — every soul has already beheld and answered), all wayfaring is return-shaped: there **was** a prior beholding. (The trace of the prior beholding is athaumasma's slot; the road it powers is this one.)
- **Japanese — junrei (巡礼), the circuit-pilgrimage.** The Shikoku 88-temple henro is a **closed loop**: finishing is arriving at the start, and pilgrims walk it again — some dozens of times. The only pilgrimage-architecture that builds the *again* into the path's own geometry: the wheel-shape of 246, poured in stone and footpath.
- **Latin scholastic — exitus-reditus.** The *Summa*'s architecture: all things go out from GoD and **return** to GoD. Reditus names the cosmic return-arc — the macro-scale of the region (contact-linked to Greek epistrophē via Neoplatonism; counted cautiously).
- **Heraclitus — ὁδὸς ἄνω κάτω μία καὶ ὡυτή (DK B60).** "The road up and the road down are one and the same." A peripheral witness, honestly marked weak: it gives the geometry (the road out is the road back; the wheel's arcs lie on one road) but not the worship-direction.

Four fully independent traditions (Hebrew, Sufi, Greek, Japanese-Buddhist) plus two contact-linked echoes circle the transit; none owns the beholding-directed, estrangement-free, iterative slot. Consolidated with modal witnesses at `convergences/return-to-behold-again.md`.

## The Forge

Construction: **epanodos** (ἐπάνοδος — the way-up-and-back; ἐπί + ἄνοδος, the up-again-road; Plato's word for the cave-return, *Rep.* 521c, 532b; LSJ "going up, return"; also the rhetorical term for recapitulation — collision recorded below) + **-ance** (the house movement/act suffix: the cluster's two named movements are **tajalliance** (descent of beauty) and **anakallance** (ascent of beauty); the worship-action canon's act-words all carry it — teshuvance, yadahance, qinance…).

**epanodance** /ɛˈpæn.ə.dæns/ (e-PAN-o-dance, 4 syllables; final syllable as in *abundance*) — *the return-movement of the beholder toward the beholding; the going-back-to-behold-again; the walked arc from doxapothos to anagnoristasis.*

Morphological note: the wheel's stations are not doxa-marked (orthophanes, anagnoristasis, metastrophesis, athaumasma); only the pole-words carry doxa-. A transit-word therefore takes the movement-class form (-ance), completing the movement-triad: **tajalliance** (beauty descending), **anakallance** (beauty ascending), **epanodance** (the beholder returning). The beauty comes down; the beauty goes up; the creature walks back.

### The wheel, with its arc named

```
        doxapothos (longing, in absence)
              │
              │  EPANODANCE — the walked return-road
              ▼
        anagnoristasis (threshold)
              │
              ▼
        doxalgia (ache, in presence)  ◀── orthophanes ◀── doxakallos
              │
              ▼
        athaumasma (trace) + metastrophesis (turning-kept)
              │  the compass-state holds; the trace deepens the longing —
              ▼
        doxapothos, deeper → the next epanodance
```

Clarity-yield: the word names what English can only praise backhandedly. The dry returner — feet faithful, heart silent — is not "going through the motions"; she is keeping the epanodance, and the traditions count the walking as worship (the office kept through acedia; the henro's twentieth circuit; the regel walked in a drought-year). It also names why pilgrimage exists at all: the festivals are corporate epanodance, the wheel turned by a whole people on a calendar.

## Near-neighbours (not to be confused with)

- **doxapothos (246)** — the engine; epanodance is the road. Two-way dissociable: the exile has the engine without the road (Ps 137); the dry returner has the road without the felt engine. Sibling, not synonym.
- **teshuvance (canon, worship-action, 8.32)** — the repentance-return: its gap-statement turns "the person who was **moving away**." Epanodance presupposes no estrangement — the pilgrim of Ps 122 is glad, not penitent. Qushayrī's tiers mark the line inside one tradition: teshuvance holds tawbah; epanodance's region is awbah — the return of those with nothing to repent of, the *awwāb*'s again-and-again.
- **metastrophesis (canon, 7.5)** — the turning-kept, a state; epanodance a transit. *Rep.* 532b sequences them: first the metastrophē (about-face), then the epanodos (road up). The returner walks **because** the compass-state holds; the compass is not the walking.
- **anakallance (primer v4.4)** — creation's beauty ascending to its source: ontological, in the beheld, the ray returning to the sun. Epanodance is creaturely, volitional, iterative, in time — the pilgrim returning to the window. Not the beauty's arc; the beholder's.
- **sankofame (234)** — the return that **fetches**: egg in beak, retrieval carried forward. Epanodance retrieves nothing — the beheld is inexhaustible and stays; the returner comes back empty-handed and changed. Fetch-return vs behold-return.
- **hiraethqing (235)** — the bond toward the **unreturnable**; epanodance requires the road to be open. Where hiraethqing stands, epanodance is exactly what cannot begin.
- **nostos / nostalgia** — homecoming-journey / its ache; backward-facing, toward what-was, completed once. Epanodance returns to what-is-always-more, and never finally.
- **ziyarah / umrah / hajj** — visit-transits, the nearest Arabic practice-words; person/place-directed, not beholding-marked, and (hajj) once-obligatory rather than wheel-iterative.
- **junrei / henro** — the circuit-practice; institution-specific, as aliyah la-regel is. Epanodance names what these institutions are instances of.
- **epektasis / epektasisme (pipeline)** — the perpetual stretching into the infinite; trajectory-shape at the scale of a life. Epanodance is one revolution of the wheel that epektasis says never stops turning.
- **anastrophance (018)** — grammar-terminology (compound-inversion mechanism); no semantic contact despite the shared shape.
- **teshuvance's English neighbours** (repentance, conversion) and **practice/observance/discipline** — the flattened English coverings; see clarity-yield.

## Trial sentences

1. The longing she could have kept in her chair; the **epanodance** began when she laced her shoes — the road to the chapel already half a psalm.
2. Through the dry years he kept the **epanodance** without the doxapothos: feet faithful while the heart was silent — and the tradition counted the walking as worship.
3. The festivals are corporate **epanodance** — three times a year the roads to Zion filled, and the fifteen songs were written for the road, not the arrival.
4. **Epanodance** is not teshuvance: she was never estranged; she goes back glad, as one re-opens a psalter to the psalm she already knows is there.

## Apply-and-break (Pattern 3, run at the forge)

- **The mathematician re-deriving a beloved proof** — holds; the word tolerates orthophanes-directed generalization, as doxalgia does.
- **The long marriage's re-courtship** — holds; the return to behold the same person more deeply is the word's domestic register.
- **The exile barred from return** — breaks correctly: that is hiraethqing + doxapothos; the word rightly refuses.
- **The tourist's repeat visit for novelty** — breaks correctly: novelty-seeking is not return-to-the-same; epanodance requires the same beheld, deeper. The strain sharpens the gap-description: *same and inexhaustible* are both load-bearing clauses.

## Verdict: refine (standing forge-attempt)

6.60 is below the 7.5 canon line; 5.50 v2 is specialized-range. No score inflated. The gap is real — proven by dissociation and by four independent traditions — and the candidate is philologically anchored exactly where it most needed anchoring (the donor-text of metastrophesis distinguishes the donors in one clause). But the word lacks the LXX-bridge its siblings carry, its root is dead in English, and two of its defining clauses are unmarked. It stands as a forge-attempt beside its sibling: the longing-word waits at the threshold, and now the road-word waits on the road. That is the correct posture for both.

### Refinement paths

1. **LXX-bridge test**: *anabathmance* (LXX ᾠδὴ τῶν ἀναβαθμῶν ↔ shir ha-ma'alot) carries the bridge epanodance lacks, at the cost of synecdoche (steps for road) and the *bath*-misread. EUMATHE trial against epanodance with learners who know the cluster.
2. **Hybrid polyphone test**: a Hebrew-Greek or Arabic-Greek hybrid (awbah-root + hodos?) could lift polyphone_balance above the cluster's chronic 3; every hybrid tried at this forge collided (alliance/bikkurim) — but the donor-region is rich enough to retry.
3. **Apply-and-break, wider**: the diaspora's generational return; the scientist's return to an old result after new instruments; liturgical year vs personal pilgrimage (corporate vs single epanodance — same word or a number-marked pair?).
4. **Companion check, recursive (Pattern 5)**: if the wheel's first arc earns a name, the descent-arc (from doxalgia's fading back into the world — the leaving-the-chapel, Plato's 520c going-down-again, the philosopher's return to the cave) may be the remaining unnamed transit. Check whether athaumasma's trace-carrying already covers it before forging.
