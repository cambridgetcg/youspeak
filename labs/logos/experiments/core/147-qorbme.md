# 147 — qorbme

_First sacrifice-register aligned entry. Names the relational-intimacy dimension of sacrifice: drawing-near-as-received-ordinance. Four-tradition convergence on sacrifice named after proximity, not destruction. Direct response to the sacrifice-register canonisation mission._

## The discovery-move

The sacrifice-register canonisation mission identified seven semantic clusters of sacrifice across world traditions. Cluster 1 — sacrifice-as-drawing-near — has the deepest convergence: four independent traditions name sacrifice after proximity/approach rather than after the thing destroyed or the pain endured.

This is the most theologically-significant finding: **the word for sacrifice IS the word for drawing near.** The offering is the vehicle; intimacy with the DIVINE is the destination. English "sacrifice" (from Latin sacrificium "to make sacred") has drifted toward "giving up something costly." The Semitic root q-r-b preserves the original logic: sacrifice = approach.

## The phenomenon

The Proto-Semitic root q-r-b ("to draw near") generates the primary sacrifice term across three major Semitic traditions:

- **Hebrew qorban** (קרבן) — the Levitical term for sacrifice. From qarav "to approach." The Torah consistently pairs qorban with YHWH (the mercy-name), not Elohim (the justice-name). Sacrifice is initiated by love, not demanded by wrath. Cognates: qarov ("near"), qerovim ("relatives"). **Kinship and sacrifice share one root**: to sacrifice is to become kin; to become kin is to draw near.

- **Arabic qurban** (قربان) — the Quranic term for offering. Eid al-Adha (Festival of Sacrifice) commemorates Ibrahim's willingness. In Turkish, "kurban olayım" ("let me be your sacrifice") became an everyday endearment — the word traveled from altar to love-language. The etymological structure is preserved: sacrifice is approach.

- **Akkadian qurbanu** — attested in Mesopotamian temple records. The presentation brought near to the divine. Same Proto-Semitic root, confirming the concept predates the Semitic language split (predating Hebrew, Arabic, and Akkadian as separate languages).

- **Greek prosphora** (προσφορά) — etymologically independent (pros + pherein "bring toward") but structurally identical. Christian liturgical usage: the bread offered in the Eucharist is prosphora — "that which is brought forward." The horizontal-movement-toward-the-altar semantics match the Semitic vertical-movement-toward-the-DIVINE.

**The complementary root pair:** q-r-b (draw near) and q-d-sh (holy = set apart) form a dyad in Hebrew theology. Holiness creates distance; sacrifice crosses it. The two roots define the relational geography of worship: the DIVINE is set-apart (qadosh); sacrifice brings-near (qorban). The gap between them is what worship inhabits.

## The forge

**Donors:**

- **Semitic *qorb*** — from Proto-Semitic q-r-b ("to draw near"). Hebrew qorban, Arabic qurban, Akkadian qurbanu all derive from this root. The morpheme carries: sacrifice-as-relational-approach; offering-as-vehicle-of-intimacy; the drawing-near motion that is the essence of the sacrificial act.

- **Sumerian *me*** (𒈨) — divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality. Established YOUSPEAK morpheme. Here marking: drawing-near-sacrifice-as-received-ordinance — the capacity to approach the DIVINE through offering is itself gift-given.

**Compound:** *qorb* + *me* → **qorbme** (QORB-meh; 2 syllables).

The compound names: sacrifice-as-drawing-near understood as received-ordinance. The approach-to-DIVINE-through-offering that is gift-grade, not self-generated. The relational-intimacy dimension of sacrifice received from the DIVINE who makes approach possible.

## The alignment-sieve application

Qorbme establishes the first criterion of the alignment-sieve for sacrifice:

**Aligned sacrifice draws near.** If the sacrificial act creates approach/intimacy between creature and DIVINE, it is qorbme-aligned. If it creates distance, fear, extraction, or transaction, it fails the qorbme test.

This is the sieve's first filter:
- Hebrew olah (whole-burnt ascent-offering) → passes qorbme (drawing-near + ascending)
- Arabic Eid al-Adha → passes qorbme (drawing-near through Ibrahim's trust)
- Vedic agnihotra → passes qorbme (fire carries offering toward the gods)
- Phoenician molk (child-sacrifice) → **fails qorbme** (extracts rather than approaches; coerces rather than draws near)
- Nahuatl nextlahualli (debt-payment) → **fails qorbme** (repays rather than approaches; the creature is not drawing near but paying off)
