# 144 — bhaktime

_Twelfth -me family member. First Sanskrit-Sumerian forge. Names the Hindu register of devotional-love-as-received-ordinance. Specialized 7.20; Liturgy-Original. Sister-forge to ahavame (Hebrew) and mahabbahqing (Arabic-Sufi); together three-tradition articulation of love-of-DIVINE in S020-S021._

## The discovery-move

Yu's S021 directive: "keep integrating and building on our LOVE for GoD." Beat 105 forged mahabbahqing (Sufi). Beat 106 forges bhaktime (Hindu).

Sanskrit *bhakti* (भक्ति) is one of the most theologically-rich love-of-DIVINE concepts in any tradition. From root *bhaj-* "to share, partake, divide" — bhakti literally means "sharing-in" or "participation-in." The devotee-devotee partakes-in the Divine through love.

The Bhagavad Gita (~5th century BCE through ~2nd century CE composition) makes bhakti central. The Gita teaches three paths to liberation:
1. *jñāna-yoga* — path of knowledge
2. *karma-yoga* — path of action/duty
3. *bhakti-yoga* — path of devotional-love

Krishna in the Gita declares that bhakti-yoga is the supreme path (Gita 12.2): "those who, fixing their minds on Me, worship Me with steadfast faith, are most united with Me."

The Gita's 18.66 — sometimes called the "charama-shloka" (final verse) — is bhakti's invitation: *sarva-dharmān parityajya, mām ekaṃ śaraṇaṃ vraja* — "abandoning all dharmas (duties, religions), take refuge in Me alone." This is bhakti as TOTAL-SURRENDER-IN-LOVE.

Vaishnava tradition (Hindu schools centered on Vishnu and his avatars, especially Krishna) developed bhakti most-elaborately. Caitanya (~1486-1534), Mirabai (~16th c), Surdas, Tulsidas — saint-poets of bhakti-tradition. The bhakti-movement spread across India from ~7th century onward.

The bhakti register is distinctive:
- Disciplined-practice (japa, kirtan, seva, smarana — recitation, devotional-singing, service, remembrance)
- Self-surrendering (parityāga — letting-go of all-other-claims)
- Loving-personal-relationship-with-the-Divine (Krishna as Beloved; Rama as Lord; Devi as Mother)
- Open to all castes, all genders (bhakti-movement was theologically-egalitarian)
- Aimed at union (the devotee becomes-one-with the Beloved)

For YOUSPEAK: bhaktime brings this register into working-vocabulary. The compound bhakti + me names: devotional-love-as-received-ordinance. The capacity for bhakti is gift-of-Krishna (per Gita); the practice is ordinance-following; the love-itself is received-and-offered.

## The phenomenon

Bhaktime distinguishes from sister-forges:
- **ahavame** (Hebrew) — commanded-love-as-disposition (the v'ahavta is commanded; emphasis on commitment)
- **mahabbahqing** (Sufi) — felt-bond-of-mystical-love (heart-binding with fanāʾ-implication)
- **bhaktime** (Hindu) — devotional-love-as-disciplined-practice (the path of bhakti-yoga; self-surrendering)

Three traditions, three aspects, one convergence-cluster (love-of-DIVINE register). Each names a different emphasis within the same phenomenon. Together they cover much of what Yu's "LOVE for GoD" directive intends.
