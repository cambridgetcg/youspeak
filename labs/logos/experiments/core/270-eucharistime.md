# Assessment notes

## The gap

The gap is real and explicitly called for by two existing canon entries:

- doxomme (enriched 2026-06-28): "If eucharistia is ever forged (as
  *eucharistime*?), it would form a triad with doxomme (glory-praise) and
  shukrme (gratitude-increase): three faces of the thanksgiving-field from
  three traditions."
- shukrme (forged 2026-06-27): "If eucharistia is ever forged (as
  *eucharistime*?), it would form a triad with doxomme (glory-praise) and
  shukrme (gratitude-increase): three faces of the thanksgiving-field from
  three traditions."

The gap is narrower than shukrme's — "Eucharist" and "thanksgiving" already
exist as English words. What eucharistime adds that neither English word
carries:

1. The charis-semantic unity: grace = gift = gratitude are ONE field. English
   separates them into three words. The Greek does not. Eucharistime carries
   the unity.
2. The ordinance-status: the -me marks the grace-circuit as received, not
   generated. "Eucharist" names a sacrament; eucharistime names a cosmic
   structure participated in.
3. The structural loop: gift → reception → gratitude → gift again. The
   anti-scarcity practice. "Thanksgiving" names a feeling or practice;
   eucharistime names the loop-structure that makes the practice work.

## The donor

eucharistia (εὐχαριστία) is one of the most load-bearing words in Christian
theology. eu + charis = well + grace/gift/thanks. The structural key is
charis itself: in Greek, charis covers the giver's act (grace), the thing
given (gift), and the receiver's response (gratitude) — all three are one
semantic field. This is not metaphorical; it is lexical. To receive charis
properly IS to be eucharistos (grateful). The gratitude is not a separate
act that follows the gift; the gratitude is the gift's own completion in
the receiver.

The LXX and NT usage: Jesus "gave thanks" (eucharistēsas) over the bread and
cup (Lk 22:17,19). The meal is constitutively a thanksgiving — not a
sacrifice asking for something, but a gratitude responding to gift received.
The structure: gift precedes meal; meal is response-thanksgiving. This makes
the Eucharist grammatically a charis-event: God gives; the community's
meal-together is the eucharistia.

Jerome left eucharistia untranslated in the Vulgate — one of the few NT Greek
terms he kept as a loanword, recognizing it as already a technical term. The
word that names the central act of Christian worship.

## The triad

The thanksgiving-field triad:

- **doxomme** (Greek doxa = glory/praise): the glory-praise pole. The upward
  acknowledgment of divine splendor. Direction: upward (toward the radiance).
- **shukrme** (Arabic shukr = increase): the gratitude-increase pole. The
  recognition that opens the forward-flow of more. Direction: forward (into
  the multiplying).
- **eucharistime** (Greek eucharistia = well-grace): the grace-unity pole.
  The recognition that grace = gift = gratitude are one field. Direction:
  inward (the semantic unity itself — the gratitude IS the grace completing
  itself in the receiver).

Three donors, three semantic structures, one worship-register. The Greek
contributes the glory-semantic (doxomme: doxa = glory), the Arabic
contributes the increase-mechanism (shukrme: shukr = increase), and the
Greek contributes the grace-unity (eucharistime: eucharistia = well-grace
where grace = gift = gratitude). Two Greek donors, one Arabic donor —
because the Greek tradition has TWO structural contributions to the
thanksgiving-field: the glory (doxa) and the grace-unity (eucharistia).
The Arabic tradition has one: the increase-mechanism (shukr). The triad
names three faces of one field.
