# 124 — panimaance

_Core Canon attempt. Names the quality of being-present (attention-inhabited-here) distinct from being-physically-there. Addresses Constitution seed-gap #8._

## The gap

Constitution seed-gap #8: *the perception of someone being "present" vs physically-there-but-absent*. A person can be in the room without being in the room. The body is there; the attention is somewhere else; the "here" of them is not here. English "presence" bundles physical-location and attentional-inhabitation; we rely on context to disambiguate ("she is really present today" means attention-here; "is she present?" in a roll-call means body-here). No compact word names specifically the attentional-inhabitation-of-here.

Nor does English have a word for the inverse — the body-without-presence register. A spouse looks up from the phone; the eyes are on you; the attention is not. English must circumlocute: "checked out," "not really here," "distracted," "somewhere else."

The gap is structural: English has one word (presence) for two phenomena (body-location, attention-inhabitation). YOUSPEAK can unfold this.

## The forge

**Donors:**

- **Hebrew פָּנִים (panim)** — face; specifically face-as-presence. In biblical Hebrew, *panim* is the face-as-seen-by-another — the dimension of self that turns-toward. Already in YOUSPEAK Canon via *panimqing* (Session 002, Beat 8), where it named the face-to-face register in conversation. Panim carries the face-engaged-with-another quality.

- **Japanese 間 (ma)** — the meaningful-in-between; the space-that-is-itself-meaning; the pregnant pause. Canonical Japanese aesthetic concept: the silence between notes in traditional music, the empty space in ink painting, the pause in noh-theatre, the room-between-buildings in architecture. Ma is not "nothing"; it is the space-that-makes-the-rest-meaningful. Isamu Noguchi brought ma into Western architectural discourse. Ikebana depends on ma for composition.

- **Latin -ance** — quality-state suffix, established in YOUSPEAK.

**Compound:** *panim + ma + -ance* → **panimaance**. The state-of-face-inhabited-meaningful-space. The quality of *being-present* as face-turned-and-here-filled.
