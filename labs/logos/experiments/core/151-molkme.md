## The discovery-move

The sacrifice-register canonisation mission requires both aligned entries (what the DIVINE approves) AND anti-vocabulary entries (what the DIVINE rejects). Drujme (S019) established the anti-vocabulary pattern: the -me morpheme extended to mark "divinely-named-anti-ordinance" — what the DIVINE has named as rejected.

Molkme applies this pattern to the sacrifice register. Sacrifice is the most ethically-stratified worship-category: the same act (offering to the DIVINE) can be aligned (qorbme, hotepme, danaqing, tapasme) or anti-aligned (molk, nextlahualli). The alignment-sieve must name both.

## The phenomenon

**Phoenician/Punic molk:**

The archaeological evidence is sobering. At the Carthage tophet alone: 20,000+ urns containing cremated remains of infants and children, and 6,000+ commemorative stelae. The inscriptional formula: "because Baal heard his/her voice" — the sacrifice presented as answered-prayer, the child's life as the costliest-votive-payment.

The compound terms in inscriptions:
- **mlk ʾdm** — "molk of a person" (human sacrifice)
- **mlk ʾmr** — "molk of a lamb" (animal substitute)

The substitution-system reveals the escalation-logic: ordinarily a lamb suffices; in crisis, the currency inflates toward human life, toward the most precious — the child. The logic is: greater need demands costlier payment. This is sacrifice-as-extraction: the divine is owed, and the debt escalates.

**The etymology is disputed** but illuminating:
- If from mlk "to promise, to vow" — molk is vow-fulfillment; the child offered because promised
- If from mlk "to possess, to rule" — molk is sovereignty-exercised; divine-sovereignty extracting its due

Both etymologies converge on coercion: whether by prior vow or by divine-sovereignty-claim, the sacrifice is DEMANDED rather than given. This is the anti-pattern to danaqing (gift-given-freely): molk is gift-extracted-by-force.

**Why the Hebrew Bible's rejection is so vehement:**

The Hebrew Bible names this practice explicitly and rejects it with unique intensity:

- **Leviticus 18:21** — "Do not give any of your children to be passed through the fire to Molek. Do not profane the name of your God."
- **Leviticus 20:2-5** — Death penalty prescribed for molk practitioners
- **Jeremiah 7:31** — "They have built the high places of Topheth in the Valley of Ben Hinnom to burn their sons and daughters in the fire — **something I did not command, nor did it enter my mind.**"

The Jeremiah text is theologically-decisive: "nor did it enter my mind." The DIVINE did not merely prohibit molk; the DIVINE states it was never even considered. The practice is so misaligned that it is alien to the DIVINE's nature — not forbidden-because-tempting but rejected-because-incomprehensible-to-the-DIVINE.

**The Ibrahim/Abraham narrative as definitive rejection:**

The Binding of Isaac (Genesis 22) / Ibrahim's sacrifice (Quran 37:100-111) — read cross-traditionally — is the DIVINE's definitive rejection of molk-logic. The narrative:
1. The test: Abraham/Ibrahim receives the apparent command to sacrifice his son
2. The willingness: he proceeds (tapasme — self-offering)
3. The substitution: the DIVINE provides a ram — "YHWH-Yireh" (the LORD will provide)
4. The revelation: what the DIVINE actually wants is the willingness (tapasme), NOT the child's death (molk)

The narrative is the alignment-sieve IN NARRATIVE FORM: tapasme passes; molk fails. The DIVINE transforms sacrifice from extraction-of-the-innocent to self-offering-of-the-willing.

## The alignment-sieve application

Molkme establishes the first ANTI-criterion of the alignment-sieve:

**Misaligned sacrifice extracts from the vulnerable.** If the sacrificial act takes from those who cannot refuse — children, the powerless, the coerced — it is molkme. The criterion is: WHO BEARS THE COST? In qorbme/danaqing/tapasme, the offerer bears the cost (drawing near, giving freely, offering the self). In molkme, someone else bears the cost.

The structural test: **does the offerer sacrifice THEMSELVES or ANOTHER?**
- Self-offering (tapasme) → aligned
- Other-offering where the other CANNOT refuse (molkme) → anti-aligned
- Other-offering where the other consents (communion-sacrifice, shared meal) → examine further

Modern applications of molkme-recognition:
- Systems that sacrifice workers' health for corporate profit → molkme-pattern
- Institutions that exploit the young for institutional prestige → molkme-pattern
- Religious systems that extract tithes from the poor while leaders prosper → molkme-pattern

The word names a pattern that persists beyond its historical origin.
