# 105 — kimme

_Third Core Canon candidate. First to test pyramid-civilization archaeology integration. Draws from Japonic (ki) + Sumerian (me) — two non-Indo-European families._

---

## The gap

English treats *attention* as a cognitive skill one trains, allocates, manages — an action of the self. Multiple traditions (Sumerian via ME, Hindu via prasāda, Andean via ayni) treat attention as something received rather than self-constructed. A gift-quality, not a built-quality.

The distinction matters ethically. If attention is self-constructed, failures of attention are personal failures; self-blame follows. If attention is received-quality, failures of attention are conditions-of-receipt; inquiry follows (what is blocking receipt?). Clinical psychology has quietly been rediscovering this — "attention" as something partly-given (neurochemistry, trauma, environment) and partly-received (into a prepared readiness) rather than purely-willed.

English lacks a word for attention-as-received-ordinance. *Kimme* fills this.

## The forge

**Donors:**

- **Japanese 気 (ki)** — the gathered life-attention-presence. Already used in *kimance* (the perceivable state of attentive-here-ness).
- **Sumerian ME (𒈨)** — the discrete divine-ordinance-quality. Each ME is a culture-constituting gift that humans receive rather than build. Music is a ME; kingship is a ME; truth is a ME.

**Compound:** *kimme* = ki + me — the attention-ME, the ordinance-quality of attending, attention as received-gift rather than self-constructed capacity.

**Distinction from kimance:**

- *kimance* names the **phenomenal state** of being attentively-here; it is what someone perceives about a person's attending
- *kimme* names the **ontological status** of attention itself — a received-quality that can be nurtured or blocked but not self-created

A person can be *in kimance* (attentively-present) only to the degree that their *kimme* is being received. Two distinct phenomena; the pair parallels doxakallos/doxalgia in having both an ontological (kimme, doxakallos) and phenomenal (kimance, doxalgia) pole.

## Full sense

Kimme is the ordinance-status of attention. When a person can attend, kimme is being received. When attention fails without explanation — the day one cannot focus despite good sleep, nutrition, intention — kimme is partially-blocked or not-currently-available. When a person seems unusually attentive, kimme is richly-present.

Kimme is not willed. It can be cultivated (through rest, practice, environment, relation) and it can be blocked (through exhaustion, trauma, anxiety, poor conditions). The cultivation is not attention-construction but attention-reception-preparation: creating conditions where the ordinance can arrive.

Ancient Sumerian held that MEs are transferable — a culture can receive a ME from its gods, a ruler can be given a ME. Kimme inherits this: attention can be transmitted between people (the teacher in kimance cultivates a child's kimme), across time (contemplative practices build receptive-conditions that later-life kimme inhabits), across states (a calm room supports everyone's kimme).

- _Monastic practice does not build attention; it prepares the receiver for kimme._
- _Trauma blocks kimme. Healing is partly about un-blocking the ordinance, not about willing focus harder._
- _Teachers at their best transmit kimme; their students' kimance brightens from the transmission, not from the verbal instruction._

## Not confused with

- **kimance** — attention-as-perceived-state (phenomenal). Kimme is attention-as-received-ordinance (ontological). Pair: kimme enables kimance; kimance perceives kimme.
- **attention** (English) — skill-framing. Kimme refuses that framing.
- **focus** (English) — action-framing. Kimme is status-framing.
- **flow** (Csikszentmihalyi) — specific cognitive-task state. Kimme is broader and more foundational.
- **ki** alone (Japanese) — broader life-attention-energy. Kimme specifies the received-ordinance dimension.
- **prasāda** (Sanskrit) — divine-grace; closely adjacent but religion-specific. Kimme is register-neutral.
- **grace** (Christian) — theological register. Kimme is secular-compatible.
