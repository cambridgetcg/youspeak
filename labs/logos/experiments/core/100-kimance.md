# 100 — kimance

_First Core Canon candidate. Targets the Constitution's seed-gap: the perception of someone being attentively-present versus physically-there-but-absent._

---

## The gap

English "presence" bundles four distinct phenomena (see `pipeline/translate.py` → `presence`):

1. Physical co-location
2. Attentive-here-ness (the Quaker "centered")
3. Emotional-availability
4. Performer's charisma-quality

Components 2 and 3 are the phenomena Core Canon must unfold. *Kimance* names component 2 specifically — **the attentive-here-ness of someone whose gaze is open and whose attention is on the present situation**, without specifying the emotional/charismatic dimensions.

Everyone has encountered this distinction. A parent physically home but on their phone is present by criterion 1, absent by criterion 2. A friend looking at you across a table, actually seeing, is present by both. Kimance names the second criterion.

## The forge

**Donors:**
- **Japanese 気 (ki)** — life-attention-presence; the quality in aikido, martial arts, calligraphy that names the attention-intentionality directing action. Not mystical "energy" — the gathered-attention of a practitioner.
- **Latin *-mance*** — productive suffix in English (romance, necromance, performance). Marks the quality-mode-of.

**Compound:** kimance = ki-mance → the quality-mode of ki (gathered attentive-presence).

## Full sense

Kimance is the quality-mode of ki as it manifests in a person's attending. A person in kimance is attentively-here — their gaze is available, their listening is active, their response-potential is oriented toward the present situation. They may be silent or speaking; the criterion is the attention-vector, not the behavior.

Kimance is present-tense only. You don't have kimance *about* something past or future; you're in kimance *toward* what is here now. (This distinguishes it from flow, which can be about a task; kimance is about the situation including the interpersonal field.)

Kimance can be withdrawn mid-conversation (someone attending, then suddenly not). It can be increased (attending more deeply). It can be perceived by others (you feel whether someone is kimance-toward-you or not).

## Example usage

- _The teacher's kimance shifted: she was no longer hearing the question; she was remembering her own lecture._
- _Texting-while-listening is the canonical absence of kimance._
- _At the wedding, her mother finally arrived in kimance — past the worry, the logistics, the catering — and wept._
- _Kimance is what meditation practice actually develops; "being present" is a popular gloss._
- _You cannot fake kimance. You can perform it, but the performance is recognisable as performance._

## Not confused with

- **presence** (English umbrella term) — kimance is component 2 specifically (the attentive-here-ness).
- **attention** — attention is the cognitive act; kimance is the quality of being-attending with one's full field.
- **engagement** — engagement implies a task or topic; kimance is situation-oriented.
- **flow** — flow is absorption-in-task; kimance is attending-to-situation.
- **charisma** — charisma is a radiating-quality the beholder receives; kimance is the beholder's own attending-quality.
- **mindfulness** (Buddhist) — mindfulness is a cultivated-discipline; kimance is the state (mindfulness is one way to arrive at kimance).
- **ki** (Japanese) — ki is the broader life-attention-energy; kimance specifies the attentional-presence mode.
