# 125 — agwame

_First Igbo-Sumerian cross-family coinage. Names character-as-received-ordinance — the understanding of agwa (community-judged conduct) as received-from-tradition rather than self-generated._

## The gap

English "character" bundles several distinct registers: achievement-register (what you've done), virtue-register (your moral dispositions), received-register (what tradition and ancestors give you), and theatrical-register (persona, performance). These are blurred.

Igbo *agwa* isolates the community-judged-conduct register: your agwa is what your community sees of you across time. But agwa does not pronounce ON THE SOURCE of the conduct — is it self-made? gifted? inherited? achieved?

Sumerian *me* (𒈨) names divine-ordinance — the civilization-constituting gifts received from the gods. Me is fundamentally RECEIVED rather than earned. In YOUSPEAK the -me morpheme has become the received-register marker (kimme: attention-as-received-ordinance; doxomme: thanksgiving-as-received-ordinance).

Compound: **agwame** = agwa + me = character-as-received-ordinance. The specific understanding of one's character as something received from tradition, ancestors, and community — not as self-made virtue and not as earned-achievement.
