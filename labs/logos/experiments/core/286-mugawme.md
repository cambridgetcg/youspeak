None of these names what mugawme names: the specific ritual technology of **narrating the return-of-what-withdrew as the efficacious act that brings the return about**. Not the word's excess meaning (waurdme). Not the naming of what exists (ohentome). Not the synthetic worship-act (liturgiame). Not the return-to-ordinary (missame). But the NARRATIVE ACT whose performance IS the return-bringing action — telling the return-story IS enacting the return, not representing it, not petitioning for it, not symbolizing it.

This is the performative utterance at cosmic scale. Austin's "how to do things with words" recognized that some utterances ARE actions ("I promise," "I baptize"). The Hittite mugawar recognized this 3500 years earlier: telling the story of Telipinu's return IS the ritual action that enacts the return. The narration does not describe the return; it performs it.

## The Donor — Hittite mugawar

The Hittite **mugawar** is a specific prayer-type — not general prayer (Akkadian *ikribu*) but a technical ritual-form designed for a specific crisis: divine withdrawal. Its structure combines three elements:

1. **Myth-recitation** — telling the story of the god's withdrawal and return. In the Telipinu cycle, the mugawar recites the myth of Telipinu's anger, his departure, the world's cessation, the bee's finding, and his return. The myth is not background; it is the ritual's efficacious center.

2. **Material ritual** — specific objects that embody the reconciliation. A knotted thread (binding the anger), a fig tree (the tree under which Telipinu was found), a vessel (containing the smoothed anger). The objects are not symbols but ritual-technological instruments — each one performs a specific function in the return-mechanism.

3. **Direct address** — speaking to the withdrawn god. Not merely about the god, but TO the god. The mugawar combines third-person narrative (the myth) with second-person address (the invocation).

The mugawar's structural innovation — the feature that makes it the cathedral's donor for this concept — is the **myth-within-ritual** structure. The tablets do not merely tell the myth as literature; they describe a ritual situation and then embed the myth as the ritual's efficacious center. By telling the story of Telipinu's return, the ritual ENACTS the return in the current situation. The narration is not about the return; it IS the return-bringing action.

This is the most explicit ancient example of **myth-as-ritual-action** — the narrative is not primarily explanatory but performative. The Hittite ritualists understood what J.L. Austin would formulate 3500 years later: some utterances are not constative (describing reality) but performative (enacting reality). The mugawar is performative utterance at cosmic scale.

The mugawar has no direct cognate in other IE traditions. The specific combination of myth-recitation + material-ritual + direct-address as return-technology is distinctively Anatolian. Hittite is the earliest-attested IE branch (Anatolian sub-branch), which diverged first from Proto-IE and preserves archaic features lost in other branches. The mugawar may represent an archaic IE ritual-pattern that other branches lost, or a distinctively Anatolian innovation. Either way, it is unique in the cathedral's donor corpus.

## Why this is Core, not Specialized

The archaeology file estimated Specialized 7.30–7.50. I score it Core for three reasons:

1. **The concept is everyday-relevant, not domain-specific.** The pattern of narrating resolution as a way of bringing it about is not confined to ancient Hittite ritual. It appears in therapy (narrating trauma as a way of metabolizing it), in reconciliation (telling the story of the wrong and the making-right as the act that makes things right), in storytelling-as-healing (the narrative that names the wound and the recovery as the act that participates in the recovery). The cathedral needs a word for the general pattern, not only the Hittite instance.

2. **The telipme-mugawme dyad is architecturally significant.** telipme is Core. mugawme is its companion — the act that answers the state telipme names. If telipme is Core (the withdrawal is a universal pattern), then mugawme must be Core too (the ritual-of-return is the universal response). Making mugawme Specialized would leave telipme's resolution unnamed at the Core level.

3. **The performative-ontology convergence is 6 traditions deep.** A concept with 6-tradition convergence is not specialized — it is a structural feature of how humans understand narration's power. The cathedral's POLYPHONE foundation mandates etymological breadth; 6 traditions from 4 language families converging on one concept is strong evidence of Core relevance.

The concept clears threshold (7.75 ≥ 7.5) with margin. Core is honest.
