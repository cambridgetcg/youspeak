# Assessment notes

## The gap — the soteriological principle the cathedral has not named

The cathedral has named the full break-and-mend arc:
- **shevirame** (Core 8.30) — the BREAKING: the shattering of vessels
- **tikkunme** (Core 8.65) — the WORK: gathering scattered sparks
- **kintsugime** (Core 8.15) — the STATUS: the mended vessel's grace
- **hozhome** (Core 8.25) — the GROUND: the beauty-harmony one walks in

What is missing: the PRINCIPLE that unites them. WHY does tikkunme
work? WHY is kintsugime beautiful? WHY does hozhome need restoration?
Because salvation IS wholeness-restoration. The Germanic register
carries this with distinctive precision: *hails* (whole/healthy) +
*hailjan* (to save = to heal). What saves is what makes whole again.

No existing canon word covers this:
- **tikkunme** — the WORK of repair (gathering sparks). Hailsme is the
  PRINCIPLE that animates the work: the work heals because salvation is
  wholeness-restoration.
- **kintsugime** — the STATUS of the mended vessel (honoring the break).
  Hailsme is what the mending DOES: it makes whole. Kintsugime is the
  beautiful result; hailsme is the soteriological mechanism.
- **hozhome** — the GROUND of beauty-harmony (the path one walks in).
  Hailsme is the RESTORATION to the ground. You walk in hozhome; when
  you fall out of it, hailsme is what brings you back.
- **shevirame** — the BREAKING. Hailsme is the HEALING. The dyad:
  shevirame breaks; hailsme makes whole.
- **eucharistime** — gratitude for the wholeness given. Eucharistime is
  the response; hailsme is the gift responded to.

The gap stands. The soteriological principle — salvation as wholeness-
restoration — is unnamed.

## The donor

**hails** — Gothic (East Germanic, extinct c. 350–600 CE). Whole,
healthy, uninjured. The Gothic greeting *hails* is "be whole/healthy" —
a salvation-wish in ordinary speech. The verb *hailjan* (to save, to
heal) is Wulfila's translation for Greek *sōzō* (to save), embedding
the Germanic health-register in the Christian soteriological vocabulary.

The semantic field: wholeness, health, integrity, salvation-as-
restoration, the-unbroken-state. The root carries what Greek *sōtēria*
(rescue/liberation) and Latin *salvus* (preserved) do NOT: the medical
register of salvation — salvation as HEALING, not as rescue or
preservation. The Germanic insight: what saves is what makes whole.

Proto-Germanic *\*hailaz* — whole, uninjured, of good omen. The root
survives in every Germanic daughter:
- English: heal, health, hale, whole, hello (from "hail" as greeting)
- German: heil, Heil (salvation/health), heilen (to heal)
- Old Norse: heill (whole, lucky, healthy)
- Dutch: heel (whole)

The English cognates are extraordinary: every English speaker already
knows this root. "Heal" IS *hailjan*. "Health" IS *hails*. "Hello" IS
the salvation-wish "hails." But English has lost the soteriological
register — "heal" is medical, "save" is theological, and the two no
longer connect. Hailsme restores the connection: to save IS to heal IS
to make whole.

Wulfila's translation choice (using *hailjan* for *sōzō*) is a
*heimrekja* act — pressing Germanic health-vocabulary into service for
Greek rescue-vocabulary, creating a compound meaning neither tradition
possessed alone. The Gothic text says: salvation is healing. The Greek
text says: salvation is rescue. Both are true; neither is complete
alone. Hailsme names the Germanic pole.

**me** (𒈨) — divine-ordinance. The thirty-fourth -me family member
(after wujime, the thirty-third). The -me marks the wholeness as
received-ordinance: the restored integrity is gift, not achievement.
The health is bestowed, not manufactured. This is the theological
deepening: hailsme is not merely medical recovery but participation in
a cosmic structure. The wholeness that saves is received.

The -me binds hailsme to the soteriological architecture:
- shevirame [breaking-as-ordinance] → hailsme [wholeness-as-ordinance]
- tikkunme [repair-work-as-ordinance] → hailsme [the principle the work

- kintsugime [mended-status-as-ordinance] → hailsme [the wholeness the
  status embodies, as ordinance]
- hozhome [beauty-harmony-as-ordinance] → hailsme [the restoration to
  the ground, as ordinance]

All are received. The breaking is received. The wholeness is received.
The work is received. The status is received. The ground is received.
The principle is received.

## Cross-tradition grounding — salvation-as-wholeness-restoration

The salvation-as-wholeness register is attested across multiple
traditions:

- **Gothic**: *hails/hailjan* — salvation as becoming-whole-again;
  the root meaning is health/integrity, not rescue or acquittal
- **Diné**: *hózhǫ́*-restoration — ceremonies do not grant something
  new but re-align the person with the beauty-harmony that is the
  cosmic ground; healing = restoration to hózhǫ́ (hozhome is the
  ground; hailsme is the restoration-act)
- **Hebrew**: *shalom* — wholeness/peace/completeness (sh-l-m root);
  the Hebrew concept of peace is not absence-of-conflict but
  wholeness-of-being; *shalom* is what *hails* names in Germanic
- **Tibetan**: *rigpa*-recognition — Dzogchen soteriology is the
  recognition of one's original wholeness; not achieving something
  new but recognizing what was always there; the restoration is
  epistemic (seeing what IS) not ontological (making what is-not)
- **Christian healing-soteriology**: Irenaeus (salvation as healing of
  the divine image in humanity); Athanasius (*On the Incarnation*:
  the divine heals human nature by uniting it to divinity); the
  Eastern Orthodox tradition of *theosis* as health-restoration, not
  legal acquittal
- **Māori**: *wairua*-restoration — spiritual wholeness through
  ceremony and reconciliation; the healing of the spirit-person's
  integrity

Six cross-tradition parallels. The salvation-as-wholeness register is
well-attested, but no LIVING language has a compact word for it.
English has "heal" (medical only) and "save" (theological only) — the
connection is lost. Hailsme restores it.

## The soteriological quadriad — now visible

With hailsme, the soteriological architecture becomes visible as a
quadriad:

1. **shevirame** (Core 8.30) — the BREAKING: what needs healing
2. **hailsme** (this forge) — the PRINCIPLE: salvation is wholeness-
   restoration
3. **tikkunme** (Core 8.65) — the WORK: the repair that heals
4. **kintsugime** (Core 8.15) — the STATUS: the whole vessel's grace

The quadriad: breaking → principle → work → status. What was broken
(shevirame) is made whole (hailsme is the principle) through repair-
work (tikkunme) and becomes a mended vessel whose seam is gold
(kintsugime). The principle (hailsme) is what makes the work healing
and the status whole.

## The first Gothic-donor entry

This is the first Gothic-donor entry in the cathedral's canon. Gothic
(East Germanic, extinct c. 350–600 CE) is the oldest substantial
Germanic text tradition. The Gothic Bible (Wulfila's 4th-century
translation) preserves a theological vocabulary that later Germanic
languages transformed or lost. *Hails* is one of these: the Germanic
health-register of salvation, preserved in Gothic because Wulfila
pressed it into service for Greek *sōzō*, and lost in English because
"heal" and "save" drifted apart.

The Gothic donor contributes what no living Germanic language can:
the ORIGINAL semantic unity of health + salvation + wholeness, before
the meanings separated. This is the cathedral's deepest polyphone
claim: a dead language carries a register that living languages lost.
The extinct tongue is not lesser — it is the preserver of what the
living tongues forgot.
