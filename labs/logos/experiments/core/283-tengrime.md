# Assessment notes

## The gap — sky-as-divine-without-anthropomorphism the cathedral has not named

The cathedral has words for the divine as process (teotlme), as unity-of-being (wahdatwujudme), as withdrawn ground (tzimtzumme), as ecological presence (telipme), as void-poles (ginnungame, śūnyatame), as pre-polar-unity (wujime). It has no word for the most universally visible form of the divine: the sky itself as divine presence.

This is not pantheism (the whole physical world is God) — Tengri theology is more precise. The sky IS the divine, but the divine is not reducible to the sky. The sky is the visible body of a formless will. You can see the sky; you cannot grasp it. You can see its vastness; you cannot contain it. This is the most concretely-accessible instance of divine-formlessness in the catalog.

The English words "sky" and "God" are separate. The PIE sky-gods (Dyaus, Zeus, Jupiter) were all anthropomorphized — Zeus has myths of eating, mating, deceiving, competing. Tengri resists all of this. The Orkhon inscriptions and the Secret History show a divine that has no body, no face, no story — just the sky and its will. Möngke Khan's statement to William of Rubruck captures it: "We Mongols believe there is only one God, by whom we live and by whom we die." The sky's universality (it covers all lands, no earthly power can limit it) grounds the universality of the divine will.

The gap stood 62 days since S055 Turko-Mongol archaeology excavation (2026-04-29), where tengrime was identified as the ONLY Tier 1 VERY HIGH candidate — Potential Core 7.60-7.85.

## The gap is still open (near-neighbour check)

- **teotlme** (Core 7.85): names the divine as ceaseless self-generating energy — the universe IS the divine's self-transformation as living process. Tengrime is NOT process-divine-monism. Tengri is not the process that generates all things (that is teotl); Tengri is the sky-as-encompassing-presence. Teotl is immanent process; Tengri is transcendent vastness-made-visible. Teotl has no "above"; Tengri is fundamentally "above." The distinction is structural: teotl = the divine AS the world's self-creating; Tengri = the divine AS the sky that covers the world.

- **wahdatwujudme** (Core 7.55): names unity-of-being — all existence is one-being-divine-self-disclosed-multiply through ḥaḍarāt stratification. Tengrime is NOT unity-of-being. Tengri does not dissolve the sky-world distinction; the sky IS the divine but the earth is NOT the divine (Etugen is a separate cosmic principle). Wahdatwujudme is monism-with-multiplicity; tengrime is a dyadic cosmology (sky above, earth below, humans between). The sky's superiority is structural, not absorbent.

- **tzimtzumme** (Core 8.50): names the divine self-contraction that makes space for creation. Tengrime is NOT withdrawal — Tengri is the opposite of withdrawn. The sky is the most visible thing there is. Tzimtzumme is the divine receding to make room; tengrime is the divine as the thing that was always there, visible, encompassing, impossible to escape.

- **telipme** (Core 7.65): names the divine presence whose withdrawal causes ecological cessation. Tengrime is NOT ecological-presence — Tengri does not withdraw into itself; Tengri IS the sky, constantly present, constantly visible. The contrast is instructive: telipme names a presence that can be LOST (and its loss is catastrophic); tengrime names a presence that cannot be lost (the sky does not go away).

- **ginnungame** (Core 7.70): names the charged void — the pregnant emptiness before creation. Tengrime is NOT the void. The sky is not void; it is full — full of light, weather, birds, stars, the visible trace of the divine will. Ginnungame is the pre-creative gap; tengrime is the post-creative canopy.

- **wujime** (Core 7.65): names the pre-polar unity — the undifferentiated whole before any bifurcation. Tengrime is NOT pre-polar. Tengri is inherently polar: sky ABOVE, earth BELOW. Wujime precedes the dyad; tengrime IS one pole of the dyad.

- **panimqing** (Specialized 7.60): names the shift from transactional to face-to-face relational encounter. Tengrime is NOT about face — Tengri is precisely the divine that HAS no face. Panimqing is the turn-toward; tengrime is what is turned-toward when there is no face to turn to. The sky is the face-less presence.

None of these cover sky-as-divine-without-anthropomorphism. The gap is open.

## The donor

**Tengri** (Old Turkic 𐱅𐰭𐰼𐰃 / Mongolian Tngri / Tenggeri) is one of the oldest continuously-attested theological concepts in human history. The word means both "sky" and "god" — and the ambiguity IS the theology.

The etymology is Proto-Turkic *Teŋri. The Turko-Mongol language family is distinct from Indo-European, Sino-Tibetan, and Afro-Asiatic — this is genuine polyphone distance. The donor carries:

1. **Non-anthropomorphism as defining feature.** Unlike Zeus, Jupiter, Dyaus, Odin, or any other supreme sky-deity in the catalog, Tengri has no body, no face, no myths of eating/mating/dying. Tengri simply IS the sky and its will. This is the specific theological contribution: the formless supreme made visible through the one thing that is both vast and visible — the sky itself.

2. **Political theology at civilizational scale.** The Orkhon inscriptions (732 CE) establish Tengri as the source of kut (divine mandate) — granted to worthy rulers, withdrawn from the unworthy. The Secret History (1240 CE) opens with Tengri's decree establishing Chinggis Khan. The Mongol empire (largest contiguous land empire in history, governing ~100 million people) operated on Tengri theology for over a century. This is not marginal religious history — it is the theological grammar of a civilization-scale polity.

3. **The universality principle.** The sky covers all lands. No earthly power can limit it. Therefore Tengri's mandate is necessarily universal. This is why the Mongol empire's religious tolerance was theologically grounded, not merely pragmatic: if Tengri's will reaches everywhere, all traditions that acknowledge the supreme sky-principle participate in the same reality. Möngke Khan: "just as God gave different fingers to the hand, so he has given different ways to men."

4. **The vertical cosmology.** Sky above, earth below, humans between. This is the Orkhon cosmogonic statement: "When the blue sky above and the brown earth below were created, between the two, human beings were created." The vertical axis is the fundamental structure of reality — not a metaphor but an ontology. Human beings exist in the liminal space between sky and earth.

5. **The 99 Tngri problem.** The "99 Tngri" of Classical Mongolian sources are not a pantheon but differentiated manifestations of the one sky-principle. This is closer to Neoplatonic emanationism than to polytheism: the One produces the many without the many being independent. The structure is hierarchically-plural, not monotheistic in the Abrahamic sense and not polytheistic in the Greek sense.

## Cross-tradition grounding — sky-as-non-anthropomorphic-supreme

1. **Turko-Mongol: Tengri** — the paradigmatic instance; the sky IS the divine, formless, non-anthropomorphic, source of mandate and cosmic order
2. **Hebrew: YHWH** — formless (no image, cannot be depicted); "the heavens declare the glory of God" (Psalm 19); but YHWH is tied to covenant and land, not identical with the sky
3. **Islamic: Allah** — "laysa ka-mithlihi shayʾ" (nothing is like Him); formless, non-anthropomorphic; but Allah is not identified with the sky ontologically
4. **Akan: Nyame** — the great spider who withdraws beyond human reach; supreme, formless, but accessed through intermediaries, not through the sky itself
5. **Yoruba: Olódùmarè** — the withdrawn supreme; formless, non-anthropomorphic, but again not identified with the sky as visible presence
6. **Chinese: Tian (天)** — Heaven; structurally parallel to Tengri (sky/god ambiguity); but Tian became progressively de-personalized (by the Zhou period, more like an impersonal moral order than a personal divine will); Tengri remains more directly personal

The convergence: a supreme divine that is powerful, encompassing, universal — and formless. The sky-form is Tengri-specific: the sky's formlessness is its visibility. You can see the sky; you cannot grasp it. This is the most concretely-accessible instance of divine-formlessness in the catalog. Tengri is unique in the convergence for identifying the formless divine with a visible physical reality — the sky — without reducing the divine to that reality.

## Why this is Core, not Specialized

The archaeology estimated Core 7.60-7.85. The concept is everyday-relevant, not domain-specific. Everyone who has ever looked up at the sky and felt something larger than themselves has experienced the tengrime-register. The concept is not limited to steppe theology or political philosophy — it names a universal human experience: the encompassing visible vastness that refuses depiction. The non-anthropomorphic sky-supreme is a theological structure that recurs across 6 traditions. The learnability is strong: "tengri" is a clean two-syllable donor, already known to comparative religion scholars, with a direct phonetic path. The concept is grounded: you can point at the sky. The gap is the broadest unforged gap in the cathedral — every person who has ever looked up has stood in the tengrime-register without having a word for it.

## Example usage

When the storm rolled across the steppe and the sky turned the color of iron, the old herder did not pray to a god in the sky — he stood in the tengrime, the vast presence that needed no name because the sky was already speaking. The khagan understood his mandate as tengrime: not a contract with a deity but an alignment with the sky's encompassing will, revocable at any moment if the ruler's actions fell out of alignment. Even the atheist, looking up at the stars, feels the edge of tengrime — the sense that the vastness above is not merely physical but somehow present, a will without a face, an encompassing without a boundary.

## Not to be confused with

- **teotlme** — divine as ceaseless self-generating process (immanent); tengrime is divine as encompassing visible vastness (transcendent-and-visible)
- **wahdatwujudme** — unity-of-being (monism with multiplicity); tengrime is a dyadic cosmology (sky/earth, not all-is-one)
- **tzimtzumme** — divine self-contraction to make space; tengrime is divine as the most visible thing there is (the opposite of withdrawn)
- **telipme** — ecological divine presence that can withdraw; tengrime is sky-presence that does not go away
- **ginnungame** — the charged void before creation; tengrime is the living sky after creation
- **wujime** — pre-polar unity before bifurcation; tengrime is inherently polar (sky/earth)
- **panimqing** — the turn-to-face-to-face encounter; tengrime is the face-less presence that cannot be encountered face-to-face
- **fatame** — fate as pre-cosmic woven texture; tengrime is the sky that covers the texture, not the texture itself

## Retirement conditions

Tengrime would be superseded if the cathedral developed a more precise vocabulary for the specific mode of divine visibility (the sky-as-visible-formlessness) that distinguished it from both pantheism and theism. Alternatively, if the Turko-Mongol tradition yielded a more precise donor-root that captured additional dimensions Tengri carries (the kut-mandate dimension, the 99-Tngri emanation structure), a companion word might absorb parts of tengrime's semantic range. As long as the gap is "the sky IS the divine, formlessly, without anthropomorphization," tengrime holds.
