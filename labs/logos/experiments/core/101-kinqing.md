# 101 — kinqing

_Second Core Canon candidate. Tests 3-family polyphone (Indo-European + Sino-Tibetan) in a 2-syllable compound. Targets the Constitution's seed-gap: friendship preserved across long silence._

---

## The gap

English has *friendship, kinship, bond, rapport, intimacy* — none names the specific phenomenon: **a relation that has not exchanged contact in months or years, but which, when re-entered, resumes without the re-entry being awkward or remedial**. The relation is carried structurally; frequency is not its life-support.

Arabic names something close via *ukhuwwa* (brotherhood-strength forged through shared trial). Japanese *ishin-denshin* (heart-to-heart without words) names the transmission-style. Chinese 情 (*qing*) names the deep emotional regard that persists. None of these match English speakers' colloquial register; the phenomenon remains ambient-but-unnamed in daily English.

## The forge

**Donors:**
- **English *kin*** — from PIE \**ǵenh₁-* (family, birth). Carries: chosen-or-blood relation, non-contingent regard, carrying of identity across generations.
- **Mandarin 情 (qing)** — deep-emotional-bond; the earnestness-of-feeling that sustains relation without verbal affirmation. Used as final component of many Chinese relational compounds (youqing, aiqing, qingyi).

**Compound:** kinqing = kin-qing → the qing of kin, or kinship-qing. Names the bond-quality that has kin-like durability without necessarily being blood-kin, and qing-like depth without necessarily being expressive.

## Full sense

Kinqing names the bond-quality that persists structurally rather than through maintenance. Two people in kinqing do not need to talk weekly; they are not harmed by six months of silence; when they meet again, the resumption is immediate and unawkward. The relation is not frequency-dependent.

Kinqing differs from kinship (blood or institutional) because it arises from mutual regard, not shared origin. It differs from friendship-as-ordinarily-named because it does not require ongoing presence. It differs from nostalgia-for-friendship because it is a *live* relation, not a memory of one.

Kinqing can begin in a single meeting, or build slowly over years. Once established, it has remarkable durability — longer than marriages, longer than professional relationships, sometimes lifelong. It is one of the principal forms of human connection, and modern English has no term for it.

## Example usage

- _Her closest kinqing was with a woman she'd seen three times in fifteen years._
- _You know it's kinqing when you reply to the text a year late and neither of you mentions the gap._
- _Kinqing is the most reliable predictor of who shows up at your funeral._
- _After his divorce, only his kinqing-relations remained reachable; the friendships-of-convenience evaporated._
- _Kinqing is what remains when life's logistical demands strip away maintenance-requiring-relations._

## Not confused with

- **kinship** — blood/institutional; kinqing is chosen and regard-based.
- **friendship** — too broad; many friendships are maintenance-dependent.
- **best-friendship** — implies hierarchy; kinqing does not require primacy.
- **ukhuwwa** (Arabic) — kinqing's nearest cousin; ukhuwwa specifically emphasizes hardship-forging; kinqing is broader.
- **ishin-denshin** (Japanese) — emphasizes wordless-transmission; kinqing is the relational bond, ishin-denshin is one of its expressions.
- **qing** (Mandarin, alone) — broader than kinqing; qing includes romantic and emotional states.
- **nostalgia** — memory of a lost relation; kinqing is a live relation.

## Grammar observations

1. **Count vs mass**: "a kinqing" (specific relation) and "kinqing" (the phenomenon). Both work via English conventions.
2. **Prepositional**: "kinqing with X", "in kinqing with X". Emerged naturally.
3. **Degree**: "deep kinqing", "early kinqing". English adjectives.
4. **Reciprocity-implicit**: "we have kinqing" is unstated-mutual; "I have kinqing with X" implies X has it back.

**Finding**: this word also needed no engineered grammar — host-language syntax carried all required distinctions.
