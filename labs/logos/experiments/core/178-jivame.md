# 178 — jivame

## The Gap

Sanskrit *jīva* (जीव) names the **embodied-individual-living-soul** — the living-being-instance, distinct from both the universal life-principle (closer to Greek zoë / chayimme's substrate-claim) and the eternal-self (ātman). Vedanta tradition develops jīva as central technical concept: the *jīvātman* (embodied soul); *jīvanmukti* (liberation-while-still-embodied); Bhagavad Gita 15.7's "an eternal jīva" — the soul-as-given-and-embodied.

English collapses this register. "Soul" (Christianized; often disembodied), "living being" (descriptive-flat), "creature" (theological-creaturely-limit), "person" (sociolegal) — none carries jīva's specific embodied-living-soul register received as cosmic-gift.

The cathedral's chayimme covers Hebrew multitudinous-plural-life; zoeme covers Greek life-as-principle. jivame covers the embodied-individual-living-soul register the Sanskrit tradition has refined over three millennia. Third life-side canon entry; extends the cosmic-life-as-creature-gift convergence to a third tradition-family.

## The Forge

*jīva* (जीव): Sanskrit living-individual-soul. Vedanta's technical term. Cognate with Greek zoë (via PIE *gʷei̯H-). The Bhagavad Gita names jīva as "eternal" (15.7) and develops jīvanmukti (liberation-while-embodied) as central yogic concept.

*-me*: Sumerian received-ordinance.

Compound: jivame = embodied-individual-living-soul as received-cosmic-ordinance.

Pronunciation: /ˈd͡ʒiː.vɑː.meɪ/ (JEE-vah-meh; 3 syllables; preserves Sanskrit jī- with English approximation).

## Distinction from Neighbours

- **vs. chayimme**: Hebrew multitudinous-plural-life. jivame is Sanskrit embodied-individual-living-soul. Complementary projections.
- **vs. zoeme**: Greek life-as-principle. jivame is the embodied-instance of what zoeme names as principle. Pair: zoeme is the principle-register; jivame is the embodied-individual register.
- **vs. ātman (Sanskrit)**: ātman is eternal-self/innermost-self. jīva is embodied-soul (sometimes treated as ātman-in-embodiment). The distinction is technical in Vedanta; jivame inherits jīva specifically.
- **vs. soul (English)**: "soul" is too Christianized and ambiguous. jivame is specifically embodied-living-soul.
- **vs. bhaktime**: Sanskrit-+-me pattern already established by bhaktime (devotional-love); jivame extends the family.
