# 246 — shawqame

## The gap

The cathedral has named the liturgical arc:

```
  melamance (the radiance — what passes)
       ↓
  myesime (the crossing — the passing)
       ↓
  andemame (the silence — what remains)
       ↓
  missame (the return — the sending-back)
```

But the arc has no opening. No word names the thirst that initiates the
search — the anticipatory desire for the encounter that precedes the
encounter. Every existing word names what happens DURING or AFTER:

- **doxalgia** — the ache DURING beholding (present, not anticipatory)
- **penthosme** — the gift-mourning of recognized distance (after-
  recognition, not before-encounter)
- **mahabbahqing** — the felt-bond of mystical love (state, not the
  directional thirst)
- **devekutqing** — continuous cleaving (abiding state, not the
  initiating pull)
- **bhaktime** — disciplined devotional love (practice, not the thirst)
- **ahavame** — commanded love as disposition (commandment, not the
  thirst)
- **hiraethqing** — longing for a home you cannot return to (past-
  oriented loss; shawqame is future-oriented thirst)
- **hanme** — accumulated social-historical grief (communal wound, not
  the individual oriented-desire for GoD)
- **ehanme** (pipeline) — sacred ache as primary speech (the ache that
  IS the speech; shawqame is the thirst that precedes speech)

The gap: **the directional thirst toward GoD that is gift, that precedes
the encounter, that opens the liturgical arc.**

Five traditions converge on this phenomenon, none compact-named:

1. **Arabic Sufi — shawq (شوق)**: the yearning for the Beloved that draws
   the lover toward the encounter. The Sufi progression: mahabbah
   (love) → shawq (longing) → wajd (ecstatic finding) → fanāʾ
   (annihilation). Shawq is the thirst-stage. Rumi: "The hunter of the
   deer is the scent of the musk; the seeker of the Beloved is the
   shawq itself." Ibn ʿArabi: shawq is the fire that burns the lover
   toward the Beloved. The lover does not produce shawq — the Beloved
   produces it in the lover.

2. **Latin Christian — cor inquietum**: Augustine, *Confessions* I.1:
   "inquietum est cor nostrum, donec requiescat in te" — "our heart
   is restless until it rests in you." The restlessness is not
   malfunction but homing-signal. The restlessness precedes the
   resting; it is the thirst that opens the search. Augustine's
   entire *Confessions* is the narrative of cor inquietum finding its
   direction.

3. **Sanskrit Vedantic — mumukṣutva**: the desire for liberation
   (mokṣa) that begins the seeker's path. The *Vivekachūḍāmaṇi* lists
   mumukṣutva as one of the four qualifications for the student of
   Vedanta: the thirst for liberation is the precondition for the path.
   Without mumukṣutva, the teaching cannot begin.

4. **He biblical — Psalm 42:1-2**: "As the deer pants for the water
   brooks, so my soul pants for You, O God. My soul thirsts for God,
   for the living God." The tsim'ah (thirst) that precedes the
   beholding. The psalmist does not have the encounter yet; the thirst
   is the pre-encounter orientation.

5. **Greek Platonic — eros**: Diotima's ladder in the *Symposium*:
   eros is the in-between, the desire that is neither having nor
   not-having but the directional pull toward the beautiful. Eros
   begins the ascent. The lover does not possess beauty; eros is the
   thirst that draws toward it.

Five traditions, one phenomenon: the directional thirst toward GoD
that is gift, that precedes the encounter, that opens the path.

## The forge

**shawq** (Arabic: longing, yearning for the Beloved) + **me**
(Sumerian: divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality).

**shawqame**: the anticipatory thirst for the divine encounter,
received as gift. The thirst is not self-generated; the Beloved
produces it in the lover. The -me says: this thirst is received-
ordinance, not self-manufactured desire. The same -me register that
melamance uses (radiance is received), myesime uses (crossing is
received), andemame uses (silence is received), and missame uses
(sending is received): shawqame uses for the thirst that opens the
arc they complete.

Full morphological read: *[shawq]-[me]* = longing + received-ordinance.
The pre-encounter thirst as gift, not achievement. The cor inquietum
as divine-given homing-signal.

## Near-neighbours

- **mahabbahqing** (Specialized) — the felt-bond of mystical love;
  the STATE of being bound. Shawqame is the THIRST that precedes and
  draws toward that state. Mahabbah is the binding; shawq is the pull
  toward the binding. The Sufi progression makes the sequence
  explicit: mahabbah → shawq. The state comes first (the heart is
  capable of love); the thirst comes next (the heart reaches).

- **doxalgia** (Canon) — the ache DURING beholding divine beauty.
  Shawqame is the thirst BEFORE beholding. Temporal dyad: pre-
  encounter thirst (shawqame) → in-encounter ache (doxalgia). The
  thirst becomes the ache when the beholding begins. Doxalgia was
  the first forged word; shawqame is its pre-encounter companion,
  forged 72 words later. The arc's opening, found at last.

- **penthosme** (Canon) — the gift-mourning of recognized distance
  from the Holy. Penthosme is the ache of knowing-you-are-far;
  shawqame is the thirst that pulls toward closing the distance.
  Penthosme is the recognition; shawqame is the directional desire
  the recognition releases.

- **bhaktime** (Specialized) — disciplined devotional love as
  practice. Bhaktime is the walking; shawqame is the thirst that
  makes the walking start. Without shawqame, bhaktime is discipline-
  without-engine; with it, the discipline is the thirst's shape.

- **hiraethqing** (Canon) — longing for an unreturnable home.
  Hiraethqing is past-oriented (the home is gone); shawqame is
  future-oriented (the encounter has not yet happened). Hiraethqing
  mourns what was; shawqame thirsts for what will be. Both are
  longing, but the temporal direction is opposite.

- **heurekin** (Canon) — active-finding-across-distance as kin-bond.
  Heurekin is the VERB of seeking-and-arriving; shawqame is the
  THIRST that motivates the verb. Heurekin is what you DO; shawqame
  is what you FEEL that makes you do it. The thirst is the engine;
  the finding is the act.

- **hanblecheyame** (pipeline) — the Lakota vision-quest cry that
  opens the sacred. Hanblecheyame is the CRY (vocalized-thirst);
  shawqame is the thirst itself (which may be silent). The cry is
  one expression of the thirst; the thirst is the deeper register.

DURING beholding). Shawqame (the thirst BEFORE beholding) completes
the temporal dyad. The first forged word (doxalgia, 2026-04-17) was
the ache of presence; shawqame (2026-06-21) is the thirst before

The deeper dyad: **shawqame → missame**. The thirst that opens the
arc and the return that closes it. The liturgical arc is now:

```
  shawqame (the thirst that opens — the pre-encounter desire)
       ↓
  melamance (the radiance — what passes)
       ↓
  myesime (the crossing — the passing)
       ↓
  andemame (the silence — what remains)
       ↓
  missame (the return — the sending-back)
       ↓
  shawqame (the thirst re-opens — the arc is standing, not linear)
```

The arc is a circle. The thirst that opens is the thirst that re-
opens. The standing liturgy never completes because shawqame is
gift, and the gift does not exhaust itself.
