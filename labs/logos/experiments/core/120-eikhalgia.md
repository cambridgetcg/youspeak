# 120 — eikhalgia

_Core Canon attempt. Names the observer's-ache at vocation-failure. The archetype: David's lament over Saul._

## The gap

Seed-gap #9. A respected figure fails at the center of their calling. The general panics; the teacher ridicules the student; the elder speaks cruelty; the surgeon trembles; the prophet lies. The observer's response is not disillusionment (too general — that covers any view-revision), not betrayal (that requires broken-covenant), not pity (that flattens). It is specifically: *they were made for this; they failed this*. The ache centers on the VOCATION-FALL, not the person's general flaws.

English circumlocutes: "hard to watch," "fallen from grace," "not who they used to be," "shadow of themselves." No compact term.

## The forge

**Donors:**

- Hebrew ʾeikh (אֵיךְ) — the lament-particle "how!" that opens the two archetypal fall-lamentations of the Hebrew Bible: David's dirge (2 Sam 1:19, "How are the mighty fallen in the midst of the battle! O Jonathan, thou wast slain in thine high places.") and Lamentations 1:1 ("How doth the city sit solitary, that was full of people!"). In Hebrew liturgical tradition, Lamentations is called *Eikhah* after its opening word — the word IS the book's name. This is the ancient-Semitic lament-voice.

- Greek algos (ἄλγος) — ache; established in YOUSPEAK via the -algia suffix (doxalgia 7.55 specialized).

**Compound:** eikh + algia → *eikhalgia*. The lament-how ache. The ache whose native cry is "how!"

## The specific near-miss

Learnability=5 is the block. The "eikh-" cluster is the cost of importing the Hebrew lament-voice. Alternatives tested:

- **eikhance** (drop the -algia; just eikh + -ance): 3 syl, learn 6, but clarity_yield drops to 6 because without -algia the observer-ache is not morphologically visible. Weighted ~7.20.
- **naflalgia** (Hebrew *naflū*, "they fell," from 2 Sam 1:19) + algia: 4 syl, but *naflū* is verb-form not particle; less universally-recognizable than eikh.
- **patitalgia** (Sanskrit *patita*, fallen-from-station + algia): Sanskrit + Greek = same-family IE polyphone 6; weighted ~7.25.
- **hamartalgia** (Greek hamartia + algia): Greek-only polyphone 5; weighted ~6.95.

*eikhalgia* scores highest of the candidates at 7.40 and carries the strongest tradition-anchor (the named biblical-liturgical form of the lament). It is the right word held at refine.

Addresses Constitution seed-gap #9. The forge is load-bearing regardless of tier — the word names a gap that the Constitution identifies as Core-priority, and the register-threshold question it raises is itself part of the Session 002 Amendment-I case.

## Etymological note

*ʾeikh* (אֵיךְ) is the Hebrew lament-particle with no compact English translation. English "how!" can carry wonder (Psalm 8), but not natively the fall-lament register. The combination with *algos* creates a word that IS its own question — eikhalgia asks "how?" as it aches. The ache is the question. The question is the ache.
