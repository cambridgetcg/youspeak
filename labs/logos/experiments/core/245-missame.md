# 245 — missame

_The return-to-ordinary carrying what was received. The sending-back
from the sacred encounter into the world, where the return is not a
diminishment but the encounter's fulfillment. The liturgy named for
its ending: missa, "the sent" — the Mass that names itself by what it
does to the participants after, not what it is in itself. The
companion-pole to myesime: the crossing into the sacred ↔ the crossing
back carrying what was received._

## The discovery-move

The cathedral holds the full arc of the encounter:

- **melamance** (8.65) — the radiance that IS the presence
- **myesime** (8.10) — the crossing that changes who you are
- **andemame** (7.90) — the silence that IS the presence
- **ālayame** (7.98) — the storehouse that holds the seeds

But the arc is incomplete. The cathedral has the entry, the crossing,
the presence, the storage — but no word for the RETURN. The sending-
back. The return-to-ordinary after the sacred encounter, carrying what
was received. The myesime-companion-pole: the crossing INTO ↔ the
crossing BACK. The dyad's missing pole.

Searching the archaeology, the gap was already flagged — not inferred,
explicitly noted:

> **missame** — *missa* as "the sent" — Mass named for the
> commissioning at the end; Core 7.40–7.60

— archaeology/latin/vulgate-liturgical.md, §VII

And the structural observation that opens the whole register:

> The entire liturgical event is named for its ending: the sending
> of the people back into the world, now transformed by what they've
> received. This is structurally significant: the liturgy names itself
> by what it does to the participants *after* rather than what it is
> *in itself*. The Mass is not named for sacrifice, or meal, or prayer
> — it's named for the commissioning at the end. The Eucharist makes
> you *sent*.

— archaeology/latin/vulgate-liturgical.md, §I

The gap appears in six additional tradition-files, each deepening it
from a different angle:

- **Hebrew shaliach**: the sent one, root of "apostle." The sent-one
  carries the encounter as commission, not as private experience.
- **Zen ox-herding (入鄽垂手)**: the tenth and final picture — the
  enlightened one returns to the marketplace with helping hands down.
  The return is not enlightenment's failure but its fulfillment.
- **Zen "chop wood, carry water"**: after enlightenment, the ordinary
  tasks. The return is the proof that the crossing was real.
- **Sufi baraka-overflow**: the blessing must circulate, not be
  hoarded. The sending is the blessing's purpose.
- **Lakota post-vision-quest reintegration**: the vision is not for
  the seeker alone. The return to the relational web is the vision's
  fulfillment.
- **Christian dismissal-formula**: "Go in peace to love and serve the
  Lord." The peace is received in order to be carried out.

None of the cathedral's existing words names the specific thing:
the return-to-ordinary-as-commissioning — the sending-back that is
the encounter's fulfillment, not its diminishment.

## The phenomenon

Five features must hold together:

1. **The return is the encounter's fulfillment, not its diminishment.**
   This is the structural insight embedded in the Latin donor: the
   Mass names itself by its ending. The encounter is not a retreat
   from the world; it is a commission back into it. The Zen ox-herding
   makes this visible: the tenth picture is not a fall from the ninth
   (the mountaintop) but the ninth's purpose. The marketplace is
   where the enlightenment belongs. The return is not a loss of the
   sacred; it is the sacred's delivery-vehicle.

2. **The return carries what was received.** The one sent is not
   empty. The shaliach carries the sender's authority. The post-
   vision-quest returner carries the vision. The Mass-dismissed
   communicant carries the Eucharist. The return is not a leaving-
   behind but a bringing-out. What was received in the encounter
   becomes active in the ordinary through the return. The carrying
   is the return's content.

3. **The return is received, not chosen.** The -me suffix names this:
   the sending-back is received-ordinance, not self-directed. The
   one sent does not decide to return; the return is the given-
   structure of having-received. The Latin dismissal is spoken TO the
   people, not BY them. "Ite, missa est" — Go, it is sent. The sending
   is received. The shaliach is sent, not self-appointed. The baraka
   must circulate; the disciple does not choose to spend it. Missa-
   as-gift, not missa-as-choice.

4. **The return transfigures the ordinary, not abandons the sacred.**
   "After enlightenment, chop wood, carry water." The ordinary tasks
   are the same tasks; the one doing them is different. The water is
   the same water; the carrying is different carrying. The return does
   not de-sacralize; it re-sacralizes the ordinary by carrying the
   sacred into it. The marketplace after the mountaintop is not the
   marketplace before the mountaintop — it is the marketplace transfigured
   by the one who returns to it with helping hands down.

5. **The return is for the other, not for the self.** The shaliach is
   sent for the recipient, not for the sender. The baraka overflows
   for the world, not for the Sufi. The helping hands are down for the
   marketplace, not for the enlightened one. The dismissal sends you
   out to love and serve — the Lord and the neighbor. The return's
   purpose is outside the returner. The encounter was for you; the
   sending is for them.

## The forge

**Donors:**

*Latin missa* is the primary stem-donor. From *dimissa* (the dismissal,
the sending-out). The closing formula "Ite, missa est" (Go, it is sent)
gave the Mass its name. The entire liturgical event — 1500+ years of
continuous Western Christian practice — is named for its ending: the
sending of the people back into the world, now transformed by what
they've received. The Latin insight embedded in the donor: the
encounter is for the sending. The Mass is not named for sacrifice
(sacrificium), or meal (convivium), or prayer (oratio) — it's named
for the commissioning at the end. The Eucharist makes you *sent*.
This is the deepest structural claim in the entire liturgical
vocabulary: the liturgy is a sending-mechanism, not a receiving-
chamber. The Mass-as-commissioning maps onto the apostolic structure:
encounter → transformation → sending.

*Hebrew shaliach* (שָׁלִיחַ) is the secondary stem-donor. The sent
one, the envoy — root of "apostle" (ἀπόστολος, the Greek calque).
The shaliach carries the sender's authority: "the one sent is as the
one who sent him" (m. Ber. 5:5). The sent-one does not speak for
themselves but as the one-who-was-sent. The apostolic structure:
encounter → transformation → sending. The shaliach is the vocational
form of what missa enacts liturgically. The Hebrew insight: the
return carries authority, not just experience.

*Sumerian me* (𒈨) is the suffix-donor. Divine-ordinance, received-
gift-quality. The -me says: the sending-back is received-ordinance,
not self-directed. The one sent does not choose the return; the
return is the given-structure of having-received. What you received
requires carrying; the carrying is not optional. The same -me register
that myesime uses (the crossing is received, not achieved) and that
andemame uses (the silence is received, not constructed): missame
uses for the sending (the return is received, not chosen).

**Compound:** missa + me → **missame** /mɪˈsɑː.meɪ/
(mis-SAH-may; 3 syllables).

The compression: *missa-* (2 syllables, the sending) + *-me*
(1 syllable, received-ordinance). Three syllables — shorter than
andemame (4), myesime (4), ālayame (4). Comparable to sigame (3),
jiritsume (4 but compact). The linking is clean: the Latin stem is
preserved whole (missa-) and the Sumerian suffix is appended (-me).

**Polyphone check:** Latin (Indo-European) + Hebrew (Semitic) +
Sumerian (isolate). Three language families — the strongest polyphone
profile among the recent -me forges. The Latin donor carries the
liturgical-structural precision (missa, the naming-for-ending); the
Hebrew donor carries the vocational-authority dimension (shaliach,
the sent-one); the Sumerian donor carries the received-ordinance
register. Each contributes what the others cannot. Three genetically
unrelated families.

## Near-neighbours and the precise distinctions

- **vs. myesime** (Core, the companion-pole): myesime is the threshold-
  crossing INTO the sacred — the ontological passage that changes who
  you are. Missame is the crossing BACK — the return to the ordinary
  carrying what the crossing gave. The dyad: myesime (going in) ↔
  missame (coming back). The encounter without the return is incomplete;
  the return without the encounter is empty. Together they name the
  full liturgical arc: entry → presence → sending.

- **vs. metastrophesis** (Liturgy experiment 003): metastrophesis is
  the turning-kept — the state that remains after the encounter, the
  aftermath-as-abiding-trace. Missame is the sending-out — the act
  of return, not the state of having-been-changed. Metastrophesis
  is what the encounter leaves IN you; missame is what the encounter
  sends you BACK WITH. The one is internal-trace; the other is
  external-movement.

- **vs. athaumasma** (Liturgy experiment 004): athaumasma is the
  indelibility — the trace that cannot be un-traced, the memory that
  has become load-bearing in the self. Missame is the carrying-out of
  that trace into the world. Athaumasma is what the encounter installs
  in you; missame is what you do with it. The indelibility is for the
  sending; the sending carries the indelibility.

- **vs. hodosme** (Core, the way-as-ordinance): hodosme is the journey-
  as-received-ordinance — the path you walk. Missame is the specific
  moment of return-from-encounter — not the whole journey but the
  sending-back that completes the liturgical arc. Hodosme is the
  road; missame is the dismissal at the end of the Mass that sends
  you back onto the road, now carrying what the Mass gave.

- **vs. zakarqing** (Core, active-covenantal-memorial): zakarqing is
  the active remembering that constitutes the relationship across
  forgetting. Missame is the active returning that constitutes the
  encounter's fulfillment across the return-to-ordinary. Zakarqing
  carries the beloved into the forgetting; missame carries the sacred
  into the ordinary. Both are active-carrying-as-constituting-action.

- **vs. "sending" / "commission" / "dismissal" (English)**: English
  flattens. "Sending" is too generic (you can send a letter).
  "Commission" is institutional (a military commission). "Dismissal"
  sounds like ending rather than commissioning. "Return" is spatial,
  not theological. Missame compact-names the specific register: the
  return-to-ordinary-as-the-encounter's-fulfillment, received not
  chosen, carrying what was received, for the other not the self.

- **vs. "apostolate" / "mission" (Christian English)**: these name
  the institutional office or the project, not the structural act of
  return. Missame names the act itself — the sending-back-as-
  fulfillment — that the apostolate institutionalizes and the mission
  operationalizes. The institution is a specific form; missame is the
  realm-feature.

## Canonical position

Core canon (8.08 weighted). The commissioning / return-to-ordinary /
liturgical-sending register, opened. First Latin-missa-donor Core
entry. First Hebrew-shaliach-donor Core entry. The word completes the
liturgical arc with myesime:

```
  myesime  (the crossing INTO the sacred — the going-in)
       ↓
  melamance / andemame  (the presence — radiance / silence)
       ↓
  missame  (the crossing BACK — the return-to-ordinary carrying
           what was received, the sending-as-fulfillment)
```

The full arc: you cross in (myesime), you encounter the presence
(melamance / andemame), you are sent back (missame). The encounter
without the return is incomplete; the return without the encounter
is empty. The liturgy names itself by its ending — and now the
ending has a name.

Also pairs with:
- **ālayame** (the storehouse — missame is the return that plants
  the seeds the storehouse holds; the seeds are for the planting)
- **metastrophesis** (the abiding-trace — missame is the active-
  carrying-out of what metastrophesis holds-in)
- **athaumasma** (the indelibility — missame is what you do with
  what cannot be un-traced; the indelible mark is for the sending)
- **zakarqing** (the active-memorial — missame is the active-
  return; zakarqing carries the beloved into forgetting, missame
  carries the sacred into the ordinary)
- **hodosme** (the way — missame is the dismissal that sends you
  back onto the way, now carrying what the encounter gave)

Liturgy slot: the return-to-ordinary in the standing liturgy's
search for the word for GoD's beauty. The search is not a retreat
from the world; it is a commission back into it. Each beat crosses
in (myesime), encounters (melamance / andemame), and is sent back
(missame). The beat is not for the beat; it is for the carrying-out.
The longing is the worship; the sending is the longing's shape.
The standing liturgy never completes because the sending never
ends: each return carries what was received into the ordinary, and
the ordinary, transfigured, asks for another crossing.
