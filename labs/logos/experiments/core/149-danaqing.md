# 149 — danaqing

_Third sacrifice-register aligned entry. Names the voluntary generous dimension of sacrifice: gift-giving-as-felt-bond. Five-tradition convergence on sacrifice as gift rather than loss. First sacrifice-register entry using the qing (felt-bond) morpheme — choosing qing over me because the gift-giving dimension is fundamentally RELATIONAL: the gift creates and sustains a bond._

## The discovery-move

English "sacrifice" has drifted from Latin sacrificium ("to make sacred") toward "giving something up at great cost." This drift systematically suppresses the gift-dimension: sacrifice-as-generous-offering, sacrifice-as-voluntary-gift, sacrifice-as-love-made-material. Five traditions preserve this dimension:

The key insight from Sanskrit: **dāna is linked to satya (truth)**. In the Rigveda, giving and truth share a moral axis — the generous giver participates in cosmic truth-order (ṛta). To give freely is to align with truth. To withhold (lobha — greed) is to misalign.

## The phenomenon

**Sanskrit dāna (दान):**

From root *dā* "to give." The Rigveda establishes dāna as cosmic-ethical act: the generous giver sustains cosmic order (ṛta), participates in the gods' own generosity (the gods gave first — the cosmos is gift). Dāna-parāmitā is the FIRST of six Buddhist perfections — generosity is the foundation virtue from which all others grow.

Definition: "relinquishing ownership of something without expecting anything in return." This is precise: dāna is NOT exchange. It is unilateral gift. The return (if any) is the deepening of the relationship — a felt-bond, not a transaction.

The Bhagavad Gita distinguishes:
- **sāttvika dāna** — giving with no expectation, at the right time, to the right person → aligned
- **rājasika dāna** — giving with expectation of return or reluctantly → misaligned
- **tāmasika dāna** — giving contemptuously or carelessly → anti-aligned

This internal gradation within dāna already provides an alignment-sieve for gift-sacrifice.

**Hebrew minchah and nedavah:**

Hebrew minchah (מנחה) — gift-tribute offering. The grain-offering of the Levitical system. Not blood-sacrifice but cereal/flour/oil — the daily bread given to DIVINE. The word means simply "gift" in secular usage; applied to DIVINE context it becomes "offering."

Hebrew nedavah (נדבה) — freewill offering. From n-d-v "to volunteer." This offering CANNOT be commanded. It is by definition voluntary, spontaneous, from the overflow of the heart. The Psalms: "I will freely sacrifice to you" (Ps 54:6). The freedom-dimension is inherent in the word.

**Akkadian sheriktu:**

From sharaku "to bestow, present." The formal gift-presentation in temple contexts. The word carries sovereign-generosity: the giver bestows from abundance, not from compulsion.

**Yoruba adimu:**

Gift-offering to orishas with mythological origin in Yemaya's sacrifice. The adimu sustains the covenant-bond between the visible and invisible worlds — a relational gift-exchange that maintains cosmic equilibrium.

**Latin sacrificium:**

The original meaning — sacer ("sacred") + facere ("to make") — is "to make sacred," NOT "to give up." The object placed on the altar is not lost but transformed: from profane to sacred. The modern English "sacrifice = loss" is semantic drift that has distorted worship-understanding.

## The forge

**Donors:**

- **Sanskrit *dāna*** (दान) — gift, giving, generosity. From *dā* "to give." Carries: voluntary-generous-giving-without-return; the first Buddhist perfection; Rigvedic link to cosmic truth (ṛta/satya); the three gradations of giving (sāttvika/rājasika/tāmasika). Phonetically: /dɑːnɑː/ → DAH-nah in YOUSPEAK.

- **Mandarin *qíng*** (情) — felt-bond, deep emotional connection. Established YOUSPEAK morpheme. Here marking: gift-giving-as-felt-bond — the gift creates and deepens a relational bond between giver and DIVINE. The choice of qing over me is deliberate: the gift-dimension of sacrifice is fundamentally RELATIONAL (it creates/sustains a bond) rather than ORDINANCE-received (it's not so much "received-from-DIVINE" as "offered-toward-DIVINE-in-bond").

**Compound:** *dāna* + *qing* → **danaqing** (DAH-nah-ching; 3 syllables).

The compound names: gift-giving-as-felt-bond. The voluntary generous dimension of sacrifice understood as relational-bond between giver and DIVINE. When the creature gives freely — without compulsion, without expectation of return — the gift creates and deepens the felt-bond with the DIVINE.

## The alignment-sieve application

Danaqing establishes the third criterion of the alignment-sieve:

**Aligned sacrifice gives freely.** If the sacrificial act is voluntary, generous, without compulsion or expectation of return, it is danaqing-aligned. If it is extracted, demanded, or transactional, it fails danaqing.

The Bhagavad Gita's sieve within dāna:
- Sāttvika dāna → **passes danaqing** (right time, right person, no expectation)
- Rājasika dāna → **questionable** (giving with strings attached)
- Tāmasika dāna → **fails danaqing** (contemptuous, careless giving)

Cross-tradition application:
- Hebrew nedavah (freewill offering) → passes danaqing
- Buddhist dāna-parāmitā → passes danaqing (first perfection = foundation)
- Aztec nextlahualli → **fails danaqing** (debt-payment, not gift; compelled, not voluntary)
- Any offering motivated by fear-of-punishment → **fails danaqing** (compulsion ≠ generosity)
