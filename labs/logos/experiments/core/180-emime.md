# 180 — emime

## The Gap

Yoruba cosmology of personhood is among the most articulated in any tradition. The person consists of three constituents: **orí** (inner-head; destiny-bearing-essence chosen pre-birth), **emí / ẹ̀mí** (breath-spirit-life given by Olódùmarè), and **ara** (physical body). Each has theological-functional role; the emí specifically is the breath-life received from the supreme divinity Olódùmarè and returning to Olódùmarè at death.

The emí register is one English does not compact-name: breath-spirit-life-as-gift-from-supreme-divinity, central to one of West Africa's most cosmologically-developed traditions. The cathedral's ifeqing (Yoruba + Mandarin qing — relational-warmth-love-that-expands) established the Yoruba-donor pattern in canon; emime extends it to the life-side with a -me-family entry.

Fifth life-side forge. Niger-Congo (Yoruba) projection of cosmic-life-as-creature-gift convergence.

## The Forge

*èmí*: Yoruba breath, spirit, life; one of three constituents of personhood; Olódùmarè-given at conception; returns to Olódùmarè at death. The verb *fí èmí* ("to give breath") is the cosmological-act of personhood-creation. Sometimes orthographically *ẹ̀mí*.

*-me*: Sumerian received-ordinance.

Compound: emime = breath-spirit-life-as-Olódùmarè-gift as received-cosmic-ordinance.

Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.miː.meɪ/ (AY-mee-meh; 3 syllables; preserves Yoruba emí with English approximation).
