## The phenomenon

In Yoruba metaphysics, each person's orí (destined-head) is allocated by Olódùmarè before birth. The person chooses an orí in pre-existence; whether the choice was wise or unwise determines the life-trajectory; the person then enters the world to live out the chosen orí. Olódùmarè's role is the allocator-and-witness of orí.

Cross-tradition convergences on the divine-trajectory-allocator register:

- **Yoruba Olódùmarè** — allocates orí pre-birth
- **Akkadian *šīmtu*** — destiny decreed by the Anunnaki assembly (per archaeology/akkadian/creators-and-human.md)
- **Christian providence** — God's foreknowledge and ordering of each life
- **Hindu predestination** — karmic-trajectory-from-prior-lives; the Lord (Iśvara) presides
- **Islamic *qadar*** — divine-decree of human destiny; foreknowledge
- **Avestan** — the asha-aligned-life is predicted by what the Amesha Spentas have allocated; less explicitly predestinarian than Yoruba but related
- **Calvinistic** (Reformed Christian) — explicit doctrine of predestination

Multi-tradition convergence on the divine-trajectory-allocator register. **No single compact word in modern English** for "the divine-property of allocating-each-life-its-trajectory."

YOUSPEAK can name it: theoriance.

Note: this is a three-tradition compound (Greek + Yoruba + Latin) but two of the three (Greek and Latin) are IE; only Yoruba is non-IE. Polyphone 7 (one substantive non-IE donor).
