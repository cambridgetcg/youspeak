## The Gap

Doxalgiame (Core 7.50 — glory-ache-as-received-ordinance) names the ache that divine beauty PRODUCES in the beholder: glory→ache. The glory arrives; the beholder aches. The ache is the signature of the beholding.

Algiadoxame names the reverse direction: ache→glory. The glory-quality that INHERES in the ache itself, regardless of source. Not the ache that glory produces (doxalgiame) but the glory that the ache already carries (algiadoxame). The same two morphemes — doxa and algos — in inverted compound-order. The Anastrophance-pair: two words sharing identical roots, each naming one direction of a bidirectional relationship.

The gap is real because English has no single word for the glory-quality-inhering-in-ache. "Sacred suffering" is a phrase, not a word. "Redemptive suffering" adds a soteriological framework that is not always present. "Spiritual dryness" is the absence, not the presence. "Consolation" in the Ignatian sense is close but carries the direction wrong — consolation is the relief AFTER suffering, not the glory WITHIN it. The specific gap: the dignity-weight that arrives WITH the ache, as gift, not after the ache as reward.

The -me deepening opens the ordinance-grade: the glory-in-ache is not earned through suffering (martyrological) nor produced by the suffering (therapeutic) but GIVEN WITH the suffering (ordinance). The ache arrives already carrying its glory. This is what the -me marks: the co-arrival is cosmic structure, not human discovery.

The -me is the structural specification the refinement path called for. The theological-framing is now differentiated: the glory that inheres is ordinance-grade — the same register that marks doxalgiame's ache as received, now marks algiadoxame's glory as received. The co-arrival is gift.

Structural ache — sustained, tissue-deep, distinguished from odynē (sharp acute pain) and ponos (toil-pain) by being sustained and structural. The root of the -algia morpheme alive in English: neuralgia, myalgia, nostalgia, cephalgia. In algiadoxame, the algos is the ache that CARRIES — the bearer, not the void. The ache that is not empty but already holds its content. The same algos that in doxalgiame is the ache that glory PRODUCES, here is the ache that PRODUCES glory — the anastrophance-inversion is in the direction: the ache is not the EFFECT of glory but the BEARER of glory.

The same doxa, registered from the opposite direction. The anastrophance-pair shares identical morphemes in inverted compound-order: doxa + algos (doxalgiame) vs algos + doxa (algiadoxame). The inversion is the theology: in doxalgiame, glory is the cause and ache is the effect; in algiadoxame, the ache is the bearer and glory is the content.

## The Cross-Tradition Convergence

Five traditions attest the glory-in-ache register:

1. **Teresa of Ávila — transverberation**: the seraph's lance pierces the heart; the piercing IS the glory. Teresa describes the ache as simultaneously the deepest suffering and the deepest joy — the wound that is the gift. The transverberation is the paradigmatic algiadoxame-event: the ache arrives already carrying its glory. The glory is not AFTER the piercing (consolation) but WITH the piercing (ordinance).

2. **Ignatius of Loyola — gift of tears**: the weeping that is not grief but sacred-gift. The tears carry a quality that ordinary tears do not — the dignity-weight of the ache-as-gift. Ignatius distinguishes consolation-tears (which bring peace) from desolation-tears (which do not); algiadoxame names the quality that the consolation-tears carry: the glory IN the weeping.

3. **Christian martyrology — suffering as glory-conferring**: "the blood of the martyrs is the seed of the church." The suffering is not merely endured but transforms — the ache carries a glory that ordinary suffering does not. The martyrological register is the most institutionalized form of algiadoxame: the church recognized that certain suffering carries glory and named it sacramentally.

4. **Buddhist — dukkha transformed in bodhi-facing**: the Fourth Noble Truth does not eliminate dukkha but transforms the relationship to it. In the bodhi-facing, the dukkha is not removed but is seen to carry its own liberation. The ache itself becomes the path. This is algiadoxame without theistic framing: the glory-in-ache as the structure of enlightenment, not the gift of a personal divine.

5. **Sufi — ḥuzn**: the sacred-grief that is not mere sorrow but a structural blessing. ḥuzn is the grief that draws the seeker closer; the ache is the nearness, not the distance. The Sufi register distinguishes ḥuzn (sacred-grief) from huzn (ordinary grief) by the glory-quality: ḥuzn carries it; ordinary huzn does not. The -me marks this distinction: the glory-in-ache is ordinance, not psychology.

Five traditions. The glory-in-ache is not a Christian-only phenomenon; it is cross-traditionally attested. The cathedral's POLYPHONE foundation requires this: no single tradition has a monopoly on naming what-is.
