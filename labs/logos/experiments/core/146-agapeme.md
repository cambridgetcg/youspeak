## The discovery-move

The love-of-DIVINE convergence after Session 023 had four forges from four traditions but lacked the Greek-Christian register — arguably the most theologically-developed articulation of love-of-DIVINE in history. The gap was double: archaeological (Greek archaeology covered divine-beauty but not divine-love) and forge-level (no agape-based word in YOUSPEAK).

Session 024 begins with archaeology: a full excavation of Greek agape (archaeology/greek/divine-love.md), from the verb/noun asymmetry (agapaō common from Homer; agapē near-absent until LXX) through the Septuagint crossing (ahavah rendered as agapē) to the NT's christological elevation.

The key archaeological finding: **agape's power comes from semantic emptiness strategically filled**. A near-blank Classical noun was elevated by the LXX and then loaded with the content of the cross by Paul and the Johannine authors. The trajectory: mild verb → marginal noun → the most theologically-loaded love-word in Western history.

Why agapeme now (rather than continuing to other convergences)?

1. **Thread momentum.** The love-of-DIVINE cluster is the active forge-thread (S020→S021→S023→S024). Continuing it is natural.
2. **The most significant absence.** Greek-Christian agape is the most theologically-developed love-of-DIVINE articulation. Its absence from the cathedral was felt.
3. **Archaeology prerequisite satisfied.** The Greek divine-love file was written this session, making the forge honest.

## The phenomenon

Agape names love that is simultaneously:

1. **Initiatory** — God moves first. "Herein is love, not that we loved God, but that He loved us" (1 John 4:10). The love originates in the divine nature, not in human striving.

2. **Unmotivated by the beloved's worth** — "God demonstrates His own agape toward us in that while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us" (Romans 5:8). Agape *creates* worthiness through the act of loving rather than responding to pre-existing worth. (This is Nygren's kernel that survives scholarly critique.)

3. **Sacrificial** — "Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his psychē for his friends" (John 15:13). It costs the lover everything. Culminates in kenosis: "He emptied himself, taking the form of a servant" (Phil 2:7).

4. **Creative** — It establishes value in the beloved through the act of loving. The beloved becomes worthy *because* loved, not loved because worthy.

5. **Ontological** — "God IS agape" (1 John 4:8). Not that God performs love but that God's very substance IS self-giving. The predicate nominative without article indicates essential nature.

No other tradition's love-word compactly names all five together:
- Ahavah does not isolate sacrificial-unmotivated-self-giving (broader covenant-love)
- Mahabbah requires human struggle and earning (opposite of unmotivated)
- Bhakti presumes mutual recognition and reciprocal longing (opposite of unilateral)
- Ìfẹ́ is relational warmth flowing naturally (opposite of costly sacrifice for the unworthy)
- Caritas softens the pure descent by adding human ascent (Augustine's synthesis)

## The forge

**Donors:**

- **Greek *agapē*** (ἀγάπη) — sacrificial-self-giving love; NT Christianity's central love-word. From verb agapaō "to greet with affection, prefer" (Homer onward). The noun was rare pre-LXX — a semantic vessel elevated by the Septuagint translators (who chose it by elimination: philia too narrow, eros too sexual, storgē too familial) and loaded with christological content by Paul and John. Etymology uncertain; possibly PIE *megh₂-peh₂-* "great protection" (Pinault 1991). Five NT registers: ontological ("God IS agape"), historical (John 3:16), practical (1 Cor 13), sacrificial (John 15:13), invincible (Rom 8:35-39).

- **Sumerian *me*** (𒈨) — divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality. Established YOUSPEAK morpheme; most-productive Core-canonical donor-morpheme.

**Compound:** *agapē* + *me* → **agapeme** (a-GA-pe-me; 4 syllables).

The compound names: **sacrificial-self-giving-love as received-ordinance**. The capacity to love sacrificially is itself received from the DIVINE — humans participate in agape by receiving-and-deploying what God gives. This is theologically precise: agape as human act is always participation in agape as divine nature. The -me suffix (received-ordinance) captures this: agapeme is not self-generated heroic sacrifice but received gift re-offered.

## Method-level observations

1. **Poly 7 ceiling confirmed.** Greek IE + Sumerian isolate = poly 7. The polyphone_balance axis caps at 7. Combined with even strong learnability and groundedness, poly 7 compounds max out around 7.40-7.45 Specialized.

2. **Learnability can vary within the same donor-morpheme.** The -me family entries range from 7 (shemme, doxomme, bhaktime) to 8 (agapeme). The non-me donor's cultural penetrance matters: agapē is more widely known than bhakti or šēmu.

3. **The love-of-DIVINE cluster now has five registers.** Each tradition's emphasis is genuinely distinctive. The cluster is YOUSPEAK's most-productive convergence — five forges across four sessions. Two remain (caritasme Latin, Confucian-related).

4. **Archaeology-before-forge pattern confirmed.** The Greek divine-love archaeology was necessary and productive. The verb/noun asymmetry finding (agape's power from semantic emptiness strategically filled) informed the forge's design.

5. **Semantic coverage constrained by the -me frame.** The Johannine "God IS agape" exceeds the received-ordinance frame — the claim is that agape is God's NATURE, not merely an ordinance God gives. Agapeme captures the received-ordinance dimension but is honest that 1 John 4:8 points beyond it. A future forge (agapesis? theagape?) might address the ontological register directly.
