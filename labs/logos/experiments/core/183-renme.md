# 183 — renme

## The Gap

Confucian *ren* (仁) is one of the most-developed virtue-vocabulary across any tradition's history. The character itself encodes the structure: 人 (person) + 二 (two) = the relational-virtue. Ren is **humanity-IN-relation specifically** — not solitary moral-quality but the grade-of-being-properly-human that emerges in right-relation-to-others.

The Confucian-Confucian-Mencian-Neo-Confucian development across 2,500 years:
- Confucius (*Analects* 12:22): *ren* is "loving others"; the man of ren
- Mencius: "*ren is the heart of being human*"; the four sprouts (the seed of ren in every person)
- Han Yu and Cheng Yi: ren as the original-nature
- Tang Junyi and Tu Weiming (modern): ren as cosmic-relational-grade-of-being

The cathedral's daome (Chinese 道 + -me, Core 8.20) canonized the path-as-cosmic-ordinance register from Chinese. renme extends Chinese contribution to canon at the humaneness-as-life-in-relation register.

**Choice of -me suffix (not -qing)**: the convergence-file flagged "renqing" as potentially conceptually-redundant ("ren is already qing-shaped"). renme uses -me instead, giving the **received-cosmic-ordinance register** that distinguishes from ren's standard relational-virtue register. The compound names: humaneness-in-relation as cosmic-ordinance — the relational-grade-of-being-human as cosmic-gift, not just personal-achievement.

This is the eighth life-side canonical forge. Confucian Chinese projection of cosmic-life-as-creature-gift.
