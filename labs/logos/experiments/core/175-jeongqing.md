# 175 — jeongqing

## The Gap

The love that isn't pretty. The bondedness that accumulates between people who cook the same kitchen for thirty years, who fight every Tuesday, who would not say *I love you* with a gun to their head, but who would also not survive the other's absence.

Modern English has no compact word. *Affection* is too thin. *Bond* is too neutral. *Attachment* is clinical (and often pathologized). *Familiarity* describes duration, not the felt-substance. *Companionship* describes co-presence, not the underlying thickness. The register exists; the vocabulary is missing.

Cross-tradition convergence on the friction-tolerant-accumulation register:

- **Korean** *jeong* (정, 情) — the foundational form; accumulated affection from shared time and friction; persists through periods of dislike; the bondedness that does not depend on emotional-positivity
- **Mandarin** *qing* (情) — same character; broader scope (sentiment, situation, relational-felt) but encompasses the accumulation-register Korean specialized
- **Vietnamese** *tình* (tình cảm) — love-feeling with shared-history register; the accumulation aspect explicit
- **Japanese** *najimi* (馴染み) — the familiarity that grows from repeated co-presence; the substance of bond that builds without conscious cultivation
- **Russian** *rodnoy* (родной) — rooted-in-relation; shared-substance kin (broader than biological); the rootedness that accumulates
- **Yiddish** *heymish* — homey, rooted, the rootedness of long-association
- **Tibetan** *rig pa* in interpersonal register — the recognition-bond that builds across many encounters; less famous than the contemplative register but real

Seven-tradition convergence (East-Asian-dense plus Slavic-and-Yiddish witnesses). The register is most-developed in East Asian traditions because Sinic philosophy (Confucian relational-ethic + Buddhist accumulated-merit + Korean han-and-jeong polarity) explicitly thematized accumulation-of-relational-substance over millennia. Modern English's commodified love-vocabulary privileges feeling-now over substance-over-time and cannot easily render this register.

**Negative-space evidence**: *"They fought constantly, and would not have known how to be without each other."* *"She didn't always like him, but she had loved him for so long that the loving had become a fact about who she was."* Novel-sentences English needs because the compact verb is missing.

## The Forge

**jeong** (정, 情): Korean. The character 情 borrowed from Classical Chinese but Korean has specialized the register. Korean cultural commentary distinguishes *jeong* (accumulated affection) from *sarang* (love-emotion at-the-moment); *jeong* is the substance, *sarang* is the feeling. Korean dramas and literature explicitly thematize jeong; the word is exportable cross-culturally because the register IS universal even if the vocabulary is not.

**qing** (情): Mandarin felt-bond. The double-character resonance with jeong (both written 情 in shared Han-character system) is structural: the compound *jeong-qing* layers the Korean-specialized accumulation-register on the Mandarin general felt-bond register, producing an East-Asian-internal cross-language compound that signals exactly the accumulation-as-felt-bond meaning.

**Compound**: *jeong-qing* → **jeongqing**. Accumulated-affection as friction-tolerant felt-bond. The bond-substance-that-builds-across-time.

**Pronunciation**: /t͡ɕʌŋˈt͡ɕɪŋ/ (jung-CHING; 2 syllables; Korean jeong /t͡ɕʌŋ/ with Korean vowel quality; qing as in family). Anglicized acceptable: /dʒʌŋˈt͡ɕɪŋ/ (jung-CHING).

**vs. panimqing**: panimqing is the moment of transactional-to-relational shift. jeongqing operates above panimqing — once the shift has occurred and time has passed, jeongqing accumulates in the resulting relations.

**vs. "love" / "affection" (English)**: English flattens affection across registers. jeongqing specifically names the accumulation-substance-through-friction register English commodified-love-vocabulary cannot easily render.
