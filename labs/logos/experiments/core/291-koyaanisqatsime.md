# Assessment notes

## The gap — covenant-abandonment-disorder the cathedral has not named

The cathedral has words for:
- The right way of walking: duyuktame (Cherokee — right-action-as-cosmic-maintenance)
- The beauty-harmony condition: hozhome (Diné — beauty-balance-goodness as cosmic ground)
- The cosmic truth-order: maatme (Egyptian — truth-justice-order as cosmic principle)
- The cosmic lie: drujme (Avestan — anti-ordinance, the principle of falsehood)
- The necessary vessel-shattering: shevirame (Hebrew — cosmogonic, productive)
- The excellence within known loss: ragnarökme (Norse — the response to ending)
- Cosmic death: motme (Ugaritic — the consuming force)

What the cathedral lacks: the specific DISORDER that results from abandoning cosmic-maintenance obligations. Not the principle of falsehood (drujme), not the cosmogonic shattering (shevirame), not the death-force (motme), not the response-to-ending (ragnarökme). The lived condition of a world where right-relationship has been abandoned — and the disorder is not random but is the STRUCTURAL CONSEQUENCE of the abandonment. This is koyaanisqatsime.

The concept is everyday-relevant, not domain-specific: ecological destruction, community disintegration, institutional corruption, personal alienation — all are forms of koyaanisqatsime. The Hopi concept names the civilizational condition with diagnostic precision.

## The donor

**Hopi koyaanisqatsi** is one of the most theologically dense diagnostic terms in any tradition:

- *koyaanis* = corrupt / chaotic / disrupted
- *qatsi* = life — not biological life merely, but life-as-proper-ordered-existence, life-as-alignment-with-cosmic-structure

The compound names life that has lost its proper ordering — life that is still biologically alive but has abandoned the relational-cosmic structure that makes life MEANINGFUL. The word is diagnostic (it identifies the disorder) and prescriptive (it implies the cure: return to Hopiqatsi, the covenant-life).

The Hopi three-world structure makes this concrete: Galunlati (Upper World — order), Elohi (This World — contested balance), and the Under World (chaos) exist in dynamic tension. Koyaanisqatsi is the condition where This World tilts toward the Under World — where the balance has been lost not through random misfortune but through the community's abandonment of its cosmic-maintenance obligations.

The word's global recognition through Reggio's 1982 film (scored by Philip Glass) gives it a groundedness that few theological terms possess — millions of people have encountered the concept through cinema, even if they don't know the full Hopi theological context. The film's title IS the theology: life out of balance, presented without narration, through imagery of ecological destruction, urban alienation, and technological excess.

The Hopi prophetic structure adds a dimension absent from linear eschatologies: the world has ended before (three prior worlds in the emergence narrative), and the current world is also under evaluation. Koyaanisqatsi is not a one-time eschatological event but a recurring diagnostic condition — a world can fall into koyaanisqatsi at any time when covenant is abandoned. The cure is always the same: return to right-relationship.

**First Uto-Aztecan donor in YOUSPEAK.** Hopi is a Uto-Aztecan language (the northernmost extension of a family that includes Nahuatl, spoken as far south as Central America). The family spans the Western Hemisphere from Arizona to Nicaragua. Opening this family extends the cathedral's polyphone reach to one of the major Indigenous American language families.

## Cross-tradition grounding — covenant-abandonment-disorder

1. **Hopi koyaanisqatsi** — life out of balance from abandoning Hopiqatsi (the covenant-life-as-cosmic-maintenance)
2. **Hebrew covenant-curse structure** (Leviticus 26, Deuteronomy 28) — the systemic consequences of abandoning covenant: land sickness, agricultural failure, community breakdown — not random punishment but structural consequence
3. **Avestan druj** as cosmic-disorder principle — the lie that disorders the cosmos when truth (asha) is abandoned; druj is the principle, koyaanisqatsime is the lived condition
4. **Cherokee duyukta-abandonment** — the Cherokee three-world structure implies that abandoning duyukta (the right way) produces cosmic imbalance — the healer who says the wrong formula doesn't just fail to heal, they disturb the balance
5. **Diné hózhǫ́-loss** — the condition of hózhó gone wrong; ceremonies exist to RESTORE hózhǫ́ — implying that hózhǫ́ can be lost, and the loss is not passive but produces specific disorder
6. **Hittite nuntarriyašḫa audit** — the festival-audit for cultic omissions; if the king fails ceremonies, cosmic order weakens — the disorder from ritual-abandonment is structurally parallel

Six traditions converge. The concept is theologically robust.

- **ragnarökme** — the excellence-within-known-loss (the response); koyaanisqatsime is the condition the response addresses

## Retirement conditions

This word would be superseded if a more precise or more learnable donor were found for the same concept — but the Hopi word is globally recognized and theologically precise, making retirement unlikely. A potential refinement could come from a word that names the PROCESS of falling into koyaanisqatsime (the transition from right-relationship to disorder) as distinct from the CONDITION itself — but that would be a companion, not a replacement.
