# Assessment notes

## The gap — the cathedral's genesis, 73 days waiting

This is the standing liturgy's FIRST word. The gap was named in
session-002 (2026-04-17): the ache-pattern in the beholder who recognizes
a beauty ordered-to-the-bottom. The word doxalgia was forged that day,
canonized 2026-04-23 in the old canon/ directory. It stood 73 days.

The v2 audit (2026-04-24) scored it 6.0 — below canon threshold. The
killer axis: polyphone_balance = 3 (both donors Greek, violating
Foundation 4 POLYPHONE). The word that started the cathedral never made
it into the living canon.

The -me deepening solves this:
1. **Polyphone**: Greek compound (doxa + algos) + Sumerian -me = cross-
   family bridge. Not as clean as a single-tradition + -me (the compound
   is intra-Greek), but the -me provides the Sumerian bridge, and the
   -algia morpheme is productive in English/medical vocabulary globally.
   Polyphone 3 → 6.5.
2. **Theological depth**: the -me marks the ache as received-ordinance.
   The original entry already gestured at this: "Doxalgia is not an
   emotion one chooses; it is the signature left in a beholder by an
   ordering too total to resist." The -me makes this structural: the ache
   is gift. The beholder does not produce it; it arrives with the
   beholding.

## The gap is still open

73 days of cathedral growth (157 words now) have not filled it:
- **penthosme** (Core 8.20) — sacramental mourning before holiness. Ache
  of sin/distance, not ache of beauty. The hesychast archaeology (line 66)
  explicitly distinguishes: "doxalgia is before-beauty; penthosme is
  before-holiness as recognition-of-distance."
- **shawqame** (Core 7.50) — anticipatory thirst BEFORE the encounter.
  Ache of absence. Doxalgiame is ache of PRESENCE.
- **darshanqing** (Core 7.80) — reciprocal sacred seeing as felt-bond.
  The seeing itself, not what the seeing produces in the beholder's body.
- **rasame** — aesthetic savoring. The savor, not the ache.
- **melamance** (Core 8.65) — visible radiance of divine presence. What
  IS seen. Doxalgiame is what the seeing DOES to the beholder.
- **doxomme** (Core 7.50) — glory-as-received-ordinance. The glory
  beheld. Doxalgiame is the ache the glory produces.
- **hiraethqing** — longing for a home that cannot be returned to. Ache
  of temporal absence. Doxalgiame is ache of what-is-present.

None is the sweet structural pressure inward at beholding beauty-ordered-
to-the-bottom. The gap stands.

## The donor

**doxa** (δόξα) — glory, manifested-weight. Via LXX of Hebrew kavod.
Already in the cathedral via doxomme. In doxalgiame, doxa is the glory
FROM THE BEHOLDER'S SIDE — not the glory-as-ordinance (doxomme) but the
glory-as-ache-producer. The same radiance, registered in the body.

**algos** (ἄλγος) — structural ache. Distinguished from odynē (sharp
pain) and ponos (toil-pain) by being sustained, structural, tissue-deep.
The -algia morpheme is productive in English: neuralgia, myalgia,
nostalgia, cephalgia. The morpheme carries the correct semantic weight
(sustained-structural, not sharp-acute). In doxalgiame, the algos is not
somatic tissue but the beholder's ordering-as-such.

**me** (𒈨) — divine-ordinance. The 30th -me family member (after
eucharistime, the 29th). The -me marks the ache as received: the capacity
to feel the ache of divine beauty is gift. The recursion: to feel
doxalgiame is to receive the capacity to feel it.

## The liturgical triad — the cathedral's founding structure

The original liturgy triad (session-002):
- **doxakallos** — the beauty-in-GoD (object-pole; what IS in GoD)
- **orthophanes** — the rightness-made-visible (appearance-pole; what
  appears)
- **doxalgia/doxalgiame** — the ache-in-the-beholder (subject-pole; what
  the beholding does to the one who beholds)

Doxalgiame is the subject-pole — the worshipper's side of the encounter.
The triad names the complete structure of worship: what is in GoD
(doxakallos), what appears (orthophanes), what happens in the beholder
(doxalgiame). The subject-pole has been absent from the living canon for
73 days. This forge brings it home.

## The liturgical arc — the affective-pole

The liturgical arc (the worshipper's journey through the encounter):
- **shawqame** (thirst that initiates — before the encounter)
- **melamance** (radiance appears — the encounter begins)
- **doxalgiame** (ache in the beholder — the encounter LANDS IN THE BODY)
- **darshanqing** (reciprocal seeing — the encounter becomes mutual)
- **myesime** (threshold-crossing — the encounter changes who you are)
- **andemame** (silence of the withdrawn — the encounter passes into silence)

Doxalgiame is the affective-pole — the moment the encounter becomes FELT
in the beholder's body. Between the radiance appearing (melamance) and
the mutual seeing (darshanqing), the ache arrives. This is the
worshipper's first bodily registration of divine beauty. The liturgical
arc now has its affective-register.
