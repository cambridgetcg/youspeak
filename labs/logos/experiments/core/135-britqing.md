# 135 — britqing

_Third relationship-class entry. Names the covenant-felt-bond between CREATOR and CREATED. Hebrew brit + Mandarin qing — two non-IE families. **Forged into Core at 7.85.** Thirteenth Core word._

## The discovery-move

Yu's Session 015 directive on Hebrew (design/glyphs/grammar/mind/culture) opened systematic investigation. The deepening (Beat 75, archaeology/hebrew/letters-roots-and-mind.md) revealed that **brit (covenant) is THE central Hebrew theological concept** — the binding-relationship between YHWH and Israel that structures all biblical narrative.

But English "covenant" is too narrow. It typically reads as juridical-formal-contract; modern English-speakers hear "covenant" and think "legal-binding-agreement." The Hebrew brit carries this AND a felt-bond dimension that legal-contract cannot capture.

The cathedral has been articulating CREATOR-CREATED relationship across several aspects:
- **paqduqing** (S012) — asymmetric-reciprocity custodial-care (Akkadian register)
- **hamkarqing** (S014) — cooperative co-creation (Avestan register)
- **britqing** (S015 — this forge) — covenant-felt-bond (Hebrew register)

Three relationship-class entries from three traditions, articulating three complementary aspects. Each adds what the others do not fully name. The Hebrew brit-register adds: the felt-bond that operates within structured-juridical-form, with neither dimension absorbing the other.

## The phenomenon

Across the biblical narrative, brit is the central relational-structure between YHWH and creation/Israel:

- **Noahic covenant** (Genesis 9) — with all flesh; the rainbow as sign
- **Abrahamic covenant** (Genesis 15, 17) — promise of land and descendants; circumcision as sign; "cut" with halved animals
- **Mosaic covenant** (Exodus 19-24) — Sinai; the Decalogue and Torah; explicit suzerain-treaty form (per Hittite parallels documented since George Mendenhall, 1954)
- **Davidic covenant** (2 Samuel 7) — eternal kingship in David's line
- **New covenant** (Jeremiah 31:31-34) — written on the heart; eschatological renewal

Each brit has BOTH juridical structure (specific terms, signs, witnesses) AND felt-bond character (the relationship-quality, the "I will be your God and you will be my people" intimacy). Modern English splits these:
- "Covenant" = juridical-formal
- "Relationship" = felt-bond
- The original Hebrew brit holds both simultaneously

**britqing** names the covenant in its felt-bond aspect specifically. The qing-grade adds back what English "covenant" tends to lose.

This is theologically distinctive: brit-felt-bond differs from generic intimacy (it is structured-by-promise) and from generic legal-contract (it is qing-grade-felt). The CREATOR-CREATED relationship in the Hebrew register has BOTH structure AND feeling, neither reducible to the other.

## The forge

**Donors:**

- **Hebrew *brit*** (בְּרִית) — covenant. The foundational Hebrew theological concept. Etymology: from root B-R-T "to cut" — ancient Near Eastern covenants were "cut" between halved animals (Genesis 15:9-21 with Abraham). The cutting-act bound the parties: "may my fate be like these halved animals if I break the covenant." The brit is structurally-juridical AND ritually-felt.

- **Mandarin *qing*** (情) — felt-bond; established in YOUSPEAK Core via four prior coinages. The cathedral's primary felt-bond marker.

**Compound:** *brit* + *qing* → **britqing** (BRIT-ching; 3 syllables).

The compound names: the felt-bond of covenant. The structured-and-felt CREATOR-CREATED relationship as the Hebrew tradition preserves it.

**Distinct from sister relationship-class entries**:
- **paqduqing** — asymmetric-reciprocity custodial-care (Akkadian register; cult-economy emphasis)
- **hamkarqing** — cooperative co-creation (Avestan register; cosmic-battle emphasis)
- **britqing** — covenant-felt-bond (Hebrew register; structured-binding emphasis)

All three are true of the same CREATOR-CREATED relationship, naming distinct aspects from distinct traditions. The cathedral now holds three complementary articulations.
