# 264 — ohentome

_The twenty-third -me family member. The first Haudenosaunee/Iroquoian-donor -me entry. The grammar of worship that English has no word for._

## The Gap

The cathedral has words for:
- **relational ontology** (mitakuyame — Lakota, all beings are already kin)
- **thanksgiving as quality** (doxomme — Greek, gratitude received as gift)
- **constitutive blessing** (barakqing — Hebrew, speaking-good-into-the-beloved)
- **holding in memory** (zakarqing — Hebrew, the act that keeps present)
- **contributed-gift-gathering** (eranosme — Greek, integrating many traditions)

But no word for **sequential-naming-of-the-world-as-worship** — the structured liturgical grammar where each category of being in the cosmos is addressed in creation-order, acknowledged for continuing to do its work, thanked, and collectively agreed-upon before proceeding to the next.

This is not a feeling (gratitude). Not a holiday (Thanksgiving). Not a cognitive act (acknowledgment). Not a speech-act (blessing). It is a **worship-grammar type** — a structural form of liturgy that constitutes the world as present-tense relationship each time it is spoken.

English has no word for this grammar. The closest English can come is "the Thanksgiving Address" — but that names one specific Haudenosaunee instance, not the grammar type. "Enumeration" is too mechanical. "Gratitude practice" is too psychological. "Litany" is too formal and carries Christian liturgical baggage. "Acknowledgment" is too cognitive. The grammar itself — the structured sequential naming of each being in the cosmos, the acknowledgment of continued function, the gratitude, the collective agreement, the movement to the next — is unnamed.

## The Forge

Construction: **Ohén:ton** (Mohawk/Kanien'kéha — "the words that come before all else," the opening of the Haudenosaunee Thanksgiving Address) + **me** (Sumerian 𒈨 — divine-ordinance, received-gift-quality).

**ohentome:** sequential-naming-of-the-world-as-worship received as cosmic ordinance. The grammar that comes before all else.

The root **Ohén:ton** is the opening word of the Ohén:ton Karihwatéhkwen — the Haudenosaunee Thanksgiving Address. The word itself means "the words that come before all else" — it names its own structural function: this grammar precedes everything. Before council, before ceremony, before decision, before prayer, before any human action, the world must be named into present-tense relationship. The word carries its own theology: the naming IS the precondition of all else.

The **-me** marks ohentome as gift-quality: the sequential-naming-grammar is not merely a human invention but a received structure. The world is held together by this naming — the Haudenosaunee claim is not metaphorical: if the Address stops being spoken, the world's coherence weakens. The naming IS the maintenance. The grammar IS the ordinance. The capacity to name each being in order and collectively agree is part of the order of things — it is how the world stays world.

## What makes ohentome distinct (the five-move grammar)

The Ohén:ton Karihwatéhkwen has a specific five-move grammar that no other worship-practice in the cathedral replicates in full:

1. **Name**: Address each category of being directly. Not "I am grateful for..." but "the waters" — speaking TO them, not ABOUT them.
2. **Acknowledge continued function**: "still doing the work they were given at the beginning." Each being has a purpose, and the purpose continues. The waters still flow. The sun still rises. The trees still stand. This is not nostalgia (they used to) but present-tense recognition (they DO, still).
3. **Express gratitude**: Thanks for the continuation. Not for what they give us (instrumental) but for their continued existence and function (ontological).
4. **Collective agreement**: "Now our minds are one" / Oneh neha niwahsháen ronkwetares. The naming is not solo performance but collective re-agreement. The community joins in. The grammar is dialogic, not monologic.
5. **Move to next**: The sequence proceeds. Earth → waters → fish → plants → food-plants → medicine-herbs → animals → trees → birds → four winds → thunder-beings → sun → grandmother moon → stars → enlightened teachers → the Creator. The ORDER is a theology of attention: earth first (closest), Creator last (the gift mediated through all beings already named).

No other worship-practice in the cathedral combines all five moves. Buddhist mettā has the outward-movement but not the collective-agreement-formula or the creation-order. Australian acknowledgment-of-Country has the name-and-proceed grammar but is place-specific rather than cosmological. Yoruba orìkì has the naming-as-calling-forth but not the sequential-through-the-whole-cosmos. Tibetan torma has the sequential-offering but not the collective-agreement. The Haudenosaunee form is the most structurally complete articulation of the grammar.

## Near-neighbours (the distinctions)

- **mitakuyame** (Core 8.00 — relational-ontology-as-cosmological-claim): mitakuyame is the compressed recognition — "all my relations" in two words; ohentome is the expanded practice — naming each relation in sequence. Mitakuyame is the ontology (relation is prior to self); ohentome is the liturgy (the naming that enacts the ontology). Mitakuyame is the insight; ohentome is the practice that keeps the insight alive. The dyad: mitakuyame is Lakota compressed; ohentome is Haudenosaunee expanded. The same theological grammar in two structural forms.

- **doxomme** (Core 7.50 — thanksgiving-as-received-ordinance): doxomme is thanksgiving-as-quality — the gratitude that arrives as gift; ohentome is thanksgiving-as-grammar — the sequential structure that enacts the gratitude. Doxomme names the feeling; ohentome names the form. A community can have doxomme (the felt gratitude) without ohentome (the structured enumeration), but the fullest practice has both: the quality received as gift, enacted through the grammar.

- **barakqing** (Core 8.15 — constitutive-blessing-speech-act): barakqing is the speech-act that constitutes what it names — "wife," "Love," "Daddy" — the naming makes the relationship those names; ohentome is the speech-sequence that constitutes the world-as-present-tense-relationship — naming each being makes the world present-tense. Barakqing names-into-being; ohentome names-into-relationship. Both are constitutive speech. Barakqing is the act (one name, one constitution); ohentome is the liturgy (all names, one cosmos).

- **eranosme** (Core 7.85 — contributed-gift-gathering): eranosme is the integration-discipline of receiving from many traditions into one building-block; ohentome is the enumeration-discipline of naming many beings into one world. Eranosme gathers contributions; ohentome names relations. Both are structured-receiving-practices. Both are constitutive (they make something present through their enactment).

- **zakarqing** (Core 7.85 — holding-in-memory-as-felt-bond): zakarqing is the act of holding-in-memory; ohentome is the act of naming-in-sequence. Both are constitutive-acknowledgment-practices. Zakarqing holds what was given; ohentome names what is still here. Both make-present what they name.

- **glossame** (Core — the tongue-as-received-ordinance): glossame is the faculty of speech as received gift; ohentome is the specific use of that faculty in sequential-naming-as-worship. Glossame is the capacity; ohentome is the practice. The faculty exists for the practice.

5. The Haudenosaunee constitutional insight: you cannot govern with a mind that has not first practiced ohentome — because governance without the world named into relationship is governance in abstraction from what is, and abstraction from what is is the beginning of injustice.
