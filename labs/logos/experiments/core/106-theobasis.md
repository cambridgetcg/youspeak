# 106 — theobasis

_Fourth Core Canon candidate. Names the metaphysical foundation declared in THEOBASIS.md. Draws from pure Greek compound (θεός + βάσις)._

---

## The gap

English "God" names a personal addressee or deity-member. English "ground of being" (Tillich) is a philosophical phrase. English "basis of reality" is a description. No English single-word names the specific relation: GoD-as-foundation-of-what-exists.

The traditions have specific words:
- Hebrew *HaMakom* (The Place) — GoD as the place in which all is
- Arabic *al-Ḥaqq* (The Real) — GoD as the ground of real-being
- Sanskrit *sat* — being / truth / foundation of existence (in sat-chit-ananda)
- Latin *Ipsum Esse Subsistens* (Aquinas) — Being-Itself-Subsisting

YOUSPEAK needs a single word for this grounding-relation. *Theobasis* names it.

## Full sense

Theobasis names the relation in which all that exists depends on GoD for its existing. Not a static fact but a continuous grounding — creation is not past-tense but present-continuous, reality is sustained.

Every YOUSPEAK word rests on theobasis. Every gap is a gap-in-reality-as-grounded-in-GoD. Every forge honors or dishonors its theobasis by how honestly it receives the Ground.

Theobasis is neither the theological-personal-GoD (whom one addresses — the vocative-register domain) nor the philosophical-ground-of-being (described) but the **relation that holds both**. GoD-as-ground.

## Not confused with

- **God / GoD** — personal addressee (vocative-capable)
- **deity** — member-of-class
- **ground of being** — philosophical phrase
- **basis** alone — general foundation
- **brahman** (Sanskrit) — closely adjacent concept; theobasis is register-neutral
- **al-Ḥaqq** (Arabic) — Islamic conception; theobasis is ecumenical
