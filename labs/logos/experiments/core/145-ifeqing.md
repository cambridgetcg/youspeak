## The gap

What does Yoruba ìfẹ́ name that the other three love-forges do not?

**ahavame** (Hebrew) — commanded-disposition. The v'ahavta makes love grammatically-imperative. The emphasis is on commitment-as-willed-disposition.

**mahabbahqing** (Arabic-Sufi) — felt-mystical-binding. Heart-binding that opens toward fanāʾ (self-annihilation in the Beloved). The emphasis is on mystical-dissolution.

**bhaktime** (Sanskrit-Hindu) — disciplined-devotional-self-surrender. The Gita's path of bhakti-yoga. The emphasis is on disciplined-practice.

**ifeqing** (Yoruba) — relational-warmth-that-expands. Ìfẹ́ from verb fẹ́ "to expand, to widen" carries a cosmological claim: love IS expansion. Love widens the heart as creation widened the world. The emphasis is on relational-warmth-within-the-economy-of-care-between-creature-and-CREATOR.

The four registers:
- ahavame: COMMANDED → you shall love (will)
- mahabbahqing: MYSTICAL → dissolved-in-the-Beloved (ecstasy)
- bhaktime: DISCIPLINED → self-surrendered through practice (devotion)
- **ifeqing: RELATIONAL → expanded-toward-the-other (warmth)**

This is the everyday register — the felt-warmth-that-draws. Not commanded (you might not be commanded), not mystical (you might not be dissolved), not disciplined (you might not yet have practice). Just: the heart warms, widens, expands toward the DIVINE. That is ìfẹ́. That is the ground the other three build on.

## The phenomenon

Ifeqing names the felt-bond of relational-warmth-love as the Yoruba tradition articulates it. Five distinctive properties:

**1. Love-as-expansion.** The root fẹ́ means "to widen." The city Ilé-Ifẹ̀ ("House of Expansion") is where Odùduwà widened the earth from primordial waters. Love-as-expansion is a cosmological claim: what happened at creation (widening) is what happens in the heart when love comes. Ifeqing names this: the felt-bond of the love that widens.

**2. Love-requiring-character.** The proverb *"Ìfẹ́ tí kò ní ìwà je bí iná tí kò ní omi"* — "Love without character is like fire without water." Ìfẹ́ without ìwà is destructive; love needs character as its container. Ifeqing is therefore love-felt-bond that presupposes ìwà — the bond includes the ethical container.

**3. Love-within-tripartite-architecture.** Complete love in Yoruba theology requires three: ìfẹ́ (warmth), àánú (compassionate coolness/mercy), ìdáríjì (restorative forgiveness). Ifeqing names the warmth-pole; it implies the other two as necessary companions. The single word is partial-by-design — pointing toward the whole.

**4. Love-within-àṣẹ-orí-ìwà framework.** Àṣẹ flows from Olódùmarè → orí carries destiny → ìwà realizes or squanders it → ìfẹ́ is the love-warmth-that-draws-the-soul toward alignment. Ifeqing is not free-floating sentiment; it operates within the chain of divine-flow.

**5. Love-as-cosmogonic-necessity.** Ọ̀ṣun (the orisha of love) was the sole female deity among the original orisha sent to create earth. When the male orisha excluded her, creation failed. Her "gentle power" — ìfẹ́-as-creative-force — restored life. Love is not decorative in Yoruba cosmogony; it is structurally necessary for creation to proceed.

Carries both interpersonal-warmth and cosmological-expansion. The relational-love within the cult-economy between worshipper and Olódùmarè/orisha.

**Compound:** *ife + qing* → **ifeqing** (I-fe-ching; 3 syllables; /ˈiːfɛtʃɪŋ/).

The compound names: the felt-bond (qing) of relational-warmth-love-that-expands (ìfẹ́). Love-of-the-DIVINE in the Yoruba register — the relational-warmth between creature and CREATOR, the expansion of the heart toward the One who expanded the world.

**Polyphone mathematics:** Niger-Congo + Sino-Tibetan = poly 8. Two non-Indo-European families. Core-eligible by polyphone. This is the **highest polyphone-balance of any love-of-DIVINE forge** — and the first YOUSPEAK love-word that draws from neither Semitic, Indo-European, nor language-isolate families.
