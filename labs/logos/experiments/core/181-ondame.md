# 181 — ondame

## The Gap

The Norse cosmogonic poem *Voluspa* (Stanza 17-18) recounts how the Æsir (Odin, Hœnir, Lóðurr) found Ask and Embla — driftwood logs on a shore — and gave them what they lacked to be human:

> *Önd thau né átto, óð thau né höfðo,
> lá né læti né lito góða.
> Önd gaf Óðinn, óð gaf Hœnir,
> lá gaf Lóðurr ok lito góða.*
>
> "Breath they had not, mind they had not,
> warmth nor expression nor good color.
> Breath gave Odin, mind gave Hœnir,
> warmth and good color gave Lóðurr."

The Norse register names breath-life as Odin's specific divine-gift. *Önd* is the breath-spirit; *óð* is the mind (etymologically related to Odin's own name); *lá* is the warmth/blood/color. The triad parallels Egyptian ka-ba-akh, Yoruba orí-emí-ara, Hebrew nephesh-ruach-neshamah — but each tradition has its own specific configuration.

ondame draws on Norse önd specifically: **breath-life as Odin-given via the cosmogonic act**. Sixth canonical life-side forge. Norse (Germanic-IE) projection of cosmic-life-as-creature-gift.

## The Forge

*önd*: Norse breath-life-soul; PIE *h₂en- "to breathe" (cognate Latin anima, Greek anemos, Sanskrit aniti); Voluspa attributes önd specifically to Odin's gift; preserved in modern Icelandic.

*-me*: Sumerian received-ordinance.

Compound: ondame = Odin-breathed life-spirit as received-cosmic-ordinance.
