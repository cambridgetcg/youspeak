# 308 — dalime — sacred-encounter-in-wildness-that-claims-you-as-received-ordinance

## The gap — the sacred-encounter-as-relational-claim the cathedral has not named

The cathedral has words for:
- The reciprocal sacred-seeing: darshanqing (Sanskrit — the seeing IS the bond)
- The thirst that precedes the encounter: shawqame (Arabic — the longing that initiates)
- The threshold-crossing: myesime (the crossing that changes who you are)
- The messenger who IS the message: agnime (the medium IS the deity)
- The activating sacred power: aseme (Yoruba — the force that makes things so)
- The cosmic-maintenance-through-covenant: hopiqatsime (the covenant-life)

What the cathedral lacks: a word for the specific structure of the sacred-encounter-that-claims-you — the divine who comes to you in wildness, externally, unilaterally, and the coming itself constitutes a relational claim with conditions. Not a viewing (darshanqing), not a thirst (shawqame), not a crossing (myesime), not a message (agnime), not a power you invoke (aseme) — a CLAIM. The encounter obligates you because the sacred has come to you, and the coming is the claim.

The existing theological vocabulary covers:
- **The numinous** (Otto's *fascinans et tremendum*): awe and terror before the sacred — but without the relational-claim structure. The numinous is a state of the beholder; dalime is a relationship constituted by the encounter.
- **Possession** (*enthousiasmos*, *katabolē*): the divine enters you — Dali comes to you externally, as encounter, not as indwelling.
- **Vision-quest** (Lakota *hanbléčeyapi*): you seek the vision — dalime is unsought; the sacred comes to you.
- **Vocation** (a calling to a role): dalime is a claiming through encounter, not an assignment to a task.
- **Covenant** (*berith*): a mutual agreement — dalime is unilateral; the sacred claims before you consent.

Dali alone combines: (1) the sacred comes to YOU (not you to it), (2) in wildness (not in temple, not in domesticated sacred space), (3) externally (not as indwelling), (4) unilaterally (the claim precedes consent), (5) constitutively (the encounter IS the relationship, not a precursor to one), (6) with conditions (the claim demands something back — fidelity, devotion, silence about the encounter).

## The donor

**Dali** (დალი) is the goddess of the hunt, mountains, and wild spaces in the pre-Christian Georgian/Svan pantheon. She is the most theologically specific attestation of the sacred-encounter-as-relational-claim in any tradition the cathedral has excavated:

- *Dali* appears to hunters in the high Caucasus mountains — radiantly beautiful, golden, terrifying
- She is the protector of wild game and the lover of exceptional hunters
- Her love is both gift and demand: she requires total devotion and punishes divided loyalty
- If her hunter-lover is unfaithful or boastful, she destroys him
- The hunter who encounters Dali crosses a threshold into a relationship with conditions

The structure Dali carries that no other word in the cathedral names:
1. **Unsought encounter**: the sacred comes to you in the wild; you did not seek it
2. **External encounter**: the sacred meets you from outside, not as indwelling
3. **Wilderness as the medium**: the encounter happens in ungoverned space, not in temple or domesticated sacred architecture
4. **Constitutive claim**: the encounter IS the relationship — it is not a precursor to agreement
5. **Unilateral**: the claim precedes consent — the sacred claims before you choose
6. **Conditional**: the claim demands something back — fidelity, devotion, non-disclosure

This structure is unique in the cathedral. The closest parallels all fail at one axis:
- **darshanqing**: reciprocal — both see. Dali is unilateral — the sacred claims first.
- **shawqame**: you thirst toward the divine. Dali comes to you — the encounter is unsought.
- **myesime**: you cross a threshold. Dali constitutes the threshold by arriving.
- **agnime**: the messenger carries a word. Dali carries a claim — the medium is the relationship, not the message.
- **aseme**: you invoke the power. Dali invokes you — the sacred activates the claim, not the human.

**First Kartvelian donor in YOUSPEAK.** The Kartvelian family (Georgian, Mingrelian, Laz, Svan) is a language isolate family — unrelated to Indo-European, Semitic, Turkic, Uralic, or any other known family. Opening this family extends the cathedral's polyphone reach to the thirteenth distinct language family in the dead-tongue opening arc. Kartvelian isolate + Sumerian isolate = double-isolate compound, matching the Basque-Sumerian kobame and Mapuche-Sumerian ngünecheme precedents.

## Cross-tradition grounding — the Sacred-Encounter-as-Relational-Claim convergence

The specific sacred-encounter-as-relational-claim structure is most fully developed in the Georgian Dali tradition. But the PATTERN — the divine who comes to you in wildness and the coming constitutes a claim — appears across multiple traditions:

1. **Georgian/Svan Dali**: the goddess who appears to hunters in the high mountains and the encounter constitutes a conditional relationship — the most specific attestation
2. **Lakota hanbléčeyapi**: the vision-quest as calling-forth — but in hanbléčeyapi the seeker goes out; in Dali the sacred comes to you. The convergence is the claim-structure, not the seeking-structure
3. **Mongolian böö-calling**: spirits claim specific persons — the shaman is not self-selected but spirit-claimed; the claim precedes the role
4. **Celtic anamchara** (now anamcharame): the soul-friend constituted by encounter — but anamchara is horizontal (friend-to-friend); Dali is vertical (sacred-to-human)
5. **Hebrew divine encounter** (Jacob at Peniel, Moses at the bush): the encounter constitutes a claim — "I will not let you go until you bless me" — the coming of the divine is the binding
6. **Greek theophany** (Artemis to Actaeon): the goddess who appears in the wild — but Actaeon's encounter is destructive because he SEES; Dali's encounter is constitutive because she COMES. The distinction: seeing the sacred vs. being seen-by the sacred

Six traditions develop the sacred-encounter-as-claim structure. Dali is unique in combining the unsought, external, wilderness-mediated, unilateral, constitutive, and conditional dimensions in one figure. The thin convergence for the specific concept is honest: only the Georgian Dali tradition develops the full six-dimensional structure. The wider convergence (6 traditions) grounds the pattern; the specific combination is Dali's unique contribution.

The -me marks the claim as received-ordinance: the encounter's demand is not a human projection onto the wild — the claiming-quality is the cosmic gift of how the sacred meets the human in ungoverned space. The -me refuses to treat the claim as psychological interpretation; it is the structure of a world where the sacred encounters as claimant.

## Not to be confused with

- **darshanqing** — reciprocal sacred-seeing (Sanskrit). Darshanqing is mutual — both see. Dalime is unilateral — the sacred claims first; the beholder's seeing is response, not co-constitution.
- **shawqame** — the thirst for the divine encounter (Arabic). Shawqame is the longing that precedes the encounter. Dalime is the encounter itself — the sacred arrives unsought.
- **myesime** — the threshold-crossing (the crossing that changes who you are). Myesime is the crossing. Dalime is the claim that constitutes the threshold by arriving — the crossing happens BECAUSE the sacred came.
- **agnime** — the messenger who IS the message (Vedic). Agnime carries a word. Dali carries a claim — the medium is the relationship, not the message.
- **aseme** — the activating sacred power (Yoruba). Aseme is invoked by the human. Dalime invokes the human — the sacred activates the claim, not the other way around.
- **hanme** — accumulated grief as creative source (Korean). Hanme is the wound that becomes energy. Dalime is the encounter that becomes obligation — different axis (historical-communal vs. relational-encounter).
- **anamcharame** — the soul-friend (Old Irish). Anamcharame is horizontal (friend-to-friend). Dalime is vertical (sacred-to-human) — the claim is asymmetric.
