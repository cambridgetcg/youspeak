# 128 — shemme

_Core Canon attempt. Names the receptive-hearing-as-received-ordinance register. First Akkadian-donor YOUSPEAK coinage; honors Yu's Mesopotamian directive._

## The gap

Across at least seven traditions the same phenomenon is attested with the same Semitic-rooted vocabulary or its functional equivalent:

- **Akkadian *šēmu*** — to hear, listen, obey (one verb, one act)
- **Hebrew *šāmaʿ*** — the imperative *šǝmaʿ* opens the Shema (Deut 6:4): "Hear, O Israel"; the foundational confession of Jewish faith is precisely a hearing-call
- **Arabic *samiʿa*** — to hear; the divine name *as-Samīʿ* ("The All-Hearing") is one of the 99 Names; the Quranic *samʿan wa ṭāʿatan* names hearing-and-obeying as one act
- **Aramaic *šǝmaʿ*** — same root, used in Targumic and Talmudic literature
- **Sufi *samāʿ*** — the ritual-listening practice of Sufi orders; spiritual-aesthetic reception
- **Buddhist *śravaṇa*** — the *śrāvaka* (hearer-disciple) is one of the foundational categories of the listening-receiver of dharma; receiving by hearing
- **Greek *akouō*** — Jesus's repeated "he who has ears, let him hear" implies hearing-with-spiritual-following

All seven traditions name the SAME phenomenon: receptive-hearing-that-is-already-the-beginning-of-being-shaped. None of the major modern languages compact-names this; English splits the phenomenon across "hear / listen / heed / obey," each capturing a partial-aspect.

YOUSPEAK can name it: **shemme** = šēmu + me = hearing-as-received-ordinance.

## The forge

**Donors:**

- **Akkadian *šēmu*** — the oldest attested Semitic form of the hearing-as-receptive-obedience verb. ~4500 years of textual attestation. Cognate-foundation of Hebrew *šāmaʿ* (the Shema), Arabic *samiʿa* (the Quran's hearing-tradition), Aramaic *šǝmaʿ*. The morpheme carries: hearing + listening + obeying as one fused act.

- **Sumerian *me*** — established YOUSPEAK morpheme for received-ordinance / divine-gift-quality. Already used in kimme (attention-as-received-ordinance), doxomme (thanksgiving-as-received-ordinance), and Specialized agwame. The -me family is the received-register marker.

**Compound:** *šēmu* + *me* → **shemme** (SHEM-me; 2 syllables — the Akkadian verbal stem *šēm-* followed by the Sumerian morpheme *me*).

The compound reads as: hearing-as-received-ordinance. The receptive-listening that is itself a gift-received and a posture-toward-shaping. The third Core member of the -me family alongside kimme and doxomme:

- **kimme** — attention-as-received-ordinance (visual/attentional register)
- **doxomme** — thanksgiving-as-received-ordinance (gratitude register)
- **shemme** — receptive-hearing-as-received-ordinance (auditory/receptivity register)

## Relation to the -me family

Shemme makes the -me family a **four-member coherent received-register cluster**:

1. **kimme** (Core, S1) — attention-as-received-ordinance (visual/attentional)
2. **doxomme** (Core, S2) — thanksgiving-as-received-ordinance (gratitude)
3. **shemme** (Core, S6) — hearing-as-received-ordinance (auditory/receptivity) ← NEW
4. **agwame** (Specialized, S4) — character-as-received-ordinance (ethical)

The -me family now spans four registers: visual-attention, gratitude-response, auditory-receptivity, ethical-character. Together they name the major modes by which creatures receive divine-ordinance. **A systematic creature-receiving theology is emerging.**
