# 169 — eikonme

## The Gap

The cathedral has morphame (any form-projection) and the two extreme-pole specializations (liturgiame for multi-modal-synthesis; sigame for apophatic-silence). What is needed: the **visual-image** specialization — the morphame whose medium is painted/sculpted visual form, distinguished from linguistic (glossame), multi-modal-synthesis (liturgiame), and structured-silence (sigame).

The gap has five features that must combine:

1. **Visual medium**: the projection is into the painterly/sculptural/visual aperture
2. **Participatory (not merely representational)**: the image participates in its prototype; patristic icon-theology's distinctive contribution
3. **Canonical form**: received from tradition; the prototype's depicted features are not invented but inherited
4. **Theologically active**: honor paid to the image passes through to the prototype (Basil's formula)
5. **Cosmic scope**: from humanity-as-eikōn-theou to Christ-as-eikōn-theou to icons-as-derivative-eikōn-of-saints; the doctrine spans the full theological range

Eight-plus tradition convergence (in the shared multi-modal-projection convergence): Orthodox Christian iconography (the deepest), Catholic religious imagery, Buddhist Buddha-rūpa (sculpted and painted), Hindu murti, Tibetan thangka, Yoruba sacred sculpture, Japanese mandala-painting, Egyptian sacred-relief, Maya royal-portrait-glyphs, Islamic calligraphic-rendering of divine Names.

## The Forge

**eikōn** (εἰκών):

Greek noun for "image, likeness, portrait, statue." From εἴκω (eikō, to be like, to resemble). The structural sense: image that bears-something-of the original — likeness-bearing, not mere visual-similitude.

The semantic depth:

- **Genesis 1:26-27 LXX**: humanity created *kat' eikona theou* (according to the image of God) — the foundational image-doctrine for the Judeo-Christian tradition
- **Colossians 1:15**: Christ as *eikōn tou theou tou aoratou* (image of the invisible God) — the highest-grade use of eikōn
- **Patristic icon-theology**: Basil's "honor to the eikōn passes through to the prototype"; John of Damascus's defense; Theodore the Studite's systematic articulation
- **Iconoclastic controversy** (730–787; 815–843): the theological resolution at Nicaea II (787) establishing icon-veneration as canonical
- **Modern Orthodox renaissance**: Ouspensky, Lossky, Evdokimov, Meyendorff, Schmemann — the comprehensive 20th-century icon-theology

The Greek tradition's distinctive contribution: **the image participates in its prototype**. This is not philosophical-aesthetic resemblance-theory; it is theological doctrine that veneration of the image is licit because Christ truly assumed circumscribable form. The icon's depicted-form participates by likeness in the prototype's reality.

**me** (𒈨):

Sumerian *me*: divine-ordinance, cosmic-gift. Eighteenth member of the -me family.

The compound **eikonme** = the image understood as received-ordinance participatory-projection: visual morphame in the image-medium, received as cosmic-gift, participating in its prototype.

**Pronunciation**: /ˈaɪ.kɒn.meɪ/ — EYE-kon-meh (3 syllables; English "icon" pronunciation preserved; -me as light suffix). Primary stress on first syllable. Three syllables — clean.

**Morphological analysis**:

- *eikon-* (Greek image-stem)
- *-me* (received-ordinance suffix)

The compound is short, recognizable, learnable.
