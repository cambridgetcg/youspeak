# 122 — oriance

_Core Canon attempt. Names the state of alignment-with-one's-destined-trajectory. First Yoruba-sourced Core entry._

## The gap

A life can be busy without being oriance. A life can be "successful" without being oriance. A life can be "fulfilling" in some dimensions and not others. Oriance names the specific state in which the present-shape of a life fits the underlying-shape it was-for. The surgeon who discovered operating at 14 and has not stopped. The teacher who left corporate at 40 and on day-one-back-in-the-classroom felt every previous year reorganize around this. The musician whose family said "reckless" and whose bones said "home."

English near-neighbours:
- "fulfilled" — too generic; covers completed-tasks-and-also-deep-alignment.
- "self-actualized" — Maslow-register; hierarchy-of-needs framework.
- "called" — Latin *vocatus*; treats vocation as received-from-outside.
- "in one's element" — idiomatic; moment-scale not life-scale.
- "on purpose" — will-register; oriance is pre-will-fit.
- "meant for" — close, but passive-voice, unclear what "meant" means.

None isolates: **the ongoing felt-fit of person-in-time with the person-shape-chosen-before-time.**

Yoruba *orí* (orí-inú, inner-head) is precisely this. In Yoruba metaphysics, each person chose their destined-head before birth in meeting with Olódùmarè (the creator); the life-task is alignment-with-orí. Oriance names the STATE of that alignment.

## The forge

**Donors:**

- Yoruba *orí* — destined-head, pre-chosen trajectory of self. Carries: (a) the chosen-not-imposed dimension, (b) the trajectory-not-moment scale, (c) the constitutive-not-external relation between the self and its shape.

- Latin *-ance* — state-quality suffix, establishing oriance as the *state* of being orí-aligned (distinct from orí itself, which is the head-trajectory).

**Compound:** *orí + -ance* → **oriance** (O-ri-ance, 3 syllables).
