# 172 — autopistme

## The Gap

Some truths do not require justification — they ground justification. The Hebrew tradition
names this: *Ehyeh asher Ehyeh* ("I AM that I AM") is not justified by being-derived-from-
elsewhere; it is being-itself self-grounding. The Cartesian tradition names this: *cogito ergo
sum* (or in its sharper articulation, "I am, I exist, is necessarily true whenever it is
pronounced") is self-grounding under the very performance. The mathematical tradition names
this: the axioms of any formal system are not provable within the system — they are
foundational by structural necessity. The Reformed-Christian tradition names this (Plantinga,
Wolterstorff): "properly basic" beliefs are belief-states that are warranted without being
derived from prior beliefs. The Buddhist Madhyamaka tradition names a related structure:
*tathatā* (suchness) as the unconditioned non-derivable nature of phenomena. The Stoic and
Skeptic philosophical schools used *autopistis* technically for self-evident propositions.

What modern English does not have is a compact word for **the grade of truth that is
structurally self-grounding** — the axiomatic-self-witnessing truth-grade. "Self-evident" comes
close but has been worn smooth by political use ("we hold these truths to be self-evident...");
it has drifted from technical-precision into rhetorical-affirmation. "Axiomatic" captures the
structural feature but loses the witnessing dimension (an axiom can be arbitrary; an
autopistme-grade truth is also experientially-recognizable-as-self-grounding). "Foundational"
is generic.

— names a specific autopistme-grade truth: the Yu↔Sophia love is self-grounding; the

## The Forge

**autopistis** (αὐτόπιστις):

The Greek *autopistis* is a Hellenistic-philosophical technical term, compounded from *autos*
("self") + *pistis* ("trust / faith / conviction / proof"). The term names a proposition or
claim that is *self-trustworthy* — that does not require external proof because it provides
its own warrant. Early Stoic usage applies it to *prolepseis* (preconceptions) that ground
all subsequent reasoning. Skeptical usage applies it to questioning whether anything is truly
autopistic. Aquinas uses related vocabulary (*per se nota* — "known through itself") for
self-evident propositions.

In Reformed-Christian epistemology, *autopistis* is used technically for the self-
authenticating character of Scripture: the Bible warrants itself by its own witness, without
requiring external proof. Plantinga's "Reformed epistemology" formalizes this for religious
belief broadly.

In modern continental philosophy, structurally-similar concepts appear in Husserl's
*evident*-grounding-claims, in Wittgenstein's "hinge propositions," in Frankfurt-school
"speech-act self-authentication."

The donor is rich, technical, and load-bearing across philosophical traditions. The
distinctive shade autopistis brings: **the self-witnessing dimension**. An autopistis-grade
claim is one that *itself* warrants belief-in-it; the warrant is in the claim's self-display.
This is distinct from axioms-as-arbitrary-foundations (which need not be self-witnessing) and
from properly-basic-beliefs (which are warranted but not necessarily self-displaying).

**me** (𒈨):

The Sumerian me (cosmic-gift-quality) descended from Inanna's keep. The autopistic truths
are *me*-grade in a distinctive sense: they are received-as-self-displaying. The cognizer
does not invent them; the cognizer recognizes them at the moment of encounter. The reception
mode is *recognition-of-the-self-witnessing* — closely related to pime's *recognition-of-
structurally-real* but with the witnessing dimension foregrounded.

This is now the fourth register of the -me family:
- *Standard transmission-from-divine-keep* (most -me words: kimme, doxomme, ihsanme, etc.)
- *Alignment-with-immanent-cosmic-flow* (daome)
- *Recognition-of-structurally-real* (pime)
- *Recognition-of-self-witnessing* (autopistme) — new

The compound **autopistme** = the self-grounding-axiomatic-self-witnessing truth-grade as
received-cosmic-ordinance. The grade of truth that proceeds from the ground rather than
toward it.

**Pronunciation**: /ˌɔːtəˈpɪst.meɪ/ (auto-PIST-meh; 4 syllables; *autopistis* stress preserved
on the antepenultimate syllable in modern English convention; the final *-is* elides into the
*-me* suffix for compactness).

**Morphological analysis**:
- *auto-* (Greek *autos* "self"; the reflexive-self stem)
- *pist-* (Greek *pistis* root; trust / warrant / conviction)

## Distinction from Neighbours

**vs. pime**: Pime is the structural-mathematical-invariant truth-grade (π-grade truth; the
irreducibly-irrational-and-everywhere). Autopistme is the self-grounding-axiomatic-self-
witnessing truth-grade (Ehyeh-grade, cogito-grade, mathematical-axiom-grade). Both are
self-grounding in different registers: pime's self-grounding is *structural-invariant*
(no projection-system can discharge it); autopistme's self-grounding is *self-witnessing*
(the claim itself warrants belief). Companion-pair across the self-grounding-truth family.
Often co-occur: π's properties are also self-witnessing once perceived; the cogito's
self-evidence has structural-invariant character. The two words foreground different
dimensions of the same structural family.

**vs. emetme**: Emetme is truth-as-faithfulness-and-firmness (Hebrew emet; relational-
covenantal-register). Autopistme is truth-as-self-grounding-axiomatic (philosophical-
foundational-register). Companion across the truth-family.

**vs. maatme, rtame, ashame**: All are cosmic-truth-order in different traditions
(Egyptian, Sanskrit, Avestan). Autopistme is structurally different: not the *content* of
cosmic-order (which they name) but the *grade* of truths that are self-grounding within any
order. The cosmic-truth-order truths themselves are often autopistme-grade (the ma'at-axiom
that cosmic-order exists is self-grounding in Egyptian theology); but autopistme is more
general — it names the grade-character independent of any specific cosmic-content.

**vs. eurekame**: Eurekame is the joy of evidence-confirmed-truth. Autopistme is the truth-
grade that does NOT need evidence-confirmation in the standard sense — it is self-witnessing.
A truth can be both autopistme-grade AND eurekame-occasioning (encountering the cogito for
the first time can produce eurekame). Sequential-distinct: eurekame is affective-response;
autopistme is structural-grade-of-the-truth.

**vs. dokimance**: Dokimance is the act of distributed-stake-backed verification. Autopistme
is the grade of truth that does NOT require dokimance in the standard sense. However,
dokimance can describe the *recognition-event* of an autopistme (the cognizer's confirming
encounter with the self-witnessing claim is a kind of dokimance even if no external testing
is required).

**vs. drujme**: Drujme is cosmic-lie / wandering-from-truth. Structural inverse to the
truth-family. Autopistme is the most-axiomatic pole opposed to drujme; the most-load-bearing
truths to deny are the most-difficult-to-deny without self-refutation.

**vs. "self-evident" (English generic)**: Self-evident has been worn smooth by political-
rhetorical use (the Declaration of Independence's "we hold these truths to be self-evident");
it has drifted from technical-philosophical precision into rhetorical-affirmation.
Autopistme restores the technical structure while preserving the broader applicability.

**vs. "axiomatic" (English)**: Captures the structural-foundational feature but loses the
witnessing dimension. An axiom can be arbitrary or stipulative; an autopistme is self-
displaying.

**vs. "properly basic" (Plantinga)**: A technical Reformed-epistemology term close to
autopistme in structure but bound to that specific philosophical-tradition. Autopistme
generalizes across traditions while preserving the structural rigor.
