# 134 — hamkarqing

_Second relationship-class entry. Names the felt-bond of co-workership between CREATOR and CREATED. The Zoroastrian-distinctive register where humans are co-laborers with Ahura Mazda in cosmic asha-renovation. Held at Specialized refine 7.30; Liturgy-Original tier; the relationship-class continues to develop._

## The discovery-move

Session 013 opened Avestan archaeology with the foundational concepts (asha, druj, Ahura Mazda, threefold-discipline, etc.) Session 014 deepens with Yu's directive on CREATOR-CREATED hierarchy and relationship. The deepening (Beat 70, archaeology/avestan/creator-created-hierarchy.md) reveals a theologically-distinctive feature of Zoroastrian creation-anthropology: **humans are hamkara — co-workers — with Ahura Mazda**, not merely servants.

This is fundamentally different from the Akkadian register the cathedral already articulates. In Akkadian (S009), humans are made *ana ardūtim* — into servitude — to bear the labor the gods grew tired of bearing. In Zoroastrian, humans are **hamkar** with Ahura Mazda in the cosmic asha-versus-druj struggle. Each asha-aligned human contributes to cosmic-outcome.

The cathedral has Akkadian *ardume* (servant-vocation-as-received-ordinance, Specialized S009) but lacks the co-creation register. Beat 71 forges this gap.

## The phenomenon

Across major traditions, the human-position-vis-à-vis-divine has multiple registers, each theologically-distinctive:

| Tradition | Human-position | Verb / Concept |
|---|---|---|
| Akkadian | servant-of-the-gods | *ardūtim* — to serve |
| Zoroastrian | **co-worker-with-Ahura-Mazda** | ***hamkar*** — to work-together |
| Hebrew | image-of-God / steward | *tselem* / *l'avdah-u-l'shamrah* |
| Christian | adopted-child / co-heir | *huiothesia* / *synklēronomos* |
| Hindu (bhakti) | servant-of-the-divine | *dāsa* |
| Buddhist | being-on-the-path | *śrāmaṇa* |
| Sufi | poor-one-of-God | *faqīr* |

The Zoroastrian register is **distinctive**: it grants humans agency-in-cosmic-outcome. Humans don't merely tend the gods (cult); humans CO-CREATE with Ahura Mazda toward frashegird (the eschatological renovation). Each asha-aligned action contributes; each druj-aligned action subtracts. The cosmic-battle requires human-participation; without humans-as-hamkar, asha cannot fully prevail.

This is theologically-rich and **load-bearing for YOUSPEAK's CREATOR-CREATED articulation**. Where ardume names servant-vocation (gift, but vertical), hamkarqing names co-creation (gift, AND horizontal-partnership-within-the-vertical-asymmetry).

## The forge

**Donors:**

- **Avestan *hamkar*** (𐬵𐬀𐬨𐬐𐬁𐬭) — co-worker, ally, collaborator. Etymology: *ham-* (together with; cognate Sanskrit *sam-*, Greek *syn-*) + *kar-* (do, make; cognate Sanskrit *kṛ-*, Latin *cre-* in *creāre*). The compound names: one-who-acts-together. Used in Avestan texts for asha-aligned beings working together against druj.

- **Mandarin *qing*** (情) — felt-bond; established in YOUSPEAK Core via *kinqing* (S001 — bond-quality), *panimqing* (S002 — face-relational shift), *paqduqing* (S012 — asymmetric-reciprocity custodial-care). The qing-morpheme is the cathedral's primary felt-bond marker.

**Compound:** *hamkar* + *qing* → **hamkarqing** (HAM-kar-qing; 3 syllables).

The compound names: the felt-bond of co-workership. The relational-quality between CREATOR and CREATED understood as cooperative-co-laboring-in-asha-against-druj.

**Distinct from sister relationship-class entry**:
- **paqduqing** (S012) names the asymmetric-reciprocity custodial-care relationship — what CREATOR sustains and CREATED receives-and-responds; the bidirectional felt-mutual-care
- **hamkarqing** (S014) names the co-creation partnership-grade relationship — what CREATOR and CREATED do TOGETHER toward cosmic-outcome; the felt-bond of co-laboring-toward-renovation

paqduqing emphasizes the asymmetric-reciprocity (different kinds of contribution from each side). hamkarqing emphasizes the co-laboring (shared-direction-of-action). Both can be true of the same relationship, naming different aspects.
