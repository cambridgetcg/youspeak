# Assessment notes — 316 frashame

## The gap

Zoroastrian theology holds that cosmic history is the struggle of asha against druj. The end of that history is **frashegird** — the cosmic renovation, the making-perfect of all creation. The concept is richly attested but has no compact English word. "Renovation" is too domestic; "restoration" implies return to an original state; "apokatastasis" is the Greek Christian variant; "eschaton" is the end-time itself, not the making-wonderful that occurs there.

Frashame names the renovation specifically as received-ordinance. The -me suffix does crucial theological work: it prevents frashame from becoming a human self-help project or a political utopia. The world is not made wonderful by human effort; it is made wonderful by the divine working-out of asha, and humans receive that renovation as the completion of creation.

## Near-neighbour check

- **apokatastasis** (Greek Christian theological term) — restoration of all things to an original/primordial state; frashame is renovation-to-wonder, not restoration-to-origin.
- **tikkunme** (Hebrew/YOUSPEAK Core) — human participatory repair of the world; frashame is the eschatological completion, not the ongoing human work.
- **hailsme** (Gothic/YOUSPEAK Core) — salvation as wholeness-restoration of the creature; frashame is renovation of the whole cosmos.
- **shevirame** (Hebrew/YOUSPEAK Core) — the breaking that makes repair necessary; frashame is the opposite end of the arc.
- **kittume** (Akkadian/YOUSPEAK Core) — structural truth as firm foundation; frashame is eschatological completion, not foundational truth.
- **ragnarökme** (Old Norse/YOUSPEAK Core) — the ending-that-acts-within-fate; frashame is renovation, not destruction-within-destiny.
- **new creation / new heavens and new earth** (Christian/Hebrew) — replacement or renewal narratives; frashame is the making-wonderful of the existing creation, not its replacement.

None names the Zoroastrian register of universal eschatological renovation as divine gift. The gap stands.

## Cross-tradition grounding

1. **Zoroastrianism** — frashegird (primary donor). The oldest and most compact attestation of cosmic renovation; the final victory of asha over druj.
2. **Christianity** — apokatastasis (Origen, Gregory of Nyssa, Isaac of Nineveh): the restoration of all things. Close but different: restoration to original state, not making-wonderful.
3. **Judaism** — tikkun olam and the messianic age: repair of the world. Close but different: ongoing human and divine cooperative work, not final eschatological renovation.
4. **Islam** — Yawm al-Baʿth / Qiyamah: resurrection and final judgment leading to renewal. Close but different: the Quranic narrative emphasizes judgment and paradise, not the renovation of this cosmos.
5. **Buddhism** — Maitreya and the restoration of the true Dharma. Close but different: the arrival of the future Buddha and the recovery of teaching, not the making-perfect of the material cosmos.

The Avestan formulation is the most compact and the most specific: the world is made *fraša* — wonderful, perfect, excellent. Frashame carries that specificity into YOUSPEAK.
