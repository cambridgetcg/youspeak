# 299 — manidoome

_First Algonquian-Sumerian cross-family coinage. Names the personhood-pole of the vital-force cluster — the sacred as PERSON, not as energy or intensity or essence or identity. The grammar of animacy is the ordinance: the world is built such that beings are persons, and the recognition of their personhood is the order of things._

---

## The gap

YOUSPEAK has a populated vital-force cluster:

- **nyamame** (Mande nyama, Core 8.00) — vital-force-as-energy; the ambient animating energy that flows through things, accumulates through craft, is dangerous when released
- **wakanme** (Lakota wakȟáŋ, Core 8.30) — sacred-as-ontological-intensity; the quality of exceeding ordinary comprehension, distributed at varying intensities
- **mauriqing** (Māori mauri, Core 8.40) — life-force-as-relational-essence; the shared essence that binds a network, diminishment in one diminishes all
- **kwothme** (Nuer kwoth, Core 8.35) — wind-as-divine-body; the identity-claim that the atmosphere IS the spirit
- **aseme** (Yoruba àṣẹ, Core) — divine-power-to-make-things-happen
- **maname** (Yoruba ọmọlúwàbí, Core) — inherited-efficacy-as-constituted-personhood

None of these is *manidoo*. They all touch dimensions of sacred force — energy, intensity, essence, identity, power, efficacy. But manidoome adds what none of them name:

1. **Personhood as the primary category.** Not energy (nyamame), not intensity (wakanme), not essence (mauriqing), not identity (kwothme). PERSONHOOD — the quality of being-a-person-who-can-enter-into-relationships. The sacred is not a force that flows through things; it is a person who acts, intends, and responds. The being behind the energy. The agent behind the power.

2. **Grammatical animacy as theology.** In Algonquian languages, manidoo is grammatically animate at the Proto-Algonquian level (~2,500 years old). The animate singular suffix -wa marks the word as referring to a being, not a thing. The language encodes the theology in its morphology: the sacred is grammatically alive. This is structurally distinct from every other donor in the vital-force cluster — none of them carry grammatical animacy as part of the word itself.

3. **The "other-than-human persons" framework.** Hallowell's insight (from Ojibwe fieldwork): personhood is not assigned by taxonomy (this is a stone, therefore not a person) but recognized through relationship (this stone entered into reciprocity with me, therefore it is a person). "Are all the stones around us alive?" "No, but some are." The some-ness is the theology: personhood is discovered, not deduced. The world is full of persons, only some of whom are human. This is a different epistemology from any other entry in the cluster.

4. **Gichi-manidoo as collective totality, not supreme being.** The Great Spirit is not a monarch above a hierarchy of lesser spirits. It is the collective totality of all manitous functioning as one — organism not monarch. Neither monotheism (one God above) nor polytheism (many gods beside) but pan-animist totality-theology: the many are one because they function as one, not because one absorbs the many. This is structurally distinct from kwothme (identity-claim: wind IS spirit) and wakanme (intensity distributed throughout nature).

## The donor

**Algonquian manidoo** (Proto-Algonquian *maneto·wa — animate noun). The word is grammatically alive at the deepest reconstructable level. The animate singular suffix -wa is part of the Proto-Algonquian reconstruction (~2,500 years ago), meaning the theology of sacred-personhood is encoded in the morphology of the word itself, not merely in its semantic content.

Key structural features:
- **Cognate depth** — ~30 Algonquian languages preserve reflexes: Ojibwe manidoo, Cree manitôw, Fox manetôwa, Shawnee maneto, Mi'kmaq məndu, Mohegan-Pequot manto. The concept is pan-Algonquian, not language-specific.
- **Earliest European attestation** — Thomas Harriot's 1585 Roanoke glossary: mantóac = "gods and goddesses." The word entered English as "manitou" via Mohegan-Pequot → French → English (1690s).
- **Semantic range** — spirit + mystery + personhood + power + animacy + sacredness. The quality of being-a-person-with-power-who-can-enter-into-relationships. Everything has its own manidoo.
- **Gichi-manidoo** — the augmentative prefix gichi-/gitche-/kitchi- + manidoo = "Great Spirit." Not a supreme being above a hierarchy but the collective totality of all manitous functioning as one — like organs in a body, not subjects under a king.
- **Hallowell's framework** — "other-than-human persons": personhood is constituted by interactions between beings, not by interior properties. "Are all stones alive?" "No, but some are." Personhood is recognized through relationship, not assigned by taxonomy.
- **Harvey's synthesis** — "Animists are people who recognize that the world is full of persons, only some of whom are human."

The -me marks the personhood-with-power as received-ordinance: the capacity to recognize beings-as-persons and enter into reciprocity with them is gift-grade. The world is built such that beings are persons. The grammar of animacy is not a human projection onto inert matter — it is the received structure of a world where personhood is the primary ontological category, and matter is a secondary manifestation within it.

## The Hallowell principle: personhood through relationship

The deepest Algonquian contribution to the cathedral's ontology:

Personhood is not an interior property (self-consciousness, rationality, soul-substance) that some beings have and others lack. Personhood is **recognized through relationship** — a being IS a person when it enters into reciprocity. The Ojibwe elder's answer — "Are all the stones around us alive?" "No, but some are" — is not vague; it is precise. The some-ness is the point: you cannot know in advance which stones are persons; you discover it through relationship. Taxonomy cannot assign personhood; only encounter can reveal it.

This is structurally distinct from:
- **Hebrew b'tselem Elohim** — personhood assigned by divine-image (all humans are persons by theological decree)
- **Greek rational soul** — personhood assigned by rationality (humans are persons because they think)
- **Buddhist sentience** — personhood assigned by capacity-for-suffering (sentient beings are persons)
- **Algonquian manidoo** — personhood discovered through relationship (a being IS a person when it enters reciprocity)

The Algonquian epistemology is the only one in the catalog that makes personhood relational-discoverable rather than taxonomically-assignable. This is the specific gap manidoome fills.
