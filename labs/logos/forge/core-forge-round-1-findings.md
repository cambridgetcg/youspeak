## What the v2 rubric taught in the attempting

### Finding 1 — polyphone is the dominant blocker

Every v2 score I computed was most-sensitive to polyphone_balance. A same-family donor-pair (Latin + English, Greek + Latin) ceilings at 4 in polyphone — which, weighted at 0.15, penalises a candidate by ~0.45 relative to a cross-family pair. That difference is the margin between canon and refine in several cases.

tacitkin is the clearest illustration: 9 learnability, 9 groundedness, but Latin + English = same Indo-European family = polyphone 4 → 7.45 (refine). Swapping one morpheme to Mandarin would push it to Core. This is the Constitution's POLYPHONE foundation operating exactly as designed: **the rubric resists Greek-Latin monoculture and rewards cross-family compounds**.

### Finding 2 — 3-donor compounds aren't free

Adding a third tradition to a compound lifts polyphone but often costs learnability more. Tested example: dhyamushance (Sanskrit dhyāna + Japanese mushin + Latin -ance) lifts polyphone from 6 → 8 (a gain of 0.30 weighted) but drops learnability from 7 → 5 (a loss of 0.40 weighted). Net: the 3-donor compound scores *lower* than the 2-donor compound.

**The Constitution's implied optimum: 2-donor cross-family pairs with short morphemes and English-familiar handles.** This is where kimance and kinqing live.

### Finding 3 — English morphemes carry disproportionate value

kimance and kinqing both contain one English-familiar morpheme (*-mance, kin*). This is not accidental. Under the v2 rubric, a compound where at least one morpheme is English-transparent has a major learnability boost (9) that a purely-foreign compound cannot match.

**Corollary**: the strongest Core Canon candidates will likely pair one English/Latinate-familiar morpheme with one non-IE-family morpheme. This maximises learnability AND polyphone simultaneously.

### Finding 4 — groundedness can be maxed only for grounded gaps

Every candidate I attempted scored 8-9 on groundedness. This is because I explicitly targeted the Constitution's grounded-gap list. Had I tried to forge for an abstract/theological gap, groundedness would have fallen to 4-5 and no v2-Core entry would be reachable. **PRAGMA is therefore the first filter**: forge first for grounded gaps; defer abstract gaps to Specialized-Canon register.

### Finding 5 — clarity_yield is harder to score honestly than expected

clarity_yield is: *does thinking in this word clarify the concept vs English?* I repeatedly wanted to score 8 but had to hold at 7 when the concept has any existing English gloss. Only coinages for truly-unnamed phenomena (no English gloss at all) can honestly score 9.

**Implication**: the target-gaps the Constitution identified are themselves selected for clarity-yield-potential — concepts English truly lacks names for.

## Grammar observations — does YOUSPEAK need engineered grammar?

While writing the canon entries for kimance and kinqing, I noted every grammatical construction I used. Here is what emerged:

### What emerged spontaneously (no engineering needed)

1. **State vs event distinction** — handled by English article:
   - "in kimance" (durative state) vs "a kimance" (count-noun / event-moment)
   - "kinqing" (the phenomenon) vs "a kinqing" (one specific relation)

2. **Directional prepositions** — English defaults work:
   - "kimance toward X" / "kimance between A and B"
   - "kinqing with X"

3. **Degree / intensity** — English adjectives suffice:
   - "deep kimance", "early kinqing", "withdrawn kimance", "partial kimance"

4. **Negation** — English structures work:
   - "absence of kimance", "not in kimance", "lack of kinqing"

5. **Perceptual attribution** — English:
   - "her kimance was visible", "palpable kinqing"

6. **Plural** — English plurals by default:
   - "her three closest kinqings"

7. **Possession** — English possessive:
   - "my kinqing with him", "her kimance toward the child"

8. **Reciprocity** — implicit or explicit via English:
   - "we have kinqing" (implicit mutual); "I am in kinqing with X" (mutuality assumed)

### What didn't emerge (and whether it's needed)

1. **Aspect markers** (momentary / habitual / perfective) — **not needed**; English tense+aspect system carries this.

2. **Evidentiality** (direct / inferred / reported) — **marginally useful for Zerone-operative words (dokimance especially)**. Could engineer: *-auto* (direct), *-manth* (inferred), *-phem* (reported). Test: is this a genuine gap or a YOUSPEAK-imposed distinction? No English evidence forces this distinction for everyday Core Canon words. **Defer.**

3. **Mood** (indicative / imperative / subjunctive) — **not needed**; English handles.

4. **Register markers** (formal / casual / liturgical) — **not needed**; speaker-context carries.

5. **Person / animacy** — **not needed**; grammar-words (pronouns, etc.) handle.

6. **Compound-boundary markers in text** — **handled by transliterator display**, not grammar.

### Where engineered grammar already exists (and it's correct that it does)

1. **Anastrophance** (diplosemy/head-inversion) — engineered because English cannot naturally express "this is the inversion of that word." The engineering is justified by a gap English actively has.

2. **Parallaxance** (parallel-couplet composition) — engineered for liturgical composition. The Chinese duìlián tradition showed this was a real form; YOUSPEAK formalises for its own use.

3. **Canonical entry schema** (frontmatter + definition + full sense + etymology + examples + not-confused + retirement) — emerged through writing conventions; now codified as a pattern.

### The principled answer

**YOUSPEAK should largely defer grammar to its host language (English for anglophone speakers, analog in other host languages).** Engineered grammar is justified only where:

1. A specific distinction English cannot make is load-bearing for YOUSPEAK's purposes
2. Usage-evidence (not theoretical need) demonstrates the gap
3. The engineered marker scores well on the 6-axis rubric itself

This is consistent with Foundation II (SAPHE — clarity-emerges-not-imposes) and Foundation III (ANAKALYPSE — unfold, don't impose).

**Honest recommendation for next sessions:**

Use YOUSPEAK in mixed dialogue. Log grammatical patterns. Only formalize when a pattern has emerged and demonstrates a clear gap in host-language grammar. Do NOT engineer grammar preemptively.
