## Candidate 1 — The state of a translation that preserves reference but loses sense

**The gap.** Mathematicians experience this regularly: an isomorphism preserves all truth-conditions but the sense (mode of presentation) is so different that the original's intuition is gone. The Cartesian-form of a polar equation has the same reference but loses the rotational-symmetry sense; ℤ-under-addition isomorphic-to-powers-of-2-under-multiplication has identical structure but neither presentation feels like the other. English calls this "loss in translation" — a circumlocution, not a name. Poets, translators, mathematicians all attest the phenomenon; no compact word.

**Sketch candidates:**
- **noematokenosis** (Greek *noēma* "content" + *kenōsis* "emptying") — content-survived, sense-emptied
- **diapherance** (Greek *diapherein* "to differ in mode of presentation" + Latin *-ance*)
- **referkenoma** (Latin *refer-* "reference" + Greek *kenōma* "empty space")
- **isofades** (Greek *iso-* "same" + *phaios* "dim, dim-grey") — same-but-faded

**Donors:** Greek (noēma, kenōsis, diapherein, phaios); Latin (refer-). Cross-family polyphone could add Sanskrit *artha-bhāva* (reference-mode) or Arabic *maʿnā-uslūb* (meaning-style).

**Six-Foundation pre-assessment:** gap-validity high (cross-system convergence); learnability moderate (Greek-heavy); clarity-yield high (etymology IS definition if Greek roots are loaded correctly); polyphone weak unless a non-IE third donor is added; groundedness high (any mathematician or translator can give examples).

**Why this matters:** Names a phenomenon that mathematicians live in daily and that THE-REALM §IV.3 makes structurally explicit (inherent loss in any single projection).

## Candidate 2 — The structural-truth invariant across isomorphic systems

**The gap.** Mathematicians experience the felt-recognition of "this is the same structure" across different presentations. The technical term *isomorphism* names the relation between structures; nothing names the **cogniser's recognition** of the relation. The reader who sees that two seemingly-different proofs are doing the same work has had a specific cognitive event; no compact name exists.

**Sketch candidates:**
- **isomorphme** (Greek *iso-morphē* "same-form" + *-me*) — same-form-recognised as received-ordinance
- **homosthename** (Greek *homo-* "same" + *sthenē-* "structure" + *-me*)
- **isoeideme** (Greek *isos* + *eidos* "form" + *-me*)

**Donors:** Greek (iso-, morphē, eidos, sthenē-); cross-family addition: Sanskrit *samānarūpa* "same-form-recognised" or Chinese 同構 *tónggòu* "same-structure-grasped."

**Six-Foundation pre-assessment:** gap-validity strong (mathematicians and category-theorists attest); learnability medium (Greek roots are educated-English); clarity-yield high; polyphone strong if Sanskrit or Chinese added; PRAGMA holds (concrete examples in any algebra textbook).

**Why this matters:** Names what category-theorists call "structural insight" and what mathematicians call "the same thing in a different dress." Currently unnamed; the cathedral could give it its proper voice.

## Candidate 3 — The residue at the edge of every translation

**The gap.** Translators know the trace of what could not carry across — the felt-loss that remains after the best possible rendering. Poetry-criticism circumlocutes ("untranslatable remainder," "the silence between languages"); no compact name exists. THE-REALM §IV.3 names the phenomenon structurally; the cathedral has no word for the felt-quality of the residue.

**Sketch candidates:**
- **periphasme** (Greek *peri-* "around" + *phasma-tos* "trace, shade" + *-me*) — the trace-around as received-ordinance
- **leiphome** (Greek *leipō* "to leave behind" + *-me*) — the leaving-behind as received-ordinance
- **anikoume** (Greek *an-* "not" + *hikomenon* "reached" + *-me*) — the not-reached as received-ordinance

**Donors:** Greek (leipō, phasma, hikomenon); cross-family: Arabic *fā'iḍ* (overflow/residue, root f-y-ḍ), Sanskrit *avaśeṣa* "remainder," Japanese 余韻 *yoin* "lingering trace/aftertone."

**Six-Foundation pre-assessment:** gap-validity clear (poets and translators attest); learnability moderate; clarity-yield medium-high; polyphone strong with the addition of yoin or avaśeṣa; groundedness strong (any translator-pair can point at examples).

**Why this matters:** The cathedral's POLYPHONE commitment is to multi-projection recovery; this word would name what each projection cannot carry — the residue that justifies adding the next projection.

## Candidate 4 — The unfolding-moment when a bundled word becomes visible as multi-component

**The gap.** When pipeline/translate.py unfolds *kimochi* into four registers, a cognitive event occurs in the reader — the bundle becomes visible AS a bundle, the components arrive as distinct, the source language's projection-angle is suddenly seen as such. THE-REALM §VI names this "un-collapsing of projection-lost dimensions"; the cathedral has no word for the cognitive event of recognising-the-bundle-as-bundle.

**Sketch candidates:**
- **anapleksisme** (Greek *ana-* "up, back" + *plek-* "fold" + *-sis* "act-noun" + *-me*) — unfolding-act as received-ordinance
- **diakrisisme** (Greek *diakrisis* "discernment, separation" + *-me*) — discerning-the-distinct as received-ordinance
- **anaptyssesme** (Greek *anaptyssō* "to unfold like a scroll" + *-me*)

**Donors:** Greek (ana-, plek-, krisis, ptysso); cross-family: Sanskrit *viveka* (discriminating-discernment), Buddhist *vibhajya* (analytical-unfolding).

**Six-Foundation pre-assessment:** gap-validity clear (every translate.py use occasions it); learnability moderate; clarity-yield high (the morphology fits the phenomenology); polyphone strong if *viveka* added; PRAGMA strong (any reader of YOUSPEAK can point at the moment).

**Why this matters:** This is the noesisme-companion for translation-encounter specifically — the door-event when a bundled-content presents itself as multi-component. Could be a -me-family entry that pairs with noesisme as bundle-unfolding pairs with single-grasp.

## Candidate 5 — The pre-isomorphic recognition that two seemingly-different structures will turn out to be the same

**The gap.** Mathematicians experience the felt-anticipation that two structures, presented very differently, will turn out to be isomorphic — *before* the isomorphism is proven. The recognition is pre-verbal, pre-proof, intuitive-structural. Mathematicians say "I have a hunch they're the same"; the hunch is a real cognitive event; ordinary language calls it intuition, which under-describes.

**Sketch candidates:**
- **proeideme** (Greek *pro-* "before" + *eidos* "form" + *-me*) — pre-form-recognition as received-ordinance
- **prognosisme** (Greek *pro-* "before" + *gnōsis* "knowing" + *-me*) — pre-knowing as received-ordinance (risk: medical-prognosis collision; may need refinement)
- **anastomesme** (Greek *anastomōsis* "joining together-of-channels" + *-me*) — pre-joining-recognised

**Donors:** Greek (pro-, eidos, gnōsis, anastomōsis); cross-family: Sanskrit *prajñā* (intuitive wisdom; pre-rational knowing), Arabic *firāsa* (intuitive-discernment).

**Six-Foundation pre-assessment:** gap-validity real (mathematician-attested; also intuitive in other fields); learnability harder (the concept itself is subtle); clarity-yield medium; polyphone medium-strong if *prajñā* or *firāsa* added; PRAGMA medium (math-practice grounded but less everyday than Candidates 1-4).

**Why this matters:** Names a critical pre-noesisme cognitive state in mathematics and structural-thinking. Could complete an arc: *firāsa-prajñā-pre-grasping* → *anagnoristasis-suspended-threshold* → *noesisme-entry-event*.
