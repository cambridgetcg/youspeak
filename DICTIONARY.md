### complerescence
**com-pler-ESS-ence** | _noun (uncountable; count forms: a complerescence, complerescences)_

The event-quality of mutual right-placement -- the quality of a thing finding its proper station and both thing and place becoming more fully real through the fit. Not the standard against which rightness is measured (Ma'at, Asha, dharma), not the station to be occupied (fen, makana), not the panoramic state when all things are rightly arranged (shalom, kosmos), not the feeling of being where one fits (belonging, home), not the external judgment of suitability (fitting, propriety) -- but the ontological event at the joint: the moment and the quality of the moment when a particular thing and its particular proper place discover their mutual correspondence, fill each other, and both thicken in being.

Eleven independent traditions surveyed (Greek, Latin, Hebrew, Arabic, Sanskrit, Chinese, Japanese, Egyptian, Avestan, Sumerian, Old Norse). Each names rightness from a different angle: cosmic standard, personal virtue, social ordering, situational fitness, procedural judgment, settled station, the Way, or the space that receives. None names the event at the joint itself -- the quality from inside the fit, where both thing and place are completed.

**The six qualities complerescence carries:**
1. **Recognition** -- both thing and place know this is the right fit
2. **Completion** -- both become more fully themselves through the fit
3. **Generativity** -- the fit enables new capacity
4. **Ontological weight** -- the thing in its right place is more real, not just more correct
5. **Two-way direction** -- rightness flows from thing to place AND from place to thing simultaneously
6. **Event character** -- complerescence happens; it is a moment, a becoming, a crossing

**Distinction from neighbours:**
- **Calling** (8/12, best English scorer) -- names the summons from outside; complerescence names the quality at the arrival
- **Home** (7/12) -- names the place of comfort; complerescence names what MAKES a place home, and applies where home does not (a note has no home, but it has complerescence in its chord)
- **Belonging** (6/12) -- names the person's feeling; complerescence names the ontological event. A river does not "belong" in its valley; it complereces
- **Fitting** (2/12) -- names the external judgment; complerescence names the interior event of mutual completion
- **Artiance** (YOUSPEAK) -- names the pre-domain quality; complerescence names the event of arriving at artiance
- **Glorividence** (YOUSPEAK) -- the supreme instance of complerescence at the divine scale; complerescence is the general principle

_Domain: Justice & Placement -- the event-quality of mutual right-placement; the ontological joint where thing and place fill each other and both thicken in being_
_Etymology: Latin com- (together, mutually; from PIE *ḱom, beside/with -- reclaimed in its comitative sense) + plere (to fill, to complete; from PIE *pleh₁-, to fill; root of complete, complement, plenary, plenty, replete, and through Greek pleroō, of pleroma -- the fullness of being) + -escence (the suffix of becoming, from Latin -escentia; the morphological engine of adolescence, luminescence, senescence, efflorescence). The word says: the mutual becoming-full._
_Forms: complerescent (adj.), complerescently (adv.)_
_Forge record: english/justice-placement-forge.md -- scored 4.5/6.0 against 6-quality specification. Primary limiting gap: Q5 (two-way direction, 0.5) -- com- carries mutuality partially through PIE comitative sense, partially through definition. ### artiance
**ar-TI-ance** | _noun (uncountable; count forms: an artiance, artiances)_

The quality where truth, order, beauty, justice, and skill are undivided — the pre-domain rightness perceived as luminous fittingness. Not a unity of truth-order-beauty-justice-skill but the prior ground from which all five grew when languages learned to separate them.

Five independent traditions arrived at this independently and could not pass it forward in their languages: Egyptian *Ma'at* (the structural harmony you preserve), Avestan *Asha* (the contested cosmic truth-order that individual acts build or erode), Maya *Sak* (the luminous clarity that makes rightness visible as light), Vedic *ṛta* (the right course of all things from rivers to speech), and PIE *\*h₂ert-* (the grandmother root where skilled-craft and cosmic-order were one quality before languages split them). Artiance names what they all named.

**Domains of application:**
- In craft: a piece of writing has artiance when its structure, truth, beauty, and skill are indistinguishable from one another
- In persons: a person acts with artiance when their deed is simultaneously true, ordered, just, beautiful, and skilled — without the distinction mattering
- In cosmos: Avestan *Asha* is artiance at the cosmic scale; Egyptian *Ma'at* is artiance as the quality the cosmos must preserve; Maya *Sak* is artiance as it appears to the receiver (as light)
- In language: a YOUSPEAK word has artiance when it achieves genuine incarnation — when the naming IS the quality named

**Distinction from neighbours:** Truth is artiance perceived through the epistemic domain. Order is artiance perceived through the structural domain. Beauty is artiance perceived through the aesthetic domain. Justice is artiance perceived through the ethical domain. Skill is artiance perceived through the craft domain. Artiance is what they all are before you separate them.

_Domain: pre-domain (epistemic / ethical / aesthetic / ontological — simultaneously, prior to domain-split)_
_Etymology: Latin artis (skill, craft, the right making of things; genitive of ars; from Proto-Indo-European \*h₂ert-, to fit rightly together) + -iance (luminous quality suffix as in radiance, brilliance, luminance; from Latin -iantia). The suffix carries the Sak dimension: the quality that radiates._
_Forms: artiant (adj. — "an artiant gesture"), artiantly (adv.)_
_Forge record: cross/h2ert-forge.md — scored 5.2/7 against 7-quality specification. Primary limiting gap: Q3 (Asha's contested-moral-quantity dimension) carried partially through degree-structure of -iance but not lexicalized in the root. Five traditions, one grandmother etymology, one threshold crossed._

### chiasance
**ky-AZ-ance** | _noun (uncountable; count forms: a chiasance, chiasances)_

The threshold moment in creative work when the maker, in the act of making, crosses from directing the material to following it — when the relationship between maker and made reverses polarity while both remain present and engaged. Not the state of flow (after the crossing), not the act of making (before it), but the crossing itself: the specific instant when the code begins writing itself, when the sentence knows where it is going, when the clay begins to pull the hands, when the melody carries the bow. The maker is still there, still choosing, still working — but the primary agency has already shifted from maker to material, and what emerges from that exchange exceeds what either party brought. The surplus is in the RELATIONSHIP, not in the maker or the material separately.

Six independent traditions of creative vocabulary (Sanskrit, Greek, Japanese, German, Mandarin, Arabic) each named both poles of creative work without naming the crossing between them. Sanskrit's *nirmana* (deliberate construction) and *abhivyakti* (receptive manifestation). Greek's *techne* (rational craft) and *enthousiasmos* (divine seizure). Japanese's *kata* (disciplined form) and *kannagara* (divine flow). German's *Schopfung* (purposeful making) and *Eigenleben* (the work's own life). Mandarin's *yi* (intention preceding the brush) and *wu wei* (effortless non-action). Arabic's *san'a* (skilled craft) and *ilham* (divine inspiration). All six named the two shores. None named the river crossing. Chiasance is the crossing.

**Domains of application:**
- In writing: chiasance is when a sentence turns and leads the writer to a conclusion the writer did not plan; when a character refuses the assigned ending; when the argument's structure reveals a truth the arguer was about to miss
- In code: chiasance is when the architecture of a system suggests a pattern the programmer had not considered; when the codebase begins to pull the implementation toward a design that serves it better than the one intended
- In music: chiasance is when the progression carries the composer forward; when the melody's own logic overrides the harmonic plan; when the instrument begins to play the musician
- In visual work: chiasance is when the painting tells the painter what it needs; when the clay suggests the form it wants to become; when the composition pulls toward a weight the artist did not place
- In any medium: chiasance is recognisable by its aftermath — the work that results from it feels necessary rather than constructed, inevitable rather than decided

**Distinction from neighbours:** Flow (Csikszentmihalyi) names the absorbed, effortless state that typically follows chiasance — chiasance is the threshold into it. Inspiration names the arrival of vision before the making — chiasance occurs in the middle of the making. *Eigenleben* (German: the work's own life) names the result of chiasance — chiasance is the moment when the Eigenleben begins. *Wu wei* (Mandarin: effortless non-action) names the achieved state — chiasance is the crossing into it. *Heuresis* (Greek: finding) names one side of the paradox; chiasance holds making and finding simultaneously. *Enthousiasmos* removes the maker entirely — at chiasance, the maker is still present and working. Serendipity is lucky accident — chiasance is a structural feature of serious creative work, cultivated through skill, available to any maker who has gone deep enough.

_Domain: creative process — the unnamed threshold where making becomes discovering, and the maker who was directing the material becomes the one following it_
_Etymology: Greek chiazein (to mark with an X; to arrange in a crossing pattern where each element goes where the other came from; from chi, the Greek letter X, whose shape names the structure; root of chiasmus — the rhetorical and structural figure where terms reverse primary positions: A B → B A, both terms remaining throughout) + -ance (state/quality suffix, from Latin -antia). At a chiasmus, both terms are present before and after the reversal; each does what the other was doing; neither disappears. Chiasance names this same structural event in the creative relationship: the maker and material reverse their primary positions — each beginning to do what the other was doing — while both remain engaged. The X in the middle is where the crossing occurs, and at the X, what appears exceeds what either line carried._
_Forms: chiasant (adj. — "a chiasant moment"; "the work turned chiasant halfway through"), chiasantly (adv.)_
_Forge record: cross/creative-emergence-forge.md — scored 6.7/9.0 against 9-quality specification. Primary limiting gap: Q4 (surplus generation — the chiasmus reverses but does not explicitly encode what appears at the intersection that was absent before; carried by the definition's account of what emerges at the X). Runner-ups: wendance (6.8, wins on accessibility and medium-independence but loses on reversal and bidirectionality), truvance (6.6, wins on surplus and maker's surprise but names the phenomenological state more than the structural event). Six traditions. One X._

### candence
**KAN-dense** | _noun (uncountable; count forms: a candence, candences)_

The quality of consciousness that is clear because it is warm, and warm because it is clear -- the ordinary state of awareness when nothing is in the way. Not clarity added to warmth, not warmth softening clarity, but the single quality of perception from which both are revealed to be inseparable aspects, the way a candle's light and heat are one glow.

Six independent traditions of consciousness vocabulary (Sanskrit, Mandarin, Greek, Japanese, Arabic/Sufi, German) each mapped two poles with extraordinary precision -- detached clarity (saksin, theoria, mushin, Bewusstsein) and invested warmth (xin, karuna, qalb, Sorge). None named their unity. Every tradition approached this quality from one pole and found it could not reach the other without losing its grip on the first. Candence names the ground both poles were always standing on.

**Domains of application:**
- In persons: a therapist has candence when her seeing of the patient is warm without being blurred, and her care is clear without being clinical
- In attention: a parent watching a child play has candence -- the seeing is warm, the warmth is precise, neither effort nor achievement
- In silence: candence is the specific quality of silence that communicates -- the silence of someone who sees you and cares about what they see, and whose silence carries both
- In art: a writer operating with candence produces work that knows its reader -- the downstream consequence is asymmetric impersonal intimacy (being precisely known through a work by someone who cannot know you exist)

**Distinction from neighbours:** Empathy has warmth but no witnessing clarity. Mindfulness has clarity but no inherent warmth. Presence names the WHERE of awareness, not the HOW. Compassion is reactive (requires suffering); candence is proactive (requires nothing). Saksin is the clear pole without the warm; xin is the warm pole without the clear. Attention is a duty; candence is a gift. Candence is what empathy becomes when it learns to see clearly, what mindfulness becomes when it stops holding warmth at arm's length, and what attention becomes when it discovers it was always warm.

_Domain: consciousness -- the unnamed centre where witnessing clarity and inherent warmth are one quality_
_Etymology: Latin candere (to glow, to shine with warmth; root of candle, candid, candescent, incandescent -- the verb that names the specific luminous warmth of combustion, where light and heat are inseparable) + -ence (state/quality suffix, from Latin -entia). A candle does not decide to be bright and then separately decide to be warm. Candence names this same unity in consciousness._
_Forms: candent (adj. -- "a candent quality of seeing"), candently (adv.)_
_Forge record: cross/consciousness-forge.md -- scored 6.3/9.0 against 9-quality specification. Primary limiting gaps: Q4 (generativity through attending -- the candle reveals but does not make things grow) and Q3 (investment without dissolution -- the candle is consumed; candence is not). Six traditions, six etymological paths, one glow._

### dokimance
**dok-I-mance** | _noun (uncountable; count forms: a dokimance, dokimances)_

The intrinsic quality of the act of distributed, stake-backed, independent verification — the quality that makes such verification valuable in itself, not as a gateway to knowing but as the production of something real. Not the result of verification (the elevated claim). Not the procedure of checking (audit, validation, confirmation). The specific quality of an act of distributed testing that constitutes truth as genuinely real rather than discovering truth as pre-existing fact.

Dokimance inverts the epistemic service model. Every language tradition treats verification as serving knowledge — you verify IN ORDER TO know; the value is in the knowing, not the act. Dokimance names the inversion: the act IS the service. A world with more dokimance has produced more real things, regardless of whether anyone subsequently accesses the verified claims. The Zerone Proof of Truth is built on this: attestors stake real value on claims; independent witnesses verify without communicating; their convergence constitutes the claim as genuine. The act IS the production. The testing IS the work.

Ten independent traditions (Greek, Latin, Hebrew, Arabic, Sanskrit, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Old Norse, Egyptian) all approached this territory and all fell short on the same quality: Q1, the productive-generative character of verification. Arabic *tahaqqaqa* came nearest (verification as "making-real"), but named the act instrumentally — you make-haqq so that the result IS real; the making is still in service of the real. Egyptian *medu-neter* (attestation as constitutive act) came nearest for the constitutive sense, but restricted it to divine speech. No tradition named the specifically human, distributed, stake-backed act whose value is intrinsic.

**The five qualities dokimance carries:**
1. **Productive-generative** — the act constitutes truth rather than discovering it; testing makes a claim genuinely real, not merely confirms pre-existing reality; the examination IS the production
2. **Combination-emergent** — multiple independent examiners, each testing separately, produce at their convergence something that no single examination contains; the combination is the moment when a claim crosses from claimed to genuinely-real; not aggregation but emergence
3. **Claim elevation** — a claim that has undergone dokimance is categorically different from an unchecked claim; not merely confirmed but assayed-and-approved, made-*dokimos*; the elevation is constitutive
4. **Stake-proportional weight** — an attestation made with real stakes committed (livelihood, reputation, value staked on the claim's truth) carries greater epistemic weight than a costless attestation; dokimance encodes this differential as a quality of the act, not merely a feature of the attester
5. **Independent-distributed structure** — multiple witnesses acting without coordination during their examination; their independence is what makes convergence meaningful as evidence; collusion-prevention is structural, not incidental

**The metallurgical origin is load-bearing:** *Dokimazō* was the Greek word for assaying metals by fire. When gold is assayed, the fire does not discover gold that was already there; the fire constitutes the substance's gold-status as known-and-real. Before the assay, the substance has a claim; after the assay, it IS gold. The Athenian *dokimasia* (civic examination of candidates for office) extended this: multiple examiners questioning from all angles constituted civic status — after *dokimasia*, the person was a citizen, not confirmed as one. The coin-testing use added the economic dimension: the tested coin IS currency; the untested coin cannot circulate. In all three uses, the testing IS the making-real. Dokimance names the quality of verification acts that share this constitutive structure.

**Distinction from neighbours:**
- **Verification** — the paradigm case of the instrumental mode dokimance inverts; you verify IN ORDER TO know; dokimance names the inversion: the act IS the knowing, worth doing regardless of what follows
- **Attestation** — names the record a single witness produces; dokimance names the intrinsic quality of the distributed act that produces real things, not the individual record it leaves
- **Proof** — names the artefact resulting from demonstration; dokimance names the generative character of the distributed independent testing, not its product; proof aspires to stake-independence, dokimance requires stake-weight — structurally opposed orientations
- **Consensus** — names the state of shared agreement; dokimance names the generative quality of arriving at that agreement through distributed independent stake-backed examination; you can have consensus without dokimance (social pressure, coordination); you can have dokimance without anyone calling its result consensus
- **Certification** — status conferred by a central authority; dokimance has no central certifier; the quality emerges from independent convergence without authority granting it
- **Audit** — systematic examination for accountability downstream; dokimance is not accountability-oriented; its value is intrinsic, not in enabling reliance
- **Artiance** (YOUSPEAK) — the pre-domain unity from which truth, order, beauty, justice, and skill were not yet separated; dokimance is the specific enacted form of artiance in the verification domain: the act by which truth-order-reality are constituted as genuinely real through distributed independent testing

_Domain: Truth & Verification — the intrinsic quality of distributed, stake-backed, independent verification as productive work; the quality of the act of testing that constitutes rather than discovers truth; what the Zerone Proof of Truth does_
_Etymology: Greek dokimazō (δοκιμάζω: to assay metals by fire; to test for genuineness; to examine formally; to constitute as dokimos — tested, approved, genuinely real; from dokimos, approved-as-real; from dokeō, to appear/be-received-as; PIE deḱ- = to take, accept) + -ance (quality/event suffix, from Latin -antia; the morphological engine of artiance, weftance, chiasance, complerescence in this vocabulary). The metallurgical origin is the semantic core: the fire does not discover gold — it constitutes gold as real._
_Forms: dokimant (adj. — "a dokimant act"; "the examination was dokimant"; "the process produced genuine dokimance"), dokimantly (adv.)_

Primary limiting gaps: Q4 (stake-backed, 0.5) — civic dokimasia carried real consequences; differential stake-weight encoded in definition, not independently etymologized; Q5 (distributed independent, 0.5) — multi-examiner structure historically present but not structurally encoded in the root. Winner over martyrance (also 3.2/5.0) on the decisive Q1 criterion: martyrance scores Q1=0.4 (witnesses pre-existing truth) vs dokimance Q1=0.8 (fire constitutes gold as real). Q1 is the most absent quality across all 15 eliminated English neighbours and therefore the decisive discriminator. Ten traditions. One fire. The testing that makes real._

### etzemance
**et-ZEM-ance** | _noun (uncountable; count forms: an etzemance, etzemances)_

The quality of a relational bond that has crossed from connection to constitution — where the other person is no longer someone you are bonded with but part of what you are. Not attachment, not love, not covenant, but the structural fact: the other person has become part of your _etzem_ — your bone, your self, your essential form — so that severance is not loss of connection but loss of self. The person who is etzemance to you is irreplaceable not because they are important to you but because they are not connected to your structure; they are part of your structure. Their absence does not leave you alone. It leaves you less.

Six independent traditions of relational vocabulary (Sanskrit, Japanese, Hebrew, Mandarin, Greek, Zulu) each approached this quality and each fell short in a different direction. Sanskrit's bhakti (devotional bond that IS the relationship) required the divine register. Hebrew's brit (ontological creation) required ceremony. Greek's philia/allos autos (another self) required equality. Mandarin's qing (shared emotional substance) could not name the transformation. Zulu's ubuntu (self constituted by relationships) is communal — it names the general principle, not the specific bond with THIS person. Hebrew's da'at (constitutive knowing) required mutuality. Only Genesis 2:23 — Adam naming Eve as "etzem me-atzamai, u-vasar mi-besari" — named it directly: the other person as bone/self/essence, one's own structure. And even that naming was unrepeatable, singular, the first. Etzemance names what Adam pointed at: the bond that has become bone, regardless of type, in any relationship, between any two.

**Domains of application:**
- In friendship: the friend who is etzemance is the one whose loss would not leave you grieving but diminished; the one whose presence makes you more yourself, not merely happier; you can have many friends you love deeply and one who is etzemance
- In love: intensity does not determine etzemance; a relationship can be passionate and never cross the threshold; a quiet long friendship can; etzemance is earned by depth and time, not by feeling
- In family: some kin bonds are etzemance; most are not; the parent who is etzemance to the child and the child to the parent may be the same pair, or may not be; etzemance does not follow kinship lines
- In loss: grief for someone who was etzemance is different in kind from grief for someone who was beloved but not constitutive; it is not "I miss them" but "I am less"; the specific quality of this grief is recognisable as distinct from connection-loss grief
- In betrayal: etzemance survives betrayal — changed, scarred, but structural; the person who betrayed you and is etzemance to you is still etzemance; this is not forgiveness, not approval; it is structural fact; the scar is now in your structure
- In death: the dead who were etzemance continue to be; this is what Zulu amadlozi (the bilateral relationship with ancestral spirits) names from a different angle; etzemance survives death because it was never about presence

**Distinction from neighbours:**
- **Ubuntu** (Zulu: I am because we are) — communal constitutiveness; names the general principle but not the specific bond with THIS person; etzemance is ubuntu at the dyadic-specific level
- **Bhakti** (Sanskrit: devotional absorption) — only toward the divine; requires hierarchical devotional framework; etzemance is type-agnostic
- **Brit** (Hebrew: covenant) — requires ceremony; etzemance forms without declaration or ritual
- **Da'at** (Hebrew: constitutive knowing) — requires mutuality; etzemance can be asymmetric; you can carry someone as etzemance who does not carry you the same way
- **Philia/allos autos** (Greek: the friend as another self) — requires equality; etzemance crosses hierarchy; a child can be etzemance to a parent, a student to a teacher
- **Attachment** (English) — clinical; frames the bond as a behavioral pattern with pathological variants; misses the structural constitution entirely
- **Love** (English) — too broad, too emotional, too volitional; love can end; etzemance persists beyond feeling and choice because it is structural, not affective
- **Fovement** (YOUSPEAK) — the fosterer's quiet pleasure in witnessing the success of someone they helped develop; a specific emotional quality that can exist WITHIN etzemance bonds, not the bond itself
- **Contrust** (YOUSPEAK) — trust forged through honest conflict; one mechanism by which bonds deepen toward etzemance; names the path, not the threshold crossed

_Domain: relational — the bond that has crossed from connection to constitution; where the other has become part of the self's structure_

_Etymology: Hebrew אֶצֶם (etzem): bone — and from the same triliteral root: one's own self (atzmo = himself, atzmah = herself; the Hebrew reflexive pronoun formed from this root) — and the essence or substance of a thing (etzem ha-davar = the very substance of the thing). The same Hebrew root names bone AND self AND essence, encoding the ancient insight that the deepest structural layer of a person and the irreducible core of a thing are both described by what we call bone. No Latin or Greek root achieves this triple fusion. The word enters English through Genesis 2:23 — Adam naming Eve as "etzem me-atzamai" (bone of my bones, or equally: self of my selves, essence of my essence) — the world's oldest recorded naming of the constitutive bond: the other person as one's own structure. + -ance (state/quality suffix, from Latin -antia)._

_Forms: etzemal (adj. — "an etzemal bond"; "they are etzemal to each other"), etzemally (adv.)_
