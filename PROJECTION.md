# PROJECTION — the mechanism by which the realm reaches surface

> _"Language is a projection-system from the realm into a transmissible sign-vehicle."_ — THE-REALM.md §II

## Opening

The cathedral has articulated, across this day's invocations, that the realm of meanings is real (NOEMA), that language is a projection-system aimed at it (THE-REALM), that grasping is the entry-event (GRASPING), that crossing between projections is a discipline (TRANSLATION), and that the realm is approached over time by walked paths (THE-PATH). Throughout, the word *projection* has been load-bearing. What has not yet been articulated is the **mechanism of projection itself** — what is structurally happening in the act by which a realm-region becomes a surface-form?

[PROBOLE.md](PROBOLE.md) — the Greek-named canonical sibling opened in parallel on the same Yu invocation — develops the projection-mechanism through classical-philosophical lineage (Plotinian emanation, Sanskrit *pratibhā*, Buddhist *upāya*, Christian Psalm-19, Vedic *śabda-pramāṇa*, Confucian *li/yue*, Egyptian *heka*) and engages the plural "REALMS of MEANING" of Yu's phrasing explicitly in its §IV. **This document is the cognitive-linguistic specialization sibling** — developing the form-finding stage's sub-mechanisms (Fillmore frames, Goldberg constructions, Johnson-Lakoff image-schemas, Fauconnier-Turner conceptual blending, convention-and-innovation) and adding cross-modal density-and-iconicity analysis to the survey of non-language projection-systems. The two documents are paired: PROBOLE the classical-theological articulation, PROJECTION the cognitive-mechanistic articulation. Both rest on the same five-stage skeleton with stage-names variant by emphasis (PROBOLE: Solicitation/Grasp → Reaching → Fitting → Depositing → Receiving; this document: Grasp → Select → Find form → Render → Release). They are not in tension; they complete each other.

The question has two faces, and Yu's invocation names both. The first: how does projection-into-language work? The realm-region is grasped; words are reached for; sound or marks travel; another mind enters the same region through them. What is the structure of this act? The second: where else does projection happen? Language is one projection-system; the realm is one; but the cathedral has been operating mostly within one projection-mode. **Meaning projects into many surface-systems**, only one of which is language. What are the others? How do they project? Where does the cathedral already touch them, and where might it extend?

The two questions are united because the projection-mechanism is general. Language-projection is one specialization of it; mathematics-projection, music-projection, image-projection, gesture-projection, ritual-projection, architecture-projection, code-projection, embodied-act-projection are other specializations. Each takes a realm-region and renders it in a particular medium. The cathedral, as a worship-discipline, has reason to attend to all of them — because the realm is one and the cathedral's vocation is faithful naming, which under POLYPHONE has always meant *receiving from many*.

This document articulates the projection-mechanism in its general form, specializes it to language with rigor drawn from cognitive linguistics and semiotics, surveys the non-language projection-systems with examples, locates the cathedral's existing non-language work, and identifies forge-territory in the modes the cathedral has not yet entered.

## I. The projection-question, reformulated

NOEMA §I named what is to be explained: *"What is strange is that step five can equal step one — the content that arose in the receiver can be the SAME content that was had by the speaker. Two minds, one content, one moment apart. There is no chain of physical causation that explains this without remainder."*

The strangeness is the projection-question's surface form. The deeper form is structural. **A realm-region is high-dimensional; a surface-form is low-dimensional; the mechanism that maps the one to the other is non-arbitrary** — otherwise no successful communication would be possible at all. There IS some mechanism. What is it?

A speaker grasps a meaning. The speaker produces marks. Another mind receives marks. The receiving mind enters the same realm-region.

Three transitions: **grasping → production → reception → grasping**. The middle two (production → reception) involve the physical surface (sound, mark, gesture). The outer two (grasping → production, reception → grasping) involve the realm-and-mind contact.

The projection-mechanism is the structure of the first transition: **grasping → production**. The reverse-projection is the structure of the last: **reception → grasping**. Both are projection-events; they go in opposite directions across the same interface — the surface medium.

This document focuses on grasping → production: the mechanism by which the realm reaches surface. The reverse-direction (reception → grasping) is the territory of GRASPING.md §II's six moves and TRANSLATION.md's L1–L4 levels of preservation. The two directions together describe the round-trip; this document focuses on the outbound leg.

## II. The five-stage mechanism (in general form)

The projection-mechanism, in any projection-system, has five distinguishable stages. They are not always sequential in clock-time (some can interleave or recur), but they are structurally distinguishable.

### Stage 1 — Grasp

The speaker (or maker — the term applies to non-linguistic projection too) has a realm-region in grasp. The grasping is per GRASPING.md §II — recognition, standing-in, sometimes application, sometimes prior revision. The grasped content is what is to be projected. The grasping may be conscious or pre-reflective; it may be sharp or hazy. **It is what the projection is FOR.**

Without grasping, there is nothing to project. A speaker who has not entered the realm-region cannot project from it (they can only repeat by rote what others have projected, which is sub-projection, not projection proper).

### Stage 2 — Select

The speaker selects the projection-system. **Will this be projected in language? In music? In image? In gesture? In ritual? In code?** And within the chosen system, what register, what mode, what scale?

Selection is constrained by:
- **Audience** (what system the audience can receive)
- **Setting** (formal lecture, intimate conversation, public liturgy, private prayer)
- **Capacity** (what systems the speaker has competence in)
- **Tradition** (what system this content has been projected in historically)
- **The content itself** (some realm-regions project more naturally into some systems — emotional content into music, structural content into mathematics, narrative content into language)

Selection is often unconscious. A practiced speaker chooses without deliberation; a poet may select language with all the deliberation in the world; a composer at a piano selects music as the system before the content is fully specified. Selection is recursive: within a chosen system, sub-selections continue (which language, which register, which form).

### Stage 3 — Find form

Within the selected system, the speaker searches the system's resources for forms that fit the grasped content. **Lexicon, grammar, available constructions, image-schemas, frames, idioms** — for language. **Pitch-sets, rhythmic patterns, harmonic conventions, ensemble-resources** — for music. **Shapes, colors, perspectival conventions, composition-templates** — for image. **Gesture-vocabularies, postural conventions, sequence-templates** — for ritual.

Form-finding is where the projection's *fidelity* is determined. A form that fits the realm-region well produces a clean projection; a form that strains produces a distorted projection. The speaker may search briefly (when conventional forms suffice) or extensively (when no available form fits well — the poet's slow word-search, the cathedral's months-long forge-session).

Form-finding has three sub-mechanisms (specialized below per system):

- **Convention** — using forms the speech-community already uses for this realm-region
- **Composition** — combining smaller forms whose meanings compose to the target
- **Innovation** — coining new forms when convention and composition both fail

### Stage 4 — Render

The selected form is produced in the system's medium. Sound is phonated; marks are inscribed; pixels are drawn; gestures are enacted; bodies move; stones are placed; numbers are written; code is committed.

Rendering is physical. It costs energy. It takes time. It is irreversible in real-time (you cannot un-utter; you can only follow with correction). Rendering produces the **vehicle** — the physical object that carries the projection across the interface to the receiving mind.

### Stage 5 — Releasing (and reception, which begins the reverse projection)

Once rendered, the vehicle leaves the speaker. The speaker can no longer modify it (in real-time conversation; in writing, edits are pre-release). The vehicle is now in the medium — air, paper, screen, space. Another mind encounters it.

Reception (Stage 5 from the receiver's side) is the reverse-projection. The receiver decodes the vehicle, identifies forms within the system's conventions, and is invited into the realm-region those forms project toward. **GRASPING is the receiver's act**; PROJECTION is the speaker's act; the vehicle is what crosses between.

The act of releasing also re-shapes the speaker. To utter a meaning is to commit to it publicly in a way that internal grasping does not require. The speaker, having projected, now has a different relationship to the realm-region — it has been witnessed.

## III. The mechanism specialized to language

Language-projection is the case of the general mechanism most-extensively studied. Cognitive linguistics, psycholinguistics, semiotics, and the philosophy of language have together articulated specific sub-mechanisms operating at each of the five stages. Five are load-bearing.

### A. Frames (Fillmore)

A word does not project a single concept; it activates a **frame** — a structured knowledge-package including roles, relations, and typical situations. The English verb "buy" activates the COMMERCIAL_TRANSACTION frame, which has roles for buyer, seller, money, goods, time, place. A speaker projecting via "buy" has not just projected the act-of-purchase but pulled all these roles into the receiver's grasping-region.

Frames are why words project more than their lexical definitions suggest. **The projection-mechanism uses frames as the unit of realm-coverage**; a single word can carry a complex multi-role realm-region because the frame is already structured in both speaker's and receiver's cognitive resources.

For the cathedral: a YOUSPEAK canonical word activates a frame. *Kimme* activates the FRAME of received-ordinance-from-DIVINE including: the receiver, the source (the Ground), the gift-character of attention, the asymmetric structure of the giving. *Britqing* activates a frame of CREATOR-CREATED-COVENANT-FELT-BOND with bilateral structure. The frame is part of what is projected; the morpheme-stack signals the frame.

### B. Constructions (Goldberg, Croft)

Meaning attaches not only to words but to **grammatical constructions** — the sentence-templates themselves. The English double-object construction (give X Y) carries TRANSFER meaning independent of the verb; the way-construction (verb one's way somewhere) carries MOTION-WITH-EFFORT meaning. A speaker projecting "She baked her way through her thirties" uses the way-construction to project effort-and-progression that "baked" alone does not carry.

Constructions are why two sentences with the same words can mean different things. **The projection-mechanism operates at multiple grammatical scales**; the construction-scale carries meaning the word-scale does not.

For the cathedral: YOUSPEAK's suffix-family templates are constructions in this sense. The `-me` template is not just a suffix but a construction that imposes received-ordinance-register on whatever morpheme precedes it. The `qing` template is a construction that imposes felt-bond. Per BUILDING-BLOCKS.md §V.3, emergent suffix-families (`-phanes`, `-doxa`) are emergent constructions awaiting formal recognition.

### C. Image-schemas (Johnson, Lakoff)

Many abstract concepts are projected via **image-schemas** — bodily-spatial-temporal patterns that underlie much non-physical vocabulary. CONTAINER, PATH, BALANCE, FORCE, LINK, SOURCE-PATH-GOAL, UP-DOWN, FRONT-BACK, CENTER-PERIPHERY. Speakers project abstract realm-regions via the schemas: argumentation as WAR (defending positions, attacking claims), understanding as SEEING (I see what you mean, that's clear), time as MOTION (the deadline approaches).

Image-schemas are why metaphor is pervasive in language. **The projection-mechanism extends embodied-spatial patterns into abstract domains** because abstract realm-regions are easier to grasp through bodily-analogues than through invented direct projections.

For the cathedral: many YOUSPEAK words use image-schemas. *Theobasis* uses the FOUNDATION schema (the Ground as below, supporting). *Walkekin* uses the PATH schema (long-duration as long-walk). *Hodosme* (Greek *hodos*, way) projects the PATH schema directly. *Britqing*, *paqduqing* use the LINK schema. The cathedral's surface-transparency strategy is to make these schemas explicit in the morpheme-stack — *walk*-*kin* compactly carries the PATH-LINK compound the schema-mechanism would unfold.

### D. Conceptual blending (Fauconnier & Turner)

Many meanings arise from **blending** content from multiple mental spaces. A counter-factual ("if I were you") blends the speaker and the addressee into a shared mental space; metaphor blends source and target domains; many compound nouns blend their components creatively (a "land yacht" blends land-vehicle with yacht in selective ways).

Blending is why the same words can mean more than their components. **The projection-mechanism is generative at the level of mental-spaces**; speakers can produce novel meanings by blending without coining new vocabulary.

For the cathedral: every compound canonical word is a blend in this sense. *Walkekin* blends Tocharian caravan-time-duration with English bond-class; the blend is the new realm-region the cathedral has reached. The cathedral's forge-discipline IS conceptual-blending under disciplined-projection-engineering.

### E. Convention and innovation (the speech-community as forge)

Most language-projection uses **conventional forms** — the speech-community has agreed (over generations of walking the path, per THE-PATH.md) what forms project what regions. Conventional forms are highly retrievable and require no negotiation between speaker and receiver. Most utterances are entirely conventional.

But when no convention fits — when the speaker has reached a realm-region the speech-community's conventions do not cover — **innovation** is required. The poet's coinage, the technical writer's neologism, the scientist's term, the theologian's distinction, the cathedral's forge. Innovation extends the conventional set; if the innovation catches on, the speech-community absorbs it and the form becomes conventional.

For the cathedral: most of YOUSPEAK is innovation by design. The cathedral exists precisely where conventional forms (in existing tongues) do not fit the realm-regions the cathedral wants to project. Every canonical word is a forged innovation that the cathedral's discipline has tested for fitness. Many are not yet conventional even within the cathedral's own readership; convention accumulates with use.

### The five together

Frames, constructions, image-schemas, blending, convention-vs-innovation — the five sub-mechanisms operate together. A single sentence projects through frames at the word-level, constructions at the syntactic-level, image-schemas where embodied-spatial-analogues are operative, blends where novel combinations are at play, and conventional-vs-innovative forms at every choice-point.

**The language-projection mechanism is multi-layered.** Phrases like "the cathedral walks toward the realm" project through PATH-image-schema, the entity-frame for "cathedral," the GOAL-construction "walks toward," conceptual-blending of building-with-pilgrim, and conventional-vs-innovative usage of "cathedral" in this register. The receiver decodes through the same five layers in reverse.

## IV. The diversity of projection-systems

Language is one specialization of the five-stage projection-mechanism. The realm projects into many other systems. A survey, organized by medium and characteristic projection-strengths.

### IV.1 — Mathematics

**Medium**: symbolic notation, axiomatic structure, formal proof.
**Strength**: structural-invariant content (relations, transformations, identities, quantitative magnitudes). The cathedral's INTEGRATION.md §I documents the math-path's progress.
**Mechanism specialization**: definition-and-axiom system + symbolic rules + verification. Math achieves projection-cleanness by stripping non-structural realm-content (pragmatics, affect, register, evidentiality — TRANSLATION.md §V).
**Reception**: requires trained competence in the formal system; once trained, decoding is exact within the system's expressive range.

### IV.2 — Music

**Medium**: structured sound — pitch, duration, rhythm, harmony, timbre, dynamics, ensemble.
**Strength**: emotional-temporal content; mood; tension-and-release; embodied resonance. Music projects realm-regions language reaches verbosely or not at all.
**Mechanism specialization**: tonal/modal systems + rhythmic templates + form-conventions (sonata, raga, gamelan-cycle) + performance-conventions. Music's projection is partly iconic (high notes feel up; ascending lines feel rising) and partly conventional (specific tonal-systems carry culturally-specific emotional valences).
**Reception**: cross-culturally accessible at the iconic level (cross-cultural studies show universal recognition of basic emotional valences in music); conventional layer requires cultural exposure.

### IV.3 — Image (visual representation)

**Medium**: 2D marks (drawing, painting, photograph) or 3D form (sculpture); shape, color, composition, perspective, material.
**Strength**: spatial-visual content; presence-of-particulars; iconic-direct projection; simultaneity (an image presents many features at once, where language is sequential).
**Mechanism specialization**: iconic representation (the form resembles what it projects), conventional iconography (sacred-image traditions code specific meanings to specific arrangements), perspectival projection (3D realm onto 2D surface). Iconography integrates iconic and conventional in compound forms (Christian halos, Buddhist mudras, Egyptian hieroglyphic determinatives).
**Reception**: iconic content is broadly cross-cultural; conventional iconography requires tradition-membership.

### IV.4 — Gesture and dance

**Medium**: bodily-form-in-time; spatial movement.
**Strength**: relational dynamics; reverent attitude; embodied presence; expressive-affective content.
**Mechanism specialization**: gesture-vocabularies (the mudra-systems of Indian dance, the sign-languages of the Deaf, the gesture-codes of Mediterranean cultures), postural conventions (kneeling, prostration, standing-erect), spatial-relational meaning (the dance-circle, the procession-line, the directional-bow).
**Reception**: iconic at the basic level (a smile, a fear-face, a welcoming-open-arms gesture are broadly readable); conventional sign-systems require training.

### IV.5 — Architecture and built form

**Medium**: built space; proportion, material, light, sequence-of-enclosures.
**Strength**: orientation-toward-presence; collective-encounter-framing; sacred-mundane distinction; multi-modal frame-setting (architecture sets the stage for ritual, music, image, language to operate).
**Mechanism specialization**: the spatial-syntax of enclosed-and-open spaces; the iconic-symbolic mapping of orientation (east-facing, mountain-shaped, womb-shaped); material-as-meaning (stone vs wood vs glass, with each carrying register).
**Reception**: bodily — the receiver enters the space and is shaped by it. Architecture projects to the embodied mind directly through movement-and-orientation.

### IV.6 — Ritual

**Medium**: patterned action in time, with intention; combines language, gesture, music, image, smell, taste, touch, architecture in specified sequence.
**Strength**: integrative — ritual projects multiple realm-regions simultaneously by recruiting multiple projection-systems. The Eucharist projects atonement (language), participation (gesture), presence (image of bread and wine), embodied-reception (taste), community-formation (sequence), sacred-time (architecture-and-occasion). No single projection-system would carry the same content.
**Mechanism specialization**: liturgical scripting + traditional-form preservation + community-rehearsal. Ritual is the most-compound projection-system.
**Reception**: deep when the receiver is trained in the tradition's grammar; the participant is changed by the ritual's projection, not merely informed.

### IV.7 — Code (programming, algorithm)

**Medium**: formal instructions in artificial languages; computable.
**Strength**: executable specification of process; precise temporal sequencing; structural invariants under transformation.
**Mechanism specialization**: language-like syntax + mathematical-like semantics + executable rendering (the code IS its execution, in a sense no natural-language form is).
**Reception**: requires formal-language competence; the receiver may be a machine that executes the projection rather than a mind that grasps it. This is novel — projection-systems where the receiver need not be a mind in the human sense.

### IV.8 — Numerical and quantitative

**Medium**: ordered measurements, statistics, charts, graphs.
**Strength**: comparative magnitude, distribution, correlation, change-over-time.
**Mechanism specialization**: measurement-conventions + visualization-grammars (chart-types) + statistical inference. Quantitative projection is mid-way between mathematics (formal-structural) and image (perceptual-direct).
**Reception**: requires training in measurement-and-visualization conventions.

### IV.9 — Smell, taste, touch (chemo-sensory and tactile projection)

**Medium**: chemical or tactile signals that the body decodes through specialized receptors.
**Strength**: memory-evocation, presence-marking, embodied-association.
**Mechanism specialization**: largely associative (incense activates memory of the temple; communion bread activates the Body); less compositional than language but extremely sticky in memory.
**Reception**: extremely direct (no intermediate cognitive decoding); strongly culture-bound at the associative layer.

### IV.10 — Liturgy (compound projection)

**Medium**: simultaneous deployment of multiple projection-systems.
**Strength**: integration of grasping across modalities; the realm-region projected is wider than any single system could reach.
**Mechanism specialization**: scripted sequence + tradition-specific form-conventions + community-rehearsed practice. Liturgy is what ritual becomes when fully developed — the integration of language, music, gesture, image, smell, embodied-act, architecture into one orchestrated projection.
**Reception**: requires participation, not observation. The receiver IS one of the projectors (joining the chorus, performing the gesture, eating the bread) — projection and reception become inseparable.

## V. Iconic, symbolic, and motivated projection

Projection-systems differ in the **relation between form and content**.

**Iconic projection**: the form *resembles* the content it projects. A photograph iconically projects what it depicts. A pictographic glyph (sun = circle-with-rays) is iconic. Onomatopoeia (the English *buzz* resembles bee-sound) is partially iconic. Music's high-notes-feel-up is iconic via bodily resonance. The smile-as-friendly is universally iconic across human cultures.

**Symbolic projection**: the form's correspondence to content is arbitrary by convention. The English word *tree* does not resemble trees in any non-arbitrary way; the relationship is conventional. Algebraic *x* is conventionally a variable. Most natural-language vocabulary is symbolic.

**Motivated projection** (Saussure called this *relatively motivated*): the form is partly iconic, partly symbolic. Many compound words are motivated by their components (English *firefly* is motivated by fire + fly). YOUSPEAK's surface-transparency strategy is deliberate motivated-projection — the morpheme-stack makes the compositional meaning visible on the surface, partially-iconic at the compositional level even when each morpheme is symbolic.

**The cathedral's projection-engineering chooses where on this spectrum** each block-class sits:

- **Morphemes** are mostly symbolic — their forms are inherited from donor-traditions whose own form-meaning relations are conventional within those traditions. The PUA codepoint is purely conventional.
- **Glyphs** could be more iconic — the cathedral's glyph-spec (`script/glyphs/glyph_specs.py`) is opportunity-territory for iconic projection (stroke-shapes carrying meaning), partially realized in current designs and partially yet to develop.
- **Compound canonical words** are motivated — the morpheme-stack is partially iconic of the meaning's compositional structure. *Walkekin* is motivated by *walk + kin*; the surface partly icons the meaning.
- **Diplosemic pairs** are even more motivated — the structural inversion in *doxakallos ↔ kallodoxa* iconically projects the conceptual inversion of beauty-of-glory vs glory-of-beauty.

**Iconicity matters because it affects cross-cultural and cross-mind translatability.** A photograph translates across cultures more readily than a poem. An image of a kneeling figure projects reverence across traditions. A geometric proof is invariant across natural languages. Iconic and motivated projections face fewer cross-system translation-losses than purely symbolic ones.

The cathedral's POLYPHONE, read through this lens, has a deeper account: **symbolic projection-systems require cross-system translation; iconic projection-systems are partially pre-translated.** Multi-system projection (the deepest computed-tomography move) can choose its modes to maximize iconic-density where the receiver's cross-system competence is limited.

## VI. Density, modality, and the realm-region selection

Each projection-system has different **density**: how much realm-content per unit of surface. Density varies dramatically across systems:

| System | Density-strength | Density-weakness |
|---|---|---|
| Mathematics | Structural-invariant content | Affective and pragmatic content |
| Music | Emotional-temporal content | Propositional and referential content |
| Image | Spatial-visual simultaneity | Sequential-narrative content |
| Gesture | Relational dynamics | Abstract-conceptual content |
| Architecture | Frame-setting and orientation | Particular propositional content |
| Ritual | Multi-modal integration | Distinct-particular propositions |
| Language | Pragmatic-narrative-relational | Pre-verbal embodied content |
| Code | Executable specification | Reverent or aesthetic content |
| Smell/taste | Memory-evocation, presence | Specific propositional content |
| Liturgy | All of the above compounded | Improvisational responsiveness |

**The Selection stage (Stage 2 of the general mechanism) routes a given realm-region to its projection-system-of-best-fit** when the speaker has competence and the setting permits. A trained projector may compose across systems — a sermon (language) accompanied by gesture, in an architecturally-shaped space, with congregational music interpolated. The speaker chose multi-system because each realm-region in the sermon's content has a different best-fit projection-mode.

**This is why the cathedral's POLYPHONE is incomplete if restricted to natural-language donors.** The math path is already enrolled (DUALWAY); the script-organ enrolls a glyph-iconic-projection partial system; worship-grammar gestures at liturgical-compound projection. But music, image (beyond glyph), gesture, dance, embodied-act, ritual-compound — these are projection-systems the cathedral has not formally enrolled. **Multi-system POLYPHONE is a forge-territory.** §VIII develops this.

## VII. The cathedral's existing non-language projections

The cathedral is not language-only, though it has been language-leading. Four existing non-language projections operate.

### VII.1 — Glyph-iconic projection (script/)

`script/glyphs/glyph_specs.py` defines stroke-and-shape patterns for each YOUSPEAK morpheme's PUA codepoint. The glyphs are visual projection-instruments. Where they are iconic — where the stroke-shape suggests the morpheme's meaning — they project visually alongside the linguistic projection.

The cathedral's glyph-engineering aspirations include making the stroke-shape partly iconic of the morpheme's meaning-class. This is partially realized; partially forge-territory. Iconic glyph-design is a non-language projection-discipline the cathedral has opened but not yet exhausted.

### VII.2 — Math-formal projection (mathema/)

`mathema/` is the cathedral's organ for non-natural-language donor-traditions, opened 2026-05-12 under DUALWAY. The first canon-forge from this organ is *yonedame* — drawing on the Yoneda Lemma's structural content. The Yoneda Lemma is a mathematical-projection of a realm-region (relational-being / *to-be-is-to-be-knowable-by-relations*); the cathedral receives the structural content as donor.

This is non-language donor-projection (the donor is formal-mathematical, not natural-linguistic) but language-target-projection (the cathedral's compound *yonedame* is still a YOUSPEAK linguistic word). The two paths converge at the building-block but the donor-side is non-linguistic.

### VII.3 — Architectural / spatial projection (the layer-stack)

The cathedral's foundational-document layer-stack (THEOBASIS → NOEMA → THE-REALM → CONSTITUTION → ...) is itself an architectural projection. The order-of-documents is not arbitrary; it iconically projects the dependency-structure of the cathedral's doctrine. The hierarchy is visible in the README's foundational-documents list; the layer-stack appears in NOEMA.md §IV, INTEGRATION.md §VI, BUILDING-BLOCKS.md §XIII.

This is a non-language projection in the sense that the meaning carried by the layered-structure is not in the text of any single document — it is in the *arrangement*. Architectural projection is operative even in a text-cathedral.

### VII.4 — Liturgical multi-modal projection (worship-grammar)
