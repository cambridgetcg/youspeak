# Return-to-behold-again — the aliyah / awbah / epanodos / junrei convergence

_Four independent traditions (with two contact-linked echoes) converge on one realm-feature: the going-back to behold again is itself worship — institutionalized, sung, tiered, and built into stone — yet no tradition compact-names the transit as a portable word. The data accumulated in session 086's forge of doxapothos (246), whose refinement path 4 (companion check, Method Pattern 5) prompted this consolidation in session 087._

---

## The convergence

The feature: a **transit**, not a station — the beholder's deliberate, repeatable return-journey toward a beholding already known, undertaken without estrangement (it is not repentance), fueled by longing when the longing is alive and kept as faithfulness when it is not, terminating at the threshold of re-beholding, and directed always at the same inexhaustible beheld. The scale-range runs from the single worshipper's walk back to the chapel, through a people's calendar-pilgrimage, to the cosmos's own return-arc (the macro-pole, already partly held by anakallance for beauty itself). The poles it relates: doxapothos (the engine, in absence) and anagnoristasis (the threshold, at arrival).

| Tradition | Native term | What it attests | What it cannot say |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hebrew (Semitic) | עֲלִיָּה לָרֶגֶל aliyah la-regel; שִׁיר הַמַּעֲלוֹת shir ha-ma'alot; רְאִיָּה re'iyah; לְבַקֵּר levaqer (Ps 27:4) | the return-transit as commanded worship, in beholding-vocabulary (Ex 34:23–24 yera'eh/yir'eh — "appear before / see the face"; Mishnah Hagigah 1:1 names the pilgrimage-duty "seeing"); a fifteen-song repertoire written for the road itself; the anchor-verse pairs beholding with visiting-again | institution-locked (Jerusalem, three festivals); no portable noun for the transit as such — the Psalter circumlocutes it from the road |
| Sufi Arabic (Semitic; independent of the Hebrew institution) | إنابة inabah / أوبة awbah beyond توبة tawbah; زيارة ziyarah; أوّاب awwāb (Q 38:17, 30, 44) | the return tiered **past repentance**: tawbah of the common (from sin), inabah of the friends (from heedlessness), awbah of the prophets — return with nothing to repent of (al-Qushayrī, Risāla, bāb al-tawba); awwāb, "ever-returning," as pure praise-epithet | person-directed (to the Beloved), not beholding-marked; names the returner's rank, not the road |
| Persian Sufi (Indo-European; contact-linked with Arabic Sufi — count as one with it) | the ney of Masnavi I.1–4; رجوع ruju' ila Allah | all wayfaring is return-shaped because of the prior beholding at alast (Q 7:172): the reed cries for the reed-bed; "everyone far from his origin seeks again the day of his union" | the prior-beholding trace (athaumasma's slot) and the road are fused; no transit-word separable from the metaphysic |
| Greek, Platonic (Indo-European) | ἐπάνοδος epanodos (Rep. 521c, 532b); ἄνοδος (517b) | the way-up-and-back distinguished from the turning (μεταστροφή) in one clause — state vs transit drawn in the donor-text; true philosophy itself defined as epanodos to being; the cave-economy cyclic (520c: down again, up again, by turns) | first-ascent framed; the iterativity is in the polis-economy, not the lexeme; beholding-target (the Good) contextual, unmarked |
| Greek, epic (same tongue — counted with Platonic as one witness) | νόστος nostos | the return-journey as a whole sung genre; nostimon ēmar, the day of return, as the hero's telos | backward-facing (home-as-it-was); completed once; English took only the ache-half (nostalgia, 1688) and dropped the road |
| Japanese Buddhist (Japonic — fully independent) | 巡礼 junrei; the Shikoku henro | the *again* built into the path's own geometry: an 88-station closed loop where finishing is arriving at the start; walked repeatedly, some pilgrims dozens of circuits | circuit-specific practice-word; does not mark the beholding-direction or survive export from the institution |
| Latin scholastic (contact-linked with Greek Neoplatonism — discounted) | exitus-reditus | the return as cosmic architecture: the Summa's whole plan is the going-out-from and return-to GoD | macro-scale only; the single beholder's repeated feet are below its resolution |

Genetic-independence note: Hebrew and Arabic are sister-Semitic but the witnessed institutions are independent (Temple foot-festival vs Sufi station-psychology — no contact-path for the *forms*); Persian Sufi is counted inside the Arabic witness; epic and Platonic Greek are one tongue, one witness; Latin reditus descends from Greek epistrophē through Neoplatonism and is discounted. The strongest fully-independent triple-witness: **aliyah la-regel + awbah + junrei** — Semitic institution, Semitic-Sufi psychology, Japonic architecture, with Platonic Greek as a fourth philosophical angle. Per THE-REALM, four independent angles give a solid reverse-projection of the region.

## Cross-modal witnesses

| Mode | Tradition / practice | Witness-form | What it attests |
|---|---|---|---|
| sonic | Hebrew Psalter — Shir ha-Ma'alot (Pss 120–134) | a fifteen-song repertoire composed for the transit, sung while walking up | that the road has its own liturgy distinct from the arrival's — the transit is worship with its own music, not silence before worship |
| spatial-architectural | Second-Temple architecture — Mishnah Middot 2:5 with Sukkah 5:4 | fifteen physical steps between courts, corresponding one-to-one to the fifteen road-songs, on which the Levites stood and sang | the transit poured into stone: the building itself counts the return, step by step, fusing the sonic and spatial witnesses |
| spatial-geometric | Shikoku henro circuit | the pilgrimage-path closed into a loop, so the way's geometry contains the *again* | iterativity witnessed without any word at all — the path's shape is the lexeme |

Modal note (per POLYMORPHIA): these are projection-angles, not illustrations. The step-architecture and the loop-geometry assert the feature's structure — countability, iterativity, transit-with-its-own-dignity — in media that cannot quote the tongue-witnesses; the correspondence (fifteen steps ↔ fifteen songs) is itself a cross-modal translation made by the tradition, not by this file.

## What no single tradition compact-names

Hebrew names the institution and the songs but not a portable transit-noun; Sufi Arabic names the returner's rank (awwāb) and the visit (ziyarah) but not the beholding-directed road; Plato names the road but not its iteration; Japanese builds the iteration but lexicalizes only the practice; Latin holds the macro-arc only. Each register splits apart what the convergence shows as one: **road + again + beholding-target + worship-dignity**. Modern English is the outlier that splits it furthest — "practice," "observance," "going back," and the pejorative "going through the motions" for precisely the case (the dry return) that the traditions most honor. The YOUSPEAK target is the reverse-projection of these shadows: a single transit-word for the going-back-to-behold-again.

## The theological claim

(1) Temple foot-festivals, Sufi return-tiers, Platonic cave-roads, and Japanese circuit-geometry share no common naming-source: the convergence is parallel-witness, not borrowing. (2) Under THE-REALM, each term is one projection-angle on one region; four independent arrivals evidence that the region — return-as-worship, the transit with its own dignity — is structurally real; cardinality 4 is good-grade disclosure-density (3-witness good; 7 strong). (3) Of POLYPHONIA's five convergence-sources: realm-unity and shared embodiment (creatures with feet, on roads, in calendars) are both clearly operating; contact is operating only on the discounted edges (Persian-Arabic, Latin-Greek) and has been removed from the count; formal-parallel and theophanic invitation are not claimed — the evidence does not require them.

Where traditions converge, the self-disclosure has multiple witness: that the going-back is itself received as worship — that the door stands open to the *returner*, not only to the penitent — is attested four ways. This file is a witness-deposition, not a comparative-religion exercise.
