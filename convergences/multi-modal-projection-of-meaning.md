## 2. Hindu — rūpa across multiple projection-media

Sanskrit *rūpa* (रूप): form, appearance, manifestation. The same term names many things in Hindu tradition:

- The **murti**'s rūpa — the sculpted/painted form of the deity, made specifically to enable *darśan* (sacred-seeing)
- The **mantra**'s rūpa — the sonic-formal projection (a mantra is not arbitrary syllables but a specific rūpa of cosmic vibration)
- The **yantra**'s rūpa — the geometric form (like the Sri Yantra) projecting cosmic structure
- The **mudrā**'s rūpa — the formal hand-gesture projecting specific cosmic-power
- The **temple**'s rūpa — vāstu-śāstra prescribes the formal-architectural projection
- The **dance**'s rūpa — the *Nātya-śāstra* prescribes formal projections (the various dance traditions: Bharata Natyam, Odissi, Kathak)

In Hindu worship, all rūpa-projections coordinate: a *pūjā* engages murti + mantra + mudrā + offering + temple-form + appropriate time-cycle simultaneously. The rūpa-doctrine implicitly names what POLYMORPHIA explicitly articulates.

**Register**: comprehensive-Hindu, multi-modal-coordination, rūpa-as-formal-projection.

## 3. Buddhist — rūpa and Buddha-rūpa across media

Buddhist tradition inherits and develops Sanskrit *rūpa* technically:

- **rūpa-skandha** — the first of the five aggregates that constitute experience; form-as-material-content
- **buddha-rūpa** — the form of the Buddha (depicted in statues, paintings, thangkas); not merely illustrative but participatory aids to meditation
- **mantra-rūpa** — the formal sonic projection (Tibetan Buddhism develops mantra-projection extensively)
- **mudrā** — formal hand-gestures in iconography and ritual
- **mandala-rūpa** — the geometric form projecting cosmic-soteriological structure
- **stupa-rūpa** — the architectural form projecting the Buddha's body and the cosmic structure

In Vajrayāna practice, multi-modal rūpa-projection is the standard mode: visualization-of-buddha-rūpa + mantra-rūpa + mudrā + ritual-procedural + temple/stupa-projection coordinate as one practice. POLYMORPHIA names this structural pattern.

**Register**: Buddhist-soteriological, Vajrayāna-comprehensive, rūpa-as-practice-form.

## 4. Sufi — ṣūra and the multi-modal Sufi practice

Arabic *ṣūra* (صورة): form, image, appearance. Sufi tradition develops the *ṣūra–maʿnā* (form-meaning) pair: every outer form (*ṣūra*) participates in but does not exhaust an inner meaning (*maʿnā*). The Sufi master's task is to lead the disciple from the outer ṣūra to the inner maʿnā without devaluing the ṣūra (which is the necessary aperture).

Sufi practice projects across media: *dhikr* (rhythmic-sonic-breath remembrance), *samāʿ* (musical audition), *qasāʾid* (poetic recitation), *raqs* (dance, especially Mevlevi whirling), Arabic calligraphy (the *ṣūra* of the divine names rendered visibly), architectural projection (the *dergah*, the *maqām* of the saint), embodied posture in prayer (sajda — prostration), communal-meal traditions. Each Sufi *tarīqa* (order) emphasizes specific combinations.

Ibn ʿArabī's doctrine of *al-aʿyān al-thābitah* (the fixed essences) holds that each created thing is a specific *ṣūra* of a particular Divine Name's manifestation. The multi-modal projection-pattern is theologically embedded in Sufi metaphysics.

**Register**: Sufi-mystical, multi-modal-tarīqa, ṣūra-maʿnā-correlation.

## 5. Confucian — li, yue, wén as coordinated multi-modal practice

Confucian tradition explicitly names three coordinated projection-modes:

- **lǐ** (禮) — rites, ritual procedure (the procedural-multi-modal medium)
- **yuè** (樂) — music (the sonic medium)
- **wén** (文) — writing, culture, refined-pattern (the linguistic-visual medium)

The *Liji* (Book of Rites) and the *Yueji* (Record of Music) develop the doctrine that *lǐ* and *yuè* together produce harmony in the polity. The gentleman is formed by all three modes; civilization depends on their integration. The Confucian Six Arts (rites, music, archery, charioteering, writing, mathematics) integrate multiple projection-media into one gentleman-forming curriculum.

The doctrine is Confucianism's explicit articulation of POLYMORPHIA. The Chinese tradition has named what the cathedral now compact-names.

**Register**: Confucian-cultivation, lǐ-yuè-wén-triad, gentleman-formation.

## 9. Jewish — multi-modal observance across speech, deed, sign, time, space
