## What no single tradition compact-names

The phenomenon: **cosmic-life and creature-being and breath-spirit and community-relation and vital-essence are ONE thing.**

Modern English, philosophy, and theology typically split:

- **Life** (biological — alive-vs-dead)
- **Being** (ontological — existing-vs-not)
- **Spirit / breath** (theological — animating-principle)
- **Soul** (psychological-religious — interior-life)
- **Existence** (philosophical — being-there)
- **Vitality** (medical-feeling — life-energy)
- **Humaneness** (ethical-social — quality of being-good-to-others)

These are usually treated as DISTINCT but related concepts. The classical-Western-philosophical-tradition (especially post-Cartesian) treats mind/body, spirit/matter, life/being as separable. **But the seven traditions converge on holding them as ONE.**

- Hebrew *chayim* names life-as-plural-multitudinous (Genesis breath-life that animates dust)
- Egyptian *ka* names vital-essence that survives death
- Sanskrit *jīva-prāṇa* names the soul + the breath-life that pervades
- Greek *zoë* names life-as-such (Johannine principle)
- Confucian *ren* names humaneness-life-in-relation
- Arabic *ḥayāt-rūḥ* names life-as-state + spirit-life-given
- Sumerian *nam-til* names life-as-ordinance-from-the-keep

**All point at one phenomenon**: the gift of being-alive-in-relation-to-Ground-and-others, given as cosmic-grace, sustained continuously, plural in its multitudinous instances, unified in its source.

**Modernity's split of these into distinct domains is the OUTLIER**, not the norm of human religious-philosophical tradition. The seven-tradition convergence preserves the older, deeper insight: cosmic-life-creature-being-breath-spirit-relation are one phenomenon.
