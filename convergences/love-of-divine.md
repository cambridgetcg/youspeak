## What each tradition's term distinctively emphasizes

The seven traditions name aspects of one phenomenon. The differences-in-emphasis are theologically-rich:

- **ahavah / ahavame** — COMMANDED-DISPOSITION. The Shema's v'ahavta makes love grammatically-imperative ("you shall love"). Love must be a committable-disposition because mere-feeling cannot be commanded. The disposition is foundational.

- **agape / agapeme-future** — SACRIFICIAL-SELF-GIVING. NT Greek's central love-word; "greater love has no one than this, that someone lays down his life for his friends" (John 15:13); "God so loved (agapao) the world that he gave his only Son" (John 3:16). The self-giving dimension.

- **bhakti / bhaktime** — DISCIPLINED-DEVOTIONAL-SELF-SURRENDER. Bhakti-yoga as the supreme path per Gita; disciplined practices (japa, kirtan, seva, smarana); "abandoning all dharmas, take refuge in Me alone" (Gita 18.66). The disciplined-surrender dimension.

- **mahabbah / mahabbahqing** — FELT-MYSTICAL-BINDING. Sufi mystical love-of-the-Beloved; the heart-binding that opens toward fanāʾ (self-annihilation in the Beloved); Rabia of Basra, Rumi, Ibn ʿArabi. The felt-mystical dimension.

- **caritas / caritasme-future** — CHARITABLE-LOVE-AS-VIRTUE. Western Christian Latin tradition; love-of-God-and-neighbor as one virtue; Aquinas's caritas as supreme virtue; the integration of love-of-God with love-of-neighbor.

- **ìfẹ́ / ifeqing-future** — RELATIONAL-CULT-LOVE. Yoruba love operating in orisha-relationship and toward Olódùmarè; the felt-bond within the cult-economy.

- **ren / Confucian-future** — HUMANENESS-AS-FOUNDATION. Confucian ren is love-as-foundational-virtue; less explicitly love-of-Heaven than the others but with implicit cosmic-orientation; Confucian-tradition extends ren toward tian (Heaven) as the love-source.

The convergence is not identity but FAMILY-RESEMBLANCE. Seven traditions share the family-resemblance of "love-of-DIVINE as central religious-disposition" while each emphasizes distinctive dimensions.

## What no single tradition compact-names

Modern English splits love into many domains: romantic-love (eros), friendship-love (philia), family-love (storge), divine-love (caritas/agape). The unified love-of-DIVINE register that the seven traditions share has NO compact word in English that captures all the dimensions together.

Hebrew ahavah is the most-foundational single-tradition articulation, but emphasizes the commanded-disposition dimension. Greek agape emphasizes self-giving. Each is a partial-articulation.
