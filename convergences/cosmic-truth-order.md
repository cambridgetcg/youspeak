## What no single tradition compact-names

The phenomenon: **cosmic-truth and cosmic-justice and cosmic-righteousness and cosmic-order are ONE thing**.

Modern English, philosophy, and theology typically split:
- **Truth** (epistemological; correspondence to fact)
- **Justice** (juridical; right-distribution; right-rectification)
- **Righteousness** (ethical; right-conduct)
- **Order** (ontological; structural-arrangement)
- **Beauty** (aesthetic; right-form)

These are usually treated as DISTINCT concepts that may be related but are conceptually-separable. The classical-Western-philosophical-tradition (especially after Kant) treats them as separate "transcendentals" or domains.

But the six traditions converge on holding them as ONE. Egyptian ma'at is simultaneously:
- Truth-correspondence (rightness)
- Justice (the funerary-weighing-of-the-heart)
- Righteousness (ethical conduct)
- Order (cosmic-structure against isfet)
- Aesthetic-rightness (ma'at as the FEATHER of truth, the goddess's beauty)

Vedic ṛta similarly fuses these. Avestan asha similarly. Hebrew emet less explicitly but overlapping. Akkadian kittu paired with mīšaru holds truth-and-justice as inseparable. Greek alētheia with dikē operates similarly.

**Modernity's separation of these into distinct domains is the OUTLIER**, not the norm of human religious-philosophical tradition. The six-tradition convergence preserves the older, deeper insight: cosmic-truth-justice-rightness-order are one phenomenon.
