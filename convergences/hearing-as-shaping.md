# Receptive-hearing-as-shaping

_The phenomenon attested across Akkadian, Hebrew, Arabic, Aramaic, Sufi, Buddhist, and Greek-Christian traditions: hearing-truly is not passive perception but receptive-shaping — the act of listening is already the beginning of being-changed by what is heard. The Shema-tradition is the most-named instance, but six other traditions name the same register. English splits it across "hear / listen / heed / obey." YOUSPEAK compact-names it shemme._

## The convergence

| Tradition | Term | Family | Partial-dimension named |
|---|---|---|---|
| Akkadian | *šēmu* (𒈠𒁇) | Afro-Asiatic East-Semitic | to hear/listen/obey as one fused verb; ~4500 years of textual attestation; Semitic-foundational |
| Hebrew | *šāmaʿ* / שְׁמַע | Afro-Asiatic West-Semitic | the imperative *šǝmaʿ* opens the Shema (Deut 6:4) — the foundational Jewish confession is a hearing-call |
| Arabic | *samiʿa* / *as-Samīʿ* | Afro-Asiatic Semitic | the divine name *as-Samīʿ* ("The All-Hearing") is one of the 99 Names; the Quranic *samʿan wa ṭāʿatan* names hearing-and-obeying as one act |

| Sufi | *samāʿ* / سماع | Arabic-derived register | ritual-listening practice of Sufi orders; spiritual-aesthetic-receptive listening |
| Buddhist | *śravaṇa* / *śrāvaka* | Indo-European Indo-Aryan | the *śrāvaka* (hearer-disciple) is a foundational category of dharma-receiving; receiving-by-hearing |
| Greek (NT) | *akouō* / ἀκούω | Indo-European Hellenic | Jesus's repeated "he who has ears, let him hear" implies hearing-with-spiritual-following |

**Cognate-network density**: the first four entries (Akkadian, Hebrew, Arabic, Aramaic) are Semitic-cognates from the common root *šm'*. The Sufi *samāʿ* derives from Arabic. The Buddhist *śravaṇa* and Greek *akouō* are independent IE forms. So: four Semitic-cognate forms + two independent IE forms = seven attestations across two major language families plus a derivative practice register.

This is **the densest single-property convergence in YOUSPEAK** as of Session 007: seven traditions across two language families plus derivative practice, all naming the same phenomenon.

## What no single tradition compact-names

The phenomenon: **hearing-truly is not passive perception; it is receptive-listening that is already the beginning of being-shaped by what is heard.**

- English "hear" — auditory perception only; lacks shaping-implication
- English "listen" — implies attentiveness but not commitment
- English "heed" — captures response-after-hearing but not the fused-as-one-act register
- English "obey" — captures active-response but not receptive-listening that precedes
- *šēmu / šāmaʿ / samiʿa / šǝmaʿ* in their Semitic register fuse all four into one phenomenon

The traditions converge on: **hearing-is-shaping**. None of the modern major languages compact-names this. YOUSPEAK can.
