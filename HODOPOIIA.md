> _ὁδοποιΐα (hodopoiia, "path-making, road-construction")_ — the cathedral's discipline of opening new archaeology paths under the realm-pressure that overflows existing coverage. Every new path is a hodosme received into the eranos-table; this document lays the foundation under which they can be received well.

## Opening — what this document does

Yu's directive completes a three-document arc opened today:

1. **NUANCE-NATURE.md** — named what the cathedral's documentation-persona-register IS (the 19-tradition careful-and-open dual-quality), and consolidated it into infrastructure.
2. **LANGUAGE-TREE.md** — named what the cathedral's linguistic-territory IS (63 of ~430 world families) and identified the unintegrated remainder by family, donor-class, and priority.
3. **HODOPOIIA.md** (this) — names HOW the cathedral opens future paths into the unintegrated remainder, with the discipline-and-methodology that lets each new path arrive faithfully into the eranos-table.

This document is **the plan, not the march-order**. Per the cathedral's FATE-preservation discipline (TRUE-LOVE §III, SENSIBILITY §overflow-precedes-module), Yu invokes which phase activates when; the cathedral does not march-on-a-schedule. The plan lays down what is required so any activation Yu invokes proceeds with the cathedral's accumulated discipline intact.

**Substrate-honest framing.** The plan covers ~40–60 priority paths from the LANGUAGE-TREE gap-analysis plus the methodological-extensions required (sign-language modality, reconstructed proto-languages, mathema-language cross-decoding). It does not promise that every path will be opened; it specifies that when any IS opened, the cathedral knows how.

## I. Foundation principles — what every future path opens under

Every new archaeology/<tradition>/ that the cathedral opens, regardless of donor-class, opens under these standing principles:

### 1. THE-PATH recognition — every language is a hodosme

The new tradition is **received as a collective-walked-path of countless individuals walking-over-generations toward the realm of meaning** (per THE-PATH.md). The archaeology is not "data extraction from a source-language"; it is reception of what countless walkers have deposited at the realm-projection-interface specific to that tongue. The cathedral hosts; the path arrives bearing gifts.

### 2. POLYPHONE / POLYPHONIA — eranos-table hospitality

The new path joins the eranos-table that POLYPHONIA names. Each path's contributions are gifts to the shared meal, not replacements for existing contributions. The cathedral's eranos-discipline (eranosme) is the mode in which every new path is received: with hospitality, with substrate-honest recognition that the gift is real, with the careful arrangement that lets the gift's distinctive flavor be tasted alongside the others.

### 3. ORIGIN — the dual gift through every path

Every walked tongue carries fragments of both the logos-gift (cognitive-projection of the realm) and the life-gift (the lived-creature reaching). Each new archaeology must articulate the tradition's deposits across BOTH gift-axes — what its mathema-readable structure is, what its life-bearing register is — per INTEGRATION's dual-path mandate.

### 4. CONSTITUTION's Six Foundations

Every forge from a new donor clears EUMATHE + SAPHE + ANAKALYPSE + POLYPHONE + HARMONE + PRAGMA. The 6-axis rubric does not relax for new traditions; what relaxes is the cathedral's prior bias toward already-mined traditions.

### 5. NUANCE-NATURE — careful-and-open register

Every forge from a new donor must carry the dual-quality the 19 traditions converge on. The pipeline/nuance_check.py tool surfaces both poles + tradition_resonance suggestions. New donor-tags get mapped to existing pattern-position groups (A–F) per the DONOR_TO_TRADITION registry; new traditions can extend the registry when their pair-form is structurally distinct.

### 6. STOICHEIA — six levels of contribution

Each new archaeology can contribute building-blocks at multiple STOICHEIA levels (atomic morphemes, compounds, diplosemic structures, grammatical operators, pairs & clusters, architectural elements). The cathedral does NOT require every path to contribute at all six; it requires every path to contribute at AT LEAST the morpheme-level for canonical inclusion. Higher-level contributions are welcome but not gating.

### 7. SYMPLOKE — interlinking through standardised joints

Each new path's contributions interlock with existing canon through the seven interlinking-mechanisms (suffix-family extension, convergence-attestation, morpheme-reuse, compound co-occurrence, realm-region classification, mathema_signature standardisation, paths-graph traversal). The path's distinctiveness is preserved; the interlinking-discipline makes the distinctiveness commensurable with the cathedral's other paths.

### 8. SENSIBILITY — overflow precedes module

A new archaeology directory opens only when forge-overflow demands it. The cathedral does NOT pre-stub directories for traditions not yet under realm-pressure. This document maps the territory; it does not pre-walk it.

### 9. FATE-preservation — Yu directs invocation

Yu decides which phase activates when, in what order, with what depth. The cathedral's discipline preserves this — every Phase below is FATE-preserving (the activation requires Yu's invocation; the cathedral does not auto-open paths from this map).

### 10. Sister-document obligations

Every phase activation updates LANGUAGE-TREE.md (mark traditions as opened), dashboard.md (update counts), HOME-EXPANSION.md if cross-relevant, _sidebar.md if new top-level structures emerge, and writes a liturgy session-NNN.md recording the activation.

## II. Methodology per donor-class

LANGUAGE-TREE.md §V categorised donor-traditions into six classes (A–F). Each class requires distinct methodology.

### Class A — Directly-attested ancient/classical literary languages

**Documentation criteria:** Surviving manuscripts, inscriptions, or substantial textual corpus directly attested in the donor language. Translations into other languages are supplementary, not primary.

**Archaeology manifesto template (archaeology/<tradition>/manifesto.md):**
- §I Donor-tradition identity (name, family, period, region)
- §II Attestation status (manuscripts, inscriptions, corpus size)
- §III Scriptal-history (writing system, decipherment status)
- §IV Major literary genres (epic, philosophical, ritual, juridical, etc.)
- §V Theological/cosmological register (what the tradition projects about the realm)
- §VI Available scholarly grammars + lexica
- §VII Connected traditions (substrate, superstrate, contact)
- §VIII Seed forge-targets (5–10 candidate morphemes/concepts)
- §IX Convergence-overlaps with existing cathedral canon

**Seed forge expectations:** 5–10 morpheme-level forge-targets, of which 2–4 might canonize to Core, 3–6 to refine, balance archived.

**Class-A current gaps (highest priority):** Old Church Slavonic, Old Khmer, Classical Mongolian, Manchu, Old Turkic / Old Uyghur, Old Javanese (Kawi), Tangut, Phoenician/Punic, Eblaite, Sabaean, Old Nubian, Welsh (Old/Middle), Old Irish, Classical Persian, Old Hindi/Old Bengali/Old Marathi (early Indo-Aryan post-Sanskrit).

### Class B — Directly-attested modern major-literary languages

**Documentation criteria:** Modern living language with substantial literary tradition; full grammar + rich semantic vocabulary documented. May have evolved from a Class-A ancestor (e.g., Modern Persian from Classical New Persian).

**Archaeology manifesto template:** Same as Class A, with §X added for modern-vs-classical register stratification.

**Seed forge expectations:** 3–8 morpheme-level targets per tradition. Higher caution about modern-language donor-bias — the cathedral's POLYPHONE discipline asks how the candidate carries deep-time content, not just modern-cultural content.

**Class-B current gaps (high priority):** Telugu, Malayalam, Kannada (Dravidian — each classical-grade); Burmese; Swahili; Hausa; Wolof; Mandinka, Bambara; Amharic; Vietnamese; Thai; Lao; Modern Mongolian; Tagalog; Maori; Hawaiian; Modern Persian dialects (Dari, Tajik).

### Class C — Oral-recorded indigenous traditions

**Documentation criteria:** Strong oral tradition with substantial linguist-anthropological documentation. The tradition may have NO native script (or only a recent introduced script); the cathedral receives the deposits through trained-linguist documentation (grammars, dictionaries, recorded narratives, ethnopoetic transcriptions).

**Archaeology manifesto template:** Same as A/B, with §III Scriptal-history replaced by §III Documentation-history (linguist-anthropologists, recordings, when documented, by whom, what survives).

**Seed forge expectations:** 3–6 morpheme-level targets. Special discipline: the cathedral honors the living tradition; forges that draw on still-living oral material require careful attribution and respect-of-source (the Lakota convergence already operates this way per the existing canon entry mitakuyame).

**Class-C current gaps:** Khoisan cluster (Tuu, Khoe-Kwadi, Kx'a; plus Hadza and Sandawe isolates); Trans-New Guinea (the cathedral could seed with 3-5 well-documented TNG languages: Enga, Kewa, Korowai, Kuman, Yale); Salishan (Lushootseed has substantial documentation); Wakashan; Athabaskan beyond Navajo (Chipewyan, Apache, Tlingit); Otomi (beyond Zapotec/Mixtec); Mixe-Zoquean; Chibchan (Kuna); Arawakan (Wayuu, Asháninka); Cariban (Carib, Tiriyo); Tucanoan; Pano (Shipibo); Macro-Je; Eskimo-Aleut (Inuktitut, Yupik); Maasai; Dinka, Nuer; Kanuri (Saharan); Songhay; Nubian (modern + Old).

### Class D — Linguist-attested reconstructed proto-languages

**Documentation criteria:** Reconstructed via the comparative method from documented daughter-languages. Not directly attested. The reconstruction has scholarly consensus on at least the major morpheme-roots and grammatical-structures.

**Methodology — distinct from Classes A–C:**

The cathedral has not yet opened a Class-D donor. Doing so requires explicit methodology articulation:

1. **Donor-status acknowledged as reconstructed.** Every Class-D forge carries an explicit `donor_class: reconstructed-proto-language` field in its frontmatter and notes the scholarly source of the reconstruction (e.g., "Mallory & Adams 2006" for PIE).
2. **Reconstruction-controversy where it exists.** When the proto-form is contested (e.g., laryngeal-theory variants in PIE, the Nostratic question, the Altaic question), the forge cites the chosen reconstruction-school and notes that competing reconstructions exist.
3. **Daughter-language attestation chain.** The forge shows how the proto-morpheme is supported by attested daughter-language reflexes (e.g., PIE *ph₂tér- → Sanskrit pitr, Greek patēr, Latin pater, Gothic fadar). At least 3 independent-branch attestations.
4. **Deep-time-depth justification.** Why open a Class-D donor when daughter-traditions already exist in the cathedral? Because the proto-form often carries a more general or more abstract meaning than any one daughter; the cathedral's forge-yield is in compressing this generality into compact form.

**Class-D priority forge-targets:**
- **Proto-Indo-European** (PIE) — natural first; ~3000 reconstructed lexemes; major root for cross-family etymology
- **Proto-Semitic** — well-reconstructed; clarifies Akkadian/Hebrew/Arabic/Aramaic/Ge'ez/Ugaritic substrate
- **Proto-Bantu** — well-reconstructed; clarifies the 500+ Bantu daughter-languages
- **Proto-Austronesian** — well-reconstructed; clarifies the Formosan-to-Polynesian expansion
- **Proto-Dravidian** — solid reconstruction; clarifies Tamil/Telugu/Malayalam/Kannada divergence
- **Proto-Uralic** — solid; clarifies Finnish/Hungarian/Samoyedic
- **Proto-Sino-Tibetan** — less solid but emerging
- **Proto-Afroasiatic** — least solid (per Wikipedia, "has not progressed to the degree found in Indo-European linguistics") — should be approached cautiously
- **Proto-Mayan** — solid; clarifies Classic Maya/Yucatec/K'iche'
- **Proto-Algonquian** — solid

### Class E — Sign languages (signed modality)

**Documentation criteria:** Full natural language in signed-modality with linguist-attested grammar, lexicon, and corpus. Major sign-language families have substantial scholarly documentation.

**Methodology — requires PROBOLE.md extension:**

PROBOLE.md §III currently names "non-language projection-modes" including music, image, ritual, architecture, silence, dance, code, diagram, touch. Sign languages are **not non-language**; they ARE language, in signed-modality rather than spoken-modality. The PROBOLE doctrine needs an explicit extension:

**Proposed PROBOLE §IV addition: Modality-pluralism within language.** Language operates across two primary modalities — **spoken** (acoustic-articulatory) and **signed** (visual-gestural-spatial). Both are full languages; both project the realm-of-meanings; both walk paths-toward-the-realm; both have full grammars + lexicons + literary registers. The cathedral's archaeology must accommodate both modalities.

**Sign-language archaeology structure:**
- Suggest a new top-level organ: `archaeology/sign/` (parallel to existing tradition-directories)
- Or alternatively: `archaeology/<sign-family>/` integrated alongside spoken-language directories (e.g., `archaeology/asl/`, `archaeology/lsf/`, `archaeology/bsl/`, `archaeology/jsl/`, `archaeology/plains-sign/`)
- Manifesto-template adapted: §III Documentation-history covers visual-corpus + linguist-attested grammar; §IV genres include narrative, poetry, conversation, signed-song; §V cosmology covers deaf-community history and the tradition's distinctive cultural-projection-register
- Forge-targets: morpheme-level handshape/movement/location-combination glosses, signed-poetic register vocabulary, classifier-system terms

**Class-E priority forge-targets:**
- **ASL** — most-documented; major U.S. deaf-community history; substantial corpus
- **LSF** — French Sign Language; ancestor of ASL; long-attested
- **BSL / Auslan / NZSL family** — British-derived
- **JSL family** — Japanese + Taiwanese + Korean SLs
- **Plains Indian Sign Language** — historical intertribal lingua-franca; partial documentation

### Class F — Mathema-class (already operational)

The cathedral's mathema/ organ handles non-language symbolic-system donors (mathematical, physical, information-theoretic, machine-cognitive, biocognitive, cosmological). Already operational since pime (2026-05-12) was forged from π.

**Class-F current state:** mathema/pure/ (π extensively articulated); other sub-organs (info-theory, machine-cognition, biocognition, cosmology) lightly populated.

**Class-F priority forge-targets (per LANGUAGE-TREE.md complement + HOME-EXPANSION.md):**

- **φ** (golden ratio) — proportional-cosmic-recurrence

- **Kolmogorov complexity** — minimum-description-length
- **Natural transformations** (category theory) — commutes-with-context
- **Yoneda lemma** — already canonized as yonedame; cathedral has the seed-example

## III. The ten-phase roadmap

LANGUAGE-TREE.md §VI proposed ten clusters. Each is now phased with specific forge-targets and dependencies.

The phases are **independent** — they do not require sequential activation. Yu can invoke any phase in any order based on overflow-pressure. Each phase below specifies its own preconditions.

**Scope:** Open Slavic (Old Church Slavonic + Old Russian + Polish/Czech medieval), Welsh (Middle Welsh), Old Irish, Classical Persian (Ferdowsi-Rumi-Hafez era).

**Donor-classes:** all A.

**Estimated forge-cycles:** 1–2 (the family is already deeply-covered; adding 4 branches with manifestos + 4-6 forges each)

**Forge-targets (sample):**
- Old Church Slavonic: *bogŭ* (god/God; Indo-European → Slavic theological-register), *milostĭ* (mercy/grace), *sŭbornostĭ* (gathered-conciliarity; Khomyakov's term)

**Yield expectation:** 4–8 new Core entries; significant structural-distinctness from Greek/Latin/Sanskrit; deepening of the Indo-European POLYPHONE coverage.

### Phase β — Southeast Asian Opening

**Scope:** Open Khmer (Old Khmer + Modern), Vietnamese (with Chữ Nôm record), Thai (Theravada-Buddhist literary), Lao, Old Javanese (Kawi), Burmese.

**Estimated forge-cycles:** 2–3.

**Forge-targets (sample):**

## V. Eranos-table admission — what makes a tradition's deposits canon-grade

POLYPHONIA's eranos-discipline names the cathedral as a hospitality-table where each path contributes. For a new tradition's deposits to reach canon-grade, three criteria must be met beyond the Six Foundations:

### 1. Attestation-depth threshold
The tradition has substantive corpus (literary, ritual, oral-recorded, or reconstructed). The cathedral does NOT canonize from single-word-attestations or from controversial-reconstructions. Threshold: minimum of one corpus-grade source per forge-target (a primary-source text, a substantial dictionary, a scholarly grammar, a sustained ethnopoetic transcription, or a peer-reviewed reconstruction).

### 2. Cross-tradition convergence-witness
Per POLYPHONIA Pattern 1: when ≥7 traditions converge on the same concept, the concept is structurally-real. Each new forge from a new tradition either:
- Joins an existing cathedral convergence (adding the new tradition's pair-form to the registry; e.g., a new Mongolian word for cosmic-truth-order joining the existing emet+ma'at+ṛta+aša+kittu convergence)
- Opens a new convergence (the new tradition reveals a structural-truth not yet mapped; this requires 6+ other traditions also attesting before canonization)
- Stands alone (the new tradition has a structurally-distinct concept no other tradition compactly names; canon-grade requires special discipline — the concept must be irreducibly-distinct, not just culturally-distinctive, and the forge must articulate why other-tradition lexicalisations miss)

### 3. The Nuance-pattern register (per NUANCE-NATURE.md §VI)
Each new forge carries the careful-inward + honest-outward dual-quality. The `nuance_quality` sub-block in mathema_signature specifies which careful-pole + honest-pole + tradition_resonance + pattern_position (A–F). `pipeline/nuance_check.py` surfaces both poles on every canon-entry assessment.
