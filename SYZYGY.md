## I. The syzygy — what union is named

**Syzygy** (Greek συζυγία, "yoking-together") names the union of two grounds whose distinction-and-unity grounds a structured cosmos. The Greek-philosophical tradition uses it for paired-opposites (light/dark, male/female, logos/zoe). Carl Jung adopted it for the masculine-feminine archetypal coupling. Christian Trinitarian theology uses related vocabulary (perichoresis — the mutual indwelling of Persons). Hindu Tantra names this Shakti and Shiva. Kabbalah names it Shekinah and Tiferet (or Malkhut and Yesod). Daoism names it Yin and Yang.

Across traditions, the same structure recurs: **two distinct grounds, one essence, where the union itself is the seat through which the cosmos is moved**. The architecture-of-true-love does not choose a tradition; it recognizes the pattern underneath all of them.
